Beliefs 11-13 of the Saved Group
Enumerates characteristics 11-13 of the saved group.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand that the saved group affirms that human actions are created by Allah, that punishment of the grave is real, and that Allah's speech is uncreated.

Core Idea
Three core beliefs: actions created, grave punishment real, Quran uncreated.

The author continues enumerating the distinguishing beliefs of the saved group. This lesson covers the eleventh to thirteenth characteristics, which address the nature of human actions, the reality of the grave's punishment, and the uncreated status of the Qur'an. These positions differentiate Sunni orthodoxy from groups like the Qadariyya and Mu'tazila.

Guided Path
1
Read the three characteristics
Read the Arabic passage listing characteristics 11, 12, and 13. Notice the pattern: each begins with 'أن يرى' (that he believes).
The term 'يرى' indicates a firm belief.
2
Understand characteristic 11
Characteristic 11 states that actions of servants are created by Allah. This affirms Allah's role as Creator of all things, including human acts, but does not deny human acquisition.
This refutes the Qadariyya who claimed humans create their own actions.
3
Understand characteristic 12
Characteristic 12 affirms the punishment of the grave as a true reality. This is a sam'iyyat belief based on revelation.
Some sects denied the punishment of the grave.
4
Understand characteristic 13
Characteristic 13 states that Allah's speech (the Qur'an) is uncreated. This opposes the Mu'tazila who held the Qur'an was created.
This emphasizes the eternal attribute of speech.

Characteristic 12 affirms the punishment of the grave (a sam'iyyat belief). Characteristic 13 states that the Qur'an (Allah's speech) is uncreated, opposing the Mu'tazila. Belief in Saints' Miracles and Divine Justice
The author lists characteristics 31 and 32 about saintly miracles and divine justice.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the Sunni position on the miracles of saints (karamat) and the balance between divine justice and grace.

Core Idea
Saints' miracles are real, and God's justice and grace work together in determining human destiny.

This lesson covers two consecutive articles of faith from Imam Samarqandi's list. The first affirms the reality of saintly miracles, a key point against those who deny them. The second explains how God's justice and grace interact: He may bring down the fortunate by justice or elevate the wretched by grace, showing His absolute sovereignty.

Guided Path
1
Read the Arabic passage
Read the two characteristics in Arabic and English. Note that the first characteristic uses 'يعتقدان' which may be a scribal variant for 'يعتقد أن'.
Some manuscripts read 'يعتقد أن'.
2
Analyze the first characteristic: miracles of saints
Identify what is being affirmed. The saved group believes that karamat (miracles performed by saints) are true and must not be denied. This distinguishes Sunni Islam from those who reject saintly miracles.
Karamat are distinct from the miracles of prophets.
3
Analyze the second characteristic: divine justice and grace
The saved group believes that God can make a fortunate person miserable by His justice and a miserable person fortunate by His grace. This reflects the balance between God's attribute of justice and His mercy/grace.
This is not a contradiction but a recognition of God's complete authority.

Core Idea
True spiritual understanding is not equal between believers and disbelievers.

This lesson focuses on characteristic 33, which addresses a crucial point about human intellect. The saved group affirms that the intellectual capacities of disbelievers do not match those of prophets and believers, highlighting a spiritual dimension to intellect.

Guided Path

Read the characteristic

2
Understand the concept of intellect in this context
'Intellect' here refers not only to reasoning ability but also to spiritual insight. Prophets have the highest intellect due to revelation, and believers follow them.
This does not deny worldly intelligence of disbelievers.
3
Reflect on the implications for faith and reason
This belief underscores that faith enhances intellectual capacity in matters of truth, and that disbelievers are deprived of this guidance.

Allah as Eternal Creator with Knowledge and Power
The author affirms Allah's eternal creatorship and His attributes of knowledge and power.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the Sunni belief that Allah is the eternal creator and that He possesses knowledge and power as attributes.

Core Idea
Allah is always the creator, and His attributes of knowledge and power are eternal and perfect.

This lesson combines two characteristics that affirm fundamental divine attributes. The thirty-fourth asserts that Allah is eternally the creator with no change in His state, while the thirty-fifth confirms that He is all-knowing and all-powerful and possesses knowledge and power as attributes. These are central to Islamic theology.

Guided Path
1
Read the two characteristics
Read the Arabic and English. Notice the emphasis on eternity and attributes.
2
Understand the concept of eternal creation
The thirty-fourth characteristic says Allah has always been and will always be a creator. This counters the idea that creation changed God or that He was not creator before creation.
This is about God's attribute of creation, not that the universe is eternal.
3
Understand the distinction between being and having attributes
The thirty-fifth characteristic states Allah is all-knowing and all-powerful, and He has knowledge and power. This affirms that these attributes are real and distinct.
Sunni theology holds that attributes are neither identical to the essence nor separate.

Temporary Punishment for Sinful Believers
Characteristic 36 states that the punishment of sinful believers in Hell is proportionate to their sins and is true.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the Sunni belief that sinful believers may be punished in Hell temporarily according to their sins, and that this punishment is real.

Core Idea
Sinful believers are not eternally damned; their punishment in Hell is measured by their sins.

This final lesson covers characteristic 36, which addresses the fate of Muslims who commit sins. The saved group affirms that such believers may be punished in Hell according to the degree of their sins, but this punishment is not eternal. This distinguishes Sunni Islam from those who believe that Muslims who enter Hell stay forever.

Guided Path
1
Read the characteristic
Read the Arabic and English. Note that this belief is based on knowledge ('يعلم').
2
Understand the concept of temporary punishment
The saved group believes that sinful believers will be punished in Hell in proportion to their sins, but eventually they will be admitted to Paradise. This is the middle position between Khawarij (eternal) and Murji'ah (no punishment).
This applies to believers who die without repentance for sins less than shirk.
3
Contrast with eternal punishment for disbelievers
Disbelievers face eternal punishment, while believers may face temporary punishment. This distinction is crucial.

Allah's Absolute Power over the Preserved Tablet
Samarqandi discusses the Preserved Tablet and Allah's power to change fates.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand that Allah has absolute power over the Preserved Tablet and can change a person's recorded fate based on their actions and final state.

Core Idea
Your final recorded destiny can change—Allah's power over the Tablet is absolute.

In this passage, Imam Samarqandi uses several examples from the Preserved Tablet to show that a person's recorded fate is not fixed. He illustrates how individuals like the magicians of Pharaoh, Abu Bakr, and Umar had their status changed based on their actions. This challenges a common misconception about predestination and emphasizes the importance of one's final deeds.

Guided Path
1
Read the passage carefully
Read the Arabic passage once without analyzing. Then read the translation. Notice the examples of people whose names were changed in the Tablet.
Pay attention to the pattern: each example shows a change from believer to disbeliever or vice versa.
2
Identify the main claim
The passage argues that Allah can erase and establish whatever He wills in the Preserved Tablet. Find the verse reference and the hadith that support this.
The key is the phrase 'yamuḥū allāhu mā yashā'u wa yuthbitu'.
3
Reflect on the implications
Consider how this teaching affects our understanding of divine decree and human free will. Does it mean predestination is conditional?
The passage does not deny predestination but affirms Allah's power to change based on deeds.

The son of Adam was a believer in the Tablet. When he killed his brother and was not pleased with Allah's decree, Allah erased his name from the believers and wrote him as a disbeliever. Commentary
This passage presents a theological argument using examples from the Preserved Tablet (al-lawh al-mahfuz). The author demonstrates Allah's absolute power to change a person's recorded fate based on their actions. He lists several examples: Cain (Ibn Adam) who was initially a believer but became a disbeliever after murder; the magicians of Pharaoh who were disbelievers but became believers after their conversion; Abu Bakr and Umar who were disbelievers before Islam; and other figures like Balaam, Qarun, and Tha'laba. The author then quotes a hadith stating that a person may begin and end life as a disbeliever but die a believer, emphasizing that the final state is not fixed. The purpose is to affirm Allah's sovereignty over destinies and the importance of one's final actions.

Misconception & Correction
Misconception: Some people think that the Preserved Tablet fixes a person's final destiny irrevocably, so actions have no effect.
Correction: The passage shows that Allah can erase and rewrite names in the Tablet based on a person's actions and final state. Predestination does not negate the effect of deeds; rather, Allah's knowledge encompasses all, and He changes as He wills.

Key Takeaway
Allah's power over the Preserved Tablet is absolute: He can change anyone's recorded destiny based on their final actions and faith.
