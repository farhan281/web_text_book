The Reality of the Quran in Writing and Recitation
Samarqandi establishes the reality of the Quran in mus'hafs and refutes metaphorical interpretation.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand that the Quran written in mus'hafs and recited is truly the actual speech of Allah, and you will be able to refute the claim that it is merely metaphorical.

Core Idea
The Quran in our hands and on our tongues is the real, uncreated speech of Allah.

Imam Samarqandi begins by firmly establishing a foundational doctrine: the Quran that we write, recite, and even what children learn is truly the Quran, not a representation. He then addresses an opponent who claims that the Quran in mus'hafs is not real, using a logical argument about how Jibril heard and conveyed it. This lesson will guide you through this crucial defense of the Quran's reality.

Guided Path
1
Read the initial assertions
Read the Arabic passage and its translation. Notice how Samarqandi repeatedly affirms that what is written, recited, and even children's tablets contain the real Quran (bil-ḥaqīqa).
The term 'ḥaqīqa' (reality) is key—it contrasts with 'majāz' (metaphor).
2
Analyze the Jibril argument
Follow the reductio ad absurdum: if Jibril heard the Quran in reality but delivered it metaphorically, that would be concealment. But revelation is in reality, so denial is invalid.
Notice how the argument forces the opponent to choose between two untenable positions.
3
Reflect on the consequence
The author concludes that denying the reality of the Quran in mus'hafs leads to denying revelation itself, which is kufr. Consider the seriousness of this position.

Commentary
This passage is a sustained defense of the doctrine that the Quran is the actual, uncreated speech of Allah, addressing and refuting opposing views. The author first establishes that what is written in mus'hafs, recited, and even written by children on tablets is truly the Quran (al-haqīqa). Then he affirms that the Quran is Allah's speech, uncreated. He refutes an opponent who claims that what is in mus'hafs is not real Quran: he argues via reductio about Jibril's hearing and revelation—if Jibril heard it in reality and sent it down metaphorically, that would be concealment; but it was sent down in reality. If part was sent down in reality and part metaphorically, that would create two Qur'ans, which is absurd. Denial of revelation is kufr. He then refutes another claim that there is no Quran in the world at all, citing numerous verses that refer to the Quran being sent down, and that the name 'Book' implies something written. He further uses the commands to recite and listen to the Quran as proof that the recited words are truly Allah's speech. He cites verses where the disbelievers 'hear the speech of Allah' and 'until he hears the speech of Allah' to show that the recited Quran is indeed Allah's speech. He argues that if the mus'hafs did not contain the real Quran, then Allah would not have bestowed the Opening and the great Quran upon the Prophet. The prohibition of touching mus'hafs except with purity (56:79) shows that what is inside is indeed the Quran. Finally, he clarifies the distinction between the uncreated divine speech (which is without letters, sounds, etc.) and its created forms of recitation and writing. Allah's speech in His essence is without letters or sounds; Jibril heard it in that manner and then recited it to the Prophet with letters and sounds. The recitation and writing are created acts, but what is recited and written is the uncreated speech of Allah. There is no difference in reality: all of it is the same uncreated speech of Allah.

Scriptural Proofs and the Distinction of Divine Speech
Samarqandi provides multiple scriptural proofs and distinguishes between uncreated speech and created recitation.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand why the Quran in mus'hafs is the uncreated speech of Allah, supported by verses, commands, and the prohibition of touching, and you will grasp the distinction between the divine speech and its created recitation.

Core Idea
The Quran is uncreated; its recitation and writing are created acts but convey the same uncreated speech.

In this second part, Imam Samarqandi provides multiple scriptural proofs that the Quran is the actual speech of Allah. He cites verses about its revelation, commands to recite and listen, and the prohibition of touching mus'hafs without purity. Finally, he clarifies a crucial point: Allah's speech in His essence is without letters or sounds, yet the recited Quran with letters and sounds is still that same uncreated speech. This lesson will take you through these proofs and the important distinction.

Guided Path
1
Examine the scriptural proofs
Read the list of verses that speak of the Quran being sent down (tanzīl). Notice how Samarqandi uses them to argue that denying the Quran in mus'hafs is denying all these verses.
Key verses include 25:1, 15:9, 20:1-2, etc.
2
Understand the commands
The author cites commands to recite (73:20) and to listen to the Quran (7:204). These commands imply that what is recited is truly Allah's speech.
Also consider the verse about hearing Allah's speech (2:75, 9:6).
3
Analyze the prohibition argument
The verse 'None touch it except the purified' (56:79) is used as evidence: if the mus'hafs did not contain the real Quran, the prohibition would be meaningless.
This is a powerful argument from sacred practice.
4
Grasp the distinction
Finally, Samarqandi distinguishes between Allah's uncreated speech (without letters, sounds, or time) and its created manifestation in recitation and writing, yet affirms they are the same speech.
This resolves the apparent tension between the uncreatedness and the created forms.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the Sunni distinction between the uncreated Quranic speech and the created physical script, and how to answer common objections.

Core Idea
