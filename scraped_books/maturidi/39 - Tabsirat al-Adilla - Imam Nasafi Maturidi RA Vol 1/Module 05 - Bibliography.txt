Catalogue of Abu al-Mu'in al-Nasafi's Works
Enumeration of Nasafi's works with manuscript locations and editorial notes.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the major works of Abu al-Mu'in al-Nasafi, their manuscript locations, and some corrections to misattributions.

Core Idea
Nasafi's works catalog the heritage of Maturidi theology.

This passage provides a bibliographical overview of the writings of the Maturidi scholar Abu al-Mu'in al-Nasafi. It lists his known works, where they are found in libraries, and corrects two common misattributions. Familiarity with this catalogue helps contextualize the primary text, Tabsirat al-Adilla.

Guided Path
1
Read the passage and note the list
Read the passage carefully, noting the eight works listed from 1 to 8. Observe the manuscript locations mentioned for each.
The passage includes footnotes with additional information.
2
Identify which works are lost
Notice that works 6, 7, and 8 are explicitly noted as not located ('لم نهتد إليه'). This shows the state of preservation.
3
Examine the footnote corrections
The footnotes correct two misattributions: al-'Alim wa al-Muta'allim is by Abu Hanifa, not Nasafi; and al-'Umda fi Usul al-Din is by a later scholar.
This demonstrates scholarly care in attribution.
4
Reflect on the significance
Consider why a modern edition includes such a bibliography. It establishes the author's corpus and critical apparatus.
This passage is part of the editor's introduction.

Commentary
This passage is a bibliographical enumeration of the works of Abu al-Mu'in al-Nasafi. It lists eight works, including Tabsirat al-Adilla (the focus of this book), Al-Tamhid li-Qawa'id al-Tawhid, Bahr al-Kalam, Sayd al-Qawa'id fi 'Ilm al-'Aqa'id, Manahij al-A'imma fi al-Furu', al-Ifsad li-Khud' Ahl al-Ilhad, Idah al-Hujja fi Kawn al-'Aql Hujja, and Sharh al-Jami' al-Kabir. For each work, the passage provides bibliographic references and manuscript locations in various libraries such as Istanbul, Cairo, and al-Azhar. It also indicates that works 6-8 have not been located. The footnotes add additional information: a commentary on Bahr al-Kalam exists, and a correction of misattributions—specifically that the book al-'Alim wa al-Muta'allim is by Abu Hanifa, not Nasafi, and that al-'Umda fi Usul al-Din is by a later 'Abd Allah al-Nasafi. This passage demonstrates the scholarly apparatus of the modern edition, preserving manuscript evidence and correcting errors.
