Introducing 'Naqd al-Mantiq': Ibn Taymiyya's Critique of Aristotelian Logic
Title page presenting the book, author, editor, and publisher.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the author, title, and scholarly context of 'Naqd al-Mantiq' and explain why this work is significant in the Islamic critique of logic.

Core Idea
Ibn Taymiyya's 'Naqd al-Mantiq' is a landmark critique of Greek logic from an Islamic epistemological standpoint.

In this lesson, we examine the title page of Ibn Taymiyya's 'Naqd al-Mantiq'. Though seemingly simple, this bibliographic information anchors the work in its historical and scholarly context. Understanding who authored it, under what editorial supervision, and within which publishing tradition helps us appreciate the weight of the arguments that follow.

Guided Path
1
Identify the main title and author
Look at the Arabic text: the title is 'نقض المنطق' and the author is 'شيخ الإسلام أحمد بن عبد الحليم بن عبد السلام ابن تيمية'. Note the honorific title 'Shaykh al-Islam'.
2
Note the author's lifespan
The dates (661-728 AH) place him in the medieval Islamic period. He was a contemporary of the Mongol invasions and a fierce defender of Sunni orthodoxy.
This historical context influenced his critique of logic.
3
Recognize the editorial and publishing context
The editing by Abd al-Rahman ibn Hasan Qa'id and approval by Bakr ibn Abd Allah Abu Zayd shows the work's continued relevance and scholarly chain.
Publishers: Dar 'Ata'at al-'Ilm and Dar Ibn Hazm.
4
Understand the book's place in the Islamic critique of logic
This book is a direct refutation of Aristotelian logic as used in Islamic theology and jurisprudence. It argues for the superiority of prophetic knowledge over Greek rationalism.
This sets the stage for the entire course.

The Opening Questions: Salaf, Ahl al-Hadith, and the Status of Logic
Author presents a question about the Salaf's madhhab and the status of logic.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the key questions this book addresses regarding the authority of the Salaf and the status of logic.

Core Idea
The author frames his critique by asking whether logic is a collective obligation and whether later sciences surpass the Salaf.

This passage opens the book with a series of questions that set the agenda. The author is asked to compare the creed of the Salaf with that of later scholars, to declare whether Ahl al-Hadith are the saved sect, and to rule on the status of logic. The answer begins with a Quranic verse that anchors authority in the way of the believers. This lesson will help you unpack these questions and understand the author's starting point.

Guided Path
1
Identify the five questions
Read the question section and list the distinct issues raised: (1) Salaf vs. later scholars in creed, (2) which madhhab the author adopts, (3) whether Ahl al-Hadith are the saved sect, (4) whether new sciences after the Salaf were unknown to them, (5) the status of logic as a collective obligation.
These questions foreshadow the entire book's content.
2
Locate the author's initial answer
Note how the author acknowledges the breadth of the questions ('These questions, their elaboration would fill volumes') but promises to point out the important part. This shows his awareness of the scope and his intention to focus on key issues.
3
Analyze the Quranic citation
Verse 4:115 warns against opposing the Messenger and following other than the way of the believers. The author uses it to imply that the Salaf's path is the standard, as they are the believers of that era. This sets the theological foundation for the rest of the work.
4
Connect to the book's purpose
Understanding these questions helps you see that the book will defend the Salafi creed and critique innovations, particularly the adoption of Greek logic as an essential tool for knowledge.

Defining the Salaf's Creed on Attributes
Author defines the salaf's creed in belief and attributes, opposing interpretation and anthropomorphism.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to define the salaf's creed regarding Allah's attributes and distinguish it from interpretive and anthropomorphic approaches.

Core Idea
The Salaf affirmed attributes without interpretation, comparison, or modality — passing them as they came.

In this opening passage, the author establishes the creedal foundation of the saved sect. He defines their method in handling Allah's names and attributes by citing key Quranic verses and a statement from Imam al-Shafi'i. This lesson unpacks that definition so you can recognize and apply it in theological discussions.

Guided Path
1
Identify the Quranic grounds
Read the three verses cited (4:115, 9:100, 48:18). What do they establish about the authority of the early believers' way?
These verses are foundational because they tie the saved way to following the Companions and those who follow them.
2
Extract the definition of the Salaf's method
Find the sentence beginning with 'فَمِنْ سَبِيلِهِمْ' — this gives the positive and negative aspects of their creed regarding attributes.
The definition includes both affirmation (iman bi sifati Allah) and negation (without tahrif, tashbih, etc.).
3
Analyze the key terms
Focus on 'الإمرار' (imrar) and 'التفويض' (tafwid): how they 'passed them on' and 'relegated knowledge' to Allah. What does this imply about attributive language?
Imrar means reciting the texts without delving into how; tafwid means consigning the true knowledge of modality to Allah.
4
Compare with other approaches
The passage contrasts the Salaf's method with those who interpret (ta'wil) or liken (tashbih). Identify these opposing methods in the text.
The author opposes any interpretation that contradicts the apparent meaning ('ma yukhalifu zahiraha') and any likening to creation ('tashbih').
5
Apply the definition
Use the definition to classify a contemporary statement about an attribute: e.g., 'Allah's mercy is like a mother's mercy but greater.' Is this consistent with the Salaf's way?
Answer: It might be considered tashbih unless stated as a comparison without affirming similarity.

The passage concludes by emphasizing that the later generations inherited this from the earlier ones, advising strict adherence and warning against deviation.

The Danger of Speculative Inquiry: The Case of Sabigh
Author recounts the story of Umar's punishment of Sabigh for asking about ambiguous Quranic verses.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the epistemological stance against speculative inquiry into ambiguous Quranic verses as illustrated by the story of Umar and Sabigh.

Core Idea
Umar's treatment of Sabigh shows that speculative logic has no place in Allah's revelation.

This lesson examines a pivotal historical incident that the author uses to set the stage for his critique of logic. The story of Umar and Sabigh illustrates the early Islamic caution against delving into ambiguous Quranic verses (mutashabihat) with human reasoning alone. Understanding this story will help you see how the author frames the limits of logical inquiry in matters of faith.

Guided Path
1
Identify the key facts of the incident
Read the story and note the sequence: Sabigh asks about ambiguous verses, Umar investigates, finds him with a shaven head (a sign of Kharijite tendencies), punishes him, and later accepts his repentance.
2
Analyze Umar's justification
Why did Umar react so harshly? The passage suggests that Sabigh's persistent questioning of mutashabihat was seen as a threat to communal unity and a potential gateway to fitna. Umar's punishment was corrective, not merely punitive.
3
Extract the underlying epistemological principle
The story implies that some matters of divine revelation are not meant to be subjected to human logical scrutiny. The author uses this to argue against the application of Greek logic to Quranic interpretation.
4
Connect to the author's broader critique
This incident is a front-matter example that the author will later use to support his argument that logic, when applied to theological questions, leads to division and error. The story stands as a cautionary tale.

Learning Objective

Core Idea
Islamic logic is superior because it is rooted in divine bayān, which integrates intellectual understanding and verbal expression.

In this opening section of Naqd al-Mantiq, Ibn Taymiyya sets the stage by contrasting the Greek logical tradition with an Islamic one. He references al-Baqillani's critique of Greek divisions and then develops the concept of bayān—clarification taught by God—as the epistemological basis for Islamic logic. For Ibn Taymiyya, true logical clarity is not merely a formal system but a divinely gifted capacity that involves both the heart (intellectual apprehension) and the tongue (articulation). Ibn Taymiyya is arguing that Islamic logic is comprehensive and divinely guided.

Commentary
This passage is a front_matter section from Ibn Taymiyya's Naqd Mantiq, where he argues for the superiority of Islamic logic over Greek logic. Commentary
This passage is the table of contents of Ibn Taymiyya's 'Naqd Mantiq'. It outlines the entire structure of the work, beginning with the general benefit of logic, then critiquing the logicians' claims about definitions (al-hudud) through sixteen 'aspects' (wujuh), followed by a discussion of syllogism (al-qiyas) in five aspects, and concluding with a comparison of Arab expression to Greek logic and indices. The sixteen aspects of definitions form the core of his critique, challenging the logicians' assertion that definitions are necessary for conceptualization and that they yield certain knowledge. Each aspect attacks a different claim: the necessity of definitions, their ability to distinguish essences, the reliance on essential vs. accidental attributes, etc. The later sections on qiyas mirror this critical approach. This TOC is valuable for understanding the argumentative flow of the book.

Navigating Ibn Taymiyya's Critique: The Structure of Naqd al-Mantiq
Lists main topics and page numbers for each section of the book.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to locate the major topics and aspects in the table of contents of Naqd al-Mantiq and identify the two main subjects of critique: definition (hadd) and syllogism (qiyas).

Core Idea
The table of contents is your roadmap to Ibn Taymiyya's systematic critique of Aristotelian logic.

This lesson examines the table of contents of Ibn Taymiyya's Naqd al-Mantiq. It is not a theoretical argument but a practical guide to the book's structure. Understanding this layout will help you trace the progression of his critique across different topics and efficiently find specific arguments.

Guided Path
1
Identify the two main sections
Scan the list for the two major headings: the sixteen 'wujuh' (aspects) on definitions starting with 'الوجه الثامن', and the section on syllogism beginning with 'الكلام على القياس في مقامين'.
The table also includes a smaller section on the superiority of Arab logic over philosophy and a list of indices.
2
Count the aspects on definitions
Note that the numbered aspects run from the eighth to the sixteenth. Count them to see the depth of critique on definitions alone.
The first seven aspects are not listed in this table; they appear earlier in the book.
3
Locate the syllogism treatment
After the definition aspects, find the heading 'ما في القياس من الحق وما فيه من التكلف' and the five later aspects on syllogism (الأول to الخامس).
The syllogism section has two stations: first a discussion of truth and artificiality, then five additional aspects.
4
Review the indices
At the end, note the extensive indices: Quran, hadith, reports, poetry, persons, sects, books, places, and topics.
These indices help locate specific citations within the book.

The table concludes with a list of indices (faharis) for Quranic verses, hadith, reports, poetry, notable persons, sects, books, places, and topics. The page numbers indicate where each section begins. This table provides the overall structure of the book, showing its critical focus on definitions and syllogism from an Islamic perspective.
