Core Idea
Suhrawardi's definition is about real existents; nominal definition is about concepts regardless of existence.

This passage from Suhrawardi clarifies a fundamental distinction in the theory of definition. While nominal definitions merely explain the meaning of a term without concern for whether the concept corresponds to anything real, Suhrawardi's own 'definition by concept and attention' deals with actually existing things. Understanding this difference is crucial for grasping Suhrawardi's unique contribution to Islamic logic and its application in Islamic sciences.
