Burhan Limmi and Burhan Inni: Two Types of Demonstration
Author defines burhan and distinguishes its two types based on the middle term

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to distinguish burhan limmi from burhan inni by identifying whether the middle term is a real cause or a mere sign.

Core Idea
The middle term decides: cause-based or sign-based demonstration?

Demonstration (burhan) is the gold standard of certainty in Islamic logic. Not all certain syllogisms are alike: the nature of the middle term changes whether we know why something is true or merely that it is. This lesson unpacks that crucial distinction.

Guided Path
1
Identify the definiendum and genus
The passage defines burhan as a syllogism (qiyas) with certain premises (muqaddimat yaqiniyya) producing certain knowledge. Note: the genus is 'syllogism' and the differentia is 'certain premises'.
Certainty (yaqin) is the goal; not all syllogisms achieve it.
2
Examine the two types of middle term
Burhan limmi: the middle term is the cause ('illa) of the major's connection to the minor, both in the mind and externally. Burhan inni: the middle term is known only in the mind (not a real cause).
The same syllogism structure can be read as limmi or inni depending on the ontological status of the middle term.
3
Map the causal vs. sign relationship
In limmi, the middle term explains why the conclusion is true (cause → effect). In inni, the middle term is only a sign or effect (effect → cause). Example: intoxicant (cause) → prohibition (effect) is limmi; smoke (effect) → fire (cause) is inni.
4
Apply the distinction to a new example
Take the argument: 'Every changing thing is temporally originated; the world is changing; therefore the world is temporally originated.' Here change is the cause of temporal origination in reality (since change implies time). This is burhan limmi.
Compare with: 'Every crescent moon is sighted after sunset; this moon is sighted after sunset; therefore it is a crescent.' The middle term 'sighted after sunset' is not the cause of being a crescent but a sign.

Demonstration (burhan) is the highest form of proof in Islamic logic, providing certain knowledge through certain premises. But not all demonstrations are the same: some reveal the reason why something is true, while others only establish that it is true. This classification is crucial for evaluating arguments in Kalam, Fiqh, and other Islamic sciences.

Burhan Limmi vs 'Inniy: Two Types of Demonstration
Author defines burhan and divides it into 'inniy and limmi types.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to distinguish burhan limmi from burhan 'inniy and construct a simple demonstrative syllogism of each type using Islamic examples.

Core Idea
Burhan limmi reasons from cause to effect; burhan 'inniy reasons from effect to cause — both yield certainty.

In this lesson, we explore the two types of burhan (demonstration) that form the backbone of Islamic proofs. Understanding the direction of reasoning is crucial for evaluating arguments in kalam and usul al-fiqh. We will see how the same facts can be arranged to demonstrate either a cause or an effect.

Guided Path
1
Identify the genus and differentia of burhan
Burhan is a composite syllogism (qiyas murakkab). Its differentia is that it is composed of certain premises (muqaddimat yaqiniyya) and yields certain knowledge.
2
Analyze the limmi example
In 'This person has putrid humors, and every person with putrid humors is feverish, so this person is feverish,' the middle term 'putrid humors' is the cause in reality. The conclusion (fever) is the effect.
Burhan limmi goes from cause to effect, both in mind and external reality.
3
Analyze the 'inniy example
In 'This person is feverish, and every feverish person has putrid humors, so this person has putrid humors,' the middle term 'feverish' is a sign (effect) that points to the cause only in the mind.
Burhan 'inniy goes from effect to cause; the middle term is a cause only in the mind (i.e., a sign).
4
Compare the two types
Limmi: cause to effect (middle term = real cause). 'Inniy: effect to cause (middle term = sign). Both are valid demonstrations because the premises are certain.
The direction of reasoning differs, but both produce certain knowledge.
5
Apply to Islamic science
In kalam, proving God's existence from creation is 'inniy (effect to cause). Proving a property of creation from a divine attribute would be limmi (cause to effect).
This distinction helps in evaluating the strength of theological arguments.

Burhan Limmi and Burhan Inni: Two Types of Demonstration
Author defines demonstration and distinguishes limmi from inni.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to distinguish between burhan limmi and burhan inni and identify the causal role of the middle term in each.

Core Idea
Demonstration is either from cause to effect or from effect to cause.

We already know that demonstration (burhan) is a certain syllogism. Now we learn its two fundamental types: one that explains why something is true by citing the real cause, and one that merely proves that it is true by reasoning from the effect. This distinction is crucial for evaluating the strength of proofs in theology and jurisprudence.
