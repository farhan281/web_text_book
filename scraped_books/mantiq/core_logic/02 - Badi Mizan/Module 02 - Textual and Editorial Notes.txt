Understanding Manuscript Sigla in Islamic Editions
Editor describes the two manuscripts used and the textual sources for the matn.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to interpret the manuscript sigla used in a critical edition of a classical Islamic text and explain the editor's rationale for source selection.

Core Idea
Critical editions use sigla to track manuscripts; decoding them reveals editorial judgment.

When studying a classical Islamic text, you often encounter symbols like (خ) or (ط) in the margins. These are not random—they are the editor's shorthand for the manuscripts used in preparing the edition. This passage from the editorial preface of Badi' al-Mizan explains exactly which manuscripts were used and why. Learning to read these sigla is essential for evaluating the reliability of any scholarly edition.

Guided Path
1
Identify the editorial sigla
Locate each siglum (symbol) used in the passage. Here we have (خ), (ط), (ش), and (الأصل). Note what each stands for.
Siglum is the technical term for the abbreviation.
2
Match siglum to manuscript type and source
For each siglum, read the editor's description: is it a manuscript, a printed edition, a commentary copy, etc.? Identify which part of the text (commentary or matn) it supports.
For example, (خ) is a manuscript of the commentary, (ط) is a printed commentary with glosses.
3
Understand the editorial rationale
The editor explains why (خ) was chosen despite others being 'superior': completeness, clarity, and the pedagogical goal (clear edition for students, no profit). Note the priority and potential limitations.
This helps you assess the edition's trustworthiness.
4
Apply to the matn sources separately
Notice that the matn has its own sigla: (ش) from the commentary edition and (الأصل) from an old print. Distinguish between sources for the commentary and sources for the base text.
Editions often use different witnesses for the commentary and the original matn.

النسخ المعتمد عليها في هذا العمل اعتمدنا في إخراج الشرح على نسختين: الأولى : نسخة خطية تامة بخط لا بأس به، نسخت عام ١٣٨٩هـ، ووقع الاختيار عليها من بين سائر المخطوطات لاكتمالها، ووضوحها، ووقفنا بعد العمل على غيرها مما هو أولى، والعذر في الاكتفاء بها أن الهدف إخراج الكتاب للطلبة بصورة واضحة بلا أي مكسب مالي والله أعلم، ورمزنا لها بحرف (خ). الثانية: النسخة المطبوعة والتي عليها حواشي عبدالحي اللكنوي، وعبدالقادر الكاكري القندهاري، وعبيد الله السليمانخيلي القندهاري، والتي صورتها مكتبة رشيدية ورمزنا لها بحرف (ط). وفي إخراج المتن على نسخيتن أيضا: الأولى: نسخة المتن التي مع شرح الطلنبي المطبوع مع الحواشي السابقة الذكر، ورمزنا لها برمز (ش). الثاني: نسخة المتن ،وحده، وقد طبعت عام ١٢٨٤هـ في مطبعة كلسان كشمنه، نسخة تامة وواضحة، وعليها تعليقات من البديع، ورمزنا لها بكلمة (الأصل)؛ وهي لقدمها.
The manuscripts relied upon in this work. We relied in producing the commentary on two copies: First: a complete manuscript copy in acceptable handwriting, copied in the year 1389 AH, and it was chosen from among the other manuscripts for its completeness and clarity, and after working we found others that were superior, but the excuse for being satisfied with it is that the goal is to produce the book for students in a clear form without any financial gain, and Allah knows best. We denoted it with the letter (خ). Second: the printed copy which has glosses by Abd al-Hayy al-Lakhnawi, Abd al-Qadir al-Kakri al-Qandahari, and Ubayd Allah al-Sulaymankhili al-Qandahari, and it was photographed by the Rashidiyya Library, and we denoted it with the letter (ط). And in producing the text (matn) we also used two copies: First: the text copy that comes with al-Talanbi's commentary printed with the aforementioned glosses, and we denoted it with the symbol (ش). Second: the text copy alone, printed in the year 1284 AH at the Kulsan Kashmir printing press, a complete and clear copy, with comments from al-Badi', and we denoted it with the word (الأصل) due to its age.

Commentary
This passage describes the manuscript sources for the edition of Badi Mizan. The editor used two copies of the commentary: a manuscript (خ) and a printed copy (ط) with glosses. Additionally, for the matn (text), two copies were used: one from the printed commentary (ش) and an older standalone print (الأصل). The editor explains the choice of the manuscript despite there being better ones, because the goal was to produce a clear student edition without profit.
