Recognizing a Publisher's Catalog in a Logic Text
Publisher's catalog listing printed books

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify a publisher's catalog page in a classical logic text and explain the significance of the works listed.

Core Idea
A publisher's list reveals the standard curriculum of logic in Islamic education.

This page is not a logical argument but a list of books published by the same press. However, it provides valuable context: it tells us which texts were considered essential for logic students. Among them is our very own 'Sharh Tahzeeb', alongside other foundational works like 'Isaghuji' (a famous introduction to logic) and 'Al-Siraji' (on inheritance law). Understanding this list helps us see where our text fits in the broader tradition.

Guided Path
1
Identify the type of page
Look at the content: it lists book titles, not arguments or definitions. This is a publisher's catalog, likely from the back matter of the book.
2
List the books mentioned
The page mentions: Sharh Tahzeeb, Isaghuji, Al-Siraji, and Sharh 'Uqud Rasm al-Mufti. Write them down.
Some titles may be abbreviated.
3
Categorize each book by subject
Sharh Tahzeeb and Isaghuji are works of logic. Al-Siraji is on inheritance (fara'id). Sharh 'Uqud Rasm al-Mufti is on legal methodology (usul al-fiqh).
This shows the range of sciences in the curriculum.
4
Note the significance
These books were commonly studied together in madrasas. The list reflects the standard syllabus for a student of logic and related sciences.
Your textbook 'Sharh Tahzeeb' was part of this ecosystem.

Commentary
This passage is a publisher's catalog, not a lesson in logic. It lists printed books including 'Sharh Tahzeeb' (the commentary we are studying) and other foundational logic texts like 'Isaghuji' (a famous epitome of logic) and 'Al-Siraji' (a work on inheritance). Also mentioned is 'Sharh 'Uqud Rasm al-Mufti' on legal methodology. The list appears in the back matter of the book, perhaps as an advertisement. No logical argument or definition is present.
