Parsing the Attachment of 'Lana' in the Author's Invocation
Commentator parses the word 'lana' as attached to either 'ja'ala' or to 'rafeeq'.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the two possible grammatical attachments of 'lana' in 'ja'ala lana' and explain the reasoning behind each attachment.

Core Idea
The same Arabic phrase can be parsed in multiple ways, each affecting meaning.

In this lesson, we examine the commentator's grammatical parsing of the author's invocation 'وجعل لنا'. The commentator considers two possible attachments for the adverb 'لنا', each leading to a different interpretation. Understanding these nuances is essential for reading the matn accurately and mirrors similar analyses in tafsir and nahw.

Guided Path
1
Identify the Phrase in Question
Locate the phrase 'وجعل لنا' in the source text. Recognize that 'لنا' is a prepositional phrase (zarf) that can grammatically attach to different elements in the sentence.
The phrase is from the matn, but the commentator is analyzing its syntactic role.
2
Recognize Two Possible Attachments
The commentator states: the adverb is either attached to the verb 'جعل' (with the lam indicating benefit) or attached to the noun 'رفيق' (in the predicate). Understand that the choice affects the meaning: attachment to verb emphasizes the act of making, attachment to noun emphasizes the companion.
The lam of benefit is similar to that in Quranic verse 'وجعل لكم الأرض فراشا'.
3
Analyze Grammatical Implications
If attached to 'رفيق', then the object of the genitive (the pronoun in 'لنا') is advanced before the genitive noun, which is allowed because the phrase is an adverb, and adverbs permit such fronting.
This is a known flexibility in Arabic syntax for ظرف.
4
Evaluate Which Attachment is Preferable
The commentator concludes: the first (attached to 'جعل') is closer to the wording, the second (attached to 'رفيق') is closer to the intended meaning. Consider why each might be favored.
This reflects a common tension between literal form and deeper meaning.

قوله وجعل لنا الظرف إما متعلق بـ"جعل"، و"اللام" للانتفاع كما قيل في قوله تعالى: ﴿وجَعَلَ لَكُمُ الْأَرْضَ فِرَاشا﴾، وإما بـ"رفيق"، ويكون تقديم معمول المضاف إليه على المضاف؛ لكونه ظرفا، والظرف مما يتوسع فيه ما لا يتوسع في غيره. والأول أقرب لفظاً، والثاني معنى.
His statement: 'and He made for us' – the adverb is either attached to 'made', and the 'lam' is for benefit as said in His statement 'and He made for you the earth a bed', or attached to 'companion', and then the object of the genitive construction is advanced over the genitive because it is an adverb, and the adverb allows expansion that others do not. The first is closer to wording, the second to meaning.

Commentary
This passage is a grammatical analysis of the author's phrase 'وجعل لنا' in the prologue. The commentator considers two possible attachments for 'لنا': either as an adverb attached to the verb 'جعل' (with the lam indicating benefit), or attached to the noun 'رفيق' in the predicate. The first option is closer to the wording, while the second is closer to the intended meaning. The commentator notes that adverbs allow more flexibility in Arabic syntax.
