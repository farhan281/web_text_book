The Scope of Truth-Value in Propositions
Author explains that truth/falsehood applies only to propositions about matters past, present, or future, not to performatives or restrictives.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify whether a given Arabic sentence is a declarative proposition (khabariyya) capable of truth-value or a performative/restrictive that is not.

Core Idea
Truth-value belongs only to propositions about what is, was, or will be—not to commands, questions, or restrictions.

Logic deals only with propositions that can be true or false. But not every sentence in Arabic is a proposition. Performatives (insha'iyyat) like commands and restrictives (taqyidiyyat) are excluded. This lesson explains why, drawing directly from our source passage.

Guided Path
1
Identify the temporal dimensions
The passage states that the two sides of the relation (subject and predicate) must be past, present, or future. Notice that truth-value requires a state of affairs that has occurred, is occurring, or will occur. For example, 'The prophets died' (past) can be checked against reality.
Temporality is the first condition for a proposition to have truth-value.
2
Distinguish declarative from performative
The author explains that performatives (insha'iyyat) do not have pre-existing meaning. In a command like 'Pray!', the obligation comes into existence only after the utterance. Therefore, there is no prior meaning to correspond to, so truth-value does not apply.
Performatives include commands, prohibitions, questions, and interjections.
3
Recognize restrictives (taqyidiyyat)
Restrictives are sentences that are conditioned or incomplete in judgment. The passage says they are 'devoid of judgment' (khaliyya min al-hukm). These are not full affirmations or denials, so they too lack truth-value.
Examples may include hypothetical or conditional statements without assertion.
4
Apply the classification
Given a sentence, first check if it asserts a state of affairs about past, present, or future. If yes, it is a khabariyya proposition. If it creates its meaning upon utterance (command, question, etc.) or is restricted in judgment, it is not subject to truth-value.
This is the fundamental filter before any logical analysis.

The matter from the two sides of the relation is past, present, or future, and there is no performance in performatives and restrictives. (1) Because the utterance conveys the meaning, so the meaning obtained must precede in order to see if the utterance corresponds to it or not. Performatives do not obtain meaning except after utterance, so correspondence is not realized. (2) Example: The prophets died. (3) Example: I am writing now. (4) Example: I am traveling tomorrow. (5) Because they are devoid of judgment in restrictives, and judgment occurs after utterance in performatives.

Commentary
This passage explains the condition under which a proposition can be evaluated as true or false. The author states that the affair (al-amr) from the two sides of the relation (i.e., the subject and predicate) must exist in the past, present, or future. This means that for a proposition to be declarative (khabariyya), the state of affairs it describes must have a temporal reality. Then he adds that there is no 'performance' (ada') in insha'iyyat (performatives) and taqyidiyyat (restrictives). The footnote clarifies: In a declarative proposition, the meaning must precede the utterance so we can check correspondence (mutabaqa). But in performatives like commands and prohibitions, the meaning only occurs after the utterance, so there is no pre-existing meaning to correspond to, hence no truth-value. Similarly, in restrictives (sentences that are not affirmed or denied?), they lack judgment, so truth-value does not apply. Examples: 'The prophets died' (past), 'I am writing now' (present), 'I am traveling tomorrow' (future). This establishes the scope of logic: it deals with declarative propositions that can be true or false. The student should note that performative sentences are not propositions in the logical sense, so they are not analyzed for truth-value.
