Who Wrote Isaghuji? The Life of al-Abhari
Biographical introduction of Athir al-Din al-Abhari

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify Athir al-Din al-Abhari as the author of Isaghuji and list his major works in logic, philosophy, and astronomy.

Core Idea
Isaghuji's author was a master of logic and astronomy, making his primer authoritative.

Before diving into logic, it is essential to know who wrote the foundational text you are about to study. Athir al-Din al-Abhari's Isaghuji has been the standard introduction to logic for centuries. This lesson introduces you to the man behind the work: his full name, his fields of expertise, and his prolific scholarly output. Understanding his background will deepen your appreciation for the text's authority and its place in the Islamic intellectual tradition.

Guided Path
1
Identify the author's full name and epithet
From the Arabic passage, locate the author's full name: al-Mufaddal ibn Umar ibn al-Mufaddal al-Abhari al-Samarqandi. Note his honorific title 'Athir al-Din' and his death date (663 AH / 1264 CE).
The name 'al-Abhari' refers to his birthplace (Abhar, in modern Iran).
2
List the fields he engaged in
The passage describes him as a logician (mantiqi) with engagement in philosophy (hikma), natural sciences (tabi'iyyat), and astronomy (falak). Recognize that he was not only a logician but a polymath.
3
Memorize the key work: Isaghuji and its significance
The passage states that many commentaries were written on Isaghuji due to its importance. This textbook is one such commentary. Appreciate that Isaghuji is a concise, accessible introduction that became the standard for centuries.
Isaghuji is derived from Porphyry's Isagoge, adapted to Islamic logic.

Shams al-Din al-Fanari: The Scholar Behind the Commentary
Biography of the commentator of Isaghuji, Shams al-Din al-Fanari

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the key biographical details of Shams al-Din al-Fanari and explain their relevance to the authority of his commentary on Isaghuji.

Core Idea
Al-Fanari’s life of learning, teaching, and service gives his commentary both scholarly and historical weight.

This passage introduces the author of our commentary, Shams al-Din al-Fanari. Before diving into logical technicalities, it is essential to know who wrote these words and why his commentary became a standard work. His biography reveals a scholar deeply immersed in both the rational and religious sciences, and his proximity to the Ottoman court added influence. Understanding his background helps us appreciate the authority of al-Fawaid al-Fanariyya in the study of logic.

Guided Path
1
Identify the full name and teachers
Start by noting al-Fanari’s full name: Muhammad ibn Hamza ibn Muhammad Shams al-Din al-Fanari. His teachers include Ala al-Din al-Aswad and Jamal al-Din al-Aqra'i. This pedigree establishes his direct link to earlier scholarly chains.
2
Recognize his judicial and scholarly roles
He served as a judge in Bursa and gained favor with Sultan Bayezid Khan. This indicates that his scholarship was not isolated but integrated into the Ottoman legal and intellectual system. His authoring of a tafsir on Surat al-Fatiha shows mastery in tafsir as well as logic.
3
Note the significance of his two pilgrimages
The first Hajj (822 AH) included a visit to Egypt where he met scholars, highlighting his engagement with the broader Islamic world. The second (833 AH) was a thanksgiving for restored eyesight. These travels enriched his learning and reputation.
His recovery from blindness is a noteworthy personal detail that adds to his stature.
4
List his major works
Besides the famous commentary on Isaghuji (al-Fawaid al-Fanariyya), he wrote treatises on rational sciences (Uwaydat al-Afkar), usul al-fiqh (Fusul al-Bada'i'), an encyclopedia of sciences (Unmudhaj al-Ulum), and a commentary on inheritance law (Sharh al-Fara'id al-Sirajiyya). This breadth shows his expertise in multiple Islamic sciences.
The commentary we study is specifically Sharh Isaghuji fi al-Mantiq, known as al-Fawaid al-Fanariyya.
5
Connect biography to commentary authority
Al-Fanari's comprehensive knowledge, his role as a judge, his travels, and his extensive writings all contribute to the credibility of his commentary. His work on Isaghuji is not merely a student exercise but the product of a mature scholar who applied logical tools to real legal and theological questions.
This explains why al-Fawaid al-Fanariyya became a standard text in Ottoman madrasas.

Definition of the commentator of the text 'Isaghuji': The scholar Shams al-Din al-Fanari (1350 - 1431 CE). He is the excellent scholar in all sciences, especially in logic and principles (usul), Muhammad ibn Hamza ibn Muhammad Shams al-Din al-Fanari (or al-Fanari al-Rumi).
