Definition of Poetry and Its Imaginative Effect
Author defines poetry as composed of imaginatives and explains its emotional effect.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the defining characteristic of poetry as a composition of imaginatives and explain how its primary aim is imaginative effect rather than truth.

Core Idea
Poetry aims to move the soul through imagination, not to convey truth.

In the classification of the five arts, the logical tradition places poetry as the fourth art. This passage from the Bahr al-Ulum defines poetry not by meter or rhyme alone, but by its material: imaginatives (mukhayyalat). These are propositions that produce images in the mind, regardless of their truth value. The poet's skill lies in composing such propositions to affect the soul emotionally. Understanding this definition helps us see why poetry is distinct from demonstration and dialectic.

Guided Path
1
Locate the definition of poetry
Find the phrase 'وهو المؤلف من المخيلات'. The definition is minimal: poetry is that which is composed of imaginatives.
2
Identify the material of poetry
The passage calls these propositions 'المخيلات'. They are propositions that create imagination, not necessarily assent.
3
Understand the effect
The soul is affected by 'القبض والبسط' (contraction and expansion). This is emotional movement, not intellectual agreement.
4
Note the role of meter
The passage adds 'especially when on a pleasing meter' – meter enhances the imaginative effect but is not part of the core definition.
5
Contrast with demonstrative propositions
Unlike demonstrative arguments that aim at certainty, poetry aims at imagination and emotional impact, even using false or impossible propositions.

Source Text

الرابع: الشعر، وهو المؤلف من المخيلات. وصناعة الشعر ملكة شعرية وما يتعلق به يقتدر بها على تأليف حجج شعرية. وهي قضايا يخيل بها، صادقة كانت أو كاذبة، مستحيلة أو ممكنة، فيتأثر النفس قبضاً وبسطاً؛ فإنها أطوع للتخييل من التصديق، سيما إذا كان على وزن لطيف.
The fourth: poetry, and it is that composed of imaginatives. And the art of poetry is a poetic faculty and what pertains to it, by which one is able to compose poetic arguments. They are propositions by which one imagines, whether true or false, impossible or possible. Then the soul is affected by contraction and expansion, for it is more obedient to imagination than assent, especially when it is on a pleasing meter.

Commentary
This passage defines the art of poetry (الشعر) as the fourth of the five arts. Poetry is composed of imaginatives (المخيلات), which are propositions that stimulate the imagination. The poetic faculty enables the composition of poetic arguments. These propositions can be true or false, possible or impossible; their purpose is to affect the soul with expansion (بسط) and contraction (قبض), i.e., emotions. The soul is more responsive to imagination than to rational assent, especially when cast in a pleasing meter. This highlights that poetry aims at emotional impact rather than intellectual conviction.
