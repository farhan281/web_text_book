Al-Bihari's Major Works: Sullam al-Ulum and Muslim al-Thubut
Author enumerates al-Bihari's major works, focusing on logic and usul al-fiqh.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the two major works of Muhabib Allah al-Bihari in logic and usul al-fiqh, and explain the significance of each.

Core Idea
Two books define al-Bihari's legacy: one on logic, one on usul al-fiqh.

Muhabib Allah al-Bihari is a towering figure in Islamic logic and usul al-fiqh. His two most famous works—Sullam al-Ulum on logic and Muslim al-Thubut on usul al-fiqh—are cornerstones of the Indian scholarly tradition. This lesson will guide you through the key facts about these works, their relationship, and their impact on later scholarship.

Guided Path
1
Identify the author
Note that Muhabib Allah al-Bihari is praised as having 'abundant knowledge' and that his biographers point to two works as proof: 'al-Sullam' and 'al-Muslim'. Keep these names in mind.
2
List the three works mentioned
The passage mentions three works: 1) Sullam al-Ulum (logic), 2) al-Ifadat (logic), and 3) Muslim al-Thubut (usul al-fiqh). The first and third are major; al-Ifadat is lesser-known but referenced by al-Bihari himself.
al-Ifadat is not usually listed in bibliographies; its mention comes from a cross-reference in Muslim al-Thubut.
3
Compare Sullam al-Ulum and Muslim al-Thubut
Sullam al-Ulum is a unique work on logic, written before Muslim al-Thubut. Muslim al-Thubut is a major work on usul al-fiqh, completed in 1109 AH. The name 'Muslim al-Thubut' is derived from the year of authorship using abjad calculation.
The author notes that Sullam al-Ulum is 'not woven upon its pattern', indicating originality.
4
Understand the printing history
Muslim al-Thubut has been printed many times in India, Egypt, and Beirut. Notably, the Bulaq press in Egypt printed it together with al-Ghazali's al-Mustasfa in a single two-volume set.
This pairing shows the high regard for Muslim al-Thubut as a standard text in usul al-fiqh.
