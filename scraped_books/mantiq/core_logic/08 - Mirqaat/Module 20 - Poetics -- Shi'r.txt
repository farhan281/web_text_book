The Logical Definition of Poetry: Imaginative Effect, Not Meter
Definition of poetry with conditions of meter and imagination.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to distinguish between the logical definition of poetry (imaginative effect) and its accidental features (meter, voice).

Core Idea
In logic, poetry is defined solely by imaginative effect; meter is an embellishment, not a condition.

In this passage, the author clarifies that the logical definition of poetry does not require meter. Ancient logicians confined poetry to imaginative effect, while moderns added meter, but the logician's concern is only imaginative speech. Understanding this distinction is crucial for applying poetic analysis in Islamic sciences.

Guided Path
1
Identify the term being defined
The term is 'al-shi'r' (poetry) in the logical context.
2
Extract the essential element
According to ancient logicians, only imaginative effect (takhyeel) is essential. Meter is not a condition.
3
Distinguish essential from accidental
Meter and good voice are accidental (arad) that enhance but do not define.
4
Recognize the shift between ancient and modern
Ancient logicians excluded meter; moderns include it, but logic only requires takhyeel.

صامت وكل مظهرها متكلم" ينتج "أنا صامت متكلم". ولا يشترط الوزن في الشعر عند أرباب الميزان، نعم، يفيده حسنا. والكلام الشعري إذا أنشد بصوت طيب ازداد تأثيره في النفوس، حتى ربما يزيل فرط البهجة العمائم عن الرؤوس. والأوائل من الحكماء اليونانيين كانوا أحرص الناس على الشعر.
ولا يشترط الوزن إلخ اعلم أن قدماء المنطقيين كانوا لا يعتبرون الوزن في حد الشعر، ويقتصرون على التخييل فقط. والمحدثون يعتبرون معه الوزن لكن المنطقي لا نظر له إلا في كونه كلاما مخيلا. نعم يفيده إلخ: ومن ثَم قيل: إن النظم الموزون يشابه الماء في السلاسة، والهواء في اللطافة، والدرر المنظومة في السلك بصوت طيب إلخ: هذا غير مشروط فيه بالاتفاق، وإنما هو من العوارض، وإفادته الحسن أمر جلي يدركه من رق طبعه ولطفت شمائله. وأما تأثيره في النفوس فنحن نشاهد أهل الصناعات الشاقة تستعين عليها بالتغني. والإبل عند كلالها ينشطها صوت الحادي والمغني. وشجعان العرب في الحروب تتمثل بالأشعار، وتلقي نفسها عند ذلك في مهالك الأخطار؛ فلا تبالي بمواقع السيوف ولا بوارق الحتوف وفي جميع ما ذكرنا حكايات ونوادر شُحنت بها الكتب والدفاتر.
"Silent and everything that manifests it is speaking" produces "I am silent speaking". And weight is not a condition in poetry according to the masters of the measure, yes, it benefits it nicely. And poetic speech when recited with a good voice increases its effect on souls, until perhaps it removes the excess of joy from heads. And the ancients among the Greek philosophers were the most eager people for poetry. And as for the condition of weight etc.: Know that the ancient logicians used not to consider weight in the definition of poetry, confining themselves to imagination only. The moderns consider weight along with it, but the logician only regards it as imaginative speech. Yes, it benefits it etc.: Hence it is said that metrical composition resembles water in smoothness, air in delicacy, and strung pearls in a necklace. With a good voice etc.: This is not a condition by agreement, rather it is an accident. Its benefit in beauty is a clear matter perceived by one of refined nature. As for its effect on souls, we see workers in difficult industries seek help from singing. Camels when exhausted are energized by the voice of the camel driver and singer. Arab warriors in battles recite poems, throwing themselves into dangers, not caring about sword strikes or arrows. In all we have mentioned are tales and anecdotes filled in books and notebooks.

Commentary
This passage defines the poetical syllogism and clarifies its conditions. The author begins with an example of a poetic statement that yields a contradiction: 'Silent and everything that manifests it is speaking' produces 'I am silent speaking', showing the imaginative nature of poetry. The author then states that weight (meter) is not a condition for poetry according to the masters of logic, but it improves it. The effect of poetry is enhanced by a good voice and can even cause ecstasy. The ancients among the Greek philosophers were very fond of poetry. The commentary explains that ancient logicians defined poetry solely by imaginative effect (takhyeel), while moderns add meter. However, the logician's concern is only that it is imaginative speech. Meter is not essential but accidental. The commentary then elaborates on the benefits of metrical composition, comparing it to water, air, and pearls, and discusses its emotional impact, citing examples from workers, camels, and Arab warriors. The passage ends by mentioning stories filled in books.

The Accidental Qualities of Poetry: Meter and Voice as Enhancers
Discussion of accidental qualities of poetry: meter and voice.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the accidental qualities of poetry (meter, voice) and explain how they enhance its emotional impact.

Core Idea
Meter and a good voice are not essential to poetry but greatly enhance its effect, as seen in Islamic and pre-Islamic traditions.

While the logical definition of poetry rests on imaginative effect, this passage also discusses the role of meter and voice in amplifying poetry's emotional power. Understanding these accidental qualities helps us appreciate the full aesthetic and rhetorical dimension of poetic speech in Islamic culture.

Guided Path
1
Identify the accidental qualities mentioned
Meter (al-wazn) and good voice (sawt tayyib) are mentioned as enhancements.
2
Determine the author's stance on their essentiality
They are not conditions (ghayr mashrut) but mere accidents (arad).
3
Observe the positive effects of meter and voice
Meter gives smoothness and delicacy; voice increases effect on souls, can cause ecstasy.
4
Examine the real-world examples
Workers, camels, and Arab warriors show how meter and voice mobilize emotions.
