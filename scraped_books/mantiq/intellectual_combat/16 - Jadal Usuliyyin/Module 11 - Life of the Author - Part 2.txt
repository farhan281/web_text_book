Theoretical Jadal and al-Baqillani's Role
Introduces theoretical jadal and al-Baqillani's pioneering contribution

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to distinguish between the practical and theoretical forms of usuli jadal and identify al-Baqillani's pioneering contribution to both.

Core Idea
Al-Baqillani pioneered both practical and theoretical usuli jadal.

This lesson introduces the theoretical dimension of jadal in usul al-fiqh, complementing its practical form. We will see how Imam Abu Bakr al-Baqillani not only applied jadal in debates but also authored the foundational theoretical work 'Sharh Adab al-Jadal', shaping the field for later scholars.

Lists scholars of jadal alphabetically with names and affiliations.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to parse a biographical index of jadal scholars, identifying key figures and their madhhab affiliations.

Core Idea
This index maps the wide network of scholars who shaped jadal across madhhabs.

This passage is a biographical index listing scholars associated with the discipline of jadal (dialectical debate), which is central to usul al-fiqh and Islamic logic. The list is organized roughly alphabetically, beginning with the letter 'qaf' (القاسمي, القرافي, etc.) and continuing through 'kaf' (الكرخى, الكعبي), 'lam' (اللآردي, اللامشي), and 'mim' (الماتريدي, مالك, etc.). Learning to read such an index helps you understand the breadth of scholarly engagement with dialectical methods across different madhhabs and eras.

Guided Path
1
Identify the alphabetical order
Notice that the list is sorted by the first letter of the scholar's name or nisba. The passage begins with names starting with 'qaf' (ق), then 'kaf' (ك), then 'lam' (ل), then 'mim' (م). This ordering helps locate a specific scholar quickly.
Some entries may have stray dots or numbers in the original; ignore them.
2
Parse the name components
Each entry typically includes a kunya (e.g., أبو الحسن), an ism (e.g., علي), and a nisba (e.g., القصار). For example, 'أبو الحسن علي بن ابن القطان' shows the kunya 'أبو الحسن', the ism 'علي', and the nasab 'ابن القطان'. Distinguish the main figure from his lineage.
The entry 'ابن القصار' appears as 'أبو الحسن علي بن ابن القطان' possibly due to OCR error; treat as a single scholar.
3
Note the madhhab or school affiliation
Some names are immediately recognizable as founders or prominent figures of a madhhab: Malik ibn Anas (Maliki), Muhammad ibn al-Hasan al-Shaybani (Hanafi), Ibn Qudama al-Maqdisi (Hanbali). This shows the index covers multiple legal schools.
4
Recognize the breadth of jadal scholars
The index includes not only jurists but also theologians (e.g., al-Maturidi) and scholars from different regions. This indicates that jadal was a cross-disciplinary field.

Purpose of a Biographical Index in Jadal
Index of scholars in jadal.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the purpose and function of a biographical index in the discipline of jadal and recognize key scholars listed.

Core Idea
A list of authorities grounds the discipline of jadal in a living tradition of scholars.

This passage presents a list of scholars who contributed to the field of jadal (Islamic dialectics). Such lists are common in classical works to establish the chain of transmission and authority. Your task is to understand why such indexes matter, not to analyze logical arguments. Learn to navigate these references as tools for further study.

Guided Path
1
Identify the genre
Recognize that this is a biographical index (fihrist or tabaqat) listing names, not a continuous argument. Each entry typically includes a name, kunya, and sometimes a famous work.
This is a reference tool, not a logical text.
2
Locate key names
Scan the list for well-known scholars. For example, 'Muslim ibn al-Hajjaj' is the compiler of Sahih Muslim, a major hadith collection. Note his appearance here.
The presence of such figures shows jadal's connection to hadith and other sciences.
3
Understand the structure
Observe how names are grouped by letter (alif, ba, etc.) and include full names. This helps in locating a scholar quickly.
The list is alphabetical by first letter of the ism (given name) or nisba.
4
Recognize the purpose
Such indexes serve to document the authorities and sources used in jadal. They show that the discipline is based on a chain of scholars (isnad).
This is similar to the isnad system in hadith.
