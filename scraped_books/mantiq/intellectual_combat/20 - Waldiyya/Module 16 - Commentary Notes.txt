How to Follow Bibliographic References in Islamic Logic Texts
Author directs advanced students to his comprehensive munazara treatise.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify a bibliographic reference in a classical logical text and determine where to find the fuller treatment.

Core Idea
Authors often point advanced students to their own detailed works; knowing this saves time and deepens study.

In the midst of a discussion on munazara, the author briefly mentions his own treatise for those who want thoroughness. This is not a mere aside—it is a deliberate pointer to a more comprehensive source. Understanding how to read such references is essential for independent research in the Islamic sciences.

Guided Path
1
Locate the reference phrase
Look for phrases like 'من أراد الاستقصاء' which signal a conditional directive.
2
Identify the recommended work
Note the title given (تقرير قوانين المناظرة) and confirm it is by the same author.
3
Understand the purpose
The author is not just citing but directing advanced students to a specialized text for deeper coverage.
4
Connect to the current text
Recognize that this reference expands the present lesson; the current text is introductory.

Commentary
This short passage is a reference to another work by the same author (probably the Waldiyya or a separate treatise) that provides a comprehensive treatment of the principles of debate. It serves as a pointer for the advanced student to consult a specialized text beyond the current introductory discussion. This is a common feature in Islamic scholarly works where an author directs the reader to a more detailed exposition.
