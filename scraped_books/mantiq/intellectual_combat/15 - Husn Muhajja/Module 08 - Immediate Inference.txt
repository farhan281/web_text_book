Refuting the Contradiction Claim via Generality and Specificity in Negation
Author refutes contradiction claim using generality-specificity in negation.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify when the negation of a specific term does not entail the negation of a more general term, using the principle that the negation of the specific is more general than the negation of the general.

Core Idea
Denying both inside and outside is not a contradiction—'outside' is more specific than 'not inside'.

In this passage, the author defends the Ash'ari position that God is neither inside nor outside the world. The opponent claims this is a contradiction (رفع النقيضين). The author uses a logical insight about generality and specificity to refute this. You will learn how to distinguish between genuine contradictions and mere differences in specificity.

Guided Path
1
Identify the opponent's claim
The opponent says denying both inside and outside is a contradiction. What logical principle do they think is violated? They assume that 'outside' is the negation of 'inside'.
The opponent commits a fallacy of assuming that two mutually exclusive terms are contradictories.
2
Find the author's correction
The author states contradiction is between a term and its negation. What is the exact negation of 'inside'? It is 'not inside', not 'outside'.
The author establishes that tanaqud requires one to be the direct negative of the other.
3
Analyze the relationship
'Outside' is more specific than 'not inside'. When we negate 'outside', we get 'not outside', which is more general than 'not inside'. The negation of the specific yields a more general statement.
The author says: 'nafy al-akhass a'amm min nafy al-a'amm' – negation of the specific is more general than negation of the general.
4
Understand the logical consequence
Since the negation of 'outside' (i.e., 'not outside') is more general than 'not inside', it does not entail 'not inside'. Therefore, denying both 'inside' and 'outside' does not constitute denying both a term and its negation.
The general does not entail the specific; so negating the specific does not negate the general.
5
Apply to the case of God
The author says the intellect can conceive an existent that does not admit these relations at all. The imagination (wahm) fails, leading to the false claim of impossibility. The logical distinction refutes the charge of contradiction.
This shows the importance of logical precision in theological arguments.

ليس داخل العالم ولا خارجه ولا متصلاً به ولا منفصلاً عنه، وَتَوُهُمُ أنّ فى هذا رفعاً للنقيضين وهو محال باطل، إذ لا تناقض بين داخل وخارج، وإنما التناقض بين داخل ولا داخل وليس خارج مساوياً للداخل وإنما هو أخص منه ، فلا يلزم مِنْ نفيه نفيه لأن نفي الأخص أعم من نفي الأعم، والأعم لا يستلزم الأخص. فإن قيل بم ينفرد هذا الأعم الذي هو لا داخل عن الأخص الذي هو خارج. قلنا : ينفرد في موجود لا يقبل الدخول ولا الخروج ولا الاتصال ولا الانفصال، وهذا يحمله العقل ولكن يقصر عنه الوهم ، وقصور الوهم منشأ الشبهة ومثار دعوى الاستحالة اهـ . وهذا هو الجواب التحقيقي
He is not inside the world nor outside it, nor connected to it nor separated from it. And the imagination that in this there is a raising of the two contradictories is impossible and false, for there is no contradiction between inside and outside; rather contradiction is between inside and not-inside, and 'outside' is not equivalent to 'inside' but is more specific than it. So from its negation, its negation does not follow, because the negation of the more specific is more general than the negation of the more general, and the more general does not entail the more specific. If it is said: By what is this general [term] which is 'not inside' distinguished from the specific [term] which is 'outside'? We say: It is distinguished in an existent that does not admit entry, exit, connection, or separation. The intellect grasps this but imagination falls short of it, and the shortcoming of imagination is the source of the doubt and the cause of the claim of impossibility. This is the verified answer.

Commentary
This passage is a critical refutation of the claim that saying God is neither inside nor outside the world is a contradiction (رفع النقيضين). The author first states the position: God is not inside, not outside, not connected, not separated. The opponent's imagination that this is a contradiction is false. The author explains that contradiction only exists between a thing and its negation (e.g., inside and not-inside). 'Outside' is not the negation of 'inside'; it is more specific. So negating 'outside' does not negate 'not-inside'; rather, the negation of the specific (outside) is more general than the negation of the general (not-inside). The general does not entail the specific. The author then answers a potential question: what distinguishes 'not inside' from 'outside'? He says: it applies to an existent that does not admit these relations at all. The intellect can conceive this, but imagination (وهم) cannot, and this imaginative failure is the source of the doubt. The author concludes that this is the verified answer (الجواب التحقيقي).

Key Takeaway
The negation of a specific term does not necessarily negate the more general term; only the exact negation of a term creates a contradiction.
