Using the Bibliography of al-Mantiq al-Kabir
Lists manuscripts, editions, and related works cited in the study.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to locate the primary manuscripts and editions of al-Mantiq al-Kabir using this bibliography.

Core Idea
Bibliographies are the map to the manuscript tradition.

This bibliography lists the manuscripts and editions used in studying al-Mantiq al-Kabir. Learning to read it will help you trace the transmission of the text and identify authoritative sources for your own research.

Guided Path
1
Identify the main sections
The bibliography has two sections: first general references (manuscript guides, works by Ibn Sina, Ibn Hazm, etc.), then a 'Study' section focusing on al-Mantiq al-Kabir itself.
2
Locate key manuscripts
Find the three listings for al-Mantiq al-Kabir: Topkapi (Ahmad III 3401), Berlin (5165), and Cairo microfilm of the Topkapi copy.
These are primary witnesses for the text.
3
Note standard reference works
Recognize Ahlwardt and Brockelmann as standard catalogues of Arabic manuscripts used by scholars.
4
Understand related works
The 'Study' section includes editions of al-Razi's other works (e.g., al-Mabahith al-Mashriqiyya) and commentaries (Sharh al-Isharat), showing the broader context.
These works help in understanding al-Mantiq al-Kabir.

Commentary
This passage is a bibliography page from the critical edition of Mantiq Kabir. It lists manuscripts, editions, and reference works used in the study. The first section lists manuscripts and general references, including works by Ahlwardt, Brockelmann, Ibn Hazm, Ibn Sina, and modern studies on al-Razi. The second section, titled 'Study' (al-Dirasa), lists specific editions and manuscripts of al-Mantiq al-Kabir itself, along with related works such as al-Mahsul fi Usul al-Fiqh, al-Mabahith al-Mashriqiyya, and commentaries on al-Razi's other works. This bibliography serves as a guide to the sources employed in the edition and analysis of the text.
