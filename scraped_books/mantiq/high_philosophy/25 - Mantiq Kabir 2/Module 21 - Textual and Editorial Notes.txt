The Quranic Verse Index in Logic Works
Index of Quranic verses cited in the volume.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to locate and cite Quranic verses relevant to jadal using an index.

Core Idea
The Quranic verse index is a practical tool connecting logical reasoning to revelation.

This lesson explores a brief index of Quranic verses found at the end of a logic volume. Though it seems simple, understanding how such indexes work helps you quickly reference the Qur'anic foundations of logical principles like jadal.

Guided Path
1
Identify the listed verses
Look at the Arabic passage: it lists three Quranic verse references. Note the Surah names and the snippets of text provided.
The verses are from al-Nahl (16:125) and al-Qiyamah (75:17).
2
Understand the purpose of an index
An index (fihrasah) is a navigational tool that allows readers to quickly find where specific verses are discussed in the main body of the work.
This index is placed at the end of the volume for easy reference.
3
Connect the verse to jadal
The verse from al-Nahl (16:125) commands arguing in the best way, which is central to the Islamic art of dialectical argumentation (jadal).
This shows that logic in Islam is grounded in revelation.
4
Recognize the index as a memory aid
For students and scholars, such indexes help memorize key prooftexts and quickly recall them during debates or study.
They serve as a 'memorization anchor' for the material.

Commentary
This passage is an index of Quranic verses cited in the volume. It lists three verses from Surah al-Nahl (16:125, 16:125 with variant wording, and 75:17 from al-Qiyamah). The inclusion of such an index demonstrates the classical habit of grounding logical and theological arguments in revelation. The verse from al-Nahl is especially relevant to the art of jadal (dialectical argumentation), as it commands arguing in the best manner.
