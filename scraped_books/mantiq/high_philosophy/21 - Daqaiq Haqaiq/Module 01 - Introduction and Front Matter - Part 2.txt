Identifying the Book and Author from the Title Page
Title page presenting Daqā’iq al-Ḥaqā’iq by Sayfuddin al-Āmidī.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to extract the author, title, death date, editor, and publisher from a classical Islamic book's title page and explain the significance of each element for textual criticism.

Core Idea
The title page is the fingerprint of a text: it establishes authorship and scholarly lineage.

Every serious study of a classical Islamic text begins with its title page. This front matter tells you who wrote the work, when he lived, and who prepared the edition you hold. Before diving into logical content, you must be able to identify these details to evaluate the authority and reliability of the source. This lesson will walk you through the typical elements found on a title page using Daqā’iq al-Ḥaqā’iq as an example.

Guided Path
1
Locate the title of the work
Find the main title (دقائق الحقائق). Often written in a larger or decorated font. This is the name by which the text is known.
2
Identify the author and his full name
The author's name, including his lineage and honorifics (سيف الدين علي بن أبي علي الآمدي). Note that the death year (631 هـ) is also given. This helps date the work and places him in a specific scholarly tradition.
3
Find the editor and publisher information
Look for the editor (المحقق) and the publisher. Here it is السيد فاضل علي الموسوي and the publisher كتات - ناشرون in Beirut. This identifies the modern scholarly edition.
4
Understand the term تحقيق (taḥqīq)
The word تحقيق on a cover means 'critical edition'. This indicates that the text has been collated from manuscripts and prepared according to scholarly standards, distinguishing it from a simple reprint.
