Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the key biographical details of Imam al-Ghazali and explain the significance of his title Hujjat al-Islam.

Core Idea
Al-Ghazali's humble beginnings shaped his lifelong pursuit of knowledge and defense of Islam.

This lesson introduces you to the early life of Imam Abu Hamid al-Ghazali, one of the most influential thinkers in Islamic history. Understanding his background helps contextualize his later contributions to theology, law, and spirituality. The passage from the editor provides essential biographical facts that form the foundation for appreciating his works.

Guided Path

The text says he was born in Tus in the year 450 AH (1058 CE). This places him in the Seljuk era, a time of political and intellectual ferment.

This reveals al-Ghazali's early exposure to both Sufi spirituality and formal education.
4

Al-Ghazali studied under Sheikh Ahmad ibn Muhammad al-Radhakani in Tus. This teacher is less known but was his first formal instructor.
5
Understand the title 'Hujjat al-Islam'

This title is a key part of his legacy.

Al-Ghazali's life is a journey from scholarly fame to spiritual depth.

Imam al-Ghazali is one of the most influential figures in Islamic intellectual history. His life story reveals not only his academic achievements but also a profound spiritual transformation that led to his masterwork, 'Ihya' Ulum al-Din'. Commentary
This passage is a frontispiece and biographical introduction. It answers the question of when and why al-Mihakk was written. The author explains that while many assume al-Mihakk is among the Imam's first works, the introduction itself describes the circumstances: a brother in faith requested a work that would serve as a touchstone (mihakk) to guard against errors in reasoning. The Imam had previously abandoned this art out of boredom, but the request spurred him to return. Then the passage discusses the dating: al-Iqtisad fi al-I'tiqad was written around age forty, and al-Mihakk before that, likely in the late thirties. Al-Tahafut and Mi'yar came earlier, but al-Mi'yar was held back until after al-Mihakk because it needed more polishing. This shows the progression of Imam al-Ghazali's works.

Key Takeaway
Al-Mihakk was written as a practical standard for testing reasoning, not as an introductory text, and it fits between al-Ghazali's earlier critical works and his later creedal summary.
