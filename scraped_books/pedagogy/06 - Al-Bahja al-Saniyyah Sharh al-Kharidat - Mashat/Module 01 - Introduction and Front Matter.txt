يَقُولُ رَاجِي رَحْمَةَ القَدِيرِ * أَي أَحْمَدُ الْمَشْهُورُ بالدَّرْدِيرِ * الْحَمْدُ للهِ العَلِيِّ الوَاحِدِ * العَالِمِ الفَرْدِ الغَنِيِّ الْمَاجِدِ * وَأَفْضَلُ الصَّلاةِ والتَّسْلِيم * عَلَى النَّبِيِّ الْمُصْطَفَى الكَريم * وَآلِهِ وَصَحْبِهِ الأَطْهَارَ * لَا سِيَّمَا رَفِيقِهِ فِي الغَارِ * وَهَذِهِ عَقِيدَةٌ سَنِيَّة * سَمَّيْتُهَا الْخَرِيدَةَ البَهِيَّةَ * لَطِيفَةٌ صَغِيرَةٌ فِي الْحَجْمِ * لَكِنَّهَا كَبِيرَةٌ فِي العِلم * تَكْفِيكَ عِلْمًا إِنْ تُرِدْ أَنْ تَكْتَفِي * لأَنَّهَا بِزُبْدَةِ الفَنِّ تَفِي * والله أَرْجُو فِي قَبولِ العَمَلِ * والنَّفْعَ مِنْهَا ثُمَّ غَفْرَ الزَّلَل
He says, hoping for the mercy of the All-Powerful, that is Ahmad known as al-Dardir: Praise be to Allah, the High, the One, the Knower, the Unique, the Rich, the Glorious. And the best of prayers and peace be upon the chosen noble Prophet, and upon his pure family and companions, especially his companion in the cave. And this is a Sunni creed; I have named it al-Kharida al-Bahiyya. It is delicate, small in size, but great in knowledge. It suffices you with knowledge if you wish to be satisfied, because it is abundant with the cream of the art. And I hope from Allah for acceptance of the deed, and benefit from it, and then forgiveness of slips.

Commentary
This passage is the opening section of the poem. It begins with the author mentioning himself as 'hoping for the mercy of the All-Powerful' and identifies himself as Ahmad al-Dardir. He then praises Allah with attributes (al-'Ali, al-Wahid, al-'Alim, al-Fard, al-Ghani, al-Majid) and sends blessings upon the Prophet Muhammad, his family, and companions, specifically mentioning Abu Bakr as the companion in the cave. Next, he names and describes his poem: 'al-Kharida al-Bahiyya' (the brilliant virgin pearl), stating that despite its small size, it contains great knowledge and suffices the reader as it contains the essence of the art (the science of kalam). He concludes by supplicating to Allah for acceptance of his work, benefit, and forgiveness of mistakes.
