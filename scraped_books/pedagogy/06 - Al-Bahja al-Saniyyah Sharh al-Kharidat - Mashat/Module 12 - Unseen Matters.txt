The poem emphasizes that upon seeing Him, they forget all other bliss, and it pronounces woe upon the Mu'tazila who deny the vision. The author then comments that this vision is the true bliss, and all other delights of paradise are merely manifestations of this supreme bliss. Finally, he cites Quran 75:22-23 as evidence: 'Faces on that day are radiant, looking at their Lord.'
