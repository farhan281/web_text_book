The Four Categories of Command and Will
Mashat enumerates the four categories of things based on command and will.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the four logical categories of created things based on divine command and will.

Core Idea
Divine command and divine will are not identical; they produce four distinct categories.

In this passage, Mashat presents a key doctrinal classification that distinguishes between what Allah commands and what He wills. By examining four categories and their examples, we see that some things are commanded but not willed, and vice versa. This distinction is central to Sunni theology.

This lesson uses a vivid historical anecdote to expose a flaw in the Qadari view that God's will does not encompass evil. Through a simple dialogue, the Bedouin shows that the Qadari's own prayer contradicts itself, paving the way for the Ash'ari affirmation of God's universal will.
