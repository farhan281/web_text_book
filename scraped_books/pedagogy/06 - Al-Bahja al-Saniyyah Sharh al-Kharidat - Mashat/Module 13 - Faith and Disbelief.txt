Learning Objective

Dhikr with adab elevates rank, and fear should outweigh hope in the seeker's path.

The poet begins by urging us to increase our remembrance (dhikr) of the word of Islam with good manners (adab), promising that this leads to the highest spiritual stations. He then gives a key principle: let fear (khawf) overcome hope (raja') while journeying sincerely to your Lord. This lesson explores these two foundational teachings.

3
Grasp the balance of fear and hope
The text commands fear to overcome hope. But this doesn't mean despair; it means prioritizing reverence while maintaining hope. Discuss how this balance is maintained in practice.

This passage from the commentary on Al-Bahja Al-Saniyya defines a key concept in Islamic theology: matters that are so well-known from the religion that they are considered necessary knowledge. This lesson explores how the word 'Islam' serves as a comprehensive term for the entire religion.
