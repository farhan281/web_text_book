Sanusi's First Three Theological Writings
The author lists Sanusi's first three writings, noting the first was written at age 18-19.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the first three theological works of Imam Sanusi and the significance of his early writings in the field of tawhid.

Core Idea
Sanusi's theological writings are his intellectual crown, beginning at age 18.

Before diving into the content of Sanusi's theological arguments, it is helpful to know the works themselves. This passage from al-Mallali's biography lists Sanusi's earliest theological writings, showing the trajectory of his scholarly career. The first work was written while still a teenager, and the second—the 'Aqidat Ahl al-Tawhid'—became the cornerstone of his reputation in tawhid.

Guided Path
1
Read the passage carefully
Read the Arabic passage once without analysing. Then read the translation. Notice how the author introduces the list with a remark about the variety of Sanusi's works.
Pay attention to the footnote markers in the Arabic.
2
Identify the three works
Find the titles of the three works mentioned. Note that the second work has two names: 'Aqidat Ahl al-Tawhid' or 'al-Aqida al-Kubra'.
The first work is a commentary on Fara'id al-Hawfi, not a theological work.
3
Understand the title clarification
The passage explains that the longer title often associated with the second work is actually a description, not part of the name. Compare the author's statement with the footnote.
This prevents a common misunderstanding.
4
Reflect on the significance
Ask yourself: Why does the author say that Sanusi's creedal writings are the crown of his scholarly output? How does the early age of his first writing show his dedication?

Commentary
This passage is a bibliographical enumeration of Imam Sanusi's early writings. The author begins by noting the variety of Sanusi's works but emphasizes that his creedal writings are the crown. The author then states that Sanusi's student al-Mallali dedicated a section to discussing Sanusi's works, and the passage lists three works. The first is 'al-Muqrib al-Mustawfi fi Sharh Fara'id al-Hawfi', which is Sanusi's first composition, written at age eighteen or nineteen (with a discrepancy). There is a note that his story with Shaykh al-Hasan Abarkan, who ordered him to hide it until age forty, will be mentioned later. The second work is 'Aqidat Ahl al-Tawhid' or 'al-Aqida al-Kubra', which is Sanusi's first work in theology, described as having dense and difficult phrasing as stated in his commentary on al-Aqida al-Wusta. The third work is 'Umdat Ahl al-Tawfiq wa al-Tasdid' or 'Sharh al-Aqida al-Kubra'. The passage also includes footnotes providing references and clarifying that the long title of the second work is descriptive, not part of the official name.

Misconception & Correction
Misconception: The full title of 'al-Aqida al-Kubra' includes the long descriptive phrase 'al-Mukharraja min Zulumat al-Jahl wa Rabaqat al-Taqlid...' as an official part of the name.
Correction: The passage explicitly states that this addition is a description, not part of the title, as held by al-'Ukari and al-Hamidi, with al-Mallali affirming it.
