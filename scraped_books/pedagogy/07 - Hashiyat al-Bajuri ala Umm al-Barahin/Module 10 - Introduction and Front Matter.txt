Bajuri's Purpose: Why This Commentary Exists
Al-Bajuri states his purpose in writing the hashia and praises the Sanusiyya.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand al-Bajuri's motivation for writing his Hashia on the Sanusiyya and the distinctive qualities he attributes to the original work.

Core Idea
A small book can be mighty: the Sanusiyya’s excellence lies in its conciseness and fullness.

Al-Bajuri opens his Hashia with a personal note: he was asked by fellow seekers of knowledge to write a commentary on the famous Sanusiyya. Before diving into theological arguments, he sets the stage by explaining why this particular text deserves such attention. In doing so, he reveals his own methodology and his veneration for the Sanusiyya as the premier work on Islamic creed.

Guided Path
1
Read the passage carefully
Read the Arabic passage once without pausing. Then read the translation to get the full meaning.
Notice the formal opening style: 'وبعد' and the author's self-description.
2
Identify the request and response
Bajuri says 'some brothers' asked him to write. This shows the commentary was a response to a community need. Note how he expresses willingness ('فانشرح صدري').
The phrase 'والله أعلم بما هنالك' shows his humility.
3
List the four praises of the Sanusiyya
Bajuri mentions four characteristics: small size, great knowledge, contains all beliefs, and is free from filler/complexity. Write them down.
These qualities explain why he chose to comment on this text.
4
Reflect on the purpose
Consider why Bajuri emphasizes conciseness and completeness. How does this set the tone for the commentary?
This connects to the goal of the hashia: to clarify without adding unnecessary complexity.

وبعد: فيقول إبراهيم البَاجُورِيُّ الفقيرُ إلى مولاه الغني القدير: سألني بعضُ الإخوان - أصلحَ الله لي ولهم الحال والشأن - أن أكتب كتابة بهيَّةً، على المقدمة المشهورة بالسنوسية، فانْشَرَحَ صدري لذلك، والله أعلم بما هنالك؛ لأنها وإن كانت صغيرةَ الجِرْمِ؛ كبيرةُ العِلْم محتوية على جميع العقائد، مع زيادة الفوائد، ولذلك كانت أحسن المؤلفات في التوحيد، وأخلصها من الحشو والتعقيد. وهذا أوان الشروع في المقصود بعون الملك المعبود، فأقول وبالله التوفيق:
After: Ibrahim al-Bajuri, the poor one to his Rich and Powerful Lord, says: Some brothers - may Allah set right for me and them the condition and state - asked me to write a beautiful writing on the famous introduction known as al-Sanusiyya, so my breast expanded for that, and Allah knows best what is there; because even though it is small in size, it is great in knowledge, containing all the beliefs, with additional benefits, and therefore it is the best of works on tawhid, and the most free from filler and complexity. This is the time to begin the intended with the help of the Worshiped King, so I say, and with Allah is success.
Source pages p. 23

Commentary
This passage is Bajuri's introduction explaining why he wrote this hashia. He states that he was asked by some brothers to write a commentary on the famous Sanusiyya. He then praises the Sanusiyya as being small in size but great in knowledge, containing all the beliefs of Ahl al-Sunna with additional benefits. He considers it the best work on tawhid and free from unnecessary complexity. He then announces his intention to begin the commentary with Allah's help.

Key Takeaway
Al-Bajuri wrote his Hashia because the Sanusiyya, though small, is a comprehensive and refined work on tawhid, worthy of a detailed commentary.

How This Lesson Applies — to MY Life?
?
Al-Bajuri calls himself 'الفقير إلى مولاه' (the poor one to his Lord). Do you truly see yourself as utterly dependent on Allah in every moment, or do you secretly rely on your own abilities? What would change in your life today if you genuinely internalized that poverty?
?
The Sanusiyya is praised for being free from 'الحشو والتعقيد' (filler and complexity). What unnecessary distractions or overcomplications have crept into your understanding of tawhid? What concrete step can you take to purify your core beliefs?
?
Al-Bajuri's 'breast expanded' when asked to serve by teaching. When has your heart felt that openness to serve others in knowledge? What is holding you back from that expansion right now, and what will you do about it today?
