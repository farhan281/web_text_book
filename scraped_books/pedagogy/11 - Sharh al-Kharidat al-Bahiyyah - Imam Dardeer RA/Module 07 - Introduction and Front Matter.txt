The Structure of al-Kharidat al-Bahiyyah
Outline of the poem's structural sections.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the overall structure and main sections of the poem al-Kharidat al-Bahiyyah as outlined by the author.

Core Idea
This poem is divided into three main theological sections plus a conclusion on Sufism.

Before diving into the theological arguments, it's helpful to have a map of the poem's terrain. Imam al-Dardir organized al-Kharidat al-Bahiyyah into clear sections: an introduction, a section on rational judgment, and then three major theological parts covering divinity, prophethood, and matters of revelation, ending with a conclusion on spiritual purification. This lesson provides that map.

Guided Path
1
Read the passage
Read the Arabic outline and its literal translation to see the section divisions and verse ranges.
Notice that the poem is divided into named parts with specific verse numbers.
2
Identify the main sections
List each section mentioned: Introduction, Rational Judgment, Divine Matters, Prophethood, Sam'iyyat, and Conclusion on Tasawwuf. Note their verse ranges.
This gives you a bird's-eye view of the poem's content.
3
Reflect on the progression
Consider why the poem moves from rational judgment to divine matters, then prophethood, then revealed matters, and finally Sufism. This progression reflects the logical order of theological inquiry.

The poem is built around three creedal pillars: divine matters, prophethood, and traditions, topped with Sufism.

Every poem has a blueprint. In this lesson, we look at the table of contents of al-Kharidat al-Bahiyyah. This overview will help you navigate the rest of the course, showing you how the poet organized belief, proof, and spiritual purification into a single flowing work.
