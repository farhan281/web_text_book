The Meaning of La Hawla Wa La Quwwata Illa Billah
Commentator explains the meaning of the phrase la hawla wa la quwwata illa billah.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the correct interpretation of the phrase 'la hawla wa la quwwata illa billah' as explained by the commentator, and why it expresses complete dependence on Allah for both avoiding sin and performing obedience.

Core Idea
No turning from sin without Allah's protection; no power to obey without His help.

This lesson examines the famous phrase 'la hawla wa la quwwata illa billah'. The commentator unpacks its two key words—hawl (turning away) and quwwah (power)—to show that every human action, whether avoiding evil or doing good, is entirely reliant on Allah's protection and assistance. Understanding this deepens our sense of tawakkul and humility.

Guided Path
1
Read the Arabic phrase
Read the Arabic text carefully. Notice that it is divided into two negation statements: 'la hawla' and 'la quwwata', both negated except with Allah.
The phrase is often recited but its precise meaning is sometimes overlooked.
2
Identify the two parts
The commentator explicitly defines 'hawl' as turning away from disobedience (ma'siyah) and 'quwwah' as power to perform obedience (ta'ah). Note the two divine attributes: 'ismah' (protection) and 'i'anah' (assistance).
3
Reflect on the theological significance
Consider why the commentator gives these specific definitions. This phrase is a declaration that neither avoiding sin nor doing good is possible by our own strength; both are gifts from Allah.
This corrects the misconception that the phrase only refers to physical strength.

Commentary
This passage gives a definitional explanation of the phrase 'La hawla wa la quwwata illa billah'. The commentator breaks it into two parts: 'hawl' (turning away) and 'quwwah' (power). 'La hawla' means no turning away from sin is possible except through God's 'ismah' (protection), and 'la quwwata' means no power to obey God except through His 'i'ānah' (assistance). Thus the phrase acknowledges human dependence on God for both avoiding evil and doing good.

Misconception & Correction
Misconception: Some think the phrase only refers to physical strength or ability, ignoring the spiritual dimension of avoiding sin.
Correction: The commentator explicitly clarifies that 'hawl' means turning away from disobedience, and 'quwwah' means power to perform obedience, both are spiritual as much as physical.

Key Takeaway
The phrase 'la hawla wa la quwwata illa billah' means that both turning away from sin and the ability to obey come solely from Allah's protection and assistance.
