Conditions for the Acceptance of Dua
The author discusses the virtues of dua and the conditions for its acceptance.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the conditions for the acceptance of supplication (dua) as derived from prophetic traditions and Quranic evidence.

Core Idea
Dua is powerful but requires sincerity, presence of heart, and patience.

This passage from the Sharh al-Aqaid al-Nasafiyyah highlights both the profound power of dua and the essential conditions for its acceptance. The author cites Quranic verses and hadiths to show that while Allah is generous and responds, the supplicant must approach with sincere intention and a focused heart, avoiding haste and sinful pleas.

Guided Path
1
Read the passage and its translation
First, read the Arabic passage carefully, then the literal translation. Familiarize yourself with the key hadiths and the verse from Surah Ghafir.
2
Identify the virtues of dua
Notice the two hadiths at the beginning: dua repels calamity, charity extinguishes Allah's anger, and the presence of scholars can lift punishment from a village.
These show the tremendous effect of dua and righteous actions.
3
Understand the conditions for acceptance
Look for the conditions mentioned: avoid praying for sin or cutting family ties, do not be hasty, and have sincerity, purity of intention, and presence of heart. The hadith about being certain of the response is key.
4
Reflect on the warning
The passage concludes by stating that Allah does not answer the dua of a heedless and distracted heart. Consider why presence of heart is crucial.

Know that the foundation in that is sincerity of intention, purity of inner self, and presence of heart, because of his (peace be upon him) saying: 'Call upon God while you are certain of the response.' Commentary
This passage discusses the importance and conditions of supplication (dua). It begins by citing two hadiths: one stating that dua repels calamity and charity extinguishes God's wrath, and another that when a scholar and student pass by a village, punishment is lifted from its graveyard. Then it affirms that God answers prayers and fulfills needs, quoting Quran 40:60 as evidence. It further cites a hadith stating that a servant's prayer is not answered if it involves sin or severing family ties, and that one should not be hasty in expecting an answer. Another hadith emphasizes God's generosity in not returning empty hands raised in supplication. The passage then stresses that the foundation for acceptance is sincere intention (sidq al-niyyah), purity of inner self (khulus al-tawiyyah), and presence of heart (hudur al-qalb), quoting the hadith to call upon God with certainty. It concludes with a warning that God does not answer the supplication of a heedless heart. Thus, the passage presents both the power of dua and the internal conditions required for its acceptance.

Correction: The passage clarifies that dua requires sincerity, presence of heart, avoiding sin and severed ties, and patience. Allah does not answer from a heedless heart.

Key Takeaway

Fourth Argument: No Distinction in General Proofs
The author gives the fourth argument against multiple correct ijtihad results.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the fourth argument against the view that every mujtahid is correct, which is based on the impossibility of contradictory rulings for a single action.

Core Idea
If every mujtahid were correct, one action could be both forbidden and allowed—impossible.

In this passage, the author presents the fourth of several arguments against the claim that every mujtahid arrives at a correct opinion. He begins by reaffirming that truth is singular when established by explicit texts, then argues that if every mujtahid were correct, a single action would be described by mutually exclusive rulings. This logical contradiction shows the position is untenable.

Guided Path
1
Read the passage carefully
Read the Arabic passage once without analysing, then read the translation. Pay attention to the logical flow.
2
Identify the consensus claim
Notice the statement that scholars agree truth is one for what is established by explicit text. This sets the stage.
3
Find the conditional argument
Look for the conditional: 'if every mujtahid were correct, then...' and list the contradictory pairs mentioned.
The pairs are: prohibition and permission, validity and invalidity, obligation and its absence.
4
Understand the logical contradiction
Reflect on why a single action cannot have both rulings simultaneously. This is the core of the proof.
5
Recognize the proof’s conclusion
The argument shows that the claim 'every mujtahid is correct' leads to absurdity, so it must be false.
The author refers to his other book for full details.

Established by the text. And they have unanimously agreed that the truth with regard to what is established by the text is one, no other. Fourth: That there is no distinction in the general proofs revealed in the law of our Prophet—peace be upon him—between persons. For if every mujtahid were correct, it would necessarily entail the description of a single action by two mutually exclusive attributes: prohibition and permission, or validity and invalidity, or obligation and its absence. And the complete verification of these proofs and the response to the proofs of the opponents is sought from our book al-Talwīḥ fī Sharḥ al-Tanqīḥ.

Commentary
This passage presents the fourth argument against the view that every mujtahid is correct. It begins by affirming that what is established by explicit textual evidence (naṣṣ) is one truth, agreed upon by consensus. The fourth proof then states that there is no distinction between persons in the general proofs of the Sharia. If every mujtahid were correct, a single action would have contradictory rulings—e.g., both prohibited and permitted, valid and invalid, obligatory and not—which is impossible. The author concludes by directing the reader to his other work for full details.

How does this argument call you to take a definitive stance? Make a concrete step today to clarify your position.
