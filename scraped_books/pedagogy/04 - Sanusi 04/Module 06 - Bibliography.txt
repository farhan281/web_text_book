Sanusi's Major Creedal Works
Lists Sanusi's creedal works and commentaries

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the major creedal works of Imam al-Sanusi, their hierarchy, and their significance.

Core Idea
Sanusi's creedal works form a graded system from concise to detailed.

This passage introduces the key creedal works of Imam al-Sanusi, from the Middle Creed to the Minor of the Minor, along with their commentaries. It shows how Sanusi tailored his writings to different levels of students, and how his works became central to the study of 'ilm al-tawhid.

Guided Path
1

The passage uses a list format, each work followed by its commentary.

3

Notice the comparative language: al-Wusta is 'above al-Sughra', and Sughra al-Sughra is 'smaller than al-Sughra'. This tells you the relative conciseness.
Sanusi wrote works of decreasing length for different audiences.
4

Each work has at least one commentary mentioned, some with high praise. Commentaries are key to the study of these texts.
Al-Malali's quotes provide external validation.
5
Reflect on the significance

The Middle Creed: more concise than the Greater and above the Lesser. The Commentary on the Middle Creed: It is among the finest works authored in the science of tawhid. The Lesser Creed (known as the Mother of Proofs, and Possessor of Proofs, and the Lesser Sanusiyya): It is the pearl of his creeds, and what is meant by their saying 'the Sanusi creed' when unqualified, and it was granted a renown that nothing else was granted, and scholars turned to it for commentary, and students for memorization and study. Al-Malali said: It is among the most sublime creeds, and no creed of anyone before or after equals it, and the Shaykh (may God be pleased with him) alluded to that in the beginning of his commentary on it. The Commentary on the Lesser Creed, also known as The Tawhid of the People of Gnosis and Knowledge of God and His Messengers by Proof and Demonstration, and as The Commentary on the Mother of Proofs; it too was granted renown and spread across the horizons, and how many glosses have been written upon it. The Minor of the Minor: Al-Malali described it, saying: (His abridged creed is extremely brief, and it is smaller than the Lesser Creed mentioned just now, and for this reason it is called the Minor of the Minor.) It was composed for my father — may God preserve him from every harm and trial, and grant him the highest rank — because when my father read the Lesser Creed with the Shaykh (may God be pleased with him) and completed it with commentary on more than one occasion, he found its study and memorization heavy due to his age and many concerns, so he asked the Shaykh (may God be pleased with him) to make for him a creed smaller than the Lesser so that he could study and memorize it. He wrote this creed for him and wrote it by his own hand, and in it he (may God be pleased with him) pointed out excellent subtleties and brilliant pearls that he had not mentioned in the previous creeds. The Commentary on the Minor of the Minor: It is a precious commentary that no profound student can dispense with. Al-Malali said: In it are amazing benefits and remarkable subtleties. The Prolegomena: Also known as the Introduction. Al-Malali said: Among them is the Prolegomena that he wrote to explain his Lesser Creed, and it is close to it in size. It consists of eight prolegomena. The Commentary on the Prolegomena: Reading this commentary is considered an excellent aid for students of usul and kalam, especially in the introductory phase.

Commentary
This passage is a bibliographical introduction listing the major creedal works of Imam al-Sanusi and their commentaries. The author (probably a later editor or compiler) presents each work with a brief description and often quotes the scholar al-Malali for evaluation. The passage proceeds in order: first, 'al-Aqida al-Wusta' (the Middle Creed) is described as being more concise than the 'al-Kubra' (the Greater) and above 'al-Sughra' (the Lesser) in terms of conciseness. Its commentary is praised as one of the finest works in tawhid. Next, 'al-Aqida al-Sughra' (the Lesser Creed), also known as 'Umm al-Barahin' or 'al-Sanusiyya al-Sughra', is introduced as the most famous of Sanusi's creeds — the one meant when 'Sanusi's creed' is mentioned without qualification. The author notes its immense popularity and the many commentaries written on it. Al-Malali's statement is quoted to emphasize its high status. Then the commentary on the Lesser Creed is described, also known as 'Tawhid Ahl al-Irfan' and 'Sharh Umm al-Barahin', and it too achieved wide distribution. Next, 'Sughra al-Sughra' (the Minor of the Minor) is presented: an even more abridged creed composed by Sanusi for the author's father, who found the Lesser Creed too heavy to study due to old age. Sanusi wrote it for him and included new insights not found in his earlier works. Its commentary is described as a precious work indispensable for students. Then 'al-Muqaddimat' (the Prolegomena) is mentioned: an introductory work explaining the Lesser Creed, consisting of eight introductory sections. Finally, the commentary on the Prolegomena is recommended as a valuable aid for students of usul and kalam, especially beginners.

Misconception & Correction
Misconception: Sanusi only wrote one creedal work, al-Aqida al-Sughra.
Correction: Sanusi authored multiple creedal works of varying lengths, including al-Wusta, al-Sughra, Sughra al-Sughra, and al-Muqaddimat, each with its own purpose and commentary.
