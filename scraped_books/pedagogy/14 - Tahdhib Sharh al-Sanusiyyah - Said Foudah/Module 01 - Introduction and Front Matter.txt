Author's Preface to the Second Edition
Author describes the second edition's corrections and additions.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the purpose of the author's second edition preface and the nature of the improvements he made.

Core Idea
The second edition preserves the original text while adding clarifying notes and refutations.

The author opens the second edition with a brief preface explaining what has changed and what has remained the same. This preface gives us insight into his scholarly priorities: clarity, conciseness, and defending sound doctrine against doubts. As you read, notice how carefully he balances the need for added explanation with the desire to keep the work focused.

Praise be to Allah, Lord of the worlds. 1. Sa'id Foudah. 1245 AH.

Commentary
This passage is the author's preface to the second edition of his work 'Tahdhib Sharh al-Sanusiyyah' (also known as 'Umm al-Barahin'). The author begins with the customary basmalah ('In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful') and praise of Allah and blessings upon the Prophet Muhammad. He then states that this is the second edition, which he presents to readers in its original form. He notes that he has not changed anything in the text except for correcting typographical errors. In addition, he has added a few clarifications, comments, and footnotes. The purpose of these additions is to provide increased confirmation of a proof, clarification, or responses to objections or doubts that may occur to some people. The author explains that he did not wish to overburden the book with excessive comments and expansions, lest it become too lengthy and deviate from its original purpose. Despite this, he has addressed some issues in certain places that he had not clarified previously and has established valuable and beneficial principles relevant to their subject. He thus considers this edition to be distinguished by benefits worthy of pursuit. The passage concludes with a prayer to Allah to benefit the author, all Muslims, and to keep them firm on the upright religion, followed by the author's name 'Sa'id Foudah' and the date 1245 AH (which corresponds to 1829-1830 CE).

The Importance of Ilm al-Aqa'id
Preface explaining the supreme importance of ilm al-aqa'id

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand why ilm al-aqa'id is considered the most important Islamic science and an individual obligation for every Muslim.

Core Idea
Knowing your creed is as essential as knowing anything- it defines your existence.

In this preface, the author sets the stage for the entire book by declaring the science of creed (ilm al-aqa'id) to be the most important of the Islamic sciences. He grounds this in its purpose: to give the Muslim certainty about his beliefs, his place in the world, and his duties. The passage is a passionate call to recognize the urgency of learning one's creed, especially in times of confusion.

He says it clarifies the creed, one's place in the world, and what is required.
3

The author says it is an individual obligation. Why? Because without it, one cannot be a true Muslim.
This sets the stage for Sanusi's book as a necessary tool.
4
Reflect on the contemporary problem

This justifies the need for a clear work like Umm al-Barahin.

ولهذا فقد رأيت من الواجب على كل من لديه القدرة على الخروج من هذا الإثم الذي وقع الناس فيه أن يفعل هذا، وأن يبذل جهده لإخراج غيره من الإثم والخطأ.

Preface to the First Edition: Praise be to God, and blessings and peace be upon our master Muhammad, the Seal of the Prophets and Messengers. To proceed: Verily, the science of creeds (ilm al-aqa'id) is among the important sciences, indeed it is the most important of Islamic sciences absolutely, because this science clarifies to the Muslim his creed (aqidah) which he must be certain of, and clarifies to the Muslim his position in this world, and what is required of him towards his religion. Therefore, it is an individual obligation (wajib 'ayn) upon Muslims, according to the detail mentioned at the beginning of this book. And without the Muslim knowing his creed and the principles of his religion, how can he truly be a Muslim? Just as a communist—without knowing the principles of his school—how can he truly be a communist? So the science of tawhid is a science of great importance, and Muslims in this time especially must increase their attention to it, because the general public—indeed even those who claim to be of the elite—have become not knowing their definitive creed, and they do not distinguish between creed and fiqh, nor do they differentiate between the science of belief and faith and the science of action. And they do not know that each of these two has types of evidence; the evidence acceptable in the first may not be acceptable in the second, and so on. Confusion has increased in our time, not only between these two sciences, but also among all the other shar'i sciences specific to this religion: creed, usul al-fiqh, tasawwuf, hadith, and other Islamic sciences. Therefore, I have seen it as an obligation upon everyone who has the ability to emerge from this sin in which people have fallen to do so, and to exert his effort to bring others out of sin and error. Hence came my interest in the book of the scholar Imam al-Sanusi entitled (Umm al-Barahin).
Source pages p. 6

Commentary
This passage is the preface to the first edition. The author begins with praise and then states that ilm al-aqa'id is the most important Islamic science because it clarifies the Muslim's creed, his place in the world, and what is required of him. It is an individual obligation. He uses an analogy: just as a communist must know his principles, a Muslim must know his creed. He criticizes the contemporary state where even elites do not know their creed and confuse creed with fiqh, and do not distinguish between evidence types. He concludes that he felt obligated to work on this book (Umm al-Barahin) to help address this confusion.
