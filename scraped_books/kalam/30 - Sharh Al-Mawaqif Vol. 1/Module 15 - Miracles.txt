The Quran as the First Miracle
Commentator explains the choice of preposition to highlight the Quran as the first miracle.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand why the preposition 'ma'ahu' (with him) is preferred over 'alayhi' (upon him) in describing the revelation of the Quran, and how this emphasizes the Quran as the first enduring miracle accompanying the Prophet's mission.

Core Idea
The Quran was the first miracle, arriving with the claim of prophethood, not after.

At first glance, the choice between 'with him' and 'upon him' may seem trivial. But in this passage, the commentator draws our attention to a deliberate theological nuance: the Quran was revealed immediately with the Prophet's claim to prophethood, making it the first and foremost miracle. This short note unpacks why that choice matters.

His statement: (And revealed with him) – he chose 'with him' (ma'ahu) over 'upon him' ('alayhi) as an indication that the Quran is the first of the miracles that did not lag behind the claim of prophethood; had he said 'upon him' ('alayhi), that would not have been understood.

Commentary
This passage is a methodological note. The author explains why the commentator chose the phrase 'revealed with him' rather than 'revealed upon him'. The reason is to emphasize that the Quran was the first miracle that accompanied the very beginning of the Prophet's mission, not delayed. Using 'upon him' might imply it was sent down later. Thus, the choice of preposition carries theological significance about the timing of the Quran as the first miracle.

Misconception & Correction
Misconception: The choice between 'معه' and 'عليه' for the revelation of the Quran is a minor grammatical detail with no theological impact.
Correction: The author explicitly states that the choice indicates the Quran was the first miracle, not delayed from the claim of prophethood. Thus, it affects our understanding of the order of miracles.
