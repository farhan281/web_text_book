The analogy draws on medieval physiology where the heart is the seat of the soul.

The Third Rank: Governing Spirits of Spheres and Earth
The author lists the spirits governing the spheres and the four divisions of the earth, citing prophetic testimony.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the classification of the third rank of souls as spirits that govern the celestial spheres and the four divisions of the earth, and the rational and revealed basis for affirming their existence.

Core Idea
Governing spirits exist for every celestial sphere and earthly region, as reason allows and revelation confirms.

This lesson unpacks the third rank of souls described by the author: spirits that govern the planets and the earth. We will trace the hierarchy from Saturn to the moon, then through the ether and air to the four divisions of the inhabited world. The author also provides the logical and scriptural foundation for accepting these beings as part of God's creation.

Guided Path
1

These angels correspond to the earth divisions and more.

Synthesize the conclusion

The prophets did not rely on their own strength against enemies; they relied on angels sent by Allah. In your daily battles against your own ego and the whispers of shaytan, do you ever consciously call upon Allah to send His angels to aid you? Or do you trust your own willpower, knowing it has failed you time and again?
?
You may think you are self-sufficient in your faith, but the greatest of creation—the prophets—declared their utter need for angelic help. When was the last time you felt a genuine, deep sense of spiritual poverty before Allah, acknowledging that without His means of guidance (angels, revelation) you are nothing? Write down one concrete way you will remind yourself of this dependence every day.
