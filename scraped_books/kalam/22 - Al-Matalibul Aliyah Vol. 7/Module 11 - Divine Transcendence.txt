Razi presents the philosophers' argument for eight carriers of the Throne based on celestial spheres, followed by an editorial rebuttal.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the philosophers’ derivation of eight carriers of the Throne from celestial spheres and the theological critique that insists on a literal angelic interpretation based on Quranic evidence.

Core Idea
The Throne’s carriers are eight angels, not abstract philosophical constructs from spheres.

This lesson presents a vivid debate within Islamic theology: the philosophers attempt to align Quranic verses about the Throne carriers with their cosmological system, but a strong rebuttal calls them back to scriptural literalism. By examining both sides, you will see how theological methodology guards against speculative interpretations that lack clear textual support.

Commentary
This passage presents two objections (sualan) to the preceding proofs and the beginning of the answer to the first objection. The first objection draws on the philosophers' (falasifa) view that a simple body (jism basit) is truly one and has no parts in actuality, though it is divisible in potentiality. The objector argues that the whole is distinct from its parts, so the parts being devoid does not entail the whole being devoid. The author begins to answer the first objection by stating that the simple body, though one in itself, still has the possibility of division. The Soul Is Not a Composite of Body and Accident
Proof that the soul cannot be a composite of body and accident

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand why the human soul cannot be a composite of body and accident, based on the principle that a composite is weaker than its parts and the immediate experience of the self as self-subsistent.

Core Idea
The soul is self-subsistent, so it cannot be a composite dependent on parts.

This lesson presents a classical kalam argument that the human soul cannot be a composite of body and accident. The argument relies on a general principle about composites and the undeniable self-awareness each person has of their own self. By the end, you'll see why identifying the soul with a body or a body-accident composite fails.

Guided Path
1
Understand the general principle
Read the first part of the passage which states that a composite quiddity is weaker than its parts because it needs them.
This principle is key to the argument.
2
Apply the principle to the soul
If the soul were composite of body and accident, it would be weaker than its parts.
An accident is not self-subsistent, so the soul would also not be self-subsistent.
3

Everyone knows by necessary knowledge that their self is self-subsistent and independent.
This contradicts the implication of the composite view.
4

The argument then eliminates any possibility that the soul is a body, either simply or with attributes.
Both are false, so the soul is not a body.

First Proof: Abstraction from Corporeality
Razi's first proof: abstraction entails superiority.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand why Razi argues that abstract essences (angels) are superior to corporeal beings (humans).

Core Idea
Abstract essences are superior because they are independent of place and space.

Razi's first proof for angelic superiority rests on a simple metaphysical principle: the abstract is better than the corporeal. Read carefully how he establishes both that angels are abstract and why abstraction entails superiority.

Guided Path
1
Identify the claim
Read the first sentence: souls and intellects are abstract essences. Notice the structure: claim then support.
The Arabic begins with 'الأول' indicating this is the first proof.
2
Understand the premise of abstraction
Why does Razi think abstraction is known? It is a given from earlier discussions. Focus on the definition: abstract means independent of corporeality and its attachments.
3
Grasp the superiority argument
The abstract is independent of place and space, while bodies need them. Independence is better than need. This is a straightforward axiological principle.
The Arabic says 'الغني عن الشيء أفضل من المحتاج إليه'.
4
Connect to angelic superiority
Since angels (as souls/intellects) are abstract, they are superior to humans who are corporeal. Confirm this conclusion in the text.

Source Text
⚠ Extracted via Advanced OCR ∼97% accuracy, minor errors may remain — always verify against original Arabic books
الأول : إن النفوس والعقول ذوات مجردة عن الجسمية وعلائقها ، فتكون أفضل من هذه الأجسام ، ومن الجسمانيات . إما أنها مجردة . فقد علمت . وإما أن المجرد أفضل ، فلان المجرد غني عن المكان والحيز . والجسم والجسماني محتاج إليه . والغني عن الشيء أفضل من المحتاج إليه
والثاني : إن الملائكة ذوات بسيطة مبرأة عن الكثرة الحاصلة بسبب المادة والصورة ، وعن الكثرة الحاصلة بسبب اجتماع الوحدات . وأما الإنسان فإنه مركب من النفس والبدن . والنفس محتاجة إلى قوى كثيرة ، وإلى آلات كثيرة ، حتى تقدر أن تفعل بواسطة كل آلة فعلاً آخر. وأما البدن فهو مركب من الأجزاء الكثيرة . والبسيط جزء من المركب . لأن أسباب العدم للمركب ، أكثر منها للبسيط
والثالث : إن تركيب الإنسان تركيب رخو ، مستعد لقبول التفرق والتمزق بأدنى سبب ، فهو كالشيء الموضوع على مرتعة الآفات ، وممر المخافات . تؤذيه البقة . وتقتله الشرقة . أوله نطفة قذرة ، وآخره جيفة مذرة وفيها بين الحالين حمال عذرة
وأما أبدان تلك الأرواح . فهي السموات الباقية الدائمة المبرأة عن الفطور والتفاوت . فأي عقل يجوز إثبات المناسبة بينها ؟
والرابع : تا قد بينا في باب [ النفس (١) أن هذه النفوس (٢) ] الناطقة معلولات لتلك العلل العالية في ذواتها وفي كمالاتها . والمعلول الضعيف ، كيف يمكن أن يقابل بالعلة القاهرة القوية ؟ وأيضاً : فالموجود لا يصل إلى المعلول ، إلا بعد أن يصل إلى العلة . ثم يفيض منها إلى المعلول . فتلك العلة كالبحار الآمنة عن التغير والفضاء. وهذه المعلولات كالقطرات التي تكون في معرض الجفاف والذهاب ، بحسب كل لحظة ولمحة . فكيف يقابل أحدهما بالآخر ؟ .
والخامس : إن المشابهة بين الجواهر الملكية المقدسة ، وبين واجب الوجود ره في الصفات السلبية ، الدالة على الاستغناء : أكثر ، فوجب أن تكون أفضل وأكمل . ومن جملتها : أنها لا تحتاج إلى الأغذية ، ولا إلى الملابس والمناكح . وبالجملة : فالحاجات هناك قليلة ، بل لا الحاجة هناك إلا إلى الله وههنا جهات الحاجات ، كأنها غير متناهية . فثبت : أنه لا مناسبة البتة بينها وبين البشر في الذوات
First: The souls and intellects are essences abstracted from corporeality and its attachments, so they are better than these bodies and corporeal things. As to their being abstracted, that is known. And as to the abstract being better, it is because the abstract is independent of place and space, while the body and corporeal are in need of it, and the one independent of a thing is better than the one in need of it. 
Second: The angels are simple essences, free from the multiplicity that occurs due to matter and form, and from the multiplicity that occurs due to the aggregation of units. As for man, he is composed of soul and body. The soul needs many faculties and many instruments in order to be able to perform through each instrument a different act. As for the body, it is composed of many parts. The simple is part of the composite, because the causes of non-existence for the composite are more than those for the simple. 
Third: The composition of man is a loose composition, ready to accept separation and tearing apart by the slightest cause. He is like something placed on the pasture of afflictions and the path of fears. A gnat hurts him and a sip kills him. His beginning is a foul drop, his end is a stinking corpse, and between the two states he carries filth. As for the bodies of those spirits, they are the heavens, everlasting and permanent, free from rents and inequality. What intellect would permit establishing similarity between them? 
Fourth: We have shown in the chapter on the soul that these rational souls are effects of those high causes in their essences and perfections. How can a weak effect be compared to a strong, dominant cause? Moreover, the existing thing does not reach the effect except after reaching the cause, then flows from it to the effect. That cause is like seas safe from change and decay, and these effects are like drops that are subject to drying up and vanishing at every moment and glance. So how can one be compared with the other? 
Fifth: The similarity between the sacred angelic substances and the Necessary Existence in negative attributes that indicate independence is greater, so they must be better and more perfect. Among them: they do not need food, clothing, or marriage. In short, needs there are few; indeed, there is no need there except for God. Here, the aspects of needs are, as it were, infinite. So it is established: there is absolutely no similarity between them and humans in essences.
Source pages p. 423

Commentary
This passage presents five proofs for the superiority of angels over humans in their essences. 

First proof: Souls and intellects (of angels) are abstract from corporeality and its attachments, making them superior. Abstraction implies independence from place and space, while bodies and corporeal things need place. The independent is better than the dependent. 

Second proof: Angels are simple essences, free from the multiplicity of matter-form composition and from aggregation of units. Humans are composed of soul and body; the soul needs many faculties and instruments, and the body has many parts. Simplicity is superior because the causes of non-existence are fewer for the simple. 

Third proof: Human composition is loose and fragile, easily torn apart, subject to harm from small things like gnats and sips, beginning as a foul drop and ending as a stinking corpse. In contrast, the bodies of angelic spirits are the heavens—everlasting, permanent, free from rents and inequality. No similarity. 

Fourth proof: Rational souls are effects of high angelic causes. A weak effect cannot be compared to a strong cause. The cause is like a vast sea, stable and unchanging; the effect is like drops that dry up. They are incomparable. 

Fifth proof: Angelic substances share more negative attributes with God (such as independence from needs) than humans do. Angels have few needs—only God; humans have infinite needs. Thus no essential similarity.

Misconception & Correction
Misconception: Some think 'abstract' means non-existent or purely imaginary.
Correction: In Islamic philosophy, 'abstract' (mujarrad) means independent of matter and its properties, not imaginary. It denotes a real, higher ontological status.

Checkpoint
According to the first proof, why is the abstract better than the corporeal?
A) Because the abstract is material.
B) Because the abstract is independent of place and space.
C) Because the abstract can be seen.
D) Because the abstract is created later.

Key Takeaway
Angels' abstraction from matter makes them superior because independence from space is a mark of perfection.

How This Lesson Applies — to MY Life?
?
You are constantly preoccupied with your body's comfort, appearance, and place in the world. What would change in your daily life if you truly believed that your soul is superior precisely because it is independent of all that?
?
Razi argues that independence from space and place is a mark of perfection. Where in your life are you most dependent on things, people, or positions for your sense of worth? Can you name one attachment you feel you could begin to detach from today?
?
If abstraction from matter makes the angels better, what does your excessive attachment to material things say about your soul? What one material indulgence will you sacrifice this week to affirm your soul's superiority?

Second Proof: Simplicity vs Composition
Razi's second proof: simplicity entails superiority.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand why simplicity (angels) is superior to composition (humans) in Razi's metaphysical framework.

Core Idea
Simple essences are superior because they have fewer causes of non-existence.

Razi's second contrast hinges on ontology: angels are simple, humans are composite. Read how he argues that simplicity entails fewer dependencies and therefore greater perfection.

Guided Path
1
Contrast simplicity and composition
Razi describes angels as simple essences free from multiplicity (matter-form, units), while humans are composites of soul and body, with many parts and faculties.
The Arabic says 'ذوات بسيطة مبرأة عن الكثرة'.
2
Analyze human composition
Humans have a soul needing many faculties/instruments and a body with many parts. This complexity implies more needs and vulnerabilities.
3
Why simplicity is better
The simple is less prone to non-existence because it has fewer causes of annihilation. Composition introduces more ways to break down.
Razi explicitly says 'أسباب العدم للمركب أكثر منها للبسيط'.
4
Connect to superiority
Therefore, angels (simple) are superior to humans (composite). This is a metaphysical rather than a moral argument.

Third Proof: Human Fragility vs Heavenly Permanence
Razi's third proof: human fragility vs heavenly permanence.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand how the contrast between human fragility and the permanence of heavenly bodies demonstrates angelic superiority.

Core Idea
Humans are fragile and transient; angels' bodies (heavens) are everlasting and incorruptible.

Razi paints a vivid picture of human weakness—easily hurt, beginning as a drop, ending as a corpse—and contrasts it with the permanent, flawless heavens that serve as the angels' bodies. This rhetorical proof underscores their essential superiority.

Guided Path
1
Note the description of human fragility
Humans are loosely composed, easily torn, harmed by tiny things, and have a foul beginning and end. Razi uses rhetorical language to evoke weakness.
Words like 'رخو', 'تؤذيه البقة', 'تقتله الشرقة' emphasize vulnerability.
2
Contrast with the heavens
The bodies of angelic spirits are the heavens—everlasting, permanent, free from rents and inequality.
The Arabic says 'السموات الباقية الدائمة المبرأة عن الفطور والتفاوت'.
3
Recognize the conclusion
Razi asks rhetorically: what intellect would permit establishing similarity between them? The implication is clear: they are incomparably different, with angels far superior.

Core Idea

Razi invokes a causal hierarchy: human rational souls are effects of higher angelic causes. Since a cause is always stronger and more perfect than its effect, the comparison shows angelic superiority.
