The Noble Subject of al-Maṭālib al-ʿĀliyah
The author defines the subject and explains its honor.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the subject matter of the book al-Maṭālib al-ʿĀliyah and why it is considered the most honorable of sciences.

Core Idea
The book's subject—God's essence and attributes—is the most honorable of all subjects.

The author begins by stating the subject of his work: discussion of God's essence and attributes. He then quotes his own introduction to establish that a science's honor derives from its subject matter. This passage lays the foundation for why theology is the highest pursuit.

Guided Path
1

The quote from the introduction is meant to reinforce the nobility of the topic.
2
Identify the subject and the claim of honor
The subject is 'kalām fī dhāt Allāh wa-ṣifātih' (discussion of God's essence and attributes). The author says this is the first reason for the science's honor.
3
Ask: why does this matter?
Reflect on why studying God is considered the highest science. This sets the tone for the entire book.
This connects to the learning objective.

Subject of the Book. And the subject of al-Maṭālib al-ʿĀliyah is discussion of the essence of God Most High and His attributes. For in the introduction of the book, in the first chapter, the author says: The first of the aspects that necessitate honor: the honor of the matter investigated in that science, and that is in this science. It is the essence of God Most High and His attributes. And Muslim scholars name the science that investigates God and His attributes: 1 - ʿIlm al-ʿAqīdah (the science of creed). 2 - or ʿIlm al-Tawḥīd (the science of divine unity). 3 - or ʿIlm al-Kalām (the science of dialectical theology). And it is called ʿIlm al-ʿAqīdah because the investigation in it concerns the existence of God. And whoever believes in the existence of God is obliged to affirm prophethood, angels, resurrection from the dead, and all other matters of the unseen (samʿiyyāt) that have been conveyed by revelation. And it is called ʿIlm al-Tawḥīd because the most prominent issue investigated in it is the proofs of the oneness of God Almighty. And it is called ʿIlm al-Kalām because the most famous issue in which debate abounded among Muslims is the issue of the speech of God Most High. The Muʿtazilites said that speech is created and eventful (makhūq muḥdath) due to His saying: 'No remembrance comes to them from their Lord newly revealed (muḥdath) except they listen to it while they play' [Quran 21:2]. And the Ashʿarites said that the remembrance intended by God Almighty is not the Quran, but rather it is the speech of the Messenger (peace be upon him) and his admonishing them. (1) [Footnote:] (1) al-Ibānah fī Uṣūl al-Diyānah by Abū al-Ḥasan al-Ashʿarī.

Commentary
This passage defines the subject matter of the book and explains the historical reasons for its various names. The author states that the subject of al-Maṭālib al-ʿĀliyah is discussion of God's essence and attributes. He then quotes the book's own introduction to emphasize the nobility of this subject. Next, he notes that Muslim scholars refer to this science by three names: ʿIlm al-ʿAqīdah (science of creed), because it establishes belief in God's existence, which logically requires accepting prophethood, angels, resurrection, and all revealed matters (samʿiyyāt); ʿIlm al-Tawḥīd (science of divine unity), because its central concern is proving God's oneness; and ʿIlm al-Kalām (theology), because the most contentious debate among early Muslims was about the nature of God's speech (kalām). The author illustrates this with the Muʿtazilite-Ashʿarite dispute: the Muʿtazila argued that God's speech is created (makhūq muḥdath), citing Quran 21:2 where 'dhikr' (remembrance) is described as 'muḥdath' (newly revealed). The Ashʿarites countered that the 'dhikr' in the verse refers to the Prophet's own speech, not to the Quran. This controversy gave rise to the name 'Kalām' for the discipline. The footnote adds a source: al-Ashʿarī's al-Ibānah fī Uṣūl al-Diyānah.

The Three Names of the Science and the Kalām Debate
The author lists the three names and explains their rationale, concluding with the kalām debate.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand why the science of God's attributes is called ʿIlm al-ʿAqīdah, ʿIlm al-Tawḥīd, and ʿIlm al-Kalām, and the historical debate that gave rise to the last name.

Core Idea
The science is called al-Kalām because of the famous debate over God's speech.

Muslim scholars have given this science three names, each highlighting a different aspect. The most intriguing is 'ilm al-kalām, which derives from the heated controversy over whether God's speech is created or eternal. This passage explains each name and outlines the positions of the Muʿtazila and Ashʿariyya.

Guided Path
1
Read the passage carefully
Read the Arabic and translation. Note the three names: ʿIlm al-ʿAqīdah, ʿIlm al-Tawḥīd, ʿIlm al-Kalām.
Each name has a rationale; focus on the reasons given.
2
Understand the first two names
ʿIlm al-ʿAqīdah: because it establishes belief in God, which entails all other beliefs. ʿIlm al-Tawḥīd: because its central issue is proving God's oneness.
3
Focus on the third name and the debate
The name ʿIlm al-Kalām comes from the debate about God's speech. The Muʿtazila said it is created; the Ashʿariyya said it is not.
The Quran verse cited is 21:2, where 'dhikr' is called 'muḥdath'.
4
Ask: why does this matter?
Understanding the naming helps appreciate the historical context and the central issues of kalām.
This connects to the learning objective.

Commentary
This passage begins by stating the subject matter of the book, Al-Matalib al-‘Aliyah, which is the discussion of God's essence and attributes, quoting the author's own words from the introduction. It then explains that Muslim scholars have given this science several names: ‘Ilm al-‘Aqida (science of creed), ‘Ilm al-Tawhid (science of divine unity), and ‘Ilm al-Kalam (science of theology). For each name, a reason is provided. It is called ‘Ilm al-‘Aqida because it investigates the existence of God, and belief in God necessitates affirming prophethood, angels, resurrection, and all transmitted matters (sam‘iyyat). It is called ‘Ilm al-Tawhid because its most prominent topic is the proofs of God's oneness. It is called ‘Ilm al-Kalam because the most debated issue among Muslims was the nature of God's speech (kalam): the Mu‘tazila argued that speech is created and originated, citing Quran 21:2, while the Ash‘ariyya interpreted the 'reminder' in that verse as referring to the Prophet's speech, not the Quran. The passage ends by noting that some also call this science 'Islamic philosophy' because philosophy aims to interpret the cosmos and investigate first causes, a goal that Muslims also pursued.

Why Some Scholars Call Kalam 'Islamic Philosophy'
Explains why kalam was named Islamic philosophy.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand why some Muslim scholars historically referred to the discipline of kalam as 'Islamic philosophy'.

Core Idea
Kalam was called Islamic philosophy because it shared philosophy's goal of exploring the universe's first causes.

In this passage, the author explains a historical naming choice. Some Muslim scholars equated kalam with 'Islamic philosophy'. Why? Because philosophy originally aimed to interpret the entire universe and investigate the primary causes of all things—exactly what kalam does when it examines God and His attributes as the First Cause.

Misconception & Correction
Misconception: One might think that extremely large finite quantities are essentially infinite, or that the intellect can conceive of eternity by extrapolating from large numbers.
Correction: The author emphasizes that no matter how large a finite quantity is, it remains finite when assembled from finite parts. The finite is fundamentally different from the infinite; the gap is like that between non-existence and existence.

Checkpoint
According to the author, why is the totality of finite quantities in the examples considered nothing compared to al-azal?
A) Because the quantities are too large to count.
B) Because finite quantities, no matter how large, are still finite and thus insignificant relative to the infinite.
C) Because the intellect cannot imagine such numbers.
D) Because the examples are only hypothetical.

Key Takeaway
Even the most immense finite quantities are utterly insignificant compared to the infinite eternity of Allah, demonstrating the limits of human intellect.

How This Lesson Applies — to MY Life?
?
You have just read that even the most immense finite quantity is like non-existence compared to Allah's eternity. In your daily life, what finite thing do you treat as if it were infinite in importance? How does that comparison challenge your priorities?
?
The author says human intellects cannot even grasp the beginnings of these meanings. When you recite verses about Allah's eternal nature, do you truly feel your own limitation, or do you slide into a comfortable assumption that you understand? What would change if you took this lesson to heart?
?
If the calculations described here—like filling the universe with millet grains—are beyond your full comprehension, how much more so is the reality of Allah? Are you willing to accept that your knowledge of Allah is as nothing compared to His true nature? What does that acceptance demand of your worship and humility?
