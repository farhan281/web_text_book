The Islamic View on Extraordinary Actions of Souls
Sanusi presents the Islamic creed on extraordinary actions and lists the philosophers' claimed methods.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand that according to Sunni Islam, extraordinary actions of souls are not caused by any inherent power but are directly created by Allah, and you will be able to distinguish the Islamic view from the philosophers' claim that souls possess independent causal efficacy.

Core Idea
Extraordinary actions are created by Allah alone, not by any soul-power.

Sanusi opens this inquiry by asserting a foundational Ash'ari principle: anything remarkable that appears from a human soul is purely a creation of Allah (bi-mahd khalq Allah). He immediately contrasts this with the philosophers' view that the soul can act independently in the world, even listing their claimed techniques. As you read, watch how the author frames the Islamic stance as a direct negation of any causal role for the soul.

This shows the philosophers' confidence in soul-power.
3
Understand the Islamic stance

Commentary
This passage introduces the sixth inquiry (mabhas) of the work, concerning extraordinary actions and perceptions attributed to human souls. It first presents the Islamic (Ash'ari) position: these are purely created by God (bi-mahd khalq Allah), with no genuine causal power in the soul. This is contrasted with the philosophers' view that the soul can act in other bodies, even causing natural phenomena like rain or earthquakes. The passage then lists various phenomena and techniques associated with these claims: the evil eye, sorcery, incantations, invocation of planets, talismans, enchantments, and geometric tricks. After this enumeration, the author reiterates the Islamic stance and states that the discussion will proceed in three sections. The first section concerns actions of souls, and it is supported by references to saintly miracles (karamat) and two hadiths (one where God says 'Be' and the famous hadith qudsi about closeness to Allah). This establishes that extraordinary actions are possible through God's creation, not through any inherent power of the soul.

The Revealed Basis for Saintly Miracles (Karamat)
Sanusi summarizes the Islamic stance and cites prophetic hadiths as evidence for saintly miracles.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand that the Islamic view of saintly miracles (karamat) is supported by scriptural evidence from the Quran and Hadith, and that these miracles are a result of Allah's direct creation, not the saint's own power.

Core Idea
Karamat are real but come solely from Allah's creation, not from the saint.

Having established the general principle, Sanusi now focuses on the category of actions, specifically the miracles of saints (karamat). He provides two key hadiths that demonstrate how closeness to Allah results in extraordinary acts, all created by Allah. This lesson examines that evidence.

Guided Path
1
Read the summary and plan
The first sentence restates the Islamic position. Note the three-part plan for discussion.
2
Examine the first hadith
The hadith about becoming a rabbani and saying 'Be'. Understand that this power is given by Allah, not intrinsic.
The servant does not create; Allah creates at his words.
3
Examine the second hadith
The hadith qudsi about nawafil. Note how Allah becomes the faculties. This shows that the saint's actions are Allah's actions.
This is a famous hadith in Islamic spirituality.

Strange soul phenomena are classified into actions, sleep perceptions, and wake perceptions.

Explains miracles, karamat, and the soul's influence as basis for extraordinary acts.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the distinction between miracles (mu'jizat) and karamat, and the theoretical basis for extraordinary acts rooted in the soul's influence on bodies.

Core Idea
Extraordinary acts come from noble souls; with prophetic claim they are miracles, otherwise karamat.

In this passage, al-Sanusi explains the first category of actions: those that are extraordinary. He begins by listing examples—miracles, karamat, the evil eye, magic—and then provides a metaphysical foundation: the human soul can influence other bodies due to a passionate connection. This sets the stage for distinguishing miracles from karamat based on whether they accompany a claim to prophethood.

Read the passage carefully

2
Understand the soul's role
Notice how the author argues that the soul can affect other bodies. Why does he compare it to abstract jewels? This is the theoretical core.
The soul's influence is compared to that of abstract substances in the material world.
3
Distinguish miracles from karamat
Identify the key condition that turns an extraordinary act into a miracle. What is the role of claiming prophethood?

فالأول مثل المعجزات والكرامات من الأنبياء، والأولياء، والإصابة بالعين (۱) ممن له تلك الخاصية بلا اختياره. ومثل السحر والعزائم ، ونحو ذلك مما يكون بمزاولة أفعال، وأعمال مخصوصة، وذلك لأن للنفس تأثيراً في البدن كما للجواهر العالية المجردة في عالم الكون والفساد وليس اقتصار تأثيرها على بدنها لانطباعها فيه، بل لعلاقة عشقية بينهما، فلا يبعد أن يكون لبعض النفوس قوة بها تقوى على التأثير في بدن آخر، بل في حيوان آخر، بل في أجسام أخر. حتى تصير بمنزلة نفس ما للعالم ، أو لبعض الأجسام، لا سيما الأجسام التي يحصل لها أولوية بها لمناسبتها لبدنها بوجه خاص، فلا يبعد أن تحيل الهواء إلى الغيم، فتحدث مطراً بقدر الحاجة أو أزيد كالطوفان، وأن تفعل تحريكاً، وتسكينا، وتكثيفاً، وتخلخلاً، يتبعها سحب ورياح وصواعق وزلازل ونبوع مياه، وعيون، ونحو ذلك، وكذا إهلاك مدن وإزالة أمراض، ودفع مؤذيات وغيرها .
وربما تكون النفس شريفة قوية تطلب خيراً، وتدعو الله تعالى، فتستحق بهيئتها واستعدادها ترجيحاً لوجود بعض الممكنات، فتوجد وأمثال هذه إذا صدرت عن نفوس خيرة شريفة . فإن كانت مقرونة بدعوى النبوة فمعجزات، وإلا فكرامات وقد يكون في بعض النفوس خاصية، تحدث فيما أعجبها أذى ظاهراً، وهـو الإصابة بالعين، وقد تستعين النفوس في إحداث الغرائب بمزاولة أعمال مخصوصة وهي السحر، أو بقوى بعض الروحانيات وهي العزائم، أو بالأجرام الفلكية، وهي دعوة الكواكب، أو بتمزيج القوى السماوية بالأرضية، وهي الطلسمات، أو بالخواص العنصرية، وهي النير نجات، أو بالنسب الرياضية، وهي الحيل الهندسية ، وقد يتركب بعض هذه مع بعض كجر الأثقال، ونقل المياه،
(۱) ولذلك أمر الرسول صلى الله عليه وسلم - أمته بالتعوذ من شياطين الإنس والجن بقراءة المعوذيتن . قال أحمد ، حدثنا محمد بن إسحاق، حدثنا ابن لهيعة عن مسرح بن ماعان، عن عقبة بن عامر. قال : قال رسول الله - صلى الله عليه وسلم اقرأ بالمعوذيتن فإنك لن تقرأ بمثلهما. تفرد به أحمد.
The first (category) is like miracles and karamat of the prophets and saints, and the evil eye from those who have that property without their choice. And like magic and incantations and similar things that are performed by practicing specific actions and works. That is because the soul has influence on the body, just as the high abstract jewels have in the world of generation and corruption. And its influence is not limited to its own body because it is imprinted in it, but rather due to a passionate relation between them. So it is not unlikely that some souls have a power by which they can influence another body, even another animal, even other bodies, until it becomes like the soul of the world, or of some bodies, especially those bodies that gain priority because of their specific suitability to its body. So it is not unlikely that it changes air into clouds, causing rain according to need or more like a flood, and that it causes movement, stillness, condensation, rarefaction, followed by clouds, winds, lightning, earthquakes, springs of water, fountains, and similar things, as well as destruction of cities, removal of diseases, repulsion of harmful things, etc. And perhaps the soul is noble and strong, seeking good and supplicating to God, so it deserves by its state and preparedness the preponderance of existence of some contingent things, so they exist. And these, when they come from good noble souls: if they are accompanied by a claim to prophecy then they are miracles, otherwise karamat. And there may be in some souls a property that causes apparent harm to that which it admires, which is the evil eye. And souls may seek help in producing wonders by practicing specific works: magic, or the powers of some spiritual beings (incantations), or celestial bodies (invocation of planets), or mixing celestial and terrestrial forces (talismans), or elemental properties (niranjat), or mathematical ratios (geometric tricks). And some of these may be combined with others, like pulling heavy loads and transporting water. (1) Therefore the Messenger – may God bless him and grant him peace – commanded his nation to seek refuge from the devils of humans and jinn by reciting the Mu'awwidhatayn. Ahmad said: narrated to us Muhammad ibn Ishaq, narrated to us Ibn Lahi'a from Sirh ibn Ma'an from 'Uqba ibn 'Amir, he said: The Messenger of God – may God bless him and grant him peace – said: 'Recite the Mu'awwidhatayn, for you will not recite anything like them.' This is unique to Ahmad.

Commentary
This passage is a detailed explanation of the first category: actions. The author lists examples: miracles of prophets, karamat of saints, the evil eye (which occurs involuntarily), magic, and incantations. He then provides a theoretical foundation: the soul has influence on its body similar to abstract substances in the material world, but its influence is not confined to its own body due to a passionate relation; thus some souls can affect other bodies, even to the extent of causing natural phenomena like rain, floods, earthquakes, etc. He then distinguishes miracles from karamat: if the action is accompanied by a claim to prophethood, it is a miracle; otherwise, it is a karamah. He also describes the evil eye as a harmful property in some souls. Finally, he enumerates various methods souls use to produce wonders: magic (sihr), incantations (`aza'im), invocation of planets (da`wat al-kawakib), talismans (talismat), niranjat (illusions), and geometric tricks (hiyal handasiyya). He adds that these may be combined, e.g., for moving heavy loads or transporting water. A footnote supports the evil eye discussion by citing a hadith about the Mu'awwidhatayn for protection.

Misconception & Correction
Misconception: Miracles and karamat are essentially the same.
Correction: They are distinguished by the presence of a claim to prophethood.

The Evil Eye, Magic, and Other Methods of Producing Wonders
Describes the evil eye and enumerates methods for producing wonders, with a hadith.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the nature of the evil eye and the various methods souls use to produce extraordinary phenomena, including magic, incantations, talismans, and geometric tricks.

Core Idea
Souls can cause harm through the evil eye or produce wonders using specific techniques like magic and incantations.

After discussing miracles and karamat, al-Sanusi turns to other types of extraordinary acts. He explains the evil eye as a harmful property in some souls, then enumerates various methods—magic, incantations, invocation of planets, talismans, niranjat, and geometric tricks—by which souls can produce wonders. The footnote adds a practical dimension, citing the Prophet's instruction to recite the Mu'awwidhatayn for protection.

Guided Path
1
Read the passage carefully
Read the Arabic passage once, then the translation. Focus on the evil eye description and the list of methods.
2
Identify the evil eye
Note that the evil eye is a property that causes harm involuntarily to what is admired.
3
List the methods
Write down the six methods mentioned: magic, incantations, invocation of planets, talismans, niranjat, and geometric tricks. Understand how each uses different forces.
4
Reflect on the hadith
Read the footnote about the Mu'awwidhatayn. Why is protection from the evil eye and other harms important?
