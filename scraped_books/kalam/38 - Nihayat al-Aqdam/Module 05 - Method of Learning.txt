Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand two approaches used by Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) in calling people: starting with tawhid then prophethood, or starting with prophethood while incorporating tawhid.

Core Idea
The Prophet used flexible methods, always combining evidence for tawhid and prophethood.

Prophetic proof is rational and considers human capacity, not blind following.

He asserts that consensus itself is a contextual indication (qarina) that points to that hidden text with certainty, similar to how mutawatir reports produce knowledge through contextual indication, not merely because of the number of transmitters. The second proof is the practice of the Companions: they rebuked (tabkit) anyone who violated consensus and considered them deviant. They also allowed those who upheld consensus to not mention the specific basis of the consensus, because that basis might be a verbal or practical contextual indication that gave them knowledge but cannot be expressed in words, or it might be an explicit statement. In either case, if they stated the basis it becomes a proof, and if they did not, the consensus itself suffices. Thus, these two arguments establish that ijma' is a valid and authoritative proof.
