The Colophon: Historical and Textual Significance
Colophon recording completion and copy dates.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the purpose and content of a manuscript colophon and its value for historical and textual scholarship.

Core Idea
A colophon preserves the story of a book's creation and transmission.

Before we dive into the theological arguments of the third volume, we pause to notice the colophon that ends the second volume. Colophons are scribal notes that record when and where a manuscript was completed or copied. They offer invaluable clues for dating texts and tracing their transmission. This colophon from al-Matalib al-Aliyah gives us two important dates and a sneak peek at the next volume's topic.

Guided Path
1
Read the colophon text
Read the Arabic colophon and its translation. Notice the two dates and the mention of the next volume's contents.
Pay attention to the names of months and the hijri years.
2
Understand the two dates
Identify the date the original work was completed (605 AH) and the date this copy was finished (636 AH). Reflect on the 31-year gap.
The original was written in Jurjaniyah Khwarazm, a region in Central Asia.
3
Preview the next volume
The colophon lists the topics of the third volume: God's positive attributes. This sets the stage for the following lessons.
4
Appreciate the colophon's value
Consider how such colophons help scholars reconstruct the history of a text and its manuscript tradition.
Without colophons, we would know much less about when and where important works were composed.

The caller (may God have mercy on him) said: This book was completed in (Jurjaniyah Khwarazm) in the latter half of Rabi' al-Awwal, year six hundred and five. And this copy of ours was completed on the night of Thursday, after the passing of the greatest night, the eleventh of Jumada al-Ula, year six hundred and thirty-six. Praise be to God, Lord of the Worlds, and His blessings be upon our master Muhammad, the unlettered Prophet, and upon all his family. The second volume of al-Matalib al-Aliyah from the Divine Science of Imam Fakhr al-Din al-Razi has been completed. It is followed by the third volume, whose subject is (the positive attributes, namely that He, glorified and exalted, is: powerful, knowing, living, hearing, seeing, speaking, everlasting.

Commentary
This is a colophon recording the completion of the original manuscript in Jurjaniyah Khwarazm in 605 AH, and the completion of this copy in 636 AH. It also provides a preview of the third volume, which will discuss the positive attributes of God: power, knowledge, life, hearing, sight, speech, and eternity.

Understanding a Classical Colophon
Al-Razi's colophon records completion and copy dates.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the purpose and typical contents of a classical Islamic manuscript colophon, using Al-Razi's colophon as an example.

Core Idea
A colophon is a manuscript's birth certificate.

This passage is a colophon, an inscription at the end of a manuscript that records details about its production. It tells us when and where the book was completed, and when this particular copy was finished. These details are invaluable for scholars tracing the history of texts. Let's examine Al-Razi's colophon to see what we can learn.

Guided Path
1
Read the Arabic colophon
Start by reading the Arabic text slowly. Notice the phrases that indicate dates, locations, and the name of the copyist (al-da'i).
Arabic colophons often begin with 'qala al-da'i' (the supplicant said).
2
Identify the two key dates
The colophon gives two dates: when the book was originally completed (605 AH) and when this copy was finished (636 AH). Note the difference.
3
Understand the location
The book was completed in Jurjaniyya of Khwarazm. This is a historical region in Central Asia. Think about the geographical spread of Islamic learning.
4
See the link to the next volume
At the end, there is a note about the third part's subject: the positive attributes of God. This shows how colophons sometimes preview subsequent content.

قال الداعي . رحمه الله عليه : وقد تم هذا الكتاب بـ ( جرجانية خوارزم ، في النصف الأخير ، من ربيع الأول ، سنة خمس وستمائة . · وقد تمت نسختنا هذه ليلة الخميس ، بعد انقضاء أعظم ليل ، الحادي عشر من جمادى الأولى ، سنة ست وثلاث وستمائة . والحمد لله ربي العالمين ، وصلواته على سيدنا محمد ، النبي الأمي ، وعلى آله أجمعين (۱) ] تم الجزء الثاني من المطالب العالية من العلم الإلهي للامام فخر الدين الرازي . ويليه الجزء الثالث . وموضوعه في : ( الصفات الإيجابية ، وهي كونه سبحانه وتعالى : قادراً عالماً . حياً . سميعاً بصيراً . متكلماً . باقية .
The supplicant, may God have mercy on him, said: This book was completed in Jurjaniyya of Khwarazm, in the latter half of Rabi' al-Awwal, year six hundred and five. And our copy was completed on Thursday night, after the passing of the greatest night, the eleventh of Jumada al-Ula, year six hundred and thirty-six. Praise be to God, Lord of the worlds, and His blessings be upon our master Muhammad, the unlettered prophet, and upon all his family. The second part of al-Matalib al-Aliyah from the divine science by Imam Fakhr al-Din al-Razi is completed. The third part follows it, and its subject is the positive attributes: that He, exalted, is powerful, knowing, living, hearing, seeing, speaking, eternal.
Source pages p. 150

Commentary
This passage is a colophon (khatima) from the copyist or perhaps the author’s supplication. It records the completion of the book in Jurjaniyya, Khwarazm, in the latter half of Rabi' al-Awwal 605 AH. Then it records the completion of this particular copy on Thursday night, after 'the greatest night' (possibly referring to a significant night like Laylat al-Qadr), on 11 Jumada al-Ula 636 AH. It ends with praise and blessings. After the colophon, an editor's note states that the second part of Al-Matalib al-Aliyah is completed and the third part follows, with its subject being the positive attributes: power, knowledge, life, hearing, sight, speech, and eternity.
