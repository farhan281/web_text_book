Volume Four: Divine Matters and the Proofs for God's Existence
Prefaces Volume Four, outlining its structure and the two methods for proving God's existence.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the structure of Volume Four of Sharh al-Maqasid and the two main methods used for proving God's existence: the philosophers' argument from existence and the theologians' argument from temporal origination.

Core Idea
Volume Four is the heart of Sharh al-Maqasid, and its proofs for God are foundational.

This opening passage introduces the fourth volume of al-Taftazani's magnum opus, explaining that it covers the most critical topic: divine matters (al-ilahiyyat). The author then previews the two traditional approaches to proving God's existence that will be detailed in the first chapter. This sets the stage for the entire volume.

Guided Path
1
Recognize the volume's significance
Read the first part of the passage where the author praises God and states that Volume Four covers the fifth objective on divine matters, which is the most important part of the book.
The author explicitly says the previous four objectives are merely preparatory.
2
Identify the structure of the volume
Note that the volume is divided into seven chapters, and the first chapter has four sections. The first section is dedicated to proving God's existence.
3
Learn the philosophers' method
The first method is from the philosophers: they argue from the undeniable existence of something existent to the necessity of a First Cause.
This is a cosmological argument.
4
Learn the theologians' method
The second method is from the theologians: they argue from the temporal origination of the world (every event has a cause) to a Creator.
This is the kalam cosmological argument.

Commentary
This passage is the introduction to Volume Four of Sharh al-Maqasid. It begins with the praise of God and blessing upon the Prophet Muhammad, emphasizing that he brought pure monotheism—the same religion prescribed to earlier prophets. The author then expresses gratitude for completing this volume. He explains that Volume Four contains the fifth objective (al-maqsad al-khamis) on divine matters (al-ilahiyyat), which is the most important part of the entire work. The preceding four objectives are merely preparatory for the discussion of God's essence and attributes. The author divided this objective into seven chapters, which include many sub-investigations and comments. The first chapter contains four sections. The first section is devoted to proving the existence of God Almighty, and it presents two methods: the method of the philosophers (al-hukama'), which starts from the indubitable existence of something existent; and the method of the theologians (al-mutakallimun), which argues from the temporal origination of the world, since there is no doubt about the existence of temporal events, and every temporal event necessarily has an originator. The second section is about inferring the existence of the Maker from the world of bodies, and it only begins here.

Misconception & Correction
Misconception: Some may think the philosophers' method is purely rational without any empirical basis.
Correction: The text shows it starts from the certainty of existence, which is an undeniable empirical observation.
