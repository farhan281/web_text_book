The Works of Imam al-Razi
Lists twenty-five works authored by Fakhr al-Din al-Razi.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will appreciate the breadth and depth of Imam al-Razi's scholarly output across multiple Islamic sciences as reflected in his 25 listed works.

Core Idea
Imam al-Razi wrote on tafsir, kalam, fiqh, logic, and more—a true polymath.

Before studying the theological arguments in al-Arba'in, it is valuable to understand the colossal intellectual legacy of its author. The following list of 25 works demonstrates that Fakhr al-Din al-Razi was not only a theologian but a master of many disciplines. This bibliography serves as a gateway to appreciating his encyclopedic scholarship.

Guided Path
1
Read the list in Arabic and English
Go through the list once in Arabic and then in translation to get familiar with the titles. Notice the use of Arabic-Indic numerals and the structure.
Some numbers are written in a non-standard way (e.g., 'ه' for 5).
2
Identify the fields of study
For each title, guess its subject based on keywords like 'tafsir', 'usul', 'kalam'. This reveals the range of al-Razi's expertise.
For example, 'تفسير القرآن الكريم' clearly belongs to Quranic exegesis.
3
Locate the present work
Find entry number 16: 'كتاب الأربعين النووية في أصول الدين'. This is the work you are about to study.
Notice that it is placed among other theological works.
4
Reflect on the author's motivation
Consider why a prolific scholar like al-Razi would write a concise work on the principles of religion. This list shows he was capable of both extensive and summary works.
This helps contextualize the purpose of al-Arba'in.
5
Connect the list to your learning journey
Recognize that this bibliography sets the stage: you are studying one of his theological masterpieces, and knowing his other writings enriches your understanding.

Commentary
This passage is a bibliography listing twenty-five works authored by Imam Fakhr al-Din al-Razi. It appears as a front-matter item before the main text. The list covers a wide range of disciplines: Quranic interpretation (e.g., 'Mafatih al-Ghayb'), theology (e.g., 'The Book of Forty'), logic (e.g., 'The Method in Dialectics'), jurisprudence (e.g., 'The Harvest in the Science of the Principles of Jurisprudence'), polemics (e.g., 'The Clear Proof and Demonstration in Refuting the People of Deviation and Transgression'), and biography (e.g., 'The Merits of Imam al-Shafi'i'). Notably, entry 16 is titled 'Kitab al-Arba'in al-Nawawiyya fi Usul al-Din' which may be a variant name for the present work or a different composition. The list demonstrates the encyclopedic nature of Razi's scholarship.
