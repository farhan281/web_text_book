Title Page and Transmission Chain of Tabyin Kidhb al-Muftari
Identifies book title, author, and chain of transmission.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the structure and significance of the title page and transmission chain of Tabyin Kidhb al-Muftari.

Core Idea
The isnad is the gold chain of scholarly authority in Islamic tradition.

This lesson introduces the opening of Ibn Asakir's famous work. The title page not only names the book and author but also includes a chain of transmission (isnad) that traces how the book reached later readers. Understanding this chain is key to appreciating how Islamic scholarship preserved and verified texts.

Guided Path
1
Read the passage and translation
Read the Arabic passage once, then read the literal translation. Notice the structure: title, author, death date, then isnad.
The isnad includes the author's son and later narrators.
2
Identify the parts
Break the passage into its components: book title, author’s honorifics and name, death date, first narrator (son), second narrator, and final hearer.
The word 'رواية' indicates narration; 'سماع' indicates hearing.
3
Reflect on the isnad's purpose
Ask yourself why the author included this chain. In traditional Islamic learning, an isnad establishes the reliability of transmission.
This is not just a header; it is a scholarly credential.

Commentary
This passage is a title page and transmission chain (isnad). It begins with the full title of the book: 'Tabyin Kidhb al-Muftari fima Nusiba ila al-Imam Abi al-Hasan al-Ash'ari' (Exposing the Falsehood of the Slanderer concerning what was attributed to Imam Abi al-Hasan al-Ash'ari). It then identifies the author, Ibn Asakir, with honorifics: 'Supporter of the Sunnah, Proof of the Hadith Masters, Historian of Syria.' His death date is given as 571 AH. Next, the chain of transmission is provided: Ibn Asakir's son, the hafiz Abu Muhammad al-Qasim, narrates the book from his father. Then another narrator, Shaykh Nasih al-Din Faraj al-Habashi, narrates from al-Qasim. Finally, Abd Allah al-Jaza'iri heard (sama') the book from al-Habashi. This chain establishes the scholarly pedigree and authenticity of the work in the traditional Islamic manner.

Praising God for Guiding the People of Verification
Ibn Asakir opens with hamd, shahada, and blessings on the Prophet.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand how Ibn Asakir opens his book by praising God for granting insights to the people of tawhid and for sending the Prophet Muhammad.

Core Idea
The true scholars are those whom God has granted insight and understanding.

Ibn Asakir begins his monumental work with a khutbah that establishes the theological foundation for refuting falsehood. In this opening, he extols God who grants the people of verification (tahqīq) deep insights into tawhid, and he sends blessings upon the Prophet through whom guidance was perfected. This lesson unpacks the opening praises and what they reveal about the author's approach.

Guided Path
1
Read the opening hamd
Read the Arabic passage from the basmala to the blessings on the Prophet. Pay attention to the key verb 'manaha' (granted) and what God grants.
The phrase 'ahl al-tahqīq' is central.
2
Identify God's attributes
In the shahada, a list of divine attributes is given. Note each one and consider what it tells us about God's nature.
3
Reflect on the Prophet's role
The blessings on the Prophet highlight his mission to eradicate idols and to bring forgiveness. Discuss how this sets the stage for the book's purpose.
The phrase 'محق به أوثانا وأصناما' is key.

The Purpose of the Book: Scholars as Guides
Ibn Asakir states the purpose: scholars clarify truth when falsehood spreads.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the purpose of Ibn Asakir's book as stated in the khutbah: to defend the truth through the insights of scholars.

Core Idea
God completed His religion and appointed scholars to guide people against falsehood.

After the opening praises, Ibn Asakir transitions with 'Amma ba'd' to state the purpose of his work. He explains that God perfected the religion and raised scholars with insights to clarify when doubts and falsehoods spread. This lesson focuses on the mission of scholars and the context for the book's refutation of lies.

Guided Path
1
Read the 'Amma ba'd' section
Read the Arabic from 'أما بعد' to the end. Notice the shift in tone from praise to purpose.

2
Identify the role of prophets
Ibn Asakir states that God chose prophets and made them guides, especially Muhammad as the seal. Understand how this establishes the need for following revelation.
3
Understand the scholars' task
The passage concludes that God gave scholars insights so they could enlighten people when falsehoods arise. This is the direct purpose of the book.
The phrase 'وآتاهم بصائر نافذة عند الشبهات' is key.

The shaykh Abu Abdullah Muhammad ibn al-Fadl al-Faqih informed us: The teacher Abu al-Qasim al-Qushayri, may God be pleased with him, said: Praise be to God, who is magnificent in His trial, abundant in His giving, just in His decree, who honors His friends, takes vengeance on His enemies, supports His religion by clarifying the truth and making it clear, who destroys falsehood and its people, uproots falsehood from its root, exposes innovations through the tongues of scholars, unveils doubts through the explanation of the wise, delays the misguided for a time but does not neglect them, and rewards each tomorrow according to his deeds. We praise Him for what He has taught us of His oneness, and we seek His success in carrying out His commands, and we seek His protection from error, nonsense, deviation, and stumbling in speech and deed. We ask Him to bless our master the Chosen One, and his family, the lamps of darkness, and his companions, the leaders of mankind. This is a story we have named 'The Complaint of the People of the Sunna through the Narration of What Befell Them of the Trial.' It informs of the concealed sorrow of a distressed one and the gasp of a vanquished one, an explanation of a painful calamity, a mention of a serious matter causing confusion, a clarification of a tremendous tragedy and an evil that has come to the hearts, wounding.

Commentary
This passage is the beginning of al-Qushayri's treatise, transmitted through a chain of narration. He begins with a formal praise of God, highlighting His attributes related to justice, generosity, support of truth, and destruction of falsehood. He notes that God exposes innovations through the scholars and unveils doubts through the wise. He then offers praise for the knowledge of tawhid (divine oneness) and asks for success in observing God's limits. He seeks protection from error and deviation. After this, he sends blessings upon the Prophet, his family, and his companions. Then he gives the title of his work: 'Shikayat Ahl al-Sunna bi-Hikayat ma Nalahum min al-Mihna' (The Complaint of the People of the Sunna through the Narration of What Befell Them of the Trial). He describes it as a story that reveals the hidden grief of a distressed person, the sigh of a vanquished one, and an explanation of a painful event that wounds hearts. This sets the tone: the treatise is both a lament and a documented plea.

Introduction and Front Matter
beginner
4 min
The Purpose of the Treatise: A Complaint of the People of the Sunna
Al-Qushayri states the title and purpose of the treatise as a complaint.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the specific purpose and tone of al-Qushayri's treatise as a complaint and a narration of the trial faced by Ahl al-Sunna.

Core Idea
The treatise is both a lament and a documented plea for justice for the people of the Sunna.

After the opening praise, al-Qushayri introduces the core of his work. He gives it a title that clearly states its purpose: 'The Complaint of the People of the Sunna through the Narration of What Befell Them of the Trial.' He then elaborates on the nature of this complaint, describing it as an expression of hidden grief and a clarification of a painful calamity. This lesson explores the rhetorical and emotional dimension of the treatise.

Guided Path
1
Read the title and description
Read the Arabic passage starting from 'هذه قصة' to the end. Notice the title and the emotive phrases.
2
Analyze the emotional language
Identify words like 'بثة', 'نفئة', 'ملم مؤلم', 'خطب فادح'. Understand how they convey grief.
The word 'نفئة' is likely 'نفثة' meaning a puff of breath, indicating a sigh.
3
Ponder the purpose
Why do you think al-Qushayri chose to call this a 'shikaya'? What does that imply about his intention?
It is both a complaint to God and a plea to the community.
