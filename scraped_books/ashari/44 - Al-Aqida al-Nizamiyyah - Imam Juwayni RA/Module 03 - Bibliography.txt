Al-Juwayni's Death and Burial
Records al-Juwayni's death and burial details.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will know the precise date and circumstances of Imam al-Haramayn al-Juwayni's death and burial.

Core Idea
Even scholars die; al-Juwayni's death is recorded with exactness.

This lesson covers the final days of Imam al-Haramayn al-Juwayni. The passage provides specific details about when he died, where he was buried, and the state of his health in his last days. This historical record shows the care with which scholars' biographies are preserved.

Guided Path
1
Read the death notice
Read the Arabic text and translation of al-Juwayni's death. Notice the precise wording: his health declined, he died on a specific night, and was buried in a named location.
The date is given in both Hijri and Gregorian calendars.
2
Understand the date format
The death date: 25th Rabi' al-Akhir 478 AH, which is August 25, 1085 CE. Note that the night of Wednesday after 'isha prayer is specified.
Islamic days begin at sunset, so the night of Wednesday means the night after Tuesday.
3
Reflect on the significance
Consider why such details are recorded. In Islamic tradition, knowing the death dates of scholars is important for historical accuracy and for praying for them.

The health of 'Abd al-Malik, Imam al-Haramayn, declined in his final days. He died on the night of Wednesday after the 'isha prayer, on the 25th of Rabi' al-Akhir of the year 478 AH, corresponding to 25th of August 1085 CE, and was buried in the city of Bishtanqan. Books of Imam al-Haramayn: 1. Al-Burhan fi Usul al-Fiqh (manuscript). 2. Al-Irshad fi Usul al-Fiqh (manuscript). 3. Al-Mujtahidin [maybe 'al-Mujtahidun'?] (manuscript). 4. Al-Waraqat (printed). 5. Mughith al-Khalq fi Ikhtiyar al-Ahaqq (manuscript). 6. Al-Irshad ila Qawati' al-Adilla fi Usul al-I'tiqad (printed; edited by Dr. Muhammad Yusuf Musa and Sayyid Ali Abd al-Mun'im Abd al-Hamid in 1950 CE; also by the Orientalist Luciani with a French translation in 1938 in Paris). 7. Risala fi Usul al-Din (manuscript). 8. Al-Shamil fi Usul al-Din; the Orientalist Kluyver published a part of this book. 9. Ghayath al-Umam (Ghiyath al-Umam), Liyath al-Zulam (manuscript). 10. Shifa' al-Ghalil fi Bayan ma Waqa' fi al-Tawrat wa al-Injil min al-Tabdil.

Commentary
This passage first reports the death of Imam al-Haramayn al-Juwayni. It gives precise details: his health declined in his last days, and he died on Wednesday night after 'isha prayer on 25th Rabi' al-Akhir 478 AH (corresponding to August 25, 1085 CE), and was buried in Bishtanqan. Then follows a list of his major works, compiled as 'Books of Imam al-Haramayn'. The list includes ten works, many of which are in usul al-fiqh and usul al-din (kalam). Notable among them are 'Al-Burhan' and 'Al-Irshad' in usul al-fiqh, 'Al-Waraqat' (a well-known primer), 'Al-Irshad ila Qawati' al-Adilla' (a major work in Ash'ari kalam), 'Al-Shamil fi Usul al-Din', and others. The list also notes which are printed and which are still in manuscript, and gives details of critical editions for some. This enumeration provides a bibliography of al-Juwayni's scholarly output.

Major Works of Al-Juwayni
Enumerates al-Juwayni's major works and their publication status.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will become familiar with the major works of Imam al-Haramayn al-Juwayni, especially in usul al-fiqh and usul al-din, and their publication status.

Core Idea
Al-Juwayni's writings shaped Ash'ari theology and usul al-fiqh for centuries.

This lesson lists the ten works of Imam al-Haramayn al-Juwayni as recorded by his biographers. The list includes foundational texts in Islamic legal theory and theology, many of which are still studied today. The passage also indicates which works are printed and which remain in manuscript form, showing the ongoing scholarly interest in his legacy.

Guided Path
1
Read the list of works
Read the Arabic list and its translation. Notice the numbering and the titles. Each work is followed by either (مخطوط) for manuscript or (مطبوع) for printed.
Some entries have additional notes about editions.
2
Identify the major works
Focus on the most famous ones: Al-Burhan, Al-Irshad fi Usul al-Fiqh, Al-Waraqat, Al-Irshad ila Qawati' al-Adilla, Al-Shamil. These are key texts in their fields.
Al-Waraqat is a popular primer on usul al-fiqh.
3
Consider the range of subjects
Notice that al-Juwayni wrote on usul al-fiqh, usul al-din (kalam), polemics (against Judaism/Christianity), and governance (Ghiyath al-Umam). This shows his breadth.
