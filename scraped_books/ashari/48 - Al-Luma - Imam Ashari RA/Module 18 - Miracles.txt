Transformation to Ruhaniyya and Karamat
Enumeration of states leading to ruhaniyya and supernatural gifts.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the spiritual transformation of the servant into a state of ruhaniyya and the limited supernatural abilities (karamat) that accompany it.

Core Idea
Through spiritual transformation, the servant gains limited knowledge of the unseen and other karamat.

Imam al-Ash‘ari outlines the Ahl al-Sunna belief in the spiritual journey of the righteous servant. This passage describes how the servant is elevated through states until attaining a spiritual nature, with accompanying supernatural gifts. Note that these gifts are partial and do not imply divinity.

Guided Path
1
Read the passage and translation
Read the Arabic passage once without analyzing. Then read the literal translation to grasp the plain meaning.
The bracketed clarification 'ay ba‘duh' is editorial.
2
Identify the key transformation
Notice that the servant is 'transferred through states' until reaching a 'spiritual nature' (ruhaniyya). This is the foundational concept.
3
List the supernatural gifts
The passage enumerates four specific karamat: knowledge of some unseen, earth folding, walking on water, invisibility. Identify each.
4
Note the clarification about the unseen
The insertion 'some of it' emphasizes that the knowledge is partial. This is a key doctrinal nuance.
5
Reflect on the significance
Consider how this belief distinguishes Sunni theology from extremes that deny or deify saintly powers.

Commentary
This passage describes the spiritual journey of the servant ('abd) through various states (ahwal) until attaining a state of ruhaniyya (spiritual or angelic quality). At that stage, the servant is granted powers: knowledge of some hidden things (ghayb), the ability to traverse great distances quickly (tay al-ard), walking on water, and becoming invisible (ghayba 'an al-absar). These are classic Sufi accounts of karamat. The insertion 'i.e., some of it' clarifies that the knowledge of the unseen is partial, not absolute.

Key Takeaway
The Sunni belief acknowledges that righteous servants can attain spiritual states and perform karamat, but these are limited and do not rival divine attributes.

Transference to Spiritual States and Miraculous Gifts
Describes the servant's transference through states leading to spirituality and powers.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the belief that a righteous servant progresses through spiritual states and may be granted miraculous abilities as a sign of piety.

Core Idea
Spiritual growth leads to extraordinary gifts from Allah.

This passage from Imam al-Ash'ari's al-Luma' outlines a key Sunni belief about the spiritual journey: the servant transitions through various states (ahwal) until reaching a state of ruhaniyya (spirituality). At this stage, he may be blessed with miraculous powers like knowledge of the unseen, folding of the earth, walking on water, and invisibility. These are not limited to prophets but can be granted to saints as karamat.

Guided Path
1
Read the passage carefully
Read the Arabic passage once without trying to analyse it. Then read the translation. Let the author's own rhythm come through before you interpret.
The passage is a single sentence but dense in meaning.
2
Identify the progression
Notice the phrase 'transferred through states' – this indicates a gradual spiritual ascent from one state to another.
The states (ahwal) refer to different spiritual stations.
3
List the miraculous abilities
The passage lists four abilities: knowledge of the unseen, folding of the earth, walking on water, and invisibility. Reflect on each as a divine gift.
These are not ordinary human capabilities.
4
Understand the significance
These abilities are signs of spiritual attainment and are considered karamat (miracles of saints). They affirm the saint's closeness to Allah.
This distinguishes Sunni belief from those who deny such gifts.
5
Address the common misunderstanding
Many think such powers belong only to prophets. This passage shows that righteous servants can also receive them as a reward for their faith.
Prophets have miracles (mu'jizat), saints have karamat.

Misconception & Correction
Misconception: Many people think that extraordinary abilities like knowing the unseen or walking on water are exclusive to prophets and cannot be possessed by ordinary believers.
Correction: The passage explicitly states that the servant (the ordinary believer) can attain such powers through spiritual progression, indicating that saints (awliya) can be granted karamat as a sign of their closeness to Allah.

Key Takeaway
The passage teaches that Allah can grant His righteous servants extraordinary abilities as a sign of their spiritual rank.
