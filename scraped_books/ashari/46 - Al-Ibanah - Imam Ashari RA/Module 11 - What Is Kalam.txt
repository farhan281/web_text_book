Al-Ash'ari's Declaration of Creed
Al-Ash'ari declares his creed and methodology.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand that al-Ash'ari's final creed is anchored in revelation and the early Sunni tradition, especially the teachings of Imam Ahmad ibn Hanbal.

Core Idea
Al-Ash'ari's creed is grounded in Quran, Sunnah, and early Sunni consensus.

Al-Ash'ari opens his book al-Ibanah with a clear declaration of his creed. He does not begin with complex theological arguments but with a simple, direct statement of what he believes and what he bases his belief on. This opening sets the tone for the entire work and shows his alignment with traditional Sunni Islam.

Guided Path
1
Read the passage carefully
Read the Arabic and English translation. Notice the structure: 'قولنا الذي نقول به، وديانتنا التي ندين بها: التمسك بكتاب ربنا...'
This is a formal declaration, so every word matters.
2
Identify the sources of creed
The passage lists three sources: the Quran, the Sunnah, and the transmitted teachings of the early generations. Also note the specific mention of Ahmad ibn Hanbal.
3
Reflect on the significance of following a specific imam
Al-Ash'ari explicitly aligns himself with Ahmad ibn Hanbal. This shows his final position is orthodox Sunni, distancing himself from Mu'tazili rationalism.

وقولنا الذي نقول به ، وديانتنا التي ندين بها : التمسك بكتاب ربنا عز وجل وسنة نبينا عليه السلام ، وما روي عن الصحابة والتابعين وأئمة الحديث ، ونحن بذلك معتصمون ، وبما كان يقول به أبو عبد الله أحمد بن حنبل ـ نضر الله وجهه ورفع درجته وأجزل مثوبته ـ قائلون
And our statement which we say, and our religion which we adhere to: holding fast to the Book of our Lord, Mighty and Majestic, and the Sunnah of our Prophet, peace be upon him, and what has been narrated from the Companions, the Successors, and the imams of hadith; and we are holding fast to that, and we are saying what Abu 'Abdillah Ahmad ibn Hanbal — may Allah brighten his face, raise his rank, and increase his reward — used to say.
Source pages p. 29

Commentary
This passage is al-Ash'ari's declaration of his creed (aqidah) at the beginning of his book al-Ibanah. He starts by affirming that his belief is based on three sources: the Quran, the Sunnah of the Prophet, and the transmitted teachings of the early generations — the Companions, the Successors, and the hadith scholars. He then specifically attaches himself to the position of Imam Ahmad ibn Hanbal, the leading Sunni imam of his time. This shows that al-Ash'ari's final position is a return to orthodox Sunni Islam, rejecting speculative theology (kalam) that deviates from revelation. The invocation for Ahmad ibn Hanbal indicates al-Ash'ari's respect and alignment with him.

The Positive Creed of Ahl al-Sunna: Method and Summary
Al-Ash'ari answers the question about his own creed by stating adherence to Quran, Sunnah, and Imam Ahmad.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand how al-Ash'ari defines the creed of Ahl al-Sunna by prioritizing adherence to Quran, Sunnah, and the tradition of the early Imams, especially Imam Ahmad, and the core beliefs that follow from that.

Core Idea
The creed of Ahl al-Sunna is defined by uncompromising adherence to divine revelation and the way of the early Imams.

Al-Ash'ari has just refuted several sects. Now an objector demands: what is your positive creed? In this passage, he answers by first defining his method: holding fast to the Quran, Sunnah, and the transmitted legacy of the Companions and Imams, particularly Imam Ahmad ibn Hanbal. Then he summarizes the core beliefs that this method yields.

Guided Path
1
Read the objection and answer
Read the Arabic passage once, then the translation. Notice the questioner's request for a positive statement of belief after hearing the refutations.
The objection sets up the need for a clear, positive definition.
2
Identify the three sources of authority
In the answer, find the three sources al-Ash'ari mentions: the Book of Allah, the Sunnah of the Prophet, and the transmitted reports from Companions, Followers, and Imams of hadith.
This establishes the traditionalist method.
3
Recognize the special status of Imam Ahmad
Al-Ash'ari explicitly aligns himself with Imam Ahmad and praises him for clarifying truth and suppressing innovation. Understand this as a declaration of methodological loyalty.
Imam Ahmad is presented as the model Imam.
4
Identify the summary of belief
The passage ends with a concise summary (jumla) affirming Allah, angels, books, messengers, and all authentic revelation. Note that this summary is universal but specified by the method.
This is the core creed that the method produces.
5
Reflect on the contrast with the sects
Compare this positive statement with the earlier refuted sects. The method avoids selectivity and rationalist reinterpretation.

الباب الثاني في إِبَانَةِ قَوْلِ أَهْلِ الحَقِ وَالسُّنَّةِ
فإن قال لنا قائل : قد أنكرتم قول المعتزلة والقدرية والجهمية والحرورية والرافضة والمرجئة، فعرفونا قولكم الذي به تقولون، وديانتكم التي بها تدينون .
قيل له قولنا الذي نقول به وديانتنا التي ندين بها : التمسك بكتاب الله ربنا عز وجل، وبسنة نبينا ، وما روي عن السادة الصحابة والتابعين وأئمة الحديث، ونحن بذلك معتصمون، وبما كان يقول به أبــو عبد الله أحمد بن محمد بن حنبل نضر الله وجهه، ورفع درجته، وأجـزل مثوبته، قائلون، ولمن خالف قوله مجانبون؛ لأنه الإمام الفاضل، والرئيس الكامل، الذي أبان الله به الحق عند ظهور الضلال، وأوضح به المنهاج، وقمع به بدع المبتدعين، وزيغ الزائغين، وشك الشاكين، فرحمة الله عليه مــن إمام مقدَّم، وجليل معظم، وكبير مفخّم، وعلى جميع أئمة المسلمين .
وجملة قولنا : إِنَّا نُقرُّ بالله وملائكته وكتبه ورسله، وبما جاء من عند الله ، وما رواه الثقات عن رسول الله ، لا نرد من ذلك شيئاً
The second chapter: On clarifying the statement of the people of truth and Sunnah.
For if one should say to us: 'You have rejected the statement of the Mu'tazila, Qadariyya, Jahmiyya, Haruriyya, Rafida, and Murji'a, so inform us of your statement that you hold, and your religion that you practice.'
It is said to him: Our statement that we hold and our religion that we practice is: adherence to the Book of Allah, our Lord, Mighty and Majestic, and to the Sunnah of our Prophet, and what has been transmitted from the noble Companions, the Followers, and the Imams of hadith. And we hold fast to that, and we are followers of what Abu Abdullah Ahmad ibn Muhammad ibn Hanbal (may Allah brighten his face, raise his rank, and multiply his reward) used to say, and we oppose anyone who opposes his statement, because he is the virtuous Imam, the complete leader, by whom Allah manifested the truth at the emergence of error, clarified the path, and suppressed the innovations of the innovators, the deviation of the deviators, and the doubt of the doubters. May Allah's mercy be upon him as a foremost Imam, a great noble, and a magnificent elder, and upon all the Imams of the Muslims.
And the summary of our statement is: We affirm Allah, His angels, His books, His messengers, and what has come from Allah, and what the reliable narrators have transmitted from the Messenger of Allah — we do not reject any of that.
Source pages p. 43

Commentary
This passage is an objection-and-answer unit that defines the creed of Ahl al-Sunna. The objector, after hearing the author's rejection of various sects, asks for a positive statement of belief. The answer begins by affirming adherence to three sources: the Quran, the Sunnah of the Prophet, and the transmitted reports from the Companions, Followers, and hadith Imams. This establishes traditionalism as the foundation. Then the author explicitly aligns himself with Imam Ahmad ibn Hanbal, praising him as the one through whom Allah clarified truth and suppressed innovation. This is significant because al-Ash'ari himself later developed a more rationalist kalam methodology, but here he emphasizes loyalty to Ahmad. Finally, a concise summary (jumla) affirms the core Islamic beliefs in Allah, angels, books, messengers, and all authentic revelation, without rejection. The passage thereby contrasts with the sects by avoiding any selective acceptance or rationalist reinterpretation.

Misconception & Correction

Correction: Al-Ash'ari's commitment to the Salaf's method does not preclude his later development of kalam; it shows his foundational loyalty to revelation and the early Imams.

Key Takeaway
Al-Ash'ari defines Ahl al-Sunna by their method: holding fast to revelation and the transmitted way of the early Imams, especially Ahmad, leading to the core belief in all that Allah and His Messenger have brought.

?
