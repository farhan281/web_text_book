Imam al-Baqillani's Polemical Works: A Bibliographic Survey
Enumeration of several of Baqillani's known books against heretical groups.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be familiar with several of Imam al-Baqillani's key works refuting heretical sects and understand their significance in Sunni polemics.

Core Idea
Baqillani's books were weapons of the pen against heresy.

This passage lists some of Imam al-Baqillani's most important refutations of deviant sects. Although it reads as a bibliography, each title reveals a front in the battle for Sunni orthodoxy. Let's examine the list and see what these books tell us about Baqillani's role as a defender of faith.

Guided Path
1
Read the list of books
Read through the Arabic passage and its translation. Notice the names of the books and the sects they target. The list includes works like 'Kashf al-Astar' against the Batiniyya.
Some parts are unclear due to OCR issues; focus on the main titles.
2
Identify the targeted heresies
Each book refutes a specific group: the Batiniyya, the Jahmiyya (implied by 'Ahl al-Jahd'), and possibly others. Note how Baqillani's polemical output covers multiple fronts.
The Batiniyya are the most explicitly named heretical group.
3
Observe the scholarly chain
The author cites later scholars (al-Isfarayini, al-Subki, Ibn Kathir) who referenced Baqillani's works. This shows the lasting impact of his writings.
4
Reflect on the significance
Consider that bibliographic lists like this are not dry records; they demonstrate the active intellectual defense of Sunni orthodoxy. Baqillani's books were tools for theological combat.
The common misunderstanding is that this is merely a list; instead, it is a statement of scholarly jihad.

Commentary
This passage is an enumeration of several works authored by Imam al-Baqillani, primarily focused on refuting heretical groups. The author begins by stating that he will list the names of the books, some of which are mentioned by Baqillani himself in the present book. The list includes: 1) 'Kitab Kayfiyyat al-Istishhad fi al-Radd ala Ahl al-Jahd wal-Inad' (a book on the method of citing evidence against the people of denial and obstinacy). 2) 'Kitab al-Usul al-Kabir' in fiqh, with references from later scholars. 3) 'Kitab Manaqib al-A'imma' or 'al-Imama al-Kabir', a large work on imamate, which the author says is comprehensive and covers events among the Companions, with a reference to Hajji Khalifa. 4) 'Kitab al-Kuffar al-Muta'awwilin' (the book on the unbelievers who interpret [the texts incorrectly]), dealing with issues of deposing an imam. 5) 'Kitab Kashf al-Astar fi al-Radd ala al-Batiniyya' (the book on unveiling the coverings in refutation of the Batiniyya). The passage then provides external citations from al-Subki and Ibn Kathir, noting Baqillani's work against the Batiniyya and its importance. The passage ends with a mention of Ibn Kathir's praise and a page reference that is unclear. The list demonstrates Baqillani's extensive polemical output against various sects including the Batiniyya, Jahmiyya, etc.
