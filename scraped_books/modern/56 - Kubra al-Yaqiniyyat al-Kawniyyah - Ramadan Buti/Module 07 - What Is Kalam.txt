The true religion is one, not multiple. As for what developed and changed with the missions of the prophets, it is legislation only.
Source pages p. 384

Commentary
This passage defines the components of the Islamic structure: creed (aqīda), worship (ibāda), and legislation (tashrīʿ). It states that creed is the pillar of all, and hence religion can be called creed alone. The content of creed has remained unchanged from Adam to Muhammad, and Islam is its eternal name. True religion is one, not multiple; only legislation evolved with different prophets.

Misconception & Correction
Misconception: Islam is a new religion that replaced previous ones.
Correction: The passage states that creed has not changed from Adam to Muhammad; Islam is the ancient name for this one true creed. Only legislation varied.

Key Takeaway

How This Lesson Applies — to MY Life?
?
The passage says creed is the pillar of the Islamic structure. Examine your own life: Is your belief (creed) truly the pillar that supports your worship and your following of Islamic law? Or have you made your worship or 'religious practices' more foundational than your core beliefs? What one thing can you change today to ensure your creed is the center?
?
Creed has not changed since Adam, yet you live in an age where many ideas about God contradict that. What are you personally doing to safeguard this unchanging creed in your own heart, especially when modern ideologies try to redefine God's attributes?
?
If only legislation changes, why do you often focus on differences in fiqh while neglecting the unity of creed? How can you shift your attention and that of your community to strengthen the common core beliefs first?
