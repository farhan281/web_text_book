Understanding the Editor's Methodology
The editor describes his editorial methodology and changes made in this edition.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the editorial decisions behind this edition of al-Bahja al-Saniyya and how to identify corrections in the text.

Core Idea
Understanding the editor's method helps you read the text critically.

This preface explains the editorial process used in preparing this edition. Knowing which changes were made and why will help you appreciate the reliability of the text and engage with it more intelligently. The editor mentions corrections of errors, filling in omissions, and improvements in layout.

Guided Path

The editor distinguishes between errors (أخطاء) and omissions (سقط). Note how he marks each.
Errors are noted in the margin; omissions are added in brackets.
3

4

The editor prays for acceptance. Consider the scholarly care in producing a reliable text.

Al-Bahja al-Saniyya. And I relied in this my print on an old edition printed in Indonesia; they had errors and some omissions which I corrected here. I was aided in correction by the grace of Allah the Exalted and my study of 'Sharh al-Kharida' by my master Abu al-Barakat Ahmad al-Dardir, may Allah be pleased with him. As for the errors, I drew attention to the most important of them in their places, mentioning the correct version in the original, and [the erroneous] footnoted in the margin. As for the omissions, I added them in the original between brackets like this [], and I benefited from 'Sharh al-Kharida' by my master Ahmad al-Dardir, and from reviewing the books upon which the commentator, the mercy of Allah, relied. And I presented the work with a brief biography of the shaykh, which I borrowed from what his student and graduate, His Excellency Professor Dr. ‘Abd al-Wahhab Ibrahim Abu Sulayman, may Allah preserve him, a member of the Council of Senior Scholars in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia, wrote. Among the changes I made in this edition over its predecessor is that I placed the verses of al-Kharida at the top of the pages, distinguishing what the author explains in bold black type. upon him. And I made brief comments, which I benefited from 'Sharh al-Kharida al-Bahiyya' and from the glosses. And in conclusion, I ask Allah the Exalted that I have been successful in my work, and I ask Him to make it and all my deeds purely for His noble face, and to grant me beneficial knowledge, good and ample provision. Indeed, He is powerful over that and worthy of response. O Allah, bless our master Muhammad and his family and companions and grant them peace.

Commentary
This passage is the editor's preface describing the methodology used in preparing this edition. The editor states that he relied on an old Indonesian edition, corrected its errors and supplied omissions. He added missing text in brackets, taken from al-Dardir's own commentary. He included a biographical sketch and changed the layout to show the poem verses at the top with explanations in bold. He also added brief marginal notes. The passage concludes with a prayer for divine acceptance.
