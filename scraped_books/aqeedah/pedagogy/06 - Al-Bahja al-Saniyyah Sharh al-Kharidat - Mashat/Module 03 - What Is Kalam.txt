What Is the Science of Tawhid? Its Subject and Scope
The author defines the science of tawhid and states its subject matter.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the definition of the science of tawhid and its subject matter: the three rational judgments regarding Allah and His messengers.

Core Idea
Tawhid is the most noble science because it teaches us what is necessary, impossible, and possible regarding Allah and His prophets.

In this opening passage, the author establishes the high status of the science of tawhid and gives a clear definition of its scope. Before diving into detailed arguments, the student learns that this science is about knowing the three rational categories—necessity, impossibility, and possibility—as they apply to Allah and His messengers. This lesson lays the foundation for all subsequent theological discussion.

Guided Path
1
Read the opening invocation and statement
The author begins with praise and then declares the subject. Read the Arabic and translation to get the flow.
2
Identify the three rational categories
The passage lists 'what is necessary, what is impossible, and what is possible' for both Allah and the prophets. Notice that these are the same categories.
These three categories are the basic tools of kalam.
3
Understand the significance
Why is this science the noblest? Because its subject is Allah and His messengers. Reflect on how this gives tawhid its high rank.

Definition of Aqidah and Description of the Poem
Defines 'aqidah, explains the name 'al-Kharidah al-Bahiyyah', describes its attributes and contents.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the meaning of 'aqidah, the significance of the poem's name, and the three categories of divine attributes it covers.

Core Idea
A small poem contains the entire framework of Islamic creed.

This passage begins by defining what a creed is, then introduces the poet's own work, explaining its name and describing its unique qualities. Despite being only seventy-one verses, the poem covers the essential categories of belief about Allah.

Guided Path
1
Read the passage carefully
Read the Arabic passage once, then follow the translation. Notice the definition of aqidah comes first.
The passage is short but packed with meaning.
2
Identify the definition of aqidah
The text defines aqidah as 'the believed proposition'. Note the example given: every perfection obligatory for Allah must be true.
This is a technical kalam definition.
3
Explore the name of the poem
The poet names his work 'al-Kharidah al-Bahiyyah'. Understand the etymology: kharidah = unpierced pearl, bahiyyah = radiant.
The name implies beauty and value.
4
Note the attributes latifah and saghirah
The poem is described as 'latifah' (delicate/small) and 'saghirah fi al-hajm' (small in size). But it contains great knowledge.
Do not mistake small size for insignificance.
5
Summarize the contents
The poem covers three categories: what is obligatory for Allah, what is permissible, and what is impossible. This is the classic kalam framework.
Memorize these three categories.
