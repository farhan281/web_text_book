Sanusi's Works in Fiqh, Tasawwuf, and Related Fields
Al-Mallali's catalogue of Sanusi's works in fiqh, inheritance, tasawwuf, sira, and related topics.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will appreciate the breadth of Imam Sanusi's scholarship across Maliki fiqh, inheritance law, ethics, prophetic biography, and tasawwuf.

Core Idea
Sanusi's pen touched every major Islamic science, not just kalam.

This passage from al-Mallali's biographical catalogue lists several of Sanusi's works in various disciplines. It provides a window into his scholarly output, showing that his expertise extended well beyond the rational theology for which he is most famous. As you read, notice the variety of subjects and the notes on completion.

Guided Path
1
Read the list of works
Read through the Arabic passage and translation. Note the titles and their fields.
2
Identify the fields covered
Categorize each work by its subject: fiqh, inheritance, tasawwuf, sira, etc.
3
Observe the completion notes
Al-Mallali often notes whether Sanusi completed each work. Pay attention to his phrases.
Some works are incomplete.
4
Reflect on scholarly breadth
Consider how this list challenges the stereotype of Sanusi as only a kalam theologian.

Commentary
This passage is a biographical catalogue listing the works of Imam Sanusi as recorded by Sheikh al-Mallali and others. It begins with a fragment about a commentary (possibly on an unspecified work), then proceeds to list specific titles with notes on their completion. The list includes: 1) A commentary on 'al-Wughlīsiyya' in Maliki fiqh, of which al-Mallali says Sanusi explained only a small part and did not complete it. 2) A poem on inheritance (fara'id) whose opening lines are given; al-Mallali notes he saw it in Sanusi's handwriting from his youth and does not know if he completed it. 3) A summary of 'Ri'ayat al-Muhasibi' in ethics and tasawwuf. 4) A summary of 'al-Rawd al-Unf' in prophetic biography, left incomplete. 5) A summary of 'Bughyat al-Salik' in tasawwuf, original by al-Sahili al-Mu'ammam. 6) A commentary on verses by Imam al-Ilbiri in tasawwuf, with opening line given. 7) A commentary on the same verses 'on the method of the people of realities'. 8) A commentary on three verses by a gnostic in tasawwuf, with opening line. 9) A commentary on two verses by a gnostic, with opening line. 10) A commentary on 'al-Aqida al-Murshida', which al-Mallali saw complete in Sanusi's handwriting. 11) 'Al-Durr al-Manzum' a commentary on Ibn Ajrum's grammar rules (al-Ajurrumiyya), seen complete by al-Mallali. 12) A commentary on 'Jawāhir al-‘Ulūm' in kalam, original by al-Iji, following al-Baydawi's method, of such difficulty that Sanusi remarked al-Baydawi's book is but a drop in its ocean. The passage thus documents Sanusi's scholarly output across fiqh, inheritance, ethics, sira, tasawwuf, grammar, and kalam, with critical notes on the state of each manuscript.

How This Lesson Applies — to MY Life?
?
Sanusi composed his poem on inheritance in his youth but may not have completed it. What unfinished spiritual or intellectual project are you carrying from your past? Will you complete it, or let it remain a fragment?
?
Al-Mallali records works that Sanusi left incomplete—yet he still achieved great influence. Does your fear of leaving something unfinished hold you back from starting? What would you begin today even if you might not finish it?
?
Sanusi commented on verses of gnostic poets, engaging with deep spiritual insight. When was the last time you allowed poetry or art to move your heart toward Allah? Are you open to spiritual nourishment from unexpected sources?

Sanusi's Works in Grammar and Kalam
Al-Mallali's listing of Sanusi's works in grammar and kalam with completion notes.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will know the titles and completion status of Sanusi's works in Arabic grammar and rational theology (kalam).

Core Idea
Sanusi's grammatical commentary and his difficult kalam work show his mastery of both language and theology.

The final entries in al-Mallali's catalogue highlight Sanusi's contributions to Arabic grammar and the peak of his theological writing. His commentary on Jawāhir al-‘Ulūm was so challenging that he compared al-Baydawi's work to a drop in its ocean.

Guided Path
1
Read the two entries
Read the Arabic and translation for the grammar and kalam works.
2
Note the completion status
Al-Mallali confirms both works were completed and seen in Sanusi's handwriting.
3
Understand the significance of Jawāhir al-‘Ulūm
Consider Sanusi's remark about al-Baydawi; this indicates the depth and difficulty of this commentary.
This work is a major kalam commentary.
