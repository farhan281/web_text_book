The Meaning of 'Wa La al-Hisar' and the Refutation of the Mu'tazilite Transmitted Argument
Explains 'wa la al-hisar' and refutes the Mu'tazilite transmitted objection from 'La tudrikuhu al-absar'.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the distinction between idrak (encompassing perception) and ru'ya (absolute vision) in the Sunni response to the Mu'tazilite argument from the Quranic verse.

Core Idea
The verse only negates encompassing perception, not all vision of Allah.

The Mu'tazila argue that since Allah is not perceived by sight in the Quran, He cannot be seen at all. The Sunni theologian clarifies that the word 'idrak' in the verse refers to a specific kind of vision that involves limitation and enclosure. By understanding this distinction, we see that the verse does not disprove the possibility of seeing Allah in the afterlife.

Guided Path
1

2
Identify the Mu'tazilite argument
Find where the Mu'tazila present their proof from the Quranic verse. Notice they claim idrak equals ru'ya.
3
Understand the Sunni response
The author distinguishes between idrak (specific, encompassing perception) and ru'ya (absolute vision). The negation of the specific does not negate the general.
This is the key logical principle: negation of the more specific does not entail negation of the more general.
4
Reflect on the conclusion
Allah can be seen without modality. Consider why the Mu'tazilite position leads to absurdities like Allah not seeing Himself.

His saying: 'and no enclosure' (wa la al-hisar): that is, no enclosure of the seen with the seer such that he encompasses it, because of the impossibility of limits and boundaries for Allah. The author's purpose with that is to answer the transmitted doubt of the Mu'tazila which they cling to in their saying that vision is impossible, namely His saying 'Vision comprehends Him not' (6:103), for it indicates that Allah is not perceived by sight, and perception is vision, so He is not seen by sight. The summary of the answer: We do not concede that perception by sight is absolute vision; rather it is a specific vision, which is that which is with encompassing such that the seen is confined within limits and boundaries. The conclusion is that Allah is seen without being conditioned by any modality. Some Mu'tazila went to commit to this false doctrine that Allah is not seen just as He is not seen (i.e., by anyone), which is the analogical extension of their doctrine as narrated by al-Allama al-Sanusi in Sharh al-Kubra (p. 206).
