Core Idea
Expiation from avoiding major sins is probable, not guaranteed.

Theologians agree that avoiding major sins leads to expiation of minor sins, but they disagree on the nature of this expiation: is it a certain outcome or merely probable? This brief passage presents the two positions and affirms the correct one. As you read, notice which groups hold each view and why the author sides with the theologians.

Commentary
This passage presents a theological disagreement: while all agree that expiation of sins is contingent on avoiding major sins, they differ on whether this expiation is certain (qat'i) or merely probable (zanni). The first view is held by some jurists, hadith scholars, and the Mu'tazila. The second view, that it is probable, is held by the imams of kalam (theologians), and the commentator affirms this as the correct position.

Misconception & Correction
Misconception: One might think that if a person avoids major sins, their minor sins are automatically and certainly forgiven.
Correction: The correct view is that expiation is probable (zanni), not certain (qat'i). It depends on Allah's mercy and will, not on a guarantee.
