Major Sins Do Not Expel a Believer from Faith
Taftazani argues major sins do not expel from faith, refuting Mu'tazila and Khawarij.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand why major sins do not remove faith in Ahl al-Sunnah, and how this position differs from the Mu'tazila and Khawarij views.

Core Idea
Faith is heartfelt affirmation; major sins don't negate it unless done with contempt.

Taftazani defends the Sunni position that a believer remains a believer even after committing major sins, as long as tasdiq remains. He contrasts this with the Mu'tazila and Khawarij, who either place the sinner in an intermediate state or expel them from faith entirely. This passage clarifies the nature of faith and the conditions under which acts become disbelief.

Guided Path
1
Read the passage and translation
Read through the entire passage once, noting its structure: a statement, refutations, a proof, and an objection-response.
Pay attention to the Arabic phrasing, especially the words for istihlal and takdhib.
2
Identify the two refuted sects
Find where the Mu'tazila and Khawarij positions are mentioned. Understand why each is wrong according to the author.
The Mu'tazila consider actions part of faith; Khawarij consider major sins as disbelief.
3
Analyze the main proof
The author argues that faith is tasdiq, so only its opposite (takdhib) can remove it. See how he explains that mere commission of sin does not contradict tasdiq.
Notice the conditions like fear of punishment and resolve to repent.
4
Examine the exception and the response
The author states that if the sin is done with istihlal or contempt, it becomes kufr. Also, some sins are legally designated as indicators of disbelief. This answers the objection that acts shouldn't cause disbelief.
This shows how the Sunni position balances mercy and legal rulings.

Commentary
This passage is a doctrinal ruling on the permissibility of praying (the obligatory or optional prayers) behind an imam who is either righteous (barr) or sinful (fajir). The author begins by stating the rule: it is permissible. He provides two proofs: first, a hadith of the Prophet (peace be upon him) commanding prayer behind every righteous and sinful person. Misconception & Correction
Misconception: Some may think that all istihlal leads to kufr regardless of evidence, but the author shows it depends on the nature of the prohibition.
Correction: The author distinguishes between haram li-aynihi and li-ghayrihi, and between definitive and speculative proof.

Checkpoint
According to the passage, what is the ruling on wishing that fornication (al-zinā) were not forbidden?
A) It is not kufr.
B) It is kufr.
C) It is fisq.
D) It depends on the person's intention.

Key Takeaway
The ruling on istihlal varies based on the type of prohibition and proof; some wishes are kufr while others are not.
