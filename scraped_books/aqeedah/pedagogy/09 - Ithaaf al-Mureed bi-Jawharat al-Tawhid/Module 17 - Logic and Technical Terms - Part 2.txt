Commentary
This passage presents the Ash'ari definition of the term 'thing' (al-shay') and proves that the non-existent (al-ma'dum) is not a thing. The commentator begins by affirming the Ash'ari position: 'And with us, the thing is the existent (al-mawjud)'. Finally, the commentator asserts that this judgment is known by necessity (bi-al-darura) from reason, because no intermediate state of establishment is conceivable. The passage thus grounds the Ash'ari ontological commitment that only what exists is a real thing—contra the Mu'tazila who consider non-existent 'things' to have some reality.

وعيَّن المُرادَ مِن النبي بإبدال (مُحَمَّدٍ) (وَصَحْبِهِ) ، أي : والصَّلاة والسَّلام على صَحْبِه (وَ) على (عِتْرَتِه ، بالمُثناة فوق ، وهم أهل بيته . ثُمَّ عمَّمَ في الدُّعاء لأفضليته ، فقال (وَتَابعٍ)، أي: والصَّلاةُ والسَّلامُ على كُلِّ مُتَّبِعِ لِنَهْجِهِ) أي: طَريقته صلى الله عليه وسلم وَسُنَّتِه (مِنْ أُمَّتِه) أي مِن جميع إجابته من أهل طاعته إلى يوم القيامة ، وهذا القيد لبيان الواقع؛ لأنَّ المُتَّبع الشريعه الله لا يكون إلا من أمته ، لعموم بعثته . هذا ، والمَرجُو مِن صاحب العقل السليم والخُلُقِ القويم، أنْ يَسْتُرَ هَفَوَاتِي ، ويُقِيلَ عَثَرَاتِي ؛ فإنَّه قلَّ أَنْ يَخْلُصَ مُصنّفٌ مِن الهَفَوَاتِ ، أَو يَنجُو مُؤَلَّفٌ مِن العَتَرَاتِ ، مع عدم تأهلِي لذلكَ ، وقُصورِي عَن الوصول إلى ما هنالك ، متوسلا بصاحب الوسيلة والمقام المحمود أن يجعله يوم الوُرُودِ ، وصلَةٌ لحوضِه المَوْرُودِ ، وأنْ ينفع به كما نفع بأصله ، وأنْ يجعلهُ خالصاً لوجهه ، مُتَفضِلاً بِقَبُولِه ، إِنَّهُ على ما يشاءُ قَدِيرٌ ، وصلى الله على سيدنا محمدٍ وعلى آله وصحبه وتابعيهم إلى يوم الدين. قال مؤلّفه وجامِعُه الفقير الحقير عبد السَّلام بن إبراهيم المالكي اللقاني: فرغتُ مِن جمعِهِ يومَ الخميس المُباركِ لعشرينَ خَلَتْ مِن رمضانَ المُعظَّم قَدْرُهُ مِن شهورِ السَّنة السَّابِعة والأربعين بعد الألف من الهجرة النبوية على صاحبها أفضلُ الصَّلاة وأتم التسليم ، ولا حول ولا قُوَّةً إلَّا بالله العلي العظيم، وهو حسبي ونعم الوكيل ، نِعْمَ المَولى ونعمَ النَّصير ، والحمد لله رب العالمين.
And he specified what is meant by the Prophet by substituting 'Muhammad' and 'his companions', i.e., and prayer and peace upon his companions and upon his family, with the dotted letter, and they are his household. Then he generalized in the supplication for his superiority, and said 'and a follower', i.e., and prayer and peace upon every follower of his path, i.e., his way, peace be upon him, and his sunnah, 'from his community', i.e., from all those who respond to him, from the people of obedience to him until the Day of Resurrection. This restriction is to indicate the reality, because the follower of the Sharia of Allah cannot be except from his community, due to the universality of his mission. This, and what is hoped for from the possessor of sound intellect and upright character is that He cover my slips and accept my apologies for my stumbles; for rarely is an author free from slips, nor an authored work saved from stumbles, along with my lack of qualification for that and my inability to attain what is there, seeking intercession through the possessor of the Wasila and the Praiseworthy Station that He make it on the Day of Approach a connection to his visited Pond, and that He benefit through it as He benefited through its origin, and that He make it purely for His face, graciously accepting it; indeed, He is over what He wills omnipotent. And may Allah bless our master Muhammad and his family and companions and those who follow them until the Day of Judgment. Its author and compiler, the poor and humble Abd al-Salam ibn Ibrahim al-Maliki al-Laqani, said: I finished compiling it on the blessed Thursday, the twentieth of the great Ramadan, whose esteem is great, of the months of the year one thousand and forty-seven after the Hijra of the Prophet, may the best prayer and most complete peace be upon him. And there is no power or strength except by Allah, the Most High, the Supreme. And He is my sufficiency and the best trustee, the best Master and the best Helper. And praise be to Allah, Lord of the worlds.

Commentary
This passage forms the concluding section of the author's commentary. It begins by explaining the last line of the poem, which is a prayer for the Prophet Muhammad, his companions, his family, and those who follow his path. The author clarifies that 'Muhammad' specifies the Prophet, and 'his companions' and 'his family' are included. The word 'follower' is explained as anyone who follows his sunnah from his community until the Day of Judgment. Then the author turns to his own supplication, asking Allah to cover his shortcomings and accept his work. He acknowledges his own inability and seeks the Prophet's intercession. He prays that the work be a means of benefit and accepted sincerely. Finally, he provides the colophon: the work was completed on Thursday, 20th Ramadan, 1047 AH (corresponding to 1638 CE), by Abd al-Salam ibn Ibrahim al-Maliki al-Laqani. He ends with praise and reliance on Allah.

Misconception & Correction
Misconception: The author is being overly self-deprecating and insincere.
Correction: This is a traditional scholarly expression of humility before Allah and acknowledgment that all perfection belongs to Him. It reflects a proper attitude of a believer.
