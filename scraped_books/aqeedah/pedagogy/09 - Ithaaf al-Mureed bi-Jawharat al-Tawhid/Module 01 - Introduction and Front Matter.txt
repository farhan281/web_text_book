The Editor's Introduction
In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful.
Praise be to Allah who raised the banner of the People of the Sunnah throughout Islamic history, and made them the imams of the religion and its renewers, and supported them through Abu al-Hasan al-Ash'ari and those who followed his path, after the people of innovation in that era had almost succeeded in covering the white highway which the Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, left, whose night is like its day.
And peace and blessings be upon the most noble of His creations, our master Muhammad, the teacher of humanity, of great character and abundant grace, the conveyer of Allah's commands and prohibitions, and upon his family and all his companions.
To proceed:
Indeed, the science of tawhid is the foundation of the religion, the structure of the sciences; through it faith is firmly rooted, through it doubts are repelled and uncertainties vanish, and through it the Muslim fortifies himself from innovative beliefs that may lead to destruction in the final abode... on the Day.
Therefore our scholars throughout the ages have paid attention to this noble science, and authored in it lengthy works and abridgements, with innovative methods suitable for all levels. Thus you find them authoring small, medium, and large matns. And the work of the righteous saintly imam, the blessing, Abu Abd Allah al-Sanusi is not hidden from us, for he authored six matns in this art: al-Muqaddimat, al-Aqida al-Kubra, al-Aqida al-Wusta, then al-Sughra, then Sughra al-Sughra, then al-Hafida. Then he wrote for each matn a suitable commentary. This is a highly beneficial method of authorship, suitable to the levels of students, so that by reading these books they ascend until the student reaches his full strength, then enters the science of kalam with a firm entry, standing on his legs firmly without fidgeting.
And among these methods also is the work of Imam Ibrahim al-Laqani, for he authored a blessed, fluent poem in the science of tawhid named Jawharat al-Tawhid, to make it easy for students to memorize and recall its issues. It is a poem that needs no introduction; none denies that Allah has placed acceptance and blessing upon it except one with a sick heart and weak vision. And since it was of such status, scholars paid attention to it with commentaries, glosses, and teaching. Among these commentaries is the commentary of his son, Shaykh Abd al-Salam, Ithaaf al-Mureed bi-Sharh Jawharat al-Tawhid. It is a hopeful commentary, fluent like its matn, and it is among the books that scholars have vied in paying great attention to. They served it, wrote glosses and notes, and taught it to their students. I have come across more than fifteen glosses and notes on this blessed commentary. Indeed, it is a book worthy of being in the educational curriculum for students of sacred knowledge, given the acceptance and service it has received from scholars. It is necessary to refer to those textbooks by which our scholars used to build their students, in order to elevate them.
(1) A mention of some names of commentaries and glosses on the matn of al-Jawhara will come under the title: An Introductory Tree of the Commentaries of Jawharat al-Tawhid, and we will mention in it also the glosses and notes that scholars have placed on this book of ours, Ithaaf al-Mureed.

والصلاة والسلام على أشرف مخلوقاته، سيّدنا محمَّدٍ مُعلم البشرية، ذو الخُلق العظيم، والفضل العميم مُبلغ أوامر الله ونواهيه، وعلى آله وأصحابه أجمعين.
أما بعد،
فإن علم التوحيد أساس الدين، ومبنى العلوم، وبه يترسخ الإيمان، وبه تندحر الشبهات، وتتلاشى الشُّكوك، وبه يُحصِّنُ المسلم نفسَه مِن بدع الاعتقاد التي قد تفضى إلى الهلاك المعاد.. يوم
ولذلك اهتم علماؤنا عبر العصور بهذا العلم الشريف، وألفوا فيه المطولات والمختصرات، بطرق مبتكرة تناسب جميع المستويات، فتجدهم ألفوا المتون الصغيرة والمتوسطة والكبيرة، وما صنيع الإمام الولي البركة أبي عبد الله السنوسي عنا بخافٍ، حيثُ ألف ستة متونٍ في هذا الفن، وهي: (المُقدمات، والعقيدة الكبرى، والعقيدة الوسطى، ثم الصغرى، ثم صُغرى الصغرى، ثم الحفيدة) ثم وضع على كل متن شرحا يناسبه، وهذه طريقة في التأليف عظيمة النفع، تناسب مستويات الطلاب فيرتقي بقراءة هذه الكتب حتى يبلغ الطالبُ أَشُدَّهُ، فيلج في علم الكلام ولوج متمكن واقف على ساقيه بإحكام لا يَتمَلْمَل.
ومن هذا الطرق أيضا صنيعُ الإمام إبراهيم اللقاني حيث ألف نظماً سَلِسًا مُباركا في علم التوحيد سماه: جوهرة التوحيد ليسهل على الطلاب حفظه واستحضار مسائله، وهو نظم غني عن التعريف، لا يُنكِرُ أَنَّ اللهَ قد وضعَ له القبول والبركة إلا ذو قلب مريض وبصرٍ ضعيف ـ وحيثُ كانَ بهذه المثابة اهتم به العلماء شرحا وتحشية وتدريسا (١)، ومن بين هذه الشروح شرحُ ولده الشيخ عبد السلام إتحاف المريد بشرح جوهرة التوحيد، وهو شرح مَرْجِيٌّ سَلِس كمتنه، وهو مِن الكتب التي توارد العلماء على الاهتمام بها أيما اهتمام، حيث خدموها ووضعوا عليها الحواشي والتقريرات ودرَّسُوها لطلابهم، وقد وقفت على أكثر من خمسة عشر حاشية وتقرير على هذا الشرح المبارك، ولعمري إنه كتابٌ حَقيق أنْ يكونَ في المنهج التعليمي لطلاب العلم الشرعي، لما لقي من قبولٍ وخِدمة لدى العلماء، لا بدَّ مِن الرُّجوع إلى تلك الكتب الدراسية التي كانَ علماؤنا يُؤَسِّسون عليها طلابهم كي يصعدوا بهم
(١) سيأتي ذكر بعض أسماء شروح وحواشي متن «الجوهرة» تحت عنوان: شجرة تعريفية بشروح جوهرة التوحيد وسنذكر فيها أيضا الحواشي والتقريرات التي وضعها العلماء على كتابنا هذا إتحاف المريد.
The Editor's Introduction
In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful.
Praise be to Allah who raised the banner of the People of the Sunnah throughout Islamic history, and made them the imams of the religion and its renewers, and supported them through Abu al-Hasan al-Ash'ari and those who followed his path, after the people of innovation in that era had almost succeeded in covering the white highway which the Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, left, whose night is like its day.
And peace and blessings be upon the most noble of His creations, our master Muhammad, the teacher of humanity, of great character and abundant grace, the conveyer of Allah's commands and prohibitions, and upon his family and all his companions.
To proceed:
Indeed, the science of tawhid is the foundation of the religion, the structure of the sciences; through it faith is firmly rooted, through it doubts are repelled and uncertainties vanish, and through it the Muslim fortifies himself from innovative beliefs that may lead to destruction in the final abode... on the Day.
Therefore our scholars throughout the ages have paid attention to this noble science, and authored in it lengthy works and abridgements, with innovative methods suitable for all levels. Thus you find them authoring small, medium, and large matns. And the work of the righteous saintly imam, the blessing, Abu Abd Allah al-Sanusi is not hidden from us, for he authored six matns in this art: al-Muqaddimat, al-Aqida al-Kubra, al-Aqida al-Wusta, then al-Sughra, then Sughra al-Sughra, then al-Hafida. Then he wrote for each matn a suitable commentary. This is a highly beneficial method of authorship, suitable to the levels of students, so that by reading these books they ascend until the student reaches his full strength, then enters the science of kalam with a firm entry, standing on his legs firmly without fidgeting.
And among these methods also is the work of Imam Ibrahim al-Laqani, for he authored a blessed, fluent poem in the science of tawhid named Jawharat al-Tawhid, to make it easy for students to memorize and recall its issues. It is a poem that needs no introduction; none denies that Allah has placed acceptance and blessing upon it except one with a sick heart and weak vision. And since it was of such status, scholars paid attention to it with commentaries, glosses, and teaching. Among these commentaries is the commentary of his son, Shaykh Abd al-Salam, Ithaaf al-Mureed bi-Sharh Jawharat al-Tawhid. It is a hopeful commentary, fluent like its matn, and it is among the books that scholars have vied in paying great attention to. They served it, wrote glosses and notes, and taught it to their students. I have come across more than fifteen glosses and notes on this blessed commentary. Indeed, it is a book worthy of being in the educational curriculum for students of sacred knowledge, given the acceptance and service it has received from scholars. It is necessary to refer to those textbooks by which our scholars used to build their students, in order to elevate them.
(1) A mention of some names of commentaries and glosses on the matn of al-Jawhara will come under the title: An Introductory Tree of the Commentaries of Jawharat al-Tawhid, and we will mention in it also the glosses and notes that scholars have placed on this book of ours, Ithaaf al-Mureed.
