Biography of Imam al-Sanusi: Name, Lineage, Birth, and Teachers
Biographical account of Imam al-Sanusi's name, lineage, birth, and teachers.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the key biographical details of Imam al-Sanusi, including his full name, lineage, birth date, and the scholars he studied under.

Core Idea
Imam al-Sanusi's life and teachers shaped the great work 'Umm al-Barahin'.

Before studying any scholar's work, it is helpful to know something about the scholar himself. Imam al-Sanusi, the author of 'Umm al-Barahin', was a renowned theologian of the 9th century AH. This lesson introduces his name, lineage, birth, and the teachers who influenced his education.

Guided Path
1
Read the biographical passage
Read the Arabic passage once to get an overview. Then read the English translation to ensure understanding.
Note the various epithets used for al-Sanusi.
2
Identify Sanusi's full name and epithets
Find his full name: Muhammad ibn Yusuf ibn Umar ibn Shu'ayb al-Sanusi. Notice the epithets al-Hasani and al-Tilimsani.
Al-Hasani links him to al-Hasan ibn Ali through his paternal grandmother.
3
Note the description of his character
The passage describes him as 'the scholar, the righteous, the ascetic, the devout' etc. This shows his high spiritual station.
4
List his teachers and what he studied
Go through each teacher mentioned and the subject he studied with them. For example, he studied Quranic readings with Sayyid al-Sharif al-Hasani.
The variety of subjects shows his comprehensive education.
5
Reflect on the significance of his education
Consider how this broad training under multiple masters prepared him to author 'Umm al-Barahin', a foundational text in Aqidah.
This connects his biography to his scholarly legacy.

A brief biography of Imam Abu Abdullah al-Sanusi. Indeed, the book before us originally is a recension (tahdhib) of the commentary written by the scholar Ahmad ibn Isa al-Ansari on the text 'Umm al-Barahin. And this text is one of the esteemed books of Ahl al-Sunnah, and its author is one of the scholars of Ahl al-Sunnah wal-Jama'ah, namely Imam Abu Abdullah al-Sanusi, may Allah have mercy on him. Below we present a summary of the biography of this imam, the scholar. His name and lineage: He is Imam Muhammad ibn Yusuf ibn Umar ibn Shu'ayb al-Sanusi, and he is known by the epithet al-Sanusi, in relation to a tribe in the Maghreb, and he is also called al-Hasani in relation to al-Hasan ibn Ali ibn Abi Talib from his paternal grandmother. He is also al-Tilimsani in relation to the town of Tilimsan. And he is the scholar of Tilimsan, its righteous one, its ascetic, the great of its scholars, the scholar, the versatile, the righteous, the ascetic, the devout, al-Sanusi, the master, the verifier, the humble, Abu Ya'qub Yusuf. His birth was after the thirties and eight hundred [i.e., after 830 AH]. His teachers and his studies: Imam al-Sanusi grew up good, blessed, virtuous, righteous. He studied under a group, including: his mentioned father, the scholar Nasr al-Zawawi, the scholar Muhammad ibn Tuzat, Sayyid al-Sharif Abu al-Hajjaj Yusuf ibn Abi al-Abbas ibn Muhammad al-Sharif al-Hasani – he studied Quranic readings under him, and from the scholar al-mu'addal Abu Abdullah al-Habdhah the science of the astrolabe, and from Imam Muhammad ibn al-Abbas the principles (usul) and logic, and from the jurist al-Jallab jurisprudence, and from the great saint, the righteous al-Hasan Abarkah al-Rashidi – he attended his gatherings much, benefited from him and his blessing much, and loved him.

Commentary
This passage provides a brief biography of Imam al-Sanusi. It begins by describing the book at hand: it is a recension (tahdhib) of Ahmad ibn Isa al-Ansari's commentary on 'Umm al-Barahin, which is a respected text among Ahl al-Sunnah and authored by Imam al-Sanusi. Then it gives his full name: Muhammad ibn Yusuf ibn Umar ibn Shu'ayb al-Sanusi. His epithet 'al-Sanusi' refers to a tribe in the Maghreb; he is also called 'al-Hasani' due to his descent from al-Hasan ibn Ali from his paternal grandmother's side. He is also 'al-Tilimsani' from Tilimsan. He is described as the scholar, righteous, ascetic, etc. His birth was after 830 AH (the thirties and eight hundred). The passage then lists his teachers: his father, Nasr al-Zawawi, Muhammad ibn Tuzat, Sayyid al-Sharif Abu al-Hajjaj al-Hasani (from whom he studied Quranic readings), Abu Abdullah al-Habdhah (astrolabe science), Muhammad ibn al-Abbas (principles and logic), al-Jallab (jurisprudence), and the saint al-Hasan Abarkah al-Rashidi (whom he attended and benefited from).

Misconception & Correction

Correction: This passage clarifies that the Sanusi in question is the classical theologian from the 9th century AH, author of 'Umm al-Barahin', not the 19th-century leader of the Sanusi order.

Imam al-Sanusi: Character and Scientific Status
Biography of Imam al-Sanusi: his character, piety, and scholarly achievements.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the scientific status and character of Imam al-Sanusi, and his emphasis on tawhid as the foundation of knowledge.

Core Idea
Imam al-Sanusi was a sign of knowledge and piety, unmatched in tawhid and character.

This passage introduces us to the remarkable scholar Imam al-Sanusi. It describes his deep knowledge in both exoteric and esoteric sciences, his unique command of tawhid, and his exemplary character. Through a quote from his student, we learn his view that tawhid is the only exoteric science that directly leads to knowledge of Allah.

He was a sign in his knowledge, guidance, righteousness, conduct, asceticism, piety, and caution. His scientific status and conduct: He had the greatest share in the exoteric sciences; he combined their branches and their principles, the arrow and the coin. He would not speak on a discipline except that the listener would think he mastered nothing else, especially tawhid and rational sciences. He shared in them with others, but he was unique in the esoteric sciences; indeed, he surpassed the jurists in his knowledge of solving difficult problems, especially tawhid. He would not read an exoteric science except that he would derive from it the sciences of the hereafter, especially tafsir and hadith, due to his great mindfulness of Allah Almighty, as if he witnessed the hereafter. His student al-Mallali said: 'I heard him say: "There is no science among the exoteric sciences that bequeaths knowledge of Him and mindfulness of Him except tawhid, and through it one opens to the understanding of all sciences, and according to one's knowledge his fear increases."' End quote. He was unique in his knowledge of tawhid to the utmost degree, and his books that he authored on creeds are sufficient in it, especially the small one (Umm al-Barahin); nothing equals it. And he was forbearing, very patient; perhaps he would hear something disliked but would pretend not to see it, and it would not affect him; rather, he would smile. This was his way in everything that angered him. He would not pay attention to it with his face, nor hold a grudge against anyone, nor frown at anyone. He would initiate whoever spoke about his honor with kind words and veneration, so that he would think he was his friend.

Commentary
This passage describes Imam al-Sanusi's character and scientific status. It states that he was a sign in knowledge and piety. Then it details his mastery: he had the greatest share in exoteric sciences, combining their branches and principles, and was especially skilled in tawhid and rational sciences. He also had a unique command of esoteric sciences. He approached exoteric sciences with a view to the hereafter, focusing on tafsir and hadith. A quote from his student al-Mallali highlights that al-Sanusi considered tawhid as the only exoteric science that directly leads to knowledge and fear of Allah. He was unique in tawhid, his books, especially Umm al-Barahin, are unmatched. The passage also describes his noble character: forbearance, patience, not reacting to insults, smiling, not holding grudges, and treating those who speak ill of him with kindness.

Prophets' Sleep: Eyes Sleep, Hearts Remain Awake
Observation with multiple hadith evidence that prophets' eyes sleep but hearts remain awake.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the unique sleep state of prophets: their eyes sleep but their hearts remain awake, a special sign of their connection to divine revelation.

Core Idea
Prophets' hearts never sleep — even in slumber, they are spiritually awake.

The author presents a distinctive observation about the prophets: although they experience physical sleep, their hearts remain vigilant. This miraculous state is supported by multiple authentic hadith from the Prophet ﷺ himself and from his Companions, highlighting a unique aspect of prophethood.

Guided Path
1
Read the observation and first hadith
Read the opening statement and the hadith from Aisha (via Bukhari). Notice how the Prophet replies 'My eyes sleep but my heart does not sleep' when asked about his sleep before witr.
This hadith establishes the principle directly from the Prophet's own words.
2
Trace the generalization to all prophets
In the night journey hadith from Anas, the statement is explicitly generalized: 'and likewise the prophets, their eyes sleep but their hearts do not sleep.' Note that this occurs in a context of revelation.
The generalization is a key point of this passage.
3
Recognize the consistency across narrations
Multiple hadith from different Companions (Aisha, Anas, Ibn Abbas, Jabir, Abu Hurayra) and multiple collectors (Bukhari, Muslim, Nasa'i, Bayhaqi, Hakim) all reinforce the same teaching. Observe the authentication by Imam al-Nawawi and al-Hakim.
The abundance of evidence strengthens the doctrinal point.
