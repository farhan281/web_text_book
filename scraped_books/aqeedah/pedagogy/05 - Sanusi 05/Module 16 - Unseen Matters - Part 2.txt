Core Idea

Al-Jubba'i proposed a speculative theory about the weighing of deeds, suggesting Allah creates jewels to represent each deed. However, the Prophet (peace be upon him) was asked about this and gave a different answer: the scrolls are weighed. This lesson examines that exchange and its implications.

The hadith suggests that deeds themselves are recorded in scrolls and those are weighed.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the Islamic doctrine that Paradise and Hell are already created, and the refutation of the Mu'tazilite claim that they are not yet created.

Core Idea
Paradise and Hell are created now, not just in the future.

The Objection that Created Paradise Would Perish and the Response
The Mu'tazilite objection that created Paradise would perish, and the Sunni response.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand how Sunni scholars respond to the Mu'tazilite objection that if Paradise and Hell are created, they would perish, using the principle of specification (takhṣīṣ) and the timing of the Hereafter.

Core Idea
The verse 'Everything perishes' is general but specified to exclude the eternal bliss of Paradise.

This lesson presents a key objection from the Mu'tazilites against the Sunni belief that Paradise and Hell are created. They argue that if these abodes were created, they would be subject to annihilation, contradicting verses that promise eternal bliss. The response clarifies how the Qur'anic statement about perishing is not absolute but specified, and how the timing of the Hereafter resolves the apparent contradiction.

Guided Path
1
Read the objection

The objection assumes that if Paradise is created, it must perish like all created things.
2
Examine the first response
The first response says the perishing applies before entering the Hereafter, not after. This resolves the timing conflict.
The verse 'Everything is perishing' refers to the state before the final judgment.
3
Examine the second response
The second response says the verse 'Everything is perishing' is general (ʿāmm) but specified (makṣūṣ) to exclude the abode of the Hereafter.
Specification (takhṣīṣ) is a key principle in Islamic jurisprudence and theology.
4
Connect to the broader theology
This shows how Muslims reconcile apparently conflicting texts using tools like specification and contextual interpretation.
The belief that Paradise and Hell are created and eternal is upheld.
