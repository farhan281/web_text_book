The author prohibits insulting the Companions and equates love with faith and hate with disbelief.

Learning Objective

The Prophet's testimony is revelation; we testify only because he testified.

In this lesson, we examine the Sunni doctrine affirming ten specific Companions as promised Paradise. The author cites the Prophet's own words as the basis, emphasizing that the Prophet does not speak from desire but from divine revelation. We'll see the list of names and the special status of Abu 'Ubayda as the trustee of the nation.

وَرَضِيتُ لَكُمُ الإِسْلَامَ دِينًا ﴾ [سورة المائدة آية: ٣]، وَهُوَ بَيْنَ العُلُوِّ وَالتَّقْصِير وَالتَّشْبِيهِ وَالتَّعْطِيلِ وَالْجَبْرِيَّةِ وَالقَدَرِ وَالأَمْنِ وَاليَأْسِ. فَهَذَا دِينُنَا وَاعْتِقَادُنَا ظَاهِرًا وَبَاطِنًا، وَنَحْنُ نَبْرَأُ إِلَى اللَّهُ تَعَالَى مِمَّنْ خَالَفَ الَّذِي ذَكَرْنَاهُ وَبَيَّنَّاهُ، وَنَسْأَلُ اللهَ تَعَالَى أَنْ يُثَبِّتَنَا عَلَيْهِ وَيَخْتِمَ لَنَا بِهِ وَيَعْصِمَنَا مِنَ الْأَهْوَاءِ المُخْتَلِطَةِ وَالآرَاءِ الْمُتَفَرِّقَةِ وَالمَذَاهِبِ الرَّدِيَّةِ ؛ كَالمُشَبِّهَةِ وَالجَهْمِيَّةِ وَالْجَبْرِيَّةِ وَالقَدَرِيَّةِ وَغَيْرِهِمْ مِمَّنْ خَالَفَ السُّنَّةَ وَالجَمَاعَةَ وَاتَّبَعَ البِدْعَةَ وَالضَّلَالَةَ، وَنَحْنُ مِنْهُمْ بَرَاءٌ وَهُمْ عِنْدَنَا ضُلَّالٌ وَأَرْدِيَاءُ. وَاللَّهُ أَعْلَمُ بِالصَّوَابِ وَإِلَيْهِ المَرْجِعُ وَالمَابُ.
And I have chosen for you Islam as a religion. And it is between elevation and deficiency, between anthropomorphism and negation, between compulsion (Jabriyyah) and free will (Qadr), and between security and despair. So this is our religion and our belief, outwardly and inwardly, and we disassociate ourselves to Allah, the Exalted, from whoever opposes what we have mentioned and explained. And we ask Allah, the Exalted, to make us firm upon it and to seal for us with it and to protect us from confused desires, scattered opinions, and evil sects: like the Mushabbihah (anthropomorphists), the Jahmiyyah, the Jabriyyah (determinists), the Qadariyyah (free-willers), and others from those who oppose the Sunnah and the Jama'ah and follow innovation and misguidance. We are free from them, and they are in our view misguided and evil. And Allah knows best what is correct, and to Him is the return and the ultimate abode.

Commentary
This passage is the conclusion of the Tahawiyyah creed. It begins by quoting a part of Quranic verse 5:3, 'And I have chosen for you Islam as a religion,' to affirm that Islam is the chosen religion from Allah. Then the author describes the correct understanding of Islam as being on a middle path between various extremes: between exaggeration and negligence (elevation and deficiency), between anthropomorphism (tashbih) and divestment (ta'til), between the extreme determinism of the Jabriyyah and the extreme free will of the Qadariyyah, and between unwarranted security from Allah's punishment and utter despair of His mercy. After stating this balanced position, the author declares that this is the religion and belief of the Sunni community, both outwardly and inwardly. He then makes a declaration of disavowal (bara'ah) to Allah from anyone who opposes what has been stated. The passage continues with a supplication that Allah make them firm (thabat) upon this creed, seal their lives with it, and protect them from confused desires (ahwa' mukhtalitah) and scattered opinions, as well as from evil sects such as the Mushabbihah (those who liken Allah to creation), the Jahmiyyah (followers of Jahm ibn Safwan), the Jabriyyah (extreme determinists), the Qadariyyah (extreme free-willers), and others who oppose the Sunnah and the united community (Jama'ah) and follow innovation (bid'ah) and misguidance. The author asserts that the Sunni community is innocent of them and considers them misguided and evil (ardiya'). Finally, the text concludes with a humble acknowledgement that Allah knows best what is correct and that to Him is the final return and abode. This ending reinforces the Sunni position of adhering to the middle path, dissociating from sectarian deviations, and relying on Allah for guidance and steadfastness.

Disavowal of Innovators and Supplication for Steadfastness
Imam Tahawi declares disavowal of deviant sects and prays for steadfastness.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the importance of disavowing deviant sects and the supplication for steadfastness in the Sunni creed.

Core Idea
Faith includes disassociation from error and reliance on Allah for guidance.

After affirming the balanced creed, Imam al-Tahawi makes a declaration of disavowal (bara'ah) to Allah from all who oppose the Sunni path. He then prays for steadfastness, sealing the creed with humility. This lesson examines the final part of the Tahawiyyah text.

Guided Path
1
Read the disavowal declaration
Notice the phrase 'نحن نبرأ إلى الله' - we disassociate ourselves to Allah. This is an act of religious distancing from falsehood.
Disavowal is not personal hatred but a theological stance.
2
List the sects mentioned
The passage names Mushabbihah, Jahmiyyah, Jabriyyah, Qadariyyah, and others. Identify what each represents.
These are examples of those who oppose the Sunnah.
3
Examine the supplication
The author asks for thabat (firmness), a good ending (khatimah), and protection from confused desires and evil sects.
This shows reliance on Allah for guidance.
