Correction of a Misidentified Narrator: al-Tabarani vs. al-Tabari
The editor corrects a misidentified narrator from al-Tabari to al-Tabarani using chronological proof.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand how textual critics identify and correct misattributions of narrators by using chronological and biographical evidence.

Core Idea
A 97-year gap proves al-Tabari could not have heard from al-Tahawi.

Textual notes in scholarly editions often include important corrections. In this passage, the editor identifies an error where the name 'al-Tabari' was mistakenly written in place of 'al-Tabarani'. By examining the death dates and biographical details, he shows that the supposed narrator could not have been a student of Imam al-Tahawi. This lesson walks through the evidence and the corrected identification.

في المطبوعتين: "الطبري" وهو تحريف؛ فإن أبا القاسم الطبري هو هبة الله بن الحسن بن منصور الطبري الشافعي المعروف باللالكائي تلميذ أبي حامد الإسفراييني، وقد توفي سنة ٤١٨ هـ، ولم يذكر في ترجمته سماع من الطحاوي ولا في ترجمة الطحاوي منه سماع وبين وفاة الطحاوي ووفاته ٩٧ عاما، وقد ذكر في ترجمة اللالكائي أنه مات كهلا والكهل من جاوز الثلاثين إلى إحدى وخمسين، والمذكور في تلاميذ الطحاوي هو أبو القاسم الطبراني، وقد سبقت ترجمته، وهو ما ذكره العلامة قاسم في تاج التراجم اول انظر: الطبقات السنية في تراجم الحنفية (١/ ٧٣-١٦٩)، تاج التراجم ص (٢١-٢٤)، طبقات الشافعية لابن قاضي شهبة (۱۹۸/۲)، سير أعلام النبلاء (٤١۹/۱۷)، طبقات الحفاظ ص (٤٢١) القاموس المحيط (كهل).
In the two printed editions [the name] 'al-Tabari' appears, but this is a corruption. Indeed, Abu al-Qasim al-Tabari is Hibat Allah ibn al-Hasan ibn Mansur al-Tabari al-Shafi'i, known as al-Lalika'i, a student of Abu Hamid al-Isfarayini. He died in 418 AH, and there is no mention in his biography of having heard from al-Tahawi, nor in al-Tahawi's biography of his having heard from him; between the deaths of al-Tahawi and himself there is a gap of 97 years. It is mentioned in the biography of al-Lalika'i that he died as a middle-aged man (kahl), and the kahl is one who has passed thirty up to fifty-one. The person listed among the students of al-Tahawi is Abu al-Qasim al-Tabarani, whose biography has preceded. This is what the scholar Qasim mentioned in Taj al-Tarajim. See also: al-Tabaqat al-Saniyya fi Tarajim al-Hanafiyya (1/73-169), Taj al-Tarajim pp. 21-24, Tabaqat al-Shafi'iyya li Ibn Qadi Shuhba (2/198), Siyar A'lam al-Nubala' (17/419), Tabaqat al-Huffaz p. 421, al-Qamus al-Muhit (kahl).

Commentary
This passage is a critical textual note correcting a misattribution. The editor explains that the name 'al-Tabari' in printed editions is a corruption. The actual person intended is Abu al-Qasim al-Tabarani, not Abu al-Qasim al-Tabari (al-Lalika'i), because the latter died 97 years after al-Tahawi and could not have heard from him. The note provides chronological evidence and references to several biographical works to support the correction.

Misconception & Correction
Misconception: The term 'الكهل' (kahl) means old or elderly.
Correction: The passage explicitly states that 'الكهل' refers to a middle-aged person, from 30 to 51 years old.
