The Three Rational Judgments: Necessary, Impossible, Possible
Dardeer defines the three rational judgments: necessary, impossible, and possible.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the definitions of the three rational judgments (necessary, impossible, possible) and how they form the foundation of kalam reasoning.

Core Idea
Everything in existence is either necessary, impossible, or possible—there is no fourth.

Dardeer begins by establishing the three categories of rational judgment that underpin all theological reasoning. Before we can discuss God or the world, we need a clear vocabulary for what is conceivable. This passage provides that vocabulary with concise definitions.

Guided Path
1
Read the passage and translation
Read the Arabic passage once, then the English translation. Notice the three terms: الواجب العقلي, المستحيل العقلي, الجائز العقلي.
The word النُّبوتَ is likely a scribal error for الثبوت (existence). Keep this in mind.
2
Understand the definition of the necessary
The necessary is that which cannot be conceived as non-existent. Ask: What things in our experience are like this? Only God.
3
Understand the definition of the impossible
The impossible is that which cannot accept existence. It is the opposite of the necessary. Example: a partner with God.
4
Understand the definition of the possible
The possible is that which can equally be conceived as existing or not existing. This covers everything in the universe.
Possible does not mean 'uncertain'; it means logically neutral.
5
Reflect on the big picture
Consider why these three categories are exhaustive and mutually exclusive. This is the starting point for all kalam arguments.
This is called the 'trilemma' of rational judgments.

Commentary
This passage defines the three rational judgments. The rationally necessary (al-wajib al-aqli) is that which cannot be conceived as not existing with respect to its essence; it is that whose non-existence is inconceivable. The rationally impossible (al-mustahil al-aqli) is that which cannot accept existence in its essence, the opposite of the necessary. The rationally possible (al-ja'iz al-aqli) is that which can equally be conceived as existing or not existing. These definitions are foundational for all kalam reasoning about God, who is necessary, and the universe, which is possible.

Three rational judgments cover all possibilities for any concept.

Thus, the three rational judgments are clearly established.

Are you willing to submit your intellect to what Allah has informed, even if it seems impossible by worldly standards? Commit to one action this week that shows you trust Allah's words over your own logic.
