Introduction to al-Madina al-Ilmiyya: Its Founding and Six Branches
Editorial introduction describing the founding and branches of al-Madina al-Ilmiyya.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the purpose of al-Madina al-Ilmiyya and the six branches it established to promote Islamic knowledge.

Core Idea
Al-Madina al-Ilmiyya was founded with six branches to revive the Sunnah and spread Islamic sciences.

This passage introduces the global non-political movement 'ad-Da'wah al-Islamiyyah' and its specialized council, al-Madina al-Ilmiyya. The council was established by scholars to systematically promote authentic Islamic teachings. They created six branches to handle different aspects of publishing, translation, and scholarship. This lesson will help you understand the structure and objectives of this important institution.

Guided Path
1
Read the passage and identify the six branches
First, read the Arabic passage and its translation. Notice that after praising Allah, the author describes the council's six branches. List them in your mind.
The branches are enumerated explicitly in the text.
2
Understand the purpose of each branch
Consider what each branch indicates: publishing classical works, reform books, translation, textbooks, inspection, and takhrij (hadith extraction). How do these cover different needs?
3
Reflect on the emphasis on Imam Ahmad Rida Khan
The council prioritizes publishing the works of Imam Ahmad Rida Khan. Why might this be important for the movement? Reflect on his role as a renewer.
4
Consider the call to cooperation
The passage ends with a call for brothers to cooperate and read the published books. How does this foster community engagement?

Praise be to Allah who sent down the Qur'an and taught eloquence, and prayers and peace be upon the people, our master and patron Muhammad al-Mustafa Ahmad al-Mujtaba, and upon his pure and holy family and his truthful and righteous companions, by Your mercy, O Most Merciful of the merciful! ... To proceed: By the praise of Allah Almighty, the global call society, the non-political movement "ad-Da'wah al-Islamiyyah" for propagating the Qur'an and Sunnah, is dedicated to the call of good and reviving the Sunnah and spreading the knowledge of the laws in the world. And to perform these matters with good action and complete methodology, councils were established, among them: the council "al-Madina al-Ilmiyya". And by the praise of Allah Blessed and Exalted, the pillars of this council are the honourable scholars, may Allah increase them in peace; they resolved with firm resolve to spread the purely scientific and investigative matter, and they established for the achievement of these matters six branches: (1) Branch for the books of the highest presence, the Imam of Ahl al-Sunnah, the renewer of the religion and the community, the protector of the Sunnah, the eradicator of innovation, the scholar of the Shari'a, Imam Ahmad Rida Khan, upon him the mercy of the Most Merciful. (2) Branch for reform books. (3) Branch for translation of books from Arabic to Urdu and vice versa, and from Arabic to Urdu and vice versa, and from Urdu to Persian and Sindhi and other world languages. (4) Branch for textbooks. (5) Branch for inspection of books. (6) Branch for takhrij. And among the first preferences of the council of "al-Madina al-Ilmiyya" is to present the magnificent precious works of the highest presence, the Imam of Ahl al-Sunnah, the great of blessing and rank, the renewer of the religion and the community, the protector of the Sunnah, the eradicator of innovation, the scholar of the Shari'a, the master of the path, the all-knowing, our master the pilgrim the memoriser the reciter, the king the Imam Ahmad Rida Khan, upon him the mercy of the Most Merciful - in easy styles according to our new era. So let every one of the Islamic brothers cooperate in these civil matters with his simplicity, and let him read the books printed by the council and encourage others of the Islamic brothers to them. May Allah Almighty grant all the councils of the Islamic call, especially "al-Madina al-Ilmiyya", continuous advancement, and make our matters in religion adorned with the ornament of sincerity, and a means for the good of both abodes, and provide us, Allah Almighty, with martyrdom under the shade of the green dome (upon its owner be prayers and peace) and burial in the garden of al-Baqi', and dwelling in the paradise of Firdaws. Amen, by the dignity of the trustworthy Prophet, may Allah the Exalted bless him and his family and grant them peace.

Commentary
This passage is an editorial introduction. It begins with the basmala and hamdala, then explains the founding of the non-political global call movement 'ad-Da'wah al-Islamiyyah' and its council 'al-Madina al-Ilmiyya'. The council's pillars are honourable scholars who established six branches: (1) publishing the works of Imam Ahmad Rida Khan, (2) reform books, (3) translation, (4) textbooks, (5) inspection, (6) takhrij. The council prioritises making Imam Ahmad Rida Khan's works accessible in modern style. The passage calls for cooperation and concludes with a du'a for advancement, sincerity, martyrdom, burial in al-Baqi', and paradise.
