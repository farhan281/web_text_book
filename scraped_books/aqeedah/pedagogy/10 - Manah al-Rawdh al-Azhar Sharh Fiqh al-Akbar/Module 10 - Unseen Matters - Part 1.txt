He clarifies what resurrection means—restoration after annihilation—and distinguishes it from other meanings of the term. Commentary
This passage defines and proves the reality of al-Qisas (retribution) between people on the Day of Judgment. It begins by stating that Qisas is punishment by equivalence between opponents (people and servants) on the Day of Resurrection. Since there are no material currencies like dinars and dirhams, the retribution takes place through good deeds: the good deeds of the wrongdoer (the one who oppressed) are taken and given to the wronged (the one who was oppressed) in proportion to the wrong. وإعادة الروح إلى العبد في قبره حق ببارقة السيوف شاهدا)) ففي الكفاية أنه لا سؤال للأنبياء عليهم السلام. وقال السيد أبو شجاع علماء الحنفية: إن للصبيان سؤالاً، وكذا للأنبياء عند البعض، وقال بعضهم: صبيان المسلمين مغفور لهم قطعاً، والسؤال لحكمة لم يطلع عليها. وتوقف الإمام الأعظم رحمه الله في سؤال أطفال الكفرة ودخولهم الجنة، وغيره حكم بذلك فيكونون خدم أهل الجنة. (وإعادة الروح) أي ردّها أو تعلّقها إلى العبد أي جسده بجميع أجزائه أو ببعضها مجتمعة أو متفرقة في قبره حق والواو لمجرد الجمعية فلا ينافي أن السؤال بعد إعادة الروح وكمال الحال، فيقول المؤمن: ربي الله وديني الإسلام ونبيي محمد صلى الله تعالى عليه وسلم، ويقول الكافر: هاه هاه لا أدري)) رواه أبو داود، وأصله في الصحيحين. وفي المسألة خلاف المعتزلة وبعض الرافضة. وقد وردت الأحاديث المتظاهرةُ في المبنى المتواترة في المعنى في تحقيق أحوال البرزخ والعقبى، قد استوفاها شيخ مشايخنا الجلال السيوطي في كتابه المسمى بـ"شرح الصدور في أحوال القبور" وفي كتابه الآخر المسمى بـ"البدور السافرة في أحوال الآخرة"، فعليك بهما إن كنت تريد الاطلاع وارتفاع النزاع عن الطباع.
And the return of the soul to the servant in his grave is true. 'With the flashing of swords as a witness.' In al-Kifaya, there is no questioning for the prophets, peace be upon them. And al-Sayyid Abu Shuja' said: The Hanafi scholars hold that children are questioned, and likewise the prophets according to some. And some said: The children of Muslims are certainly forgiven, and the questioning is for a wisdom not disclosed. And Imam al-A'zam (Abu Hanifa), may Allah have mercy on him, halted in the matter of the questioning of the children of disbelievers and their entry into Paradise, while others judged that, so they become servants of the people of Paradise. (And the return of the soul) meaning its return or its attachment to the servant, i.e., his body, either all its parts or some of them, gathered or scattered, in his grave, is true. And the waw is merely for conjunction, so it does not negate that the questioning is after the return of the soul and the completion of the state. So the believer says: 'My Lord is Allah, my religion is Islam, and my Prophet is Muhammad, may Allah bless him and grant him peace.' And the disbeliever says: 'Hah, hah, I do not know.' Narrated by Abu Dawud, and its origin is in the two Sahihs. On this issue, there is disagreement with the Mu'tazila and some of the Rafidah. Indeed, numerous hadiths, manifest in chain and recurrent in meaning, have come concerning the realities of the Barzakh and the Hereafter. Our shaykh's shaykh, al-Jalal al-Suyuti, has gathered them in his book entitled 'Sharh al-Sudur fi Ahwal al-Qubur' and in his other book entitled 'al-Budur al-Safira fi Ahwal al-Akhira'. So you must refer to them if you desire investigation and the removal of discord from natures.

Commentary
This passage is a definition and explanation of the return of the soul and the questioning in the grave. It begins by stating as a matter of doctrine that the return of the soul to the servant in his grave is true. It then cites a hadith fragment implying that martyrs are not questioned, and notes that according to al-Kifaya, prophets are not questioned either. The passage then presents various scholarly opinions: Abu Shuja' reports that Hanafis hold that children are questioned, and some extend this to prophets. Some hold that Muslim children are forgiven regardless. Imam Abu Hanifa (al-A'zam) suspended judgment on the fate of children of disbelievers, while others rule that they become servants of Paradise. The passage then provides a detailed explanation of the return of the soul: it means the soul is returned or attached to the body (all or some parts). The conjunction waw simply connects statements, so it does not contradict that the questioning occurs after the return of the soul and the completion of the state. It then quotes the answers of the believer and disbeliever during the questioning, based on a hadith in Abu Dawud and the two Sahihs. The passage notes that the Mu'tazila and some Rafidah oppose this doctrine. Finally, it recommends consulting the collections of al-Suyuti for a thorough treatment of the intermedate state (barzakh) and the afterlife.
