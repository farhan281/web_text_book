Special Guidance and the Imam's Example
Commentator distinguishes special guidance from general invitation.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the distinction between Allah's general invitation to all and His special guidance of the elect, and why the Great Imam concluded his creed with this special guidance.

Core Idea
Allah's special guidance is a pure gift, not a general invitation.

In this passage, the commentator reflects on the verse "And Allah guides whom He wills to a straight path." He distinguishes between two kinds of divine calling: the general invitation extended to all people by justice, and the special, pure guidance granted to some by grace. The Great Imam Abu Hanifa chose to end his creed with this special guidance, teaching us to pray for a good end and divine protection.

Guided Path
1
Read the passage carefully
Read the Arabic and translation provided. Notice the two kinds of guidance mentioned: one by justice (general invitation) and one by grace (special guidance).
The verse quoted (Yunus:25) is the general invitation.
2
Identify the contrast
The commentator contrasts 'guidance by grace' with 'invitation by justice'. Understand that special guidance is not for everyone; it is a pure gift from Allah.
Reflect on why the Imam emphasized this in his creed.
3
Apply the lesson
The Imam's example shows we should seek a good end (husn al-khatima) through reliance on divine care and protection. We emulate him by maintaining our faith from beginning to end.
The passage ends with a prayer for pardon, well-being, and perpetual care.

Commentary
This passage is an interpretive commentary on the verse 'And Allah guides whom He wills to a straight path.' The commentator explains that 'guides' here refers to the beauty of Allah's grace, not as a general invitation, which is for all people by His justice as in the verse 'And Allah invites to the abode of peace.' The Great Imam (Abu Hanifa) concluded his creed with this special pure guidance, and the commentator says we should follow his example in seeking a good end by maintaining the state we had at the beginning (of faith) until the end, with divine care and protection from misguidance. The commentator ends with a prayer for pardon, well-being, and perpetual care.

Misconception & Correction
Misconception: Some may think that divine guidance is only general, i.e., Allah invites everyone equally without distinction.
Correction: The commentator clarifies that while Allah does invite all people by His justice, He also grants special, pure guidance to whom He wills by His grace. This special guidance is distinct and not automatic for everyone.
