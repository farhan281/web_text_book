And in another copy on which are the collation notes in the hand of al-Birzali (Muhammad ibn Yusuf al-Ushbi), dated Dhu al-Qa‘dah of the year 630 AH, at the Husamiyya school outside Damascus, the following example: At the end of the book 'Tabyin Kidhb al-Muftari fima Nusiba ila al-Imam Abi al-Hasan al-Ash‘ari' (in the hand of al-Qasim on a separate folio, these verses, and I do not know whether they are from al-Qasim's addition or from the original):
Say to the anthropomorphists who have transgressed the arguments of intellects with every objectionable statement:
Woe to you! You have measured the attributes of your God by your own attributes; this is the measure of the most losing.
Is the maker of a thing measured by his made thing? Is a writer of lines measured by the lines?
Far be it that a maker resembles his made thing; far be it that an image resembles its depictor.
This is absurd, and whoever says such is an unbeliever, brazenly entering Hellfire.
Whoever says that Allah resembles His creation, his statement is the statement of a liar.
Or says, 'I am like Him in speech,' then he is an unbeliever without doubt, so beware.
His speech we recite in our expressions, without likening the greatest God.
Were it not facilitated for our expressions, we would not be able to recite it; it is not facilitated by Allah.
Hearing not like the hearings of people, a hand and an eye not like the eye of the quarry.
The believers will see Him, and He is not a body, nor an accident, nor a substance.
Likewise, the speech of Allah is not like our speech; so understand my saying about the attributes and reflect.
Certainly.

Commentary
This passage presents a poem that refutes the doctrine of anthropomorphism (tashbih) regarding the divine attributes. The poem is transmitted in a copy of Ibn ‘Asakir's 'Tabyin Kidhb al-Muftari', with uncertainty whether it was added by al-Qasim or is original. The poem opens by addressing the anthropomorphists (al-mushabbiha) who have overstepped rational proofs. It then attacks their method: they compare God's attributes to human attributes, which is a flawed analogy (qiyas). The poem gives examples: a maker cannot be compared to his product, nor a writer to his lines. It declares that anyone who says God resembles His creation is a liar and an unbeliever destined for Hell. The poem also addresses the divine attribute of speech: God's speech (the Qur'an) is recited in human words, but it is not like human speech. Without God's facilitation, it could not be recited. The poem affirms that God's hearing, hand, and eye are not like those of creation. God will be seen by believers in the afterlife, yet He is not a body (jism), nor an accident ('arad), nor a substance (jawhar). The poem concludes by urging reflection on the attributes. The overall argument is a defense of tanzih (transcendence) against tashbih, a key theme in Ash'ari kalam.

Misconception & Correction
Misconception: God has a hand and eye like human hand and eye.
Correction: The poem affirms hand and eye but explicitly says they are not like created things (لَا كَعَيْن ٱلْمُحْجَر), meaning they are without likeness.

Checkpoint

Key Takeaway
This poem teaches that God's attributes are real but utterly unlike creation, preserving both affirmation and transcendence without falling into anthropomorphism or denial.

How This Lesson Applies — to MY Life?
?
When you recite 'Allah's hand' or 'Allah's eye,' do you ever catch yourself picturing a physical limb? How does this poem convict you to purify your mental image of God, and what practical step will you take today to replace any creeping anthropomorphism with pure tanzih?
?
The poet says that whoever says 'I am like Him in speech' is an unbeliever. When you speak of God's speech (the Qur'an), do you ever unconsciously treat it as if it were ordinary human speech? What does this passage demand you change in your attitude toward the Qur'an?
?
You are commanded to 'understand my saying about the attributes and reflect.' What is one attribute of God that you have struggled to affirm without falling into either denial or anthropomorphism? Write down a specific prayer to God asking Him to grant you true knowledge of that attribute as He deserves.
