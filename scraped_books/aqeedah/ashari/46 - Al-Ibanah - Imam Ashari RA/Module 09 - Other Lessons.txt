٢٤٨ - مسألة : وإن سأل سائل عن قول الله تعالى: ﴿ وَأَمَّا ثَمُودُ فَهَدَيْنَهُمْ فَاسْتَحَبُّوا الْعَمَى عَلَى الْهُدَى ) [فصلت: ۱۷] فقال : أليس ثمود كانوا كافرين وقد أخبر الله أنـه هـداهم؟ والجواب في هذه الآية على وجهين : أحدهما: أن ثمود على فريقين كافرين ومؤمنين، وهم الذين أخبر الله أنه أنجاهم مع صالح بقوله عز وجل : نَجَيْنَا صَالِحًا وَالَّذِينَ ءَامَنُوا مَعَهُ بِرَحْمَةِ رَبِّنَا﴾ [هود : ٦٦] فالذين عنى الله عز وجل من ثمود أنه هداهم هم المؤمنون دون الكافرين؛ لأن الله عز وجل قد بيّن لنا في القرآن أنه لا يهدى الكافرين، والقرآن لا يتناقض، بل يصدق بعضه بعضاً، فإذا أخبرنا في موضع أنه لا يهدي الكافرين، ثم أخبر في موضع آخر أنه هدى ثمود علمنا أنه إنما أراد المؤمنين من ثمود دون الكافرين. والوجه الآخر : أن الله عز وجل عنى قوماً من ثمود كانوا مؤمنين، ثم ارتدوا، فأخبر أنه هداهم فاستحبوا بعد الهداية الكفر على الإيمان، وكانوا في حال هداهم مؤمنين . فإن قال قائل معترضاً في الجواب الأول: كيف يجوز أن يقول: ﴿فَهَدَيْنَاهُمْ﴾ ويعني المؤمنين من ثمود، ويقول: ﴿فَاسْتَحَبُّوا﴾ يعني الكافرين منهم وهم غير مؤمنين ؟ يقال له: هذا جائز في اللغة التي ورد بها القرآن أن يقول : ﴿فَهَدَيْنَاهُمْ﴾ ويعني المؤمنين من ثمود، ويقال: ﴿فَاسْتَحَبُّوا﴾ يعني الكافرين منهم، وقد ورد القرآن بمثل هذا قال الله عز وجل: ﴿ وَمَا كَانَ الله لِيُعَذِّبَهُمْ وَأَنتَ فِيهِمْ ﴾ [الأنفال: ۳۳] يعني الكافرين، ثم قال تعالى: ﴿ وَمَا كَانَ اللهُ مُعَذِّبَهُمْ وَهُمْ يَسْتَغْفِرُونَ ﴾ [الأنفال: ۳۳] يعني المؤمنين، ثم قال: ﴿وَمَا لَهُمْ أَلَّا يُعَذِّبَهُمُ اللهُ ﴾ [الأنفال : ٣٤] يعني الكافرين، ولا خلاف عند أهل اللغة في جواز الخطاب بهذا أن يكون ظاهره لجنس والمراد به جنسان، فبطل ما اعترض به المعترض، ودل على جهله .
248 - Question: And if a questioner asks about the saying of Allah Almighty: 'And as for Thamud, We guided them, but they preferred blindness over guidance' [Fussilat: 17], and says: Were not Thamud disbelievers while Allah informed that He guided them? The answer regarding this verse is in two aspects: One of them: That Thamud were of two factions, disbelievers and believers, and it is they whom Allah informed that He saved with Salih in His saying, exalted: 'We saved Salih and those who believed with him by mercy from Us' [Hud: 66]. So those whom Allah Almighty meant from Thamud that He guided them are the believers, not the disbelievers; because Allah Almighty has clarified for us in the Qur'an that He does not guide the disbelievers, and the Qur'an does not contradict itself, but rather parts of it confirm other parts. So when He informed us in one place that He does not guide the disbelievers, then informed in another place that He guided Thamud, we know that He meant only the believers from Thamud, not the disbelievers. And the other aspect: That Allah Almighty meant a people from Thamud who were believers, then apostatized, and He informed that He guided them, and they preferred, after guidance, disbelief over faith, and they were at the time of His guiding them believers. So if one says objecting regarding the first answer: How is it permissible that He says 'We guided them' meaning the believers from Thamud, and says 'but they preferred' meaning the disbelievers from among them while they were not believers? It is said to him: This is permissible in the language in which the Qur'an was revealed: that He says 'We guided them' and means the believers from Thamud, and says 'they preferred' meaning the disbelievers from them. And the Qur'an has come with such usage. Allah Almighty said: 'And Allah would not punish them while you are among them' [Al-Anfal: 33] meaning the disbelievers, then He said: 'And Allah would not punish them while they seek forgiveness' meaning the believers, then He said: 'But why should Allah not punish them' [Al-Anfal: 34] meaning the disbelievers. And there is no disagreement among linguists about the permissibility of speech of this kind: that its apparent form refers to a genus while the intended meaning is two genera. So what the objector objected with is invalid, and it indicates his ignorance.

Commentary
This passage is an objection-and-answer regarding the verse about Thamud. The questioner argues that Thamud were disbelievers yet Allah says He guided them, which seems to contradict the principle that Allah does not guide disbelievers. The author provides two answers: First, Thamud consisted of two groups – believers and disbelievers – and the guidance refers only to the believers, as evidenced by the verse in Hud (11:66) where Allah saves the believers with Salih. The Qur'an does not contradict itself; so the guidance verse must refer to believers. Second, even if taken as referring to the whole group, it could be that some were believers who later apostatized; they were guided when they were believers, then after guidance they chose disbelief. Then a further objection arises: how can the pronoun 'them' in 'We guided them' refer to believers while 'they preferred' refers to disbelievers from the same group? The author refutes this by citing linguistic precedent from Surah Al-Anfal (8:33-34), where a single pronoun refers to different groups in the same passage. Linguists agree that such usage is permissible where the apparent form covers a whole category but the intended referents are multiple subgroups. Thus the objection is baseless.

Misconception & Correction
Misconception: Some think that Allah's guidance of Thamud contradicts His statement that He does not guide disbelievers, leading to a perceived Qur'anic inconsistency.
Correction: The guidance in the Thamud verse refers specifically to the believers among them, not the disbelievers, as explained by Imam Ashari's two answers and supported by other Qur'anic verses and linguistic principles.
