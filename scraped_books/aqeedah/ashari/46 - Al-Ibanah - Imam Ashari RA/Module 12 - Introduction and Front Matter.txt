The Opening Praise and Testimony of Faith
Imam al-Ash'ari's opening praise and testimony of faith.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand how Imam al-Ash'ari's opening praise establishes the core theological principles of tawhid, divine attributes, and prophethood, setting the stage for his defense of Sunni orthodoxy.

Core Idea
God's transcendence and omnipotence are the foundation of all Islamic theology.

Imam al-Ash'ari begins his seminal work Al-Ibanah with a profound praise of God that is far from routine. This opening khutbah deliberately affirms key Ash'ari doctrines: God's absolute oneness, His eternal attributes, His complete control over creation, and His all-encompassing knowledge. It also includes testimony of faith in God and His Prophet. This passage is not merely ceremonial; it preemptively refutes deviant beliefs and establishes the theological framework for the entire book.

Guided Path
1
Read the passage and identify its four main sections
The passage is divided into four parts: praise of God, seeking help and forgiveness with shahadah, seeking guidance, and testimony to Muhammad. Read each part carefully.
Notice the author's lineage and honorifics before the praise.
2
Focus on the description of God's attributes
Pay attention to how the author describes God's attributes as eternal and uncreated. Look for phrases like 'لم يزل بصفاته' and 'قادراً قديراً'.
This is a direct refutation of those who claim attributes are created.
3
Analyze the testimony of faith
The shahadah here emphasizes God's all-encompassing knowledge and His measurement of all things. See how it connects to Quranic verses about the clear Book and knowledge of the unseen.
This establishes God's omniscience as a core doctrine.

In the name of God, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful. And by Him we seek help. And He is sufficient for me, and He is the best disposer of affairs. The master, the sheikh, the imam, the scholar Abu al-Hasan Ali ibn Isma'il ibn Ali ibn Abi Bishr al-Ash'ari al-Basri, may God have mercy on him, said: 1. Praise be to God, the One, the Unique, the Mighty, the Glorious, who is unique in oneness, glorified in magnificence, whom the attributes of servants do not reach. He has no likeness nor equal. He is the Originator and the Restorer, the Doer of what He wills. He is exalted above taking a companion or children, and sanctified above touching women. He has no slip that can be mentioned, nor any limit by which an example can be made for Him. He has never ceased to be powerful with His attributes, eternally knowing and aware. His knowledge preceded all things, His will permeated them, and the hidden matters are not concealed from Him, nor do the vicissitudes of time change Him. No weariness or fatigue touched Him in creating anything that He created, nor did exhaustion or hardship affect Him. He created things by His power, and directed them by His will, and subdued them by His might, and humbled them by His glory. So the arrogant became low before His greatness, and the self-important submitted to the glory of His lordship. And those who doubt are cut off from penetrating deeply into His knowledge. Necks are humbled to Him, and the minds of the intelligent are perplexed in His dominion. The seven heavens stand by His command, the earth as a resting place is settled, the firm mountains are fixed, the winds that bring rain blow, the clouds move in the sky, and the seas remain within their boundaries. He is God, the One, the Subduer. The powerful submit to Him, the exalted are humble before Him, and all beings serve Him willingly or unwillingly. 2. We praise Him as He praised Himself, and as He deserves and merits, and as His creation praised Him. And we seek His help, the help of one who has entrusted his affair to Him, and [we are] among those who praise Him from all [people]. There is no refuge nor escape from Him except to Him. And we seek His forgiveness, the seeking of one who admits his sin and acknowledges his mistake. And we bear witness that there is no god but God alone, with no partner, affirming His oneness and sincere to His lordship, and that He knows what the hearts conceal and what the innermost thoughts contain, and what souls hide, and what the seas store, and what secrets cover, and what the wombs diminish and increase, and everything with Him is by measure. No word is hidden from Him, and no absent thing escapes Him. Not a leaf falls but He knows it, nor a seed in the darkness of the earth, nor anything moist or dry, but is in a clear Book. And He knows what the doers do, and what the returners return to. 3. And we seek guidance from God by guidance, and ask Him for success in avoiding error. 4. And we bear witness that Muhammad is His servant, His prophet, and His messenger to His creation, and His trustee.

Commentary
This passage is the opening khutbah (sermon) of Imam al-Ash'ari's book Al-Ibanah. It serves as an introduction, beginning with the basmalah and seeking help from God, then identifying the author with his full lineage and honorifics. The core of the passage is a lengthy and elaborate praise of God (hamd) that affirms core Ash'ari theological principles: God's oneness and uniqueness (al-ahad, al-wahid), His transcendence above all attributes of creatures, His lack of any partner, father, son, or female contact, His eternal and uncreated attributes (power, knowledge, will), His absolute control over creation (power, will, might), and His complete knowledge of all things, both apparent and hidden. The praise then transitions into a declaration of seeking help and forgiveness, followed by the shahadah (testimony of faith) that there is no god but God alone, with emphasis on His oneness and lordship, and His all-encompassing knowledge. There is also an affirmation of predestination (everything with Him is by measure) and a reference to the Quranic verse about the clear Book. Finally, the author testifies that Muhammad is God's servant, prophet, messenger, and trustee. This introduction establishes the theological foundation of the book: a firm commitment to tawhid, divine attributes, and the prophethood of Muhammad, setting the tone for the rest of the work which defends Sunni orthodoxy.

Misconception & Correction
Misconception: The opening khutbah is just a formal blessing without theological content.
Correction: Imam al-Ash'ari deliberately negates anthropomorphism and affirms eternal attributes, making it a doctrinal statement.

Key Takeaway
Imam al-Ash'ari's opening establishes God's absolute oneness, eternal attributes, and all-encompassing knowledge as the bedrock of Islamic belief.
