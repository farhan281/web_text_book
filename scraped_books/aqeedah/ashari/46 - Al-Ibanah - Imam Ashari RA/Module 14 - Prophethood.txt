Testimony of Prophethood and Authority of Quran and Sunnah
Ash'ari testifies to Muhammad's prophethood, extols the Quran, and emphasizes following the Sunnah.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the Sunni testimony of Muhammad's prophethood and the fundamental authority of the Quran and Sunnah in Islam.

Core Idea
Belief in the Prophet, Quran, and Sunnah is the foundation of guidance.

Imam al-Ash'ari presents a profound testimony of the Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) and the divine scriptures. This passage not only affirms his prophethood but also elevates the Quran as the unassailable word of God and the Sunnah as its necessary complement. As you read, notice how the author weaves together praise of the Prophet, description of the Quran's perfection, and Quranic commands to follow the Sunnah.

Guided Path
1
Read the Arabic passage slowly
Read the Arabic text once without stopping. Pay attention to the rhythm and the shift from praising the Prophet to describing the Quran and then quoting verses.
2
Identify the three segments
The passage has three main parts: (1) testimony of prophethood, (2) praise of the Quran, (3) command to follow the Sunnah with Quranic evidence.
3
Reflect on the Quranic verses
Notice the four verses quoted about obedience to the Messenger. They show that the Sunnah is an authoritative source of Islamic law.
4
Consider the implications
Reflect on the statement that the Quran contains knowledge of the first and the last, and that holding fast to it saves one. This establishes the Quran as the primary source of guidance.

Commentary
This passage is the testimony of prophethood and an extended eulogy of the Prophet Muhammad, followed by a description of the Quran and the necessity of adhering to the Sunnah. The author begins by asserting Muhammad's servitude, prophethood, and messengership. He lists the miraculous signs and wonders given to him. He emphasizes that the Prophet fulfilled his mission completely, fought against obstinacy, and brought the truth until his death. Then he describes how through the Prophet, Allah made the religion clear, dispelling darkness and doubts. He extols the Quran as a divine book beyond falsehood, containing all knowledge, and as the straight path and firm rope of Allah. He then cites several Quranic verses commanding obedience to the Prophet and referring disputes to Allah and His Messenger, underscoring the authority of the Sunnah. This passage establishes the foundational Sunni belief in the complete authority of the Quran and Sunnah.

Learning Objective

Core Idea
The Prophet was sent with clear proofs to guide humanity and fulfill God's word.

Imam al-Ash'ari begins his exhortation by praising the Prophet Muhammad's role as the final messenger. He emphasizes that the Prophet was sent with overwhelming signs and that he fulfilled his duty perfectly, thereby completing God's command. This passage establishes the authority of the Prophet as the guide for all.

Commentary
This passage is an exhortation by Imam al-Ash'ari on the obligation to follow the Quran and Sunnah and a warning against those who abandon them. The passage begins by praising the Prophet Muhammad's mission: he was sent with clear proofs and overwhelming signs, fulfilled God's command, and guided the people to truth. Al-Ash'ari then highlights the role of the Prophet in explaining the laws and rulings of Islam, dispelling ignorance and doubts. The Quran is described as a mighty book that contains knowledge of all things and is the straight path and strong rope of God; clinging to it ensures salvation, while opposing it leads to error. Al-Ash'ari then cites multiple Quranic verses commanding obedience to the Prophet and adherence to his Sunnah, emphasizing that the Prophet's words are revelation and must be followed. The second part of the passage (section 5) condemns those who abandon the Sunnah and blindly follow their ancestors, accusing them of fabricating lies against God and going astray. Finally, al-Ash'ari delivers a warning about the deceptive nature of this world, citing the Quranic parable of life being like vegetation that withers away, and reminds the audience that worldly joys are temporary and will be followed by hardship. The passage ends abruptly at a verse citation, but the overall message is clear: hold fast to the Quran and Sunnah, beware of blind imitation, and do not be deceived by this world.

In His revelation, He sent him to His creation with the radiant light, the luminous lamp, the manifest proofs, the brilliant evidences and signs, and the overwhelming wonders. He delivered to them the messages of God, and he advised His creation, and he strove in God with true striving, and fought against the people of obstinacy until the word of God, mighty and sublime, was fulfilled, and His command became manifest, and the people submitted to the truth entirely until certainty came to him, neither faltering nor falling short. So may the blessings of God be upon him, a guide to guidance, a clarifier against error and blindness, and upon his pure family, and upon his chosen companions, and upon his pure wives, the mothers of the believers. Through him, God made known to us the laws (shara'i'), the rulings (ahkam), the lawful and the unlawful, and through him He explained to us the legislation of Islam, until the dense darkness was cleared from us, and doubts were removed from us through him, and obscurities were unveiled from us, and clear proofs became apparent to us through him. And he brought us a mighty Book: 'Falsehood cannot come to it from before it or behind it; a revelation from the Wise, the Praiseworthy' (Fussilat 42). In it is gathered the knowledge of the first and the last, and through it He perfected the obligations and the religion. It is the straight path of God and His strong rope. Whoever holds fast to it is saved, and whoever opposes it goes astray and errs, and falls into ignorance. And God urged in His Book adherence to the Sunnah of His Messenger, for He, may He be exalted, said: 'And whatever the Messenger gives you, take it, and whatever he forbids you, refrain from it' (al-Hashr 7). And He, may He be exalted, said: 'Let those who oppose his command beware lest a trial befall them or a painful punishment befall them' (al-Nur 63). And He said: 'If they had referred it to the Messenger and to those in authority among them, those who deduce it from them would know it' (al-Nisa' 83). And He said: 'And whatever you differ over, its judgment belongs to God' (al-Shura 10). He said: 'To the Book of God and the Sunnah of His Prophet.'

And He said: 'Nor does he speak from desire. It is only a revelation revealed' (al-Najm 3-4). And He said: 'Say: It is not for me to change it of my own accord. I only follow what is revealed to me' (Yunus 15). And He said: 'The only saying of the believers when they are called to God and His Messenger to judge between them is that they say: We hear and we obey' (al-Nur 51). So He commanded them to hear His words and obey His command and beware of opposing Him. And He said: 'Obey God and obey the Messenger' (al-Nisa' 59). So He commanded them to obey His Messenger, peace be upon him, just as He commanded them to obey Him, and He called them to adhere to the Sunnah of His Prophet, just as He commanded them to act upon His Book.

'They have gone astray, and they were not guided' (al-An'am 140). I advise you, servants of God, to fear God, and I warn you against this world, for it is sweet and green, it deceives its people and deludes its inhabitants. Whoever was in it in joy, it brought him after it a lesson; and whoever it gave from its pleasures a bellyful, it brought upon him from its hardships a backful. Commentary
This passage is an exhortation by Imam al-Ash'ari on the obligation to follow the Quran and Sunnah and a warning against those who abandon them. The passage begins by praising the Prophet Muhammad's mission: he was sent with clear proofs and overwhelming signs, fulfilled God's command, and guided the people to truth. Al-Ash'ari then highlights the role of the Prophet in explaining the laws and rulings of Islam, dispelling ignorance and doubts. The Quran is described as a mighty book that contains knowledge of all things and is the straight path and strong rope of God; clinging to it ensures salvation, while opposing it leads to error. Al-Ash'ari then cites multiple Quranic verses commanding obedience to the Prophet and adherence to his Sunnah, emphasizing that the Prophet's words are revelation and must be followed. The second part of the passage (section 5) condemns those who abandon the Sunnah and blindly follow their ancestors, accusing them of fabricating lies against God and going astray. Finally, al-Ash'ari delivers a warning about the deceptive nature of this world, citing the Quranic parable of life being like vegetation that withers away, and reminds the audience that worldly joys are temporary and will be followed by hardship. The passage ends abruptly at a verse citation, but the overall message is clear: hold fast to the Quran and Sunnah, beware of blind imitation, and do not be deceived by this world.

The Obligation to Follow the Sunnah
Al-Ash'ari cites multiple Quranic verses commanding obedience to the Prophet and adherence to his Sunnah.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the Quranic evidence al-Ash'ari provides for the obligation to obey the Prophet and adhere to his Sunnah.

Core Idea
The Quran commands obedience to the Prophet as it commands obedience to God.

Al-Ash'ari now cites a series of Quranic verses that explicitly establish the authority of the Sunnah. He shows that obeying the Prophet is part of obeying God and that opposing his command invites punishment. This lesson presents the scriptural foundations for the binding nature of the Sunnah.

The Objection About Dhihar and Its Answer
Al-Ash'ari answers the objection about dhihar and refutes the Mu'tazila by analogy.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand how al-Ash'ari defends prophetic knowledge by distinguishing between rulings that are not yet required and eternal truths, using the case of dhihar.

Core Idea
Prophets need not know what is not yet required; but eternal truths must be known.

Al-Ash'ari has argued that prophets know all necessary truths about Allah, including the impossibility of seeing Him. An objector raises a point: if the Prophet Muhammad did not know the ruling of dhihar before revelation, maybe Moses could also be ignorant of the impossibility of vision. This passage shows how al-Ash'ari carefully distinguishes between rulings that are not yet legislated and eternal truths about Allah.

Guided Path

2

Al-Ash'ari turns the objection back: the Mu'tazila claim vision is always impossible, so Moses should have known. If he didn't, they'd be claiming superior knowledge.

Reflect on the distinction
Consider the key difference: a new ruling vs. an eternal truth. This distinction is crucial for defending prophetic infallibility.

Commentary
This passage handles an objection raised against the previous proof. The objector says: 'You (the Ash'aris) know today the ruling of dhihar (a pre-Islamic form of divorce that was later legislated), but the Prophet Muhammad did not know it before it was revealed. Similarly, perhaps Moses did not know the ruling regarding the vision of Allah at the time of his request.' Al-Ash'ari answers by distinguishing the two cases. The ruling of dhihar was not yet legislated at the time of the Prophet, so he was not required to know it. Once it was revealed, he learned it. In contrast, the Mu'tazila claim that the vision of Allah was always impossible, and thus Moses, as a prophet, was obligated to know this at the time of his request. If he did not know it and they do, then they are claiming to be more knowledgeable than Moses, which is unacceptable. The key difference is that for dhihar, the ruling did not yet exist, so ignorance was natural; but for vision, the Mu'tazila assert it was always impossible, so ignorance would be a deficiency. Therefore, their objection fails.

Imam al-Ash'ari uses a dilemma based on two prophetic examples to argue for predestination.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand how Imam al-Ash'ari uses the examples of Prophet Muhammad and Prophet Joseph to argue that God does not grant the same strengthening to disbelievers, thereby supporting the doctrine of predestination (qadar).

Core Idea
God's strengthening of prophets proves that disbelief persists only because He wills it.

Imam al-Ash'ari presents a powerful argument grounded in two Quranic verses. By examining the special strengthening (tathbit) given to Prophet Muhammad and the proof (burhan) given to Prophet Joseph, he constructs a dilemma that forces his opponents to either accept predestination or face a logical contradiction. This passage is a masterclass in using scriptural evidence to defend a theological position.

قيل له : نعم. فإن قالوا فما أنكرتم أن يكون قوله تعالى: (هُدًى [؟]) أراد به هدى لهم ولغيرهم. قيل لهم إن معنى قول الله عز وجل : ( إِنَّمَا تُنذِرُ مَنِ اتَّبَعَ الذِكْرَ ﴾ [يس:۱۱] إنما أراد به ينتفع بإنذارك من اتبع الذكر، وقوله تعالى: (إِنَّمَا أَنتَ مُنذِرُ مَن يَخْشَاهَا ﴾ [النازعات : ٤٥] أراد أن الإنذار ينتفع بـه من يخشى الساعة، ويخاف العقوبة فيها، وإن الله عز وجل قد أخبر في موضع آخر من القرآن أنه أنذر الكافرين، فقال: ﴿إِنَّ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا سَوَاءٌ عَلَيْهِمْ ءَ أَنذَرْتَهُمْ أَمْ لَمْ تُنذِرْهُمْ لَا يُؤْمِنُونَ ﴾ [البقرة: ٦] وهذا هو خبر عن الكافرين، وقال تعالى : ( وَأَنذِرْ عَشِيرَتَكَ الأقربين ) [الشعراء: ٢١٤] وقال تعالى: (أَنذَرْتُكُمْ صَاعِقَةً مِّثْلَ صَاعِقَةِ عَادٍ وَثَمُودَ ﴾ [فصلت: ۱۳] وهذا خطاب للكافرين فلما أخبر الله عز وجل في آيات من القرآن أنه أنذر الكافرين، كما أخبر الله في آيات من القرآن أنه أنذر من يخشاها وأنذر من اتبع الذكر، وجب بالقرآن أن الله قد أنذر المؤمنين والكافرين فلما أخبرنا الله أنه هدى للمتقين وعمى على الكافرين وأخبرنا أنه لا يهدي الكافرين، وجب أن يكون القرآن هدى للمؤمنين دون الكافرين .
247 - A question: If someone says: Did not Allah say: 'You only warn those who follow the Reminder' and 'You are only a warner of those who fear it'? And the Prophet warned those who follow the Reminder and those who do not, and those who fear and those who do not. It was said to him: Yes. Then they said: So what prevents you from acknowledging that His saying 'guidance [unknown]' meant guidance for them and others? It was said to them: The meaning of Allah's saying 'You only warn those who follow the Reminder' is that only those who follow the Reminder benefit from your warning. And His saying 'You are only a warner of those who fear it' means that the warning benefits those who fear the Hour and dread the punishment therein. And Allah has informed elsewhere in the Qur'an that He warned the disbelievers, saying: 'Indeed those who disbelieve, it is the same to them whether you warn them or do not warn them, they will not believe.' And this is news about the disbelievers. And He said: 'And warn your nearest kindred.' And He said: 'I have warned you of a thunderbolt like the thunderbolt of 'Ad and Thamud.' And this is an address to the disbelievers. So when Allah informed in verses of the Qur'an that He warned the disbelievers, just as He informed in verses that He warned those who fear it and those who follow the Reminder, it is established by the Qur'an that Allah warned the believers and the disbelievers. But when Allah informed us that it is guidance for the pious and blindness upon the disbelievers, and informed us that He does not guide the disbelievers, it must be that the Qur'an is guidance for the believers, not the disbelievers.

Commentary
This passage is an objection-and-answer. The opponent cites verses that seem to restrict the Prophet's warning to 'those who follow the Reminder' and 'those who fear it'. Yet the Prophet warned everyone. Al-Ash'ari explains that these verses refer to the benefit of the warning: only those who follow and fear actually benefit (yantafi'), not that the warning is limited to them. He then shows from multiple verses (al-Baqarah 2:6, ash-Shu'ara 26:214, Fussilat 41:13) that Allah explicitly states He warned the disbelievers. Therefore, the warning was universal. However, the Qur'an itself is described as 'guidance for the pious' and 'blindness for the disbelievers', and Allah states He does not guide disbelievers. Consequently, the Qur'an is guidance only for believers, even though the warning reached all. This reconciles the apparent contradiction by distinguishing between the act of warning (which is universal) and the effect of guidance (which is specific).

Misconception & Correction
Misconception: The verses 'إنما تنذر من اتبع الذكر' mean the Prophet only warned those who follow the Reminder and fear the Hour, implying his mission was not universal.
Correction: Al-Ash'ari clarifies that these verses describe the benefit of the warning (only the fearful benefit) not the scope of the warning (which is universal).
