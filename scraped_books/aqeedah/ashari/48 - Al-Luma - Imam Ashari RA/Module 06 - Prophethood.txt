The Difference Between the Questions of Ibrahim and Musa
Distinguishing between the implicit and explicit requests of two prophets to see Allah.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the distinction between the implicit and explicit requests of prophets Ibrahim and Musa to see Allah, and the theological reasoning behind Allah's response.

Core Idea
Prophets ask with wisdom; Ibrahim's implicit request teaches divine transcendence.

In this passage, the author compares two Quranic verses where prophets ask to see something of Allah. While both requests involve seeing, the manner and context differ significantly. Understanding this difference clarifies important principles about Allah's transcendence and how prophets communicate with Him.

Guided Path
1
Read the two Quranic verses
Read the Arabic text and translation of both verses: Ibrahim asks 'Show me how You give life to the dead' and Musa asks 'Show me [Yourself] that I may look at You.'
Notice the difference in wording: Ibrahim asks 'how' while Musa asks directly to see Allah.
2
Identify the key terms: ta‘rīḍ and taṣrīḥ
The passage states that Ibrahim's question was ta‘rīḍ (implicit) and Musa's was taṣrīḥ (explicit). Understand that this distinction is based on the prophets' states: Ibrahim was settled, Musa was agitated.
'Ta‘rīḍ' means indirect expression, while 'taṣrīḥ' means direct expression.
3
Understand the theological explanation
Ibrahim asked 'how', so Allah showed him the living (the result) but not the act of giving life, because giving life is an attribute of Allah. Allah answered by indication as Ibrahim asked by indication.
This preserves the transcendence of Allah's attributes – they cannot be seen directly.
4
Reflect on the final verse
Allah concludes with 'And know that Allah is Almighty, Wise.' The gloss explains that al-‘Azīz means the invincible, implying that Allah's power and wisdom are beyond comprehension.
This reinforces that Allah's responses are always appropriate and wise.

Commentary
This passage discusses the two Quranic accounts of prophets asking to see something of the divine. The author first cites the verses: Ibrahim asks 'My Lord, show me how You give life to the dead' (Q2:260), and Musa asks 'My Lord, show me [Yourself] that I may look at You' (Q7:143). He then states that both are questions, but Ibrahim's is a question of one who is settled (mutamakkin), while Musa's is of one overcome and agitated (ṣāḥib ghalaba wa hayajān). Consequently, Musa's request was explicit (tasrīḥ) and Ibrahim's was implicit (taʿrīḍ). The author explains that because Ibrahim asked 'how', Allah showed him the manner (kayfiyya) of the living (al-muḥyā) but not the manner of giving life (al-iḥyā'), since giving life is an attribute of Allah and the one who gives life is His power. Thus Allah answered him by indication (ishāra) as he asked by indication. Finally, Allah added the words 'And know that Allah is Almighty, Wise', glossing al-ʿAzīz as al-manīʿ (the invincible). The passage concludes with Abu al-Hasan affirming that this is correct speech.

The Bodily Ascension and Vision of Allah
Al-Ash'ari affirms the physical ascension and the Prophet's vision of Allah.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand that al-Ash'ari affirms the Prophet's Night Journey as a physical event, not a dream, and that he saw his Lord.

Core Idea
The Mi'raj was physical, not symbolic, and included direct vision of Allah.

This lesson explores the first set of beliefs regarding the Prophet's miraculous Night Journey. Al-Ash'ari insists on its physical reality and the Prophet's direct encounter with Allah. The passage distinguishes this event from a mere dream and emphasizes the honor bestowed upon the Prophet.

Guided Path
1
Read the source passage
Read the Arabic and translation carefully. Notice the negation 'لا رؤيا' which explicitly denies a dream interpretation.
2
Identify the affirmations
List the specific actions: physical ascension, seeing Allah, speaking, being entrusted, duties imposed, permissions granted.
These are six distinct favors.
3
Reflect on the significance
Consider why al-Ash'ari emphasizes physicality. This counters interpretations that reduce the event to a vision.
The bodily nature asserts the Prophet's honor.
