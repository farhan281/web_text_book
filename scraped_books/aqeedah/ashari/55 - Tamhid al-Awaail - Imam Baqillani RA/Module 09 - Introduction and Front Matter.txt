God's Uniqueness and Absolute Sovereignty
Baqillani's opening doxology affirming God's incomparability and sovereign control.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand how Baqillani opens his work by affirming God's absolute uniqueness, sovereignty, and control over all creation as a theological foundation.

Core Idea
God's unmatched sovereignty is the bedrock of correct belief.

Baqillani begins not with abstract argument but with praise — a praise that already teaches theology. Every attribute he ascribes to God here dismantles a false belief. As you read, notice how each phrase builds a picture of a God who has no equal, no partner, and no limitation.

Guided Path
1
Read the Arabic praise aloud
Let the rhythm and repetition sink in. The author is doing theology even in his choice of words.
2
Identify the negations and affirmations
Baqillani says what God is not (no likeness, no opponent) and what God does (guides, misleads, owns, creates). List them.
3
Reflect on the implications for sovereignty
If no one can reverse God's decree, what does that mean for human power? The passage answers implicitly.
4
Connect to the purpose of the book
Think about why a book on foundational beliefs would start with this doxology. It sets the tone for everything that follows.

Commentary
This passage is the opening preface of Baqillani's work. It serves as an introductory statement of purpose and theological orientation. The author begins with the basmala, invoking God's name, and then immediately praises God using epithets that emphasize His omnipotence and uniqueness: He is the suppressor of falsehoods, the guide to truth, and the misleader of those who deviate. The praise continues with affirmations of God's incomparability—no likeness, no opponent—and His absolute sovereignty over creation: He is the owner, creator, restorer, enricher, impoverisher, and trier of all things. No one can overrule His decree or command. This establishes the theological foundation of the book: God's unparalleled majesty and control. Next, Baqillani sends blessings upon Prophet Muhammad, his family, companions, and successors, praising Muhammad as the clarifier of the path. He then asks God for success in following divine commands and the example of the righteous predecessors, and for protection from deviation. Finally, he transitions with 'Amma ba'd' to state the occasion for writing: he has recognized the preference (i.e., the desire) of a certain Amir (prince) for this work. He praises the Amir's piety, intellect, resolve, and humility before God, and expresses hope that the Amir will attain the good that God facilitates. The passage as a whole sets the tone for the treatise, blending theological assertion with a personal acknowledgment of patronage.

Purpose of the Book: The Amir's Request
Baqillani's transition to the book's purpose: blessings, prayer, and acknowledgment of the Amir's role.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the occasion for writing this book: the author's response to a pious Amir's request, and the author's prayer for guidance.

Core Idea
This book was born from a request for truth, not from a desire for fame.

After establishing theological foundations, Baqillani turns to the immediate occasion. He prays for the Prophet's intercession, asks for divine success, and then acknowledges that a noble Amir — a ruler of piety and intellect — asked for this work. This context matters: it shows that Islamic theology was not merely academic but responded to real spiritual needs.

Guided Path
1
Read the supplication for the Prophet and oneself
Notice how Baqillani asks for success in following the righteous predecessors and protection from deviation.
2
Identify the role of the Amir
Find where he says 'إيثار سيدنا الأمير'. What does that tell you about the book's origin?
3
Reflect on the author's hopes for the Amir
Baqillani prays for the Amir's continued honor and piety. How does that connect to the purpose of the book?

The Opening Doxology: Praise and Blessings
Baqillani praises Allah and sends blessings on the Prophet, embedding key theological claims.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand how Baqillani establishes the theological foundation by praising Allah and sending blessings on the Prophet, and the key attributes he emphasizes.

Core Idea
Praise of Allah is the bedrock of all theology.

Every work of kalam begins with praise. Baqillani's opening is not mere formality—it embeds core creedal statements about Allah's uniqueness and absolute sovereignty. This lesson unpacks the theological claims woven into his words of praise.

Guided Path
1
Read the passage carefully
Read the Arabic passage once without analysing. Then read the translation. Notice the flow from praise to blessings.
The passage is structured as a single extended sentence.
2
Identify the attributes of Allah
List the divine attributes mentioned: no likeness, no opponent, Owner of creation, no restrainer, etc. Notice how they affirm His uniqueness and power.
3
Notice the structure
Observe the transition from hamd (praise) to salawat (blessings) and then supplication for guidance.
This mirrors traditional Islamic scholarly introductions.
4
Reflect on the purpose
Why start with these assertions? They set the stage for the entire book's arguments.
These statements are not decorative; they are foundational.

In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful. Allah is the Helper by His mercy. Praise be to Allah, the Suppressor of falsehoods and the Refuter of errors, the Guide of whom He chooses to the straight path, and the Misleader of the one who deviates from the right course and turns away from clear proofs and luminous evidences. I praise Him with the praise of one who acknowledges that there is no likeness equal to Him, no opponent to contend with Him, and that He is the Owner of creation, its Originator, its Restorer, its Beginner, its Enricher, its Empowerisher, its Merciful, its Tester. There is no owner above Him to restrain Him, nor any subduer to forbid and command Him. All creation is in His grasp, turning according to His will, acting within His bounds and ordinances. There is no annuller of His decree, no repeller of His command, no objection from any creature to His judgment and decree. And I beseech Him to send blessings upon His chosen one among His creation, Muhammad, the Seal of the Prophets and the Imam of the God-fearing, as he clarified the path and illuminated the proof, and upon his brothers among the messengers, the members of his pure household, his chosen companions, and those who follow them thereafter. And I ask Him for success in attaining what He has commanded us, following the righteous predecessors of the nation of our Prophet, and turning us away from inclining to the one who deviates from our religion and attacks our creed. To proceed: I have known the preference of our master the prince—may Allah prolong his life and perpetuate His blessings upon him through adherence to piety and adherence to the best path—and through his guidance and direction. And may Allah make for him, from his abundant intellect and determination, an admonisher, and from his high ambition and nobility, a restrainer and a watcher—his humility before his Lord, exalted, and his submission to His obedience as one who hears and obeys, until he attains, through what He facilitates for him from that, the highest stations of nearness and honor. (He requested) the composition of a comprehensive, concise book containing what is needed for uncovering the meaning of knowledge (al-ilm), its divisions, methods, levels, types of known things, realities of existents, mention of proofs for the temporality (huduth) of the world, establishing its Originator (muhdith), that He is unlike His creation, what must be affirmed of Him—His oneness, that He is living, knowing, powerful in His eternity, and what runs along those lines of the attributes of His essence; that He is just and wise in what He originated from His creations without any need from Him for them, nor any mover, caller, thought, or causes that called Him to bring them into existence—exalted is He above that—and the permissibility of sending messengers to His creation and intermediaries between Him and His servants, and that He has done that, and cut off the excuse in requiring belief in them by the signs He manifested through them and the miracles (mu'jizat) through which He established their truthfulness; and (the book will contain) discourses on all the people of the religions opposing the religion of Islam—Jews, Christians, Magians, Dualists, materialists, and astrologers. And we will follow that with mention of the disagreements between the People of Truth (ahl al-haqq) and the People of Anthropomorphism (tajsim) and Likening (tashbih), the People of Qadr, the Mu'tazila, the Rafida, and the Khawarij. And mention of summaries of the merits of the Companions, the virtues of the four Imams, establishing their imamate, the interpretation regarding what occurred between them, and the obligation of showing allegiance to them. I will spare no effort in what our master the prince—may Allah guard his life and elevate his rank—desires: conciseness, precise expression of meanings, proofs, and phrases, and following a path that aids reflection on what this book contains and removes doubts and uncertainties. And I, with the might and power of Allah, hasten to comply with what he has outlined and stop at it. And to Allah, may His remembrance be exalted, I beseech for good success and supply of support and guidance.

Commentary
This passage is the author's preface and book outline. It begins with the basmala and hamd, where Baqillani praises Allah as the suppressor of falsehoods and guide to the straight path. He emphasizes Allah's unique attributes: no likeness, no opponent, absolute ownership over creation, and no one can annul His decree or object to His judgment. He then sends blessings on the Prophet Muhammad and his family and companions, asking for success in following the righteous predecessors.
