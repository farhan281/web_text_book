Ali's Speech on Martial Experience
Al-Baqillani quotes Ali's speech defending his martial prowess and wisdom.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand how Imam al-Baqillani uses Ali's own words to refute a claim about his lack of war strategy, highlighting a key lesson about leadership and obedience.

Core Idea
Ali's experience proves his strategy; disobedience hindered his command.

This lesson examines a passage from al-Baqillani's work where he cites a famous speech of Ali ibn Abi Talib. Quraysh had claimed Ali was brave but lacked war strategy. Ali's response not only refutes this with his lifelong experience but also points to a deeper issue: a commander cannot succeed if not obeyed.

Guided Path
1
Read the passage
Read the Arabic passage once, then read the English translation. Let the author's own words come through.
2
Understand the context
Identify what Quraysh said about Ali and how the author introduces Ali's speech.
Quraysh claimed Ali was brave but lacking in war strategy.
3
Analyze Ali's response
Notice how Ali refutes the claim: by pointing to his decades of experience (from under twenty to over sixty), and by asserting that command is impossible without obedience.
The final phrase 'لا إمرة لمن لا يطاع' is key.

Commentary
This passage presents a proof of Ali's military capability through his own words. The author notes that Ali's bravery, effectiveness, and knowledge of army management are well-known. Then he quotes Ali's speech responding to Quraysh's claim that he was brave but lacked war strategy. Ali refutes this by stating his extensive experience: he started military life before twenty and was now over sixty, implying decades of experience. He then adds a crucial point: 'There is no command for one who is not obeyed'—suggesting that his alleged lack of strategy was actually due to disobedience from his troops, not his own deficiency.

Misconception & Correction
Misconception: That Ali admitted lack of war strategy
Correction: Ali refuted this claim, pointing to his long experience and the fact that disobedience from his forces made command ineffective.

Checkpoint

B) Because he was not obeyed

Key Takeaway
Ali's speech demonstrates that a leader's effectiveness depends on being obeyed, not just personal capability.

How This Lesson Applies — to MY Life?
?
When have you failed to obey a rightful authority—whether a parent, teacher, or leader—because of your own pride or laziness? How did that disobedience harm not just your own growth but the community's well-being? Will you seek forgiveness and commit to obedience where it is due?
?
Ali dedicated decades to fighting for truth from his youth to old age. What area of Islamic knowledge or good deed have you pursued with similar consistency? If none, what is holding you back from making a long-term commitment to something that pleases Allah?
?
You often blame others for your lack of influence when calling to good. But like Ali, could the real obstacle be that people are not obeying you because of your own character or approach? What change in yourself would make others more willing to follow your guidance?
