Imam al-Razi: Name, Lineage, and Titles
Enumeration of al-Razi's names and titles.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the full name, lineage, and various titles and kunyas of Imam Fakhr al-Din al-Razi.

Core Idea
Al-Razi's many titles reflect his towering stature in Islamic scholarship.

This passage introduces us to the identity of Imam Fakhr al-Din al-Razi. It details his full name, traces his lineage back to Abu Bakr al-Siddiq, and lists the many honorifics he earned, showing how his contemporaries recognized his brilliance.

Guided Path
1
Read the passage carefully
Read the Arabic passage once to get a sense of the names and titles. Then read the translation.
Notice the structure: first his name, then lineage, then titles, then kunyas.
2
Identify the different identifiers
Look for his given name, his lineage, his titles (alqab), and his kunyas. Make a mental list.
For example, 'Fakhr al-Din' is a title, 'Ibn al-Khatib' is a kunya.
3
Reflect on the significance of multiplicity
Consider why the author mentions so many titles and names. What does it indicate about al-Razi's status?
The passage explicitly states it shows his brilliance and high rank.

ترجمة الإمام فخر الدين الرازي
اسمه ولقبه ونسبه :
تكاد المصادر التي رجعت إليها في ترجمة الإمام فخر الدين الرازي تجمع على أن اسمه محمد بن عمر بن الحسن بن علي القرشي الطبرستاني، الرازي المولد. الشافعي القرشي البكري التميمي من ذرية أبي بكر الصديق له، وقد حمل في عصره لقب الإمام حتى صار عَلَمًا عليه . وليس هذا هو اللقب الوحيد الذي لقب به الرازي. بل لقب بألقاب أخرى مثل : شيخ الإسلام والرازي، وفخر الدين
أما عن الكنى التي كني بها الرازي فهي كثيرة أشهرها ابن الخطيب أو ابن خطيب الري، كما ورد في معظم كتب التراجم، وربما اقتصرت بعض هذه الكتب عليها كما كني بأبي الفضل وأبي عبد الله، وأبي المعالي
وأكثر ما اشتهر به هذا المفسر هو فخر الدين الرازي، أو الفخر الرازي ولا شك أن في تعدد هذه الألقاب والكنى للإمام الرازي - كما يرى أحد الباحثين - ما يدل بوضوح على مدى نبوغ ،صاحبها، وعلو مكانته في العلوم الدينية
مولده ووفاته :
تكاد أغلب المصادر لا تقطع بتاريخ محدد لميلاد فخر الدين الرازي، ولكن القول الراجح أنه ولد في عام ٥٤٤ ، وقد ولد بمدينة الري (وهي أقرب إلى خراسان، وهي مشهورة في إيران بالقرب من (طهران ومنها إلى ما وراء النهر، فناظر علماءها ثم حدث له ما حدث بخوارزم ليعود ثانية إلى الري مسقط رأسه بعد ترحاله ومناظراته لعلماء الفرق المختلفة وتوفي بهراة سنة ٦٠٦هـ .
وكان الفخر الرازي فقيرًا في أول أمره وبعد ذلك تبدل حاله من الفقر إلى الغنى، ومرجع ذلك - كما يرى الصفدي - بسبب أحد الأطباء الأثرياء الذي كان له بنتان فتزوجهما ابنا فخر الدين ثم مات الطبيب الثري فانتقلت أمواله ونعمه إلى الرازي صاحب غزنة من الأموال العظيمة.
وكان للرازي له اليد البيضاء في الوعظ باللسانين العربي والفارسي، وكانت النزعة الصوفية في نفسه لا تقل - بحال من الأحوال - عن النزعة الكلامية، بل إنه قد ندم على انغماسه في العلوم الكلامية والفلسفية بعد أن أحس أنها لا تشفي عليلا ، ولا تروي غليلا فاتجه اتجاها روحانيا وتصوفياً إلى جانب حرصه على العلوم الشرعية، وكثيرًا ما كان يعتريه الوجد في خطبه فيؤثر في سامعيه، وكان يرى العقيدة السديدة في الاتجاهين معا على السواء.
Translation of Imam Fakhr al-Din al-Razi. His name, title, and lineage: The sources that I have referred to in the biography of Imam Fakhr al-Din al-Razi almost agree that his name is Muhammad ibn 'Umar ibn al-Hasan ibn 'Ali al-Qurashi al-Tabaristani, born in al-Rayy. He is al-Shafi'i, al-Qurashi, al-Bakri, al-Tamimi from the progeny of Abu Bakr al-Siddiq. He carried in his era the title of Imam until it became a sign (identifier) for him. This is not the only title by which al-Razi was called. Rather he was called by other titles such as: Shaykh al-Islam, al-Razi, and Fakhr al-Din. As for the surnames by which al-Razi was known, they are many, the most famous being Ibn al-Khatib or Ibn Khatib al-Rayy, as mentioned in most biographical books, and perhaps some of these books were limited to them. He was also called by Abu al-Fadl, Abu 'Abd Allah, and Abu al-Ma'ali. The most famous by which this exegete is known is Fakhr al-Din al-Razi, or al-Fakhr al-Razi. There is no doubt that in the multiplicity of these titles and surnames for Imam al-Razi - as one researcher sees - it clearly indicates the extent of his brilliance and his high status in religious sciences. His birth and death: Most sources are not definitive about a specific date for the birth of Fakhr al-Din al-Razi, but the preponderant opinion is that he was born in the year 544. He was born in the city of al-Rayy (which is closer to Khurasan, and it is famous in Iran as being near Tehran, and from it to Transoxiana). He debated the scholars of (various regions) then what happened in Khwarazm happened, to return again to al-Rayy, his birthplace, after his travels and debates with the scholars of different sects. And he died in Herat in the year 606 AH. Al-Fakhr al-Razi was poor at first, then his condition changed from poverty to wealth. The reason for that - as al-Safadi sees - was due to a wealthy physician who had two daughters, and the two sons of Fakhr al-Din married them. Then the wealthy physician died, so his wealth and bounties were transferred to al-Razi, the master of Ghazna, from great wealth. Al-Razi had a beneficent hand (skill) in preaching in both Arabic and Persian, and the Sufi inclination in himself was in no way less than the kalam inclination. Rather he regretted his immersion in kalam and philosophical sciences after he felt that they do not cure a sick person nor quench a thirst, so he turned a spiritual and Sufi direction alongside his care for the religious sciences. Often he would be overtaken by ecstasy in his sermons and affect his listeners. He held the correct creed in both directions equally.

Commentary
This passage provides a biographical introduction to Imam Fakhr al-Din al-Razi. It begins with his full name and lineage, tracing his descent from Abu Bakr al-Siddiq. It notes his titles, especially 'Imam' which became a distinctive label for him, and his other honorifics such as Shaykh al-Islam, al-Razi, and Fakhr al-Din. The passage also mentions his common surnames, most notably Ibn al-Khatib, and his kunyas. It emphasizes that the multiplicity of his titles indicates his high scholarly status. Then the text discusses his birth and death: he was born in Rayy around 544 AH and died in Herat in 606 AH. It recounts a story about his financial situation: initially poor, he became wealthy because his two sons married the daughters of a wealthy physician who later died, leaving his wealth to al-Razi. The passage then describes al-Razi's dual talents: eloquence in preaching in both Arabic and Persian, and his strong Sufi inclination. It notes that he regretted his immersion in kalam (dialectical theology) and philosophy, finding them unsatisfying, and turned towards spirituality and Sufism while still valuing the religious sciences. The passage concludes by stating that he held the correct creed in both directions (apparently referring to both kalam and Sufism).

Al-Razi: Birth, Death, and Wealth
Biographical account of al-Razi's birth, death, and wealth.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the details of Imam al-Razi's birth, death, and the story of how he became wealthy.

Core Idea
Al-Razi's life journey from poverty to wealth mirrors his intellectual journey.

After establishing his name and titles, the passage now turns to the key events of al-Razi's life: his birth in Rayy around 544 AH, his death in Herat in 606 AH, and an interesting account of how he became wealthy through his sons' marriages to the daughters of a wealthy physician.

Guided Path
1
Read the passage and note the dates
Read the Arabic and translation, paying attention to the year of birth (544 AH) and death (606 AH) and the places: Rayy and Herat.
2
Understand the change in fortune
The passage says he was poor at first, then became wealthy. Find the reason given.
The reason is tied to his sons' marriages.
3
Reflect on the narrative
Consider how this biographical detail helps us understand al-Razi's life context.
It shows that wealth came later, possibly allowing him more time for scholarship.

Al-Razi's Character: Preaching, Sufism, and Kalam
Description of al-Razi's preaching, Sufi inclination, and dissatisfaction with kalam.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the dual nature of al-Razi's character: his preaching skills, his Sufi inclination, and his regret over kalam and philosophy.

Core Idea
Al-Razi combined profound kalam reasoning with a deep Sufi spirituality.

The final part of the biography reveals al-Razi's inner struggle. Despite being a master of kalam, he regretted his deep involvement in it and turned toward Sufism, all while maintaining his commitment to religious sciences and his powerful preaching in both Arabic and Persian.

Guided Path
1
Read the passage and identify the two sides

Note the phrase 'النزعة الصوفية' and 'النزعة الكلامية'.
2
Understand his regret
Why did he regret his immersion in kalam and philosophy? The passage says he felt they did not heal or quench thirst.
This is a key insight into his spiritual development.
3
Reflect on the balance
The passage says he held the correct creed in both directions. What does this mean?
It suggests he saw value in both kalam and Sufism when pursued properly.

Misconception & Correction
Misconception: Ali was only known for bravery and wisdom, not comprehensive scholarship.
Correction: Al-Razi demonstrates that Ali was the fountainhead of usul al-din, tafsir, fiqh, eloquence, grammar, spirituality, and bravery, showing his all-encompassing mastery.

Checkpoint
According to al-Razi, which theological schools ultimately trace their lineage back to Ali?
A) Only the Mu'tazila
B) Only the Shi'a
C) All major schools: Mu'tazila, Ash'ariyya, Shi'a, and Khawarij
D) None of the above

Key Takeaway
Al-Razi's argument conclusively shows that Ali was the master of all Islamic sciences, with every theological school and discipline ultimately originating from him.

How This Lesson Applies — to MY Life?
?
If Ali is the fountainhead of every Islamic science, which of those sciences have you personally neglected or undervalued? What concrete step will you take this week to engage with that neglected discipline?
?
Al-Razi proves that all theological schools trace back to Ali, yet today Muslims are fiercely divided over sectarian lines. How does this passage expose the emptiness of your own bias against other groups? What will you do to reconcile with a fellow Muslim of a different school?
?
Ali's mastery was comprehensive — usul, tafsir, fiqh, grammar, spirituality, and bravery. You have likely only focused on one or two areas. If you were to face Allah today, could you honestly say you pursued knowledge with the same breadth? What will you change starting now?
