The Author's Opening: Praise, Testimony, and the Foundation of Islam
Author opens with praise and introduces the hadith of the five pillars.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand how Imam Ghazali opens his work with praise of Allah, testimony of faith, and introduces the hadith that Islam is built on five pillars as the basis for the book's discussion of Islamic creed.

Core Idea
The book's foundation is the hadith: Islam is built on five.

Imam Ghazali begins his treatise on the fundamentals of faith with a traditional Islamic opening: praise of Allah, testimony of His oneness and transcendence, and blessings upon the Prophet. He then immediately anchors the entire discussion in a famous hadith from Bukhari, where the Prophet states that Islam is built on five pillars. This hadith serves as the organizing principle for the coming exposition of Islamic beliefs and practices.

Guided Path
1
Read the opening supplication and praise
Notice the author begins with a Quranic verse (18:10) asking for mercy and guidance. Then he offers praise (hamd) to Allah, describing His attributes of power, will, and judgment.
This is a standard scholarly opening.
2
Identify the testimonies of faith
The author declares the shahada (there is no god but Allah) and affirms Allah's transcendence beyond human limitations. He also sends blessings on the Prophet and his companions.
3
Locate the hadith introduction
The author states that 'from the signs of truth' is that Islam is built on five, and attributes this to the Prophet's hadith narrated by Bukhari from Ibn Umar. The hadith text is cut off here.
This hadith will be the backbone of the book.

Praise and Tawhid: Allah's Transcendence
Ghazali opens with hamd and tawhid, affirming tanzih and divine sovereignty.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the opening praise of Allah and the key attributes of tawhid and tanzih as articulated by Ghazali.

Core Idea
Allah alone deserves praise, and He is beyond all limits, directions, and partners.

Ghazali begins his work with a powerful declaration of praise and tawhid. This opening is not mere convention; it establishes the foundation that Allah is unique in His essence, attributes, and actions. The passage affirms His absolute transcendence (tanzih) while emphasizing that He is the only object of worship.

Guided Path
1
Read the passage carefully
Read the Arabic opening and its translation, noticing the structure of praise followed by testimony.
2
Identify the two shahadas
The passage contains two testimonies: the shahada of tawhid and the shahada of Muhammad. Focus first on the shahada of tawhid.
3
Understand tanzih
Notice how Ghazali describes what Allah is not: no partner, no likeness, no delimitation by boundaries or directions.
Tanzih is negative theology within the bounds of revelation.
4
Reflect on divine will
The passage emphasizes that Allah creates and guides by His will, and His decree cannot be repelled.

Commentary
This passage is the traditional opening of the work, comprising praise of Allah (hamd), testimony of tawhid (shahada), and salawat on the Prophet. Ghazali begins by invoking a Quranic verse (18:10) as a dedication. He then declares that praise belongs solely to Allah, establishing His uniqueness in essence and action. He affirms that Allah creates by His power and guides whom He wills to tawhid, emphasizing His absolute will and decree. The passage then describes Allah's transcendence (tanzih): He is free from any opposite, partner, likeness, or similarity. His greatness cannot be encompassed; He is not delimited by spatial boundaries or directions, nor confined by names and attributes—though names and attributes are affirmed, they do not limit Him. Sights cannot perceive Him in this world, but He perceives all sights, referencing the verse 'لا تدركه الأبصار وهو يدرك الأبصار' (6:103). After the praise, Ghazali bears witness to Muhammad's servanthood and prophethood, listing his perfections: conveyance of the message, fulfillment of trust, sincere advice to the community, and true jihad. He sends blessings upon the Prophet and his companions, highlighting their qualities of understanding, truth, and correctness. The section closes with 'وبعد' (amma ba'd), the traditional transition to the main discourse.

Commentary

He describes the book's qualities: it explains the pure meanings of tawhid with radiance and inspiration, clarifies its obscure issues, details its concise points, expands its difficult principles, and opens its closed beneficial pathways.
