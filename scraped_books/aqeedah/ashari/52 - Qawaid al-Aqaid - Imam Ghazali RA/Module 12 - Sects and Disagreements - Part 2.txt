Necessity Overrides Ideal Conditions for Imamate
Argues that the imamate is valid despite missing conditions when necessary to avoid greater harm.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the principle that necessity can validate an otherwise deficient imamate to avoid greater harm.

Core Idea
Necessity overrides the ideal conditions for imamate.

The tenth principle addresses a critical scenario: what happens when a qualified imam is unavailable and appointing a deficient one is the only way to avoid chaos? Imam al-Ghazali argues that the imamate remains valid under such necessity. This passage presents the reasoning and analogy that support this rule.

The Murji'a felt secure because they believed faith alone saves. Be honest: do you sometimes feel your faith is enough without struggling to obey? If you were to die tonight, would your deeds match your claims of faith? Which specific sin have you been excusing because you 'believe'? Write it down and set a concrete step to change it before the next Friday prayer.
?
The Murji'a used general verses to support their ease. Strip away your excuses: your iman is not a shield against Allah's justice if you deliberately persist in disobedience. What is one area where you have been relying on Allah's mercy without accompanying effort to change? Today, identify that area and take one tangible action—whether it's praying on time, giving up a harmful habit, or seeking forgiveness from someone you wronged—as a sign of sincere repentance.
