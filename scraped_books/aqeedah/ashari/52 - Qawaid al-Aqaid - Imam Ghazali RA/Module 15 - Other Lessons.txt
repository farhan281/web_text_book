The Degrees of Unveiling and Striving
Al-Ghazali describes the degrees of spiritual unveiling.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand how spiritual unveiling has degrees based on striving and inner purification, and how these degrees correspond to varying levels of certainty and proximity to Allah.

Core Idea
Unveiling deepens with striving and purity.

Al-Ghazali begins by referring to the secret of tawḥīd and explains that its unveiling has degrees, just like knowledge in worldly sciences varies with effort and innate ability. This lesson explores the spiritual dimensions of striving and purification that lead to degrees of proximity to Allah.

Guided Path
1
Read the passage carefully
Read the Arabic passage about degrees of unveiling and the analogy to sciences. Note the key terms mujāhada, yaqīn, and qurb.
The passage uses a comparison to illustrate the variability of spiritual insight.
2
Identify the spiritual states described
Al-Ghazali describes those who have attained unveiling: they perceive neediness, hear all things testifying to tawḥīd, and witness Allah with their hearts. Notice the progression from external to internal perception.
The degrees of proximity (qurb) depend on certainty (yaqīn) and purity of heart.
3
Reflect on the analogy
Why does al-Ghazali compare spiritual unveiling to sciences like medicine and law? Consider how both require effort and innate disposition.
This shows that unveiling is not arbitrary but follows a cause-and-effect relationship.

وانكشاف ذلك السر (۱) ، بل تلك الأسرار له درجات بحسب درجات وفي المجاهدة ودرجات الباطن ، في النظافة والطهارة عما سوى الله تعالى الإستضاءة بنور اليقين (۲) ، وذلك كتفاوت الخلق في أسرار الطب والفقه وسائر العلوم ، إذ يختلف ذلك بإختلاف الإجتهاد ، وإختلاف الفطرة، في الذكاء (٤) تلك الدرجات فكذلك هذه (ه) والفطنة (٣) ، وكما لا تنحصر (۳) لائحة ، وعاينوا حالات الإفتقار إلى الله عز وجل واضحة ، وسمعوا جميعها تدل على التوحيد راشدة ناصحة ، ثم رأوا الله عز وجل بإيمان قلوبهم ، وشاهدوه بغيب أرواحهم ، ولاحظوا جلاله وجماله بخفى أسرارهم ؛ وهم مع ذلك في درجات القرب على قدر حظ كل واحد منهم في اليقين ، وصفاء القلب . وأما الصديقون : فهم أهل المرتبة الرابعة في التوحيد ، وهؤلاء رأوا الله عز وجل، ثم رأوا الأشياء بعد ذلك فلم يروا في الدارين غيره ، ولا أطلعوا في الوجود على سواه ؛ والمريدون في الغالب لا بد لهم أن يحلو في المرتبة الثالثة ، وهي توحيد المقربين ، ومنها ينتقلون وعليها يعبرون إلى المرتبة الرابعة . وأما المرادون: فهم في الغالب مبتدئون بمقامهم الأخير، وهي المرتبة الرابعة ، ومتمسكنون فيها ، ومن أهل هذا المقام يكون القطب ، والأوتاد ، والبدلاء ، ومن أهل المرتبة الثالثة ، يكون النقباء والنجباء والشهداء والصالحون
And the unveiling of that secret (1), nay, those secrets have degrees according to the degrees of striving (mujāhada) and the degrees of the inner self, in purity and purification from other than Allah Almighty, and illumination by the light of certainty (2). And that is like the variation of people in the secrets of medicine, jurisprudence, and other sciences, for that differs according to the difference in effort (ijtihād) and the difference in innate disposition, in intelligence and acumen (3). And just as those degrees are not limited (4) listed, they observed the states of neediness towards Allah Almighty clearly, and they heard all of them indicating tawḥīd, rightly and sincerely. Then they saw Allah Almighty through the faith of their hearts, and witnessed Him through the unseen of their souls, and beheld His majesty and beauty through the hidden aspects of their secrets; and they, with that, are in degrees of proximity (qurb) according to the share of each one in certainty (yaqīn) and purity of heart. And as for the ṣiddīqīn (truthful ones): they are the people of the fourth station (martaba) in tawḥīd. They saw Allah Almighty, then they saw things after that, and did not see in the two abodes other than Him, nor did they look upon anything else in existence. And the murīdūn (seekers) generally must pass through the third station, which is the tawḥīd of the muqarrabīn, and from it they move on and cross over to the fourth station. And as for the murādūn (desired ones): they generally begin at their final station, which is the fourth station, and reside in it. And from the people of this station are the Quṭb (Axis), the Awtād (Pegs), the Abdāl (Substitutes); and from the people of the third station are the Nuqabā' (Leaders), the Nujabā' (Nobles), the Shuhadā' (Martyrs), and the Ṣāliḥūn (Righteous).

Commentary
This passage is a classification of the degrees of unveiling and the ranks of believers. Al-Ghazali begins by referring to the 'secret' mentioned earlier, and states that it has degrees corresponding to the degrees of spiritual striving (mujāhada) and inner purification. He compares this to the varying levels of expertise in sciences like medicine and jurisprudence, which depend on effort and innate intelligence. Then he describes those who have reached these unveilings: they perceive their dependency on Allah and hear all things testifying to His oneness. They then see Allah with the faith of their hearts and witness Him through the unseen of their souls, and behold His majesty and beauty in their innermost secrets. They are at varying degrees of proximity (qurb) based on their certainty (yaqīn) and purity of heart. Next, al-Ghazali introduces the ṣiddīqīn as the people of the fourth station (martaba) in tawḥīd. They see Allah first, and then see everything else only as reflecting Him, perceiving nothing other than Him in either this world or the next. He then explains that the seekers (murīdūn) must generally pass through the third station (that of the muqarrabīn) before reaching the fourth. In contrast, the desired ones (murādūn) begin at the fourth station directly. Finally, he classifies the spiritual figures: from the fourth station come the Quṭb, Awtād, and Abdāl; from the third station come the Nuqabā', Nujabā', Shuhadā', and Ṣāliḥūn.

Key Terms
مجاهدة (mujāhada)
يقين (yaqīn)
قرب (qurb)

Misconception & Correction
Misconception: That spiritual unveiling is granted all at once without preparation.
Correction: Al-Ghazali emphasizes that unveiling has degrees and depends on striving and purification.

Checkpoint
According to the passage, on what do the degrees of proximity (qurb) depend?
A) Only on innate intelligence
B) On certainty (yaqīn) and purity of heart
C) On external knowledge of the law
D) On the number of spiritual practices performed

Key Takeaway
Spiritual unveiling follows a path of increasing purification and effort, leading to deeper certainty and closeness to Allah.

How This Lesson Applies — to MY Life?
?
You have read that unveiling has degrees based on striving and purification. Look at your own life: where is your striving directed? Is your struggle for Allah or for worldly gain? What would change if you truly pursued purity of heart with the same effort you give to other pursuits?
?
The passage says those who attain unveiling 'saw Allah through the faith of their hearts' and 'beheld His majesty and beauty through the hidden aspects of their secrets.' Do you live as though your heart can truly witness Allah? What is blocking that vision in your daily life? Be specific about the impurity or negligence you need to remove.
?
Al-Ghazali compares spiritual degrees to differences in medicine and law – some are more advanced due to effort and innate disposition. Are you content with your current level of closeness to Allah, or do you feel a painful gap between where you are and where you could be? What concrete step will you take today to increase your striving and purify your heart?

The Ranks of the Siddiqun and Spiritual Hierarchy
Al-Ghazali defines the siddiqun and classifies spiritual ranks.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the distinction between the third station (muqarrabun) and fourth station (siddiqun) in tawḥīd, and the classification of spiritual figures such as the Quṭb, Awtād, and Abdāl.

Core Idea
The siddiqun see only Allah; the muqarrabun are a preparatory stage.

Al-Ghazali now turns to the people of the highest stations: the truthful ones (siddiqun) who have attained the fourth station of tawḥīd, seeing nothing but Allah. He contrasts them with the muqarrabun of the third station, and explains the different spiritual ranks that emerge from these stations.

Guided Path
1
Read the passage on the siddiqun
Start with 'وأما الصديقون' and read through to the end. Note the definition of siddiqun: they see Allah first, then see everything else only in Him.
This station is the highest in tawḥīd according to this classification.
2
Distinguish between muridun and muradun
Al-Ghazali explains the different paths of seekers (muridun) and desired ones (muradun). Muridun must pass through the third station, while muradun begin at the fourth.
This shows that spiritual progress varies; some are chosen from the start.
3
Classify the spiritual figures
Identify which figures belong to the fourth station (Quṭb, Awtād, Abdāl) and which to the third (Nuqabā', Nujabā', Shuhadā', Ṣāliḥūn).
This hierarchy reflects different levels of proximity to Allah.
