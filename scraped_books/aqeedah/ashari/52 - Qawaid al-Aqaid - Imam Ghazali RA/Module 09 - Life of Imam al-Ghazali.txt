Faith that fills the heart must lead to action; the Prophet's seclusion was divine preparation.

This passage from Imam al-Ghazali's Qawa'id al-'Aqa'id describes the natural outcome of true creed: it compels action. Commentary
This passage is a narrative describing the effect of firm creed on the early Muslims and the Prophet's preparation for his mission. It begins by stating that when the creed fully possessed the companions—their hearing, sight, hearts, and minds—they openly proclaimed what they were commanded and called people to it as bearers of good news and warners, so that people would have no excuse after the messengers. The author then links this divine pattern to the Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him), stating that he followed this sunna. The Prophet engaged in contemplation and seclusion in the cave of Hira, worshipping for many nights, returning to Khadija for provisions, until the truth came to him there. The revelation began with commands: 'O you wrapped in garments, arise and warn' (74:1-2), 'And warn your closest kindred' (26:214), and 'O Messenger, convey what has been revealed to you from your Lord' (5:67). The Prophet then proclaimed the truth loudly and conveyed to the people the call to monotheism, as in the verse: 'O People of the Book, come to a word common between us and you: that we worship none but Allah, and that we associate nothing with Him, and that none of us take others as lords besides Allah' (3:64). The passage emphasizes that the Prophet's preparation was divinely ordained and that the call to tawhid was central.
