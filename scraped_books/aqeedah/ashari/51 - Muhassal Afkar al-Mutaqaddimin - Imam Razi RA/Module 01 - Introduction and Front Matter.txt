The True Title of al-Muhassal and Its Scope
Editor enumerates Razi's works and reveals the full title of al-Muhassal.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the authentic full title of al-Muhassal and what it reveals about the book's comprehensive scope as a compendium of earlier and later thinkers.

Core Idea
Al-Muhassal is not just a summary; it is a compendium of earlier and later thinkers across disciplines.

This passage from the editor's introduction clarifies the real name and ambition of Imam al-Razi's celebrated work, commonly called al-Muhassal. Understanding its full title helps us appreciate that al-Muhassal is not merely a personal digest but a comprehensive survey of the arguments of philosophers, theologians, and scholars from different periods. Let's read the editor's own words.

Guided Path
1
Read the passage and translation
Read the Arabic passage once without analysing, then read the literal translation. Let the editor's own description come through.
Notice that the editor lists previous works before arriving at al-Muhassal.
2
Identify the full title
Find the phrase where the editor gives the true full title of al-Muhassal. Copy it in Arabic.
The full title is longer than just 'al-Muhassal'.
3
Understand the scope indicated
The full title mentions three groups: 'al-'Ulama', al-Hukama', al-Mutakallimun'. Think about why Razi would include all three.
This shows the book is interdisciplinary.
4
Contrast with the common name
Reflect on why the book is commonly known simply as 'al-Muhassal' and what might be lost by using only the short name.
The common name is a shorthand; the full title reveals ambition.

And we have, with the help of Allah and His good guidance, begun by printing the book 'Lawami' al-Bayyinat Sharh Asma' Allah Ta'ala wa al-Sifat'. And it is the famous book known as Sharh Asma' Allah al-Husna by al-Razi, seeking blessing through Allah's beautiful names and supreme attributes. And we followed it with the book 'I'tiqadat Firaq al-Muslimin wa al-Mushrikin', and we placed upon it a subtle commentary which we named 'al-Murshid al-Amin ila I'tiqadat Firaq al-Muslimin wa al-Mushrikin'. And here is his fourth book, famously known as 'al-Muhassal', and its true name in international libraries is 'Muhassal Afkar al-Mutaqaddimin wa al-Muta'akhkhirin min al-'Ulama' wa al-Hukama' wa al-Mutakallimin'.

Commentary
This passage is the editor's description of the series of Razi's works he has published. He enumerates the first three: Lawami' al-Bayyinat (on Divine names), I'tiqadat Firaq al-Muslimin wa al-Mushrikin (with his commentary), and Ma'alim Usul al-Din. Then he arrives at the fourth, which is the present book. He points out that while it is popularly called 'al-Muhassal', its true full title is 'Muhassal Afkar al-Mutaqaddimin wa al-Muta'akhkhirin min al-'Ulama' wa al-Hukama' wa al-Mutakallimin' (The Compendium of the Thoughts of the Earlier and Later Scholars, Philosophers, and Theologians). The full title is significant because it indicates the work's scope: it summarizes not just Razi's own views but the arguments of many thinkers across disciplines and periods. The editor emphasizes this to clarify the book's ambition.

Correction: The full title reveals that al-Muhassal is a compendium of the thoughts of earlier and later scholars, philosophers, and theologians, not just Razi's own ideas.

?
Razi's compendium includes the thoughts of earlier and later thinkers from multiple disciplines. Are you open to learning from scholars across different fields and time periods, or do you confine yourself to a narrow set of views?

The Opening: Praise and Purpose of the Book
The author praises God and the Prophet, then states the request and purpose of this summary.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the author's purpose for writing this summary of kalam and the scope of its content.

Core Idea
This summary distills the essential principles of kalam without the branches.

Imam Razi begins his book with a traditional praise of God and the Prophet, establishing the tone of reverence. He then reveals the work's origin: a request from scholars for a concise summary of kalam that covers only the foundational principles and rules, leaving aside detailed branches. This lesson explores that introduction and sets the stage for the entire work.

Guided Path
1
Read the opening praise
Read the Arabic passage from the beginning through the praise of God and blessings on the Prophet. Notice the lofty descriptions of God's transcendence.
The author uses strong language to emphasize God's uniqueness.
2
Identify the request and purpose
Find where the author states the request from scholars and his response. Note what he says about the content: 'mukhtasar fi ilm al-kalam mushtamil ala ahkam al-usul wa al-qawaid'.
This is the key statement of the book's scope.
3
Distinguish principles from branches
Reflect on the contrast between 'usul wa qawaid' (principles and rules) and 'tafarI' wa zawaid' (branches and additions). Understand that this summary is selective.
The author deliberately omits detailed subsidiary discussions.
4
Note the author's humility
Observe how the author asks God for protection from error and for assistance in explanation. This shows his reliance on divine guidance.

Commentary
This passage is the opening of the book. It begins with the basmalah and a praise of God, extolling His transcendence and attributes. The author emphasizes God's uniqueness and independence from creation. Then he sends blessings upon the Prophet Muhammad. The main purpose is then stated: a group of scholars asked the author to write a summary of kalam focusing on the foundational principles and rules, leaving aside derived branches. The author complied and asks God for protection from error and for help in making the explanation clear.

Author's Preface and Purpose
Razi explains his motivation and dedicates his work.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the author's motivation for writing Talkhīṣ al-Muḥaṣṣal and the context of its dedication.

Core Idea
A good preface sets the stage for a fair theological inquiry.

This passage is the author's preface, where he criticizes previous works for bias and introduces his own effort to provide a clear, fair exposition. He also dedicates the book to a high-ranking patron, highlighting the importance of religious commitment.

Guided Path
1
Read the preface with context
Understand that this is not a doctrinal argument but an introduction. Read the Arabic and translation once to get the overall tone.
The author uses a poetic style; don't get bogged down in individual words.
2
Identify the author's critique
Find where Razi states that previous works were not fair or unbiased. What does he say about them?
Look for phrases like 'لم يجر أكثرهم على قاعدة الإنصاف'.
3
Note the dedication
The author dedicates the book to a patron. Who is that patron and what qualities does he praise?
The patron is described with many honorifics; focus on the reason for dedication.
4
Reflect on the tone
Consider the author's hope for approval and his acknowledgment of his own effort. How does this set the stage for the rest of the book?
