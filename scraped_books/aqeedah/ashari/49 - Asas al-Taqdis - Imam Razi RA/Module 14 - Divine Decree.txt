The Metaphorical Meaning of 'Finger' in Hadith
Razi explains the metaphorical basis of 'finger' as a symbol of divine power.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand why 'finger' in the hadith is a metaphor for God's complete power and control.

Core Idea
Finger symbolizes effortless power, not a physical limb.

Imam Razi begins by clarifying the correct metaphorical interpretation of the word 'finger' that appears in certain hadiths. He explains that just as a finger enables easy manipulation of an object, the term is used as a metaphor for God's complete power and control. This foundation is essential before delving into the deeper secret of divine decree.

Guided Path
1
Read the original Arabic passage
Read the short Arabic passage carefully. Notice how Imam Razi defines the literal basis of the metaphor before applying it to God.
The passage starts with 'والتأويل الصحيح فيه' and ends with 'القدرة الكاملة'.
2
Understand the analogy
The key idea: a finger easily secures and controls an object. This is extended to God's power — effortless and complete.
3
Identify the metaphorical term
The word 'finger' is a metaphor (كناية) for God's complete power. Recognize that it is not literal.
This corrects anthropomorphic misunderstandings.
4
Connect to the next lesson
This interpretation sets the stage for the deeper secret about qadar in the next passage.

والتأويل الصحيح فيه : إن الشيء الذي يأخذه الإنسان بأصابعه يكون مقدور قدرته . ومحل تصرفه على وجه السهولة ، من غير ممانعة أصلا . ولما كانت الإصبع سبباً لهذه المسكنة و القدرة ، جعل لفظ الإصبع كناية عن تلك القدرة الكاملة . إذا عرفت هذه المقدمة . فنقول : أما الحديث الأول ففيه سر لطيف . وذلك لأن المتصرف فى البدن ، هو القلب . والقلب لا ينفك عن الفعل وعن الترك . والفعل موقوف على حصول الدواعى إلى الفعل ، والترك موقوف على عدم حصول تلك الدواعى . ولا خروج عن هاتين الحالتين ، لأن الخروج عن طرفى النقيض محال . ثم إن حصول الداعي إلى الفعل من الله تعالى ، ولا حصول له من العبد . وإلا لافتقر العبد في تحصيل ذلك الداعي إلى داع آخر ، ويلزم التسلسل وهو محال . فثبت : أن القلب واقع بين هاتين الحالتين . فإن حصل فيه ما يدعوه إلى الفعل ، أقدم على الفعل . وإن لم يحصل فيه ذلك ، بقى على الترك . فحصول هاتين الحالتين في قلوب المؤمنين للفعل والترك، كالإصبعين المؤثرين في تقليب الأشياء . وتقليب القلب بسبب هاتين الداعيتين ، يشبه تقليب الشيء المأخوذ بالإصبعين من حال إلى حال . وكما أن الإنسان يتصرف في الشيء المأخوذ بإصبعه بتلك الأصابع ، فالحق سبحانه وتعالى يتصرف في قلوب عباده بواسطة خلق تلك الدواعى . وهذه النكتة هي السر الأعظم ، والقانون الأشرف في مسألة القضاء والقدر . وقد عبر النبي بهذه اللفظة الوجيزة ، والنكتة اللطيفة ، عن هذا السر اللطيف .
And the correct interpretation of it: The thing that a person takes with his fingers is under his power and the place of his action with ease, without any resistance. And since the finger is a cause for this subjugation and power, the word 'finger' was made a metaphor for that complete power. If you have understood this premise, we say: As for the first hadith, in it is a subtle secret. That is because the one who acts in the body is the heart. And the heart is never free from action and non-action. Action depends on the occurrence of motives towards action, and non-action depends on the non-occurrence of those motives. And there is no exit from these two states, because exiting from the two extremes of a contradictory pair is impossible. Then, the occurrence of the motive towards action is from Allah - exalted is He - and it does not occur from the servant. Otherwise, the servant would need another motive to obtain that motive, leading to an infinite regress, which is impossible. So it is established that the heart lies between these two states. If what calls it to action occurs in it, it proceeds to action. And if that does not occur in it, it remains in non-action. So the occurrence of these two states in the hearts of the believers for action and non-action is like two fingers effecting the turning over of things. And the turning of the heart due to these two motives resembles the turning of a thing taken by two fingers from one state to another. And just as a person acts on a thing taken by his fingers with those fingers, so the Truth - glorified and exalted is He - acts in the hearts of His servants by means of creating those motives. And this point is the greatest secret and the noblest principle in the matter of divine decree and destiny. And the Prophet expressed this subtle secret with this concise word and subtle point.

Commentary
This passage presents the correct metaphorical tafsir of 'finger' and then reveals a deeper secret about qadar. Imam Razi begins by explaining the metaphor: a finger is used to easily manipulate objects, so 'finger' is a metaphor for God's complete power and control. He then applies this to the first hadith. The secret: the heart is always between two states: action and non-action. Action requires a motive from God; without it, the heart remains still. If a motive were from the servant, it would cause an infinite regress. Thus, the two states (presence or absence of motive) in the heart are like two fingers turning objects. God creates motives in hearts, just as a person uses fingers. This is the essence of qadar: God's direct control over human actions through the creation of motives.

The Secret of Qadar: Creation of Motives
Razi reveals the secret of divine decree through the creation of motives in the heart.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the secret of divine decree: that motives for action are created by God alone, and this explains God's control over the heart.

Core Idea
God's creation of motives is the key to divine decree.

Building on the metaphor of 'finger' as power, Imam Razi now unveils the profound secret of qadar. He explains that the human heart is always between action and non-action, and that motives (دواعي) come only from God. This insight resolves the relationship between divine control and human responsibility, and it is the very secret the Prophet referred to in the hadith.

Guided Path
1
Read the Arabic passage on qadar
Read from 'إذا عرفت هذه المقدمة' to the end. Note the logical flow: heart, states, motives, divine creation.
This passage is longer and contains the main argument.
2
Understand the heart's two states
The heart is always in either a state of action or non-action. Action depends on the presence of a motive.
3
Learn why motives must come from God
If motives came from the servant, an infinite regress would occur. So motives are solely from God.
4
Appreciate the analogy with fingers
The two states (presence/absence of motive) are like two fingers turning the heart. Just as fingers manipulate objects, God manipulates hearts by creating motives.
5
Reflect on the secret of qadar
This is the greatest secret: God's direct creation of motives determines actions, but without coercion – it is a subtle point.
