The Second Opinion: Faith Does Not Require Knowledge of Transcendence
Razi presents the second opinion that knowledge of transcendence is not a condition for faith.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand why Imam Razi argues that ignorance of Allah's transcendence does not invalidate one's faith, based on the Prophet's practice.

Core Idea
The Prophet did not test people's knowledge of tanzih, so it is not a condition for faith.

In this passage, Imam Razi presents a powerful argument from prophetic practice. He contends that if knowing God's transcendence were a prerequisite for faith, the Prophet would have had to verify it in every person. Since he did not, we know it is not a condition. The passage shows how practical Sunnah can resolve theological disagreements.

Guided Path
1
Read the passage and translation
Read the Arabic passage and its English translation to grasp the argument.
Pay attention to the logical flow.
2
Identify the conditional reasoning
Notice the structure: 'if... then... but... therefore not.'
3
Reflect on the implication
Ask yourself: what does this teach about the relationship between correct belief (tanzih) and the validity of faith?
4
Consider the broader context
This is the second opinion; the first declared anthropomorphists disbelievers. How does this argument differ?

والقول الثاني إنا لا نكفرهم لأن معرفة التنزيه لو كانت شرطا لصحة الإيمان لوجب على الرسول صلى الله عليه وسلم أن لا يحكم بإيمان أحد إلا بعد أن يتفحص هل عرف الله تعالى بصفات التنزيه أولا وحيث حكم بإيمان الخلق من غير هذا التفحص علمنا أن ذلك ليس شرطا للإيمان
The second opinion: we do not declare them disbelievers, because if knowledge of transcendence were a condition for the soundness of faith, it would have been obligatory upon the Messenger (peace be upon him) not to judge anyone as a believer except after examining whether that person knew God Almighty through the attributes of transcendence or not. And since he judged people's faith without such examination, we know that that is not a condition for faith.
Source pages p. 259

Commentary
This passage presents the second opinion, which refrains from declaring anthropomorphists disbelievers. The proof is based on prophetic practice: if knowing God's transcendence were a prerequisite for valid faith, the Prophet would have had to verify this knowledge in every person before accepting their faith. Since he did not examine people in this way but accepted their faith, it follows that knowledge of tanzih is not a condition for faith. Thus, one can be a believer despite holding incorrect beliefs about God's attributes.

Misconception & Correction
Misconception: Some think that if someone holds an anthropomorphic view, they are automatically a disbeliever.
Correction: This argument shows that ignorance of tanzih alone does not nullify faith; the Prophet did not treat it as a disqualifier.

Checkpoint

B) The Prophet's practice of not testing people's knowledge of tanzih before accepting their faith.
C) The consensus of scholars that all Muslims are forgiven.
D) The impossibility of knowing God's essence.

Key Takeaway
The Prophet's practical treatment of faith shows that correct belief in divine transcendence is not a prerequisite for being a believer.

The Correct Opinion on Takfir and Intellectual Diversity
Editorial note affirming the second opinion and warning against hasty takfir.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand why the editor affirms the second opinion (that anthropomorphists should not be declared disbelievers) and the rationale based on variations in people's understanding.

Core Idea
Declaring a Muslim a disbeliever is serious because people's intellects are not equal.

This lesson examines an editorial note that endorses the second opinion on takfir of those who hold anthropomorphic beliefs. The note argues that making takfir is a grave matter, as God has not granted all people the same capacity for understanding. It reminds us to exercise caution and compassion when assessing others' faith, acknowledging the diversity of intellectual levels among believers.

Guided Path
1
Read the editorial note
Read the Arabic passage and its translation carefully. Note that this is a later addition, not from Imam al-Razi himself.
The note concludes with a prayer for forgiveness.
2
Identify the second opinion
Understand that the second opinion holds that anthropomorphists are not to be declared disbelievers due to their ignorance or misinterpretation.
3
Analyse the rationale
The note gives two reasons: takfir is serious, and people have varying levels of understanding. Reflect on how this applies to theological assessment.
Consider the implications for judging others' faith.
4
Understand the context
Recognize that this note is likely from a copyist or commentator, not the original author. This affects its authority but does not diminish its wisdom.
The lesson shows the importance of caution in takfir.

اعلم أن القول الثاني هو القول الصحيح لأن تكفير المسلم ليس هو بالشيء الهين لأن الله تعالى لم يجعل عقول الناس على درجة سواء في الفهم فمنهم العامي ومنهم العالم غفر الله لكاتبه وقارئه ومصححه ولمن قال آمين ولجميع المسلمين أجمعين

May God forgive its writer, reader, corrector, and those who say 'Amin', and all Muslims. Amen.
Source pages p. 260

Commentary
This passage is an editorial note affirming the second opinion (not declaring anthropomorphists disbelievers). It provides a rationale: takfir is serious because people vary in intellectual capacity and understanding; some are commoners, some are scholars. It concludes with a prayer for forgiveness for all involved in the book. This note likely originates from a copyist or commentator, not from Imam al-Razi himself.

Misconception & Correction
Misconception: Some may think that declaring someone a disbeliever is a simple personal judgment.
Correction: This note stresses that takfir is a serious matter that must consider the individual's level of understanding and should not be done hastily.

Checkpoint

A) Because most scholars hold it.
B) Because takfir is a serious matter and people's understanding differs.
C) Because the anthropomorphists later repented.
D) Because the first opinion was based on weak hadith.

Key Takeaway
The second opinion is correct because takfir is serious and people have varying levels of understanding, requiring caution and mercy.
