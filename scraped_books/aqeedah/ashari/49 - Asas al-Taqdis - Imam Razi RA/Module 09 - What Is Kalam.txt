From this, Razi draws a principle: ta'wil can yield certain knowledge (ma'lum) or conjecture (maznun). However, conjecture is impermissible in matters of knowing Allah, as previously argued—just as relying on a solitary report (khabar wahid) is impermissible in theology. He then presents the alternative view of the mutakallimun, who hold that detailed ta'wil is necessary because the Quran must be understandable, and ambiguous verses cannot be understood without interpretation. The passage ends with 'and Allah knows best,' indicating the issue is not definitively settled.
