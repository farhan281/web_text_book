Conclusion and Colophon of al-Iqtisad fi al-I'tiqad
Al-Ghazali concludes his book with a prayer and a note of completion.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the nature of Imam al-Ghazali's concluding statements, including his methodological reflection, humility, and the historical colophon details.

Core Idea
True scholarship balances brevity and clarity, seeking divine acceptance over human praise.

Imam al-Ghazali closes his seminal work on creed not with a doctrinal flourish, but with a humble prayer and a note on his methodology. He explains his deliberate avoidance of irrelevant verbosity and his focus on clear proofs. This conclusion reveals the author's character and his reliance on God's mercy for the acceptance of his efforts.

Guided Path
1
Read the complete passage
Read the Arabic passage once to get a sense of its flow, then read the English translation to understand its meaning.
Notice the mix of theological comment, prayer, and historical notes.
2
Identify the key components
Look for three parts: the final remark on eternity in the Fire, the methodological summary, and the colophon (date, reader, scribe).
The methodological summary highlights Ghazali's commitment to clarity and accessibility.
3
Reflect on the prayer and its significance
Consider why Ghazali asks God not to make the book a burden but to place it in the balance of good deeds. This shows his fear of accountability and hope for divine mercy.
This prayer is a model of scholarly humility.
4
Analyze the colophon details
Note the exact date, the name of the reader who corrected the text, and the scribe. These details ground the book in a specific historical context.
The year 517 AH is indicated by 'سبع عشرة وخمس'.

والخلود في النار .. نظر فقهي ، وهو المطلوب وحذفنا خاتمة الكتاب على مؤلفه ولنختم الكتاب بهذا ؛ فقد أظهرنا الاقتصاد في الاعتقاد ، الرحمة والسلام الحشو والفضول المستغنى عنه الخارج عن أمَّهاتِ العقائد وقواعدها ، واقتصرنا من أدلة ما أوردناه على الجلي الواضح الذي لا تقصر أكثر الأفهام عن دركه . فنسألُ الله تعالى ألا يجعله وبالاً علينا ، وأن يضعه في ميزان الصالحاتِ إذا رُدَّتْ أعمالنا إلينا ، بلطفِهِ وسعَةِ جوده . تم الكتاب المترجم بـ الاقتصاد في الاعتقاد » والحمد لله رب العالمين ، وصلواته على سيدنا محمد خاتم النبيين ، وعلى آله وسلامه . مئة . وذلك في السابع عشر من المحرم سنة سبع عشرة وخمس قرأه صاحبه الشيخ الفقيه أبو الحسن بن بكر الطنحي عليَّ بتفهم وتدبر وصححه حسب الإمكان والحمد لله وكتبه عبد المهيمن بن الحسين جعفر ، ومصلياً على نبيه محمد وآله ومسلماً تسليماً
And eternity in the Fire... is a juristic consideration, and that is what is sought. And we have omitted the conclusion of the book upon its author, and we will conclude the book with this: for we have presented moderation in belief, mercy and peace, removing irrelevant verbosity and superfluity external to the foundational principles of doctrines and their rules. And we have restricted ourselves from among the proofs we have mentioned to the clear and obvious that most intellects do not fall short of comprehending. So we ask God Almighty not to make it a burden upon us, and to place it in the balance of righteous deeds when our works are returned to us, by His grace and abundant generosity. The book titled 'al-Iqtisad fi al-I'tiqad' is completed. Praise be to God, Lord of the worlds, and His blessings be upon our master Muhammad, the seal of the prophets, and upon his family and peace. One hundred. That was on the seventeenth of Muharram in the year seventeen and five [?]. Its owner, the faqih Shaykh Abu al-Hasan ibn Bakr al-Tanahi, read it to me with understanding and reflection and corrected it as much as possible. Praise be to God. Written by 'Abd al-Muhaymin ibn al-Husayn Ja'far, praying upon his Prophet Muhammad and his family and granting peace profusely.

Commentary
This passage is the conclusion and colophon of Imam al-Ghazali's work 'al-Iqtisad fi al-I'tiqad'. It begins with a final remark about eternity in the Fire being a juristic consideration, then notes that the author has omitted the original conclusion of the book and instead concludes with this summary. He states that he has presented a balanced creed, avoiding superfluity, and focusing only on clear proofs accessible to most minds. He then prays to God that this work not be a burden but be accepted and placed in the balance of good deeds. The colophon records the completion of the book, with praise and blessings upon the Prophet. It then gives the date: the 17th of Muharram, year 517? (the text says 'seven and five', likely 517 AH). It also notes that the book was read and corrected by Shaykh Abu al-Hasan ibn Bakr al-Tanahi, and written by 'Abd al-Muhaymin ibn al-Husayn Ja'far.

Misconception & Correction
Misconception: Some may think that the phrase 'والخلود في النار .. نظر فقهي' is a doctrinal proposition about the eternity of punishment.
Correction: This is actually a concluding remark from a previous discussion, not a new theological stance. It signals that the preceding argument was based on legal reasoning, not a definitive creedal statement.
