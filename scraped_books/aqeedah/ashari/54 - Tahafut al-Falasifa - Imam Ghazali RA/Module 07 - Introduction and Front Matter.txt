Editor's Preface to the Second Edition
The editor lists three issues he will address in his introduction.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the three key issues the editor introduces to frame the second edition of al-Ghazali's Tahafut al-Falasifa.

Core Idea
An editor’s preface reveals the lens through which a classic work is presented.

Before diving into the main text, the editor of this second edition outlines his agenda. He highlights three critical aspects: the book's relationship to philosophy, al-Ghazali's intellectual honesty, and his lasting influence on modern thought. This preface sets the stage for the reader to approach the Tahafut with informed expectations.

Introduction to the Second Edition
It is important for me - as I present the second edition of the book al-Tahafut - to briefly point out the following issues:
First: The connection of the book al-Tahafut to philosophy.
Second: The extent of al-Ghazali's caution and his degree of honesty in depicting the opinions he will discuss.
Third: The echo of al-Ghazali's ideas in modern and contemporary philosophy:
(1) The position of Descartes, Hamilton, Russell, and the modern positivists, and al-Ghazali, on knowledge.
(B) The position of Descartes and al-Ghazali on revelation.
(C) The position of al-Ghazali and the modern positivists on experimental sciences.

Commentary
This passage is the editor's preface to the second edition of Tahafut al-Falasifa. He states his intention to highlight three issues concisely. The first issue concerns the relationship of the Tahafut to philosophy proper, the second evaluates al-Ghazali's intellectual caution and honesty in representing the views he critiques, and the third examines the influence of al-Ghazali's ideas on later Western and modern philosophers, specifically mentioning Descartes, Hamilton, Russell, and the positivists, with regard to knowledge, revelation, and experimental science. This preface sets the agenda for the introduction that follows.
