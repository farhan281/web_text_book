Ghazali on Takfir: The Three Issues
Ghazali responds to a question about takfir, limiting it to three issues.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand that Ghazali restricts takfir to three specific issues, beginning with the belief in the eternity of the world.

Core Idea
Takfir is not indiscriminate; it is limited to three foundational doctrines.

In this concluding passage of the Tahafut, Ghazali addresses a crucial question: Are the philosophers definitively disbelievers? He answers by narrowing takfir to three specific issues, the first being the eternity of the world. This passage establishes that not every philosophical error warrants excommunication.

Commentary
This passage is the conclusion (khātimah) of the work, presented as a dialogue. An interlocutor asks whether Ghazali definitively rules that the philosophers are disbelievers (kuffār) and that it is obligatory to kill anyone who holds their beliefs. Ghazali responds by stating that declaring them disbelievers (takfīr) is necessary in three issues. He then mentions the first issue: the belief in the pre-eternity (qidam) of the world and that all substances (jawāhir) are eternal. This indicates that Ghazali does not consider all philosophical positions as tantamount to disbelief; rather, takfir is restricted to specific foundational doctrines.

How This Lesson Applies — to MY Life?
?
When you hear about someone holding a different belief in Islam, do you rush to judge their faith? How does Ghazali's careful limitation of takfir to three specific issues challenge your own tendency to declare others out of Islam? Reflect on a specific time you did this. What would change if you applied this principle?
?
This passage shows Ghazali responding to a question about killing those who believe differently. Have you ever wished harm or felt righteous anger towards someone with a different theological view? How does Ghazali's precision in reserving takfir only for the most foundational issues force you to examine your heart towards those you disagree with?
?
The first issue is belief in the pre-eternity of the world. Do you truly affirm that the world was created, not eternal? Consider: does your daily life reflect the reality that this world is not eternal but created and temporary? What would change if you lived with the certainty that everything besides Allah is contingent?
