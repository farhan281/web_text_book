Return to Sin Does Not Invalidate Prior Repentance
Juwayni argues that relapsing into sin does not nullify a previously valid repentance.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand Juwayni's argument that relapsing into sin does not retroactively nullify a sincere repentance.

Core Idea
Repentance is a completed act; later sins don't undo it.

This short passage from Juwayni addresses a common concern: if someone repents but later falls back into sin, is their repentance lost? Juwayni argues firmly that it is not. The key insight is that repentance is like any act of worship once completed, it stands on its own and cannot be invalidated by future events.

Commentary
This passage is a short but complete argument. Juwayni states a principle: if a person repents and later returns to the same sin, that does not invalidate the earlier repentance. The reasoning is that repentance is considered a completed act of worship (عبادة منقضية). Once an act of worship is completed, it is no longer subject to nullification by subsequent events. For example, if someone prays and then commits a sin, the prayer remains valid. Similarly, repentance, once sincerely performed, stands as a valid act. The later sin is a new offense, but it does not retroactively cancel the past repentance.

Misconception & Correction
Misconception: Some think that after sinning, a prior repentance is nullified or was never accepted.
Correction: Juwayni clarifies that a sincere repentance, once completed, remains valid regardless of future sins; the later sin is a separate matter.

Key Takeaway
A previous repentance remains valid even if one later returns to the same sin, because repentance is considered a completed act of worship.
