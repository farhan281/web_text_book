Both rely on the opponents' reluctance to engage, which itself testifies to their knowledge of the Prophet's authenticity.

The Quran's Inimitability in an Age of Ignorance
Maturidi states the Quran's miraculous nature given the historical context.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand why the Quran's unmatched excellence in a time of widespread idolatry proves it is from Allah.

Core Idea
No human can match the Quran, not even all monotheists combined.

Maturidi starts by reminding us that the Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) was sent when the world was engulfed in idolatry. Yet the Quran emerged with such profound truth and eloquence that all monotheists, past and future, could not produce a tenth of its evidence. This lesson unpacks that powerful argument.

The phrase 'would not have reached a tenth' is a rhetorical exaggeration emphasizing impossibility.

By the end of this lesson, you will understand the two proofs from the Quran and the mubahala that Maturidi uses to establish the prophethood of Muhammad (peace be upon him).

Core Idea
The Jews' refusal to wish for death and the Christian mubahala are clear signs confirming the Prophet's truth.

Maturidi presents two powerful arguments for the prophethood of Muhammad (peace be upon him) from the Quran and from the practice of mubahala with the Christians. These proofs are drawn from the Jews' own claim and the Christians' own scriptures. Let's examine how these arguments work.

2
Identify the first proof
Identify the verse 'fatanaw al-mawt' and the two aspects Maturidi extracts: the promise of death if they wished, and the fact they will never wish despite ease.
This proof challenges the Jews directly from their own scripture.
3
Identify the second proof
Find the mubahala proof: the Prophet invoked a curse (mubahala) with the Christians, and it was known from their scriptures that he would be vindicated.
The mubahala serves as a practical demonstration of truth.
4
Reflect on the logic
Consider why each proof is compelling. The first proof is a direct challenge that the Jews cannot fulfill. The second proof involves a reciprocal curse that would have harmed the Christians if they were false.
These proofs are based on observable behavior and scriptural knowledge.
