Commentary
This passage is a historical and critical discussion of the relative neglect of Imam al-Maturidi compared to Imam al-Ash'ari. It begins by identifying the two main theological schools of Ahl al-Sunna: the Ash'aris in the western regions and the Maturidis in Transoxiana. The author then quotes al-Zabidi to affirm that when 'Ahl al-Sunna wa al-Jama'a' is used, it refers to both Ash'aris and Maturidis. The author uses these examples to highlight what he sees as an unjust historical bias.
