Completion and Elevation Through Human Creation
Maturidi explains that the creation of heavens and earth is completed and elevated by human creation.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand that according to Maturidi, the creation of the heavens and earth is completed and elevated through the creation of humans, who are the purpose of creation.

Core Idea
The cosmos gains full meaning only with the creation of humans, its purpose.

Maturidi here explains a profound theological insight: while the heavens and earth were created in six days, their true completion and elevation (تمام وعلو) is realized only with the creation of human beings. Without humans, the universe would be like a magnificent palace without inhabitants. Let's explore how Maturidi uses Quranic verses to show that humans are the ultimate purpose of creation.

Guided Path
1
Understand the two Quranic passages
Read the passage where Maturidi cites two sets of verses about the six-day creation: one detailed (Surah Fussilat) and one summary (Surah Al-A'raf). Notice that both describe the same creation event.
This shows that creation is complete in itself, but Maturidi will add a deeper layer.
2
Grasp the role of al-mumtahan
The term 'al-mumtahan' (the tested one) refers to humans. Maturidi claims that through humans the completion and elevation of the dominion appears. Reflect on why humans are called 'tested'.
Humans are tested with obligations, which gives creation its moral and teleological significance.
3
Find the proof from subjection verses
Maturidi quotes three verses about subjection (tashkheer) of the cosmos to humans. Find them in the passage and note how they support the idea that everything is for humans.
The subjection verses are: 'He who created for you all that is on the earth', 'And He subjected to you the night and day', and 'And He subjected to you whatever is in the heavens and whatever is on the earth'.
4
Consider Ibn Abbas' report
Maturidi reports that Ibn Abbas said humans were created on the seventh day. This implies that human creation is the climax of the creation week, giving completion and elevation to all that came before.
The seventh day here is not a day of rest, but the day of human creation, completing the purpose.
5
Recognize the primary role of humans
Although the jinn are also created for worship, Maturidi emphasizes that humans are the primary focus because the subjection of the cosmos is specifically for humans and their benefits.
This clarifies that humans are the central purpose, with jinn sharing the purpose of worship but not the same level of subjugation.

وأما على التمام والعلو فهو أن الله تعالى قال: قل أئنكم لتكفرون بالذي خلق الأرض في يومين إلى قوله: فقضاهن سبع سموات، فأخبر بخلق ما ذكر في ستة أيام على التفاريق، ثم أجملها في موضع فقال: إن ربكم الله الذي خلق السموات والأرض في ستة أيام ثم استوى على العرش. لمعنى خلق الممتحن من خلق الأرض والسموات، فبهم ظهر تمام الملك وعلا وارتفع، إذ هم المقصودون من خلق ما بينا، فبذلك تم معنى وعلا إذا وصل إلى الذين لهم. وقد قيل: ذا في خلق البشر خاصة، بقوله: هو الذي خلق لكم ما في الأرض الآية، وقوله: وسخر لكم الليل والنهار، وقوله: وسخر لكم ما في السموات وما في الأرض الآية. وذكر ابن عباس رضي الله عنهما أن البشر خلق اليوم السابع. فيه التمام والعلو؛ إذ خلق لهم كل شيء، وهم لعبادة الله ولحق بهم الجن بقوله: وما خلقت الجن والإنس إلا ليعبدون؛ لكن المقصود البشر، إذ تسخير ما ذكرت كله لهم، ثم بما يرجع إلى منافعهم. والله الموفق.
As for completion and elevation, it is that Allah the Exalted said: 'Say, do you indeed disbelieve in He who created the earth in two days...' up to His saying: 'And He completed them as seven heavens.' So He informed of the creation of what He mentioned in six days separately, then He summarized them in one place saying: 'Indeed your Lord is Allah who created the heavens and the earth in six days, then established Himself upon the Throne.' For the meaning of the creation of the tested one from the creation of the earth and the heavens, for through them the completion of dominion appeared and rose and ascended, as they are the intended ones from the creation of what we explained. So by that the meaning of completion and elevation is achieved when it reaches those to whom [it belongs]. And it was said that this is specifically regarding the creation of humans, by His saying: 'He who created for you all that is on the earth' and His saying: 'And He subjected to you the night and day' and His saying: 'And He subjected to you whatever is in the heavens and whatever is on the earth.' And Ibn Abbas (may Allah be pleased with them both) mentioned that humans were created on the seventh day. In it is completion and elevation, for He created everything for them, and they are for the worship of Allah, and the jinn are included with them by His saying: 'And I did not create the jinn and humans except to worship Me.' But the intended are humans, for the subjugation of all that I mentioned is for them, and then for what returns to their benefits. And Allah is the granter of success.

Commentary
This passage is a theological explanation of the concepts of 'tamam' (completion) and 'uluww' (elevation) in creation. The author begins by citing Quranic verses that describe the creation of the heavens and earth in six days, first in a detailed manner in Surah Fussilat (verses 9-12) and then summarized in Surah Al-A'raf (verse 54) which mentions the Throne. He argues that the true completion and elevation of the kingdom of Allah is manifested through the creation of humans (referred to as 'al-mumtahan', likely meaning the tested or obligated ones). The heavens and earth were created for humanity, as indicated by verses about subjection and worship. The author then quotes Ibn Abbas saying that humans were created on the seventh day, implying that the creation narrative culminates with humans. This gives the creation its purpose and perfection. The jinn are also included in the purpose of worship, but humans are the primary focus. Thus, the completion of creation is tied to the creation of humans and their role in worship and benefit.

Misconception & Correction
Misconception: Some might think that by saying creation is 'completed' through humans, Maturidi implies that Allah's act of creation was incomplete without humans or that Allah depends on creation.
Correction: Maturidi's point is teleological: the purpose and full significance of creation are only realized with humans. Allah's act of creation was perfect, but its intended meaning is brought to completion through the existence of beings who can worship and benefit.

Checkpoint
According to Maturidi, what gives the creation of the heavens and earth its completion and elevation?
A) The act of creation itself
B) The creation of humans as the purpose
C) The throne of Allah
D) The angels

Key Takeaway
Maturidi teaches that the creation of the heavens and earth reaches its full meaning and elevation only with the creation of humans, who are the purpose of all.

How This Lesson Applies — to MY Life?
?
You are part of this creation that Maturidi says is completed and elevated by human creation. Does your life reflect that purpose? What one change can you make starting tomorrow to live more consciously as the 'tested one' for whom everything has been subjugated?
?
The subjugation of the entire cosmos for you is a sign of immense generosity. When was the last time this truth moved you to sincere gratitude? What specific blessing from the subjugated cosmos (night, day, earth, heavens) have you taken for granted this week? Write down one you will thank Allah for in particular.
?
Maturidi says that the creation reaches its completion and elevation when it reaches the intended ones—that is, you. If you fail to fulfill your role as the worshipper and tested one, what happens to that purpose? Are you comfortable with the possibility that your negligence may mean that the meaning of creation remains incomplete in your own life? What is one concrete step to correct this?

The Difference Between Commanding Evil and Willing Evil
Answering the objection about willing vs. commanding evil.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand why God's willing evil is not morally wrong like commanding evil, based on three key distinctions from Imam Maturidi.

Core Idea
Command and will are different categories—command implies obligation, will implies choice.

This passage addresses a common objection: if commanding evil is bad, why isn't God's willing evil also bad? Imam Maturidi answers by showing that command and will function differently in terms of obligation and authority. The lesson explores three reasons that safeguard God's justice while affirming His will in creation.

Guided Path
1
Read the objection and answer
Read the Arabic passage once, then the translation. Notice the structure: an objection followed by multiple reasons.
The passage uses 'qīla' (it is said) to introduce the answer.
2
Identify the three reasons
Find the three distinct arguments: (1) contradiction in command, (2) God's will vs. command in His own acts, (3) different purposes of command and will.
Each reason begins with 'wa-ayḍan' (also) or 'li-wujūh' (for reasons).
3
Reflect on the implications
Consider why this distinction is important for understanding God's attributes of will and command without attributing evil to Him.

فإن قال قائل: إذ قبح الأمر بالمعاصي لِمَ لا قَبح إرادة كونها؟ قيل: لأوجه. أحدها التناقض في الأمر، وليس ذلك في الإرادة؛ لأن الفعل ربما يصير طاعة للأمر، فمحال الأمر بالمعصية لأنه يصير بالأمر طاعة، فيبطل معنى المعصية ليكون بها الأمر، وليست الإرادة كذلك. ألا يرى أن كل فاعل مريد لفعله ومحال أن يقال أمر نفسه بفعله، ثبت أنهما مختلفان. ولا قوة إلا بالله. وأيضاً إن الله يوصف بالإرادة في فعله ومحال أن يكون عليه أمر فيه، فثبت أن أحد الوجهين ليس هو دليل الآخر مع ما كان الله تعالى مريدا هلاك الأنبياء والأخيار وبقاء الأعداء والأشرار والسعة لهؤلاء في الدنيا. ولم يأمر بذلك، بل أمرنا بالدعاء بهلاك هؤلاء وبقاء أولئك. والله الموفق. وأيضاً إن فائدة الأمر رفعة الآمر وعلوه، حيث استعبد الآخر وأظهر فيه حقه وعظيم مننه التي بها استحق أن يكون سيدا له ومعبودا. وحق الإرادة الاختيار ونفي الغلبة أن لا يقهر ولا يُمنَع عن سلطانه ولا يحال بينه وبين ملكه. وفي دفع الإرادة هذا؛ لذلك بطل أن لا يريد. وكذلك في المنع عن الأمر والنهي، لذلك لزم القول بالأمر والنهي على.
If someone says: Since commanding evil is ugly, why is willing its occurrence not ugly? It is said: For reasons. One of them is the contradiction in the command, and that is not in the will; because the act might become an obedience due to the command, so commanding disobedience is impossible because it becomes an obedience by the command, thus the meaning of disobedience is nullified for the command to be through it, and the will is not like that. Do you not see that every doer is a willer of his act, and it is impossible to say that he commands himself to do his act? It is established that they are different. And there is no power except with God. Also, God is described with will in His act, and it is impossible that there be a command upon Him in it, so it is established that one of the two aspects is not the proof of the other, along with the fact that God wills the destruction of prophets and the righteous and the survival of enemies and the wicked and spaciousness for these in this world, and He did not command that, rather He commanded us to supplicate for the destruction of these and the survival of those. And God is the giver of success. Also, the benefit of the command is to raise the rank of the commander, where he enslaves the other and shows his right and the greatness of his favors through which he deserves to be a master and an object of worship. And the right of the will is choice and negation of compulsion, that He not be overpowered nor prevented from His authority, nor obstructed between Him and His kingdom. And in negating the will, there is this; therefore it is invalid that He does not will. Similarly, in preventing command and prohibition, therefore it is necessary to affirm command and prohibition upon.

Commentary
This passage is an objection and detailed answer. The objector asks: if commanding evil is morally bad (qabih), then why is God's willing evil not also bad? The answer presents multiple reasons. First, command involves a contradiction: if God commands a sin, the commanded act becomes obedience, nullifying its sinfulness. Willing does not have this effect; an act remains what it is regardless of God's will. Moreover, a person wills his own act but cannot command himself, showing will and command are distinct. Second, God is described as having will over His own acts, but it is impossible for Him to be commanded regarding them. Additionally, God wills the death of prophets and survival of enemies, but He does not command us to do that; rather He commands us to pray for the opposite. Third, the purpose of command is to elevate the commander and show his authority, while the purpose of will is choice and absence of compulsion. Denying God's will would imply that He is forced or prevented from His authority. Therefore, we must affirm both God's will and His command, but they are distinct.

Misconception & Correction
Misconception: Thinking that if God wills evil, He is evil or approves of it.
Correction: God's will is comprehensive and includes all events, but His command only obligates good. Will does not imply approval; it is simply divine decree.

Checkpoint
According to the first reason, why is commanding a sin impossible?
A) Because God cannot will evil
B) Because the command would turn the sin into an act of obedience, nullifying its sinfulness
C) Because God only commands good things
D) Because sin is defined as disobedience to command

Key Takeaway
God's will and command are distinct: will encompasses all events without moral implication, while command imposes obligation and is confined to what is good.

How This Lesson Applies — to MY Life?
?
Think of a time you were angry at God for something bad that happened to you or someone you love. This lesson teaches that God's will for that event does not equal His command or approval. How does this distinction change the way you view that trial? Will you now submit to His will while still hating the sin itself?
?
God wills the survival of enemies but commands us to pray for their destruction. When you pray against someone who has wronged you, do you truly submit to God's will, even if He wills their survival? Examine your heart: do you demand that God fulfill your desire, or do you accept His decree while still obeying His command to pray?
?
This lesson says that command elevates the commander. When you command others, do you seek elevation and recognition? How does reflecting on God's exclusive right to command and our role as servants humble your own commands and requests?
