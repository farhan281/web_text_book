Maturidi's Lost Works on Usul al-Fiqh and Their Legacy
Maturidi's two lost works on usul al-fiqh and their indirect transmission.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand that Maturidi authored two works on usul al-fiqh, that they are now lost, and how his legal thought survives through indirect transmission.

Core Idea
Maturidi's usul works are lost, but his ideas live on in later Hanafi commentaries.

This passage introduces us to Maturidi's contributions to the principles of Islamic jurisprudence (usul al-fiqh). Although his two dedicated works on the subject are lost, we learn that his views are preserved through citations in later works, particularly in his own commentary on the Quran. This lesson traces that process of transmission.

Commentary
This passage discusses Maturidi's contributions to uṣūl al-fiqh (principles of jurisprudence). It states that all biographers mention he wrote two books: 'Kitāb al-Jadal' (Book of Disputation) and 'Ma'ākhidh al-Sharā'iʿ' (Sources of Laws). However, no manuscript of these works survives, nor any description. Despite this, some Hanafi works on usul and fiqh quote Maturidi, either by citation or by detailed exposition, although these quotations are mostly attributed to his commentary 'Sharḥ Ta'wīlāt al-Māturīdī' rather than to the original works. An example is given: Abd al-Aziz al-Bukhari in his 'Kashf al-Asrār' quotes Maturidi on the issue of a solitary report (khabar al-wāḥid) conflicting with the general or apparent meaning of the Quran. This shows the indirect survival of Maturidi's usuli thought through later works.
