Author's Preface and the Need to Know the Subject
Author introduces himself and states the importance of knowing the subject matter of a science.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the author's purpose in writing this commentary and the methodological principle that every science must be defined by its subject matter.

Core Idea
Knowing the subject of a science is the foundation of all inquiry.

This lesson begins with the author's formal preface, including praise and blessings. He then explains his motivation for writing this commentary: to clarify al-Nasafi's 'Bahr al-Kalam'. Most importantly, he establishes a key methodological principle: before studying any science, one must first know its subject matter, because that defines its scope and purpose.

Guided Path
1
Read the preface
Read the Arabic passage and translation carefully. Note the author's name and the title of his work.
The preface includes both religious formality and substantive content.
2
Identify the author's motivation
Why did the author write this commentary? Look for the phrase 'فشرحته' and what he says about al-Nasafi's book.
3
Focus on the methodological statement
Find the sentence beginning with 'العلم أن البحث...'. What does it say about the necessity of knowing the subject?
This is the key intellectual contribution of the preface.
4
Reflect on the principle
Consider why knowing the subject of a science is important. How does it distinguish this science from others?
This principle applies to all fields of study.

بسم الله الرحمن الرحيم وبه تم الاعانة. الحمد لله الأزلي بذاته القديم بصفاته الفرد الصمد الذي دلت آياته على أنه محمد من عجز عن إدراك حقيقته من مخلوقاته، والصلاة والسلام على سيد الخلق محمد المبعوث لتعريف الحكم بيناته، وعلى آله وأصحابه القائمين بنصرته في جميع أوقاته. وبعد، فيقول الفقير إلى الله تعالى حسن بن أبي بكر المقدسي، عفا الله عنه بلطفه الخفي: إن الشيخ الإمام العلامة أبا المين النسفي لما كان كتابه الموسوم ببحر الكلام وهو كاسمه مشتمل على القواعد والأدلة والأحكام من المنقول والمعقول بما تنفع الأنام، معتقرا إلى شرح يوضح مشكلاته ويكمل خصاله، فشرحته بما يوصل إلى المقصود من مطالبه في هذه عائشة المرام في شرح بحر الكلام. وأسأل الله أن ينفع به كما نفع بأصله، وهو حسبي ونعم الوكيل. العلم أن البحث في الشيء لما كان مبدئي التصور فلا بد من تقديم ما يفيد ذلك وهو معرفة موضوعه، إذ به يتميز ماهيته عن غيره، وبيان ماهيته وغايته وهي الفائدة المطلوبة.
In the name of God, the Merciful, the Compassionate, and by Him is completion. Praise be to God, eternal in essence, ancient in attributes, the One, the Eternal, whose signs indicate that He is Muhammad [meaning praised?] from those who are incapable of perceiving His reality among His creatures. And blessings and peace be upon the master of creation Muhammad, sent to define the rulings of His proofs, and upon his family and companions who are steadfast in supporting him at all times. After this, the poor one to God, Hasan ibn Abi Bakr al-Maqdisi, may God forgive him with His subtle kindness, says: Indeed, the teacher Imam Ala' al-Din al-Nasafi, since his book titled "Bahr al-Kalam" (Ocean of Speech) contains, as its name implies, the principles, proofs, and rulings from transmitted and rational sources that benefit people, was in need of a commentary that clarifies its difficulties and completes its virtues, so I have commented on it in a way that leads to the intended purpose of its contents in this work, "A'ishah al-Maram fi Sharh Bahr al-Kalam" (The Delight of Aspiration in Commenting on the Ocean of Speech). I ask God to benefit through it as He benefitted through its original; He is sufficient for me and the best trustee. Know that since inquiry into a thing is fundamental to conception, one must first present what benefits that, which is knowing its subject matter, for thereby its essence is distinguished from others, and the statement of its essence and its goal, which is the desired benefit.

Commentary
This passage is an introduction by the author. He begins with the basmala and hamdala, praising God and sending peace upon the Prophet. He then identifies himself as Hasan ibn Abi Bakr al-Maqdisi and explains his motivation: the need for a commentary on al-Nasafi's 'Bahr al-Kalam'. He states that he has written this commentary, titled 'A'ishah al-Maram'. Then he transitions to a methodological point: since inquiry into any thing (i.e., any science) is fundamental to conceiving it, one must first know its subject matter, because the subject distinguishes the science from others, and knowing its essence and goal is the desired benefit.

Imam Nasafi's Opening Declaration of Faith
Imam Nasafi declares his beliefs about God's unity and attributes.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the essential elements of Sunni creed as declared by Imam Nasafi in his opening statement.

Core Idea
Imam Nasafi's creed begins with personal witness to Allah's oneness and transcendence.

In this opening passage, Imam Nasafi establishes his personal profession of faith, laying out the foundational beliefs of Ahl al-Sunnah. He affirms Allah's unity, eternity, and uniqueness while negating any likeness or form. This declaration sets the tone for the entire work.

Guided Path
1
Read the passage carefully
Read the Arabic passage once, then the translation. Notice the author's personal tone: 'اعتقَدتُ' and 'أقول'.
The opening phrase 'الله الحور العين' is likely a variant of the basmala.
2
Identify affirmations and negations
List the attributes Imam Nasafi affirms about Allah (e.g., One, Unique, Eternal) and what he negates (e.g., partner, likeness, form).
3
Reflect on personal profession
Consider why the author begins with a personal declaration rather than a general statement. How does this impact the reader?
This underscores that creed is a matter of personal conviction.

الله الحور العين
توكلتُ على الحيّ الذي لا يموت ولا يفوت أبدًا
الحمد الله ذي الجلال والإكرام والصلاة والسلام على رسوله محمد خير الأنام، وعلى آله وأصحابه الكرام. قال الشيخ الإمام الأجل، [لسان الحق] رئيس أهل السنة والجماعة، سيف الحق، [ولي النظم والنثر] أبو المعين النسفي رحمه الله: اعلموا أني اعتقدتُ معرفة الله تعالى والتوحيد [والدين]، وأقول بأن الله تعالى واحدٌ فرد قديم أزلي، وأنه صمد لا شريك له، ولا مثل [له]، ولا شبيه له، ولا شكل
Allah, the Hur al-'Ayn [?]. I rely on the Living who never dies nor ever perishes. Praise be to Allah, the Possessor of Majesty and Honor, and blessings and peace be upon His Messenger Muhammad, the best of mankind, and upon his family and noble companions. The venerable Imam, the tongue of truth, the chief of Ahl al-Sunnah wa al-Jama'ah, the sword of truth, the master of poetry and prose, Abu al-Mu'in al-Nasafi (may Allah have mercy on him) said: Know that I believe in knowledge of Allah Almighty and tawhid and the religion, and I say that Allah Almighty is One, Unique, Eternal, Everlasting, and that He is al-Samad (the Self-Sufficient), no partner for Him, nor any likeness to Him, nor any similitude, nor any form.
Source pages p. 76

Commentary
This passage is the opening declaration of the author's own creed at the beginning of his work. It begins with an invocation (possibly a corrupted basmala) and a statement of reliance on Allah, followed by praise of Allah and blessings on the Prophet. Then the author introduces himself with honorific titles and states that he believes in knowledge of Allah, tawhid (monotheism), and the religion. He explicitly affirms that Allah is One (wāḥid), Unique (fard), Eternal (qadīm), Everlasting (azalī), and al-Ṣamad (the Self-Sufficient, upon whom all depend). He negates any partner, likeness, similitude, or form for Allah. This is a standard summary of Sunni creed, emphasizing both affirmation of attributes and transcendence.

Misconception & Correction
Misconception: The author is simply summarizing standard doctrine without personal conviction.
Correction: Imam Nasafi explicitly states 'I believe' (اعتقَدتُ) and 'I say' (أقول), indicating a personal profession of faith, not merely a textbook summary.

Checkpoint

A) Allah has a form and shape.
B) Allah has partners.
C) Allah is Eternal and Unique.
D) Allah is like His creation.

Key Takeaway
Imam Nasafi's opening declaration encapsulates Sunni theology: affirming God's oneness, eternity, and self-sufficiency while negating all forms of anthropomorphism and polytheism.

How This Lesson Applies — to MY Life?
?
You regularly affirm that Allah is One and has no partner. But are there areas of your life—like your career, relationships, or ambitions—where you unconsciously act as if other things share His authority? What would it take for you to truly make Him the sole object of your trust and hope?
?
Imagine Allah without any mental image—no light, no throne, no shape. Does that thought make you uncomfortable? Why? What does your discomfort reveal about the image of Allah you may have unknowingly formed? Today, commit to purifying your conception of Allah from any form or likeness.
?
Imam Nasafi openly declared his creed. Do you openly affirm your belief in tawhid when the opportunity arises, or do you stay silent to avoid discomfort? What is one situation this week where you can explicitly declare your faith, even if it costs you something?
