Logical Proof that Allah is Not on the Throne
Imam al-Nasafi proves by logical division that Allah cannot be spatially on the Throne.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the logical proof that Allah cannot be spatially on the Throne, and why spatial attributes are impossible for the Eternal.

Core Idea
Allah cannot be on the Throne because any spatial relationship would entail division or finitude.

This lesson presents a rational proof from Imam al-Nasafi that demonstrates why Allah, being eternal and necessary, cannot occupy space or be located on the Throne. The proof uses a technique of exhaustive division to show that all possible spatial scenarios lead to impossibilities.

Guided Path
1
Read the passage carefully
Read the Arabic passage once without analysing. Then read the full translation. Pay attention to the logical structure.
Notice the three-fold division at the beginning.
2
Identify the three possibilities
The passage lists three possibilities regarding Allah's size relative to the Throne: larger, equal, or smaller. Find them in the text.
These are the branches of the logical division.
3
Understand why each is invalid
For each possibility, the author explains why it leads to an impossibility. Note the key reasons: division (tajazzu'), composition (tarkib), and finitude (hudud).
The commentary will help unpack each step.
4
Recognize the conclusion
The proof concludes that all possibilities are impossible, so Allah cannot be on the Throne. The final point about being limited and finite is a sign of creation.
This establishes tanzih (transcendence).

أن يكون أكبر من ساحة، وإما أن يكون مثل ساحة العرش ولم ينقص منها ولم يفضل عنها، وأما أن يكون أصغر منها. والأول باطل؛ لأنه يوجب كونه متبعضًا متجزيًّا، وكان بعض منه متمكنا على العرش وبعض منه غير متمكن، والقول بالتجزؤ باطل لأنه منافٍ للتوحيد على ما بينا، وكذا لو كان بمقدار العرش إذ لاقى كل جزء من أجزاء العرش جزءًا منه وكذا لو كان أصغر من العرش لما أن قدر ما يلاقيه من العرش متبعض فلاقي كل جزء منه جزءًا من الصانع وهو محال على الله تعالى لما مر من بيان منافاة التركيب والتبعض والتجزؤ على القديم، وكذا إن كان مساويًا لساحة العرش أو أصغر منه أو أكبر كان محدودًا متناهيا وهو من أمارات الحدث وثبوت شيء منها على القديم جل جلاله محال والله تعالى الموفق .
That He be larger than the area (of the Throne), or that He be equal to the area of the Throne not lacking from it nor exceeding it, or that He be smaller than it. The first is invalid because it necessitates that He be divided into parts and pieces, and some part of Him be settled on the Throne and some part not settled, and the statement of divisibility is invalid because it contradicts tawheed as we have explained. Similarly if He were equal to the measure of the Throne, then every part of the parts of the Throne would come into contact with a part of Him. And similarly if He were smaller than the Throne, because then the amount that contacts it from the Throne is divided, so each part of it contacts a part of the Maker, and that is impossible for Allah Almighty due to what has passed of the explanation of the incompatibility of composition, division, and divisibility with the Eternal. And similarly, whether He were equal to the area of the Throne, or smaller than it, or larger, He would be limited and finite, and that is among the signs of originatedness (hadath), and the establishment of any of these upon the Eternal, glorified is His majesty, is impossible. And Allah is the Grantor of success.

Commentary
This passage is a logical proof (dalil 'aqli) that Allah cannot be positioned on the Throne in a spatial sense. It uses exhaustive division (taqseem): if Allah were on the Throne, His size relative to the Throne would have to be one of three possibilities: larger, equal, or smaller. Each possibility is shown to entail impossibility. 1) Larger: would mean some parts of Allah are on the Throne and some are not, implying division (tajazzu') and parts (taba'ud), which contradicts divine oneness (tawheed). 2) Equal: would mean every part of the Throne contacts a part of Allah, again implying composition and divisibility. 3) Smaller: also entails contact with parts and thus divisibility of Allah. All three lead to Allah being limited and finite (mahdud mutanahi), which are marks of contingent beings (amarat al-hadath). Since Allah is eternal and necessary, none of these can apply. The conclusion is that it is impossible for Allah to be spatially on the Throne. The proof is built on the principle that any form of composition (tarkib) is impossible for the Divine.

Refuting Allah's Physical Relationship to the Throne
Nasafi enumerates three possibilities for Allah's size relative to the Throne and refutes each.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand why attributing physical size or location to Allah relative to the Throne is impossible, based on the logical refutation of three possibilities.

Core Idea
Allah is not bounded by size or place; any such attribution implies composition and finitude.

Nasafi presents a powerful logical argument against anthropomorphism regarding Allah's relationship to the Throne. He considers three possible ways Allah could relate to the Throne if He were physically upon it—greater, equal, or smaller—and shows each leads to impossible consequences. This lesson walks through that argument and its implications for divine transcendence.

Guided Path
1
Read the passage and translation
Read the Arabic passage once without analysis, then read the English translation. Let the logical structure of the argument emerge naturally.
Notice the use of conditional statements (if...then) and refutations.
2
Identify the three possibilities
The passage opens by listing three possibilities for Allah's size relative to the Throne. Write them down: greater, equal, smaller. These are the only logical possibilities if He were physically related.
The subsequent refutation applies to each one.
3
Understand the refutation of each possibility
For each possibility, Nasafi demonstrates an unacceptable consequence: division (taba'ud), composition (tajazzi), or finitude (mahdud). See how the first leads to parts being on and off the Throne, the second to each part touching, and the third also to touching of parts.
All these consequences contradict tawhid and the nature of the Eternal.
4
Recognize the common implication of finitude
Beyond the specific refutations, Nasafi points out that all three possibilities would make Allah bounded (mahdud) and finite (mutanahi), which are signs of contingency (hadath).
This seals the argument: size itself is incompatible with divinity.

Commentary
This passage presents a logical argument against anthropomorphism. It begins by considering three possible relationships between Allah and the Throne if He is physically upon it: He is larger than the Throne, equal to it, or smaller. Each possibility is refuted. The first (larger) would imply that Allah is composed of parts (taba'ud), with some parts on the Throne and others not—this violates tawhid. The second (equal) would imply that each part of the Throne touches a corresponding part of Allah. The third (smaller) would similarly imply a touching of parts between a portion of the Throne and a portion of Allah. All these scenarios involve composition (tarkib) and division (tajazzi), which are impossible for the Eternal (al-Qadim). Moreover, any of the three possibilities would mean that Allah is bounded and finite (mahdud mutanahi), which is a characteristic of contingent beings (hawadith). Thus, the very question of size relative to the Throne is incoherent for Allah.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the Jahmiyyah's claim that Allah is everywhere, the verses they used to support it, and the method of interpreting them in a way that preserves Allah's transcendence.

Core Idea
The Jahmiyyah's proof texts do not prove spatial location.

Nasafi opens a new chapter to establish a central tenet of Sunni theology: Allah is not confined to place or direction. The Jahmiyyah, an early sect, insisted otherwise, citing Quranic verses that speak of Allah being 'in the heaven' or 'with' people. In this lesson, we examine their claim and the scriptural evidence they presented, and we begin to see how Nasafi refutes their misinterpretations.

Guided Path

Read the chapter title and introduction

2
Identify the Jahmiyyah's claim and their proofs
The passage explicitly states: 'The Jahmiyyah said: Allah is in every place.' Then it lists five Quranic verses they adduced. Read each verse carefully.
These verses use the prepositions 'in' (fi) and 'with' (ma'a), which the Jahmiyyah took literally.
3
Observe the beginning of Nasafi's answer
Nasafi starts his refutation with the first verse, explaining that its intended meaning (taqdiruhu) is that Allah is the God of those in heaven and earth, not that He is spatially located there.
This is the method of ta'wil (interpretation) – a key tool in Sunni theology.

B) The celestial bodies are subjugated and have no management; only Allah manages.
C) Astrology is a valid science but should not be used for predictions.
D) The astrologers are mistaken about the number of planets.

Key Takeaway
Celestial bodies have no independent management; all affairs are under Allah's sole control, as affirmed by the Quran.

How This Lesson Applies — to MY Life?
?
When you face an uncertain future, do you unconsciously consult horoscopes or patterns in the stars? What does that reveal about whom you truly trust as the Manager of your life?
?
Is there any created thing—a person, a system, or a natural force—that you have been subconsciously attributing influence or control to, apart from Allah? How can you uproot that from your heart today?
?
This passage declares that the sun, moon, and stars have no management at all. If even these mighty creations are powerless, then what excuse is there for relying on anything other than Allah? Are you willing to repent and redirect your trust entirely to Him?
