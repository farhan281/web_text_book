Refutation of Astrology as Determinative
Nasafi refutes astrology's claim to causal power.

Learning Objective

Core Idea
Stars are signs, not causes; only Allah determines events.

This lesson addresses a common challenge: doesn't the Quran mention that Prophet Abraham looked at the stars and predicted his sickness? Does that validate astrology? Nasafi clarifies the Ahl al-Sunna position, showing that the stars are subservient to Allah's command, and that Abraham's knowledge came from Allah, not from star-gazing.

Notice the Quranic verse cited.

The market example shows that God may inform through signs, but signs are not causes.

Refuting the Astrologers' Claim about the Stars' Location
Nasafi refutes astrologers using Quranic verses

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand how Imam al-Nasafi uses Quranic verses to refute the astrological claim that the sun, moon, and stars reside in the fourth heaven, and why the Sunni position places them in the lowest heaven.

Core Idea
The Quran places the stars in the lowest heaven, not the fourth.

In this passage, Imam al-Nasafi confronts a claim made by astrologers that the sun, moon, and stars are located in the fourth heaven. He presents the Sunni position that they are in the lowest heaven and supports it with two Quranic proofs. He also points out inconsistencies in the astrologers' own system. Let's examine his argument.

Guided Path
1

2

The first verse explicitly mentions 'lowest heaven', the second shows the sun is observed from earth.
3
Understand the refutation of astrologers
The author notes that even astrologers place the moon in the lowest heaven, contradicting their general claim. He details their planetary spheres to highlight inconsistency.
This shows that the astrologers' own system doesn't fully align with their claim.
4
Reflect on the lunar eclipse argument
The author asks: 'Nor do we witness the eclipse of the moon with its eye in the lowest heaven?' This implies that our observation of lunar eclipses confirms the moon's proximity.
The phrase 'with its eye' may mean 'in the same heaven' or 'from our perspective'.
5
Note the author's conclusion
The passage ends by saying 'And Allah knows best', affirming that the true knowledge of the heavens belongs to Allah.
This humbly acknowledges the limits of human knowledge.

The Neutral State of Creation at the Covenant
Nasafi explains the initial neutrality and subsequent classification after the covenant.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand that human souls were created without belief or disbelief and that their response to the divine presentation determined their spiritual identity.

Humans were created neutral; faith and disbelief come from choice, not predestination at creation.

In this final section, Imam al-Nasafi clarifies the moment of the primordial covenant. He explains what exactly happened when Allah brought forth all human souls from Adam (AS). Far from being predetermined as believers or disbelievers, they were created without any attributes, and only after the presentation of faith and disbelief did their choices define them.
