Characteristics 21-23: Sirat, Paradise and Hell, and Direct Judgment
The author lists the 21st, 22nd, and 23rd characteristics regarding eschatology.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand three key Sunni beliefs about the afterlife: the reality of the Sirat, the eternal nature of Paradise and Hell, and direct accountability before Allah.

Core Idea
The saved group affirms unseen afterlife realities as true and known.

This lesson covers the 21st, 22nd, and 23rd characteristics of the saved group. These focus on eschatological certainties: the bridge over Hell, the everlasting abodes, and Allah's direct judgment. They are foundational to Sunni creed and counter doubts about the afterlife.

Guided Path
1
Read the passage carefully
Read the Arabic and translation for characteristics 21-23. Notice the verbs 'يعتقد' (he believes) and 'يعلم' (he knows).
These verbs indicate the level of certainty required.
2
Identify the three beliefs
List each belief: the Sirat, Paradise/Hell as created and eternal, and direct judgment without intermediary. Understand what each affirms.
The second uses 'يعلم' (knowledge) rather than 'يعتقد' (belief).
3
Reflect on the purpose
Ask why these specific beliefs are listed. They guard against innovations like denying the Sirat's literal reality or claiming Paradise/Hell are not yet created.
4
Connect to broader creed
Recognize these as samiyyat (matters heard from revelation). They are accepted without questioning how.
This is a pattern in Sunni creed.

Ritual Practice: Wiping Khuffs and Praying Behind Any Amir
Imam Samarqandi states two practical articles of faith.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the Sunni affirmation of wiping over leather socks during travel and residence, and the obligation to pray behind any Muslim ruler for Eid and Friday prayers.

Core Idea
Practical rites remain valid even under imperfect rulers.

Imam Samarqandi begins this section with two practical matters of ritual practice: the permissibility of wiping over leather socks (khuffs) and the obligation to pray behind any ruler, regardless of his piety, for congregational prayers. These rulings affirm the Sunni commitment to maintaining unity and following the prophetic practice even in less-than-ideal circumstances.

3
Reflect on significance
Why does Imam Samarqandi include these? They show that Sunni creed includes practical dimensions of worship and community leadership.
These are not mere rituals but expressions of faith.

Divine Sovereignty: Faith as Gift and Actions as Created
Two articles on faith as gift and actions as created by Allah.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand that faith is a gift from Allah and that all human actions are created by Allah, key Sunni stances on divine sovereignty.

Core Idea
Allah alone gives faith and creates all actions, even of humans.

The next two articles move from practical matters to theological principles about divine sovereignty. Imam Samarqandi affirms that faith (iman) is a direct gift from Allah, not earned by humans, and that all actions of servants are created by Allah. These stances counter both free-will extremes and pure predestination, representing the Sunni middle path.

Guided Path
1
Read the passage
Read the Arabic for articles 10 and 11. Note the phrase 'an yara anna' (that he consider) indicating a required belief.
These are beliefs, not actions.
2
Understand the two beliefs
First: faith is a gift from Allah (عطاء الله). Second: actions of servants are created by Allah (مخلوقة الله). Consider how these relate to free will and divine predestination.
3
Connect to Sunni creed
These affirm that even the human ability to have faith comes from Allah, and that every action, good or bad, is within Allah's creation. This avoids attributing creation to anyone else.
Do not confuse with fatalism; humans are accountable.

Blood and Pus Break Wudu
Samarqandi rules on blood/pus breaking wudu and gives a general purity principle.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand that flowing blood, pus, and similar substances break wudu and require immediate renewal, and that intentionally neglecting this renders one an innovator whose prayer is invalid.

Core Idea
Ritual purity requires immediate renewal when any internal substance exits the body.

Ritual purity is essential for prayer, and certain bodily discharges nullify it. Imam Samarqandi explains that flowing blood, pus, and similar substances break wudu regardless of intent, and failing to repeat wudu makes one an innovator. This lesson establishes the ruling based on a general principle of interior-to-exterior flow.
