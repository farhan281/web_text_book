The Obligation to Not Oppose the Mainstream Congregation
States the prohibition of opposing the mainstream congregation and its consequence.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand that a believer must not oppose the mainstream Muslim congregation, based on the prophetic guarantee that the community will not agree on error.

Core Idea
Opposing the mainstream congregation is misguidance and innovation.

Imam Samarqandi begins by establishing a core principle: a believer must not separate from the mainstream Muslim community. He invokes the Prophet's assurance that the community will never unite on error, making any separation a dangerous deviation that leads to misguidance and innovation.

Guided Path
1
Read the opening directive
Read the first part of the passage where the author states the obligation not to oppose the congregation. Notice the direct link to the hadith.
The Arabic phrase 'فمن فارق جماعة المسلمين ولا يراه حقاقاته ضال مبتدع' is key.
2
Understand the hadith proof
The hadith 'لا تجتمع أمني على الضلالة' is the primary evidence. Reflect on its meaning: the collective body of Muslims is protected from unanimous error.
3
Identify the ruling
The passage clearly labels one who separates as 'ضال مبتدع' (misguided and innovator). This is a serious condemnation.

The second question. And what we have mentioned and described, that the believer should not oppose the congregation of Muslims, because the Prophet, peace be upon him, said: 'My community will not agree upon error.' So whoever separates from the congregation of Muslims and does not consider it a right, he is misguided and an innovator. Because preserving the congregation is among the rulings of the Prophetic practice, and preserving the Prophetic practice is an obligation, due to the statement of the Most High: 'Whatever the Messenger gives you, take it, and whatever he forbids you, refrain from it,' and His statement: 'Nor does he speak from desire; it is only revelation revealed.' He says: 'O My servants, Muhammad, peace be upon him, does not speak from his own desire nor from whim, nor does he utter anything nor command anything except by revelation from Allah, the Most High, and by His command.' And the trustworthy narrated to us with their chains from Ibn Abbas from the Messenger of Allah, peace be upon him, that he said: 'Whoever performs an action for the sake of Allah within the congregation, if he is correct, Allah accepts it from him; and if he errs, Allah forgives him. And whoever performs an action for Allah in isolation, if he is correct, it is not accepted from him, and if he errs, let him take his seat in the Fire.' And know that the Prophet, peace be upon him, preserved congregational prayer and considered it a right and a duty upon him, and he commanded the creation to preserve the congregation. So whoever does not consider preserving the congregation a right is an innovator.

Commentary
This passage is a directive (mas'ala) that a believer must not oppose the mainstream community of Muslims. The proof is the prophetic hadith that the community will not agree upon error. Therefore, separating from the mainstream is an act of misguidance and innovation. The author explains that preserving the congregation is part of the rulings derived from the Prophet's Sunnah, and preserving the Sunnah is a religious obligation. This is supported by two Quranic verses: 'Whatever the Messenger gives you, take it' and 'Nor does he speak from desire.' These emphasize that the Prophet's words are divine revelation. Then a hadith is quoted: actions performed in congregation are accepted if correct or forgiven if erroneous; actions performed in isolation are not accepted even if correct, and if erroneous, the doer earns Hell. The passage concludes that the Prophet himself observed congregational prayer as an obligation and commanded others to do so; hence, anyone who does not regard preserving the congregation as a duty is an innovator.

Misconception & Correction
Misconception: One may think that individual reasoning can override community consensus, or that solitary paths are equally valid.
Correction: The hadith explicitly states the community will not agree on error, so opposing it leads to misguidance and innovation.

Checkpoint

A) He is a disbeliever.
B) He is misguided and an innovator.
C) He is forgiven if he is correct.
D) He is a hypocrite.

Key Takeaway
Separating from the mainstream congregation is misguidance and innovation.

How This Lesson Applies — to MY Life?
?

?

Are you willing to trust the collective guidance of the community over your own reasoning? Where specifically do you struggle with this?
?
The author labels those who separate from the congregation as 'misguided and innovators.' Could this description apply to any attitude you hold? If you were to take the position of the mainstream congregation as a right and an obligation, what changes would you need to make in your loyalties or behavior starting now?

The Virtue of Congregational Action and Preserving the Sunnah
Provides the hadith on congregation vs. isolation and concludes that preserving congregation is obligatory.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the immense merit of performing actions within the congregation and the obligation to preserve congregational practices as part of preserving the Prophet's Sunnah.

Core Idea
Actions in congregation are accepted or forgiven; actions in isolation are rejected.

Imam Samarqandi now deepens the argument by quoting a powerful hadith from Ibn Abbas: actions performed within the congregation are accepted if correct and forgiven if erroneous, while actions done in isolation are not accepted even if correct, and if in error lead to the Fire. He then connects preserving the congregation to preserving the Prophet's Sunnah, making it a religious duty.

Guided Path
1
Read the hadith from Ibn Abbas
Read the hadith carefully. Notice it contrasts two scenarios: within congregation vs. in isolation. Also note the outcomes.
The hadith is narrated by 'al-thiqāt' (trustworthy narrators).
2
Analyze the four outcomes
There are four possibilities: congregation-correct (accepted), congregation-error (forgiven), isolation-correct (not accepted), isolation-error (Hell). Reflect on the severity.
3
Link to preserving the Sunnah
The author explains that preserving congregation is part of preserving the Prophet's Sunnah, which is obligatory based on Quranic verses. This makes congregational worship a mandatory aspect.
Verses cited: 'وما آتاكم الرسول فخذوه' and 'وما ينطق عن الهوى'.
4
Understand the final conclusion
The passage ends by stating that the Prophet himself observed congregational prayer as a duty and commanded others; thus, anyone who does not regard preserving congregation as a right is an innovator.
