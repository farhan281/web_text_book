Prophets are higher than saints
Samarqandi affirms the superiority of prophets over saints.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the Islamic belief that prophets hold a higher rank with Allah than saints.

Core Idea
Prophets are higher than saints in rank with Allah.

In this short passage, Imam al-Samarqandi states a clear and fundamental belief of Ahl al-Sunnah: the prophets (anbiya) occupy a station with Allah that is above that of the saints (awliya). This is not a matter of personal virtue but of divine selection. We will examine this statement and its implications.

Guided Path
1
Read the passage carefully
Read the Arabic passage and its English translation. Notice the wording: 'necessary for him to know' emphasizes the importance of this belief.
The passage is incomplete due to OCR, but the core statement is clear.
2
Identify the key terms
Look at the terms 'anbiya' (prophets) and 'awliya' (saints). Understand that they are distinct categories in Islamic theology.
Saints are close friends of Allah, but prophets are messengers with a higher station.
3
Reflect on the implication
Consider why this distinction matters. It guards against elevating saints to the level of prophets, which would blur the unique role of prophethood.

Prophets Are Superior to Saints
Samarqandi asserts the superiority of prophets over saints and warns against the opposite view.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand why Sunni doctrine holds that prophets are superior to saints and what the consequences are for claiming the opposite.

Core Idea
Prophets are higher than all saints; denial of this is innovation.

Samarqandi presents a crucial doctrinal boundary: the absolute superiority of prophets over saints. He not only affirms this but warns against the opposite view, labeling it as a specific innovation (Karamiyya). The passage uses Quranic verses and a prophetic hadith to anchor this hierarchy.

Guided Path
1
Read the Arabic passage and translation
Read the Arabic passage once without over-analysing. Then read the translation to grasp the overall claim and warning.
Notice the phrase 'صار مبتدعا يسمى كراميا' – this identifies a deviant group.
2
Identify key terms and the logical structure
Find and understand the key terms: مراتب الأنبياء, مراتب الأولياء, مبتدع, كرامية. Observe the logical flow: statement of superiority → warning → reason → evidence.
3
Examine the supporting evidence
Look at the two Quranic verses and the hadith. Ask: How do these proofs support the claim that prophets are higher and that obedience to them is obedience to Allah?
The verses link obedience to Allah and the Messenger together.

Question thirty-one: What we said that he should know that the ranks of prophets with God Almighty are higher than the ranks of saints. Whoever says that the ranks of saints are higher than the ranks of prophets becomes an innovator called a Karami (Karamiyya). Because the saints do not reach the ranks of the prophets except after obedience to God Almighty and His Messenger. Because obedience to the prophets is obedience to God Almighty, as He says: {Whoever obeys God and the Messenger, they are with those whom God has blessed} (Quran 4:69), and He says: {Whoever obeys God and the Messenger, He will admit him to gardens beneath which rivers flow} (Quran 4:13). And the Prophet (peace be upon him) said: 'I am the master of the children of Adam, and I do not boast' to the end of the hadith. This is sufficient for the intelligent, and God knows best.

Commentary
This passage is a classification and refutation. Samarqandi states the doctrine that prophets are superior to saints. He then warns that anyone who claims the opposite becomes an innovator and is associated with the Karamiyya sect. The justification is that saints can only reach high ranks by following the prophets, and obedience to the prophets counts as obedience to God. He provides Quranic verses that link obedience to God and the Messenger with reward, and a hadith where the Prophet declares his precedence among humanity. The passage ends with a typical closure.

Affirmation of Karamat al-Awliya'
Samarqandi proves the miracles of saints from Quran and refutes deniers.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand why denying the miracles of saints is considered innovation (bid'a) and how the Quran provides clear evidence for their reality without diminishing prophethood.

Core Idea
Miracles of saints are real and do not diminish prophethood; the Quran proves them.

Imam Samarqandi now addresses a crucial doctrine: the miracles of saints (karamat al-awliya'). Some deny them out of misguided fear that they might undermine the miracles of prophets. In this passage, Samarqandi not only affirms the reality of these miracles but also systematically refutes any objection. He brings compelling Quranic examples and a powerful logical argument. Read carefully to see how he establishes this teaching as a matter of orthodox belief.

Guided Path
1
Read the full passage carefully
Begin by reading the Arabic passage and its translation. Notice the structure: a ruling, an analysis of deniers, evidence, and a refutation.
The passage is dense; take your time.
2
Identify the three states of the denier
Samarqandi explains that someone who denies karamat out of concern for prophethood falls into one of three states. Find these three and note the outcome for each.
This is the core logical argument.
3
Examine the Quranic evidence
List the examples Samarqandi gives: Asif bin Barkhiya, Maryam, the People of the Cave, and the Isra'. For each, ask: Was this person a prophet? What miracle occurred?
These examples show that such wonders are not exclusive to prophets.
4
Reflect on the refutation of the objection
Samarqandi responds to an objection about traveling to Mecca in one night. He uses the Prophet's Night Journey and Iblis's speed to argue. Understand why this refutes the denier.
This step ties the lesson together.

المسئلة الثانية والثلاثون: وما قلنا إنه ينبغي له أن يقر بكرامة الأولياء، لأن من أنكر كرامات الأولياء فهو مبتدع. ومن أنكر كرامة الأولياء وهو يظن أن في ذلك هدم معجزات الأنبياء، فلا يخرج عن أحد أحوال ثلاثة: إما أن ينكر الآيات التي في كتاب الله تعالى أولاً، فإن أنكر الآيات فقد كفر. وإن لم ينكر الآيات وآمن بها ولكن يقول كانوا هم أنبياء فقد كفر أيضاً. وإن لم ينكر الآيات وآمن بها ولم يقل أنبياء فقد صح عنده أن هذه الكرامة كانت لغير الأنبياء ويجوز ذلك، لأن الله تعالى قال: {قال الذي عنده علم من الكتاب إني آتيك به قبل أن يرتد إليك طرفك} وكان هذا آصف بن برخيا وكان من الأولياء ولم يكن نبياً وكان من قوم سليمان بن داود. فلما جاز أن يكون من قوم سليمان كرامة الأولياء، أليس يجوز فكرامة محمد صلى الله عليه وسلم كرامة الأولياء، ومحمد خير من سليمان وأمته خير من أمته؟ فإن قال المخالف: تلك الكرامة كانت من قبل سليمان، فتقول: هذه الكرامة من قبل محمد صلى الله عليه وسلم. وقوله تعالى: {وهزي إليك بجذع النخلة} أخرج الله تعالى من الشجرة اليابسة رطباً لأجل مريم أكرمها بذلك، ومريم لم تكن نبية. وقوله تعالى: {كما دخل عليها زكريا المحراب وجد عندها رزقا} قال: {يا مريم أنى لك هذا} قالت: {هو من عند الله}. وكذلك في قصة أهل الكهف أكرمهم الله تعالى ولم يكونوا أنبياء. فلما جاز أن يكون في الأولين، لم لا يجوز أن يكون في أمة محمد صلى الله عليه وسلم كرامة الأولياء؟ وقد قال الله تعالى: {كنتم خير أمة أخرجت للناس تأمرون بالمعروف وتنهون عن المنكر}. فإن قال المخالف: إن فلانا يذهب في ليلة واحدة إلى بيت الله ويرجع، هذا لا يكون أبداً. قلنا: إن الله أكرم النبي صلى الله عليه وسلم بكرامة لم يكرم بها أحد قط حين أسري به وعُرج به إلى السموات السبع وبلغ ما شاء الله بمسيرة أربعة آلاف سنة ورجع، فهل كرامة أعظم من هذه؟ وأيضاً يقال للمخالف: المؤمن خير من الكافر، فإنا وجدنا من كان يسير من الكفار في ساعة واحدة من المشرق إلى المغرب وهو إبليس لعنه الله. فإذا كان الكافر هكذا، لا تنكر كرامة الأولياء. وهذا كفاية للعاقل.
The thirty-second question: And we say that it is incumbent upon him to affirm the miracles of the saints (karamat al-awliya'), because whoever denies the miracles of the saints is an innovator. And whoever denies the miracles of the saints thinking thereby to undermine the miracles of the prophets, he is in one of three states: either he denies the verses in the Book of Allah Almighty – then if he denies the verses, he has disbelieved; or he does not deny the verses and believes in them but says they were prophets – then he has also disbelieved; or if he does not deny the verses and believes in them and does not say they were prophets, then he has confirmed that this miracle (karama) was for other than prophets, and that is permissible. For Allah Almighty said: {He who had knowledge from the Book said, 'I will bring it to you before your glance returns to you'} – and this was Asif bin Barkhiya, who was among the saints and was not a prophet, and he was of the people of Solomon bin David. So when it is permissible that there be a miracle of the saints from among the people of Solomon, is it not permissible that there be a miracle of the saints for the sake of Muhammad, peace be upon him? And Muhammad is better than Solomon, and his community is better than his community. If the opponent says: That miracle was through Solomon, you say: This miracle is through Muhammad, peace be upon him. And His statement: {And shake toward you the trunk of the palm tree} – Allah brought out from the dry tree ripe dates for the sake of Mary, honoring her with that, and Mary was not a prophet. And His statement: {Whenever Zechariah entered upon her in the sanctuary, he found with her provision. He said, 'O Mary, from where is this?' She said, 'It is from Allah.'} And likewise in the story of the People of the Cave, Allah honored them and they were not prophets. So when it is permissible in former times, why should it not be permissible in the community of Muhammad, peace be upon him, the miracles of the saints? And Allah has said: {You are the best community ever brought forth for mankind, commanding what is right and forbidding what is wrong.} If the opponent says: So-and-so goes in one night to the House of Allah and returns – this would never happen. We say: Allah honored the Prophet, peace be upon him, with a miracle with which He has never honored anyone when He took him on the Night Journey and ascended him to the seven heavens, and he reached what Allah willed over a distance of four thousand years and returned. Is there any miracle greater than this? And also it is said to the opponent: The believer is better than the disbeliever, for we have found one who travels from among the disbelievers in one hour from the east to the west, and he is Iblis, may Allah curse him. When the disbeliever is like this, do not deny the miracles of the saints. And this is sufficient for the intelligent.
Source pages p. 19

Commentary
This passage is a proof and defense of the reality of the miracles (karamat) of the saints (awliya'). It begins by stating that denying the karamat of the awliya' is innovation (bid'a). The author then considers the case of someone who denies these miracles out of fear that they might undermine the miracles of the prophets, and he analyzes three possible positions: (1) the denier denies the Quranic verses that mention these wonders – that is disbelief; (2) he accepts the verses but claims that those individuals were prophets – that is also disbelief, because he is ascribing prophethood to non-prophets; (3) he accepts the verses and does not claim prophethood for them – then he has correctly recognized that these were karamat for non-prophets, and that is permissible. Samarqandi then provides Quranic evidence: Asif bin Barkhiya (a saint) brought the throne of the Queen of Sheba to Solomon in the blink of an eye (Quran 27:40); Mary received ripe dates from a dry palm tree (Quran 19:25); Zechariah found provision with Mary (Quran 3:37); and the People of the Cave were miraculously protected (Quran 18). All these individuals were not prophets. If such wonders occurred in previous communities, they can occur in the Muslim community, which is the best community (Quran 3:110). The author then refutes an objection: someone says it is impossible for a person to travel to Mecca and return in one night. Samarqandi counters by mentioning the Prophet's Night Journey (Isra') which covered a distance of four thousand years in one night – a far greater miracle. Finally, he argues that if the disbeliever Iblis can travel from east to west in an hour, then a believer should not be denied such ability. This refutes the deniers.
