The Servant Has No Claimable Right on God
Affirms that no right is owed by God to humans.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand why the servant has no inherent claim or right against God, and how this doctrine underscores divine grace.

Core Idea
Humans have no claim on God; all blessings are pure grace.

This lesson examines a foundational statement in Maturidi theology: there is no right owed by God to His servants. This principle guards against the idea that God is obligated to reward human actions. Instead, it emphasizes that everything from God is a gift.

Guided Path
1
Read the original statement
Read the Arabic phrase carefully. Notice its brevity and directness. Then read the translation.
The phrase is a heading, not an argument.
2
Understand the theological implication
Ask yourself: why would anyone think humans have a right against God? The commentary refutes that idea by asserting God's freedom from obligation.
This refutes the Mu'tazili view that God must do what is best.
3
Reflect on divine grace
Consider how this statement affects your understanding of reward and punishment. If God rewards, it is by His generosity, not because He owes it.
This leads to a deeper appreciation of God's mercy.

There is no right for the servant that is due from God.
Source pages p. 1019

Commentary
This is a brief doctrinal statement serving as a heading for a new topic. It asserts that the servant (al-'abd) has no right (haqq) that is owed (mustahiq) from God. In Maturidi theology, this emphasizes that God's rewards and blessings are purely gracious; He is not obligated to grant anything to His creatures. The statement guards against the Mu'tazili claim that God owes rewards for obedience or that He must do what is best for His servants.

Misconception & Correction
Misconception: That human beings can have a claim or right against God based on their actions.
Correction: The statement denies any such right; all of God's gifts are unearned graces.

Checkpoint

A) Humans can demand good deeds from God.
B) God is obligated to reward obedience.
C) Humans have no inherent claim or right over God.
D) God must always answer prayers.

Key Takeaway
The servant has no claimable right upon God; all divine rewards and punishments are acts of grace and justice.

How This Lesson Applies — to MY Life?
?
When you perform a good deed, do you feel God owes you something in return? How does this passage challenge that feeling? What would it take for you to truly believe that nothing is owed, and every blessing is pure grace?
?
Reflect on a time you felt entitled to God's blessing—perhaps after a long prayer or a charitable act. How does the statement 'There is no right for the servant that is due from God' reshape your prayers and expectations? Will you pray differently today?
?
If God owes you nothing, then every moment of faith and every breath is an undeserved gift. How would you live today if you fully embraced that truth? What would you let go of, and what would you hold more tightly?
