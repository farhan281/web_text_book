Why Intercession Does Not Encourage Sin
Refutes the claim that intercession encourages sin, contrasting it with the Mu'tazila view on repentance.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand why the belief in intercession does not encourage sin and how it actually prevents despair.

Core Idea
Intercession does not embolden sin because no sinner is certain of receiving it.

Some opponents of intercession argue that believing in it makes people careless about sin. The author of this passage turns the argument on its head, showing that the Mu'tazila position on repentance is actually more encouraging of sin. Then he explains why intercession itself does not have this effect—and even serves a vital purpose: preventing despair.

Certainty vs. uncertainty is key.

Commentary
