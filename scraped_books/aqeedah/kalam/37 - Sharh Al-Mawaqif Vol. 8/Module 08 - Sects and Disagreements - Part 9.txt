Divine Attributes and Transcendence
Affirms divine attributes, negates imperfections, and describes God's will and actions.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the Ash'ari doctrine on divine attributes, God's transcendence, and His actions, including independence, will, and freedom from deficiency.

Core Idea
God is described with all perfect attributes, transcends all imperfections, and acts freely without obligation.

This lesson continues the creedal summary by detailing the attributes of God. Sanusi affirms that God is described with knowledge, power, and all attributes of majesty, while refuting those who deny attributes or liken God to creation. He also emphasizes God's absolute will, self-sufficiency, and that He does no injustice.

Guided Path
1
Assert positive attributes
Read the affirmations of God's attributes: knowledge, power, etc. Note the refuted groups: Mu'ammariyya (deny pre-eternity), negators of attributes.
2
Negate imperfections
Look at negations: no likeness, no opposite, no indwelling, no originated events, no locus, no deficiency attributes.
These negations protect God's transcendence.
3
Understand God's will and actions
Examine the statements about will, self-sufficiency, no obligation, reward by grace, punishment by justice, no purpose, no judge.
God's actions are not constrained by external factors.
4
Note the vision of God
The passage includes that God will be seen in the afterlife without impression or ray. This is an attribute related to the afterlife.
This affirms the Sunni doctrine of ru'ya.

Commentary
This passage is a comprehensive creedal summary of the saved sect, identified as the Ash'aris, the Salaf among hadith scholars, and Ahl al-Sunna wa al-Jama'a. It begins by explicitly naming them as the group the Prophet referred to in the hadith. Then it presents their core doctrines in a contrastive manner, often stating a belief followed by a refutation of a deviant sect. The doctrines include: the temporal origination of knowledge (against those who say it is eternal), existence of God (against Batiniyya), uniqueness as creator (against Qadariyya), description with attributes (against Mu'ammariyya and negators of attributes), no likeness (against Mushabbiha), no opposite or equal (against Habitiyya who posited two gods), no indwelling (against extremists), no originated events in God's essence (against Karamiyya), God not in a locus or direction (against those who liken Him to bodies), negation of deficiency attributes (against those who permit them), visibility of God to believers in the afterlife (without impression or ray), absolute will of God, self-sufficiency, no obligation upon God, rahma, resurrection and afterlife details (bridge, balance, paradise, hell, forgiveness, intercession), sending of messengers, special status of the people of Badr and Ridwan, the correct order of the caliphs (Abu Bakr, Umar, Uthman, Ali), and the principle of not excommunicating Muslims except for denial of God, polytheism, denial of prophethood, or denial of essential religious matters known by necessity or by consensus. The passage ends with a remark on consensus-based excommunication. The style is a running list of positive beliefs with corresponding refutations.

Eschatology: Resurrection and Afterlife
Affirms the reality of the bodily resurrection and all eschatological events.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the Sunni doctrine of the afterlife, including bodily resurrection, judgment, paradise, hell, and intercession.

Core Idea
The bodily resurrection and all eschatological events are true and will occur as described.

Having established the doctrine of God, Sanusi now turns to the afterlife. He affirms the bodily resurrection, the reckoning, the scales, the creation of paradise and hell, the eternity of believers in paradise and disbelievers in hell, and the permissibility of forgiveness and intercession.

Guided Path
1
Affirm bodily resurrection
Read the affirmation of the bodily resurrection (ma'ad jismani). This is a central tenet of Ahl al-Sunna.
2
List the events of the afterlife
Note the other eschatological events: requital, account, the Bridge (sirat), the Balance (mizan), creation of paradise and hell.
These are all real and will happen.
3
Understand eternity and forgiveness
The passage states that paradise and hell are created, and their inhabitants will remain forever. Forgiveness for sinners is possible, and intercession is true.
This balances divine justice and mercy.

Prophethood, Imamate, and Takfir
Covers prophethood, imamate, and the criteria for takfir.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the Sunni position on prophethood, the righteous caliphs, and the conditions under which a Muslim is declared a disbeliever.

Core Idea
Prophets are sent with miracles, the imams after the Prophet are Abu Bakr, Umar, Uthman, and Ali in order of superiority, and takfir is restricted to denial of essentials.

This final part of the creed covers prophethood, the special status of certain Companions, the rightful caliphs, and the critical issue of excommunication. Sanusi affirms the sending of messengers with miracles, honors the participants of Bay'at al-Ridwan and Badr, states the order of the caliphs, and sets strict limits on declaring Muslims disbelievers.

Guided Path
1
Affirm prophethood
Read the affirmation that messengers are sent with miracles from Adam to Muhammad.
2
Honor the Companions
Note the special mention of the people of Bay'at al-Ridwan and Badr as people of paradise.
This reflects Sunni veneration of the Companions.
3
Understand the imamate
The imam must be appointed; the rightful caliphs are Abu Bakr, Umar, Uthman, Ali in that order of superiority.
This is the Sunni position on the caliphate.
4
Learn the limits of takfir
Examine the conditions for declaring a Muslim a disbeliever: denial of God, polytheism, denial of prophethood, denial of necessary religious matters, or denial of consensus on prohibitions.
This principle prevents unjust excommunication.
