The Sarfah Doctrine and the Proof of Prophethood
Discussion of the sarfah doctrine and its relation to the Quran's inimitability.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the sarfah doctrine and how, whether the Quran's inimitability is intrinsic or due to divine turning away, it remains a miracle that proves prophethood.

Core Idea
Whichever explanation for the Quran's inimitability, the miracle stands.

The doctrine of sarfah offers an alternative explanation for the Quran's inimitability. Some argued that the Arabs were 'turned away' from producing a challenge, rather than the Quran being intrinsically superior. In this passage, the commentator clarifies that both possibilities—intrinsic inimitability or sarfah—are miraculous and suffice to establish Muhammad's prophethood.

Two narrations of inanimate objects glorifying Allah

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand how the Prophet's miracles included inanimate objects audibly glorifying Allah, demonstrating their responsiveness to divine power.

Core Idea
Pebbles and fruit praised Allah when the Prophet was present.

These are not metaphors but literal events that confirm the prophetic connection between the physical world and divine will. Let's examine the details of these two miracles.

Read the fruit narration

Misconception: Each miracle must be individually mutawatir to be used as proof.
Correction: The author clarifies that the common miraculous element across all types is mutawatir, which is sufficient.

Checkpoint
What is the author's main argument regarding the tawatur of these miracles?
A) Each miracle is individually mutawatir.
B) The common element of miracle-working is mutawatir across all types.
C) Only the Quran is mutawatir.
D) The miracles are not needed because of the Quran.

Key Takeaway
The tawatur of the collective miracles proves prophethood without requiring each event to be individually mass-transmitted.
