Three Testimonies on al-Matalib al-Aliya
Provides three testimonies about the authorship and significance of al-Matalib al-Aliya.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the scholarly testimony of three major Islamic scholars regarding the authorship and significance of al-Matalib al-Aliya by Fakhr al-Din al-Razi.

Core Idea
Three great scholars confirm al-Razi's authorship and the work's supreme status.

This brief passage is a treasure of bibliographic evidence. It presents three independent testimonies from Ibn Abi Usaybi'a, Ibn Taymiyya, and Ibn al-Qayyim, each affirming the authorship and value of al-Matalib al-Aliya. These statements help establish the work's place in the Islamic scholarly tradition.

Guided Path
1
Read the passage and translation
Read the Arabic passage once without analyzing. Then read the literal translation to understand the basic meaning.
Notice the numbered format of the testimonies.
2
Identify each testimony
Identify the three scholars quoted and what each one specifically says about the book al-Matalib al-Aliya.
Ibn Abi Usaybi'a gives bibliographic details, Ibn Taymiyya praises its content, Ibn al-Qayyim confirms a specific part.
3
Reflect on the significance
Consider why these three testimonies are important for establishing the authenticity and importance of the work.
These are respected scholars from different fields: a historian, a theologian, and a jurist.

Commentary
This passage provides three testimonies about the work. First, the historian Ibn Abi Usaybi'a confirms that the book is by al-Razi and consists of three volumes. Second, Ibn Taymiyya states that it was al-Razi's final work and contains the pinnacle of his knowledge. Third, Ibn al-Qayyim attests that the seventh part, titled al-Arwah al-Aliya wa al-Safila, was used by him in his book al-Ruh.

Concluding Remarks and Colophon of the First Volume
Al-Razi concludes the volume with a note on his previous work and the date of completion.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the historical and bibliographic significance of the colophon in al-Razi's al-Matalib al-Aliyah.

Core Idea
The colophon is a historical marker that also points to al-Razi's broader project.

This lesson examines the concluding remarks of the first volume of al-Matalib al-Aliyah. While brief, the colophon provides a precise date of completion and a reference to al-Razi's earlier work, offering insight into his scholarly method. It also sets the stage for the second volume on tawhid.

Guided Path
1
Read the colophon passage
Read the Arabic colophon carefully, then its translation. Notice the formal structure: a note on previous work, a closing statement, the date, blessings, and a preview of the next volume.
This is a standard form for concluding a work in the Islamic scholarly tradition.
2
Identify the date and reference
Locate the date of completion: Friday, Dhu al-Qa'dah 603 AH. Also note the reference to 'the book containing the investigation into space and time' (likely al-Razi's earlier work on physics).
This date helps contextualize the work in al-Razi's career (he died in 606 AH).
3
Reflect on the transition to the second volume
The colophon announces that the second volume will cover 'the proofs indicating tawhid.' Consider how the first volume's discussions (on divine science) lead into this next topic.
This shows the planned structure of the entire work.

And know that we have discussed the topics of azal and abad in complete detail in the book that contains the investigation into the reality of space and time.

And let this be the end of our speech in the first book of Divine Science.

And the author, our master the caller to God, may God have mercy on him, said: It was completed on Friday of Dhu al-Qa'dah in the year 603 [AH]. And praise be to God in every circumstance.

And may God bless our master Muhammad, the unlettered Prophet, the pure and virtuous, and his family and companions and grant them peace. Whenever the rememberers remember You, and the heedless are heedless of Your remembrance.

The first part of the book (al-Matalib al-Aliyah min al-Ilm al-Ilahi, by Imam Fakhr al-Din al-Razi, and al-Tanzih) is completed.

It will be followed by the second part. Its subject is: (The proofs indicating tawhid).

Commentary
This passage serves as the conclusion of the first volume of al-Matalib al-Aliyah. It contains several elements: first, a note from al-Razi referring readers to his previous work on space and time for a fuller treatment of azal and abad. Then, a formal statement ending the first book of Divine Science. Following that, the colophon supplies the precise date of completion: Friday, Dhu al-Qa'dah 603 AH (approximately July 1207 CE). The author is referred to as 'our master the caller to God' (مولانا الداعي إلى الله), highlighting his role as a guide. The passage ends with blessings upon the Prophet and a note that the second volume will deal with proofs of tawhid (divine oneness).
