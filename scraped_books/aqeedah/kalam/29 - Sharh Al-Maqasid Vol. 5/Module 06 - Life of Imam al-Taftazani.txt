Learning Objective

For the Ash'aris in the science of usul, and the exegetes in the science of tafsir, their leader is Ibn Abbas his student. And the shaykhs in the science of secret and purification of the inner, the reference in it is to the pure progeny. And the science of grammar only appeared from him. And due to this he said: If I broke the cushion then sat on it, I would judge between the people of the Torah by their Torah, and between the people of the Gospel by their Gospel, and between the people of the Psalms by their Psalms, and between the people of the Furqan by their Furqan. By Allah, no verse was revealed on land or sea, or plain or mountain, or sky or earth, or night or day, but I know about whom it was revealed and in what thing it was revealed. And also he is the bravest of them, as indicated by his frequent jihad in the path of Allah, and his good courage in the expeditions, which are well-known and need no explanation, and therefore the Prophet (peace be upon him) said: There is no youth but Ali, and no sword but Dhu al-Fiqar. And he said on the Day of the Confederates: The strike of Ali is better than the worship of the two weighty ones. And also he is the most ascetic of them, due to the mutawatir reports of his turning away from the pleasures of this world despite his ability to obtain them because of the vastness of the doors of this world opening to him. And therefore he said: O world, away from me! Do you come to me or do you desire me? No, not your time. Far, far away. Deceive someone else. I have no need of you. I have divorced you thrice with no recourse. Your life is short, your share is little, and your hope is contemptible. And he said: By Allah, this world of yours is more insignificant in my eyes than the snout of a pig in the hand of a leper. And he said: By Allah, the bliss of this world of yours is more insignificant to me than the bleat of a goat. And also he is the most worshipful of them, such that it is narrated that his forehead became like the knee of a camel due to the length of his prostration. And the most generous of them, until it was revealed concerning him and his household: 'And they give food, despite their love for it, to the poor, the orphan, and the captive' (76:8). And the most noble in character and cheerfulness of face, to the extent that he was attributed with humor. And the most forbearing of them, so that he left Ibn Muljam in his abode and neighborhood, giving him his provisions, knowing his situation, and he forgave Marwan when he was taken on the Day of the Camel despite the intensity of his enmity towards him and his statement about him: 'The nation will meet from him and his progeny a red day.' And also he is the most eloquent of them in tongue, as witnessed by the book Nahj al-Balaghah, and the earliest of them in accepting Islam, as it is narrated that the Prophet was sent on Monday and Ali accepted Islam on Tuesday. In summary, his merits are too apparent to be hidden and too numerous to be counted.
