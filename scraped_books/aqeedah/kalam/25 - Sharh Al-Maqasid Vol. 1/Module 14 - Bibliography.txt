Commentary
This passage is an enumeration (taʻdād) of the works of ʻAḍud al-Dīn al-Ījī. It lists nine titles with varying degrees of description. The first work, Akhlāq ʻAḍud al-Dīn, is a summary on ethics arranged in four treatises, and it received a commentary by his student al-Kirmānī. The second, Ādāb al-ʻAllāma, is on the etiquette of the scholar (possibly on dialectic?), with commentaries and glosses by al-Tabrīzī, Mīr Abū al-Fatḥ, and al-Jurjānī. The third, Ashraf al-Tawārīkh, is a universal history summarized from the beginning of creation, later translated into Turkish. The fourth, Jawāhir al-Kalām, is a concise kalām text similar to al-Mawāqif but smaller, written for the vizier Ghiyāth al-Dīn. The fifth is simply noted as a treatise on al-waḍʻ, a topic in logic and language regarding how words acquire meaning. The sixth, al-ʻAqāʼid al-ʻAḍudiyya, is a concise creedal work and his last composition. The seventh, al-Fawāʼid al-Ghiyāthiyya, is a work on rhetoric (al-maʻānī wa al-bayān) abridged from the third section of al-Sakkākī's Miftāḥ al-ʻUlūm, more concise than al-Khaṭīb al-Qazwīnī's Talkhīṣ al-Miftāḥ. The eighth is a commentary on Ibn al-Ḥājib's Muntahā al-Suʼl, a work on uṣūl al-fiqh and jadal (dialectic). The ninth is his magnum opus, al-Mawāqif, already mentioned in the previous lesson, dedicated to the same vizier.
