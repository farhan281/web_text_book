Refutation of the Third Argument: Necessity and Thubut

Refutation of the Fifth Argument: Generality and Fixedness
Taftazani refutes the fifth argument by denying that generality implies pure affirmation and by rebutting the objection of distinctness.

Alternative Proof: Divine Power Negates Fixedness of the Non-Existent
Taftazani presents an argument that divine power would be impossible if the non-existent had fixedness.

Learning Objective

If the non-existent were already fixed, creation would be impossible.

This lesson presents a key argument within Ash'ari theology. Some scholars did not consider the denial of thubut for the non-existent to be self-evident, so they provided rational proofs. The first proof, given here, is based on divine power (qudra) and its relationship to contingent beings.

The Imam's Argument: Non-Existence Must Be Pure Negation
Imam's syllogism linking non-existence, negation, and positivity.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the logical syllogism the Imam uses to argue that non-existence (al-'adam) cannot be a positive attribute, because that would make negation positive as well.

Core Idea
If non-existence were positive, negation would become positive — a contradiction.

The Imam begins by presenting a dense argument that non-existence is not a positive attribute but pure negation. He constructs a syllogism to show that if non-existence were positive, then negation would also be positive, which is impossible. This lesson unpacks his reasoning step by step.

Guided Path
1
Read the Imam's syllogism
Read the Arabic passage that contains the Imam's core argument. Then follow the translation. Focus on the structure: non-existence as predicate, its relation to negation.
The Imam uses a conditional: if non-existence is not pure negation, then it is positive.
2
Identify the three propositions
The Imam states a syllogism: every negation is non-existence, every non-existence is positive, therefore every negation is positive. Identify each proposition in the text.
The conclusion is said to be impossible (muhal).
3
Understand why the conclusion is impossible
The Imam asserts that the conclusion is absurd because it would mean that a negative concept (negation) is positive, which violates the law of non-contradiction.
This is a reductio ad absurdum argument.
