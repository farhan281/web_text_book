Commentary
This passage is a series of six biographical entries from the introduction of Sharh al-Maqasid, listing scholars connected to al-Taftazani. The author (the editor or commentator) provides what is known about each figure, often citing sources. The passage proceeds in numerical order:

1. Entry 13 (Shihab al-Din Muhammad): The author notes that this person is mentioned by al-Laknawi in his biography of al-Jami as having attended lessons of Shihab al-Din Muhammad, a student of al-Taftazani. Despite extensive research, no further biographical details are found.

His grandson's biography indicates he was from Transoxiana and a student of al-Taftazani. He studied under al-Taftazani, and Mahmud ibn al-'Ayni read to him works like al-Mufassal and al-Tawdih with al-Tanqih. Some students of al-Taftazani are barely known despite their scholarly contributions.

After this, these are a group of giants who studied under Sa'd al-Din al-Taftazani, sitting before him, receiving knowledge from him orally, listening to his discourse, and exchanging with him the various branches and colors of knowledge.
But there are huge groups of professors and thinkers in the Western and Eastern worlds who studied his books, drank from his springs, and they were provisions in their academic preparation and intellectual formation.
Indeed, it is a school rich in knowledge, abundant in giving, and will continue throughout history until Allah inherits the earth and those upon it.

Commentary
This passage is a biographical note and concluding remark about the students and legacy of Sa'd al-Din al-Taftazani. It first mentions Mahmud al-Sira'i, citing the authority of Imam al-Sakhawi, who in his biography of Yusuf ibn al-Hasan ibn Mahmud al-Sira'i reports that the grandfather, Mahmud, was said to have studied under al-Taftazani and others. The passage notes that al-Sakhawi, as is his custom in al-Daw' al-Lami', often does not provide detailed biographical dates or insights. The passage concludes by describing the Taftazani school as rich in knowledge, abundant in giving, and enduring until Allah inherits the earth.
