قوله : ( لا في المدة لا يخفى أن كلمة لا هذه ليست لنفي الجنس، ولا المشابهة بليس وهو ظاهر، وليست عاطفة لاختصاصها بعطف مفرد على مفرد مثبت ولا زائدة لأنها مخصوصة بتقدم واو العطف عليها أو بوقوعها بين المضاف والمضاف إليه وبالتقدم على القسم نص عليه في الرضي فالوجه أن يقدر الفعل بعده أي لا يفيد أثراً غير متناهية في المدة وتكون الجملة عطف بيان للجملة السابقة لكون الثانية مشتملة على تفصيل فإنه الأولى ولا في قوله ولا في الشدة ولا في العدة زائدة لتأكيد معنى النفي يفيد أن المراد نفي كل منها لا نفي المجموع وكلمة في متعلقة بمثناه المقدر هكذا ينبغي أن يفهم ولو ترك كلمة لا الأولى لكان أظهر إلا أن ذكره آكد .
His saying: '(Not in duration)' — It is not hidden that the word 'la' here is not for negation of the genus, nor is it like 'laysa' (which is a negative verb), and that is apparent; nor is it a conjunctive (ʿāṭifa) because it is specific to conjoining a positive singular to a singular; nor is it extra (zā'ida) because it is specifically for the precedence of the conjunction 'wāw' upon it, or its occurrence between a possessive construction (muḍāf and muḍāf ilayhi), or its precedence over an oath, as stated in al-Rāḍī. So the proper way is to estimate a verb after it: i.e., it does not produce an infinite effect in duration, and the sentence is explanatory paraphrase (ʿaṭf bayān) of the previous sentence because the second sentence contains a detail. The 'la' in 'la fi al-shidda' and 'la fi al-ʿidda' is extra (zā'ida) to emphasize the meaning of negation, indicating that the intention is to negate each one individually, not the sum. The word 'fi' is attached to its implied object. This is how it should be understood. And if the first 'la' were omitted, it would be clearer, but its mention is more emphatic.

Commentary
This passage is a grammatical commentary on the phrase 'la fi al-mudda' from the previous text. The commentator examines the function of the particle 'la' and concludes that it is neither a negation of the genus (nafy al-jins), nor a verb like 'laysa', nor a conjunctive (ʿāṭifa), nor an extra (zā'ida) in the sense of being redundant. Instead, the proper analysis is to supply an implied verb after 'la', such that the meaning is 'it does not produce an infinite effect in duration'. The phrase then serves as an explanatory paraphrase (ʿaṭf bayān) of the earlier statement. The second and third instances of 'la' in 'la fi al-shidda' and 'la fi al-ʿidda' are considered extra (zā'ida) for emphasis, reinforcing that each type is negated individually rather than the sum. The commentator also notes that the preposition 'fi' is attached to its implied object. If the first 'la' were omitted, the meaning would be clearer, but its inclusion adds emphasis. This analysis shows the precision of the Arabic language in conveying nuanced negation.
