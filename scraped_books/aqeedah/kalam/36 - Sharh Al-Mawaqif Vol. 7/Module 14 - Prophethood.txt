The Hadith Proof for the Existence of the Intellect
The author presents a prophetic hadith and reconciles conflicting versions to prove the intellect's existence.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand how the philosophers reconcile the hadiths about the first creation to prove the existence of the intellect.

Core Idea
The first creation is one entity with three names: intellect, pen, and light.

This lesson presents the transmitted (naqli) evidence for the existence of the intellect. The philosophers cite a prophetic hadith stating the first creation was the intellect, but there are other hadiths mentioning the pen and the light of the Prophet. The author explains how these are reconciled by viewing the same first effect from different aspects, establishing the intellect's foundational role.

Guided Path
1
Read the passage and translation
Read the Arabic passage once, then its literal translation. Notice that the passage is part of a larger section on proving the intellect.
The passage starts with 'The First Objective: On Proving the Intellect'.
2
Identify the three hadiths
The passage mentions three hadiths: 'The first thing God created was the intellect', 'the pen', and 'my light'. Recognize that these seem contradictory at first glance.
3
Understand the reconciliation
The philosophers say the first effect (first creation) is one entity called 'intellect', 'pen', and 'light' depending on which aspect is considered. Study the three aspects: intellect (as simple substance pen (as intermediary for existents), light (as intermediary for prophethood).
This shows the intellect's primacy as the first creation, but also its role in creation and prophethood.
4
Note the transition to rational proofs
The passage ends by stating that the philosophers also provide two rational proofs for the intellect's existence. These will be covered in the next lesson.

Commentary
This passage presents the transmitted (naqli) evidence for the existence of the intellect. It is the first part of the 'First Objective: On Proving the Intellect.' The author reports that the philosophers (al-hukama) cite a hadith: 'The first thing God created was the intellect.' There are also other hadiths saying the first creation was the pen or the light of the Prophet. The author explains a reconciliation: the first effect (the first creation) is one entity seen from different aspects. As a simple substance that intellects itself and its Creator, it is called 'intellect'; as an intermediary for the creation of other things and the souls of sciences, it is called 'pen'; as the intermediary for bestowing prophetic light, it is called 'light' of the Prophet. This shows the intellect's primacy. Then the author notes that they (the philosophers) also provide two rational proofs, which are introduced by the phrase 'with two proofs' (bi-wajhain). The actual proofs follow in the next lesson.

Misconception & Correction
Misconception: The hadiths that the first creation was the intellect, the pen, and the light are contradictory and cannot all be true.
Correction: The philosophers reconcile them by saying the first effect is one entity called by different names depending on which aspect is considered.

Checkpoint
How do the philosophers reconcile the hadiths stating the first creation was the intellect, the pen, and the light?
A) They reject two of the hadiths as weak
B) They say each refers to a different creation at different times
C) They say the first effect is one entity called by different names according to its aspects
D) They ignore the hadiths and rely solely on reason

Key Takeaway
The first creation is a single entity that can be called intellect, pen, or light depending on the perspective.

How This Lesson Applies — to MY Life?
?
If the first creation is the intellect, and it intellects its Creator, then your own intellect is the starting point for knowing Allah. Are you using your intellect to contemplate His signs, or are you neglecting this divine gift?
?
The first creation is also the pen, the means by which sciences flow. What are you doing to acquire beneficial knowledge that draws you closer to the Source of all knowledge?
?
The first creation is the light of the Prophet. Since that light mediates prophethood, do you recognize the Prophet's role as the ultimate conduit for divine guidance? How does that change your relationship with his sunnah?
