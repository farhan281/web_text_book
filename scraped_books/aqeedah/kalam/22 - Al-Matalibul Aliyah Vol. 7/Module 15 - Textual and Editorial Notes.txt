Concluding Supplication and Book Completion
The author concludes with a supplication and completion note.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the theological themes in the author's concluding supplication, including God's absolute transcendence and mercy, and how the work is formally concluded.

Core Idea
In our final prayer, we affirm Allah's absolute transcendence and our utter dependence on His pure mercy.

The author brings his work to a close with a humble supplication that encapsulates the key themes of the entire book: God's transcendence, His independence, and His mercy. This passage is not just a prayer; it is a summary of the theological posture the author wishes to leave with his reader.

Guided Path
1

Read the Arabic passage once without analysis, then read the translation. Let the devotional tone and structure sink in.
Notice the shift from awe to hope.

The supplication highlights God's transcendence (beyond intellect, bodies) and His independence. Reflect on how these themes relate to the book's earlier discussions.

4

The final sentence records the completion of the book. Consider the significance of such historical notes in scholarly works.
This provides a date and occasion for the book's conclusion.

ونختم الكتاب بهذا التضرع فأقول : ( أنت المتعالي عن علائق العقول والفهوم، وأنت المقدس عن مناسبات الأعراض والجسوم، وأنت الموصوف بأنك أنت الحي القيوم . فلا نهاية لجنودك من الروحانيات ولا غاية لأحوال عبادك من الجسمانيات. وأنت مقدس عن الاعتضاد بهذه الجنود والعساكر . وأنت الغني عن الكل في الأول وفي الآخر. فإذا كنت غنياً عنهم مع جلالـة أحوالهم، وحسن أفعالهم. فمن أين يحصل لهذه الذرة الضعيفة والنسمة النحيفة، عند طلوع نور كبريائك ؟ وكيف يمكن أن يكون له ذكر عند إشراق صفاتك وأسمائك ؟ فإذا اعتبرت هذه الحالة، ضعف رجائي ونفذ دعائي وأما إذا اعتبرت أنك عاملت الكل بالرحمة مع الاستغناء عنهم، وأمطرت عليهم سحائب الرحمة مع عدم الاحتياج إليهم. فعند هذا أقول : يا أيها الموصوف بالأزلية والقدم. ويا أيها المتعالي عن قبول الفناء والعدم : إن أعطيت أحداً لأجل أنه استحق منك تلك النعمة والرحمة، فلا تلتفت إلى دعائي وندائي وأما إن كان كل من فاز بنوع من الرحمة، ووصل إلى قسط من النعمة، فإنما وصل إليه بمحض فضلك بالخير والرحمة قبل الموت، وعند الموت، وبعد الموت . يا أكرم الأكرمين، ويا أرحم الراحمين، ويا إله الأولين والآخرين ) . قال مصنف الكتاب رضي الله عنه : تم هذا الكتاب بكرة يوم الاثنين .
And we conclude the book with this supplication, so I say: (You are transcendent above the attachments of intellects and understandings, and You are sanctified from the associations of accidents and bodies, and You are described as being the Living, the Self-Subsisting. There is no end to Your hosts of spiritual beings and no limit to the states of Your servants of bodily beings. And You are sanctified from being supported by these hosts and armies. And You are independent of all in the first and the last. So if You are independent of them despite the majesty of their states and the beauty of their actions, then how can this weak atom and thin breath obtain at the rising of the light of Your majesty? And how can it have mention at the shining of Your attributes and names? So if I consider this state, my hope weakens and my supplication ceases. But if I consider that You have dealt with all with mercy despite Your independence from them, and have rained upon them clouds of mercy despite not needing them, then at this I say: O You who are described by pre-eternity and priority, O You who are transcendent above accepting annihilation and non-existence: if You give to someone because he deserves that grace and mercy from You, then pay no attention to my supplication and call. But if everyone who obtains some mercy and reaches a portion of grace only reaches it by Your pure bounty with goodness and mercy before death, at death, and after death – O Most Generous of the generous, O Most Merciful of the merciful, O God of the first and the last.) The author of the book, may Allah be pleased with him, said: This book was completed on the morning of Monday.

Commentary
This passage contains the author's concluding supplication and the colophon. The supplication begins by affirming God's absolute transcendence: He is beyond all mental and physical categories, described as the Living and Self-Subsisting. The author acknowledges God's infinite spiritual hosts and His independence from them. Reflecting on this, the author expresses his own insignificance: how can a weak atom of a human being attain anything in the presence of God's majesty? This thought weakens his hope. However, he then considers God's mercy, which is given freely without need. This leads to a prayer: if God gives only to those who deserve, then the author's plea would be futile, but he hopes that everything is by God's pure bounty, before death, at death, and after death. The supplication ends with praises to God's generosity and mercy. The colophon states that the book was completed on Monday morning, likely providing a historical note.
