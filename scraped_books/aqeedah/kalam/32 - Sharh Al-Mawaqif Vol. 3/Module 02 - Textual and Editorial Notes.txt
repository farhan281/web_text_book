Linguistic Clarification of 'Yutakhim al-Yaqin'
Commentator explains meaning of 'يتاخم اليقين' and corrects misreadings.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the correct meaning and reading of the phrase 'يتاخم اليقين' and the common errors made by scholars.

Core Idea
يتاخم means 'borders on' not 'reaches' — a crucial distinction for certainty.

This passage from the commentary clarifies a key phrase in the original text: 'حسباناً يتاخم اليقين'. The commentator explains the linguistic roots of 'يتاخم' and corrects several misreadings that have occurred among scholars. Understanding this helps avoid misunderstanding the relationship between conjecture and certainty.

Guided Path
1

Read the Arabic passage once without analyzing. Then read the full literal translation to get the overall meaning.
Pay attention to the three 'qawl' phrases.
2
Identify the three commented phrases
The commentator comments on three parts of the original text: the existent/non-existent distinction, the sophistry remark, and the phrase 'يتاخم اليقين'. Notice that the third is the main focus.
The first two are brief clarifications.
3
Focus on the linguistic analysis
The commentator cites lexical sources (Taj al-Bayhaqi, al-Qamus, al-Asas) to define 'يتاخم' as 'bordering'. He then lists errors: some changed the meaning, some misread the word.
Note the root 'تخوم'.
4
Understand the correct meaning
From the teacher, the correct reading is 'يتاخم' with ta', meaning 'bordering on certainty'. The intent is nearness, not arrival; otherwise it would be certainty.
Misreadings include 'نخم' and 'فخم'.

قوله : (لما عرفت أن الموجود إلخ والأظهر الأخصر وبطلانه ضروري إن أريد بالموجود ما له تحقق وبالمعدوم ما ليس كذلك، إذ لا واسطة بين النفي والإثبات وإن أريد معنى آخر يكون النزاع لفظياً. قوله : ( فإن أريد نفي ذلك فهو (سفسطة لا حاجة إلى هذه المقدمة، وإنما ذكرها لمجرد الاستظهار والمبالغة. قوله : ( يتاخم اليقين في تاج البيهقي : المتاخمة حد زميني بزميني بيوسته شدن، وفي القاموس : ديارنا تتاخم دياركم أي تحادّها، وكذا في الأساس، فقد ظهر أنه زل فيه أقدام الناظرين فبعضهم غيروا المعنى، وبعضهم صحفوا اللفظ بالنون أو الفاء بدل التاء. قوله : ( يتاخم اليقين سماعنا من الأستاذ المحقق : يتاخم بالتاء المثناة من فوق من تخوم الأرضين وهي حدودها ونهاياتها على ما ذكره الفراء، ومعناه ظناً ينتهي إلى اليقين، والمقصود قربه منه لا الوصول إليه وإلا لم يكن ظناً، وبعضهم صححه بالنون من النخم قال وهو حد الأرض لكن لم يذكر في الصحاح، ومنهم من صححه بالفاء من المفاخمة والظاهر أنه تصحيف لعبارة الكتاب وإن كان له وجه بحسب المعنى.
His statement: 'For you know that the existent etc.' The clearer and more concise is that its falsity is necessary if by 'existent' is meant that which has actuality and by 'non-existent' that which is not such, for there is no intermediary between negation and affirmation; and if another meaning is intended, the dispute becomes verbal. His statement: 'For if it is intended to deny that, it is sophistry'—there is no need for this premise, he only mentioned it for extra support and emphasis. His statement: 'bordering on certainty'—in the Taj al-Bayhaqi: 'al-mutakhamah' means a boundary touching another boundary, and in al-Qamus: 'Our land borders your land' i.e., it is adjacent to it, and likewise in al-Asas. So it is clear that the feet of the scholars have slipped here; some have changed the meaning, and some have miswritten the word with a nun or a fa' instead of the ta'. His statement: 'bordering on certainty' we heard from the esteemed teacher that it is 'yutakhim' with the ta' (the dotted from above) from 'tukhūm al-ardīn' (the borders of the lands) which are their limits and ends, as mentioned by al-Farra', and its meaning is a conjecture that reaches certainty, and the intent is its closeness to it, not reaching it, for otherwise it would not be conjecture. Some corrected it with a nun from 'an-nakham', saying it means the boundary of the earth, but it was not mentioned in as-Sihah. And some corrected it with a fa' from 'al-mufākhamah', and it appears that it is a miswriting of the wording of the book, even if it has a meaning lexically.

Commentary
This passage is a commentary by the author of the Sharh on specific phrases from the original text. He provides linguistic clarifications and corrects misreadings. First, he comments on the phrase 'لما عرفت أن الموجود إلخ' (for you know that the existent etc.), stating that the clearer expression is that its falsity is necessary if 'existent' means that which has actuality, etc., and if another meaning is intended, the dispute is verbal. Then he comments on the phrase 'فإن أريد نفي ذلك فهو سفسطة' (for if it is intended to deny that, it is sophistry), saying that this premise is not strictly needed; the author only mentioned it for extra emphasis. The main focus is on the phrase 'حسباناً يتاخم اليقين' (a conjecture bordering on certainty). The commentator explains the word 'يتاخم' by citing lexical sources: in Tāj al-Bayhaqī, it means touching boundaries; in al-Qāmūs, 'our land borders your land' means it is adjacent; similarly in al-Asās. He notes that scholars have fallen into error regarding this word: some changed its meaning, others misread it with a nūn (يتناخم) or a fā' (يتفاخم) instead of the tā'. He then reports from his esteemed teacher that the correct reading is 'يتاخم' with the tā', derived from 'تخوم الأرضين' (the borders of the lands), as mentioned by al-Farrā'. The meaning is a conjecture that ends at certainty, but the intent is nearness to certainty, not reaching it, because otherwise it would be certainty, not conjecture. He also mentions that some scholars corrected it to 'يتناخم' from 'النخم', which is said to mean the boundary of the earth, but this word is not found in as-Sihāh. Others corrected it to 'يتفاخم' from 'المفاخمة', but this appears to be a distortion of the original text, even if it has some lexical basis.

Grammatical Construction of 'Wa Imtina' Istinadihi'
Jurjani explains the grammatical function of a phrase.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand why the commentator's phrase 'wa imtina' istinadihi' is not intended to validate a grammatical construction but to clarify meaning, and how the verb 'yamtan'u' is analyzed as a verbal noun.

Core Idea
A grammatical note is not always a grammatical rule.

Jurjani begins by explaining a specific phrase from the text. He wants to prevent a misunderstanding: the phrase is not an attempt to justify a particular syntactic joining. Instead, it is a clarification of meaning. He then illustrates how the verb can be interpreted as a verbal noun, using examples from poetry.

Guided Path
1
Read the passage and translation
Read the Arabic passage once, then the English translation. Note the technical terms: 'عطف الجملة على المفرد' and 'المصدر'.
The passage is dense with grammatical analysis.
2
Identify the commentator's intention
Notice that Jurjani first clarifies what the phrase does NOT mean: it is not trying to validate conjoining a clause to a single word. Then he explains its actual purpose: to clarify meaning.
3
Analyze the grammatical alternatives
Jurjani presents two grammatical interpretations of 'yamtan'u' as a verbal noun: either accusative by deleting 'an', or nominative with an implied operator. He also mentions a metaphorical treatment. Examine the poetic examples.
These are fine points of Arabic syntax.
4
Understand the larger context
This note is a gloss on a prior text. The commentator is helping the reader avoid a misinterpretation. The lesson is that sometimes grammatical explanation serves clarity, not rule-making.

قوله : ( أي وامتناع إسناده ليس مراده تصحيح عطف الجملة على المفرد السابق، أعني جواز استناده بتأويل الفعل بالمصدر إما بناء على نصب يمتنع بحذف أن، أو على رفعه بحذف أن والعدول بعده إليه لفقد العامل الصوري كما في قوله . ولولا تحسبون الحلم عجزاً. لما عدم المسيئون احتمالي. أي ولولا أن تحسبوا أو على تنزيل الفعل منزلة المصدر بإرادة جزء مدلوله مجازاً كما في قوله فقالو ما تشاء فقلت: ألهو . أي اللهو، وذلك لجواز عطف الجملة على المفرد فيما له محل من الإعراب، كما حققته في حواشي المطول، بل مقصوده توضيح المعنى . قوله : ( وأنه أي القصد إلى الإيجاد مقارن للعدم ظهر بهذا أن القصد فينا غير الإرادة، ومتقدم عليها، لما سيجيء أن الإرادة منا لا تتعلق إلا بمقدور مقارن للإرادة عند أهل التحقيق، وهذا القصد متقدم على وجود المقدور.
His saying: (i.e., and the impossibility of its dependence) does not intend to validate the conjunction of a clause to a single word (mufrad) earlier, namely the permissibility of its dependence by interpreting the verb as a verbal noun, either based on the accusative of 'yamtan'u' by deleting 'an', or on its nominative by deleting 'an' and switching to it later due to the absence of a formal operator, as in his saying: 'And were you not to think clemency a weakness, the wrongdoers would not have hoped for my forbearance' meaning 'and were you not to think', or by treating the verb as a verbal noun by intending a part of its meaning metaphorically, as in his saying: 'They said: What do you wish? I said: Play' meaning 'play'. And that is because it is permissible to conjoin a clause to a single word that occupies a grammatical position, as I have verified in my glosses on al-Mutawwal. Rather, his purpose is to clarify the meaning. His saying: (And that, i.e., the intention to bring into existence is concomitant with non-existence. By this it appears that intention (qasd) in us is other than will (irada), and precedes it, because it will come that the will in us does not relate except to something within one's power that is concomitant with the will according to the people of verification. And this intention precedes the existence of the intended thing.

Commentary
This passage is a textual note that addresses two points. First, the commentator explains the grammatical construction of the phrase 'wa imtina' istinadihi' ('and the impossibility of its dependence'). He clarifies that this is not intended to validate conjoining a clause to a single word (mufrad) in the preceding text. Instead, the verb 'yamtan'u' is interpreted as a verbal noun (masdar) either by deleting 'an' (making it accusative) or by treating it as a nominative with an implied operator, with poetic examples. The purpose is merely to clarify the meaning. Second, the commentator draws a theological distinction: the 'intention (qasd) to bring into existence' is different from 'will (irada)' in humans. Qasd precedes irada, because irada only relates to something that is concomitant with it at the time of the act. This qasd is prior to the existence of the intended thing. This nuance is important for understanding God's attributes in kalam.

Distinction Between Qasd and Irada in Humans
Jurjani distinguishes qasd from irada in humans.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand how intention (qasd) differs from will (irada) in humans, and why qasd precedes irada according to the people of verification.

Core Idea
Intention precedes will in human action.

In the second part of this passage, Jurjani draws a theological distinction between two closely related concepts: qasd (intention) and irada (will). He explains that in humans, intention to bring something into existence is concomitant with non-existence and actually precedes the will, because will only attaches to something that is within one's power at the moment of willing. This nuance is important for understanding God's attributes in kalam.

Understand the reasoning
