The Circularity Argument Against Determination Inhering in Essence
Explains why determination cannot be caused by what inheres in the essence due to circularity.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand why determination cannot be caused by something that inheres in the essence, due to circularity.

Core Idea
Inherence presupposes determination, so it cannot cause it without circularity.

This short passage from a kalam text presents a logical proof against the idea that an essence becomes determinate because of something that inheres in it. The argument is based on the simple observation that inherence requires prior determination, leading to a vicious circle. Let's read the passage and unpack its reasoning.

وإلا فلا يعلل بما يحل فيها؛ لأنه فرع تعينها؛ لأنها ما لم تتعين في نفسها لم يتصور حلول شيء فيها، فلا يجوز أن يعلل تعينها بما حل فيها، وإلا دار. قوله : ( وإن لم يعلل التعين إلخ) أي لا يكفي الفاعل مع الماهية في اقتضاء التعين، بل يكون نسبتهما إلى جميع التعينات على السواء، فلا بد من أمر آخر مخصص. قوله : (بما يحل فيها أي من حيث حلوله فيها بأن يكون ذلك الأمر باعتبار حلوله في الماهية مخصصاً لفيضان التعين المخصوص، وإنما قيدنا بالحيثية لأنه بدون اعتبار الحلول داخل في المباين. قوله : (لم يتصور إلخ على صيغة المعلوم أي لا يصير ذا صورة حلول شيء فيها إذ الحلول في الأمر المبهم محال بالبديهة، فيكون حلول شيء في الماهية موقوفاً على تعينها، وتعينها لكونه معلولاً لذلك الشيء باعتبار الحلول موقوفاً على الحلول فيدور.
Otherwise it is not caused by what inheres in it, because it is a branch of its determination; because as long as it is not determined in itself, it is not conceivable for something to inhere in it. So it is not permissible for its determination to be caused by what inheres in it, otherwise circularity occurs. His saying: (And if determination is not caused etc.) means the agent along with the essence is not sufficient for necessitating determination, rather their relation to all determinations is equal, so another specifying factor is needed. His saying: (by what inheres in it) means from the perspective of it inhering in it, such that that thing, by virtue of its inhering in the essence, specifies the flow of the particular determination. We qualified with the perspective because without considering the inherence, it falls under the separate. His saying: (it is not conceived etc.) in the passive voice means it does not become have a form of something inhering in it, for inherence in an ambiguous thing is impossible by intuition. Thus the inherence of something in the essence depends on its determination, and its determination, being an effect of that thing in terms of inherence, depends on the inherence, leading to circularity.

Commentary
This passage presents a kalam proof against the idea that determination (al-ta'ayyun) can be caused by something that inheres in the essence. The author first states the conditional: if determination is not caused by the essence itself, then it cannot be caused by what inheres in it. The reason is that inherence (al-hulul) is a branch (far') of determination: an essence must first be determined in itself before something can inhere in it. Therefore, using that inherence as the cause of determination leads to a vicious circle (al-dawr). The commentary elaborates: when the original text says 'otherwise it is not caused by what inheres in it,' it means that neither the agent nor the essence alone suffices to produce determination; they relate equally to all determinations, so a separate specifying factor is needed. The phrase 'by what inheres in it' is qualified: it refers to the factor considered precisely as inhering in the essence, not in its own essence. Without this qualification, it would fall under the category of 'separate' (al-mubayin). The commentary also explains the impossibility of inherence in an indeterminate essence by intuition (al-badiha). Thus, the dependence of inherence on determination and determination on inherence creates a circular dependency, which is invalid. The passage refutes any claim that determination could come from an inherent accident.

Misconception & Correction
Misconception: One might think that inherence can cause determination because the inherence itself is a cause.
Correction: The passage shows that inherence presupposes determination, so it cannot be the cause without circularity.

Key Takeaway
Determination cannot be caused by anything that inheres in the essence because inherence itself depends on prior determination, creating an invalid circle.
