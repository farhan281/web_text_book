Defending Created Capability: Potential Not Actual Effect
Al-Taftazani addresses the objection that created capability is not effective, citing al-Amidi's definition.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand how Ash'ari theologians affirm the reality of created capability as a potential attribute, even while maintaining that God alone creates actions.

Core Idea
Created capability is real as a potential, even if only God creates the actual act.

This passage addresses a key objection against the Ash'ari position on human capability. The objector argues that if created capability is not effective, it cannot fit the definitions of capability. Al-Taftazani responds by distinguishing actual effect from potential effect, citing al-Amidi's definition to show that capability's proper function is potential, not necessarily actual. He then uses this to refute the false dilemma that either the servant creates his own action or has no capability at all.

Guided Path
1
Understand the objection
Read the opening objection: the created capability is said to be non-effective according to al-Ash'ari, so it does not enter the two definitions given earlier.
The definitions likely require that capability be effective in some way.
2
Grasp the distinction: actual vs. potential effect
Al-Taftazani answers by clarifying that 'effect' here means potential effect, not actual. Capability is an attribute whose proper function (sha'n) is to effect and create, but this does not mean it always produces its object.
Al-Amidi's definition is quoted to support this understanding.
3
See how this refutes the false dilemma
The Ash'ari middle position avoids both Mu'tazilite self-creation and Jahmite denial of capability. The servant has a real capability as a disposition, but God creates the action in accordance with it.
This reconciles divine omnipotence with human responsibility.

For if it is said: The originated capability (al-qudra al-haditha) is not effective (ghayr mu'aththira) according to al-Shaykh [al-Ash'ari], so it does not enter either of the two definitions. We say: The intended meaning [of effect] is not actual effect but potential effect, meaning that it is an attribute whose proper function is to effect and create, as al-Amidi stated when he said: 'Capability is an existential attribute whose proper function is to effect and create by it, such that the action can be conceived to occur by it instead of omission, and omission instead of action.' And the originated capability is likewise, but it does not [actually] effect because its object occurs by the power of God Almighty, as will come if God wills. And by this is repelled what is said: 'One must either say that the servant's action is by his own capability, as is the Mu'tazilite position, or deny the servant's capability entirely, as Jahm maintained.'

Commentary
This passage presents an objection and its answer concerning the created capability (al-qudra al-haditha). The objection is that according to al-Shaykh (al-Ash'ari), the created capability is not effective (ghayr mu'aththira), so it does not fit either of the two definitions just given (the ones requiring intention/awareness or diversity of effects). The answer distinguishes between actual effect (ta'thir bil-fi'l) and potential effect (ta'thir bil-quwwa). The definition of capability, as al-Amidi explains, is that it is an existential attribute whose proper function (sha'n) is to effect and create, such that the action can be conceived to occur by it instead of omission, and vice versa. The created capability has this potential but does not actually effect because its object (the action) is brought about by God's power. This repels the false dilemma: either one must say the servant creates his own action (Mu'tazilism) or deny the servant any capability (Jahm's view). The Ash'ari middle position is that the servant has capability as a disposition, but God creates the action in accordance with that capability.

Misconception & Correction
Misconception: If a capability does not produce its effect, it is meaningless or non-existent.
Correction: The passage shows that capability is real as a potential attribute; its definition does not require actual effect. It is a real disposition even when overridden by God's direct creation.

Key Takeaway
The Ash'ari view of created capability as a real potential that does not produce its effect independently avoids the extremes of Mu'tazilite self-creation and Jahmite denial of human agency.
