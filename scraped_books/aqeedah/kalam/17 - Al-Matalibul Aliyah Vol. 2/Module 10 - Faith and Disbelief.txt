Excuse for Anthropomorphists and Idol Worshipers
Comparison of excuse conditions for anthropomorphists and idol worshipers.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the conditions under which anthropomorphists and idol worshipers are excused, and why those capable of research have no excuse if they fail to follow the investigating scholars.

Core Idea
Ignorance and upbringing may excuse, but neglecting research removes excuse.

This passage presents a nuanced comparison between anthropomorphists and idol worshipers. It argues that both can be excused due to ignorance or upbringing, but also warns that those who are capable of intellectual investigation cannot claim excuse if they reject the guidance of qualified scholars. The author invites us to consider the limits of religious excuse.

Guided Path

Read the Arabic passage once, then the English translation. Note how the author uses parallelism to compare the two groups.
Pay attention to the conditional structure.
2

The author says the same reasoning applies to idol worshipers. Identify the two reasons given for their worship.

5
Consider the concluding caveat
The passage ends with a strong statement: no excuse for one who does not follow the investigating scholars if he is deficient in research. What does this imply about responsibility?
This balances the earlier excusal.

Keep in mind: that the one who holds anthropomorphism (tajsim) is excused, because he was raised on it, or due to his ignorance and limited perception. So likewise you should believe regarding the idol worshiper, for he only worships out of ignorance, or due to upbringing on the method of their forefathers which is before them. And if you say: the apparent meaning of the text throws them into this doubt, likewise know that the idol worshiper is only called to worship them by incomplete imaginations and perceptions. So there is no excuse for one who does not follow the investigating scholars (al-muhaqqiqin al-nazirin), if he is deficient in investigation (al-nazar). End. [pp. 80-91, vol. 1, Dalalat al-Ha'irin, edited by Prof. Dr. Husayn Aqayi].

Commentary
This passage is a comparison between anthropomorphists and idol worshipers regarding their excuse. It states that an anthropomorphist is excused if he was raised on that belief or is ignorant. Similarly, an idol worshiper is excused only if he acts out of ignorance or upbringing. If one objects that scripture's apparent meaning leads to such doubts, the author counters that idol worshipers are also misled by incomplete perceptions. Therefore, no excuse remains for one who does not follow the investigating scholars if he is capable of research but fails. The passage ends with a citation from 'Dalalat al-Ha'irin'.

Misconception & Correction
Misconception: The passage allows anthropomorphism and idol worship as valid beliefs.
Correction: The passage only addresses the conditions under which people might be excused; it does not approve of the beliefs themselves. It emphasizes that those capable of research must follow the scholars.

Checkpoint
According to this passage, who is not excused for holding anthropomorphic beliefs?
A) Those raised on it.
B) Those who are ignorant.
C) Those who fail to follow investigating scholars while being capable of research.
D) Idol worshipers.

Key Takeaway
Both ignorance and upbringing can excuse, but wilful neglect of research removes any excuse before God.
