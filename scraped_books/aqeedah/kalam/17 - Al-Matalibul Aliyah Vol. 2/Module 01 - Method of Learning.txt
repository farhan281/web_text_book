Commentary

Thus the second argument reinforces the conclusion of the first: knowledge of God's essence is unattainable.

Commentary
This passage is a methodological clarification by the author. He points out the difference between two arguments used against the existence of two gods. The first method, which appears earlier in the text, considers a scenario where each of the two agents intends one of two opposites (e.g., one wants to move a body, the other wants to keep it still). The present method considers a different scenario: both agents come together to produce a single effect. This distinction is important because the logical implications differ. In the first case, the impossibility arises from conflict of wills; in the second, from the impossibility of a single effect having two independent causes without leading to contradictions, as shown in the previous lesson.
