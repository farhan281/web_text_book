Biography of Abu al-Abbas al-Nashi
Editorial footnote giving biography of the Mu'tazilite poet.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the life and contributions of Abu al-Abbas al-Nashi, a Mu'tazilite poet and scholar.

Core Idea
A Mu'tazilite poet who rejected compulsion and anthropomorphism.

This lesson introduces Abu al-Abbas al-Nashi, a notable figure from the eighth generation of the Mu'tazila. The brief biographical footnote from the editor provides key details about his life, his works, and his poetic critique of certain theological positions. Reading this passage will give you a glimpse into the diversity of Mu'tazilite scholars.

Source Text

من الطبقة الثامنة للمعتزلة . وهو عبد الله بن محمد ، وكنيته أبو العباس ، من أهل الأنبار ، نزل بغداد . وله كتب كثيرة نقض فيها كتب المنطق ، وهو شاعر ، وله قصيدة على روي واحد وقافية واحدة ، أربعة آلاف بيت ، وخرج في آخر عمره إلى مصر ، وأقام فيها بقية عمره . وله مناظرات كثيرة ، إلا أن في كلامه طولاً . ومن قصيدة له قوله : ما في البرية أخرى عند فاطرها ممن يدين بإجبار وتشبيه
He is from the eighth class of the Mu'tazila. He is Abdullah ibn Muhammad, his kunya is Abu al-Abbas, from the people of al-Anbar, he settled in Baghdad. He wrote many books in which he refuted the books of logic, and he is a poet. He composed a poem with a single meter and a single rhyme of four thousand verses. He left at the end of his life for Egypt and stayed there for the rest of his life. He had many debates, though his speech was lengthy. Among his poems is his saying: 'There is none like him in creation before its Originator among those who profess compulsion and anthropomorphism.'

Commentary
This footnote provides a brief biography of Abu al-Abbas al-Nashi, identifying him as an eighth-generation Mu'tazilite, a scholar and poet from al-Anbar who later lived in Baghdad and then Egypt. It mentions his many writings refuting logic and his poetic skill, including a long poem. The quote at the end criticizes those who believe in divine compulsion and anthropomorphism, aligning with Mu'tazilite rejection of such views.
