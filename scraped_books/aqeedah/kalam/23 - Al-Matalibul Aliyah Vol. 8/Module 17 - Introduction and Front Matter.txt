Author's Preface: The Need for Sound Principles
Author states scarcity of reliable works and introduces his compilation.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the author's purpose in writing this work and the structure he adopts.

Core Idea
This work fills a gap by providing sound principles where none existed.

The author opens with a frank assessment: reliable works in this field are rare. Through his own reflection, he has compiled a set of precise principles. This lesson introduces the preface, setting the stage for the entire treatise.

Guided Path
1
Read the passage carefully
Read the Arabic passage once without analysing. Then read the translation. Notice the author's tone and the emphasis on scarcity.
2
Identify the author's claim
What does the author say about existing people and books? He says he has not found any person with reliable knowledge nor any book with sound principles. This establishes the need for his work.
The author is not denying all existence, but reliable ones in this specific field.
3
Understand the structure
The author states that the discussion is arranged into an introduction (muqaddimah) and one article (maqalah). Note the footnote about a textual variant.
This structure will be followed throughout the treatise.

Preamble. Know that we have not seen any person who possesses anything reliable of this science, nor have we seen a book containing sound principles in this subject. However, after we reflected much, we obtained therein principles and summaries. So whoever comes after us and attains benefits and additions in this subject, let him be grateful and praise, because we have arranged for him these precise principles and known rules. The discussion therein is arranged into an introduction and an article (1) (1) [The original has] 'articles'. And note: the author mentioned only one article.
Source pages p. 141

Commentary
This passage is a preface (tamheed) in which the author sets the stage for his work. He begins with 'Know' (i'lam) — a common formula to draw attention. He states that he has not encountered any person possessing significant knowledge in this science ('ilm), nor any book that contains sound principles (usul mu'tabara) on this topic. This establishes the need for his work. Then he says that after deep reflection (ta'ammul) he himself compiled principles (usul) and summaries (jumal). He expresses hope that later scholars will be grateful for his efforts in organizing these precise principles and known rules. Finally, he states that the work is structured into a muqaddimah (introduction) and a maqalah (article). A footnote comments that the original manuscript had 'maqalat' (articles, plural) but the author only mentioned one. This editorial note indicates a possible textual variation.

Right now, make a specific intention to read this work with the aim of appreciating its value and applying its lessons, so that you may be among those who fulfill the author's hope.

The Author's Preface: Motivation and Structure
Author's preface on knowledge and structure

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the author's motivation for writing this work and the overall structure he outlines.

Core Idea
The author compiled precise principles from scattered sources to fill a gap in knowledge.

In this preface, the author sets the stage for his entire work. He describes the poor state of knowledge in this field and his own efforts to gather and organize its principles. This passage reveals his scholarly humility and his hope that future scholars will build upon his work.

Guided Path
1
Read the passage carefully
Read the Arabic passage once without trying to analyse it. Then read the translation. Let the author's own rhythm come through before you interpret.
Notice the author's direct address: 'اعلم' (Know).
2
Identify the author's claim about knowledge
The author states he has not seen any person with substantial knowledge nor a book with authoritative principles. This sets the context for his work.
3
Understand the author's method
After much reflection, he collected principles and summaries. This shows his work is a compilation, not entirely original.
The term 'أصولاً وجملاً' indicates he gathered both principles and condensed summaries.
4
Note the call for gratitude
The author expects those who benefit from his work to be grateful, as he arranged precise principles for them.
This reflects the scholarly tradition of acknowledging previous work.
5
Observe the announced structure
The work is arranged as a preface (مقدمة) and one article (مقالة). This gives a basic outline of the book.
Some editions note that the text originally said 'articles' plural, but only one is mentioned.

Know: I have not seen any person who has any substantial knowledge of this science, nor have I seen a book containing authoritative principles in this subject. But when I reflected much, I collected principles and summaries in it. So whoever comes after us and attains benefits and additions in this subject, let him be grateful in praise, because we have arranged for him these precise principles and known rules. The discussion in it is arranged according to a preface and an article.
Source pages p. 142

Commentary
This passage is the author's preface to his work. He begins by stating the deficient state of knowledge: he has not encountered any person with substantial knowledge of this science, nor a book that contains authoritative principles on the subject. Then he explains his own effort: after much reflection, he gathered principles and summaries (أصولاً وجملاً). He calls upon those who come after him to be grateful and praise him, as he has provided them with a structured compilation of precise principles and known rules. Finally, he notes the structure of his work: it is arranged as a preface (مقدمة) and one article (مقالة). (An editorial note, not included here, points out that the original text says 'articles' plural, but the author only mentions one article.)

Misconception & Correction
Misconception: The author claims absolute originality and that no work existed before him.
Correction: The author's statement is about the lack of accessible, authoritative principles, not that nothing existed. He compiled from existing material after reflection.

Key Takeaway
The author's preface establishes his role as a compiler and organizer of principles, inviting gratitude and outlining the work's structure as a preface and one article.
