The Rationale for the Qualities of Cardinal Signs
Al-Razi explains the rationale for the hot/cold/dry/wet qualities of the four cardinal signs.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand why al-Razi assigns specific hot/cold/dry/wet qualities to the four cardinal signs based on the principles of alternating active qualities and pairing passive ones.

Core Idea
The world's survival requires alternating active qualities and pairing passive ones.

Al-Razi explains the qualities of the cardinal signs not as arbitrary but as necessary for cosmic balance. He shows how the sun's transitions and the nature of active/passive qualities determine the pattern. This passage reveals the rational structure underlying astrological attributions in classical Islamic thought.

Guided Path

Read the passage carefully

Known words in incantations gain power through the reciter's knowledge and the resulting spiritual impression.

In this passage, the author of al-Matalib al-Aliyah explains a key principle of incantation: the effectiveness of known words depends on the reciter's knowledge of the celestial bodies involved. This insight bridges cosmology and psychology, showing how the mind's engagement with the cosmos influences spiritual power. The passage presents a clear causal chain that you will explore step by step.

The fifth type of matters considered in this chapter: recitation of incantations. Know that they are of two kinds: some are known words, and some are unknown words. As for the known words, the truth in my view is that the more a person's knowledge of the attributes of a particular planet is extensive, and his knowledge of its specific actions is more complete, his ability to enumerate its attributes of perfection is more complete, and the impression on his soul when mentioning those attributes is more complete. And the more complete that impression is, the more complete is the attraction of his soul to the spirit of that planet, and thus his power to influence is stronger.

Commentary
This passage presents the fifth type of practice: recitation of incantations (ruqa). The author divides recitations into two kinds: known words (معلومة) and unknown words (غير معلومة). He then explains the effect of known words. His view is that the effectiveness depends on the reciter's knowledge. The more he knows the attributes and specific actions of a particular planet, the better he can enumerate its perfect attributes, and the stronger the impression on his soul when reciting them. This impression, in turn, attracts his soul to the spirit of that planet, increasing his power to influence. Thus, knowledge and emotional engagement enhance the efficacy of the recitation.

Misconception & Correction
Misconception: The power of known words in incantations comes from the words themselves or their inherent properties.
Correction: The passage teaches that the power comes from the reciter's knowledge of the planet and the resulting impression and attraction, not from the words alone.
