The Fifth Difficulty: Uniformity of Zodiac Signs
Author argues that if the sphere is simple, all zodiac signs are identical, making stellar effects inexplicable by nature alone.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the logical argument that if the celestial sphere is a simple substance, then all zodiac signs must be identical in essence, which poses a difficulty for explaining stellar effects.

Core Idea
If the sphere is simple, the zodiac signs cannot differ—so why do planets act differently?

The fifth difficulty targets the astrological claim that planets have different effects in different zodiac signs. The argument is based on the philosophers' view that the celestial sphere is a simple substance. If that is true, then the signs cannot differ in essence, making it hard to attribute natural differences to them.

Commentary
This passage introduces the fifth difficulty, though it is incomplete. The author cites the philosophers' consensus that the celestial sphere (الفلك) is a simple substance (جوهر بسيط). If it is simple, then the twelve zodiac signs (البروج) must all be identical in essence (تمام الماهية); otherwise, the sphere would be composite, contradicting its simplicity. This leads to a problem for astrologers: if all zodiac signs are identical, then it is hard to explain why planets have different effects based on their positions in different signs. The argument suggests that our inability to fully understand the influence of stars is another difficulty in knowing celestial matters. The passage breaks off, but the intended conclusion is likely that stellar effects cannot be fully known due to these complexities.
