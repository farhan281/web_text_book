The Fourth Condition: Lunar Timing and Abu Dhwatis' Method
Sanusi describes lunar timing rules and an alternative image-moving method.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the fourth condition regarding lunar phases for magical timing and the alternative method attributed to Abu Dhwatis al-Babili.

Core Idea
Lunar phases guide magical timing; alternative methods parallel celestial motions.

This passage presents the fourth condition for magical operations: the phase of the moon determines the appropriate time for different types of spells. It also recounts an alternative method by the Babylonian magician Abu Dhwatis, who used images moved in synchrony with the sun and moon's approach. Understanding these details helps illustrate how ancient magical systems often integrated astronomical observations.

Guided Path
1
Read the passage and translation
Read the Arabic passage once without analyzing, then read the translation to get a clear sense of the content.
Pay attention to the lunar phases mentioned: first, middle, and end of the month.
2
Identify the three categories and their timings
The passage lists three types of works (love, hatred, tying the tongue) and assigns each to a specific lunar phase. Notice how the reasoning links increase/decrease of the moon with increase/decrease of the effect.
Love is at the beginning (waxing), hatred at the end (waning), silence in the middle (full moon?).
3
Understand Abu Dhwatis' alternative method
Read the description of Abu Dhwatis' procedure: using images placed diagonally and moved closer each day from mid-month. Note the parallel with the approach of the two luminaries (sun and moon) towards each other.
This method is a form of sympathetic magic based on celestial analogy.

الشرط الرابع : اجعل أعمال الحب في أول الشهر ، وأعمال البغض في آخر الشهر . وأعمال عقد اللسان في وسط الشهر . لأن أول الشهر زائد . فيناسب حصول زيادة الحب. وآخر الشهر في النقصان ، وذلك يورث النقصان في الحب . وأما « أبو ذاطيس البابلي ، فإنه قلب الأمر ، واتخذ صورتين على شكل المحب والمحبوب ، ووضعهما في زاويتي البيت على القطر ، وجعل ابتداء هذا العمل من منتصف الشهر، ثم في كل يوم يقرب كل واحد منهما من الآخر ، لأجل أن النيرين ، يقرب كل واحد منهما من الآخر في هذه المدة ، حتى إذا تم الشهر ، اتصلت إحدى الصورتين بالأخرى ، على قياس وصول أحد النيرين إلى الآخر
The fourth condition: Place works of love at the beginning of the month, and works of hatred at the end of the month, and works of tying the tongue in the middle of the month. Because the beginning of the month is increasing, so it suits obtaining an increase in love. And the end of the month is in decrease, and that causes decrease in love. As for Abu Dhawatis al-Babili, he reversed the matter and took two images in the form of the lover and the beloved, and placed them in two corners of the house on the diagonal, and made the beginning of this work from the middle of the month, then each day he brings each one closer to the other, so that the two luminaries approach each other during this period, until when the month is completed, one of the two images connects with the other, according to the measure of one luminary reaching the other.
Source pages p. 196

Commentary
This passage reports the fourth condition, which concerns the phase of the lunar month. For love, the best time is the beginning of the month when the moon is waxing; for hatred, the end of the month when the moon is waning; for tying the tongue (i.e., silencing someone), the middle. Then it recounts an alternative method by Abu Dhawatis al-Babili, who started his procedure in the middle of the month, using two images representing the lover and beloved, placed diagonally in two corners, and gradually moved them closer each day, paralleling the approach of the sun and moon (the two luminaries) towards each other during the second half of the month, so that at month's end the images touch. This is a specific magical procedure.

Correction: This passage describes a magical belief system, but Islamic theology holds that only Allah has power over events. Lunar phases are created by Allah and have no inherent causal ability.

The Fifth Condition: Matching the Element of the Ascendant
The author states the fifth condition requiring the magical work to match the element of the target's ascendant.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the fifth condition of magical practice: the necessity of aligning the magical work with the elemental nature of the target's ascendant sign.

Core Idea
Magical work must mirror the element of the target's ascendant.

This lesson presents the fifth condition from a treatise on magic, requiring the practitioner to know the astrological ascendant of the person they wish to influence. The ascendant's element determines the type of work to be performed. This reflects the magical principle of correspondence between cosmic signs and physical actions.

Guided Path
1
Read the passage carefully
Read the Arabic passage once without trying to analyse it. Then read the translation. Let the author's own rhythm come through before you interpret.
The passage is very short, so pay attention to the examples given.
2
Identify the condition and its rationale
The condition is to know the ascendant's element and act accordingly. Examples given for fire and air. Understand that the same analogy applies to water and earth.
3
Connect to the broader context
This is one of several conditions for effective magic. Understand that this reflects a belief in elemental correspondences. Consider the theological implications from an Islamic perspective.
The common misunderstanding is that elements have inherent power.

The fifth condition: Know the ascendant of the person whom you wish to activate. If it is fiery, then perform for him a work pertaining to fire. If it is airy, then perform for him a work pertaining to air. And upon this analogy.

The magician must determine the element (fire, air, water, earth) of the target's ascendant sign, then perform a magical work that corresponds to that element. The text only gives examples for fire and air, but says 'upon this analogy' for others. This shows the magicians' belief in correspondence between cosmic elements and physical actions.

Classification of Magical Works by Element
The author classifies magical acts into three elemental types with examples.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the three elemental categories of magical works as described in the text, along with examples and the author's cautious stance.

Core Idea
Magical works are classified by element: fire, air, and water.

This passage from Al-Matalib al-Aliyah presents a classification of magical practices based on the elements involved. The author lists concrete examples for each type and ends with a reminder that only Allah knows the true reality of these acts. Pay attention to the specific details and the author's concluding remark.

Guided Path
1
Read the passage carefully
Read the Arabic passage once without analyzing. Then read the translation. Notice the structure: three categories, each with examples.
The passage begins with 'واعلم' (know) signaling important information.
2
Identify the three categories
Find the words نارية, هوائية, مائية. These are the fire, air, and water types. Note the examples given for each.
Examples include writing on iron, blowing into knots, and washing feet.
3
Reflect on the author's conclusion
The author ends with 'والله أعلم بحقيقة هذه الأمور' (Allah knows best the reality of these matters). What does this imply about the author's view on the effectiveness of these acts?
This indicates caution or skepticism, leaving ultimate knowledge to Allah.
4
Connect to Islamic theology
In Islam, only Allah has true power. These acts are not considered independently effective. How does this relate to the classification?
The classification may be descriptive, not endorsing efficacy.

Source Text

واعلم : أن هذه الأعمال السحرية ، منها : نارية . مثل أن تكتب العزيمة على الحديد ، ثم توضع في النار . ومثل : أن تصب الخمر العتيق ، والنفط الأبيض ، والأدوية الحارة في كوز الفقاع ويوضع في النار . وتقرأ عليه العزيمة. ومنها : هوائية . مثل : تطيير العصفور. ومثل : النفث في العقد . ومنها : مائية . مثل : غسل الرجلين بمياه مخصوصة وصبها على باب من تريد تهييجه . والله أعلم بحقيقة هذه الأمور .
Know that these magical works include: fire-type, such as writing an incantation on iron then placing it in fire; and such as pouring aged wine, white naphtha, and hot medicines into a beer mug and placing it in fire, and reciting the incantation over it. And among them: air-type, such as flying a bird, and such as blowing into knots. And among them: water-type, such as washing the feet with specific waters and pouring them on the door of the one you wish to activate. And Allah knows best the reality of these matters.
Source pages p. 197

Commentary
This passage classifies magical acts into three elemental categories. Fire-type works involve fire and heat; examples include writing on iron and burning it, or mixing substances in a mug and heating them. Air-type works involve air and wind; examples include flying a bird (perhaps releasing it) and blowing into knots (a common magical practice). Water-type works involve water; example is washing feet with special water and pouring it at someone's door. The author ends with 'Allah knows best', indicating that the reality of these claims is known only to Allah, implying skepticism.
