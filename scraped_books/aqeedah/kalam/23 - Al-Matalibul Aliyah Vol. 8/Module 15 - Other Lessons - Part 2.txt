First Way of Dividing the Zodiac: Seasonal and Qualitative Reasoning
Presents the first way of dividing the zodiac into twelve signs based on seasons, cardinal points, and qualities.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand how the zodiac was divided into twelve signs based on seasons and qualitative principles, and the reasoning behind assigning hot/cold/dry/moist natures to the cardinal signs and ordering the signs in alternating triads.

Core Idea
The zodiac division mirrors seasonal transitions and balances active and passive qualities.

This passage from al-Matalib al-Aliyah presents the first way of dividing the celestial sphere into twelve zodiacal signs. It begins with the observation that the year has four seasons, each with a beginning, middle, and end, leading to twelve parts. Then it explains how the intersection of the celestial equator and ecliptic defines equinoctial and solstitial points, which are assigned hot and cold natures based on their relation to equilibrium and excess. The text further argues for the precedence of dryness over moisture, and uses the seasonal transitions to assign natures to Aries, Libra, Cancer, and Capricorn. Finally, it introduces a dynamic principle that heat and cold must alternate to avoid extremes, while dryness and moisture should be grouped in pairs, resulting in a specific order of signs repeated in three groups of four.

Guided Path
1
Understand the seasonal basis
Read how the division into twelve arises from the four seasons each having three parts. This is the foundation.
The year's division into seasons is directly tied to the sun's motion.
2
Identify the cardinal points
Focus on the equinoctial points (Aries, Libra) and solstitial points (Cancer, Capricorn) and why equinoxes are considered nobler.
Equilibrium is valued over excess.
3
Learn the assignment of qualities
Note how heat, cold, dryness, and moisture are assigned to the four cardinal signs based on seasonal transitions.
Aries gets hot-dry, Libra hot-moist, etc.
4
Grasp the active-passive dynamics
Understand the rule that heat and cold (active) must alternate, while dryness and moisture (passive) come in pairs to avoid nullification.
This determines the order of signs in the zodiac.
5
Apply the pattern to all signs
See how the same pattern of four signs (hot-dry, cold-dry, hot-moist, cold-moist) repeats for the second and third groups of four signs.
The entire zodiac is structured by this qualitative logic.

The third section on knowing the nature of the sphere: And they agreed on the division of the sphere from many aspects. So the first aspect: the division of the sphere into twelve parts. And the strongest reason for it is that people found the year to be divided into four seasons due to the sun's presence in the four quarters of the sphere. For this reason they made the cycle of the sphere into four parts. Then they found for each of the four seasons a beginning, a middle, and an end, so inevitably the sphere became divided into twelve parts. And know that this division occurs in two ways: the first: that one considers its state by means of the signs. And know that due to the intersection of the plane of the great sphere and the plane of the signs, two points of intersection occur. They are the points of Aries and Libra. And two points are the points of extreme divergence. They are the points of Cancer and Capricorn. And equilibrium is nobler than excess, so the two points of intersection are nobler than the two points of solstice. And heat is nobler than cold, so they made the two equinoctial points hot, and the two solstitial points cold. Then dryness is better than moisture, because dryness is the prevention of passivity, and moisture is passivity. So dryness is nobler than moisture. And it is known that when the sun moves to Aries, the season moves from winter to spring, and life and joy occur for the whole world. And when the sun moves from Virgo to Libra, the season moves from summer to autumn, and it is a transition from perfection to deficiency. So Aries is nobler than Libra. So inevitably they made Aries hot and dry, and Libra hot and moist. And Capricorn is nobler than Cancer, because when the sun moves from Gemini to Cancer, it begins to descend, the day decreases, and the night increases, and when moving from Sagittarius to Capricorn, the sun moves from descent to ascent, and from the utmost decrease of day to its increase. So Capricorn is better than Cancer. So for this they made Capricorn cold and dry, and Cancer cold and moist. So the natures of these four signs become known. Then we say: Heat and cold are active, and dryness and moisture are passive. The active is strong, the passive is weak. If two hot [signs] or two cold [signs] were consecutive, the heating or cooling would be extreme, and the world would perish. So it was necessary that there be a hot sign, then a cold sign, then hot, then cold. As for the two passive [qualities], if a dry [sign] then a moist [sign] occurred consecutively, each would be balanced by the other, and the effect of each would be nullified. So the most correct is that there be two dry [signs], then two moist [signs], and so on in order. And when these premises are clear, it is necessary that the first be hot and dry, the second cold and dry, the third hot and moist, the fourth cold and moist. And likewise for the second and third groups of four.

Commentary
This passage presents the first way of dividing the sphere into twelve parts, the method based on the seasons and the qualities of the signs. It begins by stating that the division of the sphere into twelve is agreed upon, and the strongest reason is that the year has four seasons, each with a beginning, middle, and end, giving twelve parts. Then it notes that the division can be considered in terms of the zodiacal signs. It discusses the two equinoctial points (Aries and Libra) and the two solstitial points (Cancer and Capricorn), asserting that equilibrium is nobler than excess, and heat is nobler than cold, so the equinoctial signs are assigned heat and the solstitial signs cold. It then argues that dryness is better than moisture because dryness resists passivity. Based on the seasonal transitions, Aries is associated with life and joy, Libra with decline, so Aries is hot-dry and Libra hot-moist. Capricorn is associated with ascent of the sun, Cancer with descent, so Capricorn is cold-dry and Cancer cold-moist. This establishes the natures of the four cardinal signs. Next, the text introduces a dynamic principle: heat and cold are active, dryness and moisture passive. To avoid extreme heating or cooling from two hot or two cold signs in succession, hot and cold must alternate. For the passive qualities, two dry signs in a row would neutralize each other's effect, so it is better to have two dry then two moist. Applying these premises, the order of the first four signs is determined: first hot-dry (Aries), second cold-dry (Taurus), third hot-moist (Gemini), fourth cold-moist (Cancer). The same pattern applies to the second group of four (Leo, Virgo, Libra, Scorpio) and the third group (Sagittarius, Capricorn, Aquarius, Pisces).

The Second Division of the Zodiac: Astrological Houses
Describes the second way of dividing the zodiac into twelve houses based on the ascendant, with meanings for four principal houses and two additional.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the classification of the twelve astrological houses based on the ascendant, as described in Al-Matalib al-Aliyah.

Core Idea
The astrological houses are determined by analogy between celestial positions and earthly life.

This passage presents the second method of dividing the zodiac into twelve parts: the astrological houses derived from the ascendant. The author explains the meanings and significations of the four principal houses and two additional houses linked by trine, using analogical reasoning. Although this material is part of a critique of astrology, it is important to understand the system being described.

3

4
Examine the fifth and ninth houses

Commentary
This passage presents the second way of dividing the sphere into twelve parts based on the astrological houses (the ascendant and its related houses). It first identifies four principal houses: the ascendant (1st), the 4th, the 7th, and the 10th. The ascendant indicates beginnings and is likened to the birth of a child from concealment, so it signifies life and soul. The fourth house is under the earth, representing the origin, fathers, estates, and hidden outcomes. The seventh house is opposite the ascendant, signifying opposites, partners, and spouses. The tenth house is the highest, signifying sultanate and power, and since it is opposite the fourth, it signifies mothers. The text then explains two additional houses, the fifth and ninth, which are in trine aspect to the ascendant, bestowing love and good. The fifth under the earth is assigned to children, messengers, gifts, and profit, while the ninth above is assigned to religion, prophecy, knowledge, and long journeys. The commentary notes that the underlying method is analogy (similarity between celestial and earthly phenomena). This passage is a straightforward exposition of astrological house meanings, framed as the author's own understanding ('what I have obtained').

Classification of Signs and Angular Houses
Al-Razi classifies signs into movable, fixed, and dual, and introduces the twelve houses based on the ascendant.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand al-Razi's classification of the twelve signs into movable, fixed, and dual categories, and his identification of the four angular houses based on the ascendant.

Core Idea
The zodiac signs are divided into movable, fixed, and dual; houses are defined by the ascendant.

Al-Razi begins by summarizing the classification of the twelve signs into three categories of four: movable (beginnings of seasons), fixed (middles), and dual. He then turns to the division of the celestial sphere into twelve houses based on the ascendant, noting that only four of them—the angular houses—are fundamental.

Guided Path

Identify the three categories of signs
Find where al-Razi describes the movable, fixed, and dual signs, and note how they correspond to the seasons.

3
Understand the angular houses
