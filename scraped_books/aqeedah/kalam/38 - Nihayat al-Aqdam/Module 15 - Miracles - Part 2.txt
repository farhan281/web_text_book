واعلم أن كل كرامة تظهر على يد ولي فهي بعينها معجزة لنبي، إذا كان الولي في معاملاته تابعا لذلك النبي ، وكل ما يظهر في حقه فهو دليل على صدق أستاذه وصاحب شريعته، فلا تكون الكرامة قط قادحة في المعجزات، بل هي مؤيدة لها دالة عليها راجعة عنها وعائدة إليها، وقد قال النبي ﷺ: كائن في أمتي ما كان في الزمان الأول حذو القذة بالقذة والنعل بالنعل حتى لو دخلوا جحر ضب لدخلتموه، وقال عليه السلام: إن في أمتي من مثله مثل إبراهيم الخليل، ومن مثله مثل موسى، وفي الآية: {ولما ضرب ابن مريم مثلا إذا قومك منه يصدون} [الزخرف: 57] إشارة إلى هذا المعنى. ولما كانت شريعته أكمل الشرائع ودينة أكمل الأديان كما قال تعالى: {اليوم أكملت لكم دينكم وأتممت عليكم نعمتي ورضيت لكم الإسلام دينا} [المائدة: 3] وجب أن يكون المتحلي بهذا الدين والشريعة أشرف وأكرم ممن تحلى بشريعة أخرى؛ إذ الشرائع والأديان جلابيب النفوس والأرواح، وبقدر التحلي بها والثبات فيها والإقبال عليها ينال الشرف والكرامة، قال الله تعالى: {إن تتقوا الله يجعل لكم فرقانًا ويكفر عنكم سيئاتكم} [الأنفال: 29]، وليت شعري أي كرامة تزيد على نيل الفرقان بين الحق والباطل وسبيل النجاة والهلاك، وفي الخبر: «لا يزال العبد يتقرب إلي بالنوافل حتى أحبه فإذا أحببته كنت له سمعا وبصرا ويدا ومؤيدا»، وأي كرامة أجل من حصول المحبة بثمراتها المذكورة، وفي الخبر: «لو عرفتم الله حق معرفته لعلمتم العلم الذي ليس معه جهل، ولو عرفتم الله معرفته لزالت الجبال بدعائكم وما أوتي أحد من اليقين إلا وما لم يؤت أكثر مما أوتي». قيل: بلغنا أن عيسى عليه السلام كان يمشي على الماء، قال صلى الله عليه وسلم: «لو ازداد يقينًا لمشي على الهواء».
Know that every miracle (karāma) that appears at the hand of a saint (walī) is, in itself, a miracle (muʿjiza) for a prophet, provided the saint in his conduct is a follower of that prophet. And everything that appears on his behalf is evidence of the truthfulness of his teacher and the possessor of his law. Never does the karāma detract from miracles; rather, it supports them, indicates them, refers back to them, and returns to them. The Prophet ﷺ said: 'There will occur among my community what occurred among the previous communities, exactly like the feather of an arrow to the feather, and the sandal to the sandal, to the extent that if they entered a lizard's hole, you would enter it.' And he said: 'Among my community are those who are like Abraham the Friend, and like Moses.' And in the verse, 'And when the son of Mary is presented as an example, your people at once turn away from it' (Q. 43:57) there is an indication of this meaning. Since his law is the most perfect of laws and his religion the most perfect of religions, as the Exalted said: 'Today I have perfected for you your religion and completed My favor upon you and have approved for you Islam as a religion' (Q. 5:3), it is necessary that the one adorned with this religion and law be more noble and more honored than one adorned with another law; for the laws and religions are the garments of souls and spirits, and according to the measure of adornment with them, steadfastness in them, and turning towards them, one attains nobility and honor. God the Exalted said: 'If you fear God, He will grant you a criterion and will cover your evil deeds' (Q. 8:29). Would that I knew what karāma is greater than attaining the criterion between truth and falsehood and the path of salvation and destruction. And in the report: 'The servant continues to draw near to Me with supererogatory acts until I love him. When I love him, I become his hearing, his sight, his hand, and his supporter.' And what karāma is greater than the attainment of love with its mentioned fruits? And in the report: 'If you knew God with true knowledge, you would know knowledge with which there is no ignorance, and if you knew God with His knowledge, mountains would move by your supplication. And no one has been given anything of certainty except what he was given, and more was not given.' It was said: The report reached us that Jesus, peace be upon him, used to walk on water. The Prophet ﷺ said: 'If his certainty increased, he would walk on air.'

Commentary
This passage is an argument establishing the relationship between saintly miracles (karāmāt) and prophetic miracles (muʿjizāt). Juwayni begins by stating a principle: every karāma that appears at the hand of a saint (walī) is essentially a muʿjiza for the prophet whom the saint follows. The saint's actions confirm the truthfulness of his prophetic teacher. Therefore, karāmāt never detract from prophetic miracles; rather, they support, indicate, refer back to, and return to those miracles. Juwayni then supports this with three hadiths. The first reports that the Prophet's community will follow the patterns of previous communities exactly; the second mentions that among the community are those like Abraham and Moses; the third involves the Quranic verse about the people turning away when Jesus is presented as an example. These show continuity between prophets and saints. Next, Juwayni argues that since Islam is the most perfect religion and law, its followers must be the most noble and honored. He cites Q. 5:3 on perfection, then Q. 8:29 about God granting a criterion (furqān) to the pious. He notes that the greatest karāma is the ability to distinguish truth from falsehood. He then quotes a hadith about drawing near God through supererogatory acts, which leads to God becoming the servant's hearing, sight, hand, and supporter—the greatest karāma. He adds another hadith about the fruits of true knowledge of God: mountains moving by supplication and the possession of certainty. Finally, he reports that Jesus walked on water, and the Prophet stated that with increased certainty he would have walked on air. This illustrates that karāmāt are extensions of prophetic miracles: what is extraordinary for a saint is a lesser degree of what the prophet could do. The passage thus reinforces the doctrine that saintly miracles are genuine and confirm prophecy.

Misconception & Correction
Misconception: Some think saintly miracles are only physical wonders like levitation or walking on water.
Correction: Juwayni shows that the greatest karamat are spiritual: furqan and divine love. Physical miracles are secondary.

Key Takeaway
The noblest karamat are spiritual gifts like furqan and divine love, which confirm prophetic truth.
