The Animal Spirit and the Persistence of Life
Defines animal spirit and explains the persistence of life.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand the nature of the animal spirit and how the persistence of life depends on the persistence of balanced temperament and spirit, which are maintained by the specific form.

Core Idea
Life persists as long as the form maintains balance; any imbalance brings death.

The passage concludes with a detailed description of the animal spirit and an empirical observation. It clarifies that life is not permanent but contingent on the continued balance of the temperament and spirit, both sustained by the specific form. This lesson connects the philosophical theory to observable reality.

Guided Path
1
Read the definition of animal spirit
Find the sentence that defines the animal spirit: a subtle vaporous body formed from humors, emanating from the heart and flowing through arteries.
This definition is based on ancient medical theory.
2
Understand the function of the animal spirit
The spirit carries the power of life to the organs. It is the carrier of life.
3
Comprehend the persistence condition
The passage states that the persistence of life depends on the persistence of the balanced temperament and animal spirit, which are maintained by the specific form.
This shows that life is not self-subsistent.
4
Consider the empirical example
When the temperament changes due to internal or external causes, life ceases, structure dissolves, and form disappears. This is observed in animals by experience.
This bridges philosophy and empirical observation.

Commentary
This passage explains the philosophers' position that life is conditioned by a specific bodily structure. The commentator begins by quoting the original statement that life requires a body composed of elements, a specific form, and qualities including a particular temperament. He then clarifies that the phrase 'qualities that follow it' means these qualities (including life) follow the specific form. He highlights that the original author specified 'temperamental' balance because spiritual balance belongs to the spirit, not the elemental body. He then explains two aspects of conditionality: from the agent side, life depends on the specific form which depends on temperamental balance requiring the specific structure; from the carrier side, life only emanates on the animal spirit which requires the specific structure. He then defines the animal spirit as a subtle vaporous body emanating from the heart and spreading through arteries. Finally, he explains that the persistence of life depends on the persistence of the balanced temperament and spirit, which are maintained by the specific form. When the temperament changes due to internal or external causes, life ceases, the structure dissolves, and the form disappears, as empirically observed.

Misconception & Correction
Misconception: That life is permanent and does not depend on continuous physical balance; once alive, always alive regardless of changes.
Correction: The passage explicitly says that life ceases when the temperament leaves its balanced state. Life is contingent on continued balance.
