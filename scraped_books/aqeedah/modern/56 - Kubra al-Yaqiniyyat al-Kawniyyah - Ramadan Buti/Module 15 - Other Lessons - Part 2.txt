Worship Without Correct Belief Is Worthless
Buti warns that worship is useless with wrong belief about legislation.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand that abundant worship does not benefit a person if he holds the wrong belief that humans can legislate for themselves or that some divine commands are unsuitable for today.

Core Idea
Worship without submission to divine law is hollow.

Buti issues a stark warning: performing many prayers, supererogatory acts, and remembrances does not save a person from responsibility. If someone believes that humans can legislate for themselves or that some of God's commands are unfit for today, their worship becomes worthless. This lesson explains the severity of such belief as apostasy according to Muslim consensus.

Guided Path
1
Read the passage carefully
Read the Arabic passage once, then its translation. Pay attention to Buti's strong language ('dust, of no value').
2
Identify the two wrong beliefs
Buti mentions two specific beliefs that nullify worship: (1) believing humans can legislate as they wish, and (2) believing some of God's commands are unsuitable today.
3
Understand the consequence: apostasy
Buti cites consensus based on decisive evidence that such belief removes a person from the circle of Islam.
This shows the gravity of the issue.
4
Reflect on the purpose of worship
Ask yourself: What is the point of worship if one rejects God's authority in life? Buti's warning ties worship to acceptance of divine legislation.
Internal connection helps internalize the lesson.
