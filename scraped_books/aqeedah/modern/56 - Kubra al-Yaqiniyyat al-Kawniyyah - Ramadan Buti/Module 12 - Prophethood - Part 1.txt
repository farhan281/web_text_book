The Rejection of Authentic Narration in Western Approaches

Learning Objective

Core Idea
Western thinkers discarded the very foundation of Islamic knowledge: reliable narration.

The author begins by stating that there is no hope of convergence between Muslim and Western thinkers regarding revelation. He immediately points to a fundamental difference: Western thinkers have completely abandoned the institution of narration (riwaya) and its scientific value. This lesson explores that first reason.

The central thesis is that the external and terrifying nature of the first revelation proves that it was not a product of the Prophet's own mind or a gradual inner development, but a real, external, and sudden intervention from God.

The argument proceeds as follows:
1. First, Buti sets up the context: Orientalists and opponents interpret the Prophet's experience in fantastic ways to avoid admitting his prophethood. By contrast, the authentic reports reveal divine wisdom.
2. He then poses four key questions:
   - Why did the Prophet see Gabriel with his own eyes, rather than receiving revelation from behind a veil?

The physical seeing and the embrace by the angel emphasize this external reception. The fear and uncertainty demonstrate that the Prophet was not predisposed to or eager for the message; he was genuinely surprised. The pause in revelation (fatrat al-waḥy) further underscores that this was not a self-generated progression but a divine intervention that came and went on God's terms.

If revelation were the result of the Prophet's own spiritual development, it would not have caused such violent reactions. On the contrary, the involuntary physical responses (fear, trembling, pale complexion) are impossible to feign, proving the sincerity and external origin of the experience.
5. He concludes with a syllogistic argument: all cases of gradual spiritual insight are free from such terror; the Prophet's experience involved terror; therefore it was not a case of gradual insight. The emotional reactions are involuntary and cannot be artificially produced, further supporting the authenticity of the external revelation.

The passage is a defense of the orthodox Islamic understanding of revelation against those who would reduce it to a psychological or spiritual phenomenon.

Proof that Revelation is from God via Reductio ad Absurdum
Buti proves divine origin of revelation by reductio ad absurdum.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will understand how Buti uses reductio ad absurdum to prove that revelation comes from God, by showing that alternative explanations lead to absurd consequences.

Core Idea
If revelation were human invention, it would contradict the Prophet's character and experience – an absurdity.

Buti has already established that revelation is a real phenomenon. Now he considers what it could be: is it divine, or could it be mere inspiration, poetry, or mental illness? He employs a logical technique called reductio ad absurdum: assume the negation, derive an impossibility, and thus prove the original claim. This passage walks through that reasoning.

The absurdities are both empirical (lack of physical signs) and logical (incompatible character traits).

Since the hypothesis leads to absurdity, it must be false. The remaining truth is that revelation is from an external divine source.
The argument also previews that the Quran itself confirms this.
