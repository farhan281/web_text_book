Misconception: Some might assume outsider accounts are more objective or unbiased.
Correction: The author argues that the outsider (Orientalist) is like a sneaking thief with a motive to misrepresent, while the insider (Arab historian) is the owner of the house and more trustworthy.

Checkpoint
What analogy does the author use to compare the two historical accounts of Harun al-Rashid?
A) A judge and a witness
B) The owner of the house and a sneaking thief
C) A scholar and a student
D) A king and a servant

Key Takeaway
Authentic historical sources from within a civilization are more trustworthy than external, biased accounts.
