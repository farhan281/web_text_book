Ghusl Before Ihram for a Woman with Istihadah: Wudu Suffices
Ruling on sufficiency of wudu for mustahadah before ihram

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the Hanafi ruling on ghusl before ihram for a mustahadah and explain why wudu suffices using the analogy to Jumu'ah.

Core Idea
For a mustahadah, wudu substitutes for ghusl before ihram because true purification is unattainable.

This passage addresses a specific scenario: a woman with chronic vaginal bleeding (istihadah) who wishes to enter ihram for Hajj. How should she perform purification? The Hanafi school provides a nuanced ruling based on the purpose of ghusl and the limits of purification. Let's walk through the reasoning.

Guided Path
1
Identify the person and the act
The mushkil is a mustahadah (woman with istihadah) before entering ihram. The act in question is ghusl (ritual bath).
Istihadah is non-menstrual bleeding; it does not prevent prayer or fasting but requires wudu per prayer.
2
Determine the legal effect of ghusl in this case
Ghusl before ihram is normally sunnah or recommended. For a mustahadah, ghusl cannot achieve ritual purity because the bleeding continues. Therefore, it is not wajib.
The text explicitly states: 'ghusl does not purify her before cessation of blood'.
3
Apply the principle of substitution (qaa'im maqaam)
Since ghusl is for cleanliness but not obligatory, wudu takes its place (qaa'im maqaam). The author analogizes to Jumu'ah: if one cannot do ghusl, wudu suffices.
The analogy to Jumu'ah is from Hanafi fiqh: ghusl for Jumu'ah is mustahabb, not wajib.
4
Identify the preferred practice (afdal)
Though wudu is sufficient, performing ghusl is afdal (better) for greater cleanliness and following the Prophet's (ﷺ) action.
The Prophet (ﷺ) performed ghusl before ihram, establishing a sunnah.
5
Summarize the Hanafi ruling
Ruling: Wudu suffices for a mustahadah before ihram; ghusl is mustahabb. The mufta bihi is this position.
This is the consistent Hanafi zahir al-riwayah view.

In the Book of Hajj, the ruling of ghusl is given: if from this type [of bleeding] the woman's obligatory and supererogatory worship will not be performed because washing before the cessation of blood cannot purify her. When before ihram ghusl is not obligatory but for cleanliness, then wudu takes its place, just as in Jumu'ah if one does not perform ghusl, wudu suffices in its place. But it is preferable to perform ghusl, first because there is greater cleanliness in it compared to wudu, and second because the Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) himself performed ghusl before ihram.

Commentary
This passage is a ruling concerning the ritual bath (ghusl) before entering the state of ihram for a woman suffering from chronic vaginal bleeding (istihadah). The text explains that for such a woman, the ghusl before ihram does not actually remove ritual impurity because the bleeding has not stopped; therefore, performing ghusl is not obligatory for the purpose of purification. Instead, she may perform wudu, which suffices for entering ihram. The author draws an analogy to the Friday prayer (Jumu'ah), where ghusl is not obligatory (wajib) but recommended; if one does not perform it, wudu is sufficient. However, performing ghusl is still afdal (better) because it ensures greater cleanliness and follows the practice of the Prophet (ﷺ), who himself performed ghusl before ihram. This ruling is based on the principle that the requirement for ghusl is linked to actual purification, and since istihadah prevents purification, the obligation shifts to wudu. The preferred practice is to perform ghusl out of following the Sunnah and attaining more cleanliness.

Key Terms
استحاضہ (istihadah): Chronic vaginal bleeding outside the normal menstrual period, which does not impose the same restrictions as menstruation.
قائم مقام (qaa'im maqaam): Something that takes the place or serves as a substitute for another, with the same effect.
