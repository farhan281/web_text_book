Navigating the Fihrist: Key Ihram Penalties in Ashraf al-Hidayah Volume 3
List of chapter headings and rulings with page numbers

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the correct expiation (dam or sadaqah) for common Ihram violations by extracting information from the fihrist and locate the corresponding page numbers.

Core Idea
The fihrist maps every Ihram violation to its required compensation—dam, sadaqah, or camel—instantly.

The fihrist (table of contents) of Ashraf al-Hidayah Volume 3 is not just a list of chapters—it is a quick-reference guide to the rulings on Ihram penalties. By learning to read it, you can access the Hanafi position on dozens of violations and see where each ruling is discussed in detail. In this lesson, we will practice extracting rulings from the fihrist so you can use it as a study tool.

Guided Path
1
Recognize the fihrist as a structured overview
Understand that the fihrist lists chapter headings and subheadings with page numbers. Each entry summarizes a key ruling from that section.
The fihrist is not a legal text itself; it refers to the full discussion later.
2
Identify the act involved
Read the entry to determine what act by a Muhrim is described (e.g., applying perfume, shaving the head, cutting nails).
Acts are often described in concise phrases.
3
Locate the penalty category
Find the words 'دم' (dam) or 'صدقہ' (sadaqah) to see whether the penalty is a sacrificial animal or charity. Note any conditions like 'کامل عضو' (full limb) vs 'کم' (less).
Some entries also mention 'بکری' (goat) or 'اونٹ' (camel).
4
Note differences of opinion
Look for phrases like 'اقوال فقہاء' (opinions of jurists) or 'امام شافعی کا نقط نظر' (Imam Shafi'i's viewpoint) that indicate intra-Hanafi or inter-madhhab disputes.
These differences are explored in the main text; the fihrist flags them.
5
Use the page number for deeper study
The numbers (e.g., 408, 440) refer to pages in the full commentary. Use them to find the detailed discussion with proofs and tarjih.
Cross-reference with the main text for full context.

Chapter of penalties. For a Muhrim applying perfume, expiation is obligatory: if applied on a complete limb, then a dam; if less than a limb, then sadaqah. A dam is fulfilled by a goat. The amount of sadaqah obligatory due to respect. Applying henna dye necessitates a dam. Applying olive oil necessitates a dam or sadaqah, statements of jurists. If two separate [places] on a wound or foot crack, then no expiation is obligatory. Wearing a sewn garment and covering the head for one complete day necessitates a dam, and less than a day necessitates sadaqah; the extent of a day, multiple opinions. Wearing a qamis as a sheet and doing idtiba', or making a sirwal into an izaar, nothing is obligatory. One quarter of the head or beard: a dam is sufficient; if less than one quarter, then sadaqah, opinions of jurists. Shaving the entire nape necessitates a dam; cutting hairs of both or one armpit necessitates a dam. Trimming mustache: one 'hukumat 'adl' (equitable value). Meaning of hukumat 'adl: shaving the place of cupping necessitates a dam or sadaqah, opinions of jurists. If someone shaves a Muhrim's head, with or without his command, then upon the barber is sadaqah and upon the shaved is a dam. Imam Shafi'i's viewpoint. If a Muhrim trims another non-Muhrim's mustache or cuts nails, he may give whatever food he wishes as sadaqah. Cutting fingernails and toenails necessitates a dam; cutting nails of one hand and one foot necessitates a dam; cutting fewer than five nails necessitates sadaqah. Cutting fewer than five nails from both hands and feet: sadaqah or dam, opinions of jurists. If a Muhrim's nail breaks and hangs, and he cuts it, what is the ruling? As long as in Makkah, repeating is better. If he returned to his family and performed tawaf al-ziyarah in a state of janabah, then repeating is necessary. Performing tawaf as-sadr in a state of hadath necessitates sadaqah. Tawaf al-ziyarah: if three circuits are missed, a goat is obligatory. Tawaf as-sadr: if all or four circuits are missed, a goat is obligatory. If he makes up the missed tawaf, nothing is obligatory. Tawaf al-ziyarah without wudu and tawaf as-sadr in the last days of tashriq in a state of purity: one dam; and if tawaf al-ziyarah was in a state of janabah, then two dams are obligatory. For Umrah, if tawaf and sa'y are performed without wudu, as long as he is in Makkah, if he repeats, nothing is obligatory. Abandoning sa'y necessitates a dam. Commentary
This passage is a table of contents (fihrist) for Volume 3 of Ashraf al-Hidayah, an Urdu commentary on al-Hidayah by Burhan al-Din al-Marghinani. It lists chapter headings and key rulings, especially from the chapter 'al-Jinayat' (offenses) related to Ihram violations. The rulings summarize penalties (dam, sadaqah, etc.) for various acts by a Muhrim (person in the state of Ihram). The text enumerates penalties for applying perfume, wearing sewn clothes, shaving, cutting nails, and other prohibitions. It also covers rulings on invalidating Hajj and Umrah through intercourse or other violations, and the required compensations. Differences of opinion among Hanafi jurists and with Imam al-Shafi'i are noted. The page numbers indicate where in the full text these rulings are discussed in detail. As a fihrist, this passage serves as a reference guide to the book's content.

Key Terms
دم (dam): A sacrificial animal (usually a goat or sheep) required as expiation for certain violations during Hajj or Umrah.
صدقہ (sadaqah): Charity given as expiation, often half a sa' of grain or equivalent, for minor violations during Ihram.
محرم (muhrim): A person who has entered the state of Ihram for Hajj or Umrah and is bound by its prohibitions.
