Annual Purchase from One-Third: Full Execution at Start
Ruling on annual purchase from one-third (non-specific slave)

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the Hanafi ruling on the timing of annual slave purchase from the one-third and explain why it is not distributed.

Core Idea
A bequest to buy a slave annually from the third must be executed at the start of the year, not spread out.

This passage from al-Jami' clarifies a common point of confusion: when a testator leaves one-third of his estate to annually buy and manumit a slave, must the executor spread the cost over the year? The answer is no—the full amount is taken at the beginning and the slave is manumitted immediately.

Guided Path
1
Identify the act
Recognize the testator's instruction: from his one-third share, a slave is to be purchased each year for a specified amount (here 200 dirhams) and then freed.
The key phrase is 'يشتري منه كل سنة بمائتي درهم عبداً فيعتق'.
2
Determine the timing ruling
The full amount for that year is taken at the beginning of the year, and the slave is manumitted immediately. The cost is not apportioned over the months.
Text: 'فإنه يشتري بذلك في أول السنة ويعتق عنه ولا يوزع على المدة'.
3
Understand the condition
This ruling applies only when the slave is not specifically named (غير معين). If the testator had specified a particular slave, the ruling differs, as shown in the next passage.
Condition: 'هذا إذا لم يعينه'.

Source Text

وفي الجامع : إذا أوصى بثلثه يشتري منه كل سنة بمائتي درهم عبداً فيعتق أو قال من ثلثي فإنه يشتري بذلك في أول السنة ويعتق عنه ولا يوزع على المدة. هذا إذا لم يعينه، فإن كان معيناً قال في الأصل: وإذا أوصى أن تعتق عنه جارية بعينها وهي تخرج من الثلث أو أوصى أن يشتري له نسمة بعينها وتعتق عنه فاشتريت له وجنى عليها جناية قبل العتق فإن الأرش للورثة، وإن مالاً يمكن إعتاقه يكون صارفاً وصية الميت إلى غير ما أوصى وهذا لا تجوز، اشتری به وكذلك لو كان الأرش عبداً مدفوعاً فيها فلو أعتق فإنه لا يعتق وكان ما اكتسب من مال فهو للورثة .
And in al-Jami': If he bequeaths his third, from which a slave is bought every year for two hundred dirhams and then manumitted, or he says 'from my third,' then it is bought with that at the beginning of the year and manumitted, and it is not distributed over the period. This is if he did not specify (the slave). But if he specified, it is stated in al-Asl: And if he bequeaths that a specific slave girl be manumitted and she comes out of the third, or he bequeaths that a specific soul be bought and manumitted, and it is bought for him, and a tort is committed against her before manumission, then the compensation (arsh) is for the heirs. And if there is property that can be used for manumission, it would divert the deceased's will to something not intended, and that is not permissible. And similarly, if the compensation is a slave paid for her, and then he (the executor) manumits, he does not manumit, and what is earned from the property is for the heirs.

Commentary
This passage from al-Jami' first addresses a will that stipulates an annual purchase and manumission of a slave from the testator's one-third share. The ruling: the full amount for the year's purchase is taken at the beginning of the year, and the slave is manumitted immediately; it is not spread out over the year. This applies when the slave is not specifically named (غير معين). Then the passage quotes from al-Asl regarding a specifically identified slave (معين): if a specific slave girl is bequeathed for manumission and she is within the one-third, or a specific slave is to be bought and manumitted, and before manumission a tort is committed against her (e.g., she is injured) and the perpetrator pays compensation (أرش), that compensation goes to the heirs, not to the manumission fund. The reason is that the testator's instruction was to manumit that specific slave, not to use compensation money to buy another; diverting the will is prohibited. Similarly, if the compensation is a slave, and the executor tries to manumit him, it is invalid, and any earnings from that slave belong to the heirs.

Key Terms
الأرش (al-arsh): Compensation for injury; here, the payment received when a slave is harmed before manumission.
نسمة (nasamah): A living soul; here, used metonymically for a slave to be bought and manumitted.

Checkpoint
According to al-Jami', when a testator bequeaths his third to buy a slave annually, when should the purchase be made?
A) At the end of the year to benefit from interest
B) At the beginning of the year
C) Spread evenly over the year
D) After one year has passed

Key Takeaway
When a testator bequeaths annual purchase of an unspecified slave from the third, the full amount is taken at the beginning of the year and the slave is freed immediately.

Bequest of a Specific Slave: Compensation Goes to Heirs
Ruling on compensation for a specifically bequeathed slave injured before manumission

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to explain the Hanafi ruling on compensation received for a specifically bequeathed slave that is injured before manumission.

Core Idea
If a specific slave is bequeathed for manumission and injured before freedom, the compensation goes to the heirs, not to the manumission fund.

This passage from al-Asl, quoted in al-Jami', addresses a situation where a testator specifies a particular slave for manumission. If that slave is injured before being freed, the compensation (arsh) paid by the perpetrator does not go toward purchasing another slave; instead, it belongs to the heirs. This ruling protects the testator's specific intention.

Guided Path
1
Identify the act
The testator specifies a particular slave (e.g., a named slave girl or a 'soul' ) to be manumitted from his one-third.
Text: 'إذا أوصى أن تعتق عنه جارية بعينها' or 'يشتري له نسمة بعينها'.
2
Identify the event before manumission
Before the slave is freed, a tort (jinaayah) is committed against her, and compensation (arsh) is paid.
Text: 'فاشتريت له وجنى عليها جناية قبل العتق'.
3
Apply the ruling
The compensation belongs to the testator's heirs, not to the manumission fund. It cannot be used to buy another slave or to manumit a slave received as compensation.
Reason: using it would divert the testator's will to something not intended ('صارفاً وصية الميت إلى غير ما أوصى').

Qadi Hiring for Orphan: Liability for Excessive Wage
Ruling on qadi's liability for hiring at excessive wage for orphan.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the Hanafi ruling on the qadi's liability when hiring a worker for an orphan at an excessive wage, and distinguish the consequences based on the qadi's knowledge.

Core Idea
If the qadi deliberately overpays, he bears the cost; if unaware, only market wage from orphan's estate.

This lesson examines a specific ruling from al-Dhakhira regarding the qadi's role in managing the orphan's property. It clarifies when the qadi is personally liable for hiring at a wage above the market rate (ajr al-mithl). Understanding this distinction protects the orphan's estate and ensures the qadi's accountability.

Guided Path
1
Identify the factual scenarios
The passage presents two scenarios: (1) the qadi hires at excessive wage without knowing it is excessive, (2) the qadi admits he deliberately overpaid. Note the distinction.
The key factor is the qadi's knowledge and intent.
2
Determine the ruling for each scenario
In scenario one, the worker receives only the equivalent wage (ajr al-mithl) from the orphan's property. In scenario two, the entire agreed wage is due from the qadi's personal property.
The contract is valid in both cases, but liability shifts.
3
Extract the legal principle
The qadi is a fiduciary (amin) for the orphan. If he acts within normal discretion (unaware), the orphan bears the expense at market rate. If he exceeds authority with knowledge, he is personally liable for the excess.
This protects the orphan from the qadi's negligence or misconduct.
4
Apply to another scenario
Consider a case where the qadi lends orphan's money: the passage states it is valid (presumably with proper safeguards).
Lending is distinct from hiring; the qadi's authority extends to productive use of orphan's funds.

Source Text

وفي الذخيرة : القاضي إذا استأجر لليتيم أجيراً بأكثر من أجر المثل بحيث لا يتغابن الناس ولم يعلم القاضي بذلك فللأجير أجر مثل عمله في مال اليتيم، ولو قال القاضي تعمدت الجواز تنفذ الإجارة على القاضي ويجب جميع الأجر جر في مال القاضي، وإذا أقرض مال اليتيم صح.
And in al-Dhakhira: If the qadi hires a worker for the orphan at more than the equivalent wage, to the extent that people do not practice mutual overcharging, and the qadi did not know that, then the worker is entitled to the equivalent wage from the orphan's property. And if the qadi said 'I deliberately allowed it,' the hire contract is executed against the qadi and the entire wage becomes due in the qadi's property. And if he lends the orphan's money, it is valid.
Source pages p. 320

Commentary
This passage from al-Dhakhira discusses the qadi's liability when hiring for the orphan. If the qadi hires a worker at a wage exceeding the market rate (ajr al-mithl) but within the range of what people normally accept (i.e., not grossly excessive), and the qadi is unaware of the overpayment, the worker receives only the standard wage from the orphan's property. However, if the qadi admits deliberate overpayment, the contract binds the qadi personally, and he must pay the entire wage from his own pocket. This protects the orphan's estate from the qadi's negligence or misconduct. Lastly, the qadi may lend the orphan's money as a valid transaction (presumably to a trustworthy person).

Key Terms
أجر المثل (ajr al-mithl): Market rate wage for similar work.
تغابن الناس (taghabun al-nas): Mutual overcharging commonly tolerated in the market.

Key Terms from Source Passage

Key Takeaway
The qadi's liability for overpaying a worker depends on his knowledge; if aware, he bears the full cost; if unaware, only market wage from the orphan's estate.
