Hawala of Mahr and Subsequent Gift: Invalidity Due to Right of Assignee
Ruling on invalid gift of mahr after hawala

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the Hanafi ruling on gifting mahr after hawala and explain the legal reason (the right of the assignee akin to rahn).

Core Idea
A right attached by hawala prevents the original owner from gifting the property.

In this lesson, we examine a specific scenario from the matn: a wife assigns her claim to mahr to a third party via hawala, then attempts to gift that same mahr to her husband. The Hanafi school rules the gift invalid because the assignee's right has attached, similar to a pledge (rahn). Understanding this ruling clarifies how hawala transfers rights in Hanafi jurisprudence.

Guided Path
1
Identify the two legal acts
The wife first makes a hawala (assignment of the mahr debt) to a third party, then later purports to gift the same mahr to her husband.
2
Determine the timing
The gift occurs after the hawala has already been executed. This order is critical.
3
Understand the effect of hawala in Hanafi law
Hawala transfers the right of claim from the wife to the assignee. The assignee (muhtal) acquires a right over the mahr, akin to a pledgee's right over the pledged property.
4
Apply the analogy to rahn
Just as a pledgor cannot sell or gift the pledged item without the pledgee's consent, the wife cannot dispose of the mahr after the assignee's right has attached.
5
Conclude the ruling
Therefore, the gift is invalid (batil). Any subsequent disposal by the wife is annulled.

قال رحمه الله: (ولو أحالت انسانا على الزوج بالمهر ثم وهبت المهر للزوج لا يصح لأنه تعلق به حق المحتال على مثال الرهن وإن كان أسوة الغرماء عند موتها فيرد تصرفها فيه فصار كما لو باع المرهون أو وهبه.
He said, may Allah have mercy on him: (And if she makes a hawala (assignment) of a person upon the husband for the mahr, then she gifts the mahr to the husband, it is not valid) because the right of the assignee (muhtal) has become attached to it, similar to a pledge (rahn), even if he is like other creditors upon her death, so her disposal of it is annulled. Thus it becomes as if she sold or gifted a mortgaged property.

Commentary
This passage is a ruling from the matn expanded by the commentator. It discusses the scenario where a wife assigns (hawala) her right to receive mahr from her husband to a third party (creditor). After that, she attempts to gift the same mahr back to her husband. The gift is invalid because the right of the assignee (the person to whom the hawala was made) has become attached to that mahr, similar to a pledge (rahn). Even if the assignee is merely an unsecured creditor in case of her death, her subsequent disposal (gift) is not effective. The analogy is that one cannot sell or gift something that is already pledged.

Key Terms
الحوالة (al-hawala): Assignment of a debt from one debtor to another; here the wife assigns her claim of mahr from the husband to a third party.
المحتال (al-muhtal): The person to whom the hawala is made (the assignee/beneficiary).
