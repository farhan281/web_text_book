Nushuz of the Wife by Preventing Entry
Ruling on when a wife becomes nashiza by preventing her husband from entering her house.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to distinguish between justified and unjustified prevention by a wife and state the effect on her right to maintenance.

Core Idea
A wife becomes nashiza only when she unjustly prevents her husband from entering her home, forfeiting maintenance.

Marital accommodation rights are a key area of fiqh al-usra. This passage from al-Bahr al-Raiq explains when a wife's refusal to allow her husband entry constitutes nushuz (disobedience) and when it is justified. Understanding the conditions that trigger or block nushuz is essential for correctly applying the ruling on spousal maintenance (nafaqah).

Guided Path
1
Identify the act in question
The act is the wife preventing her husband from entering her house while he resides there with her. This is the central action under scrutiny.
Focus on 'prevention' as the wife's action.
2
Determine the justification for prevention
Examine whether the wife's prevention is with right (bi haqq) or without right (bi ghayr haqq). Justified reasons include: the husband refuses to provide a separate house (which he is obligated to provide), or the husband resides in a usurped house. Unjustified prevention occurs without such reasons.
Justification is the key factor that changes the ruling.
3
Apply the ruling
If prevention is unjustified: the wife becomes nashiza (disobedient) and loses her right to maintenance for the duration of prevention. If prevention is justified: she is not nashiza and maintenance continues.
The ruling hinges on whether the prevention is bi haqq or bi ghayr haqq.

Source Text

قال رحمه الله : (منعها) كلام أضافي مبتدأ أي منع المرأة زوجها عن الدخول عليها و الحال أنه (هو) أي الزوج يسكن معها في بيتها نشوز) لأنها حبست نفسها منه بغير حق فلا تجب النفقة لها ما دامت على منعه فيتحقق النشوز منها فصار كحبسها نفسها في منزل غيرها. هذا إذا منعته ومرادها السكنى في منزلها، وإن كان المنع لينقلها إلى منزله لا تكون ناشزة لأن السكنى واجبة لها عليه فكان حبسها نفسها منه بحق فلا تسقط نفقتها لأن التقصير جاء من جهته فصار كما إذا حبست نفسها لاستيفاء مهرها بخلاف ما إذا حبست بسبب دين عليها أو غصبها غاصب وذهب بها لأن الفوات ليس من قبله، وبخلاف ما إذا كانت ساكنة معه في منزله ولم تمكنه من الوطء لأنه يمكنه الوطء كرهاً غالباً رحمه الله: (ولو سكن في بيت الغصب فامتنع لا تكون ناشزة) لأنها محقة لأن السكنى فيه حرام قال رحمه الله : (قالت لا أسكن مع أمتك وأريد بيتاً على حدة وليس لها ذلك) لأنه لا بد له ممن يخدمه فلا يمكن منعه من ذلك.
He said: 'She prevented him' — meaning the wife prevented her husband from entering upon her while he is residing with her in her house — this is nushuz (disobedience) because she has detained herself from him without right, so maintenance is not obligatory for her as long as she persists in preventing him ... If she prevented him with the intention of moving him to his own house, she is not nashiza because she has a right to accommodation from him ... If he resides in a usurped house and she prevents him, she is not nashiza because the residence is unlawful ... If she says 'I will not live with your slave-girl, I want a separate house,' she does not have that right because he needs her service.

Commentary
This passage discusses the concept of nushuz (disobedience) in the context of marital accommodation. The ruling is: if a wife prevents her husband from entering her house (where he has been living with her), she becomes nashiza (disobedient) and loses her right to maintenance because she is unjustly withholding herself. However, if she prevents him only to compel him to move to his own house (which he is obligated to provide), she is not nashiza because her action is justified. Similarly, if the husband lives in a usurped house, she may prevent him without becoming nashiza. The passage also clarifies that a wife may not demand a separate house solely to avoid living with the husband's slave-girl, as he has the right to her service.

Key Terms
نشوز (nushūz): Disobedience of the wife by preventing the husband from lawful access.

Checkpoint

Key Takeaway
A wife's prevention of her husband's entry is nushuz only when it is without right; justified prevention does not forfeit maintenance.
