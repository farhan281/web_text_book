Continuing in an Invalidated Ritual: No Tamattu' Blood Required
Ruling on invalidation and tamattu' blood

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the ruling for a mutamatti' who invalidates one of his rituals and explain the reason why tamattu' blood is not due.

Core Idea
Tamattu' blood requires two valid rituals; if one is invalidated, no tamattu' blood is owed.

This passage from al-Bahr al-Raiq addresses a specific scenario: a pilgrim who enters into tamattu' by performing Umrah and then Hajj in the same year, but then invalidates one of the two rituals. The ruling is that he must continue in that ritual because he cannot exit ihram without completing its actions. However, he does not owe the tamattu' sacrifice because the underlying cause (sabab) for that obligation—performing two valid rituals in one journey—is not fulfilled.

Guided Path
1
Identify the act and the ruling
The act is the invalidation of either Umrah or Hajj by a mutamatti'. The ruling is that the pilgrim must continue (yumḍī fīhi) and that no blood (dam) of tamattu' is due.
The 'dam' referred to here is specifically the tamattu' blood, not the penalty for invalidation itself.
2
Understand the reason for continuing
Once in ihram, one cannot simply exit; the state of ihram continues until the rituals are completed. Therefore, even if the ritual is invalidated, the pilgrim must proceed with it (after repentance if applicable) until the end.
This is a general principle in Hajj: invalidation does not allow immediate release from ihram.
3
Analyze the condition for tamattu' blood
Tamattu' blood is obligatory because the pilgrim benefits from performing two rituals in one journey. This benefit requires both rituals to be valid. If one is invalidated, the benefit is lost, and the cause for the blood is absent.
The cause (sabab) is 'intifaʿ bi-adaʾ nusukayn ṣaḥīḥayn fī safar wāḥid'.
4
Distinguish from invalidation blood
The negation of tamattu' blood does not negate other penalties. If the Hajj itself was invalidated (e.g., by sexual intercourse before the first tahallul), a separate dam (dam al-ifsaad) is due as a penalty for the invalidation, but that is a different ruling.
The text says 'wa illā fa-man afsada ḥajjah lazamahu dam' (otherwise, if one invalidates his Hajj, a dam is due). This refers to the invalidation penalty, not tamattu' blood.
5
Apply the ruling
Given a mutamatti' who invalidates his Umrah: he must complete that Umrah (though invalid) and then perform Hajj (which remains valid). No tamattu' blood is due. If Hajj was invalidated, he must continue with it (after making amends) and Umrah is already valid; still no tamattu' blood.
The key is that at least one ritual was not validly completed, so the condition for tamattu' blood is not met.

Tamattu' Sacrifice: Udhiyah Does Not Suffice
Ruling that udhiyah does not count for tamattu'.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the ruling that an udhiyah does not fulfill the tamattu' sacrifice and explain why, based on the distinct wajib status and traveler exemption.

Core Idea
The tamattu' dam is a distinct wajib; an udhiyah, even if slaughtered, cannot replace it.

When performing Hajj as a mutamatti', a specific sacrifice (dam al-tamattu') is obligatory. A common mistake is assuming any sacrifice, such as the Udhiyah of Eid, suffices. This passage clarifies that the udhiyah is a separate act and, moreover, not even obligatory for the traveler. The commentator also addresses why Imam Muhammad singled out women in the ruling.

Guided Path
1
Identify the action and its ruling
State clearly what the person did (tamattu' and slaughtered udhiyah) and the legal outcome (does not suffice).
2
Understand the reasoning
Why does udhiyah not suffice? Because the required act is the tamattu' dam; udhiyah is a different obligation. Also, udhiyah is not even obligatory for a traveler (mutamatti').
The mutamatti' is a traveler, so udhiyah is not wajib for him.
3
Note the scope of the ruling
The ruling applies to both men and women, though Muhammad specifically mentioned women because ignorance is more common among them.
The absolute wording covers all.
4
Consider the consequence of mistaken belief
If the person mistakenly thought udhiyah sufficed and performed tahallul, they owe two sacrifices: one for tamattu' and one for shaving before completing the sacrifice.
This scenario highlights the need for correct knowledge.

Muttamatti's Udhiyah Does Not Substitute for Wajib Dam
Ruling that sacrificing an udhiyah does not substitute for the wajib dam of tamattu'.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to explain why sacrificing an udhiyah does not fulfill the wajib dam of tamattu' and state the consequence if the person does so.

Core Idea
The dam of tamattu' is wajib; the udhiyah is not wajib on a traveler, so one cannot substitute the other.

In this passage from Al-Bahr al-Ra'iq, the author addresses a common confusion: Can a muttamatti fulfill his obligation of the dam of tamattu' by sacrificing an udhiyah (Eid sacrifice)? The answer is no, and the reasoning hinges on the nature of each obligation. Let's unpack the legal reasoning.

Guided Path
1
Identify the act and its ruling
The act is a muttamatti sacrificing an udhiyah. The ruling: it does not suffice for the dam of tamattu'.
The ruling is explicitly stated in the base text.
2
Identify the two obligations
One is the dam of tamattu' (wajib), the other is the udhiyah (not wajib on a traveler).
The udhiyah is not obligatory upon him because he is a traveler.
3
Locate the reasoning (talil)
The text says 'because he did something other than what is obligatory' – the wajib is the dam, not the udhiyah.
The proof is the distinction between the two sacrifices.

The ruling applies to both men and women, but Imam Muhammad mentions a woman as an example.
The example may be due to occurrence or common confusion among women.
5
Consider the consequence
If the muttamatti shaved before realizing, two dams become obligatory: one for tamattu' and one for the premature shaving.
The text explicitly states this consequence.

Menstruating Woman's Hajj: Exemption from Tawaf and Tawaf al-Sadr
Rulings for a menstruating woman: performs all hajj except tawaf; no penalty for leaving tawaf al-sadr.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the ruling for a menstruating woman regarding tawaf and explain the consequence of omitting tawaf al-sadr.

Core Idea
A menstruating pilgrim does everything except tawaf; the farewell tawaf is waived by excuse.

This passage from Al-Bahr al-Raiq clarifies the rulings for a woman who begins menstruating during Hajj. She may perform all rituals except tawaf (circumambulation), which requires purity. The farewell tawaf (tawaf al-sadr) is a wajib that is dropped due to the valid excuse of menstruation, incurring no penalty. Understanding this distinction prevents unnecessary anxiety and ensures correct practice.

Guided Path
1
Identify the act and its ruling
The passage rules on two tawafs: tawaf al-ziyarah (the visit tawaf, a rukn) and tawaf al-sadr (farewell tawaf, a wajib). Note that menstruation prevents both, but the consequences differ.
2
Locate the dalil
The proof is the hadith of Aisha in Sarif: 'Do what the pilgrim does except do not perform tawaf around the House until you become pure.' This is a clear textual evidence from the Sunnah.
The hadith is ahad (single narration), so its thubut is zanni.
3
Trace the wajh al-dalalah
The Prophet's command to perform all acts except tawaf indicates that tawaf is the only act requiring purity. This establishes that menstruation does not prevent other acts like sa'i or stoning.
The exception (except tawaf) shows the condition of purity is specific to tawaf.
4
Note the ikhtilaf
There is disagreement on whether staying in Makkah after the first departure affects the dropping of tawaf al-sadr. Also, the Shafi'i school considers tawaf al-sadr as sunnah, so no penalty regardless.
The Hanafi position is that tawaf al-sadr is wajib but waived by excuse.
5
Evaluate the tarjih
The relied-upon (mufta bihi) Hanafi position is that menstruation excuses from tawaf al-sadr with no penalty. This is from Zahir al-Riwayah and supported by the hadith.
No explicit tarjih authority is mentioned, but the text states 'nothing is due upon her'.

Intention for Dam al-Tamattu' and the Qiyas with Tawaf al-Rukn
Derivation of intention requirement for dam al-tamattu' with qiyas analysis.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to explain the Hanafi ruling that dam al-tamattu' requires intention (niyyah) and apply the qiyas with tawaf al-rukn to distinguish when supererogatory intention suffices.

Core Idea
Dam al-tamattu' requires intention; unlike tawaf al-rukn, its time is not specific, so supererogatory intention does not suffice.

In this passage, al-Bahr al-Ra'iq derives from the matn of Kanz al-Daqa'iq that dam al-tamattu' needs intention, drawing an analogy with tawaf al-rukn. However, a margin note clarifies that the time-specific nature of tawaf makes the analogy incomplete. We will dissect the reasoning and its implications.

Guided Path
1
Identify the derivation from the matn
The phrase "وَإِلَّا قَدَّمَ التَّمَتُّعَ" implies that the dam must be intended; the author states it is derived that dam al-tamattu' needs intention.
2
Understand the initial analogy
The text argues that if tawaf al-rukn can be fulfilled by intending supererogatory tawaf, then dam, being less significant, should also be covered.
3
Examine the objection from al-Sharnablaliyya
The objection: tawaf al-rukn is time-specific (days of sacrifice), so any tawaf in that time counts for the obligation; dam al-tamattu' is not similarly time-specific.
4
Evaluate the preferred response
The author concludes that tawaf's time-specificity allows supererogatory intention to suffice, whereas dam al-tamattu' lacks that specificity, so intention must be specific.
5
Apply the ruling
A mutamatti' must intend dam al-tamattu' when slaughtering; if he intends a different sacrifice (e.g., udhiyah), it does not count.

Commentary
This passage from Al-Bahr al-Ra'iq is a commentary (sharh) on the matn of Kanz al-Daqa'iq, addressing rulings related to menstruation during Hajj and the need for intention in dam al-tamattu'. The commentary opens with a discussion on the ruling that if a menstruating woman cannot perform the farewell tawaf (tawaf al-sadr), she abandons it and it is as if she stayed in Makkah (i.e., no penalty). The author then derives that dam al-tamattu' requires intention (niyyah), drawing an analogy with tawaf al-rukn where a supererogatory intention suffices for the obligatory tawaf. He argues that if tawaf can be intended as supererogatory and yet fulfill the compulsory, then dam al-tamattu' should be even more applicable since it is a financial obligation. Next, the commentary presents the ruling from the matn: if a woman menstruates at the time of ihram, she performs all Hajj rituals except tawaf. This is proven by the hadith where the Prophet (peace be upon him) told Aisha at Sarif, 'Do what the pilgrim does except do not circumambulate the House until you become pure.' The hadith indicates that tawaf is forbidden for her due to two reasons: entering the mosque in a state of impurity and abandoning the obligatory purity (taharah) for tawaf. If she were to circumambulate, she would be sinful and must repeat the tawaf; if she does not repeat, she must sacrifice a badanah (camel) and her Hajj remains valid. The commentary then addresses the case of menstruation at the time of farewell tawaf: she abandons it with no penalty because it is an obligation that falls due to the excuse of menstruation or postpartum bleeding. Similarly, if she delays tawaf al-ziyarah until she is pure, nothing is due. The author notes that the ruling is unrestricted, but there is a difference of opinion regarding the case of staying in Makkah after the first departure. The margin note (hāshiya) elaborates on the analogy between tawaf al-rukn and dam al-tamattu', responding to an objection from al-Sharnablaliyya and concluding that tawaf al-rukn's time-specific nature allows supererogatory intention to suffice, unlike dam al-tamattu'. The final portion from al-Lubab specifies that if a woman menstruates before she can perform most of tawaf al-ziyarah, she is excused; but if she is able to perform four circuits and delays, she owes a blood sacrifice. The key lesson is that a menstruating woman is excused from tawaf, and the details depend on her ability to perform most of it.

Key Terms
دم التمتع (dam al-tamattu'): The blood sacrifice (dam) required of a mutamatti' who performs 'Umrah before Hajj and then performs Hajj in the same year.
نية (niyyah): Intention, a required condition for the validity of the dam sacrifice.
طواف الركن (tawaf al-rukn): The obligatory tawaf of Hajj, also called tawaf al-ziyarah, performed on the days of sacrifice.

Rulings for a Menstruating Woman in Hajj: Tawaf and Penalties
Rulings for a menstruating woman in Hajj with proof from hadith.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the Hanafi rulings for a menstruating woman in Hajj regarding tawaf, including the prohibition, required repetition, penalty of badanah, and excusal from farewell tawaf.

Core Idea
A menstruating woman is excused from farewell tawaf without penalty; if she performs tawaf, she must repeat or pay a camel; delaying tawaf al-ziyarah may incur a dam if she could have done most of it.

This passage presents the detailed Hanafi rules for a woman who menstruates during Hajj. Based on the hadith of Aisha, she performs all rituals except tawaf. The commentary then addresses the farewell tawaf and the penalties for delayed tawaf al-ziyarah, with conditions from al-Lubab.

Guided Path
1
Identify the primary hadith
The Prophet told Aisha: "Do what the pilgrim does except do not circumambulate the House until you become pure." This is the core textual evidence.
2
Derive the prohibition of tawaf
The hadith explicitly forbids tawaf during menstruation. Two reasons: entering the mosque in impurity and abandoning the obligatory state of purity for tawaf.
3
Determine the consequences of violating the prohibition
If she circumambulates, she is sinful, must repeat the tawaf, and if she does not repeat, she must sacrifice a badanah (camel). Her Hajj remains valid.
4
Apply the ruling to farewell tawaf (tawaf al-sadr)
If she menstruates at the time of departure, she abandons it with no penalty, as it is a wajib that falls due to the excuse of menstruation/postpartum bleeding.
5
Analyze the condition for delaying tawaf al-ziyarah
If she is unable to perform most of the tawaf (4 out of 7 circuits) due to menstruation, she is excused. If she could have performed most but delayed, she owes a dam.

(١) الْمُتَمَتِّعُ وَدَمُ التَّحَلُّلِ قَبْلَ أَوَانِهِ وَإِلَّا قَدَّمَ التَّمَتُّعَ . وَقَدِ اسْتُفِيدَ مِنْ هَذَا أَنَّ دَمَ التَّمَتُّعِ يَحْتَاجُ إِلَى النِّيَّةِ، وَقَدْ يُقَالُ إِنَّهُ لَيْسَ فَوْقَ طَوَافِ الرُّكْنِ وَلَا مِثْلَهُ وَقَدْ قَدَّمْنَا أَنَّهُ لَوْ نَوَى بِهِ التَّطَوُّعَ أَجْزَأَهُ عَنِ الرُّكْنِ فَيَنْبَغِي أَنْ يَكُونَ الدَّمُ كَذَلِكَ بَلْ أَوْلَى . قَوْلُهُ : ( وَلَوْ حَاضَتْ عِنْدَ الْإِحْرَامِ أَتَتْ بِغَيْرِ الطَّوَافِ ) لِقَوْلِهِ عَلَيْهِ السَّلَامُ لِعَائِشَةَ حِينَ حَاضَتْ بِسَرِفَ : ( افْعَلِي مَا يَفْعَلُ الْحَاجُّ غَيْرَ أَنْ لَا تَطُوفِي بِالْبَيْتِ حَتَّى تَطْهُرِي ) فَأَفَادَ أَنَّ طَوَافَهَا حَرَامٌ وَهُوَ مِنْ وَجْهَيْنِ : دُخُولُهَا الْمَسْجِدَ وَتَرْكُ وَاجِبِ الطَّهَارَةِ؛ فَإِنَّ الطَّهَارَةَ وَاجِبَةٌ فِي الطَّوَافِ فَلَا يَحِلُّ لَهَا أَنْ تَطُوفَ حَتَّى تَطْهُرَ، فَإِنْ طَافَتْ كَانَتْ عَاصِيَةً مُسْتَحِقَّةً لِعِقَابِ اللهِ وَلَزِمَهَا الْإِعَادَةُ، فَإِنْ لَمْ تُعِدْ كَانَ عَلَيْهَا بَدَنَةٌ وَتَمَّ حَجُّهَا . قَوْلُهُ : ( وَلَوْ عِنْدَ الصَّدْرِ تَرَكْتَهُ كَمَنْ أَقَامَ بِمَكَّةَ ) يَعْنِي وَلَا شَيْءَ عَلَيْهَا لِأَنَّهُ وَاجِبٌ يَسْقُطُ بِالْعُذْرِ وَالْحَيْضُ وَالنِّفَاسُ عُذْرٌ، وَكَذَا إِذَا أَخَّرَتْ طَوَافَ الزِّيَارَةِ إِلَى وَقْتِ طُهْرِهَا فَإِنَّهُ لَا يَجِبُ عَلَيْهَا شَيْءٌ لِلْعُذْرِ وَقَدْ قَدَّمْنَا ذَلِكَ كُلَّهُ فِي طَوَافِ الصَّدْرِ. وَأُطْلِقَ فِي سُقُوطِهِ عَمَّنْ أَقَامَ بِمَكَّةَ فَشَمِلَ مَا إِذَا أَقَامَ بَعْدَ مَا حَلَّ النَّفْرُ الْأَوَّلُ أَوْ لَا وَفِيهِ اخْتِلَافٌ، وَقَدْ قَدَّمْنَاهُ هُنَاكَ وَاللهُ تَعَالَى أَعْلَمُ بِالصَّوَابِ وَإِلَيْهِ الْمَرْجِعُ وَالْمَآبُ . تَمَّ الْجُزْءُ الثَّانِي وَيَلِيهِ الْجُزْءُ الثَّالِثُ وَأَوَّلُهُ بَابُ الْجِنَايَاتِ
التَّصْرِيحُ بِهَذَا الْمُسْتَفَادِ عَنِ الدِّرَايَةِ قَوْلُهُ : ( وَقَدْ يُقَالُ الْخ ) ذَكَرَ فِي الشَّرَنْبَلَالِيَّةِ مِثْلُهُ قَبْلَ رُؤْيَتِهِ لِمَا ذَكَرَهُ الْمُؤَلِّفُ ثُمَّ قَالَ : لَكِنَّهُ قَدْ يُقَالُ : لَمَّا كَانَ طَوَافُ الرُّكْنِ مُتَعَيِّنًا فِي أَيَّامِ النَّحْرِ وُجُوبًا كَانَ النَّظَرُ لِإِيقَاعِ مَا طَافَهُ عَنْهُ وَتَلْغُونَ غَيْرُهُ، وَأَمَّا الْأُضْحِيَّةُ فَهِيَ مُتَعَيِّنَةٌ فِي ذَلِكَ الزَّمَنِ كَالْمُتْعَةِ فَلَا تَقَعُ الْأُضْحِيَّةُ . تَعَيُّنِهَا عَنْ غَيْرِهَا ا هـ. وَاعْتُرِضَ بِأَنَّهُ إِنْ أَرَادَ أَنَّ الْأُضْحِيَّةَ مُتَعَيِّنَةٌ فِي حَقِّ غَيْرِ ذَلِكَ الْمُتَمَتِّعِ فَمُسَلَّمٌ. وَلَا كَلَامَ فِيهِ وَإِنْ أَرَادَ أَنَّهَا مُتَعَيِّنَةٌ فِي حَقِّهِ أَيْضًا فَلَا يُسَلَّمُ إِذْ هِيَ - غَيْرُ وَاجِبَةٍ عَلَيْهِ لِكَوْنِهِ مُسَافِرًا، أَمَّا الْمُتْعَةُ فَهِيَ مُتَعَيِّنَةٌ عَلَيْهِ فَسَاوَتِ الطَّوَافَ ا هـ . فَالْأَوْلَى مَا أَجَابَ بِهِ بَعْضُهُمْ أَنَّ طَوَافَ الرُّكْنِ لَمَّا كَانَ الْوَقْتُ مُتَعَيِّنًا لَهُ لَا يَسَعُ غَيْرَهُ أَجْزَأَتْهُ نِيَّةُ التَّطَوُّعِ بِخِلَافِ دَمِ التَّمَتُّعِ، وَلَا يَخْفَى أَنَّ هَذَا غَيْرُ مَا فِي الشَّرَنْبَلَالِيَّةِ وَلَا يَرِدُ عَلَيْهِ الِاعْتِرَاضُ الْمَارِّ خِلَافًا لِمَا زَعَمَهُ الْمُعْتَرِضُ . قَوْلُهُ : ( وَكَذَا إِذَا أَخَّرَتْ طَوَافَ الزِّيَارَةِ ) أَيْ إِذَا حَاضَتْ قَبْلَ أَنْ تَقْدِرَ عَلَى أَكْثَرِ الطَّوَافِ . قَالَ فِي اللِّبَابِ : وَلَوْ حَاضَتْ فِي وَقْتٍ تَقْدِرُ عَلَى أَنْ تَطُوفَ أَرْبَعَةَ أَشْوَاطٍ فَلَمْ تَطُفْ لَزِمَهَا لِلتَّأْخِيرِ، وَلَوْ حَاضَتْ فِي وَقْتٍ تَقْدِرُ عَلَى أَقَلَّ مِنْ ذَلِكَ لَمْ يَلْزَمْهَا شَيْءٌ، فَقَوْلُهُمْ « لَا شَيْءَ عَلَى الْحَائِضِ وَكَذَا النَّفْسَاءِ لِتَأْخِيرِ الطَّوَافِ مُقَيَّدٌ بِمَا إِذَا حَاضَتْ فِي وَقْتٍ لَمْ تَقْدِرْ عَلَى أَكْثَرِ الطَّوَافِ أَوْ حَاضَتْ قَبْلَ أَيَّامِ النَّحْرِ وَلَمْ تَطْهُرْ إِلَّا بَعْدَ مَضِيِّ أَيَّامِ النَّحْرِ ا هـ . لِمَا ذَكَرَهُ فِي اللِّبَابِ أَيْضًا مِنْ أَنَّهَا لَوْ طَهُرَتْ فِي آخِرِ أَيَّامِ النَّحْرِ وَيُمْكِنُهَا طَوَافُ الزِّيَارَةِ كُلَّهُ أَوْ أَكْثَرُهُ قَبْلَ الْغُرُوبِ فَلَمْ تَطُفْ فَعَلَيْهَا دَمٌ لِلتَّأْخِيرِ، وَإِنْ أَمْكَنَهَا أَقَلُّهُ فَلَمْ تَطُفْ لَا شَيْءَ عَلَيْهَا وَاللهُ سُبْحَانَهُ وَتَعَالَى أَعْلَمُ .
(١) رَوَاهُ الْبُخَارِيُّ فِي كِتَابِ الْحَيْضِ بَابٌ ١٧ْ كِتَابُ الْحَجِّ بَابٌ ٨١ْ مُسْلِمٌ فِي كِتَابِ الْحَجِّ حَدِيثُ ١١٩. أَبُو دَاوُدَ فِي كِتَابِ الْمَنَاسِكِ بَابٌ ٢٣. النَّسَائِيُّ فِي كِتَابِ الْمَنَاسِكِ بَابٌ ٥١. الْمُوَطَّأُ فِي كِتَابِ الْحَجِّ حَدِيثٌ ٢٢٤ . أَحْمَدُ فِي مُسْنَدِهِ (١/ ٣٦٤) (٣٩/٦).
Book of Hajj, Chapter of Tamattu'. The tawaf, even if at the time of al-sadr (farewell tawaf), she abandons it like one who stayed in Makkah. (1) The mutamatti' and the blood of tahallul before its time, otherwise he gives precedence to tamattu'. And it is derived from this that the blood of tamattu' requires intention. And it may be said that it is not above the tawaf al-rukn nor like it, and we have presented that if he intended with it supererogatory, it suffices him for the rukn, so the blood should be likewise, rather more so. His statement: (And if she menstruates at the time of ihram, she performs other than tawaf) due to his saying, peace be upon him, to Aisha when she menstruated at Sarif: 'Do what the pilgrim does except do not circumambulate the House until you become pure.' So it indicates that her tawaf is forbidden, and it is from two aspects: her entering the mosque and leaving the obligatory purity; for purity is obligatory in tawaf, so it is not permissible for her to circumambulate until she becomes pure. If she circumambulates, she is disobedient, deserving the punishment of Allah, and repetition is incumbent upon her. If she does not repeat, upon her is a badanah (camel) and her Hajj is complete. His statement: (And if at the time of al-sadr she abandons it, she is like one who stayed in Makkah) meaning nothing is upon her because it is an obligation that falls due to an excuse, and menstruation and postpartum bleeding are excuses. Likewise, if she delays tawaf al-ziyarah until the time of her purity, then nothing is obligatory upon her due to the excuse, and we have presented all of that in the tawaf al-sadr. And it was left unrestricted in its falling from one who stayed in Makkah, so it includes when he stayed after the first departure or not, and there is difference in it, and we have presented it there. And Allah Almighty knows best the correct, and to Him is the return and the abode. Completed the second part, followed by the third part, and its beginning is the chapter of injuries. The explicit statement of this derived from al-Dirayah: His statement: (And it may be said etc.) He mentioned in al-Sharnablaliyya similar to it before his seeing of what the author mentioned, then he said: But it may be said: Since tawaf al-rukn is specific to the days of sacrifice as an obligation, consideration is for the occurrence of what he circumambulated for it and the cancelation of other than it. As for the udhiyah, it is specific to that time like tamattu', so the udhiyah does not occur. Its specificity from other than it — end quote. And it was objected that if he meant that the udhiyah is specific for other than that mutamatti', then it is granted, and there is no speech about it. And if he meant that it is specific for him as well, then it is not granted since it is not obligatory upon him due to his being a traveler. As for tamattu', it is specific upon him, so it equals the tawaf — end quote. So the better is what some answered: that tawaf al-rukn, since the time is specific to it and does not accommodate other than it, his intention of supererogatory suffices, unlike the blood of tamattu'. And it is not hidden that this is different from what is in al-Sharnablaliyya, and the past objection does not apply to it contrary to what the objector claimed. His statement: (And likewise if she delays tawaf al-ziyarah) i.e., if she menstruates before she is able to do most of the tawaf. He said in al-Lubab: If she menstruates at a time when she is able to circumambulate four circuits and she does not circumambulate, a penalty is incumbent upon her for the delay. And if she menstruates at a time when she is able to do less than that, nothing is incumbent upon her. So their statement 'Nothing is upon the menstruating woman and likewise the postpartum woman for delaying tawaf' is restricted to when she menstruates at a time when she is not able to do most of the tawaf, or she menstruates before the days of sacrifice and does not become pure except after the days of sacrifice have passed — end quote. Based on what he mentioned in al-Lubab also: that if she becomes pure at the end of the days of sacrifice and she is able to do the entire tawaf al-ziyarah or most of it before sunset and she does not circumambulate, then upon her is a dam for the delay. And if she is able to do less than it and she does not circumambulate, nothing is upon her. And Allah, glorified and exalted, knows best.

Commentary
This passage from Al-Bahr al-Ra'iq is a commentary (sharh) on the matn of Kanz al-Daqa'iq, addressing rulings related to menstruation during Hajj and the need for intention in dam al-tamattu'. The commentary opens with a discussion on the ruling that if a menstruating woman cannot perform the farewell tawaf (tawaf al-sadr), she abandons it and it is as if she stayed in Makkah (i.e., no penalty). The author then derives that dam al-tamattu' requires intention (niyyah), drawing an analogy with tawaf al-rukn where a supererogatory intention suffices for the obligatory tawaf. He argues that if tawaf can be intended as supererogatory and yet fulfill the compulsory, then dam al-tamattu' should be even more applicable since it is a financial obligation. Next, the commentary presents the ruling from the matn: if a woman menstruates at the time of ihram, she performs all Hajj rituals except tawaf. This is proven by the hadith where the Prophet (peace be upon him) told Aisha at Sarif, 'Do what the pilgrim does except do not circumambulate the House until you become pure.' The hadith indicates that tawaf is forbidden for her due to two reasons: entering the mosque in a state of impurity and abandoning the obligatory purity (taharah) for tawaf. If she were to circumambulate, she would be sinful and must repeat the tawaf; if she does not repeat, she must sacrifice a badanah (camel) and her Hajj remains valid. The commentary then addresses the case of menstruation at the time of farewell tawaf: she abandons it with no penalty because it is an obligation that falls due to the excuse of menstruation or postpartum bleeding. Similarly, if she delays tawaf al-ziyarah until she is pure, nothing is due. The author notes that the ruling is unrestricted, but there is a difference of opinion regarding the case of staying in Makkah after the first departure. The margin note (hāshiya) elaborates on the analogy between tawaf al-rukn and dam al-tamattu', responding to an objection from al-Sharnablaliyya and concluding that tawaf al-rukn's time-specific nature allows supererogatory intention to suffice, unlike dam al-tamattu'. The final portion from al-Lubab specifies that if a woman menstruates before she can perform most of tawaf al-ziyarah, she is excused; but if she is able to perform four circuits and delays, she owes a blood sacrifice. The key lesson is that a menstruating woman is excused from tawaf, and the details depend on her ability to perform most of it.

Key Terms
الحيض (al-hayd): Menstrual bleeding, which is a legal impurity that prevents tawaf.
طواف الصدر (tawaf al-sadr): The farewell tawaf performed when leaving Makkah after completing Hajj.
البدنة (al-badanah): A camel sacrificed as a penalty for certain violations in Hajj, such as performing tawaf while menstruating without repeating.
دم (dam): A blood sacrifice (sheep or goat) required as a penalty for certain delays or omissions in Hajj.

Key Takeaway
A menstruating woman is excused from farewell tawaf; if she performs any tawaf, she must repeat or pay a camel; delaying tawaf al-ziyarah incurs a penalty only if she could have done most of it.
