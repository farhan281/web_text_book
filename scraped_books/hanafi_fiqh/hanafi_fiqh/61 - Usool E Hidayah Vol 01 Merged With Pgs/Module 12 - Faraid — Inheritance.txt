Maxim 193: Husband's Right Does Not Affect Fixed Shares
A maxim about the husband's right in inheritance

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the meaning of the maxim and apply it to distinguish between a husband's personal rights and the fixed Quranic inheritance shares.

Core Idea
A husband's personal right does not alter fixed inheritance shares.

This short maxim is a foundational principle in Hanafi inheritance law. It clearly separates the husband's personal authority from the divinely ordained fixed shares (fara'id). Understanding this prevents confusion and ensures that inheritance is distributed according to Quranic prescriptions, not personal influence.

Guided Path
1
Memorize the Arabic
Learn the exact wording: إنَّ حَقَّ الزَّوْجِ لا يَظْهَرُ في حَقِّ الفَرائِضِ
Pronunciation: inna haqq al-zawj lā yazharu fī haqq al-farāʾiḍ.
2
Understand the meaning
The phrase 'haqq al-zawj' refers to the husband's legal rights as a husband, e.g., authority or objection. 'La yazhar' means it does not manifest or appear. 'Fi haqq al-fara'id' means in the context of fixed inheritance shares.
This does not deny the husband's inheritance share; it limits his personal power.
3
Identify the scope
This maxim applies exclusively to the determination of fixed shares. The husband's personal rights (e.g., to manage household affairs) are irrelevant when calculating the shares of Quranic heirs.
Other contexts like guardianship are not affected.
4
Apply to a scenario
In an estate distribution, a husband cannot object to his wife receiving her prescribed share simply because he is the husband. His personal right does not override the fixed shares.
The shares are determined solely by nass (text).

Source pages p. 12

Commentary
This ruling statement indicates that in Hanafi inheritance law, the husband's right as a husband (e.g., to object or have a say) is not considered when determining the fixed shares of inheritance (fara'id). The fixed shares are determined solely by the divine law, and the husband's personal rights do not affect them.

Key Terms
حَقَّ الزَّوْجِ (haqq al-zawj): The legal right of the husband, e.g., to management or objection.
الْفَرَائِضِ (al-fara'id): Fixed shares of inheritance prescribed by the Quran.
