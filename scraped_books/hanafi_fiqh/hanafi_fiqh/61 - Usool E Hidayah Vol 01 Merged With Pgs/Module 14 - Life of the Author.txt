The Life and Authority of Al-Marghinani (Sahib al-Hidayah)
Biographical introduction of Al-Marghinani

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the key biographical facts about Al-Marghinani and explain why Al-Hidayah holds such authority in the Hanafi school.

Core Idea
Al-Marghinani's Al-Hidayah is the central reference of Hanafi fiqh, and his scholarly pedigree shows why it carries unmatched weight.

To trust a fiqh work, we must know its author. In this lesson, we explore the life of Burhan al-Din al-Marghinani, the author of Al-Hidayah – the most widely taught Hanafi text in the Indian subcontinent. Understanding his teachers, students, and scholarly output gives us confidence in the rulings we derive from his book.

Guided Path
1
Identify the author and his significance
Al-Marghinani is known as Sahib al-Hidayah because his book is the primary text of Hanafi fiqh in the Dars-e-Nizami curriculum. Recognize that he is called by this title more than his own name.
Sahib al-Hidayah = 'Author of Al-Hidayah'.
2
Trace his lineage and birth
He was born in 511 AH in Marginan (Farghana), descended from Abu Bakr al-Siddiq. This connects him to the earliest generation of Islam and gives his work historical continuity.
His nisba 'al-Marghinani' comes from his birthplace.
3
Note his teachers
He studied under major scholars such as al-Nasafi, al-Sadr al-Shahid, and al-Maykandi. These teachers were giants of the Hanafi school, ensuring his training was sound.
The chain of transmission (sanad) strengthens the authority of his fiqh.
4
Identify his students and progeny
Many students learned from him, including his own three sons who became prominent scholars. This shows his direct impact on the next generation of Hanafi jurists.
His grandson authored al-Fusul al-Imadiyyah, another important work.
5
Acknowledge his scholarly breadth and works
He was a hafiz, mufassir, muhaddith, usuli, and poet. His works include Bidayat al-Mubtadi (the matn of Hidayah), Sharh al-Jami' al-Kabir, and others. This breadth confirms his mastery of both usul and furu.
Bidayat al-Mubtadi is the foundational text; Al-Hidayah is its commentary (sharh).

Introduction. Hidayah is a widely used and well-known book in the Dars-e-Nizami curriculum, included as a syllabus in most religious schools of the Indian subcontinent. This book is matchless due to its authority and usefulness. Due to its comprehensiveness of transmitted and rational evidences and its unique style, this book holds a special place in Hanafi fiqh. Because of this book's prestige, the author is known in academic circles more by the name 'Sahib al-Hidayah' than his own name 'Burhan al-Din'. His noble name is Ali, father's name Abu Bakr, grandfather's name Abd al-Jalil; his kunyah is Abu al-Hasan, and he is famous by the title Sahib al-Hidayah. He was born on 8 Rajab 511 AH on Monday after Asr prayer. His birthplace and residence is Marginan, a city in the province of Farghana in the region of Transoxiana. The Syr Darya river passes through the Ferghana Valley, and the city of Marginan lies to its east. Sahib al-Hidayah is called al-Marghinani in relation to this city. He is from the descendants of Hadrat Abu Bakr al-Siddiq (may Allah be pleased with him). He died in 593 AH / 1197 CE on the night of Tuesday, 4 Dhu al-Hijjah. Among the teachers in whose service he studied, some well-known ones are listed: Imam Najm al-Din Abu Hafs Umar ibn Muhammad ibn Ahmad al-Nasafi (d. 537 AH) who wrote a treatise on creed called al-Aqa'id al-Nasafiyyah, which Allamah Taftazani (d. 792 AH) commented upon in Sharh al-Aqa'id al-Nasafiyyah, included in Dars-e-Nizami. Imam Husam al-Din Umar ibn Abd al-Aziz ibn Mazah (d. 536 AH), known by the title al-Sadr al-Shahid. Imam Abu Umar Uthman ibn Ali al-Maykandi, who was a student of Allamah al-Sarakhsi (d. 483 AH). Imam Qawwam al-Din Ahmad ibn Abd al-Rashid al-Bukhari, who is the father of the author of Khulasat al-Fatawa. Allamah Abd al-Hayy al-Lakhnawi (d. 1304 AH) writes: 'A large group studied fiqh with him, including his own children.' He had three sons, all of whom were imams of their time: Shaykh al-Islam Imad al-Din Abu Bakr, Nizam al-Din Umar al-Farghani, and Abu al-Fath Jalal al-Din Muhammad al-Farghani. Sahib al-Hidayah's grandson, Imam Abu al-Fath Zayn al-Din Abd al-Rahim ibn Abi Bakr Imad al-Din ibn Ali Burhan al-Din al-Marghinani, is the author of the famous books al-Fusul al-Imadiyyah and al-Fusul al-Asturushaniyyah. Allamah al-Marghinani was a high-ranking scholar and jurist of his time; he was a hafiz, mufassir, muhaddith, adib, master of all sciences and arts, having complete command over usul and furu', precise, a researcher, and a discerning scholar. He had great expertise in the science of ikhtilaf (scholarly differences). Allamah al-Lakhnawi begins his biography with these titles: 'He was an imam, a jurist, a hafiz, a muhaddith, a mufassir, a comprehensive scholar, a master of arts, precise, a researcher, a profound thinker, a discerning scholar, a ascetic, an excellent, virtuous, skilled, usuli, adib, and poet. The eyes have not seen his like in knowledge and literature. He had extensive authority in ikhtilaf and deep influence in the madhhab.' That is, Sahib al-Hidayah was an imam of his time, a jurist of the age, a hafiz of the era, a muhaddith of the time, a mufassir of the Qur'an, comprehensive in sciences, master of arts, a mature scholar, researcher of deep insight, a subtle thinker, a devoted worshiper and ascetic, pious, superior and virtuous, skilled in arts, usuli, adib, and poet. In knowledge and literature, his second has not been seen. He had great command over the differences of the imams and schools. In addition to teaching and oral instruction, Sahib al-Hidayah also transmitted his knowledge to others through writing. His works are: 1. Bidayat al-Mubtadi. 2. al-Tajnis wa al-Mazid. 3. Sharh al-Jami' al-Kabir li al-Imam Muhammad ibn al-Hasan. 4. Fara'id al-Uthmani. 5. Kifayat al-Muntaha. 6. Majmu' al-Nawazil. 7. Nashr al-Madhahib. 8. Mu'jam al-Shuyukh. 9. Muntaga al-Furu'.

Commentary
This passage is a biographical introduction about the author of Al-Hidayah, Burhan al-Din Ali ibn Abi Bakr al-Marghinani. It begins by highlighting the significance of Al-Hidayah in the Dars-e-Nizami curriculum and its unique authority in Hanafi fiqh. It then gives his name, lineage, birth date (8 Rajab 511 AH), birthplace (Marginan in Farghana), and his relation to Abu Bakr al-Siddiq. It lists his teachers: Najm al-Din al-Nasafi (author of al-Aqa'id al-Nasafiyyah), Husam al-Din ibn Mazah (known as al-Sadr al-Shahid), Abu Umar al-Maykandi (student of al-Sarakhsi), and Qawwam al-Din al-Bukhari (father of the author of Khulasat al-Fatawa). It also mentions his students, including his own three sons, and his grandson who authored al-Fusul al-Imadiyyah. The passage then praises his scholarly credentials: he was a hafiz, mufassir, muhaddith, adib, and master of usul and furu'. A detailed eulogy from Allamah al-Lakhnawi is quoted, describing him with numerous titles. Finally, it lists his works, most notably Bidayat al-Mubtadi (the matn upon which Hidayah is based), Sharh al-Jami' al-Kabir, and others. This biography establishes the author's authority and the weight of his fiqh rulings.

Life and Works of Burhan al-Din al-Marghinani: Authority and Contributions
Biographical sketch with list of works

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the key biographical details of al-Marghinani, list his major works, and explain the distinctive role of al-Tajnis wa al-Mazid in Hanafi fatwa literature.

Core Idea
Al-Marghinani's authority rests on his unmatched mastery of all Islamic sciences; his works serve distinct functions in Hanafi pedagogy and fatwa practice.

This passage introduces us to the towering figure of Burhan al-Din al-Marghinani, the author of the foundational Hanafi commentary al-Hidayah. Through a detailed list of epithets and works, we learn why his scholarship is held in such high esteem. Pay attention not only to the man but to how his writings—like Bidayat al-Mubtadi and al-Tajnis wa al-Mazid—fit into the broader Hanafi tradition of matn, sharh, and nawazil literature.

Guided Path
1
Identify the author and his lineage
Read the opening lines to find the name of the author and his family connections. Note that his sons and grandson were also leading Hanafi scholars, showing a legacy of learning.
The passage mentions three sons and a grandson who authored important works.
2
Analyze the epithets praising his scholarship
The passage lists many laudatory terms (imam, faqih, hafiz, muhaddith, mufassir, etc.). For each, consider which Islamic science it represents and what level of mastery is claimed. Pay special attention to his 'extensive hand in khilaf'—this signals deep expertise in comparative fiqh.
These epithets establish al-Marghinani as a mujtahid and authoritative source.
3
List his major works
The passage enumerates a dozen titles. Focus on the most prominent: Bidayat al-Mubtadi, al-Hidayah, and al-Tajnis wa al-Mazid. Understand their relationships: Bidayat al-Mubtadi is the matn (base text), al-Hidayah is its commentary, and al-Tajnis wa al-Mazid is a later collection of fatwas on new issues.
Other works include Sharh al-Jami' al-Kabir, Fara'id al-Uthmani, etc., but we emphasise the ones with enduring influence.
4
Understand the function of al-Tajnis wa al-Mazid
Read the detailed description of this book. Notice it contains 1357 issues arranged by fiqh topics, without proofs or mention of other schools. It supplements earlier nawazil works and represents the fatwa corpus of later Hanafi scholars. This is a key reference for new cases (nawazil).
The passage clarifies that omitting proofs is typical in fatwa collections and does not indicate ignorance.
5
Reflect on the significance of the author's versatility
Re-read the list of epithets and note that al-Marghinani was not only a faqih but also a hadith scholar, mufassir, usuli, adib, and poet. This versatility means his works integrate multiple disciplines, making al-Hidayah a uniquely comprehensive text.
This holistic scholarship is a hallmark of great Hanafi authorities.

The grandson of the author of Hidayah, Imam Abu al-Fath Zayn al-Din Abd al-Rahim ibn Abi Bakr Imad al-Din ibn Ali Burhan al-Din al-Marghinani, is the author of the famous books 'al-Fusul al-Imadiyya' and 'al-Fusul al-Asturushaniyya'. Allama Marghinani was a high-ranking scholar and jurist of his time. He was a hafiz, mufassir, muhaddith, adib, master of sciences and arts, with complete command over usul and furu, precise, a researcher, and a meticulous scholar. The author of Hidayah collected the issues of al-Quduri and al-Jami' al-Saghir in Bidayat al-Mubtadi. He arranged the chapters according to the style of al-Jami' al-Saghir, mentioning the issues of al-Quduri first and those of al-Jami' al-Saghir later. He himself wrote a commentary on this text under the name 'al-Hidayah Sharh Bidayat al-Mubtadi'. This text, consisting of 267 pages, was not printed before; now it has been printed by the Muhammad Ali Subh Press in Cairo. Allama Abu Bakr ibn Ali al-Amili (d. 965 AH) composed a poetic version of this text. Among the works of the author of Hidayah, al-Tajnis wa al-Mazid has been printed. This book discusses new issues of Hanafi fiqh that later scholars have deduced, about which nothing explicit is transmitted from the early scholars. It is a supplement to the books al-Nawazil, 'Uyun al-Masa'il, and Waqi'at al-Natafi, Fatawa Ibn al-Fadl, and Fatawa A'ima Samarkand of Allama Samarkandi (d. 373 AH). Otherwise, the author of Hidayah is well acquainted with schools, fiqh, comparative fiqh, rational and textual proofs, as is not hidden from those who read Hidayah. Commentary
This passage is a biographical introduction to Burhan al-Din al-Marghinani, the author of the renowned Hanafi work al-Hidayah. Allama Abdul Hayy Lucknawi, a later Hanafi biographer, provides details of the author's life, students, and works. The author's sons and grandson were also notable Hanafi scholars. Al-Marghinani is praised as a comprehensive scholar with mastery in tafsir, hadith, usul, furu, and particularly the science of khilaf (jurisprudential differences). His works include Bidayat al-Mubtadi (the matn that al-Hidayah explains), al-Tajnis wa al-Mazid (a collection of later fatwas on new issues), and many others. The passage also describes al-Tajnis wa al-Mazid as a supplement to earlier collections of nawazil, containing 1357 issues arranged by fiqh topics. Importantly, it only presents rulings without proofs or mention of other schools, as is typical in fatwa collections. This does not indicate the author's inability, as his Hidayah demonstrates deep knowledge of proofs and comparative fiqh. The passage concludes with publication details of al-Tajnis wa al-Mazid with critical edition by Dr. Muhammad Amin. The overall purpose is to establish the author's authority and the place of his works in the Hanafi tradition.

Key Terms

بدایۃ المبتدی (Bidayat al-Mubtadi): The matn (text) compiled by al-Marghinani combining issues from Al-Quduri and Al-Jami' al-Saghir.
التجنيس والمزيد (Al-Tajnis wa al-Mazid): A later Hanafi work by al-Marghinani collecting new fatwas (nawazil) deduced by latter scholars.
علم خلاف (Ilm al-Khilaf): The science of jurisprudential differences between madhahib and among Hanafi imams.

Commentary
This passage is a front-matter introduction providing biographical and bibliographical context for the study of Al-Hidayah by al-Marghinani. It explains that Al-Hidayah is a commentary on Bidayat al-Mubtadi, which itself combines the rulings from Quduri's Mukhtasar and Imam Muhammad's Jami' al-Saghir. The author arranged the chapters following Jami' al-Saghir's order, placing Quduri's issues first. The passage notes that a 267-page metrical version of the text was written by Allamah Abu Bakr bin Ali al-Amili. Among al-Marghinani's works, 'Al-Tajnis wa al-Mazid' has been published, which collects new Hanafi issues derived by later scholars not explicitly mentioned by earlier authorities. The book is a supplement to earlier collections of nawazil (occurrences) such as those by al-Samarqandi and others. The book is structured by fiqh chapters (taharah, salah, zakat, sawm, hajj) and contains 1357 issues each marked with 'Mas'alah' for easy reference. It only states rulings without proofs or references to the three eponymous jurists (Abu Hanifa, Abu Yusuf, Muhammad) or other schools, as is typical for fatawa works. However, the author is well-versed in comparative fiqh and proofs. The passage also describes the critical edition by Dr. Muhammad Amin, which includes manuscript comparison, explanation of rare terms, issue numbering, hadith extraction, and indexes. It then extols the unmatched fame and authority of Al-Hidayah, noting it was written over 13 years while the author fasted continuously, and mentions that a massive 80-volume commentary called 'Kifayat al-Muntahi' was also written by al-Marghinani. The Structure and Sources of Al-Hidayah
Structural and textual analysis of Al-Hidayah.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to describe the structural organization of Al-Hidayah, identify its source texts, and explain why it presents rulings without proofs.

Core Idea
Al-Hidayah combines Quduri and Jami' al-Saghir, arranging over 1300 issues by fiqh chapters, stating rulings without argument—a model for fatwa works.

In this lesson, we examine the internal structure of Al-Hidayah. We learn that the matn Bidayat al-Mubtadi compiles rulings from two foundational texts, arranged in the order of Jami' al-Saghir. The commentary shares the same structure. Understanding this helps us navigate the text and appreciate its role as a fatwa manual.

Commentary
This passage is a front-matter introduction providing biographical and bibliographical context for the study of Al-Hidayah by al-Marghinani. It explains that Al-Hidayah is a commentary on Bidayat al-Mubtadi, which itself combines the rulings from Quduri's Mukhtasar and Imam Muhammad's Jami' al-Saghir. The author arranged the chapters following Jami' al-Saghir's order, placing Quduri's issues first. The passage notes that a 267-page metrical version of the text was written by Allamah Abu Bakr bin Ali al-Amili. Among al-Marghinani's works, 'Al-Tajnis wa al-Mazid' has been published, which collects new Hanafi issues derived by later scholars not explicitly mentioned by earlier authorities. The book is a supplement to earlier collections of nawazil (occurrences) such as those by al-Samarqandi and others. The book is structured by fiqh chapters (taharah, salah, zakat, sawm, hajj) and contains 1357 issues each marked with 'Mas'alah' for easy reference. It only states rulings without proofs or references to the three eponymous jurists (Abu Hanifa, Abu Yusuf, Muhammad) or other schools, as is typical for fatawa works. However, the author is well-versed in comparative fiqh and proofs. The passage also describes the critical edition by Dr. Muhammad Amin, which includes manuscript comparison, explanation of rare terms, issue numbering, hadith extraction, and indexes. It then extols the unmatched fame and authority of Al-Hidayah, noting it was written over 13 years while the author fasted continuously, and mentions that a massive 80-volume commentary called 'Kifayat al-Muntahi' was also written by al-Marghinani.
