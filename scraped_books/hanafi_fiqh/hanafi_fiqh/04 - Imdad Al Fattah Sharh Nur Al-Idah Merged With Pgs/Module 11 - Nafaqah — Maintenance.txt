The Husband's Obligation to Prepare His Wife's Funeral: An Intra-Hanafi Dispute
Intra-Hanafi dispute on husband's burial duty.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the two positions of Abu Yusuf and Muhammad regarding the husband's duty to shroud his deceased wife and identify the mufta bihi position.

Core Idea
The mufta bihi holds the husband must pay for his wife's shroud even if she has wealth; the marriage tie is not fully severed.

This passage examines an internal Hanafi dispute over whether a husband must cover his wife's burial expenses. While Imam Muhammad argues the marriage ends completely at death, Abu Yusuf and later authorities hold the husband remains obliged. The fatwa follows the unconditional obligation.

Guided Path
1
Identify the two positions
State the views of Abu Yusuf and Muhammad as given in the passage. Abu Yusuf initially conditions obligation on the wife being poor, but the fatwa from later authorities adopts his unconditional view.
Note the shift: Abu Yusuf's own statement is conditional, but later Hanafi masters (Qadi Khan, al-Kamal) interpret him as unconditional.
2
Trace the tarjih mechanism
Understand why later authorities prefer the unconditional obligation. They analogize burial expenses to nafaqah, which continues even after death? The passage does not give explicit reasoning, but the fatwa is based on the principle that the husband's obligation extends beyond death.
The key is that the marriage is not fully severed for funeral obligations.
3
Note the practical consequence
If a wife dies wealthy, under the mufta bihi, the husband pays for the shroud, not the wife's estate. Under Muhammad's view, the estate pays.
This affects who bears the financial burden and the shares of other heirs.
4
Apply to a scenario
Given a scenario: a poor husband, wealthy wife. Determine who pays according to each position and the fatwa.

Commentary
This passage addresses a dispute among early Hanafi scholars regarding the husband's duty to provide the shroud (kafan) and burial for his deceased wife. The matn states that the man is responsible for preparing his wife's funeral. The sharh clarifies that according to Abu Yusuf, this obligation is conditional on the wife being poor; if she has wealth, the expense is from her estate. However, some later authorities (al-Mughni, al-Muhit, al-Zahiriyyah) follow Abu Yusuf's unconditional obligation: the husband must pay even if the wife is wealthy. This is the mufta bihi (fatwa position). Imam Muhammad holds the opposite view: the husband is not obligated at all because the marriage is fully severed at death. The dispute centers on whether the marital obligation extends to after death or ends completely.

Key Terms
التجهيز (al-tajhiz): Preparing the deceased for burial, including washing, shrouding, and burying.
معسرة (mu'sirah): A woman without independent wealth, who is poor.
