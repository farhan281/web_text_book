Understanding the Author's Methodology: The Order of Prayer Actions
Author's explanation of the upcoming section on prayer order.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to explain why the author separates classification of prayer actions from their sequence.

Core Idea
Order (tarkib) is distinct from legal classification — knowing what to do in sequence is a separate skill.

The author now transitions from listing the individual rulings of prayer to showing how they fit together in practice. This lesson clarifies that methodological shift: he has already explained the fard, wajib, sunnah, and adab individually, and now he intends to present the order of performing these actions without repeating their legal status.

Guided Path
1
Recognize the transition
The author has completed discussing fard, wajib, sunnah, and adab individually. Now he turns to the sequence (tarkib).
2
Identify omitted material
He deliberately leaves out adab that belong to wudu (e.g., filling vessels, using ceramic), as they were covered earlier.
This shows concern for not repeating content unnecessarily.
3
Understand the purpose
The upcoming section will explain the order of performing prayer actions without re-classifying each action as fard, wajib, etc.
The need here is only for 'kayfiyyat al-tarkib' (manner of composition).
4
Appreciate the method
The author's systematic approach: first teach components (fard, wajib, sunan, adab), then integrate them in sequence.
This pedagogical method helps the student master the building blocks before assembling them.

Commentary
This passage is a methodological note by the author. He explains that he has deliberately omitted some adab that are not directly part of the prayer, such as preparations for later times and using ceramic vessels, as they were covered in the chapter on wudu. Now, after having separately discussed the fard, wajib, sunnah, and adab of prayer, he intends to present the order of performing these actions together. He does not classify each action as fard or otherwise in this section; the purpose is to show the sequence. The author indicates that he has already explained the classifications elsewhere. This is a transitional passage.

Key Terms
تركيب (tarkib): Composition; the arrangement of actions in prayer in order.
