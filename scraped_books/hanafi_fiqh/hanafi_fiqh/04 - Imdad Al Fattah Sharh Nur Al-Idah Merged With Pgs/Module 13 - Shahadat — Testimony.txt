Witness Requirements for Proving Lunar Months (Other Than Ramadan, Shawwal, and Dhul Hijjah)
Specifying testimony conditions for lunar months other than Ramadan, Shawwal, and Dhul Hijjah

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the Hanafi conditions for proving any lunar month other than Ramadan, Shawwal, and Dhul Hijjah, and distinguish between cloudy and clear sky requirements.

Core Idea
For most lunar months, two just men or one man and two women suffice in cloudy weather; a crowd suffices in clear weather.

The moon sighting for Ramadan, Shawwal, and Dhul Hijjah has special rules. For all other lunar months—like Muharram, Safar, etc.—the Hanafi school prescribes a different standard of testimony. This lesson details those conditions and explains why certain witnesses are disqualified.

Guided Path
1
Identify the subject
The passage addresses 'baqiyyat al-ahillah'—the remaining lunar months other than the three special ones.
2
Note the weather condition
The ruling depends on whether the sky is cloudy ('illa) or clear.
3
List the required witnesses for cloudy weather
For cloudy weather, two just men or one free man and two free women are required. The witnesses must be 'adl (just), free, and not have been punished for qadhf.
The witnesses must be free (not slaves) and not mardud al-shahada due to hadd.
4
Identify disqualifiers
Witnesses who have received the hadd punishment for false accusation (qadhf) are permanently disqualified unless they repent.
5
Contrast with clear weather
If the sky is clear, the requirement is different: a large crowd (jam' 'azim) is sufficient, as mentioned earlier for other cases.

Commentary
This passage specifies the conditions for proving any lunar month other than Ramadan, Shawwal, and Dhul Hijjah. When the sky is cloudy, the testimony of two just men or one man and two women is required. The witnesses must be free (not slaves) and must not have been punished for qadhf (false accusation of unchastity), as such punishment disqualifies them from testimony. If the sky is clear, a large crowd (jam' 'azim) is sufficient to establish the month, as mentioned earlier for other cases.

Key Terms
بقية الأهلة (baqiyyat al-ahillah): The remaining lunar months other than Ramadan, Shawwal, and Dhul Hijjah.
حر وحريتين (ḥurr wa ḥurratayn): One free man and two free women; the witnesses must be free persons.
محذود في قذف (maḥdūd fī qadhf): One who has received the prescribed punishment for false accusation of adultery (qadhf); such a person's testimony is rejected.
