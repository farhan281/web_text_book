Fasting Two Consecutive Months for Zihar Expiation: Conditions and Exclusions
Ruling with conditions for zihar expiation fasting

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the conditions for a valid zihar expiation fast and explain why Ramadan and prohibited days cannot be included.

Core Idea
Consecutive fasting for zihar excludes Ramadan and five prohibited days because these serve distinct obligations.

This passage from Al-Hidayah presents the second tier of expiation for zihar when slave emancipation is not feasible. The Quran mandates fasting two consecutive months, but the Hanafi school clarifies that this fast must avoid periods already assigned to other obligations or prohibitions. Understanding these exclusions is essential to correctly fulfill the expiation.

And if the muzahir does not find what to free, his expiation is fasting two consecutive months, in which there is no month of Ramadan, nor the day of Fitr, nor the day of Nahr, nor the days of Tashriq. As for consecutiveness, it is because it is textually prescribed (in the Quran). And the month of Ramadan does not count for zihar because it would necessitate nullifying what Allah has obligated. And fasting on these days is prohibited, so it cannot substitute for the complete obligation.

Commentary
This passage states the second tier of zihar expiation: if the muzahir cannot free a slave (either because no slave is available or he lacks the price), he must fast two consecutive months. The consecutiveness (tataabu‘) is explicitly required by the Quran (58:4). However, the fast cannot be during Ramadan because Ramadan is an obligatory fast in its own right; if one fasts Ramadan intending zihar, it would not fulfill the zihar obligation since Ramadan's fast is a separate fard. Similarly, the five days on which fasting is prohibited (Eid al-Fitr, Eid al-Adha, and three days of Tashriq) cannot be counted towards the expiation, as the Prophet (peace be upon him) forbade fasting on those days. Therefore, the expiation fast must avoid these periods. The Hanafi ruling is that if one begins the two months, he must ensure no prohibited days interrupt the consecutiveness; if they occur, he must break and restart after them. The text also implies that if one does not have a slave, the fasting option becomes wajib. The proof for the prohibition of fasting on those days is the hadith from Ibn Abbas reported by Tabarani in the commentary.

Key Terms
صوم شهرين متتابعين (sawm shahrayn mutatabi‘ayn): Fasting two consecutive months without interruption, except for legitimate breaks.
ايام التشريق (ayyam al-tashriq): The three days following Eid al-Adha (11th, 12th, 13th of Dhul Hijjah) when fasting is prohibited.
