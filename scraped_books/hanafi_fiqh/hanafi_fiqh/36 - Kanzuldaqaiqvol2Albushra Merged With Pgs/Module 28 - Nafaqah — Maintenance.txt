Agent Uses Own Money - Istihsan vs Qiyas
Ruling on agent who spends his own money on principal's family: set-off vs. gift

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to apply the Hanafi principle of istihsan to determine when a spending agent's use of personal funds results in set-off rather than donation.

Core Idea
An agent who spends his own money on the principal's family is not a donor; set-off occurs by istihsan.

This lesson examines a classic Hanafi case where an agent entrusted with money to spend on the principal's family instead uses his own money. Qiyas (analogy) would treat him as a donor (mutabarri'), but the Hanafi school adopts istihsan (juristic preference) to recognize set-off, reflecting practical needs. Understanding this distinction clarifies the role of istihsan in contract law.

Guided Path
1
Identify the case
Principal gives agent ten dirhams to spend on his family. Agent spends ten of his own dirhams on the family while the principal's ten dirhams are still in the agent's possession.
2
State the qiyas ruling
By qiyas (analogy to other agency rules), the agent violated his instructions because dirhams are specified. He is a donor (mutabarri') and must return the principal's ten dirhams. No set-off.
This is the 'default' rule if strict analogy is applied.
3
State the istihsan ruling
By istihsan, the ten spent by the agent are set off against the ten entrusted. The agent is not a donor. Set-off (taqaas) automatically discharges the debt.
This is the mufta bihi (relied-upon) position of the Hanafi school.
4
Understand the reasoning behind istihsan
The agent for spending is effectively an agent for buying commodities needed by the family. An agent for buying is allowed to pay from his own pocket and later claim reimbursement, as it is impractical to always carry the principal's money. Istihsan removes hardship and fulfills the principal's intent.
The 'illa (effective cause) for istihsan is the customary practice of spending agents.
5
Recognize the condition
The set-off only applies if the principal's money is still in existence at the time the agent spends his own money, and the agent does not attribute the contract to his own money (i.e., he does not intend to be a donor). If the principal's money has been consumed or the agent attributes the spending to himself, he becomes a donor and must return the principal's money.
This condition ensures the agent is acting on behalf of the principal.

Commentary
This passage presents a ruling on agency for spending on family, with the issue of set-off when the agent uses his own money. The matn states: if a man gives ten dirhams to an agent to spend on his family, and the agent spends ten of his own dirhams on them, then the ten he spent are set off against the ten he received. The commentary clarifies that this applies when the principal's ten dirhams are still in existence and the agent does not attribute the spending to himself. If the agent's own money is consumed or he attributes the contract to his own money, he becomes a buyer for himself and a donor of the spending, requiring him to return the principal's money. The text then contrasts qiyas (analogy) with istihsan (juristic preference). By qiyas, the agent's spending from his own money would be a gift (tabarru'), so he must return the principal's ten. But by istihsan, the agent is considered to have acted as an agent for buying, and he is allowed to pay from his own pocket and claim reimbursement, because it is impractical for an agent to always carry the principal's money. This removes hardship and fulfills the principal's objective. The ruling is hanafi istihsan public.

Key Terms
تقاص (Taqaas): Set-off: the ten dirhams spent by the agent are considered in exchange for the ten dirhams entrusted to him, so he does not owe anything extra.
استحسان (Istihsan): Juristic preference: a departure from qiyas (analogy) in favor of a more suitable ruling that avoids hardship and achieves the objectives of the law.
متبرع (Mutabarri'): A donor or one who acts gratuitously without expectation of compensation.
