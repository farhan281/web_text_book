This lesson explores the Table of Contents for Volume 2 of Kanz al-Daqa'iq, a foundational Hanafi fiqh text. By understanding the arrangement of chapters, you gain insight into the systematic organization of Islamic law. Use this map to navigate the volume efficiently.

Identify the major books
Scan the listing and note the books (e.g., Kitab al-'Itaq, Kitab al-Ayman, Kitab al-Hudud). These are the highest-level divisions.

Under each book, look for chapters (bab) and sections (fasl). For example, Kitab al-Ayman has chapters on oaths regarding entry, eating, divorce, etc.
These sub-topics help you find specific rulings quickly.
4
Appreciate the Hanafi legal system
The order demonstrates how Hanafi fiqh is structured: first matters concerning personal status and contracts, then criminal law, then public law, then commercial and procedural law.
This organization aids in understanding the coherence of the madhhab.
