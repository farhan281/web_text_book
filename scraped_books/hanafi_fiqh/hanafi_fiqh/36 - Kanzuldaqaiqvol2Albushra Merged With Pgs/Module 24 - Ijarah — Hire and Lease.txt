Lease Void by Void Condition
Ruling on lease invalidated by void condition

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the Hanafi ruling on a lease contract that includes a condition requiring the tenant to give a loan or gift to the lessor, and identify why such a condition renders the lease fasid.

Core Idea
A lease with a condition of a loan or gift is invalid because the condition introduces an unrelated obligation.

Lease contracts (ijara) in Hanafi fiqh are binding agreements. However, when an additional condition is attached that is not part of the rental consideration—such as requiring the tenant to lend money or give a gift—the contract becomes defective (fasid). This short passage from Kanz al-Daqa'iq illustrates a classic example of a void condition (shart fasid) that invalidates the lease. Understanding this ruling helps you distinguish between valid ancillary terms and those that corrupt the contract.

Guided Path
1
Identify the act and the condition
Identify the primary contract (ijara) and the extra condition attached (loan or gift). Recognize that the condition is not part of the rental consideration.
The condition is an additional obligation beyond the rent.
2
Determine the legal effect on the contract
According to the text, the lease becomes fasid. Understand that fasid means the contract is defective but not entirely void; the tenant must still pay fair rent (ujrah al-mithl) but the void condition is unenforceable.
Fasid is distinct from batil.
3
Understand the reason
The condition is unrelated to the rental payment and imposes an extra burden. Hanafi law considers such conditions invalid because they alter the essential balance of the exchange.
The rent must be the sole consideration for the usufruct.

Commentary
This is a ruling statement: a lease contract becomes invalid (fasid) if it includes a condition that the tenant must give a loan or a gift to the lessor. Such conditions are not part of the lease consideration and are considered void. The lease itself remains valid? Actually the text implies the lease becomes fasid. In Hanafi law, the lease with such a condition is fasid because the condition introduces an additional obligation that is unrelated to the rental payment.

Key Terms
إجارة (ijara): A contract for the use of a property or service in exchange for rent.
شرط فاسد (shart fasid): A condition that is legally invalid because it contradicts the nature of the contract or imposes an unrelated obligation.
قرض (qard): A loan of money or goods that must be repaid.
هدية (hibah/hadiya): A gift, donation, or present.

Key Takeaway
A lease contract becomes fasid if it includes a condition requiring the tenant to provide a loan or gift to the lessor.
