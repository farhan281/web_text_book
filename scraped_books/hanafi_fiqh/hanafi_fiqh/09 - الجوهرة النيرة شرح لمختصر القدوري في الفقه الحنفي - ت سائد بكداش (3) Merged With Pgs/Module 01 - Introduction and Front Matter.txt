Identifying Hanafi Commentaries: Al-Jawharah al-Nayyirah
Title page of a Hanafi commentary

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to parse a Hanafi commentary title page, identifying the matn author, commentator, editor, and publisher, and explain the relationship between Mukhtasar al-Quduri and its commentary.

Core Idea
A commentary (sharh) extends a foundational matn; knowing each link is crucial for sourcing Hanafi rulings.

This first lesson does not contain a fiqh ruling. Instead, it teaches you how to read the scholarly apparatus of a Hanafi classic. The title page tells you who wrote the original text (matn), who wrote the commentary (sharh), and who edited this edition. Understanding these roles helps you evaluate the authority of the text and trace the chain of transmission.

Guided Path
1
Identify the matn (original text)
Look for the name Imam al-Quduri and the term 'Mukhtasar'. This is the concise fiqh work that the commentary explains.
Mukhtasar al-Quduri is a foundational text in the Hanafi madhhab, written in the 5th century AH.
2
Identify the sharh (commentary)
The title 'Al-Jawharah al-Nayyirah' is the commentary; find the commentator's name: Imam Abu Bakr bin Ali al-Haddad.
Al-Haddad's work explains al-Quduri's text line by line, clarifying rulings and proofs.
3
Identify the editor and publisher
The editor (muhaqqiq) is Dr. Sa'id Bakdash, and the publisher is Arowqa. This indicates a modern critical edition.
Editors verify the manuscript, add footnotes, and ensure textual accuracy.
4
Note the volume and writing dates
This is volume three. Al-Quduri's lifespan is given (362-428 AH), while al-Haddad's dates are uncertain (marked with question marks).
Understanding dates helps place the work in historical context.
5
Understand the title's meaning
‘Al-Jawharah al-Nayyirah’ means ‘The Luminous Jewel’. Titles often reflect the value of the work.
This is not legal content but helps remember the text.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the key components of Al-Jawharah al-Nayyirah and explain its role as a commentary (sharh) on Mukhtasar al-Quduri in the Hanafi tradition.

Core Idea
Al-Jawharah al-Nayyirah is a commentary on al-Quduri's Mukhtasar, the foundational text of Hanafi fiqh.

This lesson introduces Al-Jawharah al-Nayyirah, a classic Hanafi commentary. You will learn about its author, its relationship to the matn (Mukhtasar al-Quduri), and how it fits into the Hanafi madhhab's learning tradition. Understanding the structure of such commentaries is essential for navigating classical Hanafi texts.

Guided Path
1
Identify the matn (base text)
Recognize that Mukhtasar al-Quduri is the original text by Imam al-Quduri, a foundational primer in Hanafi fiqh.
The matn is the concise text meant for memorization.
2
Identify the sharh (commentary)
Notice that Al-Jawharah al-Nayyirah is the commentary by Imam Abu Bakr bin Ali al-Haddad, explaining and expanding on the matn.
The sharh provides proofs, details, and resolutions of disputes.
3
Note the publisher and context
Understand that this edition is published by Dirasat, a research company focused on Islamic banking, indicating the continued relevance of classical Hanafi texts in modern finance.
Publisher information can hint at the contemporary utility of the work.
4
Connect to the Hanafi curriculum
Realize that commentaries like this are the standard method for learning fiqh in the Hanafi school: first memorize the matn, then study a sharh.
This two-step approach ensures both foundational knowledge and depth.

Navigating the Fiqh: Structure of al-Jawhara al-Nayyira
Table of contents listing chapters and their page numbers

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to list the main chapters covered in this volume and identify the sequence of topics from Kitab al-Buyu to Kitab al-Ijarah.

Core Idea
The table of contents is your roadmap to Hanafi fiqh—mastering the order of chapters is the first step to legal proficiency.

Before diving into the intricate rulings of sales, pledges, and hires, it is essential to have a bird's-eye view of the landscape. This table of contents from al-Jawhara al-Nayyira reveals the systematic progression of topics as arranged by the Hanafi school. Understanding this sequence helps you locate specific rulings and appreciate how each chapter builds on the previous one.

Guided Path
1
Identify the overall structure
Look at the main headings: the volume begins with Kitab al-Buyu (Book of Sales) and proceeds through Kitab al-Rahn, Kitab al-Hajr, Kitab al-Iqrar, and ends with Kitab al-Ijarah. This is a logical order—first transactions, then pledges, interdiction, confessions, and hires.
Hanafi fiqh texts typically follow a sequence from more fundamental to more specific contracts.
2
Trace the sub-chapters under Kitab al-Buyu
Under Sales, note the options (stipulation, inspection, defect), then invalid sales, conditions, rescission, murabaha, usury, currency exchange, istibra, salam, and miscellaneous rulings. This shows the thorough treatment of sales.
Each sub-chapter is a distinct legal issue; together they cover all essential aspects of sale.
3
Observe the later books and their topics
After Sales, the table lists Pledge (types, sale of pledge, injury, maintenance, growth, safekeeping), then Interdiction (puberty, prisoner treatment), Confession (especially during terminal illness), and finally Hire (types, wet nurse, disputes, invalid hire).
These later books address secondary but important practical areas of fiqh.
4
Use the table as a study aid
When you encounter a ruling in one chapter, recall its position in the sequence. For example, the rules of pledge relate closely to sales because a pledge secures a sale debt.
Memorizing the chapter order helps you retrieve rulings faster and see interconnections.
