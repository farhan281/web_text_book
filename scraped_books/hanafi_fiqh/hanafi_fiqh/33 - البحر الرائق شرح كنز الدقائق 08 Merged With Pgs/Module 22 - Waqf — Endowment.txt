Partition of a Village with Waqf and Graveyard
Ruling on partition of a mixed-ownership village

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the Hanafi ruling on partitioning a village with mixed ownership involving waqf and graveyard, and distinguish between permissible general partition and impermissible specific partition.

Core Idea
A village with waqf and graveyard can be wholly partitioned by share, but not by picking a specific spot.

When a village has mixed ownership including waqf (endowment) and a graveyard (public utility), partitioning requires care. This lesson presents a fatwa that distinguishes between partitioning the entire village proportionally versus singling out a particular area. Understanding this distinction is key to respecting the rights of all stakeholders.

Guided Path
1
Identify the ownership categories
The village has three categories: waqf (1/4), graveyard (1/4), and private ownership (1/2). Each category has different legal characteristics.
2
Understand the two partition options
The fatwa considers two approaches: partitioning the entire village by allocating shares proportionally, or partitioning only a specific spot. The ruling differs for each.
3
Apply the ruling's logic
General partition is permissible because it respects each category's share. Specific partition is impermissible because it could unfairly allocate waqf or graveyard land to private owners, defeating their public purpose.

وفي النوازل سئل أبو بكر عن قرية مشاع أهلها ربعها وقف وربعها مقبرة ونصفها ملك يريدون أن يتخذوه مقبرة قال : إن قسمت بین القرية كلها على مقدار كل نصيب جازت، وإن أرادوا أن يقتسموا موضعاً منها لا يجوز.
And in al-Nawazil: Abu Bakr was asked about a village shared among its inhabitants, one quarter of which is waqf, one quarter is a graveyard, and half is owned (private). They want to make it a graveyard. He said: If the entire village is partitioned according to each share, it is permissible. But if they want to partition a specific spot from it, it is not permissible.

Commentary
This is a fatwa in the Hanafi school regarding partition of a village with mixed ownership categories: waqf (endowment), graveyard (public utility), and private ownership. The principle is that general partition of the entire village, allocating each category's share proportionally, is valid. However, picking out a particular spot and partitioning only that part is not permissible because it would be inequitable and may violate the conditions of the waqf and graveyard. The partition must consider the overall interests of all parties.

Key Terms
وقف (waqf): An endowment for charitable purposes, inalienable
مقبرة (maqbara): Graveyard, a public utility for burial
قسمة (qisma): Partition, division of jointly owned property
