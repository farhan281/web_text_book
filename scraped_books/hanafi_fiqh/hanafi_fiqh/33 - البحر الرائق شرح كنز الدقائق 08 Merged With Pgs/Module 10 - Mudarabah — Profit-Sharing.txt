Limits of Mudarib's Authority: Non-Trade Acts Invalid
Ruling that mudarib and partner cannot marry slave or perform kitabah

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the restriction on a mudarib's authority and identify which specific acts are prohibited, explaining why based on the trade limitation.

Core Idea
A mudarib's authority is confined to trade; non-trade acts like marrying a slave or kitabah are invalid.

In mudarabah contracts, the mudarib is appointed as an agent to trade with capital. This lesson clarifies that his authority does not extend to non-commercial acts, such as marrying the principal's female slave or entering into a kitabah contract with a slave. The key principle is that such acts are not considered trade (tijarah), and thus fall outside the mudarib's mandate.

Guided Path
1
Identify the agents and prohibited acts
Note that the passage explicitly names mudarib and partner (sharik) and mentions two specific acts: marrying a female slave (tazwij al-ama) and performing kitabah (contract of emancipation). The ruling states they do not 'own' these acts, meaning they have no authority to perform them.
2
State the legal reason for prohibition
The passage gives the reason: 'because they are not from trade (tijarah)'. This sets the limit: the mudarib's authority is only for acts that are categorized as trade.
3
Apply the principle to each act
Understand that marrying a slave is a personal/non-commercial contract, and kitabah is a contract of emancipation. Neither is a sale, purchase, or profit-generating exchange; therefore, they do not fall under the mudarib's delegated authority.
4
Recognize the overall scope
The mudarib's role is to invest capital for profit through trade (buying, selling, etc.). Any act outside this scope is invalid (batil) and the mudarib has no right to do it, even if it involves the capital assets.

Commentary
This is a brief ruling confirming that a mudarib (one who manages a mudarabah investment) and a partner (sharik) – specifically those with limited authority – do not have the right to marry off the minor's female slave or to enter into a kitabah contract with a slave. The reason is that their authority is restricted to trade (tijarah) and these acts are not considered trade. This is consistent with the earlier principle that only those with general authority can perform such acts.

Key Terms
مضارب (muḍārib): An agent in a mudarabah contract who manages the investment and trade.
