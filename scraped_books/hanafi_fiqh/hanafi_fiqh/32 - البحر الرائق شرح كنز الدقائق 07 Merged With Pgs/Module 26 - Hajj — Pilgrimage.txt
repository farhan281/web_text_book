Intra-Hanafi Dispute on Slaughtering a Gifted Animal for Udhiyah or Hady
Intra-Hanafi dispute on slaughtering gifted animal for udhiyah or hady

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the positions of Abu Yusuf and Muhammad al-Shaybani regarding revocation of a gift after the donee slaughters the animal for udhiyah or hady, and identify the preferred view among later Hanafi scholars.

Core Idea
Slaughtering a gifted animal for worship does not automatically block gift revocation; the preferred Hanafi view allows revocation with compensation.

In this passage from al-Bahr al-Ra'iq, we encounter an intra-Hanafi dispute on whether a donor can revoke a gift after the donee slaughters the animal for udhiyah (Eid sacrifice) or hady (pilgrimage offering). The base text establishes that slaughtering a cow for general use allows revocation. But when the slaughter is for worship, Abu Yusuf and Muhammad al-Shaybani differ. Let's examine their proofs and see which view later scholars favoured.

Guided Path
1
Identify the unanimous case
The source notes that if the gifted animal is a cow and the donee slaughters it, the donor may revoke the gift without any dispute.
2
Pinpoint the disputed scenario
The dispute arises when the donee slaughters the animal for udhiyah (Eid sacrifice) or hady al-mut'ah (pilgrimage offering). Does the worship purpose change the ruling?
3
State Abu Yusuf's position
Abu Yusuf holds that the donor cannot revoke because the slaughter for worship changes the purpose and makes revocation inappropriate.
4
State Muhammad al-Shaybani's position
Muhammad holds that the donor may revoke because the animal's essence is destroyed but compensation can be made; the worship act does not bar revocation.
5
Recognise the preferred view
Abu Hanifa's opinion is not transmitted, but later scholars (mashayikh) considered Muhammad's view to be sahih (correct), so the mufta bihi position allows revocation with compensation.

His saying: 'and slaughtering it for udhiyah etc.' In al-Khaniyah: if there is a cow and he slaughters it, he may revoke the gift of it, and this is without disagreement. Similarly, if he sacrifices it for udhiyah or slaughters it for hady al-mut'ah, he may not revoke it according to Abu Yusuf. Muhammad said: he may revoke and it suffices for the udhiyah and mut'ah. Abu Hanifa's opinion is not transmitted, and the mashayikh differed; some said it is like Muhammad's view and that is sahih; as in al-Tatarkhaniyah.

Commentary
This passage presents an intra-Hanafi dispute regarding whether slaughtering a gifted animal for udhiyah (sacrifice) or hady (pilgrimage offering) prevents the donor from revoking the gift. The source text (al-Khaniyah) states that slaughtering a cow is unanimously agreed to allow revocation, because the meat can be returned. However, for udhiyah or hady, Abu Yusuf holds that slaughtering prevents revocation because the slaughter is a form of worship that changes the ownership intent, while Muhammad holds that the donor can still revoke because the animal's essence is destroyed but compensation can be made. Abu Hanifa's view is not recorded, but later scholars (mashayikh) preferred Muhammad's view as the sound one.

Key Terms
أضحية (udhiyah): Sacrificial animal during Eid al-Adha
هدي المتعة (hady al-mut'ah): An animal sacrificed as part of tamattu' pilgrimage
