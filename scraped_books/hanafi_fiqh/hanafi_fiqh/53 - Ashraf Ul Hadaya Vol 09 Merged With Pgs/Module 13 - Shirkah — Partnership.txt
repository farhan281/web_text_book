Creditors' Rights After Dissolution of Mufawada Partnership
Explains creditors' right after dissolution using ta'lil of suretyship

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the right of creditors after dissolution of a mufawada partnership and explain the limitation on recourse between partners.

Core Idea
Dissolution ends agency but not suretyship; creditors may claim full debt from either partner.

When a mufawada partnership ends, the mutual agency between partners terminates, but the mutual suretyship for existing debts continues. This means that creditors can still demand the full amount from whichever former partner they choose. However, if one partner pays the entire debt, his right to recover from the other is limited to the excess over half.

Guided Path
1
Identify the legal scenario
The passage discusses a dissolved mufawada partnership where debts exist. Understand that mufawada involves mutual agency and suretyship.
2
State the creditors' right
Creditors may claim the entire debt from either partner. This is because each partner is a surety for the other, and dissolution does not cancel that suretyship.
3
Derive the reason (ta'lil)
Each partner is liable for half as a principal and half as a surety, resulting in full liability. Therefore, the creditor can recover from either.
4
Apply the limitation on recourse
If one partner pays more than half, he can recover only the excess from the other. Paying exactly half gives no recourse. This mirrors the rule for two independent sureties.

Introduction to Partnership of Mufawada
When the partners of mufawada separate (dissolve the partnership), the creditors have the right to take the entire debt from whichever of them they wish. Because each of them is a surety for his partner, as is known in the book of partnership. And neither of them can recourse against his partner until he pays more than half, based on the two proofs that have passed in the case of two sureties.
Commentary: Mufawada is a trade in which two men are partners, and both are equal in terms of freedom, intellect, and religion. The partnership of mufawada includes both agency and suretyship; that is, each partner is both an agent and a surety for the other. Mufawada is derived from tafwid, which means entrusting, and since each partner entrusts disposal to his partner, it is named mufawada.
The scenario is: if the partners of mufawada dissolve the partnership and there is a debt owed to people, the creditors have the right to recover their entire debt from whichever of the two they wish. Because each of them, by virtue of the mufawada contract, is a surety for his partner. Thus each is obligated for half the debt by right of being principal and half by right of suretyship; in any case, each is liable for the full debt. When each is liable for the full debt, it can be recovered from either. However, if the creditors demand from one of them and take the debt, he can only recourse against his partner if he pays more than half. If he pays exactly half, he cannot recourse at all; if he pays more than half, he can recover only the excess. Two proofs for this have already passed in the first issue of two sureties; please consult them.

Commentary
This passage addresses the rights of creditors when a mufawada partnership is dissolved. Mufawada is a specific form of partnership in Hanafi fiqh where partners are equal in capacity, and each is both agent and surety for the other. When the partnership ends, the mutual agency and suretyship do not automatically terminate for existing debts. Therefore, creditors may claim the entire debt from either former partner. However, if one partner pays the full debt, his right of recourse against the other partner is limited: he can only recover if he paid more than half, and only the excess. This mirrors the rule for two independent sureties (as in the earlier lesson on kafalah). The passage clarifies that the suretyship persists despite dissolution, protecting creditors' rights. Authority weight is from Zahir al-Riwayah (quoted from the matn).

Key Terms
مفاوضہ (mufawada): A partnership where partners are equal in freedom, intellect, and religion, and each is an agent and surety for the other.
متفاوضین (mutafawidin): The two partners in a mufawada partnership.
