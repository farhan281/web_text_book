Navigating Volume 8: Using the Fihrist
Table of contents listing chapter headings and page numbers.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to locate specific topics in Volume 8 of Ashraf Ul Hadaya using the fihrist and identify the structure of the chapters on khiyar and fasid sales.

Core Idea
A fihrist is your map: know its order to find any ruling quickly.

This lesson introduces the table of contents (fihrist) for Volume Eight of Ashraf Ul Hadaya, the Urdu commentary on Al-Hidayah. While the fihrist itself contains no legal rulings, it is an essential navigation tool for the detailed discussions on the Option of Sight (Khiyar al-Ru’yah), Option of Defect (Khiyar al-‘Ayb), and Invalid Sales (Bay‘ Fasid). Mastering how to read a fihrist is a foundational skill for every student of Hanafi fiqh, enabling efficient study and reference.

Guided Path
1
Identify the major sections
Look at the headings in the fihrist. The first major section is 'Khiyar al-Ru’yah' (Option of Sight), followed by 'Bab Khiyar al-‘Ayb' (Chapter on Option of Defect), and then 'Bab al-Bay‘ al-Fasid' (Chapter on Invalid Sales). Notice the page numbers for each.
Page numbers in the fihrist refer to the original pagination of the Urdu commentary.
2
Recognize the subsections
Within each major chapter, the fihrist lists sub-topics. For example, under Khiyar al-‘Ayb, it lists definitions of defects and specific cases like a slave's running away, insanity, etc. Note how these sub-topics are organized.
The fihrist uses bullet-style listing; each line represents a distinct ruling or discussion.
3
Understand the flow between chapters
Observe the sequence: Option of Sight comes first, then Option of Defect, then Invalid Sales. This reflects the logical progression from immediate rights of rescission to detailed defect-based claims and finally to voidable contracts.
Al-Hidayah's structure often moves from general to specific or from common to exceptional.
4
Use the fihrist to find a specific ruling
When studying a particular issue (e.g., returning a defective slave), look for the relevant keyword in the fihrist (e.g., 'عیب' or 'غلام') and note the page number. Then turn to that page in the volume for the full ruling.
The fihrist is your index; it does not replace the text but guides you to it.

Commentary
This is the fihrist (table of contents) for Volume Eight of Ashraf Ul Hadaya, the Urdu commentary on Al-Hidayah. It lists the main chapters and sub-topics covered in the volume, primarily from the Book of Sales (Kitab al-Buyu'). The first section deals with 'Khiyar al-Ru’yah' (option of sight) and its rulings, such as when the buyer has not seen the merchandise. Then follows 'Bab Khiyar al-‘Ayb' (Chapter on Option of Defect), including definitions of what constitutes a defect in slaves and slave-girls, and rules regarding recourse when a defect is discovered after purchase. The latter part introduces 'Bab al-Bay‘ al-Fasid' (Chapter on Invalid Sales), discussing the distinction between void (batil) and voidable (fasid) sales, and specific cases like selling wine, pigs, umm walad, mudabbar, and mukatab. The page numbers listed correspond to the folios of the original text. This fihrist helps the reader navigate the volume's detailed discussions on sale transactions within Hanafi fiqh.

Key Terms
خیار العیب (Khiyar al-‘Ayb): Option of defect – the right of the buyer to return a purchased item due to a hidden defect existing at the time of sale.
بیع فاسد (Bay‘ Fasid): Voidable sale – a sale that is defective in some condition (e.g., in consideration or object) but can be rectified or has some legal effect.
خیار رویت (Khiyar al-Ru’yah): Option of sight – the buyer's right to rescind a contract after seeing the item if it was purchased without prior inspection.

Navigating Volume 8: The Book of Sales (Kitab al-Buyu) in Ashraf Ul Hadaya
Table of contents listing chapter headings and page numbers.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the main topics and structure of Volume 8 of Ashraf Ul Hadaya using its table of contents and locate specific Hanafi rulings on sales and defects.

Core Idea
A table of contents is your roadmap to mastering Hanafi fiqh — know the structure to find any ruling quickly.

This volume of Ashraf Ul Hadaya is dedicated to one of the most critical chapters of Hanafi fiqh: Kitab al-Buyu (Book of Sales). The table of contents (فہرست) lists dozens of sub-topics, from the option of defect (khiyar al-'ayb) to invalid sales (bay' fasid). Before diving into the detailed commentary, take a moment to understand how the volume is organized. This will save you time and help you see the interconnections between rulings.

Guided Path
1
Identify the main chapter
Look at the overall title and first few entries. The volume covers Kitab al-Buyu (Sales). Note that it starts with specific cases of defects and oaths before moving to invalid sales.
The volume is not just a random collection; it follows a logical sequence from valid sales to their defects to invalid types.
2
Scan for sub-chapters
Notice the headings like 'باب البيع الفاسد' (Chapter on Invalid Sales). These divide the volume into major sections. Identify at least three such sections from the entries.
Sub-chapters help you group related rulings.
3
Locate specific rulings of interest
If you want to find the ruling on a runaway slave, search for 'بھاگے ہوئے غلام کی بیع' in the list. The page number next to it tells you where to turn.
Use the Arabic terms from the index to cross-reference.
4
Note the page number format
Page numbers appear in both digits and sometimes words. Some might be mixed with text. Recognise the pattern (e.g., '۱۳۷' is 137).
Page numbers help you jump directly to the commentary.

Key Terms
بیع (bay'): Sale or exchange of property; the central topic of this volume.
عیب ('ayb): Defect in a purchased item that gives the buyer an option to return it.
باب البيع الفاسد (bab al-bay' al-fasid): Chapter on invalid sales; a section of the book detailing sales that are defective due to missing conditions.
