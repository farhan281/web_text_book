Buying One's Wife with an Option: Effect on Marriage
Intra-Hanafi dispute on marriage validity when buying wife with khiyar

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the Hanafi ruling on the validity of marriage when a man buys his wife (a slave) with a three-day option, and explain the difference between Abu Hanifa and the Sahibayn.

Core Idea
Whether ownership transfers during the option period determines if the marriage continues—Abu Hanifa says no transfer, so marriage stands; the Sahibayn say yes, so marriage ends.

This lesson examines a nuanced intra-Hanafi dispute: what happens to a marriage when a husband purchases his wife, who is a slave-girl, with a three-day option (khiyar al-shart)? Imam Abu Hanifa and Imam Muhammad in al-Jami' al-Saghir maintain that the marriage remains valid because ownership does not fully transfer during the option period. The two companions (Abu Yusuf and a different transmission from Imam Muhammad) argue that ownership transfers immediately, thereby invalidating the marriage. We will dissect the reasoning behind each position and its practical implications.

Guided Path
1
Identify the core act and its legal context
Recognize that the issue is a man buying his wife (a slave owned by another) with a stipulated option of three days. The marriage already exists, and the purchase creates a potential conflict: if the husband becomes the owner of his wife, the marriage is invalidated due to milk al-yamin.
The option (khiyar al-shart) suspends the finality of the sale.
2
Determine the Hanafi position according to the matn
According to Imam Muhammad's matn in al-Jami' al-Saghir, the marriage is not invalidated because the buyer does not become the owner during the option period. The option prevents full transfer of ownership.
This is also the view of Abu Hanifa as stated in the commentary.
3
Contrast with the Sahibayn's position and their dalil
The two companions (Abu Yusuf and another view from Imam Muhammad) hold that ownership transfers immediately upon sale, even with an option, so the husband becomes the owner of his wife, invalidating the marriage. Their evidence: here, the buyer owns the slave-girl, and intercourse is by virtue of ownership, not marriage.
The practical consequence is that under the Sahibayn, the marriage ends at the moment of purchase.
4
Analyze the exception for a virgin wife
Abu Hanifa adds that if the wife is a virgin and the husband has intercourse, the option is voided because the defect prevents return. This makes the sale binding, and then ownership transfers, ending the marriage. For a non-virgin, intercourse does not void the option.
This shows that the thamarah al-khilaf depends on the wife's virginity status.

Explanation: All these previous issues are based on one principle: that in the case of khiyar for the buyer, the sold item leaves the seller's ownership but, according to Imam Abu Hanifa, does not enter the buyer's ownership, while according to the two companions, it enters the buyer's ownership. One of these issues is that a man bought his wife (who is a slave of another) from her master with a three-day option. According to Imam Abu Hanifa, the marriage is not invalidated because due to the stipulated option, the buyer did not become her owner. Since he did not become her owner, the marriage is not invalidated, because marriage is invalidated by right hand possession. And right hand possession was not obtained by the buyer due to the option. Therefore, the marriage is not invalidated. And if the buyer had intercourse with her during the option period, his option is not voided by that intercourse; rather, the buyer still has the right to rescind the sale under his option, because the intercourse he performed was by virtue of her being a married woman (wife), not by virtue of her being a slave-girl. So this intercourse is not considered as a consent to the sale, and the buyer's option is not nullified. However, if she was still a virgin and the buyer, who is also the husband, had intercourse with her, then this intercourse voids the buyer's option and the sale becomes binding. When the sale becomes binding, the marriage becomes invalid, because after right hand possession, the marriage cannot remain. The proof is that intercourse with a virgin makes her defective, and a defective sold item cannot be returned. So when returning this slave-girl with the defect of intercourse becomes impossible for the buyer, his option is voided and the sale becomes binding.

Commentary
This passage presents an intra-Hanafi dispute between Imam Abu Hanifa and the two companions (Abu Yusuf and Imam Muhammad) regarding the effect of buying one's wife (who is a slave of another) with a three-day option. The core issue is whether ownership transfers to the buyer during the option period. Abu Hanifa holds that ownership does not transfer because the option prevents complete transfer; hence the marriage remains valid, and if the husband has intercourse, it is by virtue of marriage, so he can still return the slave. However, if the slave is a virgin, intercourse diminishes her value and prevents return, thus voiding the option and making the sale binding, which then invalidates the marriage. The Sahibayn hold that when a buyer has an option, ownership transfers immediately (subject to the right to rescind), so the buyer becomes the owner of his wife, which invalidates the marriage. Intercourse by virtue of ownership prevents return. The relied-upon (mufta bihi) position in the Hanafi school is the view of Imam Muhammad (as stated in al-Jami' al-Saghir) which is in line with the Sahibayn? Actually, the text attributes the quoted matn to Imam Muhammad and presents his view as 'the marriage is not invalidated', which matches Abu Hanifa. The Sahibayn differ. So the mufta bihi is the view of Abu Hanifa and Imam Muhammad? The text says 'This according to Abu Hanifa, and the two companions said...' so it's a dispute. Later practice may follow the Sahibayn. The commentary in the text seems to explain the principle and then gives the detailed ruling. The passage is an expansion probe of a matn from al-Jami' al-Saghir.

Key Terms
ملک یمین (milk al-yamin): Right hand possession, i.e., ownership of a slave, which is a legal impediment to marriage between the owner and the owned.
خیار شرط (khiyar al-shart): A stipulated option in a sale contract, giving one party the right to cancel within a specified period.
