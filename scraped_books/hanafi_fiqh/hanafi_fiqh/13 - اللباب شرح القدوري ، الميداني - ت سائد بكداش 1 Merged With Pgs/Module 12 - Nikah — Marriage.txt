Correcting a Textual Variant: The Ruling on Mut'ah in Al-Quduri
Textual-critical analysis of Al-Quduri's ruling on mut'ah

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the correct Hanafi ruling on mut'ah for divorced women and identify the textual error in some manuscripts of Al-Quduri's Mukhtasar.

Core Idea
Mut'ah is recommended for all divorcées except the mufawwidah, whose mut'ah is obligatory.

This lesson examines a textual variant in Al-Quduri's Mukhtasar regarding mut'ah for the divorced woman. The correct Hanafi position distinguishes between the general recommendation of mut'ah and the specific obligation for the mufawwidah (a woman married without dower specification and divorced before consummation). Understanding this textual correction is essential for accurate reading of classical Hanafi texts and proper fatwa.

Guided Path
1
Identify the original statement
Read Al-Quduri's statement as quoted in al-Lubab: 'Mut'ah is recommended for every divorced woman except one, and she is the one divorced before consummation and no dower was specified for her.'
This exception refers to the mufawwidah.
2
Note the variant reading
Some manuscripts have '... and he had specified a dower for her' instead of '... and he did not specify a dower for her.' This would make the exception apply to a woman with a specified dower.
This variant is a scribal error (ghalat min al-nasikh).
3
Understand why the variant is erroneous
The variant contradicts the established Hanafi ruling that mut'ah is wajib (obligatory) for the mufawwidah, not mustahabb. If the exception were for a woman with a dower, that ruling would be incorrect since her mut'ah is mustahabb.
Refer to the principle that mut'ah for the mufawwidah is compensation for half the mahr al-mithl.
4
Confirm the correct text using authorities
Multiple scholars (Najm al-A'imma, Rukn al-A'imma al-Sabbaghi, Abu al-Raja') affirmed that the correct reading is 'and he did not specify a dower for her,' as found in precise copies and consistent with usul and tafsir works.
Al-Hidayah's commentator later grappled with the erroneous reading, but the consensus favors the correction.
5
State the correct ruling
Mut'ah is mustahabb (recommended) for every divorced woman except the mufawwidah (divorced before consummation without dower), for whom it is wajib (obligatory).
This is the mufta bihi position and the basis for fatwa.

Commentary
This passage is a textual-critical note from the commentary al-Lubab on al-Quduri's Mukhtasar. It addresses a variant reading in the text concerning the ruling on mut'ah (a gift) for a divorced woman. Al-Quduri's original statement in the chapter on Nikah reads: 'Mut'ah is recommended (tustahabb) for every divorced woman except one, and she is the one divorced before consummation and no dower was specified for her.' This exception refers to the mufawwidah (a woman married without dower specification). Her mut'ah is wajib (obligatory) because it compensates for half of the mahr al-mithl (the proper dower). However, in some manuscripts, the text reads 'and he had specified a dower for her' (qad sammā lahā mahrān), which would make the exception apply to a woman with a specified dower—contradicting the well-known ruling. Al-Maydani reports that Najm al-A'imma and other scholars confirm that the correct reading is 'and he did not specify a dower for her' (lam yusammi lahā mahrān). The variant is a scribal error. The passage then notes that al-Hidayah even records the erroneous version without comment, causing later commentators difficulty. The correct view is that all divorced women receive recommended mut'ah except the mufawwidah, whose mut'ah is obligatory.

Key Terms
متعة (mut'ah): A gift given to a divorced woman as consolation or benefit, separate from mahr.
مفوضة (mufawwidah): A woman married without specification of mahr in the marriage contract.
استحباب (istihbab): Recommendation; an act whose performance is rewarded but omission is not sinful.
