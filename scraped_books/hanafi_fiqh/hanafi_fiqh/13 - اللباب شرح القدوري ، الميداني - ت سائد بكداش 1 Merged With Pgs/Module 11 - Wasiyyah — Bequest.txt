Emancipation in Death Illness: Valid Act or Bequest?
Textual criticism on a variant in al-Quduri's Mukhtasar

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the ruling on emancipation during death illness, distinguish the textual variant in al-Quduri's Mukhtasar, and explain why both readings lead to the same practical consequence.

Core Idea
A manuscript variant changes the wording but not the ruling: emancipation in death illness is valid and counted from the third.

This passage from al-Hidayah discusses a textual variant in al-Quduri's Mukhtasar. The standard text says such acts are 'jā'iz' (valid) and limited to one-third of the estate. Some manuscripts replace 'jā'iz' with 'wasiyya' (bequest). At first glance, this might seem to alter the legal characterisation, but the practical effect remains identical. Understanding this variant reveals the Hanafi tradition's concern for precise transmission of legal matns and the stability of rulings even across textual differences.

Guided Path
1
Identify the act and the standard ruling
Determine what act is being discussed (emancipation, sale at undervalue, gift) and the ruling according to the standard text: 'fadhalika kulluha jā'iz' (all that is valid). Note that the ruling includes a limitation: it is counted from one-third of the estate.
The act occurs during marad al-mawt (fatal illness).
2
Recognize the textual variant
Some manuscripts of al-Quduri have 'fa huwa wasiyya' (so it is a bequest) instead of 'jā'iz'. Identify the source of this variant report: al-Hidayah, al-Binayah, al-Itqani, and al-Mujtaba.
This is a manuscript variation, not a different legal opinion.
3
Compare the two wordings and their implications
Examine whether the change from 'jā'iz' to 'wasiyya' alters the ruling. Since both share the same consequence — counts from the third and competes with bequests — the practical outcome is identical. The variant may simply emphasize that the act takes effect after death, but it does not change the one-third limitation.
The passage explicitly states that both versions lead to the same treatment.
4
Determine the mufta bihi position
According to al-Mujtaba, the first reading ('jā'iz') is more correct (al-awwal asahh). Thus the relied-upon position is that the act is valid and considered from the third, without changing its nature to a formal bequest.
This reflects the Hanafi method of preferring the more widespread and early manuscript reading.

Commentary
This passage is a textual criticism note regarding a variant reading in al-Qudūrī's Mukhtasar. It presents the standard ruling first: if a person in his death illness (marad al-mawt) emancipates a slave, sells at an unfair price, or gives a gift, all such actions are valid (jā'iz) but are subject to the limitation of one-third of the estate, and they compete with other bequests for distribution. Some manuscripts replace 'jā'iz' with 'wasiyya' (bequest), changing the wording but not the practical effect. Al-‘Aynī, al-Itqānī, and al-Kākī report these variants, with al-Mujtabā favoring the original reading 'jā'iz' as more correct. The passage illustrates the Hanafi tradition of manuscript verification and the concern for accurate transmission of the matn.

Key Terms
العتق في المرض (al-'itq fi al-marad): Emancipation of a slave by a person in a fatal illness, subject to the one-third rule.
جائز (jā'iz): Legally valid and effective, though subject to limitations.
وصية (wasiyya): A bequest taking effect after death, limited to one-third.

Key Takeaway
The standard Hanafi position on emancipation during death illness is that it is valid (jā'iz) and limited to one-third of the estate, and the manuscript variant 'wasiyya' does not alter this ruling.
