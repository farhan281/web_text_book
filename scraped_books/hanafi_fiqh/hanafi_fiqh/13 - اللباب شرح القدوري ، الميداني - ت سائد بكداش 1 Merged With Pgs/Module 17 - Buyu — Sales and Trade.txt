Intra-Hanafi Dispute: Imam's Permission for Ihya al-Mawat
Explaining the dispute on whether Imam's permission is necessary to own revived dead land.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the two Hanafi positions on the requirement of Imam's permission for ownership of revived dead land and identify the mufta bihi position.

Core Idea
Abu Hanifa requires Imam's permission; Sahibayn do not: the mufta bihi follows the general hadith.

Reviving dead land (ihya al-mawat) is a recognized means of acquiring ownership in Islamic law. However, the Hanafi school is divided on whether the reviver must obtain the ruler's permission to become the owner. This lesson explores the arguments of both sides and explains the operative position for fatwa.

Guided Path
1
State the Legal Issue
The issue is whether reviving dead land without the Imam's permission confers ownership. Identify the two opposing views within the Hanafi school.
2
Examine Abu Hanifa's Position
Study Abu Hanifa's view that permission is required. Locate his evidence: the hadith 'A person has nothing except what his Imam consents to' and the analogy with public treasury.
His position prioritizes the ruler's authority over individual initiative.
3
Examine the Sahibayn's Position
Study the view of Abu Yusuf and Muhammad that permission is not required. Locate their evidence: the hadith 'Whoever revives a dead land, it is his.'
They give precedence to the general command of the Prophet.
4
Identify the Mufta Bihi Position
Determine which view is relied upon for fatwa. Note that later Hanafi authorities prefer the Sahibayn's view due to its ease and alignment with practice.
The mufta bihi is that the reviver owns the land without the Imam's permission.
5
Analyze the Thamarah al-Khilaf
Consider the practical consequence: if someone revives land without permission, according to Abu Hanifa the land does not become owned, while according to Sahibayn it does. This affects the possibility of the Imam reclaiming the land.

Commentary
This passage from al-Lubab Sharh al-Quduri, quoting al-Jawhara al-Nayyira, presents an intra-Hanafi dispute on the requirement of the Imam's permission for ownership of revived dead land (ihya al-mawat). Abu Hanifa holds that without the Imam's permission, the reviver does not own the land, based on a hadith that restricts ownership to what the Imam consents to, and because it is a collective right of Muslims. Abu Yusuf and Muhammad (the Sahibayn) hold that the reviver owns it regardless, based on the general hadith 'Whoever revives a dead land, it is his.' However, even according to Abu Hanifa, if the Imam subsequently grants the land to the reviver, it becomes his property. It is recommended for the Imam to grant it and not take it back, especially if the reviver acted out of ignorance. The relied-upon position (mufta bihi) in the Hanafi school is that of the Sahibayn due to the general nature of the hadith and the prevalence of practice, though the ruling of the passage is presented as a dispute.

Key Terms
إحياء الموات (ihya al-mawat): Bringing dead land to life by cultivation, building, or other beneficial use to make it productive.
إذن الإمام (idhn al-imam): Permission from the ruler (Imam) to revive dead land; required by Abu Hanifa for ownership.

A) He does not own the land.

Key Takeaway
The mufta bihi Hanafi position does not require the Imam's permission for ownership of revived dead land, in contrast to Abu Hanifa's original view.
