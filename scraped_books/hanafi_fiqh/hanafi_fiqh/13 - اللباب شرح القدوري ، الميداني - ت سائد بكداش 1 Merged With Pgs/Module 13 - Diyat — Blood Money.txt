Diyat — Blood Money
intermediate
6 min
Textual Dispute on Diyat Payment Rate
intra-Hanafi textual dispute on diyat per-year cap

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the two narrations within the Hanafi school regarding the maximum annual diyat payment per person and evaluate the tarjih (preferred position) based on scholarly authority.

Core Idea
The maximum diyat paid per person per year is 4 dirhams according to al-Quduri's well-known narration, not 1 or 1.33 dirhams.

This lesson examines a textual dispute within the Hanafi school regarding the division of blood money (diyat) over three years. The point of contention is the maximum amount that can be taken from a single payer each year. By studying the different narrations from al-Quduri and Imam Muhammad al-Shaybani, and the harmonization by later commentators, you will understand how textual variants are resolved in the Hanafi tradition.

Guided Path
1
Identify the legal issue
What is the exact point of dispute? It is the maximum amount that can be taken from each member of the 'aqila (tribe) per year when diyat is spread over three years.
The base text from al-Quduri states 4 dirhams, but another narration from Imam Muhammad gives a lower figure.
2
State the two narrations
Clearly articulate each position. Al-Quduri's narration: no more than 4 dirhams per person per year. Muhammad al-Shaybani's narration: total over three years is 3-4 dirhams, so 1 or 1.33 dirhams per year.
Both are attributed to early Hanafi authorities.
3
Analyze the harmonization attempt
Al-Aqta', in his commentary on al-Quduri, provides a variant reading that specifies 'dirham and two daniqs' (about 4 dirhams), thereby reconciling the two positions.
Al-Atrazi endorses this harmonization, stating it removes the apparent contradiction.
4
Determine the relied-upon position
Identify which narration is considered 'mashhoor' (well-known) and mufta bihi (operative for fatwa). Al-Atrazi explicitly declares al-Quduri's version as the well-known one.
Thus, the operative ruling is 4 dirhams per year per person, not the lower figure.
5
Appreciate the thamarah al-khilaf
Understand the practical difference: under one view, each payer contributes 4 dirhams/year; under the other, only 1-1.33 dirhams/year. This affects the number of payers needed to cover the full diyat.
If diyat is 1000 dirhams, 4 dirhams/year requires 250 payers/year; 1 dirham/year requires 1000 payers/year.

Commentary
This passage presents a textual dispute within the Hanafi school regarding the division of diyat (blood money) payments. The base text from al-Quduri's Mukhtasar, as cited in al-Hidayah, states that diyat is divided over three years, and no individual is required to pay more than four dirhams per year. However, Imam Muhammad al-Shaybani is reported to have said that the total payment over three years should not exceed three or four dirhams per person, meaning each person pays either one dirham or one and one-third dirham per year. Al-Atrazi clarifies that al-Quduri's version is the well-known one, and al-Aqta' in his Sharh provides a narration that reconciles the two by stating that the maximum is four dirhams per year, with a possible additional detail of two daniqs. This shows the care later scholars took to preserve the correct text of al-Quduri.

Key Terms
دية (diya): Blood money paid by the killer's tribe (aqila) to the victim's heirs as compensation for unintentional homicide or injury.
مختصر القدوري (Mukhtasar al-Quduri): The concise legal manual by Abu al-Hasan Ahmad al-Quduri (d. 428 AH), a foundational text of the Hanafi school.
