Manuscript Variant on Confession of Debt or Trust: Interpreting 'قبلي' in Al-Quduri
Discussion of manuscript variation on the legal interpretation of 'قبلي' in confessions

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to explain the Hanafi position on the interpretation of the word 'qibli' in confessions and evaluate the intra-madhhab dispute regarding the manuscript variant in Al-Quduri.

Core Idea
The word 'قبلي' primarily indicates guarantee (daman), thus a confession using it is admission of debt, not trust.

The passage from Al-Hidayah addresses a textual variation in Al-Quduri's Mukhtasar. While the standard text treats 'قبلي' as indicating a debt (dayn), some manuscripts report that it indicates a trust (amanah). This lesson unpacks the linguistic and legal reasoning behind the preferred Hanafi view.

Guided Path
1
Identify the two possible rulings
Determine the two interpretations of the word 'قبلي' in a confession: debt (dayn) or trust (amanah).
Both are discussed in the passage.
2
Understand the primary meaning
Recognize that 'قبلي' linguistically implies guarantee (daman), which aligns with debt.
The passage states 'قبلي ينبئ عن الضمان'.
3
Note the metaphorical possibility and condition
The word can bear the metaphorical meaning of trust, but only if the confessor connects his explanation (wasl) at the time of confession.
يُصدق موصولاً لا مفصولاً.
4
Analyze the manuscript variant
Some copies of Al-Quduri interpret 'قبلي' directly as trust, using the argument that the phrase 'لا حق لي قبل فلان' releases both debt and trust, and trust is the lesser obligation.
This is the opposing view within the Hanafi school.
5
Determine the mufta bihi position
Identify which view is preferred (the first is 'asahh') and understand the tarjih reasoning: the primary meaning of 'قبلي' is guarantee.
The author of Al-Hidayah declares the first opinion more correct.

It came in Al-Hidayah in the book of confession: He said: If he says 'upon me' or 'before me', then he has confessed to a debt; because 'upon me' is a formula of obligation, and 'before me' indicates guarantee. And if the confessor says 'it is a trust' and connects [the statement], he is believed, because the word can bear it metaphorically, since it refers to that which is guaranteed for preservation, and the property is in his possession; so he is believed when connected, not when disconnected. He (may Allah have mercy on him) said: And in some copies of the Mukhtasar, regarding his saying 'before me': it is an admission of trust, because the word encompasses both, so much so that when he says 'I have no right before so-and-so', it is a release from both debt and trust, and trust is the lesser of the two, and the first is more correct. End from Al-Hidayah. And see for the effect of manuscript variation here the words of Al-‘Ayni in Al-Binayah.

Commentary
This passage discusses a second example of manuscript variation in Al-Quduri, this time on the interpretation of the word 'قبلي' (before me) in confessions. The Hidayah initially states that if someone says 'علي' or 'قبلي', it constitutes a confession of debt, because 'علي' is a direct obligation phrase and 'قبلي' implies guarantee (daman). However, if the confessor explains that it was a trust (wadi'ah) and connects his statement, he is believed due to the metaphorical possibility of the phrase. Then the Hidayah notes a variant in some copies of the Mukhtasar: in those copies, the word 'قبلي' is itself interpreted as an admission of trust (amanah), not debt, because the phrase 'لا حق لي قبل فلان' operates as a release from both debt and trust, and trust is the lesser. But the first interpretation (that it is admission of debt) is deemed more correct (al-awwal asahh). The passage thus highlights how a textual variation in Al-Quduri's Mukhtasar could lead to a different legal categorization of the same utterance. The author of Al-Lubab directs readers to Al-‘Ayni's commentary for further analysis.

Key Terms
قبلي (qibli): Literally 'before me'; in legal confession, it indicates a property right (debt or trust) held by the speaker.
إقرار (iqrar): Confession; acknowledging a right of another upon oneself.
