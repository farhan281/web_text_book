The commentator al-Ayni argues that the variant reading is a scribal mistake, not from al-Quduri, and cites a miraculous incident from al-Quduri's Hajj as evidence of his accuracy. Understanding this ruling and the textual integrity of classical works strengthens your trust in the transmitted fiqh.

Sacrifice for Tawaf al-Ziyarah with Impurity
Presenting the Hanafi ruling on sacrifice for tawaf al-ziyarah without wudu or after janabah

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the Hanafi ruling on tawaf al-ziyarah performed without purity, distinguish between minor and major impurity, and apply the preferred option of repetition.

Core Idea
Performing tawaf without purity requires sacrifice; but repeating it excuses the sacrifice.

This lesson examines a specific ruling from Al-Hidayah concerning a common scenario during Hajj: a pilgrim performing the obligatory tawaf al-ziyarah while not in a state of ritual purity. The Hanafi school distinguishes between minor impurity (without wudu) and major impurity (janabah), each with a different animal sacrifice as compensation. However, the preferred course is to repeat the tawaf instead, and this eliminates the need for any sacrifice. Understanding this hierarchy of obligations and their alternatives is essential for every pilgrim.

Guided Path
1
Identify the act and its category
Determine what act is being discussed: tawaf al-ziyarah, which is a wajib act of Hajj according to the Hanafi school.
Tawaf al-ziyarah is also known as tawaf al-ifadah.
2
State the ruling when performed with impurity
The passage gives two cases: for minor impurity (muhdithan) the penalty is a goat (shah); for major impurity (junub) the penalty is a camel (badanah). Note the difference in the animal.
A badanah is typically a camel, but can be a cow; here it is specified as camel.
3
Identify the preferred alternative
Al-Marghinani states that the better (afdal) is to repeat the tawaf as long as one is still in Makkah, and then no sacrifice is due. This shows that repetition overrides the compensation.
Some textual variants say 'upon him is to repeat' indicating obligation, but the author presents it as preferred (afdal).
4
Understand the underlying rationale
The Hanafi school holds that purity (taharah) is a condition for the validity of tawaf, but it is not a rukn. Therefore, the act is not invalidated, but compensation (dam) is required for omitting the condition. However, the original obligation can be fulfilled by repeating the tawaf in a pure state, thus lifting the need for compensation.
This is consistent with the Hanafi distinction between fard, wajib, and shart.

Tawaf al-Ziyarah Without Purity: Hadath vs Janabah in Hanafi Fiqh
Hanafi intra-school dispute on tawaf repetition

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the Hanafi ruling for Tawaf al-Ziyarah performed in hadath vs janabah, explain why repetition is wajib for janabah and sunnah for hadath, and identify the mufta bihi position among the textual variants.

Core Idea
Purity during Tawaf al-Ziyarah is wajib, not rukn: deficiency requires compensation, but repetition can substitute for sacrifice.

This lesson examines a key ruling from al-Hidayah regarding Tawaf al-Ziyarah performed without ritual purity. The Hanafi school distinguishes between minor impurity (hadath) and major impurity (janabah), each requiring different sacrifices. However, a manuscript variant raises the question: is repeating the tawaf recommended or obligatory? By analyzing the commentaries of al-'Ayni and Ibn al-Humam, we will clarify the mufta bihi position and understand the reasoning behind the Hanafi stance.

Guided Path
1
State the base ruling
Identify the ruling from al-Hidayah: if one performs Tawaf al-Ziyarah while in hadath, he owes a sheep; if while in janabah, he owes a camel. This is the default compensation.
The ruling is clear on the sacrifice, but the issue is about repetition.
2
Identify the manuscript variant
Some manuscripts of al-Quduri and al-Mabsut read 'wa 'alayhi an yu'ida' (he must repeat) instead of 'al-afdal an yu'ida' (it is better to repeat). This creates a dispute over whether repetition is obligatory or recommended.
The variant is mentioned by al-Marghinani and discussed by al-'Ayni.
3
Apply the reconciliation logic
Commentators al-'Ayni and Ibn al-Humam reconcile the two readings: the obligation ('wa 'alayhi') applies to janabah (where deficiency is grave), while the recommendation ('al-afdal') applies to hadath (where deficiency is minor).
The ta'lil (reasoning) is based on the severity of deficiency – janabah is worse than hadath.
4
Confirm the mufta bihi
The operative position: for hadath, repetition is sunnah (mustahabb) and a sheep is due if not repeated; for janabah, repetition is wajib and a camel is due if not repeated. If repetition is performed, no sacrifice is due in either case.
This is the established fatwa based on the later Hanafi authorities.
5
Understand the ta'lil for waiving sacrifice
Ibn al-Humam explains that the obligatory compensation (for missing a wajib) is not specific to tawaf; blood or charity can also compensate. Recommending tawaf (the same kind as the deficient act) is preferred because the remedy matches the deficiency. If the pilgrim has left Makkah, sending a sheep is better due to benefit to the poor.
This shows the Hanafi approach to balancing obligations and flexibility.

Commentary
This passage is a detailed Hanafi ruling and intra-school dispute regarding Tawaf al-Ziyarah (the essential tawaf of Hajj) performed without ritual purity. The core ruling from al-Hidayah states: if one performs tawaf in a state of minor impurity (hadath, e.g., without wudu), he must sacrifice a sheep (shah); if in major impurity (janabah, e.g., after sexual intercourse without ghusl), he must sacrifice a camel (badana). The better practice (al-afdal) is to repeat the tawaf while still in Makkah, and if he repeats, no sacrifice is due. The dispute arises over a variant reading in some manuscripts: instead of 'al-afdal an yu'ida' (it is better to repeat), some have 'wa 'alayhi an yu'ida' (he must repeat). Commentators reconcile these: the version with 'must' applies to janabah (where repetition is obligatory), while 'better' applies to hadath (where repetition is recommended but not obligatory). The reasoning (ta'lil) is based on the severity of deficiency: major impurity renders the tawaf more deficient than minor impurity. Ibn al-Humam adds that since the obligatory compensation (for missing a wajib of tawaf) can be either tawaf, blood sacrifice, or charity, recommending tawaf as the specific compensation makes the remedy of the same type as the deficiency. However, if the pilgrim has returned home, sending a sheep is preferable because of the benefit to the poor. Thus, the mufta bihi position is: for hadath, repetition is mustahabb and a sheep is due; for janabah, repetition is wajib and a camel is due; but if repetition is done, no sacrifice is required. This illustrates the Hanafi method of balancing textual variants and applying legal maxims.

Repeating Tawaf After Impurity: Obligation vs Recommendation
Explaining when repeating tawaf is obligatory or recommended based on the type of impurity.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to distinguish between the Hanafi rulings for repeating tawaf performed in minor impurity (hadath) versus major impurity (janabah), and explain the rationale based on the severity of the deficiency.

Core Idea
Tawaf repetition is wajib for janabah but mustahab for hadath—the deficiency's severity drives the ruling.

In the Hanafi school, purification (taharah) is a condition for the validity of tawaf. But what happens if one performs tawaf while impure? This passage from al-Hidayah and the commentaries of al-‘Aynī and Ibn al-Humām clarifies that the ruling differs based on the type of impurity: minor impurity (hadath) only makes repetition recommended, while major impurity (janabah) makes it obligatory. The reasoning lies in the degree of deficiency caused by each impurity. This lesson unpacks the textual evidence and the underlying rationale.

Guided Path
1
Identify the two cases
Recognize that the text distinguishes between tawaf performed with hadath (minor impurity) and with janabah (major impurity).
These are distinct legal scenarios.
2
State the ruling for each
For hadath, repetition is mustahab (recommended); for janabah, repetition is wajib (obligatory). No sacrifice is required if the tawaf is repeated.
The text explicitly says 'istihbaban' for hadath and 'ijaban' for janabah.
3
Understand the rationale
The deficiency (nuqsan) in janabah is gross (fahish), while in hadath it is slight (qasir). This difference determines the ruling: the greater the deficiency, the stronger the remedy.
This is based on the principle of jabar (compensation).
4
Analyze the textual variants
Some copies of al-Quduri read 'upon him to repeat' (obligation), others read 'it is better to repeat' (recommendation). Al-‘Aynī reconciles by applying the first to janabah and the second to hadath.
This shows the Hanafi method of harmonizing conflicting transmissions.
5
Apply Ibn al-Humām's deeper reasoning
Even though purification is obligatory, compensation can be achieved by blood or charity. Specifying tawaf is recommended because it matches the type of deficiency (jins al-mujbūr).
This illustrates the maqasid-oriented approach.

والأصح أنه يؤمر بالإعادة في الحدث استحباباً، وفي الجنابة إيجاباً ؛ لفحش النقصان بسبب الجنابة ، وقصوره بسبب الحدث. ثم إذا أعاده وقد طاف محدثاً : لا ذبح عليه». اهـ من الهداية. قال الإمام العيني : قوله : ( وفي بعض النسخ): أي وفي بعض نسخ القدوري، وقال الكاكي : أي نسخ المبسوط، وما ذكرناه هو الصحيح. قوله: (وعليه أن يعيده) أي الطواف وهو يدل على وجوب الإعادة، والنسخة التي فيها الأفضل أن يعيد الطواف بمكة): يــدل على الاستحباب، لا الوجوب، فهذه على ما إذا كان الطواف مـع الحدث، وتلك تُحمل على ما إذا كان مع الجنابة؛ لأن النقص في الحدث يسير، وفي الجنابة كثير». اهـ
قال الإمام الكمال ابن الهمام: «وإنما لم يؤمر تلك الرواية، مع أن الطهارة في الطواف واجبة؛ لأنه لم يتعين الطواف جابراً، فإن الدم والصدقة مما يُجبر بهما ، فالواجب أحدهما غير عين، واستحباب المعيَّن - أعني الطواف - ؛ ليكون الجابر مــن جـنس المجبور، بخلاف ما إذا رجع إلى أهله ولم يطف، فإن البعث بالشاة أفضل ؛ لأن النقصان كان يسيراً، وفي الشاة نفع للفقراء». اهـ
Chapter Two: Study on Mukhtasar al-Quduri. And the most correct (view) is that he is commanded to repeat (tawaf) in the case of minor impurity (hadath) as recommended (istihbāban), and in the case of major impurity (janābah) as obligatory (ījāban) – because of the gross deficiency due to janābah and the slight deficiency due to hadath. Then if he repeats it after having performed tawaf while in a state of hadath, there is no slaughtering (of an animal) upon him. End quote from al-Hidāyah. Imam al-‘Aynī said: His saying: (In some copies): i.e., in some copies of al-Qudūrī, and al-Kākī said: i.e., copies of al-Mabsūṭ, and what we have mentioned is the correct (reading). His saying: (And upon him is to repeat it) i.e., the tawaf, and it indicates the obligation of repetition. The copy in which is (It is better to repeat the tawaf in Mecca) indicates recommendation, not obligation. So this (copy) is for when the tawaf was with hadath, and that (other copy) is interpreted for when it was with janābah because the deficiency in hadath is slight, and in janābah it is great. End quote. Imam Kamāl Ibn al-Humām said: “And he was not commanded (to repeat) in that narration, even though purification in tawaf is obligatory, because the compensatory tawaf is not specifically required. For blood and charity can also compensate. So the obligatory is one of them, not specified. And the specification of recommending (the tawaf) – i.e., the tawaf – is so that the compensatory act be of the same type as the deficient act. Contrary to when he returns to his family and does not perform tawaf: then sending a sheep is better because the deficiency was slight, and in the sheep there is benefit for the poor.” End quote.

Commentary
This passage is a ruling with proof regarding the repetition of tawaf performed without proper purification. It begins by stating the authoritative position (al-asahh) that if one performs tawaf while in a state of minor impurity (hadath), it is recommended (istihbāban) to repeat it, but if one is in a state of major impurity (janābah), it is obligatory (ījāban) to repeat it. The reason given is that the deficiency caused by janābah is gross, while that of hadath is slight. If one repeats the tawaf after having done it with hadath, no sacrifice (dam) is required. This is quoted from al-Hidāyah. Al-‘Aynī then explains a textual variant: some copies of al-Qudūrī read “And upon him is to repeat it” (implying obligation), while others read “It is better to repeat the tawaf” (implying recommendation). Al-‘Aynī reconciles them by saying the first applies when tawaf was done in janābah, the second when done in hadath. Finally, Ibn al-Humām provides a deeper rationale: even though purification is obligatory for tawaf, the command to repeat is not absolute because the deficiency can be compensated by other means (blood or charity). The recommended tawaf is preferred because it matches the type of the deficiency. This shows the Hanafi method of balancing textual variants and rational objectives.

Key Terms
الحدث (al-hadath): Minor ritual impurity requiring wudu, such as passing wind or touching with desire.
الجنابة (al-janabah): Major ritual impurity requiring ghusl, resulting from sexual intercourse or seminal emission.
استحباباً (istihbāban): As a recommended act, not obligatory; omission is not sinful.
إيجاباً (ījāban): As an obligatory act; omission is sinful and requires compensation.
