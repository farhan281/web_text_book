Textual Criticism in Hanafi Fiqh: The Case of al-Lubab
Discussion of editorial sources and methodology

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to explain the method used by the editor of al-Lubab to produce a critical edition and identify the key sources he relied on.

Core Idea
The critical edition of a Hanafi text requires comparing multiple manuscripts and printed editions to reconstruct the most reliable version.

This passage from the critical edition of al-Lubab explains the editor's approach to textual criticism. By understanding his method, you will appreciate how Hanafi texts are carefully transmitted and verified. The editor used seven copies, including five printed editions and two manuscripts, to produce the most accurate text.

Guided Path
1
Identify the number of sources used
The editor lists five printed editions and two manuscripts, totaling seven copies. List them in order: two Istanbul editions during author's life (1275 AH, 1286 AH), one posthumous Istanbul edition (1316 AH), a Cairo edition (1331 AH), a later Cairo edition by Abd al-Hamid, plus manuscripts from Damascus and Cairo.
The exact numbers matter: 5 printed + 2 manuscripts = 7.
2
Distinguish printed editions from manuscripts
Printed editions are published copies; manuscripts are handwritten. The editor notes that the printed editions were made in Istanbul and Cairo, while the manuscripts were copied during the author's lifetime. This distinction affects reliability.
Manuscripts copied in the author's lifetime are generally closer to the original.
3
Analyze the editor's methodology
The editor states that none of the copies is perfect; they complement each other. He used the Damascus manuscript as the best but relied on all seven to correct errors. His method was 'ijtihad and scrutiny' in choosing the sound text.
This reflects the principle of tahqiq: critical comparison, not blind reliance on one copy.
4
Recognize additional reference to the base text and sources
The editor also consulted critical editions of Mukhtasar al-Quduri and the original sources quoted by al-Maydani. This cross-referencing helped correct many errors that were common to all copies.
Knowing the base text is essential for editing a commentary.
5
Understand the goal
The ultimate aim is to produce a text as close as possible to the author's original. The editor's careful work ensures that students of Hanafi fiqh have a reliable version of al-Lubab.
Textual criticism is a form of amanah (trust) in preserving scholarly heritage.

Fifth Section: The authorized copies used in the critical edition of al-Lubab. Allah the Exalted granted great acceptance to the book al-Lubab by the scholar Sheikh Abd al-Ghani al-Maydani. No sooner had he finished its composition in 1266 AH (or 1268 AH) than the admirers of the author, those acquainted with his jurisprudence and his great scholarly stature, began to copy manuscripts for themselves and circulate them among themselves. Rather, some of his admirers sought to print it in the capital city of the Ottoman Empire, Istanbul, and obtained the approval of one of the high officials of the Ottoman Sultanate. Thus al-Lubab was printed twice in Istanbul during the author's lifetime: the first edition in 1275 AH and the second in 1286 AH. It was also printed a third time in Istanbul after the author's death in 1316 AH. There was a fourth edition in Cairo, Egypt in 1331 AH. And a fifth edition in Egypt edited by Professor Muhammad Muhyi al-Din Abd al-Hamid, which spread among students of knowledge, and many publishing houses in Beirut, Egypt, Pakistan, and elsewhere reproduced it. By the grace of Allah, I obtained these five copies, praise be to Allah. As for the manuscripts of al-Lubab, I obtained images of two handwritten copies: the first from the Zahiriyya manuscripts in Damascus, copied during the author's lifetime in 1290 AH, and the second from the Egyptian National Library in Cairo, also copied during the author's lifetime in 1269 AH. I searched extensively for the author's own copy in Damascus through admirers and descendants of Sheikh al-Maydani, as well as outside Damascus, but I did not succeed. Thus my reliance in the critical edition of al-Lubab was on these seven copies. My methodology was diligence and scrutiny in choosing the sound text from among these copies, since there is no single strong pillar among them to which I could resort, which I could make a reliably sound original without others. Rather, the copies were such that they complement each other and correct each other. They vary in quality and condition, and all contain strange omissions and very many errors and distortions, but the best of them is the Damascus manuscript. My major reliance in the critical edition of al-Lubab was on the critical edition of its original, which is Mukhtasar al-Quduri. Allah honored me with very precious old handwritten copies of it, some of which were read aloud to some Hanafi jurists. I will, Allah willing, describe these copies in detail after describing the copies of al-Lubab. Also, my reference to the sources from which al-Maydani quoted directly or indirectly had a great effect in correcting many errors that were transmitted in the copies. Below is a description of the copies of al-Lubab.

Commentary
This passage is front matter from the editor of the critical edition of al-Lubab, explaining the sources he used. The text first notes the widespread acceptance of al-Lubab immediately after its composition, leading to multiple printings during the author's lifetime and later. The editor lists five printed editions: two from Istanbul during the author's life (1275 and 1286 AH), one posthumous Istanbul edition (1316 AH), a Cairo edition (1331 AH), and a fifth Cairo edition by Muhammad Muhyi al-Din Abd al-Hamid. He then mentions two manuscripts: one from Damascus (Zahiriyya) and one from Cairo (Egyptian National Library), both copied during the author's lifetime. Despite extensive searching, the author's autograph copy was not found. The editor relied on these seven copies, noting that none is flawless; they complement each other. The Damascus manuscript is considered the best. He further relied on critical editions of Mukhtasar al-Quduri (the base text) and on the sources quoted by al-Maydani to correct errors. The passage sets the methodological foundation for the critical edition, emphasizing careful comparison and correction.

Key Terms
اللباب (al-Lubab): A commentary (sharh) on Mukhtasar al-Quduri by Abd al-Ghani al-Maydani.
مختصر القدوري (Mukhtasar al-Quduri): A concise Hanafi fiqh manual by Imam al-Quduri, the base text that al-Lubab explains.
تحقيق (tahqiq): Critical edition of a classical text by comparing manuscripts and printed editions to produce a reliable version.

Manuscript Variations and Scholarly Vigilance in the Hanafi School
Discusses manuscript variations and scholarly corrections in Hanafi texts.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to explain why manuscript variations in Mukhtasar al-Quduri are significant for Hanafi fiqh and how scholars corrected them.

Core Idea
Errors in manuscripts are inevitable, but Hanafi scholars vigilantly corrected them to preserve the school's rulings.

This passage from al-Lubab discusses the textual variations found in copies of Mukhtasar al-Quduri. It highlights that even the great Imam al-Marghinani relied on faulty copies, yet later commentators rectified his mistakes. This demonstrates the meticulous care of Hanafi scholars in safeguarding the school's legal rulings.

Guided Path
1
Identify the author's purpose
The author is introducing the issue of manuscript differences and their impact on the text of Mukhtasar al-Quduri.
2
Categorize the types of variations
Distinguish between trivial variations (no impact on meaning), serious variations (affecting meaning or ruling), and intermediate ones. Also note additions/omissions of conditions or whole issues.
Refer to the author's three categories: trivial, serious, intermediate.
3
Note the scholarly response
Commentators of both al-Quduri and al-Hidayah paid great attention to these variations, correcting errors and clarifying correct rulings.
4
Analyze the al-Marghinani example
Even the leading Hanafi authority al-Marghinani could unknowingly adopt an erroneous reading from a faulty copy, but later scholars corrected him.
Example: the issue of a confessor to adultery needing to be asked about the time.
5
Understand the significance
This shows the communal scholarly effort in preserving correct rulings, and that perfection belongs to Allah alone.

الفصل الثاني : دراسة عن مختصر القدوري
وقد صرّح الأقطعُ بهذه الاختلافات في خاتمة شرحه قائلاً: ... وقد وجدتُ في أكثر نُسَخ الأصل خللاً في بعض المسائل، وهو خطأ من الناسخ، وقد أصلحت جميع ذلـك علـى مـا يقتضيه مذهب أصحابنا رحمهم الله تعالى. اهـ

وهذه الفوارق والاختلافات التي وقفت عليهـا بـيـن نـسـخ القدوري، منها ما أمره سهل، وليس له أثر ذو بال على النص، ومنها جداً، وله أثر كبير، إما في تغيير المعنى، أو تغيير الحكم الشرعي، ومنها ما بين ذلك في الأهمية، ومنها ما يتعلق بزيادة شرط في المسألة، أو في زيادة مسألة كاملة، وسقوطها من أخرى، أو تتعلق بنسبة الأقوال لأئمة المذهب، ونحو هذا.

وهذه الفوارق المهمة أيقظت انتباه علماء المذهب، مـن شـراح القدوري، وشراح الهداية، وأولَوْها رعاية كبيرة، وعناية بالغة. وقد بلغ الحدُّ في اختلاف هذه النسخ وتغييرها للحكم، أن صاحب الهداية وهو من هو في المذهب : إمامةً وعلماً واعتماداً، قد وقع منه في الهداية اعتماد لنُسَخ خاطئة من القدوري، فيها خطأ في الحكم، وجرى على ذلك، وفاته التنبه إليه، لكن الله حافظ وشرعه، فقد هيأ سبحانه من بعد صاحب الهداية من جاء مـن شـراح الهداية ونبهوا إلى ذلك، وأعادوا الأمور إلى نصابها، وبينوا الصواب، كما هو في مسألة إقرار الزاني بالزنا، وهل يشترط أن يسأل متى زنى أم لا(۱)؟

وقد يستغرب طالب العلم أن يقع من الإمام المرغيناني مثل هذا، والجواب: أن هذا لا ينافي جلالة قدره، وغزارة علمه، فالكمال الله وحده، وأبى أن يصح كتاب غير كتاب الله.

وسأذكر فيما يلي شواهد على ما تقدم، معزوةً إلى مكانها، وقائلها، ليقف عليها القارئ الكريم، ولنوقن تمام اليقين، كيف هيا الله لهذا الدين العظيم علماء أمناء مخلصين جهابذة، أفنوا أعمارهم في خدمته وبيانه وتحريره، وكيف أن الله حفظ دينه وأحكامه، وأن ما فات فلاناً فإن الله يهيئ غيره للاستدراك عليه، وبيان الصواب في الأمر.

وهكذا، فهم سبيكة ذهبية متصلة مُحكمةٌ من وراث النبوة، موصولون من الله تعالى بحبل متين، يكمل بعضهم بعضاً.

ولعل من أهم أسباب اختلاف نسخ القدوري: كثرتها، بسبب صغر حجمه، وانتشارها الكبير في مشارق الأرض ومغاربها، وفي هؤلاء النساخ المُتقِنُ وغيرُ المتقن، والضابط وغير الضابط، وبين ذلك كثيراً. ويبعد جداً احتمال أن القدوري نفسه أَلَّف حين المختصر وانتشر عنه، أنه كان يعدل فيه ويغيّر، وأن يده كانت تعمل فيـه دائماً، انتشرت عنه النسخ المختلفة، فيبعد هذا، بل كان القـدوري متنبهـاً(۱) ينظر الهداية ٩٥/٢ ، وسيأتي بعد قليل في الأمثلة التي ذكرتها.

ومنه
Chapter Two: Study of Mukhtasar al-Quduri. Al-Aqta' explicitly stated these differences in the conclusion of his commentary saying: '...I found in most copies of the original some defects in certain issues, it is a scribal error, and I corrected all of that according to what the school of our companions (may Allah have mercy on them) requires.' End quote. These variations and differences that I have encountered between the copies of al-Quduri: some are trivial and have no significant impact on the text; some are serious and have a major effect, either changing the meaning or changing the legal ruling; and some are in between in importance. Some involve an added condition in an issue, or an added complete issue and its omission from another copy, or relate to attribution of opinions to the imams of the school, and the like. These important differences awakened the attention of the scholars of the school, from the commentators of al-Quduri and the commentators of al-Hidayah, and they gave them great care and intense attention. The extent of the variation in these copies and their alteration of the ruling reached such a degree that the author of al-Hidayah—who is a leading authority in the school in imamate, knowledge, and reliance—happened to rely in al-Hidayah on erroneous copies of al-Quduri containing errors in the ruling, and he proceeded accordingly, failing to notice it. But Allah is the Guardian of His law. He, exalted, prepared after the author of al-Hidayah those who came from among the commentators of al-Hidayah and alerted to that, restored matters to their proper place, and clarified the correct view, as in the issue of a confessor to adultery: whether it is required that he be asked when he committed adultery (1)? A student may find it surprising that Imam al-Marghinani could make such an error. The answer: that does not detract from his exalted status and vast knowledge; perfection belongs to Allah alone, and He wills that no book other than the Book of Allah is free from error. I will cite below evidence for what has been mentioned, attributed to their places and authors, so that the noble reader may see them, and we may be fully certain how Allah prepared for this great religion trustworthy, sincere, and profound scholars who spent their lives serving it, explaining it, and refining it; and how Allah preserved His religion and rulings; and that what one scholar missed, Allah prepared another to correct it and to clarify what is correct in the matter. Thus, they are a solid, interconnected golden chain of inheritors of prophethood, connected to Allah Almighty by a strong rope, each completing the other. Perhaps one of the most important reasons for the differences in copies of al-Quduri is their abundance due to its small size and its wide spread across East and West. Among these copyists are precise and imprecise, careful and careless, and many between extremes. It is highly unlikely that al-Quduri himself, when he authored the Mukhtasar and it spread, constantly revised and changed it, and his hand worked on it continuously, producing different versions. This is unlikely; rather, al-Quduri was careful... (1) See al-Hidayah 2/95, and it will come shortly in the examples I have mentioned. And from it.

Commentary
This passage is a preface (front matter) by the author of al-Lubab, discussing the manuscript variations in al-Quduri's Mukhtasar. He begins by citing al-Aqta', who noted errors in the copies he used and corrected them according to the Hanafi school. The author then categorizes the types of differences: trivial, serious (affecting meaning or ruling), and intermediate. Serious differences include additions/omissions of conditions or whole issues, and misattribution of opinions. He emphasizes that these variations caught the attention of commentators, including those of al-Hidayah. Remarkably, even the great Hanafi authority al-Marghinani (author of al-Hidayah) relied on faulty copies of al-Quduri, leading to errors in his work. Later commentators from the Hidayah school corrected these mistakes, e.g., in the issue of a confessor to adultery needing to be asked about the time. The author defends al-Marghinani, noting that perfection is only for Allah, and that the scholarly chain ensures errors are rectified. He promises to provide examples later. Finally, he explains that the main cause of variation is the Mukhtasar's small size and wide distribution, leading to many copyists of varying skill. He dismisses the idea that al-Quduri himself constantly revised his work, asserting that the author was precise.

Key Terms
نسخ (nusakh): Copies/manuscripts of Mukhtasar al-Quduri that have textual variations.
القدوري (al-Quduri): The author of Mukhtasar al-Quduri, a foundational Hanafi text.
الهداية (al-Hidayah): A famous Hanafi fiqh work by al-Marghinani, which relies on al-Quduri's Mukhtasar.
