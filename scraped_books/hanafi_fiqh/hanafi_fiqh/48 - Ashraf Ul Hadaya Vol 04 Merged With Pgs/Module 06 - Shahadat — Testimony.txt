Witness Requirements for Proving Fosterage
Ruling with inter-madhhab dispute on witness requirements for fosterage

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the Hanafi position on witnesses for fosterage and explain why the standard of marriage dissolution applies.

Core Idea
Fosterage dissolves marriage, so its proof requires the same testimony as divorce.

This passage presents an inter-madhhab dispute: what testimony is needed to prove fosterage (rida')? The Hanafi school requires two men or one man and two women, rejecting women alone. Imam Malik accepts one just woman; Imam Shafi'i requires four women. The Hanafi reasoning links fosterage to the dissolution of marriage (zawal al-milk), which demands a higher evidentiary standard. Let's unpack the arguments.

Guided Path
1
State the Hanafi position
Identify the exact witness requirement: two men or one man and two women. Note that women alone are insufficient.
The text begins with: 'و لا يقبل في الرضاع شهادة النساء منفردات'
2
Identify the opposing views
List the Maliki position (one just woman) and Shafi'i position (four women).
Malik and Shafi'i differ on both the number and gender of witnesses.
3
Follow the Hanafi dalil
The Hanafi proof is qiyas (analogy) with marriage dissolution: fosterage causes prohibition (hurmah) which nullifies marital rights, thus requires the same testimony as divorce.
Key phrase: 'ثبوت الحرمة لا يقبل الفصل عن زوال الملك'
4
Examine the opposing proofs
Malik: analogy to religious matters like meat prohibition. Shafi'i: men cannot witness breastfeeding, so four women substitute for two men.
Note the difference in illa (effective cause).
5
Analyse the Hanafi responses
Hanafi refutes both: fosterage is not purely religious but tied to marital property; men can know through mahram relationships.
The meat analogy is rejected because consumption prohibition is separable from ownership.

Commentary
This passage presents an inter-madhhab dispute regarding the witness requirements to prove fosterage (rida'). The Hanafi position is that fosterage is proven by the testimony of two men or one man and two women; women alone are not sufficient. Imam Malik holds that one just woman suffices, analogizing to other religious matters where a single report is accepted. Imam Shafi'i requires four women, arguing that since men cannot see the breastfeeding, women's testimony must double to replace a man. The Hanafi response distinguishes fosterage from other religious matters: fosterage creates a prohibition that dissolves marriage (zawal al-milk), and the dissolution of marriage requires the higher standard of testimony (two men or one man and two women). Unlike the example of meat, where the prohibition of consumption is a purely religious matter separable from ownership, fosterage is inseparably linked to marriage rights. The Hanafi proof relies on the principle that any matter that leads to the nullification of marital ownership must be proven with the same evidence as the nullification itself.

Key Terms
رضاع (rida'): Fosterage; the suckling of a child that creates a mahram relationship and prohibits marriage between foster relatives.
شهادة (shahadah): Legal testimony in court; the formal act of bearing witness to establish a fact.
حرمت (hurmah): Prohibition; the legal status of being forbidden, here specifically the prohibition of marriage due to fosterage.
