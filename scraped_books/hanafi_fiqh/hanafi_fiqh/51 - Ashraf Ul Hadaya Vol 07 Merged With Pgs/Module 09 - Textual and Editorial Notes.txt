The Attitude of a Believer Towards Martyrdom: The Story of Hajjaj and Sa'id ibn Jubayr
Biographical narrative illustrating martyrdom attitude

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to explain the Hanafi position on seeking martyrdom and apply it to a scenario of persecution.

Core Idea
A martyr is honored by Allah, but one should not rush to death; preserve life until it is taken unjustly.

This lesson explores a narrative from Al-Hidayah that illustrates the attitude of a believer towards martyrdom. The story of the tyrant Hajjaj and the scholar Sa'id ibn Jubayr highlights that while martyrdom is a great reward, the believer does not voluntarily seek death but preserves life. This aligns with the Hanafi position that one should not desire death even in hardship.

Guided Path
1
Identify the key figures and their roles
Recognize that Hajjaj is a notorious oppressor and Sa'id ibn Jubayr is a righteous scholar. Understand the context of persecution.
This is a historical illustration, not a legal source.
2
Analyse Sa'id's statement about life and death
Sa'id says those already killed had no aversion to life, but he still has a 'remnant of life'. This shows he values life and does not actively seek martyrdom.
Contrast with the eagerness for martyrdom sometimes found in other traditions.
3
Connect to Hanafi fiqh principles
The Hanafi school holds that preserving life is a duty (hifz al-nafs). Actively seeking death is discouraged, as life is a trust from Allah.
This is derived from Quran 2:195: 'Do not cast yourselves into destruction.'
4
Derive the ruling on facing oppression
If persecution is inevitable, accepting martyrdom is a great reward. But if there is a way to avoid it without compromising faith, one should take it.
Sa'id did not resist being killed but also did not provoke Hajjaj.

Ḥajjāj ibn Yūsuf al-Thaqafī, who is the famous tyrant who killed more than one hundred thousand innocent people. When he wanted to kill Saʿīd ibn Jubayr as well, he said: 'Beware of killing me, because this act will not be good for you.' He said: 'Be quiet. I have killed those better than you.' Commentary
This passage is a biographical narrative about the tyrant Ḥajjāj ibn Yūsuf and the martyrdom of the great scholar Saʿīd ibn Jubayr. It serves as an illustration of the preceding discussion on the Companions' eagerness for martyrdom. Saʿīd's words reflect that he still had a desire to live, unlike those who had already fulfilled their vow. The story ends abruptly but drives home the point that even the tyrant was haunted by his crime.

Key Terms
شہید (shahīd): A martyr killed in the cause of Allah, especially in jihad or persecution.

Key Takeaway
