Tadakhul of Jizyah: Abu Hanifa vs. the Sahibayn
Intra-Hanafi dispute on whether two years' jizyah overlap

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to explain the intra-Hanafi dispute on tadakhul of jizyah and identify the mufta bihi position.

Core Idea
When multiple years of jizyah go unpaid, Abu Hanifa merges them into one; the Sahibayn require each year separately.

This passage introduces an intra-Hanafi dispute on whether unpaid jizyah for multiple years overlaps (tadakhul) or is cumulative. The matn states Abu Hanifa's view that they merge, while the Sahibayn disagree. Understanding this difference hinges on the nature of jizyah: is it a punishment or a substitute?

Guided Path
1
State the Hanafi position of Abu Hanifa
Abu Hanifa holds that if two years' jizyah accumulate, they overlap and only one jizyah is due. This is the statement of the matn and is supported by a reference in al-Jami' al-Saghir.
The key term is tadakhul (تداخل), meaning overlap/merger.
2
Identify the opposing view
Abu Yusuf and Muhammad (the Sahibayn) disagree: they hold that each year's jizyah is due separately. Imam al-Shafi'i agrees with them.
This is an intra-Hanafi ikhtilaf where two of Abu Hanifa's leading students differ.
3
Examine the opposing proof (talil)
The Sahibayn view jizyah as a substitute (iwad) that becomes due each year, like rent or a contractual obligation. Therefore, non-payment does not cancel the debt; all years must be collected.
Their reasoning treats jizyah as a financial liability, similar to other annual dues.
4
Analyze the Hanafi response
Abu Hanifa views jizyah as a punishment (uqubah) for persistence in kufr. Punishments overlap when multiple causes exist—just as multiple hudud sentences merge. Therefore, only one jizyah is due regardless of the number of years.
The humiliating method of collecting jizyah (taking with degradation) supports its punitive nature.
5
Determine the thamarah al-khilaf
The practical consequence: if a dhimmi fails to pay for two years, under Abu Hanifa's view only one jizyah is taken; under the Sahibayn's view, two jizyah are taken.
In the Hanafi school, the mufta bihi (operative position) is Abu Hanifa's view.

If two years accumulate upon him, the two jizyahs overlap. And in al-Jami' al-Saghir: Whoever from whom jizyah was not taken until the year passed and another year came, it is not taken from him. Commentary
This passage deals with tadakhul (overlapping/merger) of jizyah when a dhimmi fails to pay for multiple years. The matn states that if two years' jizyah accumulate, they overlap (tadakhala) meaning only one jizyah is due. This is the position of Abu Hanifa, as reported in al-Jami' al-Saghir. Abu Yusuf and Muhammad (the Sahibayn) hold that the previous year's jizyah must also be collected. Imam al-Shafi'i agrees with the Sahibayn. The commentary explains that Abu Hanifa views jizyah as a punishment (uqubah) for persistence in kufr, and punishments overlap (like hudud), so only one is due. The Sahibayn consider jizyah as a substitute (iwad) that becomes due each year and must be collected if possible.

Key Terms
تداخل (tadakhul): Overlap or merger of multiple obligations into one, specifically here that multiple years of unpaid jizyah merge into a single jizyah.
