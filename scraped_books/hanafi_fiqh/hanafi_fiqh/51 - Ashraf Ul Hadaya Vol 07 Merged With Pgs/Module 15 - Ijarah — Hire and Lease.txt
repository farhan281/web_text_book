Adjustment of Kharaj by the Imam: Reduction and Increase
Ruling on kharaj adjustment and intra-Hanafi dispute

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the Hanafi ruling on reducing kharaj when yield is low and distinguish the positions of Imam Muhammad and Imam Abu Yusuf on increasing kharaj when yield rises.

Core Idea
Reduction of kharaj is agreed upon; increase is disputed between Muhammad (permitted) and Abu Yusuf (disallowed) based on Umar's precedent.

Kharaj is a land tax imposed by the Imam based on the land's productive capacity. This passage from al-Hidayah addresses two situations: when the land cannot bear the imposed tax (yield is low) and when yield increases significantly. While reduction is unanimously permitted, a noteworthy intra-Hanafi dispute arises regarding increase—Imam Muhammad allows it by analogy, while Imam Abu Yusuf forbids it based on the silent precedent of Umar (may Allah be pleased with him).

Guided Path
1
Identify the two scenarios
The passage discusses two distinct cases: reduction of kharaj when yield is low, and increase of kharaj when yield is high. Recognize that the first is agreed upon, the second is disputed.
2
Understand the consensus on reduction
Note that reduction is permissible by ijma (consensus). The proof is the action of Umar: when he asked Hudhayfah and Ibn Hanif about the burden, they confirmed the land could bear the tax, and Umar accepted that. This indicates that if the land could not bear it, reduction would be required.
The principle is justice—the Imam must ensure the tax does not exceed the land's capacity.
3
Examine the intra-Hanafi dispute on increase
Imam Muhammad permits increase by qiyas (analogy) with reduction: if reduction is allowed when yield is low, increase should be allowed when yield is high. Imam Abu Yusuf disallows it because Umar did not increase the tax even when informed that the land could bear more.
This is an ikhtilaf between two major disciples of Abu Hanifa.
4
Analyze the reasoning: analogy vs. precedent
Muhammad uses qiyas: the illah (effective cause) for adjustment is the change in capacity. Abu Yusuf relies on the precedent (athar) of Umar, which restricts the Imam's discretion in increasing.
The dispute reflects a methodological difference in prioritizing qiyas over the action of a Companion.
5
Determine the practical consequence
The thamarah al-khilaf: under Muhammad's view, the Imam may raise the tax if yield increases dramatically; under Abu Yusuf, the Imam must maintain the original assessment. Later Hanafi fatwa works may prefer one view, but this text does not resolve it.
Students should be aware that both positions exist within the school.

The Finder's Authority to Hire Out the Foundling: An Intra-Hanafi Dispute
Intra-Hanafi dispute on hiring out the foundling.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the two positions within the Hanafi school regarding the finder hiring out the foundling and explain the qiyas (analogical reasoning) used to support the preferred view.

Core Idea
The finder cannot hire out the foundling because he lacks authority over benefits, like a paternal uncle.

In this passage from Al-Hidayah, the author Al-Marghinani presents an intra-Hanafi dispute: can the person who takes in an abandoned child (multaqit) hire out that child for wages? Two narrations exist – one allowing it, the other prohibiting it. The author clarifies which view is relied upon and why. This lesson unpacks the legal reasoning behind the dispute, highlighting the role of analogical reasoning in Hanafi fiqh.

Guided Path
1
Identify the two narrations
Recognize that Al-Quduri in his Mukhtasar permits the hiring, while Imam Muhammad in al-Jami' al-Saghir prohibits it. The passage notes that the prohibition is the more correct view.
The author (al-Marghinani) prefers the Jami' al-Saghir position.
2
Understand the basis for the permissive view
The permissive view argues that hiring out the foundling falls under disciplining and training (tathqif), which is part of the finder's duty.
3
Analyze the prohibitive view's qiyas
The prohibitive view uses analogy: the finder cannot consume the child's benefits, so he is like a paternal uncle (al-amm), who also cannot hire out his nephew.
The mother has full authority over her child's benefits, so she can hire him out.
4
Note the exception of the mother
The mother is an exception because she has natural guardianship and ownership over the child's benefits.
This shows that the rule is about degree of guardianship.
5
Determine the mufta bihi
The relied-upon position is non-permissibility, supported by the transmission in Jami' al-Saghir (Zahir al-Riwayah).
Al-Marghinani explicitly states this is the correct view.

Commentary
This passage presents an intra-Hanafi dispute on whether the finder (multaqit) may hire out the foundling (laqit) for wages. Two positions are reported:
1) From Imam al-Quduri's Mukhtasar: It is permissible because it falls under disciplining/training (tathqif).

The mother is an exception because she has specific guardianship over her child's benefits. The issue is referred to Kitab al-Karahiyya for further detail. This shows the nuance in Hanafi fiqh: pure benefit actions are allowed, but hiring involves risk (gharar) and consumption of benefits, requiring stronger guardianship. The relied-upon position (mufta bihi) is the prohibition.

The nuance: Quduri's report may have been about permissibility, but the stronger view is impermissibility. However, the passage states the reason for Quduri's narration is that the multaqit cannot waste benefits, so he is like a guardian. This seems to support impermissibility. The author likely means that Quduri also reported impermissibility, but the Jami' al-Saghir makes it explicit. The passage is a commentary expanding on the matn, using analogy to justify the ruling.

Key Takeaway
The multaqit (finder) of a foundling cannot hire out the child for wages because he lacks the authority to waste the child's benefits, analogously to the child's guardian.
