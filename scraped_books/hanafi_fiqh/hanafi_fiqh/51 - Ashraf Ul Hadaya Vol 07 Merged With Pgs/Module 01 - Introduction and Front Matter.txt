Orientation to Kitab al-Siyar in Ashraf Ul Hadaya
Front matter and table of contents for Kitab al-Siyar

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the scope and structure of the Hanafi fiqh subject known as al-Siyar and list the major topics it covers.

Core Idea
Kitab al-Siyar is the Hanafi legal manual on jihad and international relations.

This lesson introduces Kitab al-Siyar, the chapter on jihad and state relations in the Hanafi fiqh tradition. The table of contents reveals the breadth of topics, from the definition of siyar to rules of engagement and treatment of non-Muslims. Understanding this structure will help you navigate the detailed rulings that follow in the volume.

Guided Path
1
Identify the book and its subject
Read the table of contents heading 'كتاب السير' (Kitab al-Siyar) and note that it is a distinct division of Hanafi fiqh covering jihad and related matters.
2
Scan the major topics listed
Quickly read through the entries in the table of contents, from the definition of siyar to the final topic on treachery and mutilation.
The topics are arranged sequentially, moving from general rulings to specific rules of engagement.
3
Categorize the themes
Group the topics into categories: definition and obligation of jihad, exemptions, rules of invitation to Islam and jizyah, conduct of war (siege, catapults, targeting prisoners), and prohibitions (treachery, mutilation).
This categorization helps in recalling the structure of the book.
4
Recognize the legal categories in use
Note key legal terms like 'jihad', 'jizyah', 'dar al-harb', which are central to Hanafi fiqh of international relations.
These terms will recur throughout the volume.

Commentary
This is the front matter of Volume 7 of Ashraf Ul Hadaya, a Hanafi fiqh commentary on Al-Hidayah. The first part contains publication details: the edition, publisher, addresses of distributors in Pakistan, the UK, and other locations, and a request to readers to report any errors. The second part presents the table of contents for Kitab al-Siyar (The Book of Jihad). The table lists topics ranging from the definition of siyar, the rulings of jihad (including offensive and defensive jihad), who is exempt from jihad (children, slaves, women, blind, lame, amputees), rules of engagement (invitation to Islam before fighting, demand for jizyah, siege warfare, use of catapults, targeting enemy property, and the treatment of Muslim prisoners used as shields). It also covers prohibitions such as using women and copies of the Quran in the army and rules against treachery and mutilation. This front matter sets the stage for the detailed exposition of Hanafi fiqh on jihad that follows in the volume.

Key Terms
السير (al-Siyar): The plural of 'sirah'; in Islamic law, it refers to the rules governing warfare and relations between Muslim and non-Muslim states.
جهاد (jihad): Exerting effort in the path of Allah; in this context, it refers to military struggle against non-believers.
جزيہ (jizyah): A tax paid by non-Muslim subjects (dhimmis) in exchange for protection and exemption from military service.

Navigating Volume 7: Key Chapters on Jihad, Aman, and Ghanima
Table of contents for Volume 7, listing chapter headings

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the major chapters of Volume 7 of Ashraf al-Hidayah and state the fiqh topics covered in each chapter, using the table of contents as a roadmap.

Core Idea
The table of contents is your map to Hanafi rulings on war, peace, and spoils.

Before diving into detailed legal discussions, a scholar must first understand the landscape. This table of contents for Volume 7 of Ashraf al-Hidayah outlines the sequence of chapters on jihad (warfare), aman (granting protection), and ghanima (spoils of war). By familiarizing yourself with these headings, you will be able to locate specific rulings and appreciate the systematic organization of Hanafi fiqh in this domain.

Guided Path
1
Locate the main sections
Scan the table of contents to identify the three major clusters: rulings on jihad (from 'عورت، بچے...' to 'باب الْمُوَادَعَةِ'), the chapter on aman ('فصل' section), and the chapter on spoils ('باب الغنائم وقسمتها'). Note the page numbers.
2
Catalog subtopics within each section
Under each major heading, list the subtopics mentioned. For example, under jihad, you see rules about killing non-combatants, truce, and peace deals. Under aman, subtopics include who can grant protection and conditions. Under spoils, subtopics include distribution methods and treatment of captives.
3
Identify Hanafi-specific rulings
Look for terms that signal distinctive Hanafi positions, such as 'امان' (protection by any Muslim) or 'مال فی ء' (fai property). These are key concepts in Hanafi siyar (international law).
Hanafis allow aman by a mature Muslim male or female, and treat conquered land as fai.
4
Connect to the Hidayah structure
Recall that al-Hidayah is a matn (core text), and Ashraf al-Hidayah is its Urdu sharh (commentary). The table of contents mirrors the chapters of the original Hidayah's Kitab al-Siyar. This helps you cross-reference with other commentaries.
5
Use for retrieval
When studying a specific ruling, refer back to this table to see its context within the broader chapter. For example, the ruling on selling weapons to belligerents appears right after the truce section, showing its link to peace treaties.
