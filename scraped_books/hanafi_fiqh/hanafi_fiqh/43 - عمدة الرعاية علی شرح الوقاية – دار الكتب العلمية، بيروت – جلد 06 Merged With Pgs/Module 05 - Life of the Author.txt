Abu Mansur al-Maturidi: Theologian and Hanafi Jurist
Biographical notice inserted in a fiqh commentary to identify a quoted authority.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the scholarly lineage of Abu Mansur al-Maturidi and explain why he is significant in the Hanafi tradition.

Core Idea
Abu Mansur al-Maturidi links Hanafi fiqh to systematic theology; his chain goes back to Imam Muhammad al-Shaybani.

This lesson introduces you to a towering figure in Hanafi scholarship: Abu Mansur al-Maturidi. While primarily known as a theologian, his biography in a fiqh commentary underscores his deep roots in Hanafi jurisprudence. Understanding his scholarly lineage and contributions helps you appreciate the integration of creed and law in the Hanafi school.

Guided Path
1
Identify the Scholar
Read the full name: Muhammad ibn Muhammad ibn Mahmud al-Maturidi. Note the place-name 'al-Maturidi' refers to a village near Samarqand.
The linguistic note on pronunciation (dammah on ta, kasrah on ra) is typical for non-Arabic place names.
2
Trace the Scholarly Chain
The text lists his teachers: Ahmad al-Juzjani → Abu Sulayman al-Juzjani → Imam Muhammad al-Shaybani. This chain connects al-Maturidi directly to the founding generation of the Hanafi school.
Imam Muhammad al-Shaybani was a direct student of Abu Hanifa and Abu Yusuf.
3
List His Major Works
Note the works mentioned: 'Kitab al-Tawhid' (theology), 'Ta'wilat al-Qur'an' (exegesis), 'Kitab al-Maqalat', and refutations of the Mu'tazila, Qaramita, and Shi'a.
These works show his engagement with both theology (kalam) and polemics.
4
Identify His Students
The text names Abu al-Qasim al-Samarqandi, Abu Muhammad al-Bazdawi, and Ali ibn Sa'id as his prominent students who studied fiqh under him.
Al-Bazdawi is a key figure in Hanafi usul al-fiqh.
5
Note His Death Date
The preferred date is 333 AH, with a variant of 332 AH. This places him in the late 3rd/early 4th century AH, a formative period for theological schools.
Further details can be found in 'A'lam al-Akhyar' and 'al-Tabaqat' by al-Kafawi.

Commentary
This is a biographical notice on Abu Mansur al-Maturidi (d. 333/944), the eponym of the Maturidi school of Sunni theology and a prominent Hanafi jurist. The text describes his scholarly lineage: he studied under Ahmad al-Juzjani, who studied under Abu Sulayman al-Juzjani, who studied directly under Imam Muhammad al-Shaybani, thus linking him to the founding generation of the Hanafi school. Al-Maturidi authored foundational works in theology (Kitab al-Tawhid) and Quranic exegesis (Ta'wilat al-Qur'an). He also wrote polemical works refuting the Mu'tazila, Qaramita, and Shia. Many later Hanafi scholars studied under him or under his students. The notice is part of a commentary on a fiqh text, inserted to identify the authority of a quoted opinion.

Key Terms
أبي منصور الماتريدي (Abu Mansur al-Maturidi): Muhammad ibn Muhammad al-Maturidi, a Hanafi jurist and theologian, founder of the Maturidi school of kalam.
قدوة المتفقهين (qudwat al-mutafaqqihīn): Leader of the jurists; a title indicating his preeminence in fiqh.
إمام المتكلمين (imām al-mutakallimīn): Imam of the theologians; recognizing his authority in Islamic theology (kalām).

Biography of Hasan ibn Ziyad
Biographical note on Hasan ibn Ziyad

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the key biographical facts about Hasan ibn Ziyad and explain his significance in the Hanafi tradition.

Core Idea
Hasan ibn Ziyad: top Hanafi jurist, judge, and collector of 12,000 Hadith.

This passage introduces us to one of the towering figures of the early Hanafi school: Hasan ibn Ziyad al-Lu'lu'i. He was not only a beloved student of Abu Hanifa but also a judge and a prolific Hadith scholar. Understanding his life helps us appreciate the transmission of Hanafi fiqh and the weight of his opinions.

Guided Path
1
Identify the person
Read the passage to find the full name and lineage of Hasan ibn Ziyad, including his epithets.
He is al-Lu'lu'i, al-Kufi, al-Ansari.
2
List his achievements
Note his status as a top student, his love for Sunnah, his collection of 12,000 Hadith, and the praise from contemporaries.
Yahya ibn Adam called him the most juristic person.
3
Note his judicial role and timeline
He became judge in 194 AH, resigned, and died in 204 AH. This situates him in the generation after Abu Hanifa.
He was appointed after Hafs ibn Ghiyath.

Effect of Recipient's Death Before the Sick Person on Acknowledgment
Ruling on acknowledgment validity when the acknowledgee dies first

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the Hanafi ruling on acknowledgment when the acknowledgee dies before the sick person, identify the intra-Hanafi dispute, and apply the ruling to both heir and non-heir acknowledgee scenarios.

Core Idea
An acknowledgment to an heir is valid if the heir dies before the sick person, because death severs the heir relationship.

This passage examines a specific scenario where the acknowledgee (muqar lahu) dies before the sick person (mariḍ) making the acknowledgment. The key question is whether the acknowledgment remains valid when the recipient ceases to be an heir by dying first. The Hanafi school has an internal disagreement resolved in favor of validity. We will unpack the reasoning and its practical implications.

Guided Path
1
Identify the two scenarios
Read the passage and note that it discusses two cases: (1) the acknowledgee is an heir of the sick person at the time of acknowledgment, and (2) the acknowledgee is a non-relative (ajnabi). In both, the acknowledgee dies before the sick person, and the acknowledgee's heirs are among the sick person's heirs.
The key variable is whether the recipient was an heir at the time of acknowledgment.
2
Understand the intra-Hanafi dispute
For the first scenario (heir acknowledgee), Abu Yusuf initially held the acknowledgment invalid because the recipient was an heir throughout. He later agreed with Muhammad that it is valid. Muhammad consistently held it valid.
The later view of Abu Yusuf and Muhammad's view are the relied-upon position.
3
Trace the reasoning for validity
The rationale: by dying before the sick person, the acknowledgee ceases to be an heir at the moment of the sick person's death. The prohibition on acknowledging for an heir applies only if the recipient remains an heir at the time of death.
This reasoning also applies to the second scenario: the acknowledgment was validly made for a non-relative, so it is not invalidated by the subsequent death.
4
Apply the ruling to the non-relative scenario
If the acknowledgee was a non-relative at inception, the acknowledgment is complete and valid. His subsequent death and the fact that his heirs are among the sick person's heirs do not affect validity.
The ruling is stated in al-Tabyeen and other authoritative texts.
5
Memorize the relied-upon ruling
The commentator urges memorization: 'فاحفظه فإنه ينفعك' – memorize it for benefit. The mufta bihi is the valid position.
This underscores the practical importance of this rule in estate and inheritance matters.

Commentary
This passage addresses the scenario where the acknowledgee dies before the sick person, and the acknowledgee's heirs are among the sick person's heirs. There are two cases: first, the acknowledgee was an heir of the sick person at the time of acknowledgment (e.g., a son or brother). Abu Yusuf initially said the acknowledgment is invalid because the recipient was an heir throughout. Later he agreed with Muhammad that it is valid because by dying first, the acknowledgee ceases to be an heir at the sick person's death. Second, if the acknowledgee was a non-heir (ajnabi) at the time of acknowledgment, then dies before the sick person, and his heirs are among the sick person's heirs, the acknowledgment remains valid because it was initially for a non-heir. The text emphasizes this ruling from al-Tabyeen and urges memorization.

Key Terms
المقر له (al-muqar lahu): The person in whose favor an acknowledgment is made; the recipient of the acknowledgment.
