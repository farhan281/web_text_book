Termination of Agency by Apostasy and Joining Dar al-Harb: An Intra-Hanafi Dispute
Intra-Hanafi dispute on apostasy and agency

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to explain the effect of apostasy on agency according to the Hanafi school, distinguishing between the positions of Abu Hanifa, Abu Yusuf, and Muhammad al-Shaybani, and state the thamarah al-khilaf for each scenario.

Core Idea
Apostasy alone does not terminate agency; only a judicial ruling of joining Dar al-Harb does, with disagreement on suspension vs. effectiveness before ruling.

This passage presents an intra-Hanafi dispute on whether an agency contract ends when the agent or principal apostatizes and joins enemy territory. The ruling depends on a judge's verdict, and the three founding imams differ on the status of the apostate's actions before that verdict. Understanding this ikhtilaf reveals how Hanafi principles of suspended versus effective transactions apply to agency.

Guided Path
1
Identify the core issue
Determine what triggers the termination of agency according to each imam: is it apostasy itself, death, or a judicial ruling?
All agree that a judicial ruling of joining Dar al-Harb definitively terminates agency.
2
State the position of Abu Hanifa
Describe why Abu Hanifa considers the apostate's transactions suspended, and how that applies to agency before a ruling.
Agency is suspended (mawqufah) until the apostate returns, is killed, or a ruling is issued.
3
State the position of the Sahibayn
Explain why Abu Yusuf and Muhammad hold that the apostate's transactions are effective, so agency continues until death or judgment.
Agency remains valid unless one of the parties dies or receives a judicial ruling of joining.
4
Analyze the subsidiary dispute on revival
Compare Abu Yusuf's and Muhammad's views on whether an agent's return after judgment restores the agency.
Abu Yusuf: judgment equals death, so no revival; Muhammad: temporary impediment, so revival is possible.
5
Derive the thamarah al-khilaf
Identify the practical consequences of each position for real-life agency scenarios involving an apostate party.
For example, under Abu Hanifa, the agent's actions are on hold; under the Sahibayn, they remain valid until termination.

Commentary
This passage is an intra-Hanafī dispute (ikhtilāf) within the school regarding the effect of apostasy (riddah) and joining the abode of war (Dār al-Ḥarb) on an agency contract. The author first clarifies that the ruling applies after a judge's verdict (ḥukm al-ḥākim) confirming the apostate's joining; before that, all agree the agency is not terminated. Abū Ḥanīfa holds that the agency is suspended (mawqūfah) because the apostate's acts are suspended, so agency is also suspended; if he returns to Islam it is effective, if killed or joins Dār al-Ḥarb it is invalidated. The two disciples (Abū Yūsuf and Muḥammad) hold that the apostate's transactions are effective (nāfidhah), so the agency is not invalidated simply by apostasy; it is only invalidated by death, being killed for apostasy, or a judicial ruling of joining. A special case: if the principal is a woman apostate, her agency remains until she dies or joins Dār al-Ḥarb because apostasy does not affect her contracts. Then there is a dispute between Abū Yūsuf and Muḥammad on whether an agent who returns to Islam after judgment revives the agency: Abū Yūsuf says no, because judgment of joining is like death, so agency is permanently nullified; Muḥammad says yes, because the principal's right remains and the impediment is temporary. Similarly, if the principal apostatizes and joins and returns, Abū Yūsuf says the agency does not revive, while Muḥammad distinguishes based on whether ownership of the principal ceased. The relied-upon position is not explicitly stated, but the reasoning reflects standard Hanafī principles.

Key Terms
مرتد (murtadd): An apostate who has left Islam and, in this context, joins the abode of war (Dār al-Ḥarb).
لحاق بدار الحرب (līḥāq bi-dār al-ḥarb): Joining the enemy territory; this is established only by a judge's ruling and has the effect of terminating agency.
