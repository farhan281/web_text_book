Hajj by Proxy is Not a Hire Contract
Ruling on Hajj by proxy not being Ijarah

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the Hanafi ruling on Hajj by proxy and explain the legal consequence of leftover expenses based on ijma.

Core Idea
Hajj by proxy is deputyship, not a hire contract; leftover expenses must be returned.

The passage from 'Umdat al-Ri'aya clarifies a fundamental principle: performing Hajj on behalf of another (niyabah) is not a commercial hire (ijarah). This distinction has a practical legal consequence—any unspent expenses belong to the principal and must be returned, unlike a wage which would be the deputy's own. This ruling is supported by consensus (ijma), making it a settled matter in the Hanafi school.

Guided Path
1
Identify the act and ruling
The act is Hajj performed by proxy. The ruling is that it is not a hire contract (ijarah).
2
Locate the evidence
The author cites ijma (consensus) as the proof for this ruling.
Ijma is a strong source of evidence in Hanafi fiqh.
3
Understand the consequence
Because it is not hire, any leftover expenses (nafaqa) must be returned to the principal or his heirs.
4
Contrast with hire
If it were a wage, the deputy would keep the surplus; this contrast reinforces the ruling.
This helps memorize the distinction between niyabah and ijarah.
5
Memorize the principle
Commit to memory: Hajj deputyship is niyabah, not ijarah; leftover funds are not wages.
This is a key ruling for practical fiqh.

(1) قوله : والحج ; أجمعوا على أن الحج عن الغير بطريق النيابة لا للاستئجار، ولهذا لو فضل مع ا النائب شيءٌ من النفقة يجب عليه ردّه للأصيل أو ورثته، ولو كان أجرة لما وجب ردّه.
(1) His saying: And Hajj; they have consensus that Hajj on behalf of another by way of deputyship is not for hire; therefore if something of the expenses remains with the deputy, he must return it to the principal or his heirs; and if it were a wage, it would not be obligatory to return it.

Commentary
This passage is a concise ruling on Hajj by proxy. It states that there is consensus (ijma) that performing Hajj on behalf of another through deputization is not a contract of hire (istijar). The practical consequence is that if any expenses (nafaqa) given to the deputy remain after the Hajj, they must be returned to the principal or his heirs. Were it a wage, the deputy could keep the surplus. This reinforces that the relationship is one of deputization (niyabah), not employment (ijarah).

Key Terms
نيابة (niyabah): Deputyship; acting on behalf of another in performing a deed.
استئجار (istijar): The act of hiring someone for a wage.
نفقة (nafaqa): Expenses provided to the deputy for the journey and Hajj.

Hajj by Proxy is Agency, Not Hiring
Ruling that Hajj by proxy is niyaba not ijarah

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the Hanafi ruling on Hajj by proxy and identify the proof from consensus and the consequence regarding surplus expenses.

Core Idea
Hajj by proxy is agency, not hiring; any surplus expenses must be returned.

This passage clarifies the legal nature of performing Hajj on behalf of another. The commentator uses a consensus (ijma) to establish that the relationship is one of representation (niyaba), not a hiring contract (ijarah). The practical consequence is clear: any unspent expense must go back to the principal, not kept as wages.

Guided Path
1
Identify the act and its ruling
What exactly is being ruled upon? The act is performing Hajj on behalf of another (Hajj al-ghayr). The ruling is that it is valid as representation (niyaba), but invalid if treated as a hiring contract (ijarah).
2
Locate the dalil
What proof does the author cite? He claims ijma (consensus) among scholars that Hajj by proxy is by way of niyaba, not ijarah.
The text says: 'أجمعوا على أن الحج عن الغير بطريق النيابة لا للاستئجار'
3
Trace the wajh al-dalalah
How does this dalil prove the ruling? The proof is the consequence: if a proxy has surplus expenses, he must return them to the principal. If it were a wage, he would keep it. This shows the relationship is fiduciary (amana), not hire.
4
Note the ikhtilaf
Does any school disagree? Some later Hanafis allowed hiring for Hajj under necessity, but the relied-upon position is the consensus view: niyaba is valid, ijarah is not.
5
Evaluate the tarjih
Which position is mufta bihi? The text's position is the operative one for fatwa. The proof from ijma and the practical consequence strengthen the Hanafi view.

قوله : والحج؛ أجمعوا على أن الحج عن الغير بطريق النيابة لا للاستئجار، ولهذا لو فضل مع النائب شيء من النفقة يجب عليه ردّه للأصيل أو ورثته، ولو كان أجرة لما وجب ردّه.

Source pages p. 560

Commentary
This passage is a ruling with proof. The commentator clarifies the matn statement 'and Hajj' by asserting a consensus (ijma) that performing Hajj on behalf of another is an act of representation (niyaba), not a hiring contract (ijarah). The proof is the consequence: if the proxy receives expenses for travel and has a surplus, he must return it to the principal or heirs. If the relationship were one of hiring, the surplus would be his wage and non-returnable. This reasoning distinguishes between a proxy (na'ib) who acts as an agent and a hired worker (ajir) who is entitled to full payment. The Hanafi ruling is clear: the contract for Hajj by proxy is valid as tawkee' (delegation), but invalid as ijarah.

Key Terms
النيابة (al-niyābah): Acting on behalf of another, as a proxy or agent.
الاستئجار (al-istiʾjār): Hiring someone for a wage; a contract of ijarah.

Key Terms from Source Passage

Key Takeaway
