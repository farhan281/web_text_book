Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the key manuscripts and printed editions used for a critical edition of a Hanafi fiqh text and evaluate their reliability.

Core Idea
A fiqh ruling is only as sound as the text it comes from — faulty prints corrupt legal knowledge.

Before diving into the fiqh content of al-Jawharah al-Nayyirah, it is crucial to understand its textual history. The editor describes the manuscripts and printed editions he relied on, highlighting their strengths and flaws. This lesson will train you to read such bibliographical material critically, so you can assess the authority of any fiqh text you use.

2

3
List the printed editions

All are described as defective.
4
Understand the editor's critique
The editor states that all previous prints are 'defective and ailing'.
This justifies the need for a new critical edition.
5
Connect to fiqh practice

Navigating the Bibliography of a Hanafi Critical Edition
List of reference works used in critical editing

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the types of sources listed in a Hanafi fiqh bibliography and explain their relevance to legal scholarship.

Core Idea
A critical edition's bibliography reveals the scholarly network that preserves and transmits Hanafi fiqh.

This passage is a bibliography (fihris masadir al-tahqiq) listing the reference works used in the critical edition of al-Jawhara al-Nayyira, a Hanafi fiqh commentary. It includes commentaries, hadith collections, and biographical works that are essential for verifying and understanding Hanafi rulings. Learning to read such a bibliography helps you navigate the sources of the madhhab.

Guided Path
1
Identify the Source Type
Scan each entry: is it a sharh (commentary), a hadith collection, a biographical dictionary, or a lexicon? Note the genre.
For example, entries 97-99 are Maliki commentaries; 100-101 are Tahawi's works important for Hanafi hadith.
2
Note Author and Date
Each entry gives the author's name and death date (e.g., al-Tahawi, d. 321 AH). This helps gauge the authority and period of the work.
Earlier works (e.g., Sahih al-Bukhari, d. 256 AH) are primary sources; later commentaries are secondary.
3
Assess Hanafi Relevance
Determine which works are directly connected to the Hanafi school. For example, al-Tahawi's works are foundational for Hanafi hadith criticism, while Tabaqat al-Hanafiyya is a biographical source for Hanafi scholars.
Some works are from other madhhabs (e.g., Maliki commentaries) but may be cited for contrast or broader fiqh context.
4
Understand the Role of Tahqiq
The bibliography is part of a critical edition (tahqiq). It shows that the editor verified texts against reliable manuscripts and editions. This adds scholarly credibility to the printed book.
The tahqiq process is a modern academic practice, but it relies on classical manuscripts.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify key Hanafi fiqh works and their authors from a critical edition bibliography, and explain the significance of source verification in Hanafi scholarship.

Core Idea
A critical edition's bibliography is a roadmap to the authority and reliability of transmitted rulings.

This lesson examines the bibliography from the critical edition of al-Jawhara al-Nayyira, a commentary on Mukhtasar al-Quduri. This list is not a mere appendix; it is a window into the scholarly apparatus that ensures we are studying authentic Hanafi rulings. Understanding the types of sources – from primary fiqh works to hadith collections and linguistic references – is essential for any serious student of the Hanafi school.

Guided Path
1
Identify the Structure of the Bibliography
Notice that entries are numbered sequentially, with author names, death dates (hijri), and publication details (editor, publisher, edition). This standard format allows for precise identification.
Numbers like '6' may be footnotes or page markers; ignore for now.
2
Distinguish Primary Hanafi Fiqh Sources
Look for works that are foundational to the Hanafi school, such as al-Mabsut (al-Sarakhsi), Mukhtasar al-Quduri, al-Muhit al-Burhani, and Mukhtasar al-Tahawi. These are the core texts from which rulings are derived.
Mukhtasar al-Quduri is the base matn of the commentary; al-Mabsut is a comprehensive work by a leading Hanafi scholar.
3
Recognize Supporting Sources
Hadith collections (e.g., Musnad Ahmad, Sunan Abi Dawud) and linguistic works (e.g., Lisan al-Arab) are used to verify hadith and define legal terms. Their inclusion shows the breadth of evidence needed for fiqh.
Non-Hanafi works like al-Muhalla (Ibn Hazm) may appear for comparative purposes.
4
Assess Reliability Indicators
Check the editor, publisher, and edition number. A critical edition prepared by a respected scholar (e.g., Dr. Sa'id Bakdash) and published by a reputable house (Dar al-Basha'ir) indicates higher reliability.
Multiple editions or references to manuscripts suggest thorough verification.
5
Understand the Role of Such Bibliographies
The bibliography allows users to trace the sources of citations, identify authoritative versions, and assess the scholarly rigor of the edition. It is a tool for academic accountability.
When using a critical edition, always consult the bibliography to understand the source base.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the key Hanafi fiqh sources listed in the critical edition's bibliography and explain their relevance to the commentary.

Core Idea
The bibliography maps the scholarly sources used to verify the text and rulings in al-Jawhara al-Nayyira.

This lesson examines the bibliography (fihris masadir al-tahqiq) from Dr. Sa'id Bakdash's critical edition of al-Jawhara al-Nayyira. While it may seem like a dry list, understanding which sources are cited reveals the depth of the editor's research and provides a window into the Hanafi literary tradition. You will learn to recognize important Hanafi works and appreciate their role in the critical edition.

Guided Path
1
Identify the structure of the bibliography
The bibliography lists sources in numerical order (185-201), each with author, title, editor, publisher, and edition details. Notice the consistent format and occasional cross-references (e.g., entry 187 equates Minhat al-Khaliq with al-Bahr al-Ra'iq).
Entry 197 has a typographic variation: 'الوقف المدني الخيري' is correct despite potential OCR issues.
2
Recognize key Hanafi sources
Look for works explicitly Hanafi: Minhat al-Khaliq (Ibn Abidin), al-Manzumah fi al-Khilafiyyat (al-Nasafi), Munyat al-Musalli (al-Kashghari), and al-Nahr al-Fa'iq (Ibn Nujaym). These are primary references for Hanafi fiqh rulings.
Some works, like al-Muwatta' by al-Shaybani, are Hanafi recensions of Maliki texts.
3
Understand the significance of each source type
The bibliography includes primary fiqh texts, hadith works (e.g., Nasb al-Rayah), and reference works (e.g., al-Mawsu'ah al-Fiqhiyyah al-Kuwaitiyyah). Each serves a purpose: verifying hadith, clarifying rulings, or providing modern scholarly context.
The inclusion of Shafi'i works (e.g., al-Muhadhdhab) shows cross-madhhab engagement.
