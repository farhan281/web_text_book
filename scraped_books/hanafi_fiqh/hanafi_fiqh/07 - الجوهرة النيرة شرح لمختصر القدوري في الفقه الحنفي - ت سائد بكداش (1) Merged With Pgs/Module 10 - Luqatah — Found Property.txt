Ruling on Compensation for Returning Lost Animals
Explains when finder of lost animal is entitled to reward

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the Hanafi ruling on when a finder of a lost animal is entitled to compensation and identify the key conditions that entitle or preclude the reward.

Core Idea
Compensation for returning a lost animal is due only if the finder had prior authorization from the owner or a judge.

This passage from al-Jawhara al-Nayyira addresses a practical question: when does the finder of a lost animal deserve a reward? The Hanafi school sets clear conditions that balance the incentive to return lost property with the risk of unauthorized expenses. Understanding these conditions helps avoid disputes and ensures fairness for both owner and finder.

Guided Path
1
Identify the categories of returner
List who can return: owner/relatives, strangers, tenant, husband, dependents.
The text divides returners into those related to the owner and those who are strangers.
2
Determine the ruling for each category
State whether ju'l is due or not for each category. Relatives and certain others are not entitled; strangers are entitled only under conditions.
3
Apply the condition of permission
Note that strangers only get ju'l if they spent with owner's permission or judge's order.
4
Analyze the rationale
Understand why relatives and certain others are not entitled—they are presumed to act voluntarily out of obligation.
The text says 'because they do not usually volunteer to return.'
5
Contrast with the Shafi'i position
Note that Shafi'is may grant compensation without permission; this highlights the Hanafi emphasis on explicit authorization.
This contrast is a legitimate ikhtilaf.

If the returner is a stranger, then he has the wage if he spent on the animal with the owner's permission or by a judge's ruling. If he spent without permission, then no wage for him. Commentary
This passage from al-Jawhara al-Nayyira discusses the ruling on compensation (ju'l) for returning lost animals (luqatah). The passage type is a ruling statement that specifies who is entitled to a reward. The ruling states: If the person who finds and returns the lost animal is the owner himself, or his father, or his son, then no reward is given because they are not considered 'volunteers' in the transaction; it is presumed they would return it out of familial obligation. Similarly, if the finder is a tenant or the husband of the owner, no reward. Also, if the animal is found among the finder's own dependents, no reward, as the care is already his responsibility. However, if the finder is a stranger, he is entitled to compensation only if he incurred expenses with the owner's explicit permission or by a judge's order. Without such authorization, even if he spent on the animal, he receives no wage. This follows the Hanafi principle that compensation for luqatah is not automatic but requires prior consent.

Key Terms
الجعل (al-ju'l): The compensation or wage given to the finder of a lost item for his effort and expenses in returning it.
اللقطة (al-luqatah): Lost property, specifically lost animals in this context, which have special rulings.
المولى (al-mawla): The master or owner of the animal.
