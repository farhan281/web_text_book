The Absolute Prohibition of Anal Intercourse
Ruling and proofs for the prohibition of anal intercourse

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the Hanafi ruling on anal intercourse and identify the primary Quranic and hadith proofs.

Core Idea
Anal intercourse is absolutely forbidden—the wife is a field for cultivation, not waste.

This lesson examines the clear prohibition of anal intercourse with women in any state. The Hanafi school, based on Quran and Hadith, rules it as haram, rejecting any notion that it is permissible outside menstruation.

Guided Path
1
Identify the ruling
State the explicit ruling from the passage: anal intercourse is haram during menstruation and purity.
The ruling covers all states, not conditional on menstruation.
2
Locate the Quranic proof
Review verse 2:222 which commands coming to women 'from where Allah has commanded'.
This verse originally addressed menstruation but is used to define permissible intercourse.
3
Interpret the phrase 'from where'
Understand that the interpretation restricts permissible intercourse to the vagina, excluding the anus.
The phrase is interpreted in light of the context of menstruation and purity.
4
Examine the hadith proofs
Read the two hadiths cited: one declares it ‘haram’ and the other curses the perpetrator.
These hadiths are additional evidence beyond the Quran.
5
Analyze the analogy
Understand the analogy of the wife as a tilth (field for cultivation) – the vagina is for procreation, the anus is for waste.
This logical reasoning reinforces the prohibition.

As for anal intercourse: it is forbidden during menstruation and purity. Due to the Almighty's saying: 'So come to them from where Allah has commanded you' (Al-Baqarah 2:222), meaning from where Allah has commanded you to avoid during menstruation, which is the vagina. Commentary
This passage establishes the ruling that anal intercourse (wat' fi al-dubr) is absolutely forbidden (haram), whether the woman is menstruating or in a state of purity. The proof is from multiple sources: (1) The Quranic verse (2:222) commands coming to women 'from where Allah commanded you' — interpreted as the vagina, the place permissible during purity and prohibited during menstruation. Anal intercourse falls outside this command. (2) Hadith explicitly declare it forbidden, even stating that the one who does it is cursed. (3) Another verse (2:223) allows approaching the wife 'whenever you wish' but restricts it to the 'tilth' (harth), which is the vagina, the place of procreation. The author uses an analogy: the wife is like a field for cultivation; anal intercourse is like planting seeds in a place of waste (excrement), not cultivation. Thus, it is unlawful.

Key Terms
الوطء في الدبر (al-wat' fi al-dubr): Anal intercourse with a woman.
حرث (harth): Tilth or field for cultivation; metaphorically, the vagina as the place for conception.
