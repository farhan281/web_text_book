Biography of Abu Bakr al-Haddad: Name, Lineage, and Birth
Biographical introduction to Imam al-Haddad

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the full name, lineage, nisbahs, and birth year of Imam Abu Bakr al-Haddad and explain the meaning of each nisbah.

Core Idea
Imam Abu Bakr al-Haddad was a Hanafi faqih from Yemen; his nisbah 'al-Haddad' comes from blacksmithing, not lineage.

Knowing the author of a classical text is essential for understanding its authority and context. Imam Abu Bakr al-Haddad, the author of al-Jawhara al-Nayyira, was a Hanafi scholar from 8th-century Yemen. This lesson walks you through his full name, the meaning behind his various attributions, and the details of his birth as recorded in biographical sources.

Guided Path
1
Identify the full name and lineage
Start with the complete name as given: Abu Bakr bin Ali bin Muhammad bin Dawud bin al-Shaykh Ali bin Abd al-Rahman al-Haddad. Note that most biographers only go back to Muhammad, but a fuller lineage was found on a manuscript cover.
The kunya is Abu al-'Atiq; the laqab is Radi al-Din.
2
Analyze each nisbah
Examine each nisbah: al-'Abbadi (from the village al-'Abbadiyya), al-Zabidi (from Zabid, Yemen), al-Yamani, al-Hanafi. Understand that 'al-Haddad' is an occupational nisbah (blacksmith), not a tribal one.
This helps distinguish between lineage-based and craft-based attributions.
3
Verify the birth details
Note the birth year: Rajab 720 AH, as recorded in Taj al-Tarajim by Qasim bin Qutlubugha. The text mentions that this source is the only one that gives the month and year.
Birth place: al-'Abbadiyya village; later he moved to Zabid.
4
Understand the source of the biography
The passage lists several works that contain his biography: Tabaqat al-Khawass, al-Siraj al-Wahhaj (cover), al-Badr al-Tali', Kashf al-Zunun, etc. This shows the scholarly transmission of his life story.
The editor also found a more complete lineage on a manuscript cover.

Commentary
This passage is a biographical introduction to the author of al-Jawhara al-Nayyira, Imam Abu Bakr al-Haddad. It begins by listing the sources used for his biography: a large work (presumably the Tabaqat al-Khawass) that was copied, and another biography found on the cover of a manuscript of al-Siraj al-Wahhaj. Then it lists several reference works: al-Badr al-Tali', Kashf al-Zunun, Hadiyat al-'Arifin, al-A'lam, and Mu'jam al-Mu'allifin. The author then presents the name and lineage of al-Haddad: Abu Bakr bin Ali bin Muhammad bin Dawud bin al-Shaykh Ali bin Abd al-Rahman al-Haddad. His kunya is Abu al-'Atiq, and his laqab is Radi al-Din. His nisbahs are al-'Abbadi (from the village al-'Abbadiyya), al-Zabidi (from Zabid in Yemen), al-Yamani, and al-Hanafi. The passage notes a lacuna: most biographers only mention up to his grandfather Muhammad, but the editor found a fuller lineage on a manuscript cover.

Commentary
This passage is a biographical introduction to the author of al-Jawhara al-Nayyirah, Imam Abu Bakr ibn Ali al-Haddad. It begins by stating his full name and lineage, tracing back five generations, and notes his various epithets including 'al-Abbadi' (from the village of al-Abbadiyah) and 'al-Zabidi' (after the city of Zabid in Yemen), and 'al-Haddad' (blacksmith, indicating his family's trade). This establishes the scholarly pedigree and environment that shaped the author.
