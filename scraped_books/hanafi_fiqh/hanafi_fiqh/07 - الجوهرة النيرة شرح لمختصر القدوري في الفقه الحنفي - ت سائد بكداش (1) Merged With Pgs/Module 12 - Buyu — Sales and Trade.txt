Definition of Excessive Loss (Ghabn Fahish) in Hanafi Fiqh
Defines excessive loss in transactions

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the three definitions of excessive loss (ghabn fahish) in the Hanafi school and explain the legal consequence (khiyar al-ghabn).

Core Idea
Excessive loss in sale gives the defrauded party a right to rescind – three definitions exist.

This passage, though placed under the chapter of Tayammum in the source, defines a key concept in sales: ghabn fahish (excessive loss). When the price disparity is extreme, the law protects the defrauded party. The Hanafi scholars give different thresholds; we explore them here.

Guided Path
1
Identify the act and its ruling
The ruling is that a party is not obligated to bear excessive loss. This implies a right to rescind (khiyar al-ghabn).
The ruling is permissive for the defrauded party.
2
List the definitions
The text gives three definitions: (1) half the price, (2) double the market value, (3) a difference so great that experts cannot appraise.
These are given with 'قيل' (it is said), indicating different opinions.
3
Understand the consequence
If the loss meets any of these thresholds, the defrauded party may rescind the contract.
This is known as khiyar al-ghabn.
4
Note the ikhtilaf
The three definitions represent different Hanafi views. The operative (mufta bihi) is often the 'half' definition, but all are considered.
These are not contradictory but variations based on custom.

Commentary
This passage provides a ruling on excessive loss (ghabn fahish) in contracts. It defines it through three opinions: (1) a discrepancy of half the price, (2) double the market value, or (3) a difference so great that experts cannot appraise it. The ruling states that a party is not obligated to tolerate such a loss, implying a right to rescind. Although placed under 'Chapter of Tayammum', this topic belongs to sales (buyu'). Key Terms
