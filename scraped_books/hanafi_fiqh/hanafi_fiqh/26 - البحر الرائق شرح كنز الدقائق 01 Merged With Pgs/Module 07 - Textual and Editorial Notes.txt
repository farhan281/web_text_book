Six Things That Cause Forgetfulness: A Warning Against a Fabricated Hadith
A marginal note listing actions causing forgetfulness

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the six actions listed as causing forgetfulness and state why this list is not religiously significant.

Core Idea
A popular list of six causes of forgetfulness is a fabricated hadith — it has no basis in the Sunnah.

In the margins of fiqh texts, scholars sometimes include beneficial notes (nukta) that are not part of the main legal discussion. This note lists six actions that are popularly believed to cause forgetfulness. However, as the author warns, this list is attributed to the Prophet (peace be upon him) through a fabricated hadith. Therefore, it carries no legal weight.

Guided Path
1
Identify the author's purpose
Recognize that this is a marginal note (nukta) meant to inform readers of a common but baseless belief.
Not a ruling but a cautionary note.
2
List the six actions
Memorize the six items: leftover water of a live mouse, urinating in stagnant water, cutting urine stream, chewing gum, eating apples, and throwing a louse.
Some variations exist in other sources.
3
Evaluate the source
Note that some claim it's a hadith, but Ibn al-Jawzi declared it fabricated (mawdu').
Fabricated hadith cannot be used as evidence in fiqh.
4
Derive the fiqh implication
These actions are not prohibited due to forgetfulness. Their permissibility depends on other fiqh rulings.
For example, urinating in stagnant water is makruh for other reasons.

Some of them mentioned it as a hadith, but Abu al-Faraj ibn al-Jawzi said: It is a fabricated hadith.

Commentary
This passage is a brief marginal note (nukta) appended to the discussion on leftover water. It lists six actions that are popularly believed to cause forgetfulness (nisyan). The list includes: drinking the leftover water of a mouse while it is alive, urinating in stagnant water, cutting the flow of urine (read: interrupting urination before completion), chewing gum (or resin), eating apples, and throwing away a louse. The author notes that some people consider this as a hadith (prophetic saying), but Imam Ibn al-Jawzi (d. 597/1201), a prominent Hanbali scholar and hadith critic, declared it to be fabricated (mawdu'). Therefore, this list has no basis in authentic Sunnah. The inclusion of this note in a fiqh text like al-Bahr al-Ra'iq serves as a warning to students not to treat this as religiously significant. The ruling implication is that these actions are not religiously prohibited due to causing forgetfulness, though some may be discouraged for other reasons (e.g., urinating in stagnant water is disliked).

Key Terms
نكتة (nukta): A brief, beneficial point or note, often marginal, in a fiqh text.
حديث موضوع (hadith mawdu'): A fabricated hadith, falsely attributed to the Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him).
