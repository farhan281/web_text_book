Imprisonment for Wife's Maintenance and Exemption for Father
States imprisonment for wife's maintenance and father's exemption

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state when a husband may be imprisoned for non-payment of maintenance and identify the exception for a father.

Core Idea
A husband is imprisoned for unjust refusal to pay his wife's maintenance, but a father is exempt unless he refuses essentials for his child's survival.

This passage from al-Lubab addresses the enforcement of maintenance obligations. It explains that a husband who unjustly withholds nafaqah is subject to imprisonment. However, a father is generally not imprisoned for debts to his child, as it is a punishment. Yet the child's dire need creates an exception.

Guided Path
1
Identify the ruling
Determine who is subject to imprisonment for non-payment of maintenance. The passage states that a man is imprisoned for his wife's maintenance.
Imprisonment is a consequence of injustice (ẓulm) in withholding nafaqah.
2
Understand the rationale for the husband
The husband's imprisonment is due to his injustice in refusing to pay. This is a punishment for wrongful conduct.
The focus is on the act of refusal, not merely the debt.
3
Note the general rule for a father
A father is not imprisoned for a debt owed to his child because imprisonment is punitive, and a child should not inflict punishment on a parent.
This applies to debts generally, not just maintenance.
4
Recognize the exception for the father
If the father refuses to provide essential support (e.g., food) for the child's survival, he can be imprisoned to prevent the child's destruction.
The exception is based on necessity, not the father's general liability.
5
Understand the consequence of delay
Note that maintenance lapses with the passage of time if not claimed. This means past-due maintenance is forfeited.
This is a separate rule that interacts with the imprisonment ruling.

Commentary
This passage addresses imprisonment for financial obligations. A husband who refuses to pay his wife's maintenance is imprisoned as a punishment for his injustice. However, a father is not imprisoned for a debt owed to his child, since imprisonment is punitive and a child should not inflict punishment on his parent. An exception arises if the father refuses to provide necessaries for the child's survival; in that case, imprisonment is allowed to prevent the child's destruction. Additionally, if maintenance is not claimed for a period, it lapses (is forfeited) and cannot be claimed later.

Key Terms
نفقة زوجته (nafaqah zawjatih): The financial support a husband is obligated to provide to his wife, including food, clothing, and shelter.
ظلم (ẓulm): Injustice; here, the husband's refusal to pay maintenance is considered an act of injustice.
والد (wālid): Father; the parent is exempted from imprisonment for a debt to his child.
دين (dayn): Debt; includes both monetary debt and maintenance obligations.
