Interpreting a Bequest for a Ribat
Determines proper recipient of a bequest for a ribat.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the Hanafi rule for interpreting a bequest for a ribat and apply it to resolve ambiguous bequests.

Core Idea
The testator's intention governs; in doubt, the bequest goes to the building.

This passage from al-Bahr al-Ra'iq (شرح كنز الدقائق) addresses a practical issue: a person bequeaths one-third of his wealth for a 'ribat'. The term can refer either to the physical building or to the people residing in it. How should the executor spend the funds? The fatwa of al-Faqih Abu Ja'far provides a clear guideline based on the testator's intention.

Guided Path
1
Identify the ambiguity
Recognize that the word 'ribat' can mean both the building and its inhabitants. This ambiguity is the root of the question.
2
Determine the default ruling
If there is no evidence of the testator's specific intention, the default is to spend the bequest on the physical maintenance of the ribat (عمارة الرباط).
3
Note the exception
If there is a clear indication (دلالة) that the testator intended the residents (المقيمين), then the funds are given to them instead.
4
Extract the principle
The underlying principle is that ambiguous bequests are interpreted according to the testator's apparent intention; in the absence of evidence, the default is the physical structure rather than the users.

رجل أوصى بثلث ماله للرباط فإلى من يصرف قال الفقيه أبو جعفر إن كان هناك دلالة أنه أراد به المقيمين يصرف إليهم وإلا يصرف إلى عمارة الرباط اهـ .
A man bequeathed one third of his wealth for the ribat; to whom should it be spent? Al-Faqih Abu Ja'far said: If there is an indication that he intended thereby the residents, it is spent on them; otherwise, it is spent on the maintenance of the ribat. End.

Commentary
This short fatwa clarifies the interpretation of a bequest (wasiyyah) for a ribat. The general word 'ribat' can refer either to the building itself or to its inhabitants. Abu Ja'far rules that the intention of the testator must be sought. If there is evidence that he meant the people residing in the ribat, the one-third is given to them; otherwise, it is spent on the physical upkeep of the building. This follows the Hanafi principle that ambiguous bequests are interpreted according to the apparent intention of the testator, and in the absence of evidence, the default is the physical structure (building) rather than the users.

Key Terms
وصية (wasiyyah): A bequest or will, usually limited to one third of the estate.
رباط (ribat): A fortified outpost for Muslim fighters, often serving as a hostel for mujahidin.
دلالة (dalala): An indication or evidence of intention.
