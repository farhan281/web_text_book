Ruling on Will Directing a Loan from the Estate
States a ruling about a will to give a loan from the testator's estate

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to explain why a will to lend money from the estate is binding on heirs from the one-third and cannot be demanded before the term, based on analogy to wills of service or dwelling.

Core Idea
A bequest to lend money binds the heirs; they must lend from the one-third and cannot demand early repayment.

This lesson clarifies a specific ruling from al-Hidayah about a bequest (wasiyyah) that directs a loan of money from the testator's estate. You will see that such a bequest is binding on the heirs like any other bequest of gratuitous benefit, and they must execute it without demanding repayment before the specified term. The reasoning uses analogy (qiyas) to wills of service or dwelling.

Guided Path
1
Identify the act and its ruling
What is the testator's instruction? A loan of 1000 dirhams to a named person for one year. The ruling: it is binding (lazim) on the heirs from the one-third of the estate.
The term 'يلزم' indicates obligation.
2
Locate the evidence (dalil)
The passage states the ruling is by analogy to wills of service (khidma) and dwelling (sukna). It does not cite a specific Qur'an or hadith but uses a qiyas jaliy.
3
Trace the effective cause (illa)
The illa is that this is a will of gratuitous benefit (tabarru'), just like service or dwelling. All such wills create a right for the testator that must be fulfilled from the one-third.
The illa is jaliyyah (clear) and extracted by tahrir al-manat (determining the underlying reason).
4
Note the contrast with a previous case
The passage begins with 'وهذا بخلاف' meaning this is different from a prior case where the ruling might differ. Understand that the bindingness depends on the nature of the bequest.
The contrast highlights the scope of the rule.
5
Apply the ruling
If a testator leaves a will to lend a sum of money for a period, the heirs must execute it from the one-third portion and cannot demand repayment before the term ends.
This shows that a bequest of a loan is not a gift of the money, but a right to use it for the specified time.

Commentary
This passage presents a specific ruling from al-Hidayah concerning a will (wasiyyah) where the testator instructs that a loan of one thousand dirhams be given to a named person from his estate, with a one-year term. The ruling is that this bequest is binding on the heirs from the one-third of the estate (the portion available for bequests). The heirs are obligated to give the loan and cannot demand repayment before the specified term. The reasoning is that this is a will of gratuitous benefit (tabarru'), analogous to a will of service (khidma) or dwelling (sukna). Such wills create a right for the testator that must be fulfilled. The passage contrasts this with a previous case (not shown here) where the ruling might differ.

Key Terms
وصية (wasiyyah): A bequest or will directing disposition of property after death, binding from one-third of estate.
تبرع (tabarru'): Gratuitous benefit or donation without compensation.
