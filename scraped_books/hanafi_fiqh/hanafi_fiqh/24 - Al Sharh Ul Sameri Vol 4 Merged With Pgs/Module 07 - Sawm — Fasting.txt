Consecutive vs. Separate Fasting for Oath Expiation (Kafarah)
Ruling with proof and inter-madhhab dispute

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the Hanafi position on consecutive fasting for oath expiation, identify the Hanafi proof (Ibn Mas'ud's qira'ah), and explain the thamarah al-khilaf with the Shafi'i school.

Core Idea
Hanafi requires three consecutive fasts for oath expiation; Shafi'i allows separation—the difference hinges on an additional qira'ah.

This passage examines a key difference between the Hanafi and Shafi'i schools regarding the kafarah for a broken oath. When a person cannot free a slave, feed ten poor, or clothe them, the Quran prescribes fasting three days. But must those days be consecutive? The Hanafi school says yes, relying on the qira'ah of Ibn Mas'ud. The Shafi'i school says no, holding to the apparent meaning of the verse. Understanding this dispute sharpens your ability to evaluate how textual variants influence fiqh rulings.

Guided Path
1
State the Hanafi position
Identify the exact ruling: three consecutive days of fasting are wajib when one cannot perform the other expiations.
The term 'mutatabi'at' (consecutive) is central.
2
Identify the opposing school and their view
Imam al-Shafi'i holds that the three fasts may be separated or consecutive; both are valid.
The Shafi'i rely on the absence of 'mutatabi'at' in the Quranic text.
3
Follow the Hanafi proof
The Hanafi proof is the qira'ah of Ibn Mas'ud: 'fasiyam thalathati ayyamin mutatabi'at' (three consecutive days). This is considered a sunnah (explanatory addition). Also athar from Ibn Abbas supports consecutiveness.
The qira'ah is mutawatir in meaning? Actually it's a shadhdh qira'ah but accepted by Hanafis as sunnah.
4
Analyse the Hanafi response to the opponent
The Hanafi response: the Quranic verse is general (mutlaq), and the qira'ah specifies (muqayyad) it. The athar of Hasan al-Basri (allowing separation) is considered weaker because it contradicts the stronger evidence.
This illustrates the Hanafi principle of taqyid al-mutlaq bi al-sunnah.
5
Determine the thamarah al-khilaf
The practical consequence: According to Hanafi, if someone fasts three separate days, the kafarah is not valid and must be repeated. According to Shafi'i, the kafarah is valid regardless of consecutiveness.
This difference affects the validity of the expiation and the liability of the person.

Commentary
This passage is an inter-madhhab dispute on whether the three-day fast for oath expiation must be consecutive. The Hanafi position, supported by Ibn Mas'ud's qira'ah and several athar, requires consecutive days. Imam Shafi'i allows them to be separate, citing the apparent meaning of the verse and an athar from Hasan al-Basri. The author presents proofs for both sides without explicit tarjih, but the Hanafi view is based on the qira'ah which is considered a sunnah.

Key Terms
متتابعات (mutatabi'at): Consecutive days without break.
كفارة اليمين (kafarat al-yamin): Expiation for breaking an oath.
