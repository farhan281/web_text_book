A well-structured commentary combines textual proofs, rational analysis, and clear explanation to make Hanafi fiqh accessible.

The author Maulana Umaruddin Qasimi presents a list of twelve features that define his commentary's approach. These features are not merely cosmetic; they reflect a deliberate pedagogical method rooted in Hanafi tradition. Understanding this methodology will help you use the commentary effectively and appreciate how classical fiqh can be taught in a modern context.

Guided Path

Commentary
This passage is the preface (front matter) of the commentary 'Al-Sharh al-Thamiri' by Maulana Umaruddin Qasimi. The author outlines twelve distinctive features of his work, demonstrating a pedagogical approach to Hanafi fiqh. He emphasises clarity by numbering each issue, providing idiomatic translation, and including both rational (dalil 'aqli) and textual (dalil naqli) proofs. He also supplies hadith references, identifies the underlying usul (principles) of each ruling, explains difficult vocabulary, summarises ikhtilaf among the four imams, simplifies complex matters, avoids excessive length, uses easy language, and relies on authoritative sources such as al-Hidayah and the six authentic hadith collections. Notably, he introduces a calculator-based method for solving inheritance cases (fara'id) efficiently. The preface reflects a modern yet traditional methodology aimed at facilitating understanding for students.

Key Terms
دلیل عقلی (dalil 'aqli): Rational proof or analogical reasoning used to deduce a legal ruling.
دلیل نقلی (dalil naqli): Textual proof from the Qur'an or Sunnah.
اصول (usul): Principles or maxims from which rulings are derived, e.g., 'al-umur bi-maqasidiha'.

In this instance, the author also includes a call to support an academy dedicated to protecting the doctrine of the finality of prophethood from false claimants. This lesson places that effort within the broader Hanafi tradition of safeguarding the foundations of faith.

Khatm-e-Nubuwwat Academy (London). Brief Introduction. Highwaymen who have attacked the finality of prophethood have appeared in various styles from the era of prophethood to the present, but Allah, the Exalted, has adorned the crown of the finality of prophethood only on the head of the son of Aminah and Abdullah, Hazrat Muhammad (peace and blessings be upon him and his family), and humiliated all other claimants of prophethood from Musaylimah al-Kadhdhab to Musaylimah Qadiani. In every stratum of the Ummah, such individuals were chosen who sacrificed even their lives in defense of the finality of prophethood, and by the grace of Allah, connected their day-and-night efforts and abilities with the sacred bond of the honor of the Messenger and the finality of prophethood. The purpose of establishing the Khatm-e-Nubuwwat Academy (London) also encompasses these same aims and objectives. Thus, it is not hidden from anyone how the global preacher of the finality of prophethood, Hazrat Abdur Rahman Yaqub Bawa, devoted his life to this good work to alert Muslims to the reality of Qadianism. It is through his tireless efforts that the academy came into existence. Alhamdulillah, this institution has strengthened the defense of the finality of prophethood worldwide. Through speeches, literature, newspapers, journals, and the internet, it has informed Muslims about Qadianism and its intrigues and delivered the message of the finality of prophethood throughout the world. May Allah grant this institution further progress with sincerity. Ameen, then Ameen! Muslims are requested to fully cooperate with this institution. Administration: Khatm-e-Nubuwwat Academy (London).

Commentary
This is the concluding page of the fourth volume of Al-Sharh al-Thamiri. The author, Thamir al-Din Qasimi, completes the volume with a traditional prayer of praise and blessing upon the Prophet. He then identifies himself and his credentials. The date given is 5th Ramadan 1424 AH, corresponding to 31 October 2002 (though the year is printed as 3002, likely a typo). Following the completion note, there is a separate section promoting the Khatm-e-Nubuwwat Academy in London, an institution dedicated to defending the Islamic doctrine of the finality of prophethood against the claims of the Qadiani (Ahmadiyya) movement. This academy was founded by the efforts of Abdur Rahman Yaqub Bawa. The passage describes the academy's activities and calls for support from Muslims. Since this page does not contain any fiqh rulings or legal analysis, it serves as front matter and a concluding supplement.

Key Terms
