Imprisonment of Husband for Wife's Maintenance
Ruling on imprisoning a husband for non-payment of nafaqah

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the conditions under which a husband can be imprisoned for non-payment of nafaqah and explain the legal rationale.

Core Idea
A husband is imprisoned only after maintenance is fixed and he delays payment—it becomes a debt.

This lesson examines the Hanafi ruling on imprisoning a husband who fails to pay court-ordered maintenance. The text clarifies when imprisonment is permissible and why, grounding the ruling in the transformation of maintenance into a debt once fixed.

Guided Path
1
Identify the act and its ruling
The act is imprisoning the husband for non-payment of nafaqah. The ruling is that it is permissible (mubah) under specific conditions.
2
List the conditions for imprisonment
The text states two conditions: (1) the maintenance must be fixed by a judge or agreed upon by the spouses, and (2) the husband must delay payment after it becomes due.
3
Understand the legal cause
Once fixed, the maintenance becomes a debt (dayn) on the husband. Procrastination in debt payment justifies imprisonment in Hanafi law.
4
Note the exception
If the maintenance is not fixed, the husband is not imprisoned immediately; the judge may first determine the amount.
5
Consider the cross-madhhab perspective
Imam al-Shafi'i may hold that maintenance debt does not warrant imprisonment, reflecting a difference in the treatment of debt-based imprisonment.

[۲۹۰۹] (۲۲) ويُحبس الرجل في نفقة زوجته
آدمی بیوی کے نفقے میں قید کیا جائے گا۔ قاضی نے بیوی کا نفقہ متعین کر دیا ہو یا میاں بیوی کے درمیان کسی مقدار پر صلح ہوگئی ہو پھر وہ نفقہ ادا نہ کرے تو اس پر شوہر کو جبس کیا جائے گا۔ کیونکہ قاضی کے متعین کرنے کے بعد یا صلح ہونے کے بعد یہ نفقہ شوہر کے ذمہ دین ہو گیا۔ اور دین کی ادائیگی میں ٹال مٹول کے بعد جبس کیا جا سکتا ہے۔ اگر قاضی نے متعین نہ کیا ہو تو اتنی جلدی جس نہیں کیا جائے گا۔
A man is imprisoned for his wife's maintenance. A man will be imprisoned for his wife's maintenance. If the judge has fixed the wife's maintenance or the couple has settled on an amount, and then he does not pay it, then the husband will be imprisoned. Because after the judge's determination or settlement, this maintenance becomes a debt on the husband. And after procrastination in paying a debt, imprisonment is permissible. If the judge has not fixed it, he will not be imprisoned so quickly.

Commentary
This passage states the ruling that a husband can be imprisoned for failing to pay his wife's maintenance, but only after the maintenance has been determined by a judge or agreed upon by the spouses. The reason given is that once fixed, it becomes a debt, and a debtor who delays payment can be imprisoned. If the maintenance is not fixed, the husband is not imprisoned immediately.

Key Terms
نفقہ (nafaqah): maintenance for the wife including food, clothing, and shelter
دین (dayn): debt; an obligation that is fixed and enforceable
حبس (habs): imprisonment
