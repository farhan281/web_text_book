Zahir al-Riwayah and the Biography of 'Ata'
Definition of apparent transmission and a biographical note

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the six books of Zahir al-Riwayah and explain their authoritative status in the Hanafi school.

Core Idea
Zahir al-Riwayah: six books reliably transmitted from Imam Muhammad, forming the highest authority for Hanafi fatwa.

This passage introduces a key methodological concept in Hanafi fiqh: 'Zahir al-Riwayah' — the apparent transmission. Knowing which books constitute this corpus and why they are authoritative is essential for understanding how Hanafi rulings are derived and relied upon. The passage also gives a brief biography of the tabi'i 'Ata' ibn Abi Rabah, highlighting his role as a teacher of Abu Hanifa, underscoring the school's chain of knowledge.

Guided Path
1
Identify the six books of Zahir al-Riwayah
List the books mentioned: al-Jami' al-Kabir, al-Jami' al-Saghir, al-Siyar al-Kabir, al-Siyar al-Saghir, al-Ziyadat, and al-Mabsut. These are the core texts compiled by Imam Muhammad.
These six are the primary sources for the relied-upon positions of the Hanafi school.
2
Understand the transmission categories
The passage states that the rulings in these books are transmitted from Imam Muhammad via trustworthy narrators, either mutawatir (indubitable) or mashhur (well-known). This gives them high epistemological certainty.
Mashhur transmissions are still authoritative for practice, though not as certain as mutawatir.
3
Recognize the biographical link
Note that 'Ata' ibn Abi Rabah was a teacher of Abu Hanifa and a student of Ibn 'Abbas. Abu Hanifa praised him as the most comprehensive in knowledge, indicating the chain of transmission from the Companions to the school's founders.
This biographical note reinforces the school's connection to the early generations.
4
Apply the concept to fatwa practice
Understand that when issuing a fatwa, a Hanafi mufti first looks to the rulings found in Zahir al-Riwayah. These are given precedence over narrations from other sources (nawadir).
This hierarchy is unique to the Hanafi school and central to its legal methodology.

Commentary
This passage serves two purposes. First, it provides a methodological definition crucial for understanding the Hanafi school: 'Zahir al-Riwayah' refers to the six foundational books compiled by Imam Muhammad al-Shaybani, which contain the relied-upon positions of the school. These books are considered highly authoritative because their transmission from Imam Muhammad is through trustworthy chains, either mutawatir (reported by many) or mashhur (well-known). They form the basis for fatwa. The passage directs the reader to the introduction of the book for further detail. Second, it offers a brief biography of the tabi'i 'Ata' ibn Abi Rabah, highlighting his stature as a teacher of Abu Hanifa. Abu Hanifa is reported to have praised 'Ata' as the most comprehensive in knowledge. This biographical note underscores the chain of knowledge transmission in the Hanafi school and the reverence for earlier scholars.

Key Terms
ظاهر الرواية (zahir al-riwayah): The apparent/well-known transmission; the corpus of rulings found in Imam Muhammad's six books, reliably transmitted from Abu Hanifa and his disciples.
متواترة (mutawatirah): Narration reported by so many independent chains that collusion is impossible; yields certain knowledge.
مشهورة (mashhurah): Well-known narration; reported by three or more but less than tawatur; yields strong probability.
