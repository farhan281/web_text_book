Ruling on a Condition in a Bequest (Iysa')
Quotes a ruling from al-Bazzaziyya on a condition in a bequest and analyzes it.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the Hanafi ruling when a bequest contains a void condition and explain why the bequest remains valid.

Core Idea
A void condition does not invalidate the bequest; the bequest stands if the condition is separable.

This passage from al-Bazzaziyya, analyzed in Bahr al-Ra'iq, presents a classic scenario: a testator says 'You have one hundred dirhams on condition that you be my executor.' At first glance, the condition seems to tie the gift to an office, but the Hanafi school distinguishes between the condition and the principal bequest. The ruling teaches that a void condition does not nullify the bequest itself, a principle applicable in many contractual contexts.

Guided Path
1
Identify the act and its ruling
The act is a bequest (iysa') of one hundred dirhams. The passage states that the one hundred dirhams are a valid bequest for the recipient.
The bequest is sahih (valid).
2
Identify the condition and its ruling
The condition attached is 'on condition that you be my executor' (wasi). The passage rules that the condition is batil (void).
The condition conflicts with the nature of executorship, which should be gratuitous.
3
Understand the relationship between condition and bequest
The commentator explains this as an inversion: the testator intended to appoint an executor and give a gift, but combined the wording. The void condition does not affect the validity of the bequest because the bequest is not contingent on the condition.
The bequest is separable; the executor can accept the bequest regardless of the condition.
4
Apply the principle
Generalize: a void condition in a bequest (or similar contracts) does not automatically void the principal gift. The gift remains valid if the condition can be severed.
This aligns with the Hanafi maxim that a condition that is void does not necessarily render the contract void.

Commentary
This passage is a ruling about a bequest with a condition. It quotes a case from al-Bazzaziyya: someone says 'You have one hundred dirhams on condition that you be my executor.' The ruling is that the person becomes an executor, the condition is void, but the one hundred dirhams are a valid bequest for him. The commentator explains that this is like an inversion: the giver intended to make him executor and give him a gift, but the wording combined them. The condition is void because it contradicts the nature of bequest (the executor is not supposed to pay for the office). However, the bequest remains valid; if the executor accepts, he gets the money. This shows that a void condition does not necessarily invalidate the entire contract. The source then mentions that suspending a bequest or executorship on a condition is permissible (as per other rulings).

Key Terms
الإيصاء (al-iysa'): A bequest or appointment of an executor; the act of making a will or appointing a wasi.
الوصي (al-wasi): An executor appointed to manage the bequest or the testator's affairs after death.
