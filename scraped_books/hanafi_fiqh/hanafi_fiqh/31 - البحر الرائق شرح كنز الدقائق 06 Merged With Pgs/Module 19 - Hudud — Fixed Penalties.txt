Invalidity of Kafala with Hadd and Qisas
Explains invalidity of kafala for hadd and qisas punishments.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the Hanafi ruling on kafala involving hadd and qisas and explain the rationale based on the lack of deputyship (niyaba) in corporal punishment.

Core Idea
Kafala for hadd and qisas is invalid because niyaba cannot operate in punishment.

This lesson examines a specific ruling in Hanafi fiqh regarding kafala (guarantee) when it involves corporal punishments such as hadd and qisas. The passage from al-Bahr al-Raiq explains the invalidity of guaranteeing the actual punishment itself, distinguishing it from guaranteeing the person of the offender.

Guided Path
1
Identify the act and its ruling
Determine what act is being ruled upon (kafala with hadd or qisas) and what the ruling is (batil/invalid).
The text explicitly states the invalidity.
2
Locate the dalil
Identify the proof: a talil (reason) based on the lack of niyaba (deputyship) in punishment.
No direct textual evidence is cited; the argument is juristic reasoning.
3
Understand the rationale
Explain why niyaba is not allowed: the purpose of deterrence (zajr) would not be achieved if the punishment is carried out on a different person.
The deterrent effect is personal to the offender.
4
Note the qualification
Recognize that the invalidity applies to the punishment itself, not to the person of the offender. Kafala for producing the person is valid.
Al-Binaya and al-Hidayah support this distinction.
5
Recognize the nuance
For pure hadd punishments, even kafala for the person may be invalid as previously stated in the text.
This shows a layered ruling within the Hanafi school.

قوله : (والكفالة بحد وقود أي بطل التكفيل بحد وقود لأنه يتعذر إيجابه عليه لعدم جريان النيابة في العقوبة لعدم حصول المقصود منها وهو الزجر قيد الكفالة بنفس الحد والقود لأن الكفالة بنفس من عليه يجوز؛ وصرح به في البناية وأشار إليه في الهداية. وقدمنا أنه لا يجوز بنفس من عليه في الحدود الخالصة فليراجع في شرح قوله «ولا يجبر على الكفالة بالنفس في حد وقود
His statement: (and kafala with hadd and qisas) i.e., the guarantee with hadd and qisas is invalid, because it is impossible to impose it on him due to the lack of deputyship in punishment, due to the lack of achieving its objective, which is deterrence. He qualified kafala with the actual hadd and qisas, because kafala for the person of the one upon whom it is due is permissible; and it is stated explicitly in al-Binaya and alluded to in al-Hidayah. And we have previously stated that it is not permitted for the person of the one upon whom it is due in pure hadd punishments. So refer to the commentary on his statement 'And he is not compelled to accept kafala with person in hadd and qisas'.

Commentary
This passage explains the ruling that kafala (guarantee) for the actual corporal punishment of hadd or qisas is invalid. The reason is that deputyship (niyaba) cannot operate in punishments, as the purpose of deterrence would be lost if the punishment is carried out on a different person. The author qualifies that this refers to the punishment itself, not the person of the offender. Kafala for producing the person of the offender is valid, as stated in al-Binaya and al-Hidayah. However, for pure hadd punishments, even producing the person may not be valid, which was previously discussed.

Key Terms
حد (hadd): Fixed corporal punishment prescribed by Sharia
قصاص (qisas): Retaliatory punishment for homicide or injury
نيابة (niyaba): Deputyship or representation in legal acts
