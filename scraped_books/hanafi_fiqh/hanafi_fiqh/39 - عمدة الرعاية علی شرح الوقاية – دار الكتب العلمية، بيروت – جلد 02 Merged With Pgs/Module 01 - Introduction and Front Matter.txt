Navigating the Layers of Hanafi Commentary: Matn, Sharh, Hashia, and Gloss
Describes the arrangement of texts in this edition

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify and distinguish the four textual layers (matn, sharh, hashia, gloss) in a Hanafi fiqh edition and explain their relationship and purpose.

Core Idea
The matn is the core, the sharh expands, the hashia critiques, and the gloss updates.

This passage is a publisher's description of a multi-layered edition of a Hanafi fiqh text. Understanding this structure is essential for studying classical works, as each layer serves a distinct role: the matn provides the bare rulings, the sharh explains them, the hashia refines the commentary, and the gloss adds modern scholarly input. By the end of this lesson, you will be able to navigate such editions with confidence.

Guided Path
1
Identify the four textual layers
From the passage, list the four distinct texts: Wiqayat al-Riayah (matn), Sharh al-Wiqayah (sharh), Umdat al-Riayah (hashia), and Ghayat al-Inayah (gloss).
Note that the gloss is placed in the margin, while the hashia is at the bottom of the page.
2
Determine the author of each layer
Match each text to its author: Burhan al-Shari'ah for the matn, Sadr al-Shari'ah for the sharh, Imam Abdul-Hayy al-Laknawi for the hashia, and Dr. Salah Muhammad Abu al-Hajj for the gloss.
Pay attention to the chronological order and scholarly rank.
3
Analyze the physical layout on the page
The passage explains that the matn and sharh are placed at the top, the hashia at the bottom, and the gloss in the margin. This layout allows simultaneous consultation.
Visualize the page to understand how to locate each layer quickly.
4
Define the function of each layer
The matn contains the core legal rulings. The sharh provides commentary and explanation. The hashia offers critical refinement and additional insights. The gloss (marginal commentary) supplies modern scholarly updates and corrections.
Each layer builds upon the previous one, adding depth.
5
Apply the structure to fiqh study
When studying a chapter like Prayer, start with the matn for the basic ruling, then use the sharh for reasoning and conditions, refer to the hashia for ikhtilaf and tarjih, and consult the gloss for contemporary applications.
This layered approach ensures comprehensive understanding.

Commentary
This passage is a publisher's introduction describing the structure of the edition. It explains that the volume contains several layers: the original matn (Wiqayat al-Riayah), its well-known commentary (Sharh al-Wiqayah), a super-commentary (Umdat al-Riayah), and a marginal gloss (Ghayat al-Inayah). The second part covers the books of Salah, Zakat, Sawm, and Hjr. The arrangement places the matn and sharh at the top of each page and the hashia at the bottom, with the gloss in the margin. This allows the reader to consult all four texts simultaneously. The authors are identified: Burhan al-Shari'ah Mahmud al-Mahbubi for the matn, Sadr al-Shari'ah Abdullah al-Mahbubi for the sharh, Imam Abdul-Hayy al-Laknawi for the hashia, and Dr. Salah Abu al-Hajj for the marginal commentary.

Key Terms
وقاية الرواية (Wiqayat al-Riayah): The matn (core text) by Burhan al-Shariah Mahmud ibn Ahmad al-Mahbubi, containing legal rulings based on Hidayah.
شرح الوقاية (Sharh al-Wiqayah): The commentary on Wiqayat al-Riayah by Sadr al-Shariah Abdullah ibn Mas'ud al-Mahbubi.
عمدة الرعاية (Umdat al-Riayah): The hashia (super-commentary) by Imam Abdul-Hayy al-Laknawi on Sharh al-Wiqayah.
غاية العناية (Ghayat al-Inayah): The marginal gloss on Umdat al-Riayah by Dr. Salah Muhammad Abu al-Hajj.
