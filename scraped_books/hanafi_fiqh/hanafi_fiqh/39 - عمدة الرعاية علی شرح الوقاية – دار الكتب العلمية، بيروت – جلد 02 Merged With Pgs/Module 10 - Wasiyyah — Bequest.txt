Intra-Hanafi Dispute on Hajj Bequest After Agent's Death
Presents three Hanafi imams' rulings on a wasiyyah case

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the three Hanafi positions on executing a hajj bequest when the agent dies en route and identify the practical difference (thamarah al-khilaf) between them.

Core Idea
When the hajj agent dies, Abu Hanifa takes bequest from remaining third; Abu Yusuf from total third; Muhammad makes it conditional on leftover funds.

In this lesson, we examine a well-known intra-Hanafi dispute concerning a bequest (wasiyyah) for hajj. The case involves a testator who directs funds to hire someone to perform hajj on his behalf, but the hired agent dies before fulfilling the task. The three eponymous imams of the Hanafi school — Abu Hanifa, Abu Yusuf, and Muhammad al-Shaybani — differ on how the bequest should be executed from the estate. Understanding this dispute clarifies the Hanafi principles regarding delivery (taslim), loss, and the allocation of bequest funds.

Guided Path
1
Identify the case and the point of ikhtilaf
The case: a testator bequeaths funds to hire a hajj agent. The executor gives the funds to the agent, but the agent dies en route. The question is from which portion of the estate the bequest is executed. All agree the bequest itself is valid; the dispute is about the calculation.
2
State Abu Hanifa's position and his reasoning
Abu Hanifa holds that the bequest is executed from one-third of the remaining wealth after the initial allocation is set aside. His reasoning: the executor's allocation is not valid until delivery to the specified purpose; since delivery did not occur (the agent died), the allocated funds are considered lost, so the bequest must come from the remaining third.
3
State Abu Yusuf's position and his reasoning
Abu Yusuf holds that the bequest is executed from one-third of the total estate (the original third). He considers that the right to the bequest attaches to the total estate from the moment of death, regardless of the subsequent loss of allocated funds. He gives a specific numerical example: from a 1000-dirham estate, 333⅓ dirhams are taken.
4
State Muhammad's position and his reasoning
Muhammad al-Shaybani takes a middle view: if any of the funds given to the first agent remain (i.e., not spent or lost), that remaining amount is used for hajj; if nothing remains, the bequest becomes void. His reasoning: the bequest's execution depends on the existence of the allocated funds after the agent's death.
5
Determine the thamarah al-khilaf (practical difference)
Under Abu Hanifa, if the initial allocation is lost, the bequest amount is reduced because it comes from a smaller remaining third; under Abu Yusuf, the bequest remains fixed at one-third of the total; under Muhammad, the bequest is only executed if actual funds remain. This affects how much the heir actually receives.

Commentary
This passage is an intra-Hanafi dispute regarding a bequest (wasiyyah) for hajj. The case: A testator bequeaths funds to hire someone to perform hajj on his behalf. The executor gives the funds to an agent, but the agent dies en route before performing the hajj. The question is how to execute the bequest from the estate. Imam Abu Hanifa rules that the bequest is executed from one-third of the remaining wealth after setting aside the initial allocation. His reasoning: the executor's segregation and allocation of the funds is not valid until delivery to the specified purpose occurs. Since the funds were not delivered (the agent died), the allocated money is considered lost, so the bequest must come from the remaining third of the estate. Imam Abu Yusuf disagrees: the bequest is executed from one-third of the total estate (i.e., the original third, including the lost funds), because the right to the bequest attaches to the total wealth at death. He calculates a specific amount: 333⅓ dirhams, implying a total estate of 1000 dirhams. Imam Muhammad takes a middle position: if any of the funds given to the first agent remain (e.g., not spent), that remaining amount should be used for hajj; if nothing remains, the bequest becomes void. The footnotes clarify key terms: 'بثلث ما بقي' means the third of the remaining wealth; 'الوصي' is the executor; 'عزله' refers to separating the bequest from the estate; 'عينه الموصي' means the testator's specification of using the funds for hajj; and 'مما دفع' refers to the wealth given to the first agent. This dispute is recorded in the Zahir al-Riwayah, and the mufta bihi (relied-upon view) is not specified here, though later Hanafi practice often follows Abu Yusuf in cases of total loss.

Key Terms
وصية (wasiyyah): A bequest made by a testator, effective after death from one-third of the estate.
ثلث (thulth): One-third; the maximum fraction for a bequest in Islamic law.
أجير (ajir): An agent hired to perform hajj on behalf of another.
