Definition and Textual Proofs of Hunting (Sayd)
Definition and three textual proofs for hunting

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the linguistic and legal definitions of sayd, explain the Hanafi ruling on hunting (mubah for non-muhrim outside Haram), and cite the three textual proofs (two Quranic verses and one hadith) that establish its permissibility.

Core Idea
Hunting is mubah for a non-muhrim outside the Haram, proven by Quranic commands and a prophetic hadith.

This lesson opens the chapter on hunting (Kitab al-Sayd) from the Hidayah commentary. The author first defines the term 'sayd' and then establishes its fundamental ruling of permissibility by presenting three arguments from the Quran and Sunnah. Understanding these proofs is essential before exploring the detailed conditions that follow in the book.

Guided Path
1
Define 'sayd'
Identify the two meanings of sayd: the act of hunting and the animal that is hunted. This lexical clarification anchors the legal discussion.
The term is used in both senses in fiqh texts.
2
State the general ruling
The act of hunting is mubah (permissible) but only for a person who is not in ihram and outside the Haram. The passage explicitly states this condition.
The ruling changes for muhrim and inside Haram.
3
Identify the first textual proof
The Quranic verse 'When you are halal, then hunt' (Surah al-Ma'idah 5:2) is cited as a direct command indicating permissibility after leaving ihram.
The imperative form (fasatadu) indicates ibahah in Hanafi usul.
4
Identify the second textual proof
The verse 'Forbidden to you is the game of the land as long as you are in ihram' (5:96) implies that hunting is permissible when not in ihram, by the principle of opposite implication (mafhum al-mukhalafah).
Some schools use this verse differently.
5
Examine the third textual proof
The hadith of 'Adi ibn Hatim describes the permissibility of eating game caught by a trained dog, provided conditions are met. This hadith also establishes the foundational rules for hunting with jawarih.
The hadith introduces conditions: training, tasmiyah, dog not eating, and no sharing with another dog.

Commentary
This passage is an introduction to the Book of Hunting (Kitab al-Sayd) from the Hidayah commentary Ashraf al-Hidayah. It begins with a comparison between the chapters on drinks and hunting, noting that just as drinks have both halal and haram, so does hunting. The passage then defines the term 'sayd' linguistically and legally, stating that the act of hunting is permissible (mubah) for a person not in ihram and outside the Haram. The author provides five proofs for this permissibility: three textual (naqli) and two rational. The first two proofs are Quranic verses: 'and when you are halal, then hunt' (Surah al-Ma'idah 5:2) which indicates permissibility when not in ihram, and 'forbidden to you is the game of the land as long as you are in ihram' (Surah al-Ma'idah 5:96) which indicates prohibition during ihram. The third proof is the hadith of 'Adi ibn Hatim regarding hunting with a trained dog, which also provides conditions for permissibility. The fourth proof is ijma' (consensus of the scholars) that hunting is permissible. The fifth proof is rational: hunting is a form of acquisition and benefit from animals created for that purpose, and it aids the mukallaf in fulfilling his religious duties, thus it is mubah like gathering firewood. The passage then outlines the structure of the book, which has two chapters: one on hunting with predatory animals (jawarih) and the other on hunting by shooting. Finally, it lists fifteen conditions for permissibility, divided into five related to the hunter and five related to the dog (with a note that the full details will follow). The commentary explains each of these points, emphasizing that the Hanafi position is that hunting is mubah for those not in ihram and outside the Haram, with specific conditions.

Key Terms
صيد (sayd): Linguistically means hunting; technically refers to the act of hunting or the animal hunted.
جوارح (jawarih): Predatory animals that hunt, such as trained dogs, falcons, etc.
محرم (muhrim): A person in the state of ihram for Hajj or Umrah.

Additional Proofs and Structure of Kitab al-Sayd
Fourth and fifth proofs, book structure, and conditions preview

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to explain the fourth and fifth proofs for the permissibility of hunting (ijma and rational argument), describe the two chapters of Kitab al-Sayd, and list the general conditions for hunting with predatory animals.

Core Idea
Fourth proof: ijma; fifth proof: hunting is a legitimate form of benefit, like gathering firewood.

After the three textual proofs, the author provides two additional evidences: the consensus of the scholars (ijma) that hunting is permissible, and a rational argument based on the principle that hunting is a benefit and acquisition from animals created for human use, analogous to gathering firewood. The passage also outlines the two major divisions of the book: hunting with predatory animals (jawarih) and hunting by shooting. A note at the end lists fifteen conditions for permissibility, split between hunter and dog.

Guided Path
1
Understand the fourth proof (Ijma)
The author states that consensus has been established on the permissibility of hunting. This ijma is a strong proof because the community of scholars does not agree on an error.
Ijma is a primary source of law in Sunni jurisprudence.
2
Analyze the fifth proof (Rational argument)
Hunting is a form of acquisition and benefit using animals created for that purpose. It helps preserve the mukallaf (obligated person) and enables him to fulfill religious duties, so it is mubah like gathering firewood.
This is an istihsan or istidlal bi al-maslahah type argument.
3
Note the book structure
Kitab al-Sayd contains two chapters: first on hunting with jawarih (predatory animals), second on hunting by shooting (e.g., arrows, guns).
Most of the Hidayah's Kitab al-Sayd focuses on the first type.
4
Review the conditions for permissibility
Fifteen conditions are listed: five related to the hunter (e.g., being an expert in dhabh, not omitting tasmiyah) and five related to the dog (e.g., being trained, not eating from the game). The full details will follow in later chapters.
These conditions are previewed here but explained later.

Book of Hunting. Translation: This book is about the explanation of hunting. Explanation: Just as wine brings pleasure, so hunting brings pleasure (as is not hidden). So the relationship between them is apparent. Also hunting is among foods, which has an apparent connection with drinks. Then just as some wine is halal and some haram, similarly some hunting is halal and some haram. Then the reason for giving precedence to drinks is that in it the predominance of prohibition is there, and in this (hunting) the predominance of permissibility (as is apparent). The lexical meaning of sayd and three proofs for permissibility of hunting: Al-sayd is hunting, and it is also applied to what is hunted. And the act is permissible for one who is not in ihram, outside the Haram, due to the saying of Allah Most High: 'And when you are halal, then hunt' and due to the saying of Allah Almighty: 'And forbidden to you is the game of the land as long as you are in ihram', and due to the saying of the Prophet (peace be upon him) to 'Adi ibn Hatim al-Ta'i (may Allah be pleased with him): 'When you send your trained dog and mention the name of Allah over it, then eat; but if it eats from it, then do not eat, for it has caught it for itself. And if another dog joins your dog, then do not eat, for you have recited the tasmiyah over your dog and not over the other dog.' Translation: Sayd means to hunt, and it is also said about the animal that is hunted. And this act is permissible for a non-muhrim in a non-Haram place, due to the command of Allah (and when you are halal then hunt) and the command of Allah Almighty (and forbidden to you is the game of the land as long as you are in ihram), and due to the command of the Prophet (peace be upon him) to 'Adi ibn Hatim al-Ta'i: When you send your trained dog and you have recited the name of Allah over it, then eat; and if the dog has eaten from it, then do not eat, because the dog has restrained it for itself; and if another dog joins your dog, then do not eat, because you recited the tasmiyah over your dog and not over the other dog. Explanation: Sayd has two meanings: (1) to hunt, (2) the animal that is hunted. Then hunting is a permissible act, but on condition that the hunter is not in a state of ihram and does not hunt in the Haram. The author has mentioned five proofs for the permissibility of hunting: three are in this passage, and two after it. These three proofs are naqli (textual): the first two are verses of the Quran, and the third is a hadith. From the first verse it is known that hunting is halal in the state of halal; from the second it is known that hunting is haram in the state of ihram; and in the hadith there is further detail, which is apparent from the translation. Fourth and fifth proofs: And upon its permissibility is established Ijma' (consensus), and because it is a type of acquisition and benefit from what is created for that purpose, and in it is the preservation of the mukallaf and his ability to fulfill the obligations, so it is permissible, like gathering firewood. Then the total of what the book contains is two chapters: one concerning hunting with predatory animals (jawarih), and the second concerning hunting by shooting (with arrows). Translation: And consensus has been established on the permissibility of hunting, and because it is a type of earning and benefit from an animal created for that purpose, and in it is the preservation of the mukallaf and enabling him to fulfill the obligations, so it is permissible, in the category of gathering firewood. Then all the discussions that the book comprises are two chapters: one concerning hunting with jawarih, and the second concerning hunting by shooting arrows. Explanation: These are the fourth and fifth proofs: (4) Consensus has been established on the permissibility of hunting. (5) Hunting is a method of earning and it is permissible; also in hunting, using the animal for that for which it was created, also it preserves the mukallaf and gives him the ability to fulfill the shariah obligations. Therefore hunting becomes permissible, like gathering firewood is permissible. Then the author says that in this book there are two chapters. The first chapter contains a detailed explanation of hunting with jawarih (predatory animals), and the second chapter contains a detailed explanation of hunting with arrows etc. Note: There are fifteen conditions for permissibility: five of them are related to the hunter: (1) the hunter be an expert in dhabh (slaughter), (2) the sending (of the animal) be from his side, (3) no person whose hunting is not halal should participate with him in sending, (4) he should not omit the act of tasmiyah, (5) he should not engage in any other work between sending and catching. And five conditions are related to the dog: (1) it be trained (mu'allam), details of which are coming, (2) the dog should follow the path on which it was sent, (3) no dog whose killing of game is not halal should join it in catching, (4) it should wound the game and not kill it by its weight, (5) it should not eat from it.

Commentary
This passage is an introduction to the Book of Hunting (Kitab al-Sayd) from the Hidayah commentary Ashraf al-Hidayah. It begins with a comparison between the chapters on drinks and hunting, noting that just as drinks have both halal and haram, so does hunting. The passage then defines the term 'sayd' linguistically and legally, stating that the act of hunting is permissible (mubah) for a person not in ihram and outside the Haram. The author provides five proofs for this permissibility: three textual (naqli) and two rational. The first two proofs are Quranic verses: 'and when you are halal, then hunt' (Surah al-Ma'idah 5:2) which indicates permissibility when not in ihram, and 'forbidden to you is the game of the land as long as you are in ihram' (Surah al-Ma'idah 5:96) which indicates prohibition during ihram. The third proof is the hadith of 'Adi ibn Hatim regarding hunting with a trained dog, which also provides conditions for permissibility. The fourth proof is ijma' (consensus of the scholars) that hunting is permissible. The fifth proof is rational: hunting is a form of acquisition and benefit from animals created for that purpose, and it aids the mukallaf in fulfilling his religious duties, thus it is mubah like gathering firewood. The passage then outlines the structure of the book, which has two chapters: one on hunting with predatory animals (jawarih) and the other on hunting by shooting. Finally, it lists fifteen conditions for permissibility, divided into five related to the hunter and five related to the dog (with a note that the full details will follow). The commentary explains each of these points, emphasizing that the Hanafi position is that hunting is mubah for those not in ihram and outside the Haram, with specific conditions.

Key Terms
اجماع (ijma): Consensus of the scholars on a ruling.
جوارح (jawarih): Predatory animals trained for hunting, like dogs, falcons, etc.
محرم (muhrim): A person in a state of ihram for Hajj or Umrah.
