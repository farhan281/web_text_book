Claiming Water Rights Without Land
Ruling on validity of claiming water rights independently of land

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to explain why the Hanafi school permits a claim to water rights (shurb) without land ownership and identify the role of istihsan in this ruling.

Core Idea
Water rights can be owned separately from land; thus a claim of shurb without land is valid by istihsan.

The Hanafi school recognizes that water rights (shurb) may be inherited or retained after selling land. This lesson examines the ruling that such a claim is admissible in court, relying on istihsan (juristic preference) over strict analogy. Understanding this shows how Hanafi fiqh accommodates practical ownership structures.

Guided Path
1
Identify the ruling
Locate the hukm in the text: 'ويصح دعوى الشرب بغير ارض' – it is valid (sahih) to claim shurb without land.
The ruling is about the admissibility of the claim, not the water right itself.
2
Recognize the basis
The text says 'استحسانا' – the ruling is based on istihsan, not qiyas. Understand that istihsan departs from strict analogy for a stronger reason.
Other schools may use qiyas to deny such a claim.
3
Understand the dalil
The proof is aqli (rational): 'لانه قد يملك بدون الارض ارثا' – because shurb can be owned without land by inheritance, and 'وقد يبيع الارض ويبقى لشرب له' – a person may sell land but retain the water right. These two examples show independent ownership.
This rational proof supports the validity of the claim.
4
Note the cross-madhhab perspective
The Hanafi position is mukhtalaf fih – other schools (e.g., Shafi'i) may require land ownership for a valid claim. Istihsan is the key point of difference.
This ikhtilaf has practical consequences in water disputes.
5
Apply to a scenario
Consider a person who inherits only the water right without land. Apply the ruling: his claim to the water right is valid via istihsan.
This shows the real-world application of the ruling.

Commentary
This is a new section on claims (da'wa) regarding water rights. The ruling: it is legally valid (sahih) for someone to bring a claim asserting a right to water (shurb) even if they do not own the adjacent land. This is based on istihsan (juristic preference), which departs from strict analogy (qiyas). The reason: such water rights can be owned independently of land. For example, a person may inherit a water right without inheriting the land, or may sell the land but retain the water right. Therefore, such a claim is admissible in court and is considered desirable (marghubun fihi).

Key Terms
شرب (shurb): The right to water for irrigation or drinking, often a servitude attached to land but can be separate
استحسان (istihsan): Juristic preference; departing from clear analogy (qiyas) for a stronger reason
