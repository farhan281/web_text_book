Sulh with Shurb in Qisas Claims
Clarifies that sulh with shurb is valid in qisas claims

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to explain why sulh with shurb is valid in qisas claims and distinguish the ruling from financial claims.

Core Idea
In qisas, the compensation's validity is irrelevant—acceptance alone drops the right.

This lesson addresses a critical exception in Hanafi sulh law. While shurb (a drink) cannot be valid compensation in financial sulh because it cannot be owned, the same restriction does not apply to qisas (retributive punishment) claims. Here, the mere acceptance of the sulh (qubul) causes the qisas to drop, even if the compensation itself is not validly owned. Understanding this distinction sharpens your grasp of how the school weights legal causes (ta'lil) and avoids universalizing a specific ruling.

Guided Path
1
Identify the legal issue
The passage presents a ruling on whether a sulh agreement involving shurb (drink) in a qisas claim is valid. Recognize that this is an exception to the general rule that shurb cannot be used in sulh because it is not owned property.
2
Recall the general rule on shurb in sulh
In financial claims, sulh with shurb is invalid because shurb is not an owned commodity (mal) and cannot be transferred. This rule is based on the necessity of valid compensation (maqbul).
Refer to previous lessons on sulh conditions.
3
Apply the exception for qisas
The text states that for qisas, the presence of acceptance (qubul) is sufficient to drop the right, not the receipt of valid compensation (maqbul). Acceptance has occurred when both parties agree, so qisas drops.
This is a key ta'lil: in qisas, the ruling is based on acceptance, not ownership transfer.
4
Analyze the illa (legal cause)
The effective cause (illa) for dropping qisas is the consent of the victim or heir, not the nature of the compensation. Shurb, though invalid property, still conveys the victim's acceptance, so qisas drops.
This contrasts with financial claims where ownership transfer is the illa for validity.
5
Conclude the Hanafi position
Thus, sulh with shurb in qisas is valid (sahih) and the qisas is dropped. The mufta bihi (operative position) is as stated in Zahir al-Riwayah.
Other schools may disagree, requiring valid compensation even in qisas.

Commentary
This passage provides a critical refinement of the previous ruling. While shurb cannot be used in sulh for financial claims because it cannot be owned, the situation differs in qisas (retaliatory punishment) claims. In a qisas claim, the legal cause for dropping qisas is the acceptance of the sulh by the victim or heir, not the actual delivery of the compensation. Since acceptance (qubul) is present when both parties agree, the qisas drops even though the compensation (shurb) is invalid as property. The Hanafi reasoning: in financial claims, ownership transfer matters; in qisas, it is about waiving a right, and acceptance suffices. This is a specific exception where shurb sulh is valid.

Key Terms
قبول (qubul): Acceptance of the sulh offer, which is the effective cause for dropping qisas.
مقبول (maqbul): The object of acceptance; the compensation offered in sulh. In qisas, its validity is not required.
شرب (shurb): A drink; an example of something that is not owned property and thus not valid mal in financial sulh.

Key Takeaway
In Hanafi law, sulh with shurb (a drink) in a qisas claim is valid because the dropping of qisas depends on acceptance (qubul), not on the validity of the compensation as owned property.
