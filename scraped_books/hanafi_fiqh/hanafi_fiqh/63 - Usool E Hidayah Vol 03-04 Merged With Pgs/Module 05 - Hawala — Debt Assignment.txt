Introduction to the Book of Ḥawāla (Transfer of Debt)
Chapter heading for the book of hawala (transfer of debt)

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to define ḥawāla and explain its role in Hanafi fiqh.

Core Idea
Ḥawāla transfers a debt from one party to another, shifting liability without the creditor's loss.

The chapter heading 'Kitāb al-Ḥawāla' marks the beginning of a crucial section in Hanafi commercial law. Ḥawāla, or transfer of debt, is a contract that shifts a debt from the original debtor to a third party. This mechanism facilitates debt settlement and credit relationships. In this lesson, we lay the groundwork for understanding its conditions and effects.

Guided Path
1
Recognize the function of a chapter heading
Understand that 'Kitāb al-Ḥawāla' signals a new legal topic in Hanafi fiqh.
Chapter headings organize the text into thematic units.
2
Define the core term
Learn the linguistic and technical meaning of ḥawāla.
Linguistically it means 'transfer'; technically it is a specific debt transfer contract.
3
Identify the parties involved
Determine the roles: creditor, original debtor, and transferee.
Each party has specific rights and obligations.
4
State the basic legal effect
After a valid ḥawāla, the debt is transferred and the original debtor is released.
The transferee becomes the sole debtor to the creditor.
