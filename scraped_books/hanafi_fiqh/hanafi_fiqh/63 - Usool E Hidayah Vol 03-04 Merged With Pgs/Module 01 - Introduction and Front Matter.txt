Introduction to Kitab al-Buyu' – 27 Core Maxims (Qawa'id)
Table of contents listing 27 legal maxims with page numbers.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the 27 maxims of Kitab al-Buyu' and explain why studying these qawa'id is essential for mastering Hanafi sales law.

Core Idea
A table of contents is a blueprint: 27 maxims unlock the entire Hanafi law of sales.

This lesson introduces the table of contents for Kitab al-Buyu' from Usool e Hidayah Volume 3. The 27 maxims (qawa'id) listed here are not mere headings; they are the foundational principles from which all detailed rulings of sales are derived. In this lesson, we will survey these maxims and understand their role as pillars of Hanafi fiqh.

Guided Path
1
Survey the TOC structure
Look at the overall organization: the maxims are grouped under chapter headings (e.g., 'Bab al-Khiyar al-Shart', 'Bab al-Bay' al-Fasid'). Identify the page numbers to see the distribution of topics.
The TOC is divided into 6 sections, each with a cluster of maxims.
2
Categorize each maxim by type
For each maxim, determine whether it states a condition (shart), a prohibition (man'), a rule of interpretation (tafsir), or a general principle. For example, maxim #3 (jahalah) is a prohibition, while #7 (mutlaq expressions refer to common usage) is a rule of interpretation.
This helps in understanding the function of each maxim in deriving rulings.
3
Identify key themes across chapters
Note recurring themes: the importance of certainty, the treatment of options, the effect of conditions, and the criteria for valid delivery. These themes underpin the entire book.
For instance, 'gharar' and 'jahalah' are central to understanding invalid sales.
4
Memorize the maxim numbers and their core ideas
Create a mental map: associate each number with a one-phrase summary. For example, #1: 'khabar used for insha', #2: 'majlis unites scattered items', #3: 'jahalah prevents sale'.
This memorization anchor will facilitate quick recall when studying detailed cases.
5
Preview the chapters using maxims
Based on the maxims, predict what each chapter will discuss. For 'Bab al-Bay' al-Fasid', the maxims focus on delivery, gharar, and separate sale of accessories.
Active previewing enhances comprehension when you later read the full text.

Commentary
This passage is the table of contents for the fourth volume of Usool-e-Hidayah, a foundational text in the Hanafi school's legal methodology (usul al-fiqh). The volume covers several legal topics: Pre-emption (Shuf'ah), Division (Qismah), Sharecropping (Muzara'ah), Watering (Musaqah), Slaughterings (Dhaba'ih), and Sacrifice (Adhiyah). Each numbered statement is a key legal principle or rule that will be elaborated in the respective chapter. For example, the statement 'Taking by pre-emption is like purchase' (الاخذ بالشفعة بمنزلة الشراء) indicates that the legal effects of exercising pre-emption are analogous to those of a purchase contract. The table of contents serves as a roadmap for the volume, helping the student identify the major themes and rulings that will be studied.

Key Terms
شفعة (shuf'ah): The right of pre-emption, allowing a co-owner or neighbor to purchase a sold property by substituting the buyer.
قسمة (qismah): The division of jointly owned property among co-owners.
مزارعة (muzara'ah): A sharecropping contract where the landowner provides land and the worker provides labor, sharing the produce.
