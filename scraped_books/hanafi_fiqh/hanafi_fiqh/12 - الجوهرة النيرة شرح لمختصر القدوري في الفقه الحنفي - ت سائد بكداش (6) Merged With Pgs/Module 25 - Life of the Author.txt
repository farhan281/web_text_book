Bidayat al-Mubtadi: The Foundation of al-Hidayah
Editorial introduction describing the text's composition and significance.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the sources and structure of Bidayat al-Mubtadi and explain its place in the Hanafi textual tradition.

Core Idea
Bidayat al-Mubtadi combines two classic Hanafi texts and forms the base for al-Hidayah.

This lesson introduces a foundational matn in Hanafi fiqh: Bidayat al-Mubtadi by Imam al-Marghinani. Understanding its composition – what it includes and how it led to al-Hidayah – helps you navigate the Hanafi textual hierarchy. This passage from a modern edition explains the book's importance and its relationship to earlier and later works.

Guided Path
1
Identify the book and author
Note the title 'Bidayat al-Mubtadi' and its author, Imam Ali ibn Abi Bakr al-Marghinani (d. 593 AH). Recognize that this is a foundational text in the Hanafi school.
Al-Marghinani is also the author of al-Hidayah, the most famous Hanafi fiqh commentary.
2
Recognize the sources compiled in the book
The passage states that al-Marghinani combined two earlier authoritative works: Mukhtasar al-Quduri (by al-Quduri, d. 428 AH) and al-Jami' al-Saghir (by Imam Muhammad al-Shaybani, d. 189 AH), with additions.
Mukhtasar al-Quduri is a concise matn; al-Jami' al-Saghir is part of Zahir al-Riwayah.
3
Understand the relationship to al-Hidayah and Kifayat al-Muntahi
Al-Hidayah is al-Marghinani's commentary on Bidayat al-Mubtadi. He first wrote a massive commentary called Kifayat al-Muntahi (80 volumes), then abridged it to produce al-Hidayah. Thus Bidayat is the base matn for the medium-level text.
Al-Hidayah became the standard teaching text for Hanafi fiqh across the Islamic world.
4
Appreciate the editorial work on this edition
The modern edition was prepared from eight manuscript copies, correcting a previous corrupted print. It includes study of the book and author, and commentary on difficult points.
This ensures the text is reliable for study and reference.

Commentary
This is an editorial note introducing the book 'Bidayat al-Mubtadi' by Imam al-Marghinani. It states that this text is one of the most important foundational texts in Hanafi fiqh, closely linked to the famous book 'al-Hidayah', which is al-Marghinani's own commentary on this very text. Al-Hidayah itself is an abridgement of his larger commentary 'Kifayat al-Muntahi', which is 80 volumes. The author compiled Bidayat al-Mubtadi by combining two earlier authoritative texts: Mukhtasar al-Quduri (by al-Quduri) and al-Jami' al-Saghir (by Imam Muhammad al-Shaybani), along with additional material. The note mentions that this edition was prepared from eight valuable manuscripts, correcting an earlier corrupt print, with detailed arrangement and commentary, including a study of the book and its author, totaling 728 pages in one volume published in 1436 AH/2015 CE.

Key Terms
بداية المبتدي (Bidayat al-Mubtadi): A foundational Hanafi fiqh matn by al-Marghinani, combining Mukhtasar al-Quduri and al-Jami' al-Saghir with additions.
الهداية (al-Hidayah): Al-Marghinani's commentary on his own Bidayat al-Mubtadi, the most widely used medium-level Hanafi fiqh text.
كفاية المنتهي (Kifayat al-Muntahi): Al-Marghinani's large commentary on Bidayat al-Mubtadi, in 80 volumes, of which al-Hidayah is an abridgement.
مختصر القدوري (Mukhtasar al-Quduri): A concise Hanafi fiqh text by Ahmad al-Quduri (d. 428 AH), one of the main sources for Bidayat al-Mubtadi.
الجامع الصغير (al-Jami' al-Saghir): A collection of hadith and fiqh rulings by Imam Muhammad al-Shaybani (d. 189 AH), a key source for Hanafi law.
