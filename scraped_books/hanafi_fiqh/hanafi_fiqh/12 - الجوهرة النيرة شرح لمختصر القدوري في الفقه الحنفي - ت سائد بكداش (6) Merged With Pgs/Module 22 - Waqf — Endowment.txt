Ruling on Gilding the Quran and Decorating Mosques with Gold
Ruling on embellishing Quran and mosque with gold.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the Hanafi ruling on gilding the Quran and decorating mosques with gold and identify the conditions that affect its permissibility.

Core Idea
Gilding the Quran and mosque is permissible if fund source is proper; makruh if for ostentation.

This lesson examines the Hanafi ruling on embellishing the Quran and mosques with gold. The classical texts declare it permissible in principle, but later jurists clarify crucial conditions regarding the source of funds and intention. Understanding this ruling requires distinguishing between general permissibility and prohibition when conditions are violated.

Guided Path
1
Identify the basic ruling
The matn states 'la ba'sa' (no harm) for gilding the Quran and decorating the mosque with gold water. This indicates permissibility (ibaha).
The ruling is general and unconditional at this level.
2
Examine the rationale
The commentary explains that the purpose is tazim (veneration) and tashrif (honoring) of the sacred objects. This justifies the permissibility.
Intention matters: veneration vs. ostentation.
3
Consider the exception for riya'
If the act is done for ostentation (riya') or worldly show, it becomes makruh (disliked). This is a condition related to intention.
Riya' changes the ruling from mubah to makruh.
4
Apply the waqf fund condition
According to al-Khujandi, if the funding comes from the mosque's endowment income (ghallah al-waqf), the act is not permissible (ghayr ja'iz) and the trustee (mutawalli) is liable for the expense.
This condition overrides the general permissibility.
5
Determine the mufta bihi
The operative ruling is that permissibility depends on the source of funds. If from non-waqf funds and with proper intention, it is mubah. If from mosque waqf ghallah, it is prohibited and the mutawalli must compensate.
Later fatwa practice relies on al-Khujandi's distinction.

Commentary
This passage states the ruling on embellishing the Quran and mosque with gold. The matn declares it permissible (la ba's). The commentary explains the rationale: it is for tazim (veneration) and tashrif (honoring). However, it is makruh if done for riya' (ostentation) or worldly decoration. The commentary then transmits a view from al-Khujandi differentiating the source of funds: if from non-waqf revenue, it is permissible; if from the mosque's endowment income (ghallah al-waqf), it is not permissible and the mutawalli (trustee) is liable. This illustrates the Hanafi principle that waqf property must be spent according to the donor's conditions, and that extravagance in decoration from waqf funds is not allowed.

Key Terms
تحلية (tahliyah): gilding or plating with gold
غلة (ghallah): income or revenue generated from waqf property
وقف (waqf): endowment; property dedicated for religious or charitable purposes
