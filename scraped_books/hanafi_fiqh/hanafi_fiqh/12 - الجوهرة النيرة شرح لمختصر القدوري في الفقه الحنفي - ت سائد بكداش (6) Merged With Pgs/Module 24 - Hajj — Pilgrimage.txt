Executing a Will for Hajj al-Islam: Starting Point and Funding
Ruling on executing Hajj will from deceased's town or nearest feasible point

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to determine the correct starting point for performing Hajj on behalf of a deceased person based on his hometown, multiple residences, or lack thereof, and apply the rule of insufficient one-third funds.

Core Idea
The deceased's obligatory Hajj must begin from his hometown, unless funds restrict the starting point.

This lesson explains the Hanafi ruling on fulfilling a will to perform Hajj al-Islam (the obligatory Hajj) for a deceased person. The text clarifies that the starting point for the hired pilgrim depends on the deceased's residence(s) and the sufficiency of the bequest from the one-third of the estate. Understanding these rules ensures the bequest is executed correctly and in accordance with the deceased's obligation.

Guided Path
1
Identify the act and its ruling
Recognize that the will is for Hajjat al-Islam, which was fard on the deceased. The execution of the will from the one-third is wajib on the heirs.
2
Locate the default starting point
The text states: 'because the obligation on him is Hajj from his town.' Thus, the default starting point is the deceased's hometown.
This rule is based on the principle that the obligation of Hajj is tied to one's place of residence.
3
Apply exceptions for special situations
Work through the scenarios: multiple homelands (closest to Mecca), Makkan origin (from Mecca unless qiran willed), no homeland (from place of death), and insufficient one-third (from where funds reach).
The riding condition (rakiban) is also derived from the principle that walking is not obligatory.
4
Synthesize the complete ruling
Combine the scenarios to determine the starting point in any given case, considering the one-third limitation.
If the one-third is insufficient for the required start point, the Hajj is performed from the nearest location the funds can cover.

ومن أوصى بحَجَةِ الإسلام : أَحَجُوا عنه رجلاً من بلده، يحج عنه راكباً (۱) وصية تُعتبر من الثلث. قوله: (ومن أوصى بحجة الإسلام: أحجُّوا عنه رجلاً من بلده، يحج عنه راكباً)؛ لأن الواجب عليه الحج من بلده. وإنما قال راكباً؛ لأنه لا يجب عليه الحج ماشياً، فوجب عليه أن يحج عنه كذلك. وهذا إذا كان الثلث يتسع لذلك، فإن كان له أوطان كثيرة: حَجَّ عنه راكباً من أقرب أوطانه إلى مكة. وإن كان مكياً، فمات بخراسان، فإن أوصى أن يُحَجَّ عنه : حُج عن من مكة، إلا أن يوصي بالقِران: فيُحَج عنه قارناً من خراسان. وإن لم يكن له وطن: حُجَّ عنه من حيث مات. وإن كان ثلث ماله لا يفي بذلك: حُجَّ عنه من حيث يبلغ.
And whoever makes a will for the Hajj of Islam: let them perform Hajj on his behalf by a man from his town, performing Hajj on his behalf riding. (1) A will is considered from the one-third. He said: 'And whoever makes a will for the Hajj of Islam: let them perform Hajj on his behalf by a man from his town, performing Hajj on his behalf riding' because the obligation on him is Hajj from his town. And only he said 'riding' because it is not obligatory on him to perform Hajj walking, so it is obligatory to perform Hajj on his behalf likewise. And this is if the one-third is sufficient for that. If he has many homelands, perform Hajj on his behalf riding from the closest of his homelands to Mecca. And if he was a Makkan and died in Khurasan, if he made a will that Hajj be performed on his behalf, then Hajj should be performed on his behalf from Mecca, unless he makes a will for qiran, then perform Hajj on his behalf as a qarin from Khurasan. And if he has no homeland, perform Hajj on his behalf from where he died. And if his one-third does not cover that, perform Hajj on his behalf from where it reaches.

Commentary
This passage discusses the execution of a will to perform Hajjat al-Islam (the obligatory Hajj) for a deceased person. The ruling: the will is valid from the one-third of the estate. The executor must hire a man from the deceased's hometown to perform Hajj on his behalf, and the man must go riding (not walking) because the deceased would have been obligated to ride if he performed it himself (walking is not obligatory). If the deceased had multiple residences, the starting point is the closest to Mecca. If he was a Makkan by origin but died elsewhere (e.g., Khurasan), the Hajj is performed from Mecca because his obligation starts from his original home, unless he specifically willed for qiran (combining 'Umrah and Hajj), in which case it starts from where he died to include the 'Umrah. If he has no fixed homeland, the Hajj starts from his place of death. If the one-third is insufficient for the full journey from the prescribed starting point, the Hajj is performed from the location that the available funds can reach, ensuring the bequest is fulfilled to the maximum extent possible.

Key Terms
حجة الإسلام (Hajjat al-Islam): The obligatory Hajj that every Muslim must perform once in a lifetime if able.
قِران (qiran): A type of Hajj where 'Umrah and Hajj are performed with a single ihram and no break in between.
بلد (balad): Hometown or place of residence; the starting point for the Hajj obligation.

Insufficient Bequest for Hajj: Performing Hajj Halfway
Ruling on performing Hajj from halfway when bequest insufficient

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to apply the Hanafi principle of executing a bequest to the extent possible when funds are insufficient.

Core Idea
If a bequest cannot cover full Hajj, perform Hajj from the point the funds reach.

This lesson addresses a practical scenario: a deceased person leaves a bequest for Hajj, but the amount is insufficient to cover the entire journey from his home. The Hanafi school rules that the bequest is not void; rather, the executor must perform Hajj on his behalf from the location that the bequest can reach. This reflects the legal principle that obligations are executed as much as possible.

Guided Path
1
Identify the act and its ruling
What exactly is being ruled upon? The act is performing Hajj on behalf of a deceased person using his bequest. The ruling is that if the bequest is insufficient, the Hajj is performed from where the funds allow.
The ruling is wajib on the executor.
2
Understand the proof (dalil)
The commentator states the reason: we know the testator intended to execute the bequest, so it is obligatory to execute it as much as possible.
The proof is qiyas (analogy) based on the testator's intention.
3
Recognise the legal principle
The underlying principle is that when full fulfillment is impossible, partial fulfillment is obligatory.
This is a form of masqat al-ghayr (execution to the extent possible).
4
Note the practical application
The executor must calculate the bequest amount, determine the furthest point on the Hajj route it can cover, and then arrange Hajj from that point on behalf of the deceased.
The bequest must be used solely for this purpose.

Commentary

The commentator explains that the testator's intent was to have the Hajj performed, and since full execution is not possible, we execute it to the extent possible. This is based on the principle of reprioritizing the testator's intention. The ruling is specific to the Hanafi school.

Hajj from Homeland vs Death Site: Hanafi Intra-Dispute
Intra-Hanafi dispute about location for Hajj when testator died en route

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the positions of Abu Hanifa and the Sahibayn regarding the location of Hajj when a testator dies en route, and apply the ruling to the case of a hired pilgrim dying en route.

Core Idea
When a testator dies en route, Abu Hanifa insists on Hajj from homeland; the Sahibayn permit from the death site.

This passage examines a classic intra-Hanafi dispute about the location of Hajj when the testator dies before reaching Mecca. The ruling determines from where the Hajj must be performed—the original homeland or the place of death. The commentator also extends the dispute to the case of a hired pilgrim who dies en route, revealing a consistent pattern of reasoning.

Guided Path
1
Identify the basic scenario
The scenario is a person who leaves his city intending Hajj, dies en route, and had bequeathed that Hajj be performed on his behalf.
The key phrase is 'خَرج من بلده حاجاً' – left as a pilgrim.
2
State the two positions
Abu Hanifa: Hajj from the homeland. The Sahibayn (Abu Yusuf and Muhammad): Hajj from the place of death.
This is the core dispute.
3
Note the restriction
If the person left for trade (not specifically for Hajj), then all agree it is from the homeland.
The restriction is important to limit the dispute.
4
Apply to the hired pilgrim case
If a person performing Hajj on behalf of another (hired pilgrim) dies en route, the same dispute applies: Sahibayn say from death site; Abu Hanifa says add remaining funds to testator's estate, take one-third, and perform from homeland.
This extension shows the principle is consistent.
5
Determine the practical consequence
The difference affects cost and logistics. Performing from homeland may be more expensive; from death site may be cheaper.
This is the thamarah al-khilaf.

ومَن خَرج من بلده حاجاً، فمات في الطريق، وأوصى أن يُحَج عنه : حج عنه من بلده عند أبي حنيفة، وقالا : يُحَج عنه من حيث مات . * قوله: (ومن خرج من بلده حاجاً، فمات في الطريق، وأوصى أن يُحج عنه: حُجَّ عنه من بلده عند أبي حنيفة، وقال أبو يوسف ومحمد: عنه من حيث مات). وقيد بقوله : خرج :حاجاً لأنه لو خرج للتجارة: فإنه يُحجُّ عنه من بلده هناك، بالاتفاق. كذا في «النهاية». وعلى هذا الخلاف إذا مات الحاج عن غيره في الطريق: فعندهما: يُحَج عنه بالباقي من حيث مات. وعند أبي حنيفة : يُضَمُّ ما بقي في يده إلى مال الموصي، ويُؤخذ ثلثه، ويُحَجِّ به عنه من وطنه، ولا ضمان على الأول فيما أنفق إلى وقت الموت.
And whoever left his city as a pilgrim and died on the way and bequested that Hajj be performed on his behalf: according to Abu Hanifa, Hajj is performed on his behalf from his city; and the two (Abu Yusuf and Muhammad) said: Hajj is performed on his behalf from where he died. Commentator says: (Whoever left his city as a pilgrim and died on the way and bequested that Hajj be performed on his behalf: Hajj is performed on his behalf from his city according to Abu Hanifa, and Abu Yusuf and Muhammad said: from where he died). And he restricted (his saying) 'as a pilgrim' because if he left for trade, then Hajj is performed on his behalf from his city by agreement. As in al-Nihaya. And according to this dispute, if the person who performed Hajj on behalf of another died on the way: according to the two, Hajj is performed on his behalf with the remaining (money) from where he died. And according to Abu Hanifa, what remains in his hand is added to the testator's wealth and one-third is taken, and Hajj is performed on his behalf from his homeland, and there is no liability on the first (deceased) for what he spent until the time of death.

Commentary
This is an intra-Hanafi dispute. The matn presents the issue: a person leaves his city intending Hajj, dies en route, and had bequested that Hajj be performed on his behalf. Abu Hanifa holds that the Hajj must be performed from the original homeland, while Abu Yusuf and Muhammad (the Sahibayn) hold it should be performed from the place of death. The commentator notes a restriction: if the person left for trade (not specifically for Hajj), then all agree it is performed from the homeland. Then the commentator applies the same dispute to a case where a person who was already performing Hajj on behalf of another (as a hired pilgrim) dies en route. The same ikhtilaf applies: the two say perform from death site, Abu Hanifa says add remaining funds to the testator's estate and take one-third, then perform from homeland. No guarantee is required for what the first deceased spent before death.

Key Terms
حاج (hajj): Pilgrim performing Hajj.
مات في الطريق (mata fi al-tariq): Died en route.
ضمان (daman): Liability; here, no liability for what was spent before death.
