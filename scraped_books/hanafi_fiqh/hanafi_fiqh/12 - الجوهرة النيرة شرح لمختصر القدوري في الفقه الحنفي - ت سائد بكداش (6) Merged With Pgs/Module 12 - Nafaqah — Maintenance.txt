No Maintenance While Seeking Witnesses
States a ruling that a wife is not entitled to maintenance while she is seeking witnesses

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the Hanafi ruling on a wife's maintenance during litigation and identify the condition that suspends the obligation.

Core Idea
A wife seeking witnesses for a claim forfeits maintenance during that period.

This short ruling from a Hanafi text clarifies that a wife's entitlement to maintenance is not absolute. When she is actively engaged in litigation—specifically seeking witnesses to support her claim—the husband's obligation to provide support is temporarily suspended. Understanding this exception helps grasp the conditions that trigger maintenance obligations.

Guided Path
1
Identify the subject
The ruling addresses the wife (لها) in the context of seeking witnesses.
The subject is the wife who is engaged in litigation.
2
Identify the ruling
The ruling states 'لا نفقة' (no maintenance), meaning the husband is not obligated to provide financial support.
Maintenance normally includes food, clothing, and shelter.
3
Identify the temporal condition
The period is defined as 'في مدة المسألة عن الشهود' (during the period of seeking witnesses).
This is the specific activity that triggers the suspension.
4
Understand the context
This applies when the wife is actively searching for witnesses to prove her claim, likely in a court setting.
The claim may be for maintenance, divorce, or other rights.
5
Note the legal consequence
Maintenance is not due during that period, implying the husband's obligation is temporarily lifted.
Once the witness search ends, maintenance may resume.

ولا نفقة لها في مدة المسألة عن الشهود.
And there is no maintenance for her during the period of seeking witnesses.

Commentary
This is a brief ruling statement: a wife is not entitled to maintenance (nafaqah) during the time she is engaged in seeking (musa'alah) witnesses (shuhud) to prove her claim. The context is likely a woman who has a claim (e.g., for maintenance or divorce) and is seeking witnesses to support it. The ruling means that the obligation of the husband to provide maintenance is suspended during this period, perhaps because she is in the process of litigation. This statement appears without further explanation in the scanned text, so it may be part of a larger discussion elsewhere.

Key Terms
النفقة (al-nafaqah): Maintenance, i.e., financial support for the wife, including food, clothing, and shelter.
المسألة عن الشهود (al-musa'alah 'an al-shuhud): The act of seeking witnesses to establish a claim in court.
