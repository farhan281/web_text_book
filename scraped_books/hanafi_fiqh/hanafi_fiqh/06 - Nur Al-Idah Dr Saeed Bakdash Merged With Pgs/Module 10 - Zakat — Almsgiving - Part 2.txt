Distribution of Zakat al-Fitr
Rulings on distributing Zakat al-Fitr to recipients

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the rules for distributing Zakat al-Fitr according to the Hanafi school and identify the relied-upon position on combining or dividing fitrah.

Core Idea
Each person gives their fitrah to one poor, but combining multiple fitrahs for one poor or dividing one among many is permissible.

Zakat al-Fitr is a wajib charity given at the end of Ramadan. While the standard practice is to give each person's fitrah to a single poor recipient, there are legitimate variations. This passage clarifies the Hanafi rulings on combining multiple fitrahs for one poor and dividing a single fitrah among several poor, including an intra-madhhab difference on the latter.

Guided Path
1
Identify the default rule
The text states as the first point: each person gives their fitrah to one poor person. This is the basic and recommended method.
2
Note the intra-Hanafi ikhtilaf on dividing one fitrah
There is a disagreement on whether a single fitrah can be split among multiple poor. Two views exist: permissible without dislike (the majority, relied-upon) and permissible with tanzihi dislike.
3
Identify the correct position on combining fitrahs
The third point clearly states that it is correct (sahih) to give the fitrah of a group of people to a single poor person. This is the authoritative ruling.
4
Recognize the mufta bihi view
Ibn Abidin confirms that the relied-upon position for fatwa is permissibility without dislike for both combining and dividing, as the majority and the school's madhhab.

Commentary
This passage presents the rulings on how to distribute Zakat al-Fitr. The first point is that each person should give their entire fitrah to a single poor person, which is the standard practice. The second point notes an ikhtilaf (disagreement) within the Hanafi school regarding the permissibility of dividing one person's fitrah among multiple poor recipients. The footnote clarifies that there are two positions: the majority and relied-upon view is that it is permissible without any dislike, while another view allows it but with tanzihi (lightly disliked) karahah. The third point states that it is permissible to combine the fitrah of multiple people and give it all to one poor person; this is the correct (sahih) position. The footnote reference to Ibn Abidin indicates that the latter view is the mufta bihi (operative fatwa) in the later Hanafi tradition. Thus, the key practical takeaway: it is better to give one fitrah to one poor person, but combining multiple fitrahs for one poor is fully permissible; dividing one fitrah among multiple poor is also permissible according to the stronger view, but some scholars discourage it mildly.

Key Terms
فطرة (fitrah): Obligatory charity given at the end of Ramadan, consisting of one sa' of staple food per person.
فقير (faqir): A poor person eligible to receive zakat and fitrah; one who does not possess the nisab or sufficient wealth.

Key Takeaway
The Hanafi school permits combining multiple fitrahs for one poor and dividing one fitrah among multiple poor, with the relied-upon view being that it is permissible without dislike.
