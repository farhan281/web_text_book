Third Category of Hunting Penalties: Less Than Half Sa'
Defines the third category of penalties for killing small insects

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the penalty for killing a louse or locust while in ihram and identify the discretionary nature of the amount.

Core Idea
Killing a louse or locust requires minimal charity, less than half a sa', left to the donor.

The Hanafi school categorizes hunting penalties based on the severity of the creature killed. This lesson addresses the third category—the most minor—involving small insects like lice and locusts. The penalty is a small charitable donation, the exact amount of which is left to the pilgrim's discretion, provided it does not exceed half a sa'.

Guided Path
1
Identify the act
Determine what action triggers this penalty: killing a louse or a locust while in the state of ihram.
These are small, insignificant creatures compared to larger game.
2
Note the penalty category
Recognize that this is the third and most minor category of hunting penalties, requiring less than half a sa' of food.
Half a sa' is approximately 1.5 kg of grain.
3
Understand the discretionary nature
The text says 'he gives charity as he wishes' (فيتصدق بما شاء), meaning the exact amount is not fixed; he may give any amount less than half a sa'.
This reflects the insignificance of the offense.

Core Idea
A sheep suffices for all Hadi except two major violations: janabah tawaf and post-Arafat intercourse before halq.

The Hadi is an integral part of Hajj, especially for those performing Tamattu or Qiran. This passage from Nur al-Idah outlines the minimum acceptable animal, the general rule of permissibility, and the specific exceptions where a higher compensation is required. Types and Conditions of Hady
States types and conditions of hady

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the minimum type of hady, identify the two exceptions where a camel is required, and apply the rule that conditions valid for udhiyah also apply to hady.

Core Idea
A sheep suffices for hady except for two major sins requiring a camel.

The hady is the sacrificial animal offered in the Haram during Hajj, obligatory for Tamattu' and Qiran. This passage from Nur al-Idah lays out the types of animals that can be offered, their minimum ages, and two specific situations where a sheep is insufficient, requiring a camel. Mastering these categories ensures the validity of the sacrifice.

Guided Path
1
Identify the minimum animal
The text states 'أدناه: شاة' (the minimum is a sheep). Note that it can also be from camels, cows, or goats.
Sheep includes both goats and sheep in general.
2
Note age requirements
For sheep: at least one year old (bint makhad) or a jadha' from da'n (6-7 months). For camels: 5 years. For cows: 2 years.
These ages are the same as for udhiyah.
3
Apply the rule that udhiyah conditions extend to hady
The text says 'وما جاز في الضحايا جاز في الهدايا' – whatever is permissible for udhiyah is permissible for hady. Thus, soundness of the animal (free from blindness, lameness, etc.) is required.
Defects that invalidate udhiyah also invalidate hady.
4
Identify the two exceptions to the sheep
A sheep is sufficient for all cases except (a) Tawaf al-Rukn while in a state of janabah, and (b) intercourse after standing at Arafat but before shaving. Each requires a camel.
These are major violations requiring a greater sacrifice.
5
Understand the rule for defective voluntary hady
If a voluntary hady becomes defective on the way, it is slaughtered where it is, not necessarily in the Haram.
This is an exception to the rule that hady must be slaughtered in the Haram.

Hady must be slaughtered in the Haram on the Day of Nahr, except a defective voluntary hady slaughtered where it breaks down.

Once the animal is selected, the pilgrim must observe specific rules about when and where to slaughter it, and how to treat the animal and its products. This passage from Nur al-Idah clarifies that the hady of Tamattu' and Qiran is limited to the Day of Nahr, all hady must be within the Haram (with an exception), and provides guidance on marking, feeding, and using the animal.

These rules ensure the hady is treated with respect and its benefits go to the poor.

Core Idea

The Hanafi school encourages visiting the sacred relics within Masjid al-Nabawi as a means of drawing closer to Allah and the Prophet.
