Obligation of Eid Prayer and Takbir (Intra-Hanafi Dispute)
Intra-Hanafi dispute on Eid prayer obligation and takbir ruling

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the two Hanafi positions on the obligation of Eid prayer, identify the relied-upon (mufta bihi) view, and apply the thamarah al-khilaf in a practical scenario.

Core Idea
Eid prayer is wajib on every obligated person according to the mufta bihi view of Abu Yusuf and Muhammad.

This passage from Nur al-Idah presents an intra-Hanafi dispute about who must perform the Eid prayer. Imam Abu Hanifa restricts the obligation to those praying in congregation with a resident imam, while the two Sahibayn extend it to all who are obligated to pray even if alone. The text declares the Sahibayn's view as the operative position for fatwa, though later scholars debated this tarjih. Understanding this dispute is crucial for correctly applying the ruling in diverse situations.

Guided Path
1
Identify the two Hanafi positions
Read the passage and locate Abu Hanifa's view (conditional on congregation) and the Sahibayn's view (obligatory on every obligated person).
The passage uses 'Qala' (Abu Hanifa) and 'Qala' (the two) – not typical pronoun, but contextual.
2
Analyze the conditions for obligation
For Abu Hanifa, obligations requires: (a) imam resident in a city, (b) congregation. For Sahibayn, no such conditions; simply being obligated to pray (mukallaf) suffices.
The Sahibayn even extend to travelers, slaves, and women who pray alone.
3
Note the tarjih and its authority
The matn states 'Wa bihi yu'mal wa 'alayhi al-fatwa', indicating the Sahibayn's view is the mufta bihi. The footnote shows later disagreement but confirms the predominant practice.
This tarjih is supported by al-Zahidi and many later authorities.
4
Apply the thamarah al-khilaf
Consider a traveler in a non-Muslim country: Abu Hanifa would excuse him; the Sahibayn would require him to pray. The fatwa follows the stricter view.
Knowing the dispute helps you adapt rulings to different circumstances.
5
Memorize the takbir formula
The passage provides the recommended takbir after the Eid prayer. Note its structure and significance.
This takbir is also recited after fard prayers from 9th to 13th Dhul Hijjah.

Rulings of the Eclipse Prayer (Salat al-Kusuf) – A Sunnah Mu'akkadah
Lists the sunnah rulings and method of eclipse prayer

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the sunnah rulings of the eclipse prayer and list its conditions and recommended acts.

Core Idea
Eclipse prayer is a confirmed sunnah, not obligatory, with unique features like no adhan and no khutbah.

Eclipses are natural signs of Allah's power. The Hanafi school prescribes a specific prayer for solar and lunar eclipses, distinguished by its absence of adhan, iqamah, and sermon, and by its extended recitations. This lesson details the rulings as set forth in Nur al-Idah, covering when it is performed, how it is announced, and what acts are recommended.

Guided Path
1
Identify the act and its ruling level
The eclipse prayer is classified as sunnah muakkadah (confirmed sunnah) in the Hanafi school. Recognize that it is not fard or wajib.
This distinguishes it from obligatory prayers; missing it is not sinful but is a loss of reward.
2
List the conditions (shurut) for congregational performance
The prayer is performed in congregation led by the same imam who leads Friday prayers or someone appointed by the authority. The eclipse must be occurring.
If the imam is not present, people pray individually.
3
Note the pillars (arkan) of the prayer
It consists of two rak'ahs, each with standing (qiyam), bowing (ruku'), and prostration (sujud), just like a regular nafl prayer.
No additional rukn beyond normal prayer structure.
4
Memorize the sunnan (recommended acts) specific to eclipse prayer
Lengthening recitation, bowing, and prostration; no adhan/iqamah; no loud recitation; no khutbah; calling 'As-Salatu Jami'ah'; imam's du'a after prayer until the eclipse clears.
These are sunnan, not fard. Omitting them does not invalidate the prayer.
5
Understand the alternative when imam is absent
If the Friday imam (or appointee) is not available, the community prays individually (furāda), not in congregation.
This ruling also applies to lunar eclipse, severe darkness, strong winds, and fears.

Learning Objective

Core Idea

Understanding the expiations for Hajj violations is essential for any pilgrim. This passage from Nur al-Idah systematically lists common offenses—from cutting a nail to killing game—and their required compensations. As you study, focus on how the Hanafi school ties the severity of the violation to the type of expiation.

Identify the violation category

2

Commentary

The text first covers violations that require a dam (blood): cutting a nail (each nail half a sa' of food, but if total reaches value of a dam, he may reduce as he wishes), performing tawaf with hadath (impurity) or janabah (major impurity) — tawaf in janabah requires a sheep.
