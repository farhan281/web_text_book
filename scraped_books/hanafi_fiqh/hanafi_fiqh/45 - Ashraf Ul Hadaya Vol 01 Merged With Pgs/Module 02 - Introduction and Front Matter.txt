Defining Adhan: Linguistic and Shari'i Meanings
Introducing adhan with definitions and sources.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to distinguish between the linguistic and shar'i definitions of adhan and identify the source of its specific wording.

Core Idea
Adhan is linguistically any announcement; in Shari’ah it is the prescribed call to prayer, whose wording comes only from the Sunnah.

This opening of the chapter on adhan establishes the conceptual and juristic foundation. Before studying the rulings, we must understand what adhan means in Arabic and what it means in Islamic law, and where its precise formulation comes from. The author also explains why the chapter on prayer times precedes this one.

Guided Path
1
Understand the Chapter Order
The author placed adhan after prayer times because adhan announces the entry of time. Grasp this logical sequence: first the times, then their announcement.
This reflects the principle that means follow ends.
2
Define Adhan Linguistically
In language, adhan means i'lam (announcement) and i'lan (proclamation). The Quran uses it in this general sense, as in 'wa adhanun min Allahi wa rasulihi' and 'wa adhin fi al-nasi bil-hajj'.
The linguistic meaning is broader than the technical.
3
Define Adhan in Shari'ah
In Islamic law, adhan is giving news of the entry of prayer time with specific words in a specific manner. It is a defined act of worship, not just any announcement.
The technical definition is more precise and restricted.
4
Identify the Source of Its Specifics
The obligation (asl) of adhan is proven by both the Quran and Sunnah, but the exact wording and form (ta'yin) are established solely by the Sunnah (hadith). No Quranic verse prescribes the specific phrases.
This is a key usul point: the proof of the thing vs. its detail.

Chapter of Adhan
Translation: This is the chapter on the rulings of adhan.
Commentary: Since the adhan is the announcement of the entry of time, therefore the times were mentioned first, and after that the adhan is mentioned. Adhan, in language, means announcement and proclamation; then, by predominant usage, it came to be used for the announcement of prayer. Thus, whenever the word adhan is used, it means the announcement of prayer. In this linguistic sense, the words adhan and adhin are used in the sayings of Allah: 'And an announcement from Allah and His Messenger to the people on the day of the greater Hajj' and 'And proclaim to the people the Hajj; they will come to you on foot.' In Shari'ah, adhan is giving news of the entry of prayer time with specific words in a specific manner. The proof of adhan itself is from both verses and hadith, but its specific form is only from hadith. (Verses:) The saying of Allah: 'And when...'

Commentary
This is front matter introducing the chapter on adhan. It explains the logical order: first the times of prayer were discussed, then the announcement of those times. The linguistic meaning of adhan is announcement, and in Shari'ah it refers to the precise formulation used to indicate that prayer time has entered. The obligation of adhan is established by Quran and Sunnah, but the specific words come from hadith. The citation of Quranic verses shows the linguistic usage; the actual fiqh rulings will follow in the chapter.

Key Terms
اذان (adhan): The call to prayer, consisting of specific phrases announced at the beginning of each prayer time.
اعلام (i'lam): Announcement, making known.
اعلان (i'lan): Public proclamation.
