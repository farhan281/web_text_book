Al-Hakim al-Shahid: A Pillar of Hanafi Scholarship
Biographical sketch of a Hanafi authority

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the key biographical details of al-Hakim al-Shahid and explain his contribution to the Hanafi madhhab.

Core Idea
Al-Hakim al-Shahid: a Hanafi jurist and hadith master whose works became usul al-madhhab after Imam Muhammad.

Who are the scholars whose works we rely on besides Imam Muhammad? Al-Hakim al-Shahid is one such towering figure. His biography not only inspires but also helps us understand the chain of transmission and authority in Hanafi fiqh. In this lesson, we will meet a man who memorized 60,000 hadiths and authored foundational texts, all while praying for martyrdom—a prayer that was granted.

Guided Path
1
Identify the full name and lineage
Extract the complete name as given: Abu al-Fadl Muhammad ibn Muhammad ibn Ahmad ibn Abdullah... This establishes his scholarly lineage.
The name shows he is a Marwazi (from Merv), a Hanafi.
2
Note his scholarly qualifications
He is described as a hafiz of hadith (memorizer) and a mutajarr fiqh (practicing jurist). He had 60,000 hadiths on the tip of his tongue.
This contrasts with pure hadith scholars; he was both a muhaddith and a faqih.
3
Recognize his teachers and students
His contemporaries include al-Hakim al-Naysaburi (author of al-Mustadrak) who narrated from him. This shows his stature.
Do not confuse al-Hakim al-Shahid with al-Hakim al-Naysaburi—they belong to different madhhabs.
4
Identify his major works
Al-Muntaqa and al-Kafi are his two renowned works. They are considered principles of the madhhab (usul al-madhhab) after Imam Muhammad's books.
This means they are authoritative sources for deriving rulings.
5
Recall his martyrdom
He consistently prayed for shahadah, and was martyred in 334 AH due to a false accusation while reciting the Quran.
His death reflects the trials faced by early scholars.

Commentary
This is a biographical note from the Urdu commentary on al-Hakim al-Shahid, whose full name is Abu al-Fadl Muhammad ibn Muhammad al-Marwazi al-Hanafi. He was a prominent Hanafi jurist and hadith scholar, known for his immense memory (60,000 hadiths). His teachers and contemporaries included the famous hadith scholar al-Hakim al-Naysaburi (author of al-Mustadrak). His works al-Muntaqa and al-Kafi are highly regarded in the Hanafi school as foundational texts after the works of Imam Muhammad al-Shaybani. He was martyred in 334 AH due to a false accusation, while engaged in recitation of the Quran, after having consistently prayed for martyrdom. This biography reminds students of the sacrifice and scholarship of the early Hanafi authorities.

Key Terms
حاكم شھید (Al-Ḥākim al-Shahīd): Abu al-Fadl Muhammad ibn Muhammad al-Marwazi (d. 334 AH), a Hanafi jurist, hadith master, and author of al-Muntaqa and al-Kafi.
منتقى (Al-Muntaqā): One of his works, a compilation of Islamic law.
كافي (Al-Kāfī): Another major work of al-Hakim al-Shahid, used as a reference in Hanafi fiqh.
