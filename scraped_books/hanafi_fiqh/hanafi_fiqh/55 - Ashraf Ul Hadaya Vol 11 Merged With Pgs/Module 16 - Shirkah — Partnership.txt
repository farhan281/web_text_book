Sulh for Excess in Joint Manumission: The Textual Value Principle
Ruling on batil excess in sulh for manumission compensation

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the ruling on sulh with excess in joint manumission and explain the Hanafi reasoning distinguishing textual value.

Core Idea
Excess over the fixed slave value in sulh is batil.

This passage from Al-Hidayah addresses a specific case of joint slave manumission where one co-owner frees the slave and the other seeks compensation via sulh. The ruling that any excess over half the value is void is agreed upon, but the underlying reasoning reveals a key principle: certain values are textually fixed by Shari'ah and cannot be exceeded.

Guided Path
1
Identify the case and the ruling
The case involves two co-owners of a slave. One frees the slave while wealthy, and the other makes a sulh agreement for more than half the slave's value. The ruling is that any excess over half is void (batil).
2
Note the agreement among the Three Imams
Abu Hanifa, Abu Yusuf, and Muhammad all agree that the excess is batil. The difference lies only in the reasoning behind it.
This is an intra-Hanafi ikhtilaf in istidlal, not in the final hukm.
3
Understand the Sahibayn's reasoning
The two disciples base their ruling on a general principle explained earlier (likely regarding prohibition of excess in sulh). The passage does not repeat it but references it.
The exact earlier case is not given here; it refers to a prior discussion.
4
Understand Abu Hanifa's distinction
Abu Hanifa argues that the value (qima) in manumission is textually fixed (mansus alayha) by Shari'ah, based on the hadith requiring the slave to be valued fairly. Therefore, exceeding that fixed value is impermissible, unlike cases where the value is not textually fixed.
The Shari'ah's determination is as binding as a judge's ruling.
5
Apply the distinction to other cases
If the sulh is made for goods (urud) of a different type, it is valid because the excess is not apparent. Also, cases where the value is not textually fixed may allow excess.
The distinction hinges on whether the Shari'ah has set a specific value.

قَالَ وَإِذَا كَانَ الْعَبْدُ بَيْنَ رَجُلَيْنِ اعْتَقَهُ اَحَدُهُمَا وَهُوَ مُوْسِرٌ فَصَالَحَهُ الْآخَرُ عَلَى أَكْثَرِ مِنْ نِصْفِ قِيمَةِ فَالْفَضْلُ بَاطِلٌ وَهَذَا بِالْاِتِّفَاقِ أَمَّا عِنْدَهُمَا فَلِمَا بَيَّنَا وَالْفَرْقُ لَابِي حَنِيفَةٌ أَنَّ الْقِيْمَةَ فِي الْعِلْقِ مَنْصُوصٌ عَلَيْهَا وَتَقْدِيرُ الشَّرْعِ لَا يَكُونُ دُونَ تَقْدِيرِ الْقَاضِي فَلَا يَجُوزُ الزِيَادَةُ عَلَيْهِ بِخِلَافِ مَا تَقَدَّمَ لِأَنَّهَا غَيْرُ مَنْصُوصٍ عَلَيْهَا وَإِنْ صَالَحَهُ عَلَى عُرُوضِ جَازَ لِمَا بَيَّنَا أَنَّهُ لَا يَظْهَرُ الْفَضْلُ
He said: When a slave is between two men, one of them frees him while he is wealthy, and the other makes sulh with him for more than half of his value, then the excess is void (batil). This is by agreement. As for the two [disciples], it is for what we have explained. The distinction for Abu Hanifa is that the value in manumission is textually fixed (mansus alayha), and the determination of the Shari'ah is not below the determination of a judge, so exceeding it is not allowed, unlike the previous case because [the value] is not textually fixed. If he makes sulh for goods (urud), it is valid for what we have explained that the excess does not appear.

Commentary
This passage addresses a case where a slave is jointly owned by two people. One of the co-owners frees the slave while being wealthy (musir). The other co-owner then makes a sulh with the freeing co-owner for an amount greater than half the slave's value. The ruling is that the excess over half the value is invalid (batil), and this is agreed upon by all (bil-ittifaq), meaning Abu Hanifa, Abu Yusuf, and Muhammad. The reasoning according to Sahibayn is what was previously explained regarding the prohibition of excess. For Abu Hanifa, the distinction is that the value (qima) in the context of manumission ('itq) is textually fixed (mansus alayha) by the Shari'ah (through the hadith requiring the slave to be evaluated and the co-owner compensated accordingly). The determination of the Shari'ah is no less binding than the determination of a judge; therefore, exceeding that textual value is not permitted. This differs from the earlier case (the Jewish cloth) where the value was not textually fixed. However, if the sulh is made for goods (urud) of a different genus, it is valid because the excess is not apparent.

Key Terms
قيمة منصوص عليها (qima mansus alayha): A value that is textually fixed by the Shari'ah, such as the value of a slave in manumission by a wealthy co-owner.
عتق (itq): Manumission or freeing of a slave.
