Mapping the Fiqh: From Guarantee to Divorce
Table of contents listing chapters and sub-topics.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the sequence of fiqh topics from Kitab al-Kafalah to Kitab al-Talaq as organized in the Hanafi curriculum and explain the logical progression of these chapters.

Core Idea
Hanafi fiqh is arranged in a logical sequence from financial transactions to family law.

This table of contents from al-Jawhara al-Nayyira reveals the structure of Hanafi fiqh as codified by al-Quduri. Notice how the topics progress: from contracts of security (kafalah, hawalah, sulh) through property transfers (hibah, waqf), torts (ghasb), trusts (wadi'ah, 'ariyah), personal status (laqit, luqtah, khuntha, etc.), agricultural partnerships, and culminating in marriage and divorce. Recognizing this architecture helps you navigate the madhhab holistically and see the interdependencies of different areas of fiqh.

Guided Path
1
Identify the broad divisions
Scan the list and group the books into categories: financial transactions (kafalah, hawalah, sulh, hibah, waqf), property and torts (ghasb), trusts and personal property (wadi'ah, 'ariyah), personal status (laqit, luqtah, khuntha, mafqud, abiq, ihya' al-mawat, ma'dhun), agricultural partnerships (muzara'ah, musaqah), marriage (nikah), and divorce (talaq).
This grouping is not explicit but can be inferred from the content of each kitab.
2
Trace the logical progression
Observe how the sequence moves from obligations and contracts of security (kafalah), to transfers of debt (hawalah), to settlement and compromise (sulh), then to voluntary gifts (hibah) and endowments (waqf). After property matters, it covers wrongful taking (ghasb), then entrustments (wadi'ah, 'ariyah). Then follows personal status issues like foundlings and missing persons, then agricultural partnerships, finally marriage and divorce.
This order reflects the typical arrangement in Hanafi mutun, beginning with financial dealings and ending with family law.
3
Note the sub-topics
Under Kitab al-Nikah, many sub-topics are listed (e.g., prohibited women, marriage in ihram, guardian permission, minimum dowry, etc.). This shows the depth covered within each major book. Recognizing that each kitab contains multiple subsidiary issues helps in locating specific rulings quickly.
Sub-topics are often listed in the index but not always exhaustive.
4
Recognize the curriculum
This sequence is standard in Hanafi madrasa curricula. Memorizing the order of the books (kutub) aids in recalling where a particular ruling falls. For example, knowing that Kitab al-Nikah appears after Kitab al-Musaqah helps contextualize marriage regulations within the broader fiqh framework.
The order may vary slightly between different mukhtasar works, but the general progression is consistent.

Commentary
This passage is a table of contents (fihris) for the current volume of al-Jawhara al-Nayyira, a commentary on Mukhtasar al-Quduri. It lists the major books (كتاب) and subtopics covered from Kitab al-Kafalah (Book of Guarantee) through Kitab al-Talaq (Book of Divorce). The numbers that originally appeared (likely page references) have been omitted for clarity. This index shows the systematic organization of Hanafi fiqh according to the order of al-Quduri's matn. The topics include financial transactions (kafalah, hawalah, sulh, hibah, waqf), property crimes (ghasb), trusts (wadi'ah, 'ariyah), personal status (laqit, luqtah, khuntha, mafqud, abiq, ihya' al-mawat, ma'dhun), agricultural partnerships (muzara'ah, musaqah), marriage and its rulings (nikah, mahram, mahr, khuluww, mut'ah, kafa'ah, shurut, rada'), and divorce (talaq). This index serves as a roadmap for the student of Hanafi fiqh.

Key Terms
كتاب (kitāb): A major section or chapter of the fiqh work, typically covering a broad set of related rulings.
فهرس الموضوعات (fihris al-mawḍūʿāt): Table of contents or index of subjects.
