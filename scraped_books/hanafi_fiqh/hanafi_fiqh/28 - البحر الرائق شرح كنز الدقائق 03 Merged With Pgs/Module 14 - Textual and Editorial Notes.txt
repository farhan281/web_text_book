Parsing 'من الرضاع': Hal vs. Sifah in Fosterage Constructions
Grammatical dissection of a fosterage phrase

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to distinguish between hal and sifah in Arabic idafah constructions and apply that distinction to interpret legal phrases involving fosterage.

Core Idea
The definiteness of the noun determines whether a following prepositional phrase is hal or sifah, which changes the legal implication.

In Hanafi fiqh texts, precise grammatical parsing can alter the ruling. The phrase 'أم أخته من الرضاع' (the mother of his sister through fosterage) is ambiguous: does 'من الرضاع' describe the mother, the sister, or both? This passage from Al-Bahr al-Raiq dissects the grammar to show how definiteness governs the interpretation.

Guided Path
1
Identify the components
Break down the construction 'أم أخته من الرضاع' into mudaf (أم), mudaf ilayh (أخته), and the prepositional phrase (من الرضاع). Note the definiteness of each element.
أم is definite by idafah; أخته is also definite by pronoun.
2
Apply the hal vs sifah rule
In Arabic, a prepositional phrase after a definite noun is a حال (circumstantial adverb), while after an indefinite noun it is a صفة (adjective). Here, 'أم' is definite, so 'من الرضاع' must be a hal describing the mother.
The text also notes that 'أخته' has no motivator for hal, so it cannot be described by a hal.
3
Consider both mudaf and mudaf ilayh
From a semantic perspective, 'من الرضاع' can attach to either element or both. Grammatically, only attachment to the definite 'أم' as hal is straightforward.
The author cites grammarians allowing hal from mudaf ilayh in special cases.
4
Determine the legal effect
If 'من الرضاع' modifies 'أم', she is the foster-mother; if it modifies 'أخته', she is the foster-sister. This changes the prohibition status in marriage and inheritance.
Refer to Fath al-Qadir for similar examples.

Commentary
This passage is a grammatical dissection of the phrase 'إلا أم أخته من الرضاع' (except the mother of his sister through fosterage). The author explains that from a meaning perspective, the phrase 'من الرضاع' can attach to either 'أم' (mother) alone, 'أخته' (his sister) alone, or both. But from an i'rab (syntax) perspective, since 'أم' is definite, 'من الرضاع' functions as a حال (circumstantial adverb) describing 'أم', not as a صفة (adjective). The author then cites various grammarians on when a prepositional phrase can be a حال or صفة, concluding that because the idafah (possessive construction) here is like a generic definite, it can be parsed as either. This has legal implications: if 'من الرضاع' attaches only to 'أخته', then 'أم أخته' is the mother of his foster-sister, which changes the prohibition ruling.

Key Terms
حال (hal): An adverbial circumstance describing the subject or object, typically in the accusative, and always after a definite noun.
صفة (sifah): An adjective describing a noun, agreeing in definiteness; follows an indefinite noun.
مضاف إليه (mudaf ilayh): The second term of a genitive construction (idafah), always in the genitive case.
معرفة (ma'rifah): Definite noun, which triggers hal when followed by a prepositional phrase.
نكرة (nakirah): Indefinite noun, which triggers sifat when followed by a prepositional phrase.
