Ruling on Disliked Days for 'Umrah
States that 'Umrah is valid any time but disliked on five specific days.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the five disliked days for 'Umrah and explain the ruling of karahah tahrimiyyah, including the exception for the qarin.

Core Idea
'Umrah is valid year-round but disliked on five days — its performance then is valid but sinful.

This passage from Al-Bahr al-Ra'iq clarifies a key ruling: while 'Umrah may be performed any day of the year, certain days carry legal dislike. Understanding the distinction between validity and permissibility is essential for a Muslim seeking to perform 'Umrah, especially during the Hajj season.

Guided Path
1
Identify the ruling and its scope
State that 'Umrah is valid throughout the year but disliked (makruh) on five specific days: Day of 'Arafah, Day of Nahr, and the three days of Tashriq.
This is the relied-upon Hanafi position.
2
Locate the dalil
The proof is the hadith of 'Aisha (ra) which explicitly excepts these days from general permissibility.
The hadith is ahad, thus zanni al-thubut, but its meaning is clear.
3
Understand the level of dislike
In Hanafi fiqh, absolute karahah (when mentioned without qualification) implies makruh tahrimi – prohibitively disliked, close to haram.
This is supported by the explicit prohibition in the hadith.
4
Note the intra-Hanafi ikhtilaf
Abu Yusuf held that dislike does not apply before noon on the Day of 'Arafah, but the relied-upon view (mufta bihi) is that it applies all day.
The tarjih is from Al-Marghinani in Al-Hidayah.
5
Apply the exception for the qarin
The dislike applies only to non-qarin pilgrims. A qarin who combines Hajj and 'Umrah with one ihram is exempt because his 'Umrah is not independently initiated.
Some authorities restrict this exception to the Day of 'Arafah, not all five days.

Commentary
This passage is a ruling with proof. It first states the ruling that 'Umrah is valid at any time of the year but is disliked on five specific days: the Day of 'Arafah, the Day of Nahr (10th Dhul Hijjah), and the three days of Tashriq (11th, 12th, 13th). The commentator then provides proof from the Prophet's practice: he performed four 'Umrahs, all in Dhu al-Qa'dah, showing it is not restricted to a particular season except for the one combined with Hajj. The passage also discusses a dispute with Imam Malik regarding the nature of the Prophet's 'Umrat al-Qada', siding with Abu Hanifa that it was a make-up for the missed 'Umrah of Hudaybiyyah. The dislike is specified as makruh tahrimi (prohibitively disliked) based on the hadith of 'Aisha and Ibn 'Abbas. The ruling applies equally to residents of Makkah and others. It also notes that the best time for 'Umrah is Ramadan, based on the hadith that 'Umrah in Ramadan equals a Hajj. Finally, it mentions a qualification from Fatwa Qadikhan that the dislike applies only to non-qarin; the qarin is exempt because his 'Umrah is part of Hajj and not independently initiated. The commentator suggests this exception should be restricted to the Day of 'Arafah, not all five days.

Key Terms
عمرة (‘Umrah): A pilgrimage to Makkah performed at any time, involving tawaf, sa'y, and halq or taqsir.
كراهة (Karāhah): Legal dislike; when absolute, it implies makruh tahrimi (prohibitively disliked) in Hanafi fiqh.
قارن (Qārin): A pilgrim who combines Hajj and ‘Umrah with one ihram.

Ruling on 'Umrah: Sunnah Mu'akkadah vs Wajib
Commentary on the matn statement that 'Umrah is a Sunnah

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the Hanafi ruling on 'Umrah and explain why it is a sunnah mu'akkadah rather than wajib, citing the key evidence and reconciling the Quranic verse.

Core Idea
'Umrah is a confirmed Sunnah in the Hanafi school, based on the Prophet's explicit statement that it is not obligatory.

In this passage from Al-Bahr al-Raiq, the commentator clarifies the Hanafi position on 'Umrah, addressing an internal debate. While some Hanafi scholars considered 'Umrah wajib, the relied-upon view is that it is a highly recommended Sunnah. We will examine the evidence and reasoning behind this position.

Guided Path
1
Identify the ruling and its classification
State the relied-upon Hanafi ruling on 'Umrah: sunnah mu'akkadah. Note that some scholars held it is wajib, but the majority view is Sunnah.
The passage calls it 'sunnah mu'akkadah' and mentions that the correct view in the school is this.
2
Locate the intra-Hanafi ikhtilaf
Identify the minority position that 'Umrah is wajib, cited from al-Jawharah and al-Bada'i'. Understand that the commentator prefers the majority view.
The passage says 'Qila bi wujubiha wa sahhabahu fi al-Jawharah wa ikhtarah fi al-Bada'i'.
3
Examine the hadith evidence
The primary evidence is the hadith from Jabir (narrated by al-Tirmidhi) where the Prophet explicitly said 'Umrah is not obligatory and performing it is better.
The hadith is a clear negation of obligation ( 'la' ) and encouragement ( 'huwa afdal' ).
4
Reconcile the Quranic verse
The verse 'And complete the Hajj and Umrah for Allah' (2:196) is interpreted as referring to completion after commencement, not initiation. The obligation to complete only applies once one has started, so it does not prove initial obligation.
The commentator says 'al-itmam ba'd al-shuru' wa al-kalam fima qabl al-shuru'.'

Commentary
This passage is a commentary on the matn's statement that 'Umrah is a Sunnah. It clarifies that the relied-upon position in the Hanafi school is that 'Umrah is a confirmed Sunnah (Sunnah Mu'akkadah), though some scholars like those of al-Jawharah and al-Bada'i' considered it obligatory (wajib). The commentator reconciles by noting that those who call it Sunnah do not necessarily negate obligation, but the stronger transmission from Imam Muhammad (in al-Mukhtasar) states it is a supererogatory act (tatawwu'). The proof is the hadith of Jabir where the Prophet explicitly said 'Umrah is not obligatory, and performing it is better. The verse 'and complete the Hajj and 'Umrah for Allah' refers to completing after commencement, not initiating, as one is bound once they begin. The key point: 'Umrah is a Sunnah once in a lifetime, and its best time is Ramadan, where it equals Hajj in reward. It also notes that performing an independent 'Umrah is different from combining it with Hajj (Qiran) which is better for Hajj.

Key Terms
سنة مؤكدة (sunnah mu'akkadah): A confirmed Sunnah; an act that is highly recommended but not obligatory in the Hanafi view.
عمرة (umrah): The lesser pilgrimage to Makkah involving specific rituals (ihram, tawaf, sa'i, halq/taqsir).
