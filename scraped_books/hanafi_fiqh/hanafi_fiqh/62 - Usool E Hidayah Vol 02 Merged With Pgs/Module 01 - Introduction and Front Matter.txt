Introduction to the 16 Usool of Kitab al-Nikah
Table of contents listing 16 Hanafi marriage maxims.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the 16 usool from Kitab al-Nikah and explain the practical import of at least two principles.

Core Idea
The 16 usool of Nikah are maxims that anchor Hanafi marriage law, derived from the Hidayah.

This lesson orients you to the structure of Usool al-Hidayah Volume 2. The table of contents lists 16 fundamental principles (usool) that govern Hanafi marriage law. While the full explanation lies ahead, these maxims—like 'One person may officiate both sides of a marriage' and 'Fosterage creates lineage prohibitions'—are the backbone of the rulings you will study. Let us unpack their significance.

Guided Path
1
Identify the three sections
The usool are grouped: Kitab al-Nikah (1-6), Fasl fi Bayan al-Muharramat (7-15), and Bab fi al-Awliya' wa al-Akfa' (16). Note the page numbers scattered between them.
The page numbers indicate where each principle is discussed in the book.
2
Read Principle 1 closely
Principle 1: 'One person may officiate both sides of a marriage contract unlike a sale.' This means in Nikah, the same guardian can act for both bride and groom, which is impermissible in sale. Ask: Why this difference?
Consider the role of walayah and the nature of the marriage contract.
3
Read Principle 7 closely
Principle 7: 'What is forbidden by lineage is also forbidden by fosterage.' This is the Hanafi rule of rada' (foster relationships). Foster siblings are like blood siblings for marriage prohibitions.
The hadith 'Yahrumu min ar-rada'i ma yahrumu min an-nasab' supports this.
4
Scan the remaining principles
Quickly review Principles 2-6 and 8-16. Note their topics: causality, witnessing, contract effects, prohibited women, guardianship. You will study them in detail later.
Do not memorize now; this is a map for future lessons.

Commentary
This is the table of contents for the second volume of Usool al-Hidayah, listing 16 usul (legal principles) from the Book of Marriage. It is divided into three sections: Kitab al-Nikah (principles 1-6), Fasl fi Bayan al-Muharramat (principles 7-15), and Bab fi al-Awliya' wa al-Akfa' (principle 16). Each principle is a key maxim in Hanafi marriage law. For example, principle (1) states that one person can act as both guardian and agent in marriage, unlike sale contracts. Principle (7) states the well-known rule that fosterage creates the same prohibitions as lineage. The page numbers scattered throughout indicate where each principle is explained in the book.

Key Terms
أصول (usool): Legal principles or maxims derived from the Hidayah
نكاح (nikah): Marriage contract in Islamic law.
رضاعة (rada'ah): Fosterage; breastfeeding by a woman other than the mother.
