Combining Substitute and Original is Not Permissible
Legal principle forbidding combining substitute and original

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the principle that combining a substitute with its original is impermissible and apply it to a real-case scenario such as tayammum and wudu.

Core Idea
A substitute cannot be combined with the original — when the original becomes available, the substitute ceases to be valid.

This lesson introduces a foundational legal principle: when something serves as a replacement (badal) for an original act or condition (mubdal), you cannot perform both simultaneously. The substitute is only valid in the absence of the original. For example, tayammum substitutes for wudu when water is unavailable; once water is present, tayammum is no longer viable.

Guided Path
1
Identify the act and its substitute
In a given scenario, determine which act or condition is the original (mubdal) and which is the substitute (badal). For instance, wudu is the original and tayammum is its substitute in taharah.
2
Check if both are being performed simultaneously
Determine whether the substitute and original are being used at the same time. The principle only applies when both exist concurrently.
3
Apply the principle
If both are being combined, the ruling is that the act is not permissible. The substitute becomes invalid, and the original must be used.

Commentary
This usool states a general principle: when something stands in for something else (like tayammum for wudu, or kaffarah for fasting), one cannot simultaneously perform both the original and the substitute. For example, if one has done tayammum due to lack of water, and then water becomes available during the prayer, he cannot continue with tayammum and also use water; the substitute is only valid in the absence of the original.

Key Terms
البدل (al-badal): Substitute; alternative. E.g., tayammum is badal for wudu.
المبدل (al-mubdal): Original that is substituted. E.g., wudu is mubdal for tayammum.
