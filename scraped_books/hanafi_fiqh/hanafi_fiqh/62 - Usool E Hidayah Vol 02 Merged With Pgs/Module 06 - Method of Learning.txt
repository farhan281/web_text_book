The Prevalent is Like the Actual (الغالب كالواقع)
Legal maxim establishing presumption of prevalence

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the legal maxim that the prevalent is like the actual and apply it to a fiqh scenario.

Core Idea
The law treats what normally occurs as if it were certain.

This lesson introduces a foundational legal maxim in Hanafi fiqh: 'Indeed, the prevalent is like the actual.' It means that in cases where exact knowledge is difficult or impossible, the law relies on what is most common or usual. This principle is used in many areas of fiqh to establish presumptions.

Guided Path
1
Memorize the phrase
Learn the Arabic maxim and its English translation: 'Indeed, the prevalent is like the actual.'
2
Understand the meaning
The prevalent (ghalib) is treated as if it were reality (waqi') for legal purposes when certainty is lacking.
3
Recognize the scope
This maxim applies across fiqh: ibadat, muamalat, judiciary, etc. It is a tool for istihsan.

Commentary
This is a well-known legal maxim in Hanafi usul. In matters where exact knowledge is difficult, the law relies on what is normally or predominantly the case. For example, if a woman gives birth after six months of marriage, that is the minimum period for viability, so the child is attributed to the husband even if there is doubt. The prevalent (ghalib) is treated as if it were certain.

Key Terms
الغالب (al-ghalib): What is prevalent, majority, or most common.
الواقع (al-waqi'): What actually occurs; reality.

Key Takeaway
The legal maxim 'al-ghalib ka al-waqi'' is a foundational tool for establishing presumptions in Hanafi fiqh, allowing law to function where certainty is impossible.
