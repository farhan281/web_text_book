Nafaqa from a Safih's Wealth for Dependents
Ruling that nafaqa for family must be paid from a safih's wealth

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the ruling on nafaqa from a safih's wealth and identify the party responsible for its payment.

Core Idea
A safih's dependents must be supported from his wealth; the guardian is obligated to spend on their nafaqa.

When a person is declared safih (foolish squanderer) and placed under interdiction, his wealth is not frozen for basic necessities. This lesson clarifies that the appointed guardian must use the safih's wealth to fulfill the wajib maintenance of his children, wife, and certain relatives, ensuring that the dependents are not left without support.

Guided Path
1
Identify the act and its ruling
What is the act being commanded? The text says 'he spends' (ywnfq) on children, wife, and those whose nafaqa is wajib. This indicates that spending on these dependents is obligatory (wajib).
The agent is the guardian (judge or trustee), not the safih himself.
2
Identify the source of funds
The funds come from the safih's own wealth. Despite his inability to manage finances, his wealth must be used for his dependents' nafaqa.
This is an exception to the general rule of interdiction.
3
List the eligible dependents
The text mentions: his children, his wife, and those relatives (dhawi al-arham) whose maintenance is wajib upon him. This includes parents and possibly others depending on conditions.
The specific list of wajib relatives is detailed in other fiqh texts.
4
Understand the role of the guardian
The guardian (appointed by judge or family) manages the safih's wealth and is personally responsible to ensure nafaqa is paid. The safih cannot be trusted to do so.
The guardian should ideally be an amin (trustworthy person) to prevent waste.

Commentary
This ruling clarifies that despite interdiction, the wealth of a safih must be used to fulfill his obligations of maintenance (nafaqa) towards his immediate family: his children, his wife, and other relatives (e.g., parents) whose support is wajib. This is because providing for dependents is a primary need (hajat asliyyah), and the guardian (judge or trustee) is authorized to spend from the safih's wealth for such purposes. The sharh recommends that the wealth be given to a trustworthy person (amin) to manage the spending, to prevent waste by the safih.

Key Terms
نفقہ (nafaqah): Maintenance, including food, clothing, and shelter for dependents.
سفيه (safih): A foolish person who squanders wealth, placed under interdiction (hajr).
