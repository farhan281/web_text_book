Hajj of the Interdicted Safih: Permission and Financial Safeguards
Ruling on Hajj for the interdicted safih

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to apply the Hanafi ruling that a safih may perform obligatory Hajj but the judge must entrust his expenses to a trustworthy person.

Core Idea
A safih is not prevented from Hajj, but his expenses are entrusted to a reliable pilgrim.

This lesson examines the ruling for a person who is under financial interdiction (safih) yet wishes to perform Hajj. Although the safih is restricted from managing his own wealth due to poor judgment, the obligation of Hajj remains. The Hanafi school provides a balanced solution: the judge does not give the funds directly to the safih but to a trustworthy person who spends them appropriately. This preserves both the religious duty and the financial protection.

Guided Path
1
Identify the act and the subject
The safih intends to perform Hajjat al-Islam (obligatory Hajj). Recognize that this is a fard obligation, not an optional act.
2
Recognize the primary ruling
The safih is not prevented from going. Despite his interdiction, the religious duty takes precedence.
3
Identify the financial safeguard
The judge does not hand over the travel expenses directly to the safih due to the risk of waste.
4
Determine the trustee mechanism
The judge gives the money to a trustworthy person (thiqa) from among the pilgrims, who will spend it on the safih's needs during the journey.
The trustee must be reliable and preferably already going for Hajj.
5
Understand the wisdom
This mechanism ensures the safih can fulfill the obligation while protecting his wealth from mismanagement.

Commentary
This ruling addresses the case where a safih wishes to perform the obligatory Hajj (Hajjat al-Islam). Because Hajj is a primary need in religion, he is not prevented from going. However, due to his financial imprudence, the judge (qadi) does not give him the travel expenses directly. Instead, the judge entrusts the funds to a reliable and trustworthy person (thiqa) among the pilgrims, who will spend the money on the safih's needs along the journey. This prevents the safih from wasting the money while still enabling him to fulfill the religious obligation.

Key Terms
حجة الإسلام (hajjat al-islam): The obligatory Hajj that is fard upon every capable Muslim once in a lifetime.
ثقة (thiqa): A trustworthy person; one who is reliable to handle funds properly.
