Ruling on Selling During Friday Adhan
Stating the ruling that selling during the Friday adhan is makruh

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the Hanafi ruling on selling during the Friday adhan and explain why the sale remains valid despite the prohibition.

Core Idea
Selling during the Friday adhan is prohibited but the sale contract remains valid.

This lesson examines a specific ruling that combines two disciplines: salah (prayer) and buyu' (sales). The Friday adhan signals the obligation to attend Jumu'ah prayer, and engaging in sales at that time is prohibited by the Quran. However, the Hanafi school holds that the prohibition does not affect the validity of the contract itself. Understanding this distinction is crucial for avoiding both sin and unnecessary disputes in transactions.

Guided Path
1
Identify the act and its ruling
The act is selling (al-bay') after the Friday adhan has been called. The ruling is makruh (disliked), specifically makruh tahrimi according to the Hanafi school.
Makruh tahrimi means prohibitively disliked, close to haram.
2
Cite the Quranic evidence
The author quotes Surah al-Jumu'ah (62:9): 'O you who believe! When the call is made for the prayer on Friday, then hasten to the remembrance of Allah and leave off selling.' The imperative 'leave off' (dharu) indicates prohibition.
The verse is qat'i al-thubut (certain transmission) and qat'i al-dalalah (clear meaning).
3
Analyze the wajh al-dalalah (legal implication)
The prohibition is on the act of selling at that specific time, not on the contract itself. The contract's elements (offer, acceptance, consideration) are not inherently defective; the prohibition is external, tied to the obligation of Jumu'ah prayer.
Hanafi usul distinguishes between prohibitions attached to the act and those attached to the contract. Here it is the former.
4
Determine the effect on the contract
Because the prohibition does not affect the contract's essence, the sale is valid (sahih) but the seller commits a sin if he sells without a valid excuse. The contract is not fasid or batil.
This is the mufta bihi (operative ruling) in the Hanafi school.
5
Summarize the practical implication
A sale made during the Friday adhan is legally binding; ownership transfers, and the price is due. However, the seller should repent for violating the command of Allah.
The ruling applies to both the first and second adhan? The passage says 'Friday adhan' — generally understood as the adhan that signals the start of the khutbah.

Priority of Debts in Terminal Illness: Health Debts Over Acknowledged Debts
Ruling with proof from Quran and Hadith

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the priority of debts in terminal illness and apply the rule that debts from health and known causes take precedence over a debt acknowledged during terminal illness without a cause.

Core Idea
Debts from health and known causes are paid first; an acknowledgment during illness follows only after prior debts are settled.

This lesson examines a critical ruling in Hanafi fiqh regarding the priority of debts when a person acknowledges a new debt during terminal illness. The core principle is to prevent harm to previous creditors by giving precedence to debts established during health or through clear causes. Understanding this ruling safeguards the rights of all creditors according to Islamic law.

Guided Path
1
Identify the debts involved
Distinguish between three categories: debts from the period of health, debts from known causes during illness (e.g., documented loans), and debts acknowledged during terminal illness without any specified cause.
Known causes include transactions like sales or loans that are established by clear evidence.
2
State the ruling on priority
The Hanafi position is clear: debts from health and debts with known causes are given priority over a debt acknowledged during terminal illness without a cause. The acknowledged debt is paid only after those prior debts are fully satisfied.
This is the mufta bihi (operative ruling) in the school.
3
Understand the rationale
The rationale is the suspicion of harm (ضرار) to previous creditors. Acknowledging a debt without a cause during terminal illness could be a tactic to favor one creditor over others, thereby causing loss to legitimate creditors.
This is based on the principle of preventing harm, derived from Quranic and Prophetic guidance.
4
Identify the proofs
The primary proof is the Quranic verse 4:12, which commands that bequests and debts be fulfilled without causing harm. Additionally, a hadith from Abu Hurairah warns against causing harm in wills.
The verse: 'فإن كانوا أكثر من ذلك فهم شركاء في الثلث من بعد وصية يوصى بها أو دين غير مضار' (Quran 4:12)
5
Apply the exception
If the deceased had no debts from health or known causes, then the acknowledgment during terminal illness is valid and must be paid from the estate. However, if there is any suspicion of harm, the acknowledgment is invalid.
The exception shows that the rule is based on protecting prior creditors, not on an inherent defect in the acknowledgment.

کیونکہ یہ نہ مرد ہے اور نہ عورت ۔ اگر مرد کے ساتھ کھڑا ہو تو عورت ہونے کی وجہ سے ان کی نماز مکروہ ہوگی۔ اور اگر عورت کے ساتھ کھڑا ہو تو مرد ہونے کی وجہ سے ان کی نماز خراب ہوگی۔ اس لئے دونوں کے درمیان کھڑا ہو۔
[1644] (8) And if he stands behind the imam, he stands between the row of men and women. Commentary: And if he stands behind the imam, he stands between the row of men and women. If due to a sign preference was given to one side, then its rulings would apply, but due to the absence of a sign, no preference can be given to any side, so now he will be between man and woman and intermediate rulings will apply to him. Because of this, when he stands behind the imam, he stands between the rows of men and women. Because he is neither man nor woman. If he stands with men, due to being a woman their prayer would be makruh; and if with women, due to being a man their prayer would be invalid (fasid). Therefore he stands between them.
Source pages p. 420

Commentary
This is a ruling for a khuntha mushkil in congregational prayer. Since his gender is unknown, he cannot stand in either the men's or women's row. Standing with men would be makruh if he is female, and with women would invalidate their prayer if he is male. Therefore he stands between the rows. This is an intermediate ruling.

Key Terms
صف (saff): Row in congregational prayer. The khuntha mushkil stands between the men’s and women’s rows.
