Husband's Obligation to Shroud His Wife: The Hanafi Intra-School Dispute
Details the dispute between Abu Yusuf and Muhammad on husband's duty to provide kafan for wife

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the positions of Abu Yusuf and Muhammad al-Shaybani on the husband's duty to shroud his wife, explain the basis of each view, and identify the mufta bihi position.

Core Idea
The fatwa obliges the husband to shroud his wife absolutely, rejecting Muhammad's view that death severs all marital duties.

This lesson examines a well-known internal Hanafi dispute: must a husband provide burial expenses (kafan and burial) for his deceased wife? The two primary students of Abu Hanifa — Abu Yusuf and Muhammad al-Shaybani — disagreed, and later Hanafi authorities chose one view as the basis for fatwa. The passage from Hashiyat al-Tahtawi also introduces a refinement by Ibn Amir Hajj, showing that the dispute may be narrower than it first appears.

Guided Path
1
Identify the Ikhtilaf
Pinpoint the exact legal question: Is a husband obligated to shroud and bury his wife? Note that Abu Yusuf and Muhammad give different answers, and there is also a conditional variant within Abu Yusuf's own position.
The ikhtilaf is intra-Hanafi, so both views come from the same school.
2
State Abu Yusuf's Position
Abu Yusuf holds that the husband must shroud his wife. According to the later authorities (sahib al-Mughni, etc.), this obligation is absolute: it applies even if the husband is poor and the wife is rich. There is also a variant limiting it to a poor wife, but this is not the relied-upon view.
The base text only mentions Abu Yusuf's view as conditional; the commentator upgrades it to absolute.
3
State Muhammad's Position
Muhammad al-Shaybani holds that the husband is not obligated at all, because the marital bond is severed completely at death (inqita' al-zawjiyya min kull wajh). The husband becomes like a stranger.
Muhammad's reasoning is based on the nature of marriage: it ends with death, so no obligations remain.
4
Analyze the Refinement and Tarjih
The commentator (Ibn Amir Hajj) refines the dispute: it applies only when there is no preventing factor like nushuz or if the wife dies during iddah (where nafaqa continues). The text then concludes that Abu Yusuf's absolute view is 'al-ashahh' (most correct) and upon it is the fatwa.
This shows that later Hanafi authorities actively chose between the positions, and their choice is normative for practice.

Commentary
This passage presents a dispute within the Hanafi school regarding the husband's obligation to provide burial expenses (kafan and dafn) for his wife. The base text states that according to Abu Yusuf, the husband is obligated only if the wife is poor. However, the commentator notes that the preferred view (mukhtar) of later authorities (sahib al-Mughni, etc.) is that Abu Yusuf's position obliges the husband in all cases, regardless of wealth. The text then presents Muhammad al-Shaybani's contrary view: the husband is not obligated at all because marriage ends completely with death, making the husband a stranger. The fatwa is on Abu Yusuf's view. The commentator (Ibn Amir Hajj) suggests that the dispute applies only when there is no preventing factor like nushuz (disobedience) or if the wife dies during iddah, in which case the husband's obligation may still hold by analogy to nafaqa. The ruling distinguishes the husband's liability from that of other relatives, emphasizing the ongoing maintenance obligation during iddah.

Key Terms
انقطاع الزوجية (inqita' al-zawjiyya): The complete termination of the marital bond, according to Muhammad's view.
التجهيز (al-tajhiz): Preparation of the deceased including shrouding and burial.
