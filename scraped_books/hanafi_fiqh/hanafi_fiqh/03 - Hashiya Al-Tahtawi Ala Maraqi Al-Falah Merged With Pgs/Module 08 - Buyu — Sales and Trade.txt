The Ruling on Trade During the Friday Adhan: Valid But Sinful
States the ruling and discusses contract validity

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the Hanafi ruling on selling during the Friday adhan and explain why the contract remains valid despite the prohibition.

Core Idea
Selling during the Friday adhan is a sin, but the contract is valid; the prohibition is on the act, not the transaction.

In this passage, al-Tahtawi explains the ruling on buying and selling after the Friday call to prayer. The base text states it is obligatory to leave off selling, and the commentator clarifies that this is a prohibition that carries sin but does not invalidate the contract. We will examine the Hanafi position, the evidence, and the disagreement with the Maliki and Hanbali schools.

Guided Path
1
Identify the act and the ruling
The act is buying/selling after the Friday adhan. The Hanafi ruling: it is makruh tahrimi (prohibitively disliked) for both parties.
The text also notes that some sources (like al-Hidaya) call it haram to emphasize severity.
2
Locate the dalil
The primary evidence is the Quranic command in Surah al-Jumu'ah (62:9): 'Leave off selling' (وذروا البيع).
Thubut is qat'i (Quran), dalala is zanni because it requires interpretation.
3
Trace the wajh al-dalalah
The command indicates prohibition, but the Hanafi school interprets it as non-invalidating: the sin is on the act of transacting at that time, not on the contract itself, since all essential elements of the sale are present.
The contract is considered sahih (valid) because the prohibition applies to the time, not the contract's conditions.
4
Note the ikhtilaf
Malik and Ahmad hold that the contract is batil (void), except for marriage, gift, and charity. They view the command as nullifying the transaction itself.
The Hanafi response is that the word 'ذروا' does not necessarily void the contract; it prohibits the act but does not affect the contract's essential elements.
5
Evaluate the tarjih
The Hanafi position (contract valid but sinful) is the relied-upon view (mufta bihi) in the madhhab, as stated 'على المذهب'. The opposing view is not followed.
The practical consequence (thamarah al-khilaf): Hanafis require payment and ownership transfers; Malikis/Hanbalis require undoing the transaction.

His saying: 'And it is obligatory to leave off selling' – so it is makruh tahrimi for both parties according to the madhhab, and it is correct to apply the term 'haram' to it as occurred in al-Hidaya. The contract is valid according to us (Hanafis), and it is the view of the majority: the price is due, and ownership is established before delivery. Commentary
This passage discusses the ruling on buying and selling after the Friday adhan has been called. The matn (base text) states it is obligatory to leave off selling, meaning it is makruh tahrimi (prohibitively disliked) for both buyer and seller according to the Hanafi school. Some texts like al-Hidaya even call it haram. Despite the prohibition, the contract is still valid (sahih) in the Hanafi school; the buyer is obliged to pay the price and ownership transfers before taking possession. The commentator emphasizes that makruh here means tahrimi (prohibited) and sinful, but the contract is not corrupted (fasid). In contrast, Malik and Ahmad consider the contract invalid (batil), except for marriage, gift, and charity.

Key Terms
مكروه تحريماً (makrūh taḥrīman): Prohibitively disliked – an act forbidden by a non-definitive proof; sinful but the contract is not invalidated.
فاسد (fāsid): Corrupt – a contract that is defective but can be rectified; contrasted with batil (void). The text says the sale is not fasid but makruh tahrimi.

Checkpoint
According to the Hanafi school, what is the ruling on a sale contract made after the Friday adhan?
A) It is batil (void).
B) It is makruh tahrimi but valid.
C) It is completely permissible.
D) It is fasid (corrupt).

Key Takeaway
The Hanafi school distinguishes between the sinfulness of an act and the validity of a contract: selling during Friday adhan is sinful but the sale is valid.
