Biography of Sa'id ibn al-Musayyab and His Halala Opinion
Biographical sketch of Sa'id ibn al-Musayyab and his controversial halala view

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the biographical significance of Sa'id ibn al-Musayyab and explain why his controversial opinion on halala contradicts the well-known sunnah.

Core Idea
Sa'id ibn al-Musayyab's halala opinion contravenes the established hadith and is not accepted.

This lesson introduces Sa'id ibn al-Musayyab, a towering figure among the Tabi'in and one of the seven jurists of Medina. The passage highlights his profound knowledge and piety, then recounts his unique view that a valid marriage contract alone renders a thrice-divorced woman permissible to her first husband. We will examine his biography, his opinion, and why it stands against the famous sunnah.

Guided Path
1
Identify Biographical Details
List the key facts about Sa'id ibn al-Musayyab: his lineage, birth, teachers, and scholarly accolades.
He was born during 'Umar's caliphate and was known for his memory of 'Umar's judgments.
2
Recognize His Scholarly Status
Note the praises from Qatada and Makhul, and his place among the seven jurists of Medina.
His status makes his opinion noteworthy even if rejected.
3
Extract His Halala Opinion
Quote Sa'id's view: 'If she marries him with a valid marriage, she becomes permissible to the first husband.' Contrast with the common view requiring consummation.
The passage says 'the people say' indicates the majority position.
4
Compare with the Established Sunnah
Recall the hadith of 'asila that requires actual intercourse for halala. Identify why Sa'id's view contradicts it.
The commentator suggests the hadith may not have reached Sa'id.
5
Understand the Reaction
Note that his opinion was deemed strange (mustaghrab) and that it was said the hadith had not reached him.
This illustrates the priority of authentic sunnah over individual ijtihad.

[٣] قوله : على مذهب سعيد بن المسيب ; - بفتح الياء المشددة – ، وقيل : بكسرها ابن حَزَن بن أبي وهب بن عمرو بن عائد بن عمران بن مخزوم القرشي المدني، ولد بسنتين مضتا من خلافة عمر الله له ، كان من أفقه التابعين، وأحد الفقهاء السبعة بالمدينة. وكان أحفظ النَّاسِ لأحكام عمر الله وأقضيته ، وكان له بضاعة يتجر بها، وحجّ أربعين حجة، وما فاته التكبيرة الأولى منذ خمسين سنة، وصلى الصبح بوضوء العشاء خمسين سنة. قال قتادة في شأنه : ما رأيتُ أحداً قط أعلم بالحلال والحرام منه، وقال محمد بن إسحاق عن مكحول : طفتُ الأرض كلها فما رأيتُ أعلم منه ، وروى عن جماعة من الصحابة ؛ كعمر، وعثمان وعلي وسعد بن أبي وقاص، وابن عباس، وابن عمر، وأبي هريرة وغيرهم. وتوفي في سنة ثلاث وتسعين، أو أربع وتسعين، رضي الله تعالى عنه وعنهم أجمعين، هذا والتفصيل في تاريخ ابن خلكان» (۲) ، وتهذيب الكمال»(۳)، وغيرهما من التواريخ المعتبرة. قال الزَّيْلَعِيُّ » في «تخريجه»(4) : في سنن ابن منصور»: عن ابن المسيب له قال : الناس يقولون حتى يجامعها، وأما أنا أقول إذا تزوجها نكاحاً صحيحاً، فإنها تحل للأول، واستغرب هذا من سعيد حتى قيل : إن الحديث لم يبلغه كما استغرب من الحسن الله أنه يشترط الإنزال نظراً إلى معنى العسلية. انتهى.
[3] His saying: according to the school of Sa'id ibn al-Musayyab – with a fat-ha on the ya' mushaddadah, and some say with a kasra – he is Ibn Hazan ibn Abi Wahb ibn 'Amr ibn 'A'id ibn 'Imran ibn Makhzum al-Qurashi al-Madani. He was born two years into the caliphate of 'Umar (may Allah be pleased with him). He was one of the most learned of the Tabi'in and one of the seven jurists of Medina. He was the most knowledgeable of people concerning the judgments and rulings of 'Umar (may Allah be pleased with him). He had capital with which he traded, performed Hajj forty times, never missed the first takbir for fifty years, and performed Fajr prayer with the wudu of 'Isha for fifty years. Qatada said about him: I have never seen anyone more knowledgeable of the halal and haram than him. Muhammad ibn Ishaq reported from Mak-hul: I traveled the whole earth and I have not seen anyone more knowledgeable than him. He narrated from a group of Companions: 'Umar, 'Uthman, 'Ali, Sa'd ibn Abi Waqqas, Ibn 'Abbas, Ibn 'Umar, Abu Huraira, and others. He died in the year 93 or 94 H. May Allah be pleased with him and with them all. This is detailed in 'Ta'rikh Ibn Khallikan' and 'Tahdhib al-Kamal' and other reliable histories. Al-Zayla'i said in his Takhrij: In the Sunan of Ibn Mansur: from Ibn al-Musayyab: The people say, 'until he has intercourse with her,' but I say, if she marries him with a valid marriage, she becomes permissible to the first husband. This was deemed strange from Sa'id, and it was said that the hadith had not reached him, just as it was deemed strange from al-Hasan that he stipulated orgasm, considering the meaning of 'asila'. End.

Commentary
This passage is a biographical notice about Sa'id ibn al-Musayyab (d. 93-94 H), a prominent Tabi'i and one of the seven jurists of Medina. The text praises his deep knowledge of Islamic rulings, his piety, and his extensive travels. It then quotes his opinion regarding the issue of making a thrice-divorced woman permissible to her first husband (halala). The famous hadith (the 'asila hadith) requires that the second marriage be consummated. However, Sa'id held that a valid marriage contract alone suffices for the woman to become permissible to her first husband. The commentary notes that this opinion was considered strange, and it was thought that the hadith might not have reached him. This biographical entry serves as an example of a well-known sunnah being contradicted, as mentioned earlier in the matn.

Key Terms
السنة المشهورة (al-sunnah al-mashhurah): The well-known and widely transmitted hadith; here referring to the hadith of 'asila regarding halala.
عسيلة (asila): Honey or sweetness; metaphorically, the pleasure of marital intimacy, used in the hadith to mean consummation.
