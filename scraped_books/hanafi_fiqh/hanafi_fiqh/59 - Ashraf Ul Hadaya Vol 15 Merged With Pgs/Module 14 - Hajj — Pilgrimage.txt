Liability for Shooting Game in Ihram: Timing of Transgression
Ruling on liability when shooter's state changes between shot and hit.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to apply the Hanafi rule that liability for hunting in ihram is determined by the shooter's state at the moment of shooting, not at the moment the arrow hits.

Core Idea
Liability for hunting in ihram is fixed by the act of shooting, not by the hit.

This passage from Al-Hidayah clarifies a common scenario: a pilgrim shoots game while in ihram but becomes halal before the arrow strikes. Does he still owe a penalty? The ruling turns on the key concept of ta'addi (transgression) and when it occurs. Understanding this helps us appreciate how Hanafi fiqh consistently applies the principle that responsibility attaches to the prohibited act itself.

Guided Path
1
Identify the two scenarios
Read the passage and note the two contrasting cases: (1) a muhrim shoots game then becomes halal before the arrow hits; (2) a halal person shoots game then becomes muhrim before the arrow hits.
The passage explicitly contrasts these to show the legal criterion.
2
State the ruling for each scenario
The first case obligates the penalty (jaza'), the second case does not. Confirm this from the text.
The text uses 'fa-'alayhi al-jaza' and 'fa-la shay' 'alayhi'.
3
Analyze the reasoning (ta'addi)
The passage explains: liability is due to transgression (ta'addi), which is defined as the act of shooting while in ihram. Therefore, the state at the time of shooting determines liability, regardless of when the arrow hits.
Ta'addi is not the hitting, but the prohibited act itself.
4
Apply the principle to similar cases
Consider if the arrow hits after the shooter has left ihram. The transgression has already occurred, so liability remains. Conversely, if the shooter was halal at the time of shooting, no transgression occurred even if he later enters ihram.
This principle is consistent with Hanafi tort law where liability depends on the act, not subsequent events.

And if a Muhrim shoots game, then becomes halal, then the arrow hits the game, then he is liable for the penalty (jaza). And if a halal person shoots game, then becomes Muhrim, then there is nothing upon him, because liability is only obligated due to transgression (ta'addi), and that is his shooting in the state of ihram. Commentary
This passage addresses the liability (jaza, a penalty for hunting while in ihram) for shooting game when the shooter's state (ihram vs. halal) changes between shooting and hitting. The ruling is that liability is determined by the state at the moment of shooting. If a person shoots game while in ihram (which is prohibited) and then becomes halal (completes umrah/hajj) before the arrow hits, he still incurs the penalty because the act of shooting itself was a transgression (ta'addi) committed in ihram. Conversely, if a halal person shoots game (permissible) and then enters ihram before the arrow hits, no penalty is due because at the time of shooting there was no transgression. This is consistent with the overarching principle that the moment of the act (shooting) governs liability.

Key Terms
جزاء (jaza'): A penalty for hunting while in ihram, typically the sacrifice of an equivalent animal or its value.
تعدي (ta'addi): Transgression, an act that exceeds the bounds of the law; here, shooting while in ihram.
