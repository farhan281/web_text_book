Ruling on Qisas for a Mortgaged Slave in Mortgagee's Custody
Explaining the condition for qisas when a mortgaged slave is killed while in the mortgagee's possession

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the condition required for qisas when a mortgaged slave is killed in the mortgagee's possession and explain the legal reasoning behind it.

Core Idea
Qisas for a mortgaged slave requires both mortgagee and mortgagor to consent; the mortgagee lacks ownership.

This lesson examines a specific ruling in Hanafi fiqh regarding a mortgaged slave who is killed while in the custody of the mortgagee (creditor). The question arises: who has the right to demand qisas (retributive punishment) for the slave? The Hanafi position provides a nuanced answer that balances the rights of both the mortgagor (debtor) and mortgagee, requiring their joint presence.

Guided Path
1
Identify the Act and the Parties
Recognize that the act is the intentional killing of a mortgaged slave held by the mortgagee. The parties are the mortgagor (owner-debtor) and the mortgagee (creditor-possessor).
The slave is an asset rahn.
2
State the Ruling
The text states that qisas is not obligatory (la yajib al-qisas) until both mortgagor and mortgagee are present together. This is the condition for the obligation of qisas.
No qisas until their joint presence.
3
Understand the Reasoning (Ta'lil)
The mortgagee has no ownership (la mulka lahu) so he cannot administer qisas alone. If the mortgagor alone demanded qisas, it would nullify the mortgagee's right to hold the slave as security for the debt. Hence both must be present so that the mortgagee can waive his right by consent.
The reason balances both interests.
4
Derive the Condition
The presence of both parties is a shart (condition) for the execution of qisas. Without this condition, the killer cannot be subjected to qisas, though the act of murder itself is still a crime.
This is a procedural condition.

Commentary
This passage addresses a specific case: a slave who has been given as collateral (rahn) is killed while in the custody of the mortgagee (the creditor). The ruling is that qisas (capital punishment for the killer) is not obligatory until both the mortgagor (the debtor) and the mortgagee are present to claim it. The reason is that the mortgagee does not own the slave, so he alone cannot demand qisas; but if the mortgagor demands it, it would cancel the mortgagee's right to hold the slave as security for the debt. Therefore, both must agree so that the mortgagee can waive his security interest.

Key Terms
رهن (rahn): A contract of pledge where a debtor gives an asset (here a slave) as security for a debt; the creditor holds it as mortgagee (murtahin).
قصاص (qisas): Retributive punishment for intentional homicide, where the killer is subject to death or equivalent penalty.
راهن (rahin): The mortgagor, i.e., the debtor who pledges an asset as security.
مرتهن (murtahin): The mortgagee, i.e., the creditor who holds the pledged asset as security.
