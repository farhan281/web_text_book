Helping Mahram Mount and Dismount: Conditions and Precautions
Conditions for touching mahram when helping on/off ride

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to apply the Hanafi rulings on touching a mahram when helping her mount or dismount, distinguishing between permissible and prohibited situations based on the presence of desire.

Core Idea
Even doubt of desire forbids touching a mahram; only need and safety permit it over clothes.

During Hajj travel, a mahram woman may need physical assistance to mount or dismount a riding animal. While Islam recognizes necessity, it also guards against any means that could lead to sin. This lesson examines the Hanafi position on this issue, detailing the conditions that make such physical contact permissible and the precautions required when the possibility of desire arises.

Guided Path
1
Identify the act and its default ruling
The act is helping a mahram woman mount/dismount. The default ruling is mubah (permissible) when all conditions are met.
2
Determine the conditions for permissibility
Touching must be over clothes, only back and belly (not beneath), and both parties must be safe from desire.
These are the shurut (conditions) for the act to be permissible.
3
Identify the trigger that changes the ruling
The presence of desire (certainty, presumption, or doubt) makes the act prohibited. This is a mani' (preventer).
Hanafi school includes even shakk (doubt) as a trigger.
4
Apply the precautionary hierarchy
First try to have the woman mount herself. If not, use a cloth barrier. If no cloth, the man must suppress his desire mentally while helping.
This hierarchy is derived from the text's sequential instructions.
5
Summarize the mufta bihi
The relied-upon position is to follow these precautions strictly. The ruling is conditional and becomes haram if desire is present.

If she needs mounting and dismounting, there is no harm in him touching her over her clothes, and taking her back and belly, not what is beneath them, when they are safe from desire. If he fears desire upon himself or upon her, whether certain, presumed, or doubtful, then he should avoid that with his effort. Then if she can mount by herself, he should refrain entirely. If she cannot, he should use clothes to avoid touching her skin. If he finds no clothes, he should repel the desire from his heart as much as possible.

Commentary
This passage details the ruling when a mahram woman needs help getting on or off a riding animal. The general principle: it is permissible to touch her over her clothes, and to grasp her back and stomach, but not the parts beneath (like the buttocks), provided both are safe from desire. However, if there is fear of desire (certainty, presumption, or even doubt), then the man must avoid touching as much as possible. Ideally, the woman should mount herself. If not, the man should wrap his hands in cloth to avoid direct skin contact. If cloth is unavailable, he must exert himself to suppress any desire. The commentary emphasizes that these precautions are to prevent any means leading to zina, which is even more severe with mahrams.

Key Terms
اركاب (irkāb): Helping a woman mount a riding animal.
شهوة (shahwa): Sexual desire; its presence or even doubt changes the ruling.
