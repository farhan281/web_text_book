Overview of Volume Three: Marriage and Fosterage
Table of contents listing chapters

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to list the chapters of Kitab al-Nikah and Kitab al-Rada' as they appear in the table of contents of Volume Three.

Core Idea
The TOC reveals the logical progression from marriage to fosterage, forming the foundation of family fiqh.

This lesson focuses on the first part of the volume's table of contents, covering Kitab al-Nikah (Book of Marriage) and Kitab al-Rada' (Book of Fosterage). Understanding this sequence helps you navigate the text and grasp the structure of Hanafi family law.

Guided Path
1
Identify the opening book
The TOC begins with 'Kitab al-Nikah'. Note that it is the first major book in the volume.
2
List the chapters within Nikah
After the book title, the TOC enumerates chapters: Bab al-Muharramat (Prohibited Women), Bab al-Wali wa al-Kuf' (Guardian and Compatibility), Bab al-Mahr (Dowry), Bab Nikaah al-Raqiq wa al-Kafir (Marriage of Slaves and Disbelievers), and Bab al-Qasm (Division of Co-wives).
These chapters are in sequence; note the order.
3
Continue to Kitab al-Rada'
Following Kitab al-Nikah's Bab al-Qasm, the TOC lists 'Kitab al-Rada'' (Fosterage). This is a shorter book, usually one chapter.
4
Observe the transition
The movement from Nikah (marriage) to Rada' (fosterage) is logical because fosterage creates prohibited relationships similar to blood relations, affecting marriage rulings.
This reflects the interconnectedness of fiqh topics.

Commentary
This passage is the table of contents for the third volume of 'Umdat al-Ri'ayah 'ala Sharh al-Wiqayah. It lists the major chapters (kutub, abwab, fusul) covered in the volume. The volume begins with Kitab al-Nikah (Book of Marriage), including chapters on prohibited women, guardianship, dowry, marriage of slaves and disbelievers, and division of co-wives. Then it continues with Kitab al-Rada' (Book of Fosterage) and Kitab al-Talaq (Book of Divorce), which covers pronouncement, delegation, swearing by divorce, revocation, ila', khul', zihar, li'an, impotence, waiting period, and mourning. The volume also includes Bab al-Nasab wa al-Hadanah (Lineage and Custody), Bab al-Nafaqah (Maintenance), and finally Kitab al-'Itaq (Book of Emancipation) with chapters on partial emancipation, ambiguous emancipation, swearing by emancipation, emancipation for a consideration, and tadbir/istilad. This TOC is a standard feature of the matn or sharh to orient the reader to the structure of the work.

Structure of Volume Three: Divorce and Related Chapters
Table of contents listing chapters

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to sequence the chapters and sections of Kitab al-Talaq as listed in the table of contents.

Core Idea
The TOC of Kitab al-Talaq shows a meticulous progression from pronouncement to revocation, covering all major divorce-related rulings.

This lesson focuses on the second major section of the TOC: Kitab al-Talaq (Book of Divorce) and its many sub-chapters. Understanding the order helps you grasp the Hanafi treatment of divorce, from the initial pronouncement to post-divorce issues.

Guided Path
1
Identify the start of Kitab al-Talaq
After Kitab al-Rada', the TOC begins 'Kitab al-Talaq'. Note that it is a lengthy book with many chapters and sections (fusul).
2
List the main chapters within Talaq
The first chapter is 'Bab Iqa' al-Talaq' (Pronouncement of Divorce). Then follow sections on attaching divorce to time, simile and description, allusions (kinaayat), and delegation (tafwid).
Sections are indented under chapters.
3
Identify chapters after delegation
After delegation, the TOC lists 'Bab al-Hilf bi al-Talaq' (Swearing by Divorce) with a section on exception, then 'Bab Talaq al-Mari' (Divorce of the Sick), 'Bab al-Raj'ah' (Revocation), and a section on what makes a woman permissible (fima tahl bihi).
4
Continue to special types of divorce
Then come 'Bab al-Ila'' (Vow of Abstinence), 'Bab al-Khul'' (Divorce by Redemption), 'Bab al-Zihar' (Injurious Comparison), with a section on expiation, 'Bab al-Li'an' (Imprecation), 'Bab al-'Unnin' (Impotence), and 'Bab al-'Iddah' (Waiting Period) with a section on mourning (hidad).
The TOC ends the divorce section with 'al-Mutallaqah' (The Divorced Woman).

Structure of Volume Three: Lineage, Maintenance, and Emancipation
Table of contents listing chapters

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to describe the sequence of topics from lineage and custody through emancipation as listed in the table of contents.

Core Idea
The final portion of the volume covers post-divorce family matters and the freeing of slaves, completing the family and slavery fiqh.

This lesson covers the last chapters of Volume Three: Bab al-Nasab wa al-Hadanah (Lineage and Custody), Bab al-Nafaqah (Maintenance), and Kitab al-'Itaq (Book of Emancipation). Understanding this sequence shows how Hanafi fiqh moves from marriage and divorce to the resulting responsibilities and slavery issues.

Guided Path
1
Identify the transition from divorce to lineage
After 'al-Mutallaqah' (The Divorced Woman), the TOC jumps to 'Bab al-Nasab wa al-Hadanah' (Lineage and Custody). This is the natural next step after divorce — determining parentage and custody of children.
2
Note the sections within Nasab wa Hadanah
The TOC lists two sections: 'Fasl fi Thubut al-Nasab' (Proof of Lineage) and 'Fasl fi al-Hadanah' (Custody).
These are critical for post-divorce arrangements.
3
Continue to Kitab al-Nafaqah
Then comes 'Bab al-Nafaqah' (Maintenance), which includes a section on maintenance of relatives ('Fasl fi Nafaqat al-Aqarib').
Maintenance is tied to family relations.
4
End with Kitab al-'Itaq
The volume concludes with 'Kitab al-'Itaq' (Book of Emancipation), which has chapters on partial emancipation, ambiguous emancipation, swearing by emancipation, emancipation for a consideration, and tadbir/istilad.
This is a separate book within the volume.
