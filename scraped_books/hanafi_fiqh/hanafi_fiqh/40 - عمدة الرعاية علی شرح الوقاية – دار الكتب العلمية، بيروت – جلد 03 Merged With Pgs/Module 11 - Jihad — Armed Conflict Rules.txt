Emancipation of a Harbi's Slave upon Conversion and Hijra
Ruling on freedom of a harbi's slave through Islam and hijra

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the Hanafi ruling on a slave of a non-Muslim enemy who becomes Muslim and emigrates, and explain the proof from the Sunnah.

Core Idea
A slave of a non-Muslim enemy becomes free automatically upon embracing Islam and migrating.

This passage presents a specific ruling in the law of jihad (siyar). It addresses the status of a slave belonging to a harbi (non-Muslim enemy) who converts to Islam and then emigrates to Muslim territory. The Hanafi school rules that such a slave is freed immediately, without any further action by a master or judge. The legal reasoning is rooted in the principle that a Muslim cannot be enslaved, and is supported by a prophetic precedent from the incident of the slaves of Ta'if.

Guided Path
1
Identify the act and its ruling
The subject is the slave of a harbi who comes out to Muslims as a Muslim. The ruling is that he becomes free (يعتق).
The ruling is automatic, not contingent on any external act.
2
Locate the dalil
The proof is the hadith narrated by 'Abd al-Razzaq: the slaves of Ta'if came to the Prophet (s) as Muslims, and he declared them free.
This is a Sunnah ahad with zanni thubut and dalala.
3
Trace the wajh al-dalalah
The Prophet's statement 'they are the free ones of Allah' indicates that their Islam and hijra were the cause of their freedom.
The causal link is inferred from the context.
4
Analyze the ta'lil
The author provides two reasons: the slave secured himself (ahraza nafsahu) by Islam and migration, and there is no enslavement of a Muslim initially.
The second reason is a general principle.
5
Note the ikhtilaf
Some other schools may require a judicial decree or ransom, but the Hanafi position holds automatic freedom.
The cross-madhhab status is mukhtalaf fih.

He said: Like a slave of a harbi who comes out to us as a Muslim; he is free, because he secured himself by his Islam and his immigration to us, and there is no enslavement of a Muslim initially; and therefore it is narrated by 'Abd al-Razzaq and others: 'Indeed, the slaves of the people of Ta'if when they came out to the Messenger of Allah (peace be upon him) as Muslims, he said to them: They are the free ones of Allah.'

Commentary
This passage provides a ruling_with_proof. It addresses the case of a slave belonging to a non-Muslim enemy (harbi). If the slave embraces Islam and emigrates to the Muslim state (dar al-Islam), he becomes free. The reasoning (ta'lil) is that by his conversion and migration, he has secured himself (ahraza nafsahu). The principle is that a Muslim cannot be enslaved ab initio (ibtida'an). The proof is the historical incident from Ta'if where the Prophet (s) declared those slaves free. This is a specific rule of siyar (Islamic law of nations). The Hanafi school holds that if a slave of a harbi becomes Muslim and migrates to dar al-Islam, he attains freedom automatically, regardless of his former master's wishes.

Key Terms
حربي (harbi): A non-Muslim who is in a state of war with the Muslim state.
أحرز نفسه (ahraza nafsahu): Secured himself; by embracing Islam and migrating, the slave gains inviolability (hurma) of person and freedom.
