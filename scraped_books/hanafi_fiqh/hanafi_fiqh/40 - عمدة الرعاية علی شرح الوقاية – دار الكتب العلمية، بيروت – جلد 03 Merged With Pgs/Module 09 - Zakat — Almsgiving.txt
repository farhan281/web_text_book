Feeding Sixty Poor as Dhihar Expiation
Ruling on amount of food and permissibility of value payment

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the Hanafi ruling on the amount of food and the permissibility of giving cash value in dhihar expiation, and explain the evidential basis and inter-madhhab disagreement.

Core Idea
In Hanafi fiqh, dhihar expiation allows cash value instead of food because value achieves the same purpose and may be more beneficial.

This passage from a Hanafi commentary explains the ruling on feeding sixty poor as expiation for dhihar when one cannot fast. It clarifies the prescribed amount (fitra) and the permissibility of paying its cash value, a point of disagreement with the Shafi'i school. The commentary also addresses agency in performing the feeding and an intra-Hanafi dispute regarding freeing a slave.

Guided Path
1
Identify the ruling and its context
The passage deals with the expiation (kaffara) for dhihar when a person is unable to fast two consecutive months. The ruling is that one must feed sixty poor individuals, each receiving the amount of fitra (sadaqat al-fitr) or its cash value.
The context is the sequence of expiation: free a slave, then fast two months, then feed sixty poor.
2
Determine the prescribed amount
Each poor person receives the amount of fitra: half a sa' of wheat or one sa' of dates or barley. This measure is taken from the expiation for shaving the head in ihram, as specified in authentic hadith.
The amount is fixed by analogy (qiyas) from the explicitly mentioned case.
3
Understand the Hanafi position on value
It is permissible to give the cash value (qima) equivalent to the fitra amount. The Hanafi school reasons that value is like the item in meaning and often more beneficial to the poor.
This is a point of contention with the Shafi'i school, which requires actual food.
4
Analyze the proof
The proof is based on a hadith (reported in all Sahih works) that explicitly mentions feeding with fitra in the expiation for shaving the head. The Hanafis extend this by qiyas to other expiations, including dhihar.
The hadith is ahad (zanni al-thubut) but its indication is clear (qati al-dalala).
5
Note the intra-Hanafi and inter-madhhab disagreements
Within the Hanafi school, Abu Yusuf allows agency in freeing a slave, while Abu Hanifa and Muhammad do not. Externally, the Shafi'i school rejects the permissibility of giving value.
These differences affect practical fatwa: Hanafi muftis may allow cash payment, Shafi'i muftis require food.

And if he is unable to fast, then let him feed – he or his agent – sixty poor people, each one the amount of fitra (zakat al-fitr) or its value. This is according to us (Hanafis). As for according to al-Shafi'i, it is not permissible to give the value. (Commentary: His saying 'he or his agent' indicates that feeding is a financial act of worship, so agency and delegation apply in it, and similarly in freeing a slave according to Abu Yusuf, contrary to the two (Abu Hanifa and Muhammad). His saying 'each': meaning he gives each poor person the amount of fitra, i.e., sadaqat al-fitr, which is half a sa' of wheat or a sa' of dates or barley, as detailed previously. This is the estimation according to us in expiations, by analogy from what is explicitly mentioned to what is not mentioned. Indeed in the narration of all the authors of the Sahihs, it is explicitly stated in the expiation of shaving the head while in ihram, so similarly in other cases. His saying 'or its value': meaning it is permissible to pay the value of the amount of fitra. This is according to us in all prescribed amounts and expiations, based on the fact that the value of a thing is considered like it in meaning, and it may be more beneficial for the poor, so it is permissible to pay it.)

Commentary
This passage presents a ruling from the Hanafi school on the expiation for dhihar when one is unable to fast two consecutive months. The ruling states that the person must feed sixty poor individuals, giving each the amount of sadaqat al-fitr (approximately half a sa' of wheat or one sa' of dates/barley) or its cash value. The commentary explains that this feeding is a financial act of worship, allowing agency (the person can appoint a representative to carry out the feeding). There is an intra-Hanafi disagreement: Abu Yusuf allows agency in freeing a slave as well, while the majority (Abu Hanifa and Muhammad) do not. The text also highlights an inter-madhhab dispute: the Shafi'i school does not permit giving the value (qima) instead of the actual food. The Hanafi position is based on qiyas (analogy) with the expiation for shaving the head during ihram, where the amount of fitra is explicitly mentioned in authentic hadith. The value is considered equivalent in meaning and often more beneficial to the poor.

Key Terms
الفِطْرَة (al-fitra): Amount of food given as sadaqat al-fitr: half a sa' of wheat or one sa' of dates/barley.
قيمته (qimatu): The monetary value equivalent to the prescribed amount of food.
نائبه (na'ibuhu): Agent or representative authorized to perform the feeding.
