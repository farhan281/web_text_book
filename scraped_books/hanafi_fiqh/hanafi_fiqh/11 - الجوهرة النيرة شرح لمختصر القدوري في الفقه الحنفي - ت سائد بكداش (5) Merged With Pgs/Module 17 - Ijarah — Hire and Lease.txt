Qisas for Hired and Sold Slaves
States rulings for qisas when a hired or sold slave is killed

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state who is entitled to qisas when a hired slave is killed and apply the rule for a sold slave killed before possession, depending on the buyer's choice.

Core Idea
The right to qisas follows possession and ownership at the time of killing, with subsequent choices affecting entitlement.

This passage from Al-Jawharah al-Nayyirah presents two clear rules regarding qisas for slaves in special contractual contexts. Understanding these rulings clarifies how ownership and possession determine who has the right to demand retribution (qisas).

Guided Path
1
Identify the two scenarios
The passage discusses (1) a hired slave killed during the hire period, and (2) a sold slave killed before delivery (qabd) while still in the seller's possession.
These are distinct contractual settings that affect entitlement.
2
State the ruling for the hired slave
Qisas belongs to the hirer (mu'jir), not the owner. The hirer has possession and beneficial interest at the time of killing.
The owner receives the rent but does not have the right to qisas.
3
State the ruling for the sold slave
If the buyer chooses to confirm the sale after the slave is killed, the buyer gets qisas because ownership is confirmed retroactively. If the buyer rescinds, the seller gets qisas.
The buyer's election determines the ownership at the time of killing.
4
Explain the rationale
The right to qisas is tied to ownership and actual possession at the time of killing. For the hired slave, the hirer is in possession. For the sold slave, ownership is conditional on the buyer's choice.
This is consistent with Hanafi principles of risk and ownership.
5
Summarize the underlying principle
The right to qisas follows the one who has ownership and/or possession at the time of the homicide, but subsequent election can retroactively fix ownership.
Memorize this as a concise rule.

Commentary
This passage presents two distinct rulings without dispute or proof, serving as a concise statement of the Hanafi position. First, if a slave who is hired out (e.g., for service) is killed, the right to demand qisas belongs to the hirer (the one who hired the slave), not the owner. This is because the hirer has a beneficial interest (manfa'ah) and the actual possession of the slave at the time of the killing. Second, if a slave that has been sold but not yet delivered (qabd) to the buyer is killed while in the seller's possession, the entitlement to qisas depends on the buyer's choice after the killing. If the buyer chooses to confirm the sale (i.e., accept the loss and keep the contract), then he becomes the owner retroactively and thus has the right to qisas. If he chooses to rescind the sale (because the slave has been destroyed), then the seller retains ownership and the right to qisas rest with the seller. These rulings illustrate the Hanafi principle that the right to qisas follows ownership and possession at the time of the killing, with consideration of subsequent choices.

Key Terms
إجارة (ijārah): A contract of hire or lease where the hirer (musta'jir) pays for usufruct of the slave; the owner (mu'jir) receives rent.
قبض (qabḍ): Physical or constructive possession of the sold item, necessary for transfer of risk and some rights.
