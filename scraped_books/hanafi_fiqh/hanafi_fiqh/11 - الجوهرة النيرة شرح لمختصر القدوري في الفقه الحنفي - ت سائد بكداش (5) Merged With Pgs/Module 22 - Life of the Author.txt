Eight Issues of Tawaqquf: The Intellectual Honesty of Imam Abu Hanifa
Listing eight issues of tawaqquf with a reason for pausing

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to list the eight issues on which Imam Abu Hanifa paused and explain the reason for his pause (wara').

Core Idea
Abu Hanifa paused on eight issues out of extreme piety, not ignorance.

Imam Abu Hanifa is known not only for his prolific legal reasoning but also for his intellectual honesty. He famously refrained from issuing rulings on eight specific issues where he felt the evidence was insufficient or uncertain. This pause, known as tawaqquf, reflects his deep piety (wara') and his commitment to only speak when absolutely sure. Understanding these eight issues gives us a window into the Imam's methodology and humility.

Guided Path
1
Understand the concept of tawaqquf
Tawaqquf means refraining from a ruling due to insufficient or conflicting evidence. In Hanafi usul, it is a valid and respected position. Abu Hanifa's pauses were not admissions of ignorance, but acts of caution.
Tawaqquf is different from 'I don't know' – it is a deliberate methodological stance.
2
Categorise the eight issues
Group them to aid memorisation: (1-2,5) purity/animal rulings (when is jalalah meat good? when is a dog trained? donkey's leftover water); (3) rites (time for circumcision); (4) gender (hermaphrodite rulings); (6-8) theological (denier of fate, angels vs prophets, children of polytheists).
Categorising helps see the types of evidence gaps: some are textual, some are rational, some are empirical.
3
Memorise the list using a mnemonic
Create an acrostic from Arabic first letters or English words. For example: 'Jalalah Dog, Time, Khuntha, Donkey, Dahr, Angels, Kids'.
Repetition and association are key for memorisation anchors.
4
Reflect on the lesson of wara‘
The text explicitly says the pause was due to extreme wara'. This teaches that a scholar should not rush to give fatwas without clear evidence. It is better to pause than to err.
This is a practical adab (etiquette) for anyone issuing legal opinions.

And Abu Hanifa paused on eight issues and did not specify a time for them: First: this one—when does its meat become good? Second: the dog—when does it become trained? Third: what is the time of circumcision? Fourth: the ambiguous hermaphrodite. Fifth: the leftover water of the donkey. Sixth: the person who denies fate (dahr). Seventh: are angels better or prophets? Eighth: do the children of polytheists enter Hellfire? He paused on these issues due to the extent of his piety (wara').

Commentary
This is a well-known list of eight issues on which Imam Abu Hanifa did not give a definitive ruling or fix a time. The first issue refers to the jalalah animal discussed previously. The second is about when a dog becomes trained for hunting. The third is the recommended time for circumcision. The fourth is how to rule on an intersex person whose gender is unclear. The fifth is whether the leftover water of a donkey is pure or impure. The sixth is the ruling on someone who denies predestination (al-dahr). The seventh is the comparison of the rank of angels versus prophets. The eighth is the fate of children of polytheists who die before puberty. The reason for his pause is his extreme wara' (piety and caution), as he would only speak when he had clear evidence. This list is often cited to show the Imam's intellectual honesty.

Key Terms
توقف (tawaqquf): The act of refraining from giving a ruling or fixing a time due to lack of clear evidence or out of caution.
الخنثى المشكل (al-khunthā al-mushkil): A person born with ambiguous genitalia, whose gender is not clear.
سؤر الحمار (su'ur al-himār): The leftover water after a donkey drinks from a vessel.
الورع (al-wara‘): Scrupulous piety; refraining from doubtful matters to avoid falling into sin.

Key Takeaway
