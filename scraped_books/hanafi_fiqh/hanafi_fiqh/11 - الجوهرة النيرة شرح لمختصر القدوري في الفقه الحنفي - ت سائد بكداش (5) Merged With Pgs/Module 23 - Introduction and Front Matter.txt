Table of contents listing book and chapter titles

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to use the table of contents to locate the correct chapter for a given Hanafi family law or criminal law query.

Core Idea
A table of contents is a roadmap: know which book holds your ruling.

This passage is a table of contents from Al-Jawhara al-Nayyira, a commentary on Mukhtasar al-Quduri. It lists all the major books (kutub) and chapters (abwab) covered in the text. Rather than providing legal rulings, it serves as an index to help you quickly find the section you need. In this lesson, you will learn how to use this map to navigate the work efficiently.

Guided Path
1
Identify the major book (kitab) related to your query
Scan the table of contents for the broad category that matches your topic, such as Kitab al-‘Iddah for waiting periods or Kitab al-Jinayat for criminal offenses.
The table is organized by these kitab headings.
2
Locate the specific chapter (bab) within that book
Under each kitab, look for subheadings (e.g., 'Rulings on Ihdad' under Kitab al-‘Iddah). Use the listing to pinpoint the exact section.
Some bab headings are listed directly after the kitab title.
3
Match key terms from your query to the index entries
For example, if searching for maintenance of minor children, look for 'Nafaqat al-Awlad al-Sighar' under Kitab al-Nafaqah.
This step requires familiarity with fiqh terminology.
4
Use the page numbers (if available) to navigate
In a printed edition, the table of contents includes page numbers. In this digital version, use the section headings to scroll within the text.
This lesson does not include page numbers, but in practice they are essential.
5
Verify that the section indeed addresses your query
Once you open the chapter, skim the first few lines to confirm the topic matches. The table of contents is a guide, not a substitute for reading.
This ensures accuracy.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the major books (kutub) of fiqh covered in al-Jawhara al-Nayyira and locate specific topics within the larger structure of the text.

Core Idea
A fiqh index reveals the author's classification of topics—understanding it is the first step to mastering the madhhab.

This lesson introduces you to the table of contents of al-Jawhara al-Nayyira, a classic Hanafi commentary. While not a legal ruling itself, the index reflects the logical progression of fiqh topics from crimes and penalties to dietary and ritual matters. By learning to read this index, you gain a roadmap to the entire work.

Guided Path
1
Identify the major divisions (kutub)
Scan the index for headings starting with 'Kitāb' (e.g., Kitāb al-Maʿāqil, Kitāb al-Ḥudūd). These are the main sections of the book.
In this index, each Kitāb covers a broad area of fiqh.
2
Note the sub-chapters (abwāb)
Sub-headings preceded by 'Bāb' further break down each Kitāb into specific topics. For example, 'Bāb al-Qasāmah' appears within the context of Kitāb al-Maʿāqil.
Some sub-topics are listed without explicit 'Bāb' but are indented or follow from previous entries.
3
Observe the logical sequence
The index moves from Jināyāt (homicide and bodily harm) to Ḥudūd (fixed punishments) to dietary laws and finally to ʿIbādāt like Udḥiyah. This reflects the typical Hanafi ordering of prioritizing disputes and punishments before acts of worship.
Hanafi fiqh works often start with ʿIbādāt, but this commentary begins with Maʿāqil—likely because the author chose a different arrangement.
4
Highlight notable entries
Look for the entry 'ثمانية مسائل توقف فيها الإمام أبو حنيفة' (eight issues in which Imam Abu Hanifa withheld). This notes a unique methodological point—cases where the Imam did not issue a ruling.
These are famous masāʾil where Abu Hanifa paused due to conflicting evidence or lack of clear precedent.
