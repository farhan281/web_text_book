Time of Sadaqat al-Fitr and Its Obligation
Ruling on paying sadaqat al-fitr before 'Eid prayer with evidence

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the ruling and time for paying sadaqat al-fitr and identify the evidence from the hadith and Quran.

Core Idea
Sadaqat al-fitr must be paid before the 'Eid prayer to free the poor from asking.

This passage from Al-Hidayah explains the obligation of sadaqat al-fitr and its optimal time. It emphasizes that the main purpose is to enable the poor to participate in the 'Eid celebration without worry. The ruling is based on the hadith of Ibn 'Umar and the Quranic verse.

Guided Path
1
Identify the act and ruling
What exactly is being ruled upon? Sadaqat al-fitr is classified as wajib, not fard. Note its purpose: to make the poor self-sufficient so they can focus on 'Eid prayer.
The Hanafi school distinguishes between fard (qat'i) and wajib (zanni); the evidence here is zanni.
2
Locate the evidence
The hadith of Ibn 'Umar in Bukhari and Muslim: 'The Prophet ordered zakat al-fitr to be paid before the people go out to the prayer.' Also the Quranic verse 'Qad aflaha man tazakka' interpreted as giving zakat al-fitr.
The verse is cited as supporting the success of the one who gives sadaqat al-fitr and remembers Allah.
3
Understand the wisdom
The obligation is tied to freeing the poor from begging on 'Eid day. The Prophet said: 'Make them independent from begging.' This objective is best achieved by paying before the prayer.
4
Note the time flexibility
While it is best to pay before the 'Eid prayer, the Prophet himself paid one or two days before. Paying after the prayer is valid but contrary to the sunnah.
The hadith indicates the command is for payment before prayer, but the time can be extended slightly.

Time of Paying Sadaqat al-Fitr. And he should pay the sadaqat al-fitr to make the rich self-sufficient so that his heart may be free for prayer and he may proceed to the prayer ground. And according to Abu Hanifa, he should not say takbir on the way to the prayer ground, while according to the two companions (Abu Yusuf and Muhammad), he should say takbir by analogy to al-Adha. And for him (Abu Hanifa), the principle in praise and remembrance is concealment, and the Sacred Law came with it (loud takbir) for al-Adha because it is a day of takbir, and al-Fitr is not like that. Translation: And he should pay sadaqat al-fitr to make the poor self-sufficient so that his heart may be free for prayer and he may turn to the prayer ground. And according to Abu Hanifa, he should not say takbir on the way to the prayer ground, and according to the two companions, he should say takbir by analogy to al-Adha. The proof of Imam Abu Hanifa is that the original principle in praise and remembrance is concealment, and the Sacred Law came with it (loudness) for al-Adha because it is a day of takbir, and 'Eid al-Fitr is not like that. Explanation: It is wajib to pay sadaqat al-fitr before the 'Eid prayer because of the hadith of Ibn 'Umar in the two Sahihs: 'The Prophet ordered zakat al-fitr to be paid before the people go out to the prayer.' And he used to pay it one or two days before. Another proof is that it is a hastening to good and freeing the heart of the poor. Also the Prophet said: 'Make them independent from begging.' And this happens when people pay sadaqat al-fitr to them. Also the command of Allah: 'Qad aflaha man tazakka' meaning he gave zakat al-fitr and mentioned the name of his Lord and said the takbir of 'Eid on the way, then prayed the 'Eid prayer. Meaning: that person becomes successful who pays sadaqat al-fitr, recites the takbir of 'Eid mentioning his Lord, then prays the 'Eid prayer. After paying sadaqat al-fitr, he should proceed to the 'Eid ground. It is clear that walking to the 'Eid is mustahab. Because 'Umar said that walking to the 'Eid ground is sunnah. And if some people are excused due to weakness from going to the 'Eid ground, then the imam of the time should appoint someone to lead them in prayer in the mosque in the city. Because it is narrated that when 'Ali came to Kufa, he appointed a deputy to lead the weak in the two 'Eid prayers in the congregational mosque, and he went out to the open ground with fifty old men, walking and they walked. Meaning: 'Ali, may Allah be pleased with him, when he came to Kufa, he appointed a person to lead the weak in the two 'Eid prayers in the congregational mosque, and he himself took the children and elderly and went out to the open ground; he himself walked and those fifty persons also walked. There is a disagreement in this matter: on the day of 'Eid al-Fitr, while going to the 'Eid ground, whether to say takbir aloud or softly. Imam Abu Hanifa said: do not say it aloud. And the two companions said: say it aloud. The proof of the two companions is analogy on 'Eid al-Adha: just as in 'Eid al-Adha loud takbir is legislated, similarly in 'Eid al-Fitr loud takbir is legislated. The proof of Imam Abu Hanifa is that the original principle in remembrance is concealment, as Allah says: 'And remember your Lord within yourself in humility and fear and without loudness of speech' and the Prophet said: 'The best remembrance is the hidden remembrance, and the best provision is that which suffices.' So the original is concealment. But for the days of 'Eid al-Adha, a text came against analogy for loud takbir: Allah said: 'And remember Allah during the numbered days.' The exegetes said that this refers to loud takbir on the days of al-Adha. And 'Eid al-Fitr is not the same meaning as 'Eid al-Adha because 'Eid al-Adha is associated with the rites of Hajj, while 'Eid al-Fitr is not. So when 'Eid al-Fitr is not in the same meaning as 'Eid al-Adha, then analogy from 'Eid al-Adha is not appropriate. At this point an objection can be raised: Imam Abu Hanifa's saying that loud takbir on 'Eid al-Fitr is not established by the Sacred Law is not accepted, because Allah said: 'And to complete the period and to magnify Allah for having guided you' – this verse indicates takbir after completing Ramadan, and takbir is known (to be) when it is said aloud. And it is narrated from 'Umar that the Prophet used to go out on the day of 'Eid al-Fitr and 'Eid al-Adha raising his voice with takbir. So it is established that on 'Eid al-Fitr also takbir (is said aloud).

Commentary
This is a ruling with proof and an intra-Hanafi dispute. The passage begins by stating the ruling on the time of sadaqat al-fitr: it should be paid before the 'Eid prayer to free the poor from preoccupation. This is based on the hadith of Ibn 'Umar (in Bukhari and Muslim) and the Quranic verse 'Qad aflaha man tazakka'. Then it addresses the dispute on saying takbir aloud on the way to the 'Eid prayer. Abu Hanifa holds that takbir should not be said aloud on the way to 'Eid al-Fitr, while the two companions (Abu Yusuf and Muhammad al-Shaybani) hold that it should be said aloud, drawing an analogy (qiyas) from 'Eid al-Adha. Abu Hanifa's argument is based on the principle that the original ruling for dhikr is concealment (ikhfa'), and the text that allows loudness is specific to 'Eid al-Adha because it is a day of takbir associated with Hajj. The passage then raises an objection: the Quranic verse 'And to complete the period and to magnify Allah' is general and includes 'Eid al-Fitr; also a hadith from 'Umar mentions the Prophet raising his voice with takbir on both 'Eids. The Hanafi response, implicit in Abu Hanifa's position, is that the verse and hadith are understood in the context of 'Eid al-Adha only, as the general practice of the Prophet on 'Eid al-Fitr was not to say takbir aloud. The relied-upon (mufta bihi) position in the Hanafi school is that of Abu Hanifa, but both views are transmitted. The passage also includes the recommendation to walk to the 'Eid ground and the exception for the weak to pray in the mosque, based on the practice of 'Ali.

Key Terms
صدقه الفطر (ṣadaqat al-fiṭr): A charity obligatory upon every free Muslim who has enough to support himself and his dependents, to be paid before the 'Eid al-Fitr prayer.
تکبیر (takbīr): The utterance 'Allahu Akbar' on the way to the 'Eid prayer.

The Intra-Hanafi Dispute on Takbir on 'Eid al-Fitr
Intra-Hanafi dispute on saying takbir aloud on 'Eid al-Fitr

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to explain the intra-Hanafi dispute on saying takbir aloud on 'Eid al-Fitr, state the arguments of Abu Hanifa and the Sahibayn, and identify the mufta bihi position.

Core Idea
Abu Hanifa holds takbir on 'Eid al-Fitr should be silent; Sahibayn analogize it to 'Eid al-Adha and say aloud.

This passage presents a classic intra-Hanafi disagreement over the recitation of takbir on the way to the 'Eid al-Fitr prayer. Imam Abu Hanifa and his two main disciples differ in their reasoning, with Abu Hanifa relying on the principle that dhikr is best done silently, while the Sahibayn extend the rule from 'Eid al-Adha via qiyas. The passage also addresses an objection from the opponent citing general texts.

Guided Path
1
State the positions
Abu Hanifa: do not say takbir aloud on the way to 'Eid al-Fitr. Sahibayn: say it aloud.
2
Analyze Abu Hanifa's dalil
The original principle (asl) for dhikr is concealment (ikhfa'). Quran 7:205 and hadith support this. Loudness is a departure from the principle, only legislated for 'Eid al-Adha because of its connection to Hajj (Quran 2:203). Thus, it does not apply to 'Eid al-Fitr.
Abu Hanifa's argument is based on maintaining the original rule unless a specific text (nass) overrides it.
3
Analyze Sahibayn's dalil
They use analogical reasoning (qiyas) from 'Eid al-Adha: both are 'Eids involving a congregational prayer, so the same ruling of loud takbir should apply. They also cite the general verse 'And to complete the period and to magnify Allah' (2:185) and a hadith from 'Umar that the Prophet raised his voice on both 'Eids.
The Sahibayn see a shared 'illa (effective cause) between the two 'Eids.
4
Examine the Hanafi response
Abu Hanifa's response: the 'illa (being a day of takbir) is not shared because 'Eid al-Adha is specifically associated with Hajj rites (the 'illa is the Hajj connection). 'Eid al-Fitr lacks this. The general verse and hadith are specific to 'Eid al-Adha as per the exegetes. Hence, qiyas is invalid.
This is a case of rejection of qiyas due to difference in 'illa.
5
Determine the mufta bihi
Al-Marghinani in Al-Hidayah favors Abu Hanifa's view, and it is the relied-upon (mufta bihi) position in the school. However, both opinions are accepted and valid.
The intra-Hanafi dispute shows the richness of the school's ijtihad.

Commentary
This is a ruling with proof and an intra-Hanafi dispute. The passage begins by stating the ruling on the time of sadaqat al-fitr: it should be paid before the 'Eid prayer to free the poor from preoccupation. This is based on the hadith of Ibn 'Umar (in Bukhari and Muslim) and the Quranic verse 'Qad aflaha man tazakka'. Then it addresses the dispute on saying takbir aloud on the way to the 'Eid prayer. Abu Hanifa holds that takbir should not be said aloud on the way to 'Eid al-Fitr, while the two companions (Abu Yusuf and Muhammad al-Shaybani) hold that it should be said aloud, drawing an analogy (qiyas) from 'Eid al-Adha. Abu Hanifa's argument is based on the principle that the original ruling for dhikr is concealment (ikhfa'), and the text that allows loudness is specific to 'Eid al-Adha because it is a day of takbir associated with Hajj. The passage then raises an objection: the Quranic verse 'And to complete the period and to magnify Allah' is general and includes 'Eid al-Fitr; also a hadith from 'Umar mentions the Prophet raising his voice with takbir on both 'Eids. The Hanafi response, implicit in Abu Hanifa's position, is that the verse and hadith are understood in the context of 'Eid al-Adha only, as the general practice of the Prophet on 'Eid al-Fitr was not to say takbir aloud. The relied-upon (mufta bihi) position in the Hanafi school is that of Abu Hanifa, but both views are transmitted. The passage also includes the recommendation to walk to the 'Eid ground and the exception for the weak to pray in the mosque, based on the practice of 'Ali.

قیاس (qiyās): Analogical reasoning used by the Sahibayn to extend the ruling of loud takbir from 'Eid al-Adha to 'Eid al-Fitr.

Paying Zakat al-Fitr Before Eid Prayer
Ruling with proof on the timing of zakat al-fitr before Eid prayer.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the preferred time for paying zakat al-fitr according to the Hanafi school and explain the proofs.

Core Idea
Zakat al-fitr must be given before the Eid prayer to enrich the poor for the day.

This passage explains when zakat al-fitr should be paid. The Hanafi school emphasises that it must be given before the people go out for the Eid prayer, based on the Prophet's command and practice. We will examine the evidence and the underlying wisdom.

Guided Path
1
Identify the act and its ruling
The act is paying zakat al-fitr. The Hanafi ruling is that it is wajib and must be given before the Eid prayer.
2
Locate the dalil from the Prophet's practice
The Prophet commanded and used to give zakat al-fitr one or two days before Eid, showing the preferred time.
This is a primary evidence.
3
Locate the dalil from the Prophet's saying
The Prophet said, 'Make them independent from begging' (aghunuhum), implying that payment before Eid prevents the poor from asking on the day.
The command 'aghunuhum' indicates obligation.
4
Locate the dalil from the Quran
The verse 'Indeed successful is he who purifies himself' is interpreted to refer to giving zakat al-fitr before the Eid prayer.
This verse is indirect and requires interpretation.
5
Understand the wisdom behind the timing
The purpose is to free the poor from needing to ask on Eid, allowing them to celebrate, which aligns with the spirit of the occasion.
