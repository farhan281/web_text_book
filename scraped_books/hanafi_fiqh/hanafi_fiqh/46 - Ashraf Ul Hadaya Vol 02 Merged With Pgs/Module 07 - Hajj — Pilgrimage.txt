Ta'rif: Why Imitating Pilgrims on Arafah Day is Invalid
Declares ta'rif invalid, proves by qiyas on location-specific Hajj rites.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state why the Hanafi school rules ta'rif as invalid and apply the qiyas reasoning to similar cases of location-specific rituals.

Core Idea
Standing at Arafah is location-specific; imitating it elsewhere is not worship and earns no reward.

Many people gather on the day of Arafah outside of Arafat to make du'a and humble themselves, believing they are following the pilgrims. This lesson examines the Hanafi ruling on this practice, known as ta'rif. The text declares it 'laysa bi shay' (nothing) and provides a clear qiyas argument: just as other Hajj rites cannot be performed outside their designated places, the standing at Arafah is tied to the location of Arafat. Understanding this distinction protects the sanctity of the specific rites of Hajj.

Guided Path
1
Identify the act and its ruling
Recognise what ta'rif is: a gathering on the day of Arafah in a field other than Arafat to imitate the pilgrims. The text rules it as 'laysa bi shay' – nothing valid, no reward.
The ruling is that it is not a legitimate act of worship; it is makruh tahrimi and a bid'ah.
2
Locate the dalil (proof)
The proof is a qiyas jaliy: wuquf at Arafah is an act of worship specific to the location of Arafat. Therefore, performing it elsewhere is invalid.
The source explicitly compares it to other Hajj rites (safa, marwa, etc.) that cannot be moved.
3
Trace the wajh al-dalalah (aspect of evidence)
The effective cause (illa) is that wuquf is 'makhsus bi makan makhsus' (specific to a particular place). This illa is shared with all Hajj rites that are location-bound.
The text uses the phrase 'كالسائر المناسك' (like the other rites) to confirm the analogy.
4
Note potential ikhtilaf
Some may argue that gathering for du'a on Arafah day is permissible as an independent act of worship. The Hanafi school responds that such imitation distorts the specific nature of Hajj rites and may lead to innovation.
Sahib al-Kifayah even warns that circumambulating a mosque other than the Ka'bah could lead to kufr if done as alteration.
5
Evaluate tarjih (preferred view)
The relied-upon (mufta bihi) position is that ta'rif is not a valid act; it should be avoided. The authority is zahir al-riwayah (attributed to Sahib al-Quduri).
This is consistent with the principle that acts of worship are tawqifi (based on revelation) and cannot be innovated.

Commentary
This passage is a ruling with proof that the practice known as 'ta'rif' (i.e., gathering on the day of Arafah outside of Arafat to imitate the pilgrims) is legally baseless and not an act of worship. The term 'ta'rif' refers to people assembling in some open space on the 9th of Dhul Hijjah to make du'a and show humility, mimicking the pilgrims at Arafat. The text declares it 'laysa bi shay' (nothing), meaning it has no legal standing and no reward. The proof is based on the principle (qiyas jali) that standing at Arafah is an act of worship that is specifically tied to the location of Arafat. Just as other Hajj rites (like sa'i between Safa and Marwa) cannot be performed elsewhere, this imitation is invalid. The commentary from Sahib al-Quduri reinforces this: since the act is location-specific, doing it elsewhere cannot earn reward. The commentary further cites Sahib al-Kifayah who warns that mimicking a specific worship in a different place could even lead to kufr if done as a deliberate alteration (like circumambulating a mosque other than the Ka'bah). Note: The text cuts off mentioning a possible objection about Ibn Abbas, but the ruling remains that ta'rif is not a recognized act of worship.

Key Terms
تعریف (ta'rif): The practice of gathering on the day of Arafah in a field other than Arafat to imitate the pilgrims in supplication, which the text declares invalid.
موقوف عرفہ (wuquf 'arafah): Standing at Arafat is a specific Hajj rite that must be performed at the location of Arafat on the 9th of Dhul Hijjah.

The Ruling on al-Ta'rīf: Imitating Wuqūf Outside 'Arafat
Declares al-Ta'rīf invalid via qiyas

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the Hanafi ruling on al-Ta'rīf and explain the qiyas reasoning that invalidates it.

Core Idea
Standing at 'Arafah is place-specific; imitating it elsewhere is not worship.

You may have seen gatherings on the day of 'Arafah where people assemble in fields to pray and supplicate, imitating the pilgrims. What is the Islamic ruling on such practices? This lesson examines al-Ta'rīf through the Hanafi lens, learning why the school considers it a blameworthy innovation and how the principle of qiyas on location-specific rites leads to that conclusion.

Guided Path
1
Identify the Practice
Read the text and define al-Ta'rīf: gathering on the day of 'Arafah in a place outside 'Arafat, imitating the pilgrims' standing (wuqūf).
The Arabic text states: 'وهو ان يجمع الناس يوم عرفة فى بعض المواضع تشبيها بالواقفين بعرفة'.
2
State the Hanafi Ruling
The text declares it 'ليس بشئ' (nothing) and explicitly denies reward. State the ruling: it is not a valid act of worship; it is discouraged and potentially haram if done with the intent of imitation.
See the hanafi_position_box: hukm_level = haram.
3
Understand the Qiyas Argument
The proof is qiyas: The asl is wuqūf at 'Arafah during Hajj, which is an ibadah specific to that place (عبادة مختصة بمكان مخصوص). The far' is gathering elsewhere. The illa is place-specificity. Since the asl's ruling (being worship) depends on the place, the far' cannot have that ruling.
The text analogizes to other Hajj rites (سائر المناسك).
4
Address the Objection from Ibn Abbas
The text anticipates an objection: Ibn Abbas gathered people in Basra on 'Arafah day. The response distinguishes between intentional imitation (تشبه) and mere supplication (دعاء). The Hanafi response preserves the principle while acknowledging the Companion's action.
This shows the importance of niyyah in differentiating between permissible and impermissible acts.

Commentary
This passage addresses the practice of al-Ta'rīf, where people gather on the day of 'Arafah outside 'Arafat to pray and supplicate, imitating the pilgrims. The Hanafi ruling is that this practice is not a valid act of worship and carries no reward. The proof is that standing at 'Arafah (wuqūf) is a specific rite tied to that particular location, analogous to other Hajj rites that cannot be performed elsewhere. The text cites al-Quduri and al-Kifayah for emphasis. An objection is raised: Ibn Abbas did something similar in Basra. The response is that his intent was pure supplication, not ritual imitation. Thus, the practice is rejected as a bid'ah.

Key Terms
التعريف (al-ta'rīf): The practice of gathering on the day of 'Arafah outside 'Arafat to imitate the pilgrims.
الوقوف بعرفة (al-wuqūf bi-'Arafah): Standing at 'Arafat on the 9th of Dhul Hijjah, a fundamental rite of Hajj.
