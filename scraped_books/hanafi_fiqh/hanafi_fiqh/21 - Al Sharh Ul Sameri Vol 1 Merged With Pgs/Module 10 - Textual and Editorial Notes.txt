Footnotes for Hadith References
Textual footnotes explaining the hadith and athar cited in previous lessons

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the three footnote hadith/athar and identify which ruling each supports.

Core Idea
Footnotes anchor rulings to their proofs – knowing the hadith text is essential for verifying the ruling.

In the previous lessons, we learned rulings about hunting in ihram and prohibitions in the Haram. Those rulings were based on specific hadith and athar. This lesson presents the full text of those proofs as footnotes, so you can see the exact wording and strengthen your understanding of the evidence.

Guided Path
1
Identify the footnote letter
Each footnote is labeled (الف), (ب), or (ج). Start by locating each letter in the passage and reading the attached text.
2
Read the hadith or athar
Read the full Arabic and English translation. Note the key phrases: for (الف) it's about eating wild donkey while in ihram; for (ب) it's about the prohibitions in the Haram; for (ج) it's about the penalty for a pigeon killed in the Haram.
3
Recall the ruling it supports
Connect each footnote to the ruling it proves. (الف) supports the permissibility of eating wild donkey for a muhrim. (ب) supports the prohibition of cutting trees, hunting, etc. in the Haram, with an exception for idhkhir. (ج) supports the ruling that killing a pigeon in the Haram requires a penalty of three sheep.

حاشیہ : (الف) میں نے کہا یارسول اللہ ! میں نے وحشی گدھا شکار کیا ہے اور میرے پاس کچھ بچا ہوا ہے۔ آپ نے قوم سے فرمایا کھاؤ حالانکہ وہ محرم تھے (ب) آپ نے فرمایا حرم کے کانٹوں کو نہ کاٹے ۔ اس کے شکار کو نہ بھگائے ، اس کے لقطے کو نہ اٹھائے مگر اعلان کرنے کے لئے نہ اس کا گھاس اکھیڑے۔ حضرت عباس نے کہایا رسول اللہ ! نگر از خر گھاس تو آپ نے فرمایا مگر اذخر گھاس اکھاڑ سکتے ہو (ج) حضرت عطاء نے فرمایا ایک آدمی نے کبوتر اور اس کے دو بچے پر دروازہ بند کر دیا پھر عرفات اور مٹی چلا گیا پھر واپس آیا تو کبوتر مر چکے تھے۔ پس حضرت عبداللہ بن عمر کے پاس آئے اور اس کا تذکرہ کیا تو حضرت ابن عمر نے اس پر تین بکریاں لازم کی اور ان کے ساتھ ایک آدمی نے بھی فیصلہ کیا۔
Footnote: (الف) I said: O Messenger of Allah, I hunted a wild donkey and I have some left. He said to the people: Eat, while they were in ihram. (ب) He said: Do not cut its thorns, do not chase its game, do not pick up its lost items except to announce them, and do not uproot its grass. Abbas said: O Messenger of Allah, except idhkhir grass. He said: Except idhkhir grass. (ج) Ata said: A man closed his door on a pigeon and its two chicks, then went to Arafat and Mina, and returned to find them dead. He came to Abdullah ibn Umar and mentioned that, so Ibn Umar imposed on him three sheep, and a man (co-judge) judged with him.

Commentary
This passage contains the footnotes (الف), (ب), and (ج) that were referenced in the earlier rulings. They provide the full text of the hadith and athar. (الف) is the hadith of Abu Qatada regarding eating wild donkey meat while in ihram. (ب) is the hadith of Ibn Abbas regarding the prohibitions in the Haram. (ج) is the athar of Ata about the penalty for killing a pigeon in the Haram. These footnotes are essential for verifying the proofs.

Key Terms
إحرام (ihram): The state of consecration for Hajj or Umrah during which certain actions are prohibited.
حرم (haram): The sacred territory of Makkah; specific rules apply within its boundaries.
أثر (athar): A narration from a Companion or a later authority, as opposed to a hadith from the Prophet.
إذخر (idhkhir): A type of grass that is exempted from the prohibition of uprooting within the Haram.
