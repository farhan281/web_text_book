Nafal, mut'ah, and qiran hady must be slaughtered only on the 10th of Dhul Hijjah, as indicated by Q. 22:29.

This passage from the matn and its commentary establishes a critical timing rule for three specific types of hady. While many sacrificial animals during Hajj can be slaughtered over several days, these three are confined to yawm al-nahr. Slaughter of Other Hady at Any Time

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state which types of hady may be slaughtered at any time and explain the proof from the Prophet's action at Hudaybiyya.

Core Idea
Penalty, ihsar, and substitute hady can be slaughtered any time; they are not tied to yawm al-nahr.

In the previous rule, you learned that nafl, tamattu', and qiran hady are restricted to yawm al-nahr (10th Dhul Hijjah). But what about other hady? This lesson covers penalty (jinyat), ihsar (being blocked), and game-substitute hady. The matn states they can be slaughtered at any time. What is the proof? The Prophet's own action at Hudaybiyya, where he sacrificed his hady in Dhul Qa'dah, not on yawm al-nahr.

Identify the types of hady covered
The rule applies to jinyat (penalty for violations), ihsar (compensation for being blocked), and substitute for game (fidyah). These are distinct from nafl, tamattu', and qiran hady which are time-restricted.
The commentary explicitly lists these three categories.

State the ruling
For these three types, slaughtering is permissible at any time—any day of the year. There is no requirement to slaughter on yawm al-nahr or even within the Haram (according to the Hanafi school).

The proof is a fi'li (action) of the Prophet. At the Treaty of Hudaybiyya (Dhul Qa'dah, 6 AH), the Prophet slaughtered his hady despite it not being the 10th of Dhul Hijjah. This establishes that these hady are not time-specific.
Dalil type: Sunnah ahad (though widely transmitted). Thubut: qati' (authentic), dalala: zanni (interpretation of the action).

All Hady Must Be Slaughtered in the Haram
Ruling on the location for slaughtering all types of hady

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the Hanafi ruling on the location of hady slaughter and identify the Quranic proofs and qiyas reasoning that establish it.

Core Idea
Every hady, of any type, must be slaughtered within the Haram of Makkah — the location is a condition of validity.

This passage from Al-Sharh al-Sameri lays down a clear rule: no hady may be slaughtered outside the Haram. The author lists every category of hady and provides two Quranic verses as evidence. We will walk through the proofs and also note the one area where Imam Malik disagreed, to sharpen our understanding of the Hanafi position.

Guided Path
1
Identify the ruling and its scope
State the rule: all hady must be in the Haram. List the types mentioned (nafal, tamattu', qiran, substitute for game, jinyat, and ihsar).
The rule is general and applies to all categories without exception in the Hanafi school.
2
Analyze the primary evidence (Quran)
Examine Surah al-Baqarah 2:196. The term 'mahill' (place of sacrifice) is interpreted as the Haram. The verse ties shaving the head to the hady reaching its mahill, implying the Haram is the required location.
This verse is cited as a direct proof.
3
Analyze the supporting evidence (Quran and Athar)
Surah al-Ma'idah 5:95 mentions 'hady reaching the Ka'bah', further indicating the Haram. The athar from Muwatta' Imam Malik reinforces that substitute for game must be slaughtered in Makkah.
The athar is used despite its source being Imam Malik, showing the evidence is accepted generally.
4
Understand the Hanafi reasoning (qiyas)
Slaughtering an animal for worship requires specification by either time (like udhiyah) or place. Since hady is not tied to a specific time, it must be tied to the Haram. This qiyas supports the ruling.
This reasoning is explicitly mentioned in the text: 'it is only an act of worship if specified by time or place'.

Imam Malik holds that ihsar hady can be slaughtered where the pilgrim is prevented, based on the Prophet's action at Hudaybiyya. The Hanafi position maintains the general rule that all hady must be in the Haram, and the details of ihsar are discussed elsewhere.

Riding the Hadi Animal When Necessary
Ruling with proof on riding a Hadi animal when compelled

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the Hanafi ruling on riding a hadi animal and apply the condition of necessity derived from the hadith.

Core Idea
A hadi animal may be ridden only when forced; using it without need violates its charitable purpose.

This passage addresses a practical issue during Hajj: if someone is driving a sacrificial animal (hadi) to the Haram and becomes in need of a mount, may he ride it? The Hanafi school, based on a clear prophetic permission, allows riding only under compulsion and in a fitting manner, till another mount is found. The ruling balances the sanctity of the dedicated animal with human necessity.

Guided Path
1
Identify the act and ruling
The passage states: 'Whoever drives a badanah and is forced to ride it may ride it.' The ruling is permissibility (mubah) under the condition of necessity.
The default prohibition is lifted by necessity.
2
Determine the default rule
Without necessity, riding is not allowed because the hadi is dedicated to charity; one should not benefit from it unnecessarily.
The hadi is a charitable object (sadaqah).
3
Locate the dalil
The proof is a hadith from Jabir ibn Abdullah: the Prophet (ﷺ) said: 'Ride it in a fitting manner when you are forced to, until you find a mount.'
Hadith references: Muslim no.1324; Abu Dawud no.1761.
4
Analyze the wajh al-dalalah
The command 'arkabha' (ride it) indicates permissibility, but it is qualified by the condition 'idha ajta't ilayha' (when you are forced to). This restriction shows that riding is only allowed in necessity.
Without the condition, the command would be general; the condition limits it.

Milking a Hadi Animal: Rules and Proofs
Ruling on milking the hadi animal

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the Hanafi ruling on milking a hadi animal and apply the dalil (hadith and athar) to determine when milk must be given in charity.

Core Idea
Milk of a hadi belongs to charity; use requires compensation.

When a hadi (sacrificial animal) produces milk, the owner may wonder whether to milk it or not. The Hanafi school provides a detailed ruling based on the proximity of slaughter and the principle that everything from a charity animal must go to charity. This lesson unpacks the matn and its proofs from the Sunnah and the practice of the Companions.

Guided Path
1
Identify the act and its ruling
The passage rules on milking a hadi animal. Determine the primary ruling: avoid milking if slaughter is near; if many days remain, milk and give in charity.
The default is not to milk; milking is only permitted when necessary.
2
Locate the primary dalil
The commentary cites a hadith (Bukhari and Muslim) that items like the covering and bridle of the hadi are given in charity. This is used via qiyas awla (a fortiori) to argue that parts of the hadi itself, including milk, must also be given.
The hadith is about external items; the text applies it to internal parts.
3
Examine the supporting athar
An athar from Ali (RA) is quoted: a man bought a cow for sacrifice and it gave birth; Ali said not to drink its milk except what is left over (surplus). This reinforces that milk must be given in charity, not consumed by the owner.
The athar shows the practice of a Companion, considered a secondary proof.
4
Understand the exception clause
If slaughter is imminent, milking is avoided by sprinkling cold water on the udder to stop milk production. This is a recommended method; if milking occurs anyway, the milk must be given in charity.
The cold water technique is a practical remedy to prevent unnecessary milking.
5
Apply the ruling to a scenario
Suppose a hadi camel is purchased 10 days before Eid and gives milk. Since slaughter is delayed, the owner should milk it every day and give the milk in charity. If he accidentally drinks some, he must give its value in charity.
Compensation applies even if the use was accidental.

When a hadi animal dies or is lost before it can be sacrificed, the obligation to replace it depends on the original nature of the offering. Core Idea
Voluntary hady is sacrificed on the spot for the poor; obligatory hady must be replaced, and the owner keeps the original.

The passage from Al-Sharh al-Sameri addresses a practical hajj concern: what to do if a hady animal becomes exhausted and cannot proceed. The Hanafi school differentiates between voluntary (nafl) hady and obligatory (wajib) hady, citing distinct hadith evidence. This lesson will walk through the rulings and their proofs, clarifying the actions required and the underlying reasoning.
