Learning Objective

Core Idea
Touching the Prophet's minbar for blessing is a permissible act of love, not a required worship.

In this brief passage from Thamaratun Najah, the author describes the practice of seeking blessing by placing one's hand on the carved pomegranate knob of the Prophet's minbar. 2

The purpose is 'tabarruk' (seeking blessing) by means of a physical trace of the Prophet (athar). This is a religiously encouraged way to express love and veneration.

Determine the Hukm Level
The Hanafi school classifies this act as mubah (permissible) and even mandub (recommended) when done with proper intention. It is not wajib or fard.
The authority weight is from fatawa mutakhkhirin.

The commentary states that this rummanah no longer exists. The ruling applies to the original relic; touching later replicas is not the same.
This underscores the importance of authenticity in relics.

Section on visiting the Prophet (peace be upon him). (1503) It is recommended to come to Masjid Quba on Saturday or another day and pray therein, and after his supplication to say whatever he likes: O Hearer of cries for help, O Granter of relief to the distressed, O Reliever of the grief of the afflicted, O Answerer of the prayer of the desperate, send blessings upon our master Muhammad and his family, and relieve my grief and sorrow just as You relieved Your Messenger's grief and sorrow in this place, O Tender, O Bestower, O Abundant in kindness and beneficence, O Perpetual of blessings, O Most Merciful of the merciful. And may Allah send blessings upon our master Muhammad and his family and his Companions, and grant them abundant peace forever, O Lord of the worlds, Ameen. It is narrated from Ibn Umar that the Messenger of Allah (peace be upon him) used to visit Quba riding and walking. (Muslim, Chapter on virtue of Masjid Quba, p.585, no. 3389/1399) And that Ibn Umar used to go to Quba every Saturday and would say: I saw the Prophet (peace be upon him) go to it every Saturday. (Muslim, Chapter on virtue of Masjid Quba, p.585, no. 3389/1399).

Commentary
This passage is a ruling with proof. It states the recommended (mustahabb) act of visiting Masjid Quba, located near Madinah. The Prophet (peace be upon him) used to visit it every Saturday, riding or walking. The author recommends going on Saturday or any other day, performing prayer there, and then reciting a specific dua. The dua is a comprehensive supplication seeking relief from distress. The proof is from Sahih Muslim, showing the Prophet's own practice and that of Ibn Umar. In the Hanafi madhhab, such visitation is mustahabb, not wajib, as established by these ahadith.

Key Terms
مستحب (mustahabb): An act that is recommended and rewarded, but not obligatory.
مسجد قباء (Masjid Quba): The first mosque built by the Prophet in Quba, a suburb of Madinah.
صريخ المستصرخين (sarikhu al-mustasrikhin): One who responds to those who call for help.
