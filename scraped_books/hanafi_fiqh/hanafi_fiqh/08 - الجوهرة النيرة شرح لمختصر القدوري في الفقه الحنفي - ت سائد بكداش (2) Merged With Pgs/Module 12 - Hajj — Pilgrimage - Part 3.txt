When performing the ritual stoning (ramy) during Hajj, what can you throw at the jamarat? The Hanafi school permits any substance from the genus of the earth—dirt, clay, sand—as long as it is thrown in a contemptuous manner (istihanah). Precious stones like turquoise or ruby are thus invalid because they are valuable and not contemptuous. The Shafi'i school, however, restricts the material to stone (hajar) only. Identify the Hanafi condition
The Hanafi ruling hinges on the condition of 'istihanah' (contempt). The substance must be something that is thrown as a degrading object, not something honored or valuable.
This condition is derived from the general permissibility of earth materials.

Purity (Taharah) in Tawaf: Hanafi Position and Dispute
The ruling on purity, awrah, and najas in Tawaf

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the Hanafi ruling on purity in Tawaf, explain the evidence and the dispute with Imam al-Shafi'i, and identify the intra-Hanafi ikhtilaf on whether purity is wajib or sunnah.

Core Idea
Purity is not a condition for Tawaf according to Hanafis, but it is wajib (obligatory) to perform; neglecting it does not invalidate the Tawaf but is sinful.

This lesson examines the passage concerning purity (taharah) during Tawaf. The author presents the Hanafi position that cleanliness from hadath and najas is not a condition for validity, contrary to Imam al-Shafi'i. Within the Hanafi school, there is also a difference of opinion on whether it is wajib or sunnah, with the relied-upon view being wajib.

Guided Path
1
Identify the ruling on purity in Tawaf according to Hanafis
The text states that if one neglects purity, his Tawaf is permissible (ja'iz) meaning it is valid. This is based on the analogy with standing at Arafah, which is also a pillar of Hajj and does not require purity.
The proof is qiyas: Tawaf is a rukn of Hajj, and purity is not a condition for other arkan like wuquf.
2
Analyze the hadith used by Shafi'is
The hadith 'Tawaf around the House is prayer' is cited. The Hanafi response is that this hadith encourages reverence but does not impose all legal conditions of salah.
The hadith is zanni al-thubut and zanni al-dalalah; Hanafis give it a limited scope.
3
Understand the intra-Hanafi dispute
Later Hanafi scholars debated whether purity is wajib or sunnah. Ibn Shuja' said sunnah; Abu Bakr al-Razi said wajib. The relied-upon (mufta bihi) view is that it is wajib.
This means one should perform Tawaf in a state of purity, but if not, the Tawaf is valid and only a sin is incurred (if wajib) or no sin (if sunnah).
4
Examine the rulings for awrah and najas
The text also briefly covers covering awrah and impurity on clothes. Exposing the awrah beyond the limit for salah requires a dam (compensatory sacrifice) but does not invalidate Tawaf. Najas more than a dirham size is disliked but no expiation.
This further supports that purity and covering awrah are not conditions.

Times for Stoning the Jamarat
Time classification for stoning over four days

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to classify the permitted and preferred times for stoning the jamarat over each of the four days according to the Hanafi school.

Core Idea
The best time for stoning is after noon; stoning before sunrise or after sunset is disliked.

This passage from Al-Jawharah al-Nayirah explains the times for stoning the jamarat over the four days of Hajj. Understanding these time categories ensures that you perform this wajib act at the optimal time, avoiding makruh times. We will trace the classifications for each day.

Guided Path
1
Identify the four days
The text lists the Day of Nahr (10th) and three days after it (11th, 12th, 13th). Each day has different time rulings.
2
Analyze first day classifications
On day one, after dawn until sunrise is makruh, after sunrise until noon is sunnah, after noon until sunset is mubah, and after sunset until dawn is makruh.
3
Analyze second and third days
On days two and three, from dawn until noon is la yajuz (not permissible), after noon until sunset is sunnah, and after sunset until dawn is makruh. Stoning at night before dawn is permissible with no penalty.
4
Understand the fourth day per Abu Hanifa
According to Abu Hanifa, the entire day from dawn to sunset is valid for stoning. However, before noon is makruh and after noon is sunnah.
Other scholars restrict the fourth day to before noon.

Times for Stoning the Jamarat on the First Three Days
Explains the permissible and recommended times for ramy on the first three days.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the Hanafi ruling on the permissible, recommended, and disliked times for stoning the jamarat on the Day of Nahr and the following two days.

Core Idea
Stoning before noon on the second and third days is invalid; the recommended time is afternoon until sunset.

Stoning the jamarat (ramy) is a wajib act of Hajj performed over several days. The time for stoning varies each day, and performing it outside the designated time may invalidate the act or render it disliked. This lesson covers the Hanafi timings for the Day of Nahr and the first three days of Tashreeq, as explained in the commentary on Al-Quduri's Mukhtasar.

Guided Path
1
Identify the days of ramy
Ramy is performed on the Day of Nahr (10th Dhul Hijjah) and the following three days (11th, 12th, and 13th). The passage covers the first three days: Day of Nahr and the next two days (11th and 12th).
The 13th day has its own rulings not detailed here.
2
Understand the time categories
The text categorizes times as: makruh (disliked), sunnah (recommended), mubah (permissible), and la yajuz (not allowed). Know these terms.
Makruh here is tanzihi (disliked but not sinful).
3
Distinguish the Day of Nahr from the following days
On the Day of Nahr, stoning is only at Jamrat al-Aqaba. On the following days, all three pillars are stoned. The times differ: on Day of Nahr, stoning is allowed from dawn to sunset, with different parts of that window being makruh, sunnah, or mubah.
After sunset on Day of Nahr, stoning is makruh.
4
Apply the ruling for the second and third days
On the 11th and 12th, stoning before midday (zawal) is not allowed. The sunnah time is from midday to sunset. Stoning after sunset until dawn of the next day is permissible but makruh.
If one stones before midday, it must be repeated after noon.
5
Understand the principle of standing (wuquf)
The passage mentions that after stoning the first two pillars on days when another ramy follows, one should stand and make du'a. After the last stoning (Jamrat al-Aqaba on the last day), no standing is done.
This is based on practice of the Prophet (ﷺ).
