The Five Categories of Shar'i Rulings
Definition and classification of shar'i rulings

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the five categories of shar'i rulings and identify which subcategories belong to each.

Core Idea
Shari'ah rulings are five: beliefs, worship, transactions, penalties, and expiations.

Fiqh is often approached through individual rulings, but every ruling belongs to one of five overarching domains. This passage from al-Jawhara al-Nayirah lays out the entire landscape of shari'ah in a clear taxonomy. Mastering this classification gives you the mental map for organizing all fiqh knowledge.

Guided Path
1
Identify the five major categories
Read the first sentence and list the five categories: i'tiqadat, ibadat, mu'amalat, uqubat, kaffarat. These form the complete set of mashru'at.
Memorize these five as the skeleton of all fiqh.
2
Break down each category into its five subtypes
For each category, note its five subcategories as listed in the passage. For example, beliefs include faith in Allah, angels, books, messengers, and the Last Day.
Some subtypes may vary across madhabs; here it is the Hanafi enumeration.
3
Analyze the reclassification of worship
The author notes that the five acts of worship reduce to three types: purely physical (salah, sawm, jihad), purely financial (zakat), and composite (hajj). Consider why this reclassification is useful for understanding obligations.
This shows how fiqh categorizes acts by their nature.

وترجع العبادات الخمس إلى ثلاثة أنواع:
بدني محض، كالصلاة والصوم والجهاد.
ومالي محض، كالزكاة.
ومركب منهما، كالحج.
The legislated matters are five: beliefs, acts of worship, transactions, penalties, and expiations. Beliefs are five: belief in Allah, His angels, His books, His messengers, and the Last Day. Acts of worship are five: prayer, fasting, zakat, pilgrimage, and jihad. Transactions are five: exchanges, marriages, disputes, trusts, and partnerships. Penalties are five deterrents: deterrent for taking life, like retribution; deterrent for taking property, like amputation for theft; deterrent for violating modesty, like flogging and stoning; deterrent for slandering honor, like the hadd for false accusation; deterrent for renouncing allegiance, like execution for apostasy. Expiations are five: expiation for killing, expiation for zihar, expiation for breaking fast, expiation for oath, expiation for violations during pilgrimage. The five acts of worship reduce to three types: purely physical, like prayer, fasting, and jihad; purely financial, like zakat; and composite of both, like hajj.

Commentary
This passage is a definition and classification of all shar'i rulings into five overarching categories. The author begins by stating that the Shar'i injunctions (al-mashru'at) are five types: beliefs (i'tiqadat), acts of worship (ibadat), transactions (mu'amalat), penalties (uqubat), and expiations (kaffarat). Each category is then further subdivided into five subcategories. Beliefs are the six articles of faith. Acts of worship include the five pillars (prayer, fasting, zakat, hajj, jihad). Transactions cover exchange contracts, marriage, litigation, trust contracts, and partnerships. Penalties are deterrents for specific crimes: retribution for murder, amputation for theft, flogging/stoning for zina, hadd for false accusation, and execution for apostasy. Expiations are required for killing, zihar (a form of divorce), breaking a fast, breaking an oath, and violations during hajj. Finally, the author notes that the five acts of worship can be classified into three types based on the nature of their performance: purely physical (like prayer and fasting), purely financial (zakat), and a combination of both (hajj). The commentary serves to clarify the structure of Shari'ah as taught in the Hanafi school.

Key Terms
المشروعات (al-mashru'at): The matters legislated by Shari'ah, i.e., rulings that are prescribed for the community.
الاعتقادات (al-i'tiqadat): Matters of faith and belief, the foundation of iman.
العبادات (al-ibadat): Acts of worship, including prayer, fasting, zakat, hajj, and jihad.
المعاملات (al-mu'amalat): Transactions between people, covering exchanges, marriages, disputes, trusts, and partnerships.
العقوبات (al-uqubat): Penalties and deterrents for crimes.
الكفارات (al-kaffarat): Expiations to compensate for certain violations.
المزاجر (al-mazajir): Deterrent penalties intended to prevent crime.
