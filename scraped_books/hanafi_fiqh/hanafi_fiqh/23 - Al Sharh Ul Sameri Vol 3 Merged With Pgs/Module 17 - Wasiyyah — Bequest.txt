Mudabbar's Freedom Limited to One-Third: The Ruling and Calculation
Ruling with proof and calculation example

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to apply the Hanafi ruling that a mudabbar is freed from one-third of the master's estate and calculate the slave's obligation to earn the remainder.

Core Idea
Tadbir is like a will — freedom is limited to one-third; the slave must earn the excess.

When a master declares his slave a mudabbar and then dies, the slave does not automatically become fully free. Instead, his freedom is limited to one-third of the master's total estate, because tadbir is treated as a bequest (wasiyyah). This lesson unpacks the ruling, walks through a concrete calculation, and examines the prophetic proof. Understanding this ensures that the heirs' inheritance rights are protected while the slave earns his complete freedom through labor.

Guided Path
1
Identify the Act and Ruling
Recognize that the mudabbar's freedom upon the master's death is effective but confined to one-third of the estate. This is because tadbir is analogous to a will, which is limited to one-third.
The full freedom is contingent on the slave's value being covered by one-third of the estate.
2
Understand the Analogy to Wasiyyah
Tadbir is treated as a bequest of freedom. In Hanafi law, a will cannot exceed one-third of the estate unless the heirs consent. Thus, the mudabbar's freedom is capped accordingly.
The heirs' share is two-thirds, so the slave's labor (sa'y) compensates for any shortfall.
3
Apply the One-Third Calculation
Follow the text's example: if the total estate (including the slave's value) is 900 and the slave is worth 300, one-third of 900 equals 300 → the slave is fully freed. If only the slave remains worth 600, one-third of that (200) is freed, and the slave must earn the remaining 400.
The master's estate includes the slave as an asset; one-third of the total estate determines the freed portion.
4
Cite the Dalil
The hadith from Ibn Umar states: 'The mudabbar cannot be sold nor given as a gift, and he is free from one-third.' This directly establishes the one-third limit.
The hadith's phrase 'free from one-third' (huwa ḥurrun min al-thulth) is the core evidence.
5
Determine the Slave's Obligation
If the mudabbar's value exceeds the freed portion, he must work (sa'y) to earn the excess and pay the heirs, who then distribute it as inheritance.
The slave's labor is a form of compensation for the heirs' lost asset.

Commentary
This ruling explains how the mudabbar is freed upon the master's death. He is not automatically free entirely; rather, his freedom is limited to one-third of the master's estate because tadbir is analogous to a will (wasiyyah). The text gives a clear example: if the total estate including the mudabbar is 900 pounds and the slave's value is 300 pounds, then one-third of the estate equal the slave's value, so he is fully free. If the only asset is the slave worth 600 pounds, one-third of that (200 pounds) is freed, and the remaining two-thirds (400 pounds) must be worked off (sa'ya) by the slave, paid to the heirs. The hadith of Ibn Umar is cited again supporting that the mudabbar is free from one-third. The footnote also contains the same hadith. The calculation demonstrates the application of the one-third rule.

Key Terms
سعی (saʿy): Earning money by working to pay off a debt; here, the mudabbar works to pay the two-thirds value to the heirs.
وصیت (wasiyyah): A will; tadbir is treated as a bequest of freedom, limited to one-third of the estate.
