Fasting Two Consecutive Months for Zihar Kaffarah: Conditions and Excluded Days
Ruling on fasting as alternative kaffarah with forbidden days

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the conditions for valid zihar kaffarah fasting and identify which days invalidate consecutiveness.

Core Idea
Zihar kaffarah fasting must be consecutive with no prohibited days in between.

This lesson examines the second tier of zihar kaffarah: fasting two consecutive months. The text specifies that certain days—Ramadan, both Eids, and the days of Tashriq—cannot be included within the two-month period. Understanding these exclusions is essential because they affect the validity of the fast. We will also look at an athar from Ata that reinforces this rule.

Guided Path
1
Identify the act and its ruling
When the person who made zihar cannot free a slave, the kaffarah becomes fasting two consecutive months. This fasting is wajib (obligatory) as an alternative.
The ruling is conditional on inability to free a slave.
2
Recognize the condition of consecutiveness
The two months must be fasted consecutively (mutatabi'ayn). Any break without a valid excuse invalidates the sequence and requires restarting.
Valid breaks include menstruation or illness, but these may require restarting according to some views.
3
List the excluded days
The text explicitly excludes the month of Ramadan, the day of Eid al-Fitr, the day of Eid al-Adha (Yawm al-Nahr), and the three days of Tashriq (11-13 Dhul Hijjah). These days cannot fall within the two-month period.
Fasting on these days is either prohibited (Eids, Tashriq) or already obligatory (Ramadan) and thus cannot serve as kaffarah.
4
Apply the consequence
If any of these excluded days occurs during the two months, the fast is not considered consecutive. The athar from Ata states that the person must restart the fast.
This ensures that the two months are entirely voluntary and uninterrupted by prohibited or already occupied days.

Commentary
This passage states the second option for zihar kaffarah: if the person cannot find a slave to free (or lacks the means), he must fast two consecutive months. The commentary explains that the fast must be consecutive; if any day of Ramadan, Eid al-Fitr, Eid al-Adha, or the three days of Tashriq (11-13 Dhul Hijjah) occurs during these two months, the fast is broken or not valid because fasting on those days is prohibited or incomplete. Thus, one must start the fast after these days or ensure they do not fall in between. An athar from Ata is cited: if Ramadan or Nahr occurs in between, the person must restart the fast.

Key Terms
متتابعين (mutatabi'ayn): Consecutive, without interruption, except for permissible breaks like menstruation.
ايام التشريق (ayyam al-tashriq): The three days following Eid al-Adha (11-13 Dhul Hijjah), during which fasting is prohibited.

Later Specification (Takhṣīṣ) for Fasts of the Same Genus
Ruling on sufficiency of general intention with later specification.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the Hanafi ruling on later takhṣīṣ for fasts of the same genus and apply it to examples.

Core Idea
A general intention suffices for multiple fasts of the same type; later specification is valid.

This passage from a Hanafi text addresses a practical facilitation: when a person has multiple outstanding obligatory fasts that belong to the same category (e.g., several missed Ramadan fasts), he does not need to specify which particular fast he is performing at the time of fasting. A general intention is sufficient, and he can later assign the fast to any of the obligations. This ruling demonstrates the Hanafi school's flexibility in matters of intention.

Guided Path
1
Identify the obligation type
First, determine whether the fasts owed are of the same genus (jins) or not. The ruling depends on this.
Fasts of the same jins include multiple missed Ramadan fasts or multiple qada fasts from the same cause.
2
Recall the general rule
If the obligations are of the same jins, a general intention at the time of fasting is sufficient. Specific intention for each is not required at the outset.
This is a facilitation to avoid hardship.
3
Apply later specification
After performing the fast with a general intention, the person can later specify which particular obligation the fast fulfilled. This takhṣīṣ makes the fast valid for that obligation.
The later specification is accepted retroactively in the Hanafi school.
4
Contrast with different genus
If the obligations are of different types (e.g., a missed Ramadan fast and a kaffara fast), then the intention must be specific at the time. Later specification would not be sufficient.
This is the logical opposite of the ruling.

Source Text

بھی متعین کر سکتا ہے۔ جیسے رمضان کے دو روزے ہوں اور ایک روزہ قضا رکھا لیکن کس دن کا قضا ہے متعین نہیں کیا تو بعد میں متعین کر سکتا ہے۔ جس دن کا متعین کرے گا اس دن کا متعین ہو جائے گا، اور اس دن کی ادائیگی ہو جائے گی۔ جنس ایک ہو تو ہر ایک کو خصوصی طور پر متعین کرنا ضروری نہیں ہے۔ بعد میں تخصیص کرنا بھی کافی ہو جائے گا۔
He can specify it later. For example, if one owes two fasts of Ramadan and one qada fast, but did not specify which day the qada is for, then he can specify later. Whichever day he specifies, that day becomes specified, and that day's performance will be fulfilled. If the type is one, it is not necessary to specify each individually. Later specification is also sufficient.
Source pages p. 171

Commentary
This passage presents a ruling from the Hanafi school regarding the intention for multiple obligatory fasts. When a person has several fasts of the same type, such as multiple missed Ramadan fasts or multiple qada fasts, and he performs a fast without specifying which obligation he intends, the fast is valid. He can later specify which particular fast he was performing. This is because when the obligations are of the same genus (jins), a general intention at the time of fasting is sufficient, and the specific allocation can be made later. The example given: if one owes two Ramadan fasts and one qada fast, and he fasts without saying which is which, he can later state that a particular day's fast was for a specific obligation. This ruling facilitates the performance of multiple outstanding fasts without the burden of precise pre-specification.

Key Terms
تخصیص (takhṣīṣ): The act of specifying which particular obligation a performed act fulfills after the act has been done.
جنس (jins): Type or genus of obligation, e.g., all Ramadan fasts are one jins, all qada fasts are another jins.

Key Takeaway
