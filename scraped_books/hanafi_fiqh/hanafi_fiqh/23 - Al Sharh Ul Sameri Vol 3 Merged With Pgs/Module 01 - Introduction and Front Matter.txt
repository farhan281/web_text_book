Commentary
This is the title page and table of contents for the third volume of 'Al-Sharh al-Meer 'ala al-Mukhtasar al-Quduri' (also called 'Al-Sharh al-Samiri'), a Hanafi fiqh commentary. The volume covers from the Book of Nikah (Marriage) up to the Book of Ashriba (Beverages), encompassing a wide range of family law, criminal law, and other topics. The work is known for its extensive hadith references ('a great treasure of hadith'). The commentator is referred to as 'al-Meer' or 'al-Samiri'. This volume includes chapters on marriage, suckling, divorce, khul', zihar, li'an, 'iddah, maintenance, emancipation, tadbir, istilad, mukatab, wala', injuries, blood money, qasamah, 'aqil, hudud (including hadd for drinking and slander), theft, highway robbery, and beverages. The numbering 228-362 likely refers to page numbers or hadith numbers.

Key Terms
النكاح (al-nikah): Marriage contract in Islamic law; the first major topic in this volume.
الاشربة (al-ashriba): Beverages, specifically intoxicating drinks; the last topic in this volume.
المختصر القدوري (al-Mukhtasar al-Quduri): The concise fiqh text by Imam al-Quduri (d. 428 AH) which is the base matn being commented upon.
الشح المير (al-Sharh al-Meer): The title of this commentary, also known as al-Sharh al-Samiri.

Methodology of Al-Sharh al-Thamiri: 12 Features
Lists twelve distinctive features of this commentary

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the distinctive features of Al-Sharh al-Thamiri's commentary methodology and explain how each feature facilitates the study of Hanafi fiqh.

Core Idea
Numbered issues, proofs, principles, and even a calculator make Hanafi fiqh accessible.

When you open a commentary, the author's method shapes how you learn. This passage from the front matter of Al-Sharh al-Thamiri presents twelve deliberate features — from numbering rulings to using a calculator for inheritance. Understanding these features helps you navigate the commentary efficiently and appreciate how the author bridges classical sources with modern pedagogy.

Guided Path
1
Identify the overall purpose
Read the passage as a methodology statement — the author is telling you how he designed the book. Ask: what problem is he solving?
2
Group the features into categories
Categorize the twelve points into: structural (numbering, translation), evidential (proofs, hadith, principles), pedagogical (linguistics, differences, simplification), and modern (calculator).
This helps see the multi-layered approach.
3
Relate each feature to a student benefit
For each feature, articulate how it aids a student of Hanafi fiqh. For example, feature 4 (hadith collection) saves time in finding textual proofs.
4
Assess the source authority
Note feature 11: proofs are taken from al-Hidayah and the Six Authentic Books. This signals the commentary's reliability and its grounding in Zahir al-Riwayah and authentic hadith.
Al-Hidayah is a canonical Hanafi text.
5
Reflect on the calculator innovation
Feature 12 uses a calculator for inheritance. Consider how this modern tool aligns with the faraid methodology — does it replace or supplement the manual method?
This is a practical time-saver but requires understanding the underlying rules.

(1) Each issue has been written separately and numbered so that understanding and extracting the issue is easy.

Commentary
This passage from the front matter of Al-Sharh al-Thamiri Vol. 3 lists twelve distinctive features of the commentary. The author, Maulana 'Umair al-Din Qasimi, explains his methodology: each legal issue (mas'alah) is individually numbered and accompanied by an idiomatic Urdu translation, rational (aqli) and textual (naqli) proofs, hadith citations, the underlying legal principles (usul), linguistic analysis of difficult words, concise notes on differences among the Hanafi imams (faa'idah), and simplified explanations (tashrih) of complex rulings. To maintain student engagement, proofs are kept concise. The sources are authoritative books such as al-Hidayah and the Six Authentic Hadith Collections (Sihah Sittah). Notably, for inheritance (fara'id) calculations, the commentary uses a modern calculator method to expedite solving the entire inheritance case instantly. The passage concludes with the commentator's name and his previous position as a hadith teacher in Manchester, England.

دلیل عقلی (dalil 'aqli): Rational proof based on intellect used to support a ruling
دلیل نقلی (dalil naqli): Textual proof from Quran or Hadith

Navigating the Third Volume of Al Sharh al-Thumairi
Table of contents for Volume 3

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify and locate the major fiqh chapters (kutub) covered in this volume using the table of contents.

Core Idea
The table of contents is your map to the fiqh rulings—knowing the chapter sequence is essential for efficient study.

The third volume of Al Sharh al-Thumairi begins with a detailed table of contents (fihrist) that lists the chapters (kutub) and their page numbers. This front matter is not merely a formality; it is a practical tool for navigating the dense fiqh material. By familiarising yourself with this index, you will be able to quickly locate rulings on emancipation, crimes, prescribed punishments, and more. This lesson ensures you can use the fihrist effectively as you progress through the volume.

Guided Path
1
Identify the structure
The table of contents is organised by consecutive chapter numbers (e.g., 24, 25…) with titles in Arabic/Urdu. Each chapter (kitab) covers a major fiqh topic. Scan the list to see the order: custody, slave expenses, emancipation, contracts, patronage, crimes, blood money, prescribed punishments, theft, and drinks.
Note that some chapters have sub-chapters (bab) like 'Bab al-Tadbir' and 'Bab al-Istilad' under Kitab al-'Itaq.
2
Match page ranges
Each chapter entry is followed by a page range (e.g., '2187 to …'). These refer to the volume's page numbers. Use these ranges to jump directly to the beginning of a chapter you need.
The page numbers are typically numeric (e.g., 2187). Some ranges are incomplete in the scan; check the physical book for full ranges.
3
Recognise the fiqh categories
The chapters cover three broad fiqh areas: 'Ibadat (transactions like emancipation), 'Uqubat (penal law: hudud, diyat), and Mu'amalat (contracts like kitabah). Identify which category each chapter belongs to for a mental map.
Kitab al-'Itaq deals with freeing slaves, a form of 'ibadah. Kitab al-Hudud deals with punishments, a separate discipline.
4
Apply to your study
When you need to study a specific ruling, first consult the fihrist to locate the correct chapter and page range. Then flip to that section. This habit saves time and reinforces your understanding of the book's structure.
For example, to find rulings on false accusation (qadhf), look for 'Bab hadd al-qadhf' under Kitab al-Hudud.
