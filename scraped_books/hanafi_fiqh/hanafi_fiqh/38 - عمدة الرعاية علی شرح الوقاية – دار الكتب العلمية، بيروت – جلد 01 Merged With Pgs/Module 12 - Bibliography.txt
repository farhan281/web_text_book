The Lineage of Sadr al-Shari'ah and Its Significance
Biographical lineage of Sadr al-Shari'ah traced to a Companion

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to trace the lineage of Sadr al-Shari'ah to the Companion 'Ubada ibn al-Samit and explain the importance of such scholarly genealogies in the Hanafi tradition.

Core Idea
Sadr al-Shari'ah's lineage connects him to a Companion, grounding his authority in both blood and scholarship.

This lesson examines a biographical passage that establishes the noble descent of Sadr al-Shari'ah, the commentator of al-Wiqayah. Understanding scholarly lineages is crucial in the Hanafi tradition because it demonstrates the transmission of knowledge through trustworthy chains. By studying this passage, you will appreciate how Hanafi scholars valued both intellectual and genealogical heritage.

Guided Path
1
Identify the key figures
List all the named individuals in the lineage from Sadr al-Shari'ah back to the Companion. Note their titles (e.g., Taj al-Shari'ah, Sadr al-Shari'ah al-Akbar).
This helps you understand the hierarchy of scholars within the family.
2
Map the lineage chain
Draw a vertical mental chart from 'Ubaydullah bin Mas'ud down to 'Ubada ibn al-Samit. Note the number of generations and any gaps.
The chain includes about 12 generations, showing a long scholarly tradition.
3
Examine the sources
Identify the works cited by al-Dumyati and al-Kafawi. Note that al-Dumyati references 'Tarikh Bukhara' as his source.
This demonstrates the reliance on written historical records for verification.
4
Reflect on the significance
Consider why Hanafi scholars emphasized genealogical connections to the Prophet's Companions. How does this strengthen the authority of Sadr al-Shari'ah's legal opinions?
Genealogy provided both social prestige and a link to the early Muslim community.

Sixth Study: On the Biographies of the Author of al-Wiqayah and Sadr al-Shari'ah, the Commentator of al-Wiqayah, their fathers and grandfathers, along with mention of their lineage and attribution. 'Abd al-Mawla al-Dumyati said in Ta'liq al-Anwar 'ala al-Durr al-Mukhtar: 'I saw in the chains of our teacher al-Sayyid Murtada al-Husayni the mention of the lineage of Sadr al-Shari'ah: that he is 'Ubaydullah bin Mas'ud bin Taj al-Shari'ah Mahmud bin Sadr al-Shari'ah al-Akbar Ahmad bin Jamal al-Din Abi al-Makarim 'Ubaydullah bin Ibrahim bin Ahmad bin 'Abd al-Malik bin 'Umayr bin 'Abd al-'Aziz bin Muhammad bin Ja'far bin Khalaf bin Harun bin Muhammad bin Muhammad bin Mahbub bin al-Walid bin 'Ubada bin al-Samit, the Companion, the Ansari, al-Mahbubi. Our teacher said: Thus I saw his lineage in Tarikh Bukhara, and he took from his grandfather Mahmud from his father Ahmad from his father Jamal al-Din 'Ubaydullah bin Ibrahim al-Mahbubi, and this Ahmad is the author of al-Furuq, called al-Talqih.' End of al-Dumyati's statement. Al-Kafawi al-Rumi said in the book A'lam al-Akhyar fi Tabaqat Fuqaha' Madhhab al-Nu'man al-Mukhtar: 'The Imam, the scholar Sadr al-Shari'ah 'Ubaydullah bin Mas'ud bin Taj al-Shari'ah Mahmud bin Sadr al-Shari'ah al-Akbar Ahmad bin Jamal al-Din al-Mahbubi, author of Sharh al-Wiqayah, known...'

Commentary
This is a biographical study, the sixth section of the work, focusing on the authors of al-Wiqayah and its commentator Sadr al-Shari'ah. It traces the lineage of Sadr al-Shari'ah (whose full name is 'Ubaydullah bin Mas'ud) back through several generations to his ancestor Mahbub, then to al-Walid, and finally to the Companion 'Ubada ibn al-Samit al-Ansari. This chain establishes his noble descent. The passage quotes two sources: 'Abd al-Mawla al-Dumyati (from his marginal notes on al-Durr al-Mukhtar) and al-Kafawi al-Rumi (from his biographical dictionary of Hanafi jurists). The lineage is taken from Tarikh Bukhara, confirming the reliability. The passage also notes that the Ahmad mentioned is the author of al-Furuq (known as al-Talqih). This establishes the scholarly pedigree of Sadr al-Shari'ah within the Hanafi tradition.

Key Terms
صدر الشريعة (Sadr al-Shari'ah): Title of 'Ubaydullah bin Mas'ud, the commentator of al-Wiqayah, a major Hanafi jurist.
الوقاية (al-Wiqayah): A famous Hanafi fiqh text (al-Wiqayah fi Masa'il al-Hidayah) by Taj al-Shari'ah Mahmud, on which Sadr al-Shari'ah wrote a commentary.
