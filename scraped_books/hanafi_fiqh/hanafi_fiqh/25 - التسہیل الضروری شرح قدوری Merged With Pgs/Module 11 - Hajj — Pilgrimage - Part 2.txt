The penalty for wearing sewn garments during ihram is graduated by duration: a full day or night requires a sheep, less than that but over an hour requires sadaqah, and under an hour requires a handful of wheat.

Continuity and Multiplicity in Stitched Clothing Penalties
Ruling on wearing stitched clothes for multiple days or multiple types in one day

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the number of dams required for wearing stitched clothing over multiple days or multiple garments in one day, and explain the condition that triggers a new obligation.

One continuous period of wearing stitched clothes requires only one dam; breaking continuity with a sacrifice triggers a new obligation.

This lesson addresses two common scenarios for a muhrim who wears prohibited stitched clothing. The Hanafi answer in both cases is that only one dam is required, provided the wearing is continuous and no expiatory sacrifice has been made in between. Understanding the condition of continuity is key to correctly applying these rulings.

Guided Path

Identify the act and its ruling

Interruption means offering a sacrifice for the violation; simply removing the garment does not break continuity.
3

If the person sacrifices a sheep for the violation after some days, then resumes or continues wearing stitched clothes, a new violation begins and a second dam becomes obligatory.
The sacrifice acts as a legal break; the subsequent wearing is a new, independent violation.

Wearing different stitched garments (e.g., shirt and trousers) on the same day does not multiply the penalty. One dam covers all garments worn in a single continuous period.
The violation is the state of wearing stitched clothing, not the number of garments.
