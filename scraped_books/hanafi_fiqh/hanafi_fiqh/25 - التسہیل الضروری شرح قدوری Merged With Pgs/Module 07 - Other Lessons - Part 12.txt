Permissible and Prohibited Body Parts for Mahrams
Defines permissible and prohibited body parts for mahram looking.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the parts of a mahram woman's body that a Hanafi man may permissibly look at and identify the condition that limits this permission.

Core Idea
A man may look at his mahram's face, head, chest, and legs, but only when safe from desire.

This brief yet precise ruling from a later Hanafi authority clarifies the extent of permissible looking between a man and his permanently unmarriageable female relatives. It is a practical rule based on the principle of necessity and avoidance of fitna, balancing modesty with familial ease. The condition of safety from desire, implied in the source, is critical for correct application.

Guided Path
1
Identify the legal subject
Recognize that 'dhawat maharim' refers to female relatives a man is permanently prohibited from marrying (e.g., mother, sister, daughter, aunt). This is a specific category with relaxed rules compared to non-mahrams.
Mahram status can arise from blood, marriage, or fosterage.
2
List the permissible parts to look at
The text explicitly states: face, head, chest, and legs. Memorise these four. Note that 'legs' (saqayn) includes the lower legs, but not the thighs.
The chest (sadr) is upper torso; some scholars include neck? The text is specific.
3
List the prohibited parts
The text forbids looking at the back (zahr), belly (batn), and thigh (fakhdh). These are considered 'awrah even before mahrams, unless necessary.
The thigh is specifically excluded; it remains 'awrah.
4
Note the implicit condition
Although not repeated in this answer, the earlier commentary (al-Durr al-Mukhtar) conditions permissibility of looking at legs on safety from desire. This applies to all permissible parts: if desire arises, it becomes impermissible.
This condition is rooted in the principle of sadd al-dhara'i' (blocking means to evil).
5
Extend to touching
The text states that touching is allowed on the same parts that may be looked at. This follows the general rule: permissibility of looking implies permissibility of touching if no desire arises.
Touching here means non-sexual contact, e.g., helping with clothing.

Question: There remains the ruling of a man looking at his mahram females; explain it. Answer: It is permissible for a man to look at his mahram females at the face, head, chest, and legs. He should not look at her back, belly, and thigh. There is no harm in touching what he is permitted to look at from them.

Commentary
This passage gives the ruling for looking at mahram female relatives (such as mother, sister, aunt, etc.). The answer specifies the permissible parts: face, head, chest, and legs. Impermissible are the back, belly, and thighs. Touching the permissible parts is also allowed. Note that the earlier commentary (from al-Durr al-Mukhtar) conditioned looking at legs on safety from desire. This condition applies here as well.

Key Terms
ذوات محارم (dhawat maharim): Female relatives whom a man is permanently prohibited from marrying, such as mother, sister, daughter, aunt, etc.
ساقين (saqayn): Legs, from knee to foot? In Hanafi context, the legs are considered 'awrah but subject to conditional permission for mahrams.

Key Takeaway
