Master's Rights Over the Mudabbar and Status of His Children
States the master's retained rights over the mudabbar and the child's status.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to explain the master's retained rights over the mudabbar (istikhdam, mu'ajarah, wat') and derive that the child of a mudabbara is mudabbar, citing the Hanafi proofs from hadith and qawl al-sahabi.

Core Idea
Tadbir limits sale but preserves usufruct; the mudabbar's child shares the mother's contingent freedom.

In this lesson, we examine the precise legal effects of tadbir (postponed manumission). While the master cannot sell the mudabbar, he retains significant usufructuary rights. The passage from Al-Quduri lists these rights and extends the tadbir status to the mudabbar's offspring. We will explore the textual proofs from hadith and companion practice.

Guided Path
1
Identify the master's rights
Read the first part of the passage and list the three actions the master may perform over the mudabbar: istikhdam (employment), mu'ajarah (hiring out), and wat' (intercourse) for a female mudabbara.
Note that tadbir does not make the slave free immediately.
2
Understand the dalil for these rights
The passage cites a hadith from Jabir that permits selling the services of a mudabbar, and the practice of Ibn Umar who had intercourse with his mudabbara slaves. These prove the permissibility.
The hadith and companion saying are zanni in transmission but sufficient for mubah rulings.
3
Apply the rule for the child
The passage states that the child of a mudabbara is mudabbar. This follows the principle that the child's status follows the mother's, as confirmed by Ibn Umar's statement.
This is analogous to the rule for umm walad.
4
Distinguish tadbir from other manumission forms
Compare tadbir with immediate freeing (itaq) and kitabah. Tadbir does not sever the master's usufruct rights, whereas full freedom does.
The master cannot sell or gift the mudabbar, but he can use and hire.

Commentary
This passage highlights the master's ongoing rights over the mudabbar. Although the master cannot sell the mudabbar, he can still use his services (istikhdam), hire him out (mu'ajarah), and in the case of a female mudabbara, he may have intercourse with her (wat') and marry her off (tazwij). These rights are affirmed by a hadith from Jabir and the practice of Ibn Umar. The passage also states that the child of a mudabbara follows the mother's status: the child is mudabbar like the mother. This is based on the general principle that the child of a female slave follows her in slavery or freedom, and since the mother has a contingent right to freedom, so does the child. The companion's statement from Ibn Umar explicitly confirms this.

Key Terms
استخدام (istikhdam): The master's right to employ the mudabbar for his own service.
مؤاجرة (mu'ajarah): The master's right to hire out the mudabbar to others and collect the wages.
ولد المدبرة (walad al-mudabbara): The child born to a female mudabbara during the period of tadbir. The child follows the mother's status as mudabbar.
