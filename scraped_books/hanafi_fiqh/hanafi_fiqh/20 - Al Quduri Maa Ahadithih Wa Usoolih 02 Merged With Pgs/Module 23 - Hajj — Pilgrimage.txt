Executing a Bequest for Hajjat al-Islam
Ruling on performing Hajj on behalf of deceased from bequest

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the Hanafi ruling on performing Hajj on behalf of a deceased person from a bequest, including the procedure when funds are insufficient.

Core Idea
A bequest for obligatory Hajj is executed as far as the funds allow.

In this lesson, we examine a concise ruling from the Hanafi tradition on how to fulfill a bequest for the missed obligatory Hajj (Hajjat al-Islam). The text provides clear guidance: the executor must hire a man from the deceased's own town to travel mounted, and if the bequest does not cover the full journey, the Hajj is performed from the farthest point the money reaches. This ruling demonstrates the school's practical approach to honoring the deceased's obligation while respecting the limits of the bequest.

Guided Path
1
Identify the obligation
The bequest is for Hajjat al-Islam, the obligatory Hajj that was due on the deceased during his lifetime. This is a right of Allah that becomes binding on the executors.
Hajjat al-Islam is also called Hajjat al-Fard.
2
Determine the default execution
The executor must hire a man from the deceased's own town to perform Hajj, and that man should travel riding (i.e., mounted on an animal). This ensures the journey is made on behalf of the deceased.
The hired man should be a resident of the deceased's town to maintain connection to the deceased.
3
Address insufficient funds
If the bequest amount is not enough to cover the full journey from the deceased's town to Makkah, the Hajj is performed from the farthest point that the money can reach. This may mean starting from a city en route rather than from the deceased's town.
The ruling avoids voiding the bequest; instead, it is fulfilled to the maximum extent possible.

وَمَنْ أَوْصَى بِحَجَّةِ الْإِسْلَامِ أَحَجُوا عَنْهُ رَجُلًا مِنْ بَلَدِهِ يَحُجُّ رَاكِبًا فَإِنْ لَمْ تَبْلُغَ الْوَصِيَّةُ النَّفَقَةَ أَحَجُوا عَنْهُ مِنْ حَيْثُ تَبْلُغُ
And whoever bequeaths the Hajjat al-Islam (obligatory Hajj), they should perform Hajj on his behalf with a man from his town who performs Hajj riding. If the bequest does not cover the expenses, they perform Hajj on his behalf from where it reaches.

Commentary
If a person bequeaths for his missed obligatory Hajj (Hajjat al-Islam), the executors should hire someone from the deceased's own town who will travel mounted (riding a mount) to perform Hajj. The bequest covers travel costs. If the amount is insufficient to reach Makkah, the person should start from the farthest point the money can take him, and complete the Hajj from there. This ruling shows consideration for making the bequest feasible.

Key Terms
حَجَّة الْإِسْلَام (ḥajjat al-islām): The obligatory pilgrimage that was due on the deceased during his lifetime.
