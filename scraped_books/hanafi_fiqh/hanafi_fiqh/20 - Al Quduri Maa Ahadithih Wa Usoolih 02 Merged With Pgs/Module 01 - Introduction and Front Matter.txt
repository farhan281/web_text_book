Introduction describing the book's features

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the key features of this edition of Al-Quduri and explain how each fiqh ruling is connected to its primary evidence.

Core Idea
Every ruling in this book has a Quranic verse, hadith, or Companion's saying to show its strength.

This lesson introduces the unique methodology of Al-Quduri Ma'a Ahadithihi, a book that bridges the gap between matn and dalil. You will learn how the author has arranged the text to make the study of Hanafi fiqh both evidence-based and accessible.

Guided Path
1
Identify the primary text (matn) and its vowel marking
Recognize that the base text is Mukhtasar al-Quduri, now presented with full i'rab (vowel marks) to aid correct recitation and understanding.
The i'rab is a pedagogical aid for students new to Arabic.
2
Locate the supporting evidence for each ruling
For every legal ruling, the author provides a Quranic verse, a hadith, or a saying of a Companion or Successor. Your task is to identify which type of evidence accompanies the ruling.
The source is explicitly named to help evaluate the ruling's strength.
3
Evaluate the source strength through identification
The author clarifies exactly which verse, which hadith collection, and which narrator chain is used. This allows you to assess whether the evidence is strong (sahih) or weak (da'if).
This is a core skill in Hanafi fiqh: knowing the weight of the dalil.
4
Note the restricted hadith sources
All hadiths are taken from only 13 early primary hadith works. This ensures authenticity and avoids later compilations.
Memorize the number 13 as a key feature of this edition.
5
Understand the additional learning aids
The book includes usul principles to explain the reasoning behind rulings, and a glossary for difficult words. These help you grasp the fiqh beyond rote memorization.
These aids make the book suitable for classroom teaching.

Front Matter and Endorsements: The Gateway to Al-Quduri
Front matter overview of a Hanafi introductory text.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the components of a classical Hanafi fiqh book's front matter and explain the purpose of taqriz.

Core Idea
The taqriz establishes the authority of a text before you read a single ruling.

When you open a classical fiqh work, you are not thrown directly into the rulings. The book begins with endorsements (taqriz) and an introduction (muqaddimah). This front matter serves as a scholarly passport: it tells you who vetted the work and what the author intends to cover. In this lesson, we examine the opening pages of Al-Quduri Maa Ahadithih Wa Usoolih, a key Hanafi text, to understand how the tradition packages its knowledge.

Guided Path
1
Identify the title and its significance
The title 'Al-Quduri with its Hadiths' signals that the text includes hadith references alongside the legal matn. This is a pedagogical feature that strengthens the proof structure.
The title indicates a dual focus: the core matn of al-Quduri and the supporting hadith evidence.
2
Recognize the first taqriz
The first endorsement (taqriz) is written by a respected scholar who vouches for the book's reliability. This establishes the chain of scholarly approval.
The taqriz serves as a 'scholarly seal of approval' and is common in classical works.
3
Note the muqaddimah
The introduction (muqaddimah) typically outlines the author's methodology, scope, and the importance of the work. It sets expectations for the reader.
In Hanafi works, the muqaddimah may also clarify the school's position on certain usul.
4
Recognize the second taqriz
A second endorsement reinforces the authority of the text. Multiple endorsements from different scholars strengthen confidence in the work's authenticity.
The repetition of endorsements indicates thorough vetting by the scholarly community.

Navigating Volume 2 of Al-Quduri
Table of contents charting the second volume's chapter sequence.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the sequence of major chapters (kutub) in Volume 2 of Al-Quduri and estimate their relative positions using the page numbers.

Core Idea
Al-Quduri’s Volume 2 starts with Marriage and ends with Testimonies — a systematic journey through Hanafi family law and penal code.

Before diving into the detailed rulings of Volume 2, it is essential to understand the overall structure of the text. A table of contents is a roadmap: it shows which chapters (kutub) are covered and in what order, allowing you to locate topics quickly and appreciate the logical progression of Hanafi fiqh from family matters to penal law and evidence.

Guided Path
1
Identify the very first book
Look at the top of the list after the Introduction. The first book is Kitab al-Nikah (Book of Marriage) on page 10. This is the starting point of the volume.
2
List the family law sequence
Follow the list from Marriage through Maintenance. Note the order: Nikah → Rada‘ → Talaq → Raj‘ah → Ila’ → Khul‘ → Zihar → Li‘an → ‘Iddah → Nafaqat.
Some sections (fusul) are interspersed within these books.
3
Identify the transition to other topics
After Nafaqat, the list moves to emancipation (al-‘Itq), then to injuries and blood money (al-Jinayat, al-Diyat), then to fixed punishments (al-Hudud), and finally to procedure (Da‘wa, Shahadat). Observe how the focus shifts from family to penal and judicial matters.
This reflects a typical Hanafi fiqh arrangement: ibadat first, then mu‘amalat, then jinayat, then judicial matters.
4
Note the page ranges
Observe the approximate page numbers: Marriage starts low (10), while later books like Testimonies are near the end (288). This gives a sense of the volume's allocation.

The Scholarly Methodology Behind Al-Quduri ma'a Ahadithihi wa Usoolihi
Colophon detailing compilation methodology.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the key features of the methodology used in this book: citing three hadiths per issue, distinguishing levels of narrations, and providing usul.

Core Idea
A rigorous Hanafi work cites multiple proofs per ruling and shows the chain of evidence.

This colophon is not a fiqh ruling but a window into the scholarly process behind one of the most important Hanafi texts. Understanding how this book was compiled will deepen your appreciation for the evidentiary structure of Hanafi fiqh and the meticulous work required to ground rulings in primary sources.

Guided Path
1
Identify the book's method
The compiler states that for every single mas'ala (legal issue) in Al-Quduri, three hadiths were brought from twelve specific books. What does this tell you about the evidentiary standard in Hanafi fiqh?
This is a systematic approach to takhrij al-hadith.
2
Distinguish the levels of evidence
The work clarifies whether each citation is a hadith, a qawl sahabi (saying of a Companion), or a qawl tabi'i (saying of a Successor). Why is this distinction important in fiqh?
Each level has different weight in istidlal.
3
Recognize the addition of usul
Beyond hadith citations, the compiler also wrote usul (principles) for each mas'ala. How does this help the reader understand the derivation of the ruling?
Usul al-fiqh principles are made explicit.
4
Reflect on the collaborative effort
Mufti Muhammad Tabarak extracted the hadiths, set them, and wrote the usul in just four months. What does this say about the teamwork required in scholarly production?
Even great works rely on collective scholarship.

In this regard, I am also grateful to Hadhrat Maulana Mufti Muhammad Tabarak Sahib (may Allah preserve him) that he extracted the hadiths, did the work of setting them, then also wrote the principles for them, and did this work very quickly in only a period of four months and handed it to me. I thank him from the bottom of my heart; may the Generous Lord grant him the best reward, Ameen. And our final call is that all praise is due to Allah, Lord of all worlds, and blessings and peace be upon His noble Messenger, and upon his family and all his companions. Key Terms
استخراج (istikhraj): The process of extracting hadiths from primary sources.
قول صحابی (qawl sahabi): A saying or legal opinion of a Companion of the Prophet ﷺ.
قول تابعی (qawl tabi'i): A saying of a Successor (Tab'i) who met the Companions.
اصول (usul): Principles of jurisprudence; here, the usul al-fiqh principles that justify the ruling.
