Mahr al-mithl is based on paternal blood relatives and personal similarity, not on maternal side unless same tribe.

When a marriage contract lacks a specified mahr or the specified mahr is invalid, the wife is entitled to mahr al-mithl — the equivalent dower. But how do we determine what is 'equivalent'? Tribal custom varies, so same-tribe ensures comparability.

Equal in: age, beauty, chastity, wealth, intelligence, religion, country (region), and time (era). These affect customary mahr.
All eight must be considered, not just one or two.
5
Understand the reasoning
The Hanafi method ensures fairness by matching the woman with peers who share her social and personal circumstances.
This prevents arbitrary or excessive mahr.
