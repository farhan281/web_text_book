Saying 'This is my son' and confirming it, or saying 'This is my master' or 'O my master' effects freedom without requiring separate intention. These are treated as effective by custom.

Calling a slave 'O my son' or 'O my brother' does not free him, unlike 'O my master' (addressed to slave) which does. This reflects customary understanding.

When one partner frees his share, the other partner's options depend on the manumitter's solvency and on which Hanafi imam's view is followed.

This passage from al-Lubab presents a classical intra-Hanafi dispute regarding the rights of a co-owner when his partner manumits his share of a jointly owned slave. State Abu Hanifa's position

The Five Categories of Homicide in Hanafi Jurisprudence

Not all killing is equal — the weapon and intent determine whether retaliation, blood-money, or expiation applies.

This passage from al-Lubab defines the foundational taxonomy of homicide. Understanding these categories is essential because each carries distinct legal consequences: qisas, diyah, kaffarah, and sin.
