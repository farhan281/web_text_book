Core Idea
A full set of nails (both hands or feet) requires a dam; less than five requires sadaqah; five scattered nails is disputed.

In this short but precise passage from Al-Quduri's Matn, we learn the graduated penalties for cutting nails while in ihram. The key is to understand the thresholds: the number of nails cut and whether they form a complete set of a limb determine whether the expiation is a major penalty (dam) or a minor one (sadaqah). There is also a notable intra-Hanafi difference on the case of five scattered nails.

Identify the four scenarios
The passage lists four distinct cases: (1) cutting all nails of both hands and feet, (2) cutting all nails of one hand or one foot, (3) cutting fewer than five nails, and (4) cutting five nails scattered across hands and feet.
Each scenario has a specific penalty.
2

Scenarios (1) and (2) both require a dam (sacrifice of a sheep). Scenario (3) requires sadaqah (charity). Scenario (4) is disputed: according to Abu Hanifa and Abu Yusuf it is sadaqah, but according to Imam Muhammad it is a dam.
All penalties are wajib (obligatory) as expiation.

The difference arises because the two imams consider five nails from different limbs as not reaching the threshold of a full limb (which would be a dam), while Imam Muhammad considers that five nails, regardless of distribution, are equivalent to the nails of one hand.
The mufta bihi (relied-upon position) is the view of the majority (Abu Hanifa and Abu Yusuf) – sadaqah.
