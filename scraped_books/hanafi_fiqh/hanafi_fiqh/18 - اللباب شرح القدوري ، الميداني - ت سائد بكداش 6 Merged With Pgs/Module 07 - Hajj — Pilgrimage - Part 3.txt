Person Who Died While Traveling for Hajj: Bequest from Original Town
Ruling on bequest for Hajj when death occurs en route

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the Hanafi ruling on the bequest of Hajj for a traveler who dies en route and identify the starting point for the proxy Hajj.

Core Idea
A Hajj bequest from a traveler who dies en route must be fulfilled from his original town, not from where he died.

This lesson examines a specific scenario in Hajj bequests: a person sets out for Hajj but dies before reaching the destination, having willed that Hajj be performed on his behalf. According to Imam Abu Hanifa, the proxy Hajj must commence from the deceased's town of origin, emphasizing continuity of intention. Understanding this ruling clarifies the importance of the starting point in fulfilling Hajj obligations.

Guided Path
1
Identify the core ruling
The text states that according to Abu Hanifa, if a person leaves his town for Hajj, dies en route, and bequeaths that Hajj be performed on his behalf, the proxy Hajj must be performed from his original town (min baladihi).
This is a specific condition attached to the bequest.
2
Understand the condition of the bequest
The bequest explicitly requires the proxy Hajj to start from the deceased's town of origin. The ruling applies only when the bequest specifies this condition.
If the bequest does not mention the town, the ruling may differ.
3
Analyze the legal significance
Abu Hanifa insists on the original town to ensure that the bequest is fulfilled exactly as intended, preserving the continuity of the deceased's intention from the start of the journey.
This reflects the principle that bequests must be executed as specified.
4
Distinguish from alternative views
Other Hanafi authorities (Abu Yusuf, Muhammad) may hold differing opinions, but this passage only presents Abu Hanifa's view. The student should be aware of potential intra-madhhab ikhtilaf.
This is an expansion probe: further research may reveal alternative rulings.

ومن خرج من بلده حاجاً، فمات في الطريق، وأوصى أن يُحَجَّ عنه من بلده عند أبي حنيفة.
Whoever left his town as a pilgrim (for Hajj) and died on the way, and bequeathed that Hajj be performed on his behalf from his town: according to Abu Hanifa.

Commentary
This ruling, attributed to Abu Hanifa, deals with a specific scenario: a person sets out for Hajj but dies en route, and in his will, he instructs that Hajj be performed for him from his original town. According to Abu Hanifa, the bequest is valid and must be executed from his town, not from the place of death. This ensures continuity of intention. The views of Abu Yusuf and Muhammad may differ, but they are not mentioned here.

Key Terms
من بلده (min baladihi): From his own town (place of residence), not from where he died.
