Key Hanafi Fiqh Texts: A Bibliographic Overview
Bibliography of eight Hanafi fiqh texts with publication details.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to list the eight Hanafi fiqh texts from al-Maydani's bibliography and state the scope and significance of each.

Core Idea
Mastering the Hanafi school requires knowing its foundational texts — from al-Quduri to Ibn al-Humam.

This lesson introduces a list of eight key Hanafi fiqh works that have been critically edited and published by the researcher al-Maydani. These range from early matns (concise compendia) to later commentaries and specialized treatises. Understanding these texts is essential for navigating the Hanafi literary tradition and for effective study of fiqh.

Guided Path
1
Identify the Text Type
Determine whether the work is a matn (core text), sharh (commentary), or a specialized treatise (e.g., on worship only).
For example, Mukhtasar al-Quduri is a matn, while al-Lubab is its sharh.
2
Note the Author and Era
Record the author's name and death date to place the text in the historical development of the Hanafi school.
Earlier authors like al-Quduri (d. 428) represent the formative period; later ones like al-Shurunbulali (d. 1069) represent the later period.
3
Assess the Scope and Content
Check what the text covers: all of fiqh, only worship, or specific issues. Also note the number of issues mentioned.
Kanz al-Daqa'iq claims 40,000 issues; Nur al-Idah covers only acts of worship.
4
Understand the Authority Level
Recognize whether the text is a relied-upon source for fatwa (mufta bihi) and how it relates to the school's preferred views.
Al-Mukhtar lil Fatwa and Kanz al-Daqa'iq are known as mufta bihi texts.

The Significance of Zad al-Faqir
Description of a rare Hanafi text on prayer rulings by Ibn al-Humam

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the significance of Ibn al-Humam's 'Zad al-Faqir' and explain why it is a unique resource for Hanafi salah rulings.

Core Idea
Ibn al-Humam's 'Zad al-Faqir' is a rare, specialized Hanafi text on salah that includes his independent ijtihad and departures from mainstream Hanafi positions.

This lesson introduces a unique Hanafi fiqh text: 'Zad al-Faqir' (Provisions of the Poor) by the renowned scholar Kamal al-Din Ibn al-Humam. Unlike general fiqh works, this concise book focuses exclusively on the rulings of salah (prayer) and contains many important issues that are hard to find elsewhere. Moreover, the author's attainment of ijtihad and his occasional disagreements with established Hanafi opinions make this text a valuable source for understanding the depth and flexibility within the Hanafi school.

Guided Path
1
Recognize the author and his stature
Identify Ibn al-Humam as a major Hanafi jurist (790-861 AH), known for his commentary 'Fath al-Qadir' on al-Hidayah. Note his rank of ijtihad mentioned in the text.
His ijtihad status means he could derive rulings independently, which explains his occasional departures from the standard Hanafi view.
2
Understand the scope of the book
Realize that 'Zad al-Faqir' is not a general fiqh manual but is restricted to the rulings of salah only. This specialization makes it rare and concise.
It is a mukhtasar (concise text) filling a niche for detailed prayer rules.
3
Appreciate the rarity and importance
The text contains many important issues that are 'yazz al-wuqoof 'alayha fi ghayrihi' (hard to find elsewhere). This indicates unique content not present in standard works.
The edition was verified against ten manuscripts and includes an extensive biography of the author.
4
Analyze the author's analytical independence
Ibn al-Humam not only reached ijtihad but also 'khalafa fiha ra'y al-Hanafiyya' (contradicted the Hanafi opinion) in certain fiqh and usul issues. This shows scholarly autonomy within the madhhab.
His disagreements are recorded in the biography, highlighting the dynamic nature of Hanafi jurisprudence.

26. Zad al-Faqir, a rare, fine concise text specific only to rulings of prayer according to the Hanafi madhhab. It contains many important issues that are hard to find elsewhere. By al-Imam al-Kamal Ibn al-Humam Muhammad bin Abd al-Wahid al-Siyasi (790-861 AH), author of 'Fath al-Qadir' commentary on al-Hidayah. It was verified with ten manuscript copies, along with an extensive biography of Ibn al-Humam, showing his attainment of the rank of ijtihad, mentioning his preferred fiqh rulings and issues where he contradicted the Hanafi opinion, and also mentioning his choices in usul al-fiqh and principles of deduction where he opposed Hanafi principles. It was published in one volume, comprising 208 pages, 1st edition 1434/1 AH.

Commentary
This entry describes 'Zad al-Faqir' (Provisions of the Poor), a specialized Hanafi text on the rulings of salah (prayer) by the renowned scholar Kamal al-Din Ibn al-Humam (d. 861 AH), known for his commentary 'Fath al-Qadir' on al-Hidayah. The book is rare and contains unique issues. The edition includes an extensive biography highlighting Ibn al-Humam's ijtihad status and his occasional departures from the established Hanafi positions in both fiqh and usul.

Key Terms
زاد الفقير (zād al-faqīr): Title meaning 'Provisions of the Poor', a Hanafi fiqh text on salah.
ابن الهمام (ibn al-humām): Title of the author, Kamal al-Din Muhammad al-Siyasi, a leading Hanafi jurist.
اجتهاد (ijtihād): Independent legal reasoning to derive rulings; the text notes Ibn al-Humam attained this rank.
