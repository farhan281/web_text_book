Procedure for Resolving Difficult Cases via Maliki Fiqh
Procedural rule for inter-madhhab fatwa consultation

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to apply the Hanafi mufti's procedure for resolving cases not found in Hanafi sources by identifying the Maliki texts to consult and determining when to seek direct scholar guidance.

Core Idea
When Hanafi sources fail, consult Maliki fiqh; if still unsure, ask Maliki scholars in Makkah or Madinah.

As a Hanafi mufti, you may encounter cases where the Zahir al-Riwayah and later Hanafi texts do not provide a clear ruling. This lesson presents a practical procedure from Ashraf Ali Thanwi's work: first consult key Maliki fiqh books, and if uncertainty remains, correspond directly with Maliki scholars in the holy cities. This rule demonstrates the practical unity of the madhahib and the mufti's duty to seek certainty before issuing a fatwa.

Guided Path
1
Identify the gap in Hanafi sources
Recognize when a case cannot be resolved from standard Hanafi references (Zahir al-Riwayah, Nawadir, or Fatawa Mutakhkhirin). This triggers the need for external consultation.
The mufti must first exhaust all Hanafi resources before moving to another madhhab.
2
Consult the listed Maliki books
Refer to the authoritative Maliki texts mentioned: Mukhtasar al-Khalil with Dardir's commentary, Muntaqa, Sharh Muwatta, Mudawwana, Hawi, Hathi, or Aqrab al-Masalik. These are primary sources for Maliki rulings.
The order suggests starting with the most concise and then expanding.
3
Seek certainty from the texts
The goal is to resolve the issue with certainty (al-yakin). If the answer is found clearly, issue the fatwa. If doubt remains, do not proceed with a tentative ruling.
Certainty is a key principle in fatwa; a ruling based on doubt is not valid.
4
Postpone the case if unresolved
If the books do not provide a certain answer, delay the fatwa. Do not issue based on personal reasoning or incomplete knowledge.
Postponement is better than risking an incorrect fatwa.
5
Contact Maliki scholars directly
Correspond with the supervisors of Madrasah Sawlatiyyah in Makkah or Madrasah al-Uloom al-Shariyyah in Madinah. These are recognized authorities for Maliki fiqh.
The method of correspondence is specified to ensure reliable and authoritative answers.

If there is doubt, first resolve the issue from the following Maliki fiqh books: Mukhtasar al-Khalil with commentary by Allama Dardir, Muntaqa, Sharh Muwatta, Mudawwana, and other Maliki fiqh books like Hawi, Hathi or Aqrab al-Masalik etc. If the issue cannot be resolved with certainty from the above books, then postpone the case and correspond with the honored Makkah or Madinah Munawwarah, and benefit from the Maliki scholars themselves. Commentary
This passage provides a procedural rule for a mufti when faced with a problem that cannot be resolved from Hanafi sources. The author recommends first consulting major Maliki fiqh texts. If still unresolved, the mufti should postpone the case and seek direct guidance from Maliki scholars in Makkah or Madinah via correspondence. This reflects the Hanafi recognition of other madhhabs as valid sources for fatwa in times of need, while maintaining a methodical approach.

Key Terms
فقہ مالکی (Fiqh Maliki): The Maliki school of Islamic jurisprudence, used as a secondary source when Hanafi sources do not provide a clear ruling.
مختصر الخليل (Mukhtasar al-Khalil): A concise Maliki fiqh text by Khalil ibn Ishaq al-Jundi (d. 1365), widely studied and commented upon.
مدونہ (Mudawwana): A foundational Maliki fiqh compilation by Imam Malik's student Sahnun, containing his responses to Malik's teachings.
اقرب المسالک (Aqrab al-Masalik): A Maliki fiqh manual by Ahmad al-Dardir (d. 1786), known for its clarity and practical rulings.
