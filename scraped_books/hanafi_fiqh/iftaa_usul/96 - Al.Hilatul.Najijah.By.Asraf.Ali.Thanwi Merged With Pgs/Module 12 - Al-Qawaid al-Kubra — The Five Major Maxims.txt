Exception as Evidence: Permissibility of Hiring for Quran Recitation
Qawida with furuat: exception as proof

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to apply the usul principle 'al-istithna' min adawat al-umum' to derive permissibility of hiring for acts of worship that have been explicitly excepted by Hanafi scholars.

Core Idea
Exception to a general prohibition is itself evidence of permissibility in cases of necessity.

The general Hanafi rule prohibits hiring for purely devotional acts (ta'at) because they require sincere intention. However, the fuqaha' made clear exceptions for Hajj, teaching Quran, and other acts where need has removed the prohibition. This lesson examines how the usul principle that 'exception is a tool of generality' serves as a strong proof for extending permissibility to similar cases, such as hiring for Quran recitation.

Guided Path
1
State the general rule
Identify the general prohibition: hiring for acts of worship is impermissible because worship requires sincerity (ikhlas) and cannot be done for wages.
The general rule is found in Hanafi fiqh under the chapter of ijarah.
2
Identify the exceptions (mustathnayat)
List the acts that Hanafi scholars explicitly excepted from the prohibition: Hajj, teaching Quran, teaching fiqh, leading prayer, adhan, iqamah.
These are cited from texts like al-Hidayah, al-Kanz, and others.
3
Apply the usul principle
Recognize that the existence of an exception (istithna') to a general rule (umum) proves that the excepted matter is permissible, and the general rule is not absolute.
The principle: 'al-istithna' min adawat al-umum' – the exception is a tool that limits generality.
4
Extend to the case at hand
Use the principle to argue that hiring for Quran recitation (the specific case) is permissible by analogy to the excepted acts, as it shares the same 'illa of necessity (darurah) and need (hajah).
This is Ibn Abidin's reasoning in 'Shifa' al-Alil'.

Commentary
This passage is a qawida (legal maxim) with its furuat (applications). The author quotes Ibn Abidin's treatise 'Shifa' al-Alil' (Cure for the Ailing) on the issue of hiring for Quran recitation (isti'jar 'ala al-tilawah). The general rule is that acts of worship (ta'at) cannot be hired for wages because they are purely devotional. However, Hanafi scholars made an exception for Hajj, permitting hiring for its performance due to necessity. They then extended this exception to teaching the Quran, teaching fiqh, leading prayer (imamah), adhan, and iqamah. The key usul principle here is: 'al-istithna' min adawat al-umum' (exception is from the tools of generality). This means that when scholars make an exception to a general prohibition, that exception is evidence that the prohibited thing is actually permissible in cases of necessity. Thus, hiring for Quran recitation is permissible by analogy to the exceptions made for other acts of worship. The maxim 'الاستثناء من أدوات العموم' is a well-known usul principle meaning that an exception indicates that the general rule is not absolute but has specific permissible cases.

Key Terms
استثناء (istithna'): An exception made by scholars to a general ruling, indicating permissibility in specific cases.
عموم (umum): Generality, a general ruling that applies to all cases unless an exception is made.
استیجار (isti'jar): Hiring for wages, specifically for performing acts of worship.
