Tasdiqat: Scholarly Verification in Hanafi Fatwa Collections
Endorsements and verification letters

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to explain the role of tasdiqat (endorsements) in the Hanafi fatwa tradition and identify their function in establishing the credibility of a fatwa collection.

Core Idea
Endorsements (tasdiqat) are scholarly verifications that confirm the reliability of a fatwa work, essential in the Hanafi ifta hierarchy.

This lesson examines the endorsements (tasdiqat) prefixed to Ashraf Ali Thanwi's treatise Al-Hilatul Najijah. These endorsements are not mere formalities; they represent a careful scholarly review and approval of the fatwa content. Understanding their purpose and weight is crucial for anyone engaged in fatwa work, as they form an integral part of the Hanafi tradition of verifying and authenticating legal responses.

Guided Path
1
Identify what tasdiqat are
Tasdiqat are written certifications by reputable scholars attesting to the correctness, reliability, and compliance of a fatwa collection with the Hanafi school. Locate the specific endorsements in the source: the letter from Maulana 'Ashiq Ilahi and the formal endorsement from Madrasa Imdadiya.
2
Understand their purpose in the Hanafi ifta tradition
In the Hanafi tradition, especially for lesser-known authors or complex topics, tasdiqat serve to establish the credibility of the work. They assure the reader that qualified scholars have reviewed the content and found it sound, thus providing a chain of verification.
The endorsers often mention their careful perusal and correction of errors, as seen in 'Ashiq Ilahi's letter.
3
Recognize how tasdiqat relate to the fatwa hierarchy
In the Hanafi hierarchy of fatwa sources (zahir al-riwayah, nawadir, fatawa mutakhkhirin), tasdiqat elevate a work to a more reliable level. When a later collection has endorsements from respected authorities, it gains additional weight and can be relied upon for fatwa.
This lesson associates tasdiqat with the 'fatawa mutakhkhirin' category, as they are common in later compilations.
4
Apply this understanding to the given text
Read the endorsements in the source and note the language used: 'fawadtu al-ajwibata kullaha sahihatan' (I found all answers correct). Consider why scholars like 'Ashiq Ilahi hesitated to endorse (due to lack of expertise) but eventually did so, and how this reflects the adab of endorsement.

The Role of Tasdiqat in Hanafi Fatwa Practice
Scholars certify the treatise's rulings as correct

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to explain the function of scholarly endorsements (tasdiqat) in Hanafi fatwa collections and distinguish their authority from the legal proofs themselves.

Core Idea
Endorsements certify a work's accuracy, not create new law.

This passage presents endorsements from scholars of Jalandhar for a treatise by Ashraf Ali Thanwi. Such endorsements (tasdiqat or taqrizat) are common in Hanafi fatwa literature, serving as a mark of approval from qualified muftis. However, they are not independent legal proofs; they only confirm that the work has been reviewed and found sound. Understanding their role is crucial for correctly assessing the authority of a fatwa collection.

Guided Path
1
Identify the document and genre
Recognise that this passage is front matter—a collection of endorsements appended to Thanwi's treatise. It is not a legal ruling but a form of scholarly certification.
Look for humility phrases like 'I am only a lowly student' to understand the genre.
2
Analyse the language of the endorsements
Note the wording: 'all the answers are correct and alive' (sahih wa hayy) and the petitioners' deference. This indicates approval and confidence in the work's content.
The phrase 'correct and alive' suggests the rulings are both valid and applicable.
3
Interpret the legal weight
In Hanafi usul, endorsements (tasdiqat) do not create law; they only attest that the work aligns with the school's principles. They function as a form of ijazah (certification) for publication, not as legal proofs.
Contrast with independent proofs like Quran, Sunnah, Ijma, Qiyas.
4
Recognise the limitations
An endorsement does not replace the mufti's duty to verify each ruling. It is a secondary support, strengthening the work's reliability but not overriding original evidence.
The endorsers themselves are not providing new evidence, only confirmation.
5
Apply to contemporary practice
When a modern mufti includes endorsements, they enhance credibility but each fatwa must still be justified by authentic dalil. Tasdiqat are testimonials, not hujjah (proof).
Think of them as peer review, not legislative authority.

Purpose and Legal Method of Al-Hilatul Najijah
Author's preface explaining necessity and contents

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the purpose of Al-Hilatul Najijah and explain how it provides operative fatwa solutions within the Hanafi school for difficult cases like mafqud, majnun, and muta'anni.

Core Idea
Al-Hilatul Najijah solves fiqh bottlenecks by applying existing Hanafi rulings, not creating new law.

This lesson examines the opening of a vital Hanafi fatwa treatise. The author introduces Al-Hilatul Najijah as a timely solution for pressing family law issues—cases where a husband is missing (mafqud), insane (majnun), or recalcitrant (muta'anni). The preface stresses that the treatise does not invent new rulings but unlocks relief from within the established Hanafi tradition, countering accusations of rigidity. Understanding this purpose is essential for any mufti navigating similar complex cases today.

Guided Path
1
Identify the treatise's purpose
Read the preface to determine why the author compiled Al-Hilatul Najijah. Look for phrases like 'ضرورت محسوس ہوتی تھی' (necessity was felt) and 'مظلوم اور بے نہیں طبقہ' (oppressed and helpless class).
The purpose is to provide practical fatwa solutions, not theoretical exposition.
2
List the difficult cases addressed
From the preface, extract the specific categories: mafqud (missing), majnun (insane) with its subtypes, and muta'anni (recalcitrant). Note that the treatise addresses these because they cause prolonged hardship.
These are areas where classical Hanafi rulings are strict, leading to deadlock unless specific legal devices (hiyal) are applied.
3
Understand the legal method
The author says 'تنگی مذہب کا الزام دور کردیا' (removed the allegation of constriction of the school). This shows the treatise works within the Hanafi school, not outside it. The reference to Badai' al-Sana'i' indicates reliance on classical authoritative texts.
The method is to identify existing Hanafi positions that can be combined or applied to unblock difficult cases.
4
Grasp the audience and application
The preface addresses scholars and muftis in India, urging them to act upon these solutions and give fatwa accordingly. It emphasizes that this is the only recourse in the current situation.
The treatise is a practical manual for muftis, not a general public work.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the function of tasdiqat in authenticating a fatwa collection and evaluate their role in the Hanafi mufti's fatwa hierarchy.

Core Idea
Scholarly endorsements verify that a fatwa collection aligns with the Hanafi madhhab, guiding muftis in relying on its rulings.

This lesson examines the endorsements (tasdiqat) from various scholars praising the book 'Al-Hilat al-Najizah' by Ashraf Ali Thanwi. These endorsements are not mere formalities; they serve as a quality assurance mechanism in the Hanafi fatwa tradition. By understanding what these scholars praise—adherence to the madhhab, clarity, and addressing urgent needs—you will learn how muftis use such endorsements to assess the reliability of fatwa collections.

Guided Path
1
Identify the endorsers and their credentials
List the names and positions of the scholars who wrote endorsements. Note their roles (teacher, mufti, imam) and locations to assess their authority.
For example, Muhammad Chiragh and Muhammad Khalil were teachers at Madrasa Anwar al-Ulum; Maulana Abdul Jabbar was a mufti and imam.
2
Extract the criteria they use to praise the book
Identify the specific qualities mentioned: correct answers according to the madhhab of early and later jurists, clarity, filling a need, unique compilation, etc.
Look for phrases like 'made very clear', 'such detailed problems... not seen before'.
3
Connect each praise to a Hanafi iftaa principle
Map each criterion to a principle: 'adherence to madhhab' corresponds to the requirement that fatwas follow the Hanafi school; 'filling a need' relates to the mufti's duty to address contemporary issues; 'unique compilation' speaks to comprehensiveness.
This helps understand why endorsements matter for the mufti's reliance.
4
Assess the authority weight of these endorsements
Determine the authority of the endorsers: Are they from Zahir al-Riwayah scholars? Later authorities? The endorsements here are from contemporary teachers and muftis, which gives practical credibility but not the same as classical Zahir al-Riwayah.
Consider how this fits into the fatwa hierarchy (zahir riwayah > nawadir > fatawa mutakhkhirin).
5
Conclude how these endorsements affect the mufti's reliance on the book
Synthesize: Because respected scholars confirm the book's correctness and madhhab alignment, muftis can use it with confidence. However, they must still check for any errors (as noted by the endorsers about scribal mistakes).
This illustrates that endorsements are part of the tarjih (preferral) process.

The Petition Form (ʿArẓī) in Sharʿī Proceedings
A blank petition form for Shariah court

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the components of a Shariah court petition form and state its procedural purpose.

Core Idea
A petition form documents the claim and initiates Shar'i adjudication.

In Sharʿī courts, written petitions are essential for formalizing disputes. This lesson examines a blank petition form (ʿarẓī dhuʾī) as presented in the text. Understanding its structure helps the mufti and qadi ensure proper documentation of claims.

Guided Path
1
Identify the header
Look at the top of the form: it addresses the Shariah judiciary court or committee and includes fields for case number and nature of claim.
The header sets the procedural context.
2
Locate the plaintiff's details
Find fields for the plaintiff's name, place, parentage, address, and community. These identify the party filing the petition.
Accurate identification is crucial for lineage and jurisdiction.
3
Recognize the claim statement space
The form includes a section titled 'عرض ہے کہ' (It is submitted that) where the full content of the claim will be written.
This is the substantive part of the petition.
4
Note the signature and witness lines
The form ends with a space for the plaintiff's signature and witnesses. This formalizes the submission.
Witnesses are not always required but add authentication.
5
Understand the overall function
The petition form is a procedural tool to initiate a case by documenting the claim and submitting it to the court.
It does not contain legal reasoning; it is a blank template.

The Power of Attorney Form (Mukhtārnāma) and Wakāla in Hanafi Law
A blank power of attorney form for Shariah court

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the components of a Sharʿī power of attorney form and explain the Hanafi concept of wakāla as reflected in the form.

Core Idea
A power of attorney form documents the appointment of a representative, rooted in Hanafi wakāla.

Appointing a representative (wakīl) is common in Sharʿī proceedings. This lesson analyzes a blank mukhtārnāma form. The form records the principal, agent, and the scope of authority. Understanding this form helps muftis verify the validity of representation.

Guided Path
1
Identify the principal (appointing party)
Find the section where the litigant's name, parentage, and address are filled. This person is the principal who appoints the representative.
In Hanafi law, the principal must have legal capacity (ʿāqil bāligh).
2
Identify the agent (mukhtār/wakīl)
Locate the field for the name of the person being appointed as representative. This is the agent.
The agent must also have capacity and accept the appointment.
3
Note the binding statement
The form includes a sentence where the principal binds themselves completely and authorizes the agent. This expresses the offer and acceptance.
In Hanafi wakāla, the contract is formed by offer and acceptance.
4
Recognize the signature and witness requirements
The form requires the principal's signature and witnesses. Witnesses are not always obligatory but strengthen the document.
The presence of witnesses can prevent disputes.
5
Relate to Hanafi wakāla rules
The form embodies key elements of wakāla: principal, agent, scope (implied by the case number), and consent. It is a practical application of the fiqh rules.
Wakāla is permissible in all contracts and litigation according to the Hanafi school.
