The Role of Scholarly Endorsements (Taqriz) in Hanafi Fatwa Practice
List of scholars who endorsed the book, with a note explaining the location of full endorsements.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to explain the purpose of scholarly endorsements (taqriz) in Hanafi fatwa literature and identify the types of scholars who provide them.

Core Idea
Scholarly endorsements anchor a book's authority within the living tradition of 'ulama, ensuring its rulings are grounded in the madhhab.

This list of scholars endorsing Al-Hilat al-Najiza is more than a mere catalog of names. It reflects the rigorous process of verification and communal acceptance that a Hanafi fatwa work undergoes. Through endorsements (taqriz), the scholarly community vets the author's reliability and the book's conformity with the established madhhab. In this lesson, we explore the function of these endorsements in the Hanafi tradition and what this specific list tells us about the network of 'ulama.

Guided Path
1
Identify the purpose of the list
Read the note at the end of the passage: it states that these names are endorsements (tasdiqat) placed at the end of the book. Understand that this summary serves to establish the book's credibility.
The list is a condensed version of the full endorsements.
2
Categorize the endorsers
Notice the geographical spread (Punjab, Gujarat, Kashmir, Bengal) and institutional affiliations (madrasas, Dar al-Ifta). This shows the breadth of acceptance across the subcontinent.
Many endorsers are muftis or heads of madrasas, indicating authority.
3
Connect to fatwa practice
Reflect on how endorsements relate to the concept of mufta bihi and tarjih. Endorsements act as communal verification that the book's rulings are reliable and within the madhhab.
This is similar to ijaza in hadith transmission.
4
Evaluate contemporary relevance
Consider how modern Hanafi fatwa works still include endorsements. What does this signify about the continuity of the tradition?
Online fatwa portals often display institutional endorsements.

Commentary
This passage is a list of scholars who endorsed the book Al-Hilat al-Najiza li al-Hilat al-'Ajiza by Ashraf Ali Thanwi. The list includes names and affiliations of various 'ulama from across the Indian subcontinent, particularly from Punjab, Gujarat, Kashmir, and Bengal. Many are associated with madrasas such as Madrasa Khayr al-Madaris, Madrasa Nu'maniyya, and Madrasa Aliya Dhaka. The note at the end clarifies that the detailed endorsements are placed at the end of the book (under 'Tasdiqat 'Ulama Hind') and that in older editions they appeared at the beginning. This practice of collecting scholarly endorsements (ijazat or taqriz) is common in Islamic scholarly works to establish the book's credibility and authenticity. The list here is likely a summary of those endorsements.

Key Terms
تقر يظ (taqriz): A formal endorsement or commendation of a book by a scholar, verifying its content and authority.
تصديقات (tasdiqat): Certifications or endorsements; in this context, the full list of scholarly approvals.
اجازت (ijazat): Permission or license to transmit a text or qualify to issue fatwa; endorsements resemble ijaza for the book.

The Role of Taqriz in Hanafi Iftaa Tradition
Personal endorsement letter

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the function of a taqriz (endorsement) in Hanafi legal literature and explain how it validates the methodology of a fatwa collection.

Core Idea
A taqriz signals scholarly approval and strengthens the authority of a work's legal method.

This short endorsement from a contemporary scholar of Maulana Ashraf Ali Thanwi is not merely a polite gesture. It explicitly approves the core methodology of Al-Hilat al-Najizah: using the fatwas of early Hanafi authorities (mutaqaddimin) to address practical needs of later Muslims. Understanding such endorsements helps us appreciate how Hanafi scholarship circulates and validates innovative yet tradition-bound solutions.

Guided Path
1
Identify the author and recipient
Note the opening: the writer calls himself 'al-afqar Muhammad al-War…' and addresses 'Mawlana al-muhtaram…'. This is a personal letter from a scholar (possibly Muhammad Fadil) to the author of the risala.
The identity of the writer is less important than the endorsement itself.
2
Extract the purpose
The writer thanks Allah that there are still people who 'keep in mind the needs of Muslims (zaruriyat) and benefit from the fatwas of the predecessors (mutaqaddimin).' This directly affirms the risala’s method of using earlier authoritative verdicts for contemporary problems.
The phrase 'mutaqaddimin' is key – it shows reliance on early Hanafi masters.
3
Analyse the level of agreement
The writer says he agrees with the contents (mazamin) even though he hasn't examined it fully. This indicates a general endorsement based on the reputation of the work and its known approach.
Full examination is not required for a taqriz; good faith and shared scholarly circles suffice.
4
Connect to the Iftaa tradition
Such endorsements are not binding but add 'barakah' and social authority. In Hanafi iftaa, the acceptance of a book by contemporary scholars influences its use in fatwa.
Taqriz is a form of informal ijma' (consensus) among later scholars.

Commentary

The letter is a personal note but adds authority to the treatise.

Key Terms

المتقدمين (al-mutaqaddimin): The early Hanafi authorities (e.g., Abu Hanifa, Abu Yusuf, Muhammad, and their immediate students).
