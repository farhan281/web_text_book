Refining Conditions for Haramat Musaharat
Margin note refining prohibition conditions

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to apply the refined Hanafi conditions for haramat musaharat in cases of touching, kissing, and glancing, and identify when a barrier or ejaculation negates prohibition.

Core Idea
For prohibition of marriage by affinity, one party's lust suffices, but a thick barrier or ejaculation blocks the ruling.

This margin note from a classical Hanafi manuscript clarifies several subtle conditions for haramat musaharat. It addresses three key refinements: the sufficiency of lust in only one party, the effect of a thick cloth barrier, and the disqualifying effect of ejaculation. These nuances are critical for a mufti advising on marriage prohibition cases.

Guided Path
1
Identify the act
Determine whether the act is touching, kissing, or glancing. Each has slightly different conditions for lust.
For glancing, only the looker's lust counts; for touching/kissing, either party's lust suffices.
2
Check lust condition
For touching and kissing, it is sufficient that one of the two parties experiences lust. For glancing, only the person who looks must experience lust.
Lust on the other side alone does not trigger prohibition from glancing.
3
Check for barrier
If there is a thick cloth that prevents feeling body heat, touching or kissing over it does not cause prohibition, regardless of lust.
The barrier must block body heat; thin cloth may not suffice.
4
Check for ejaculation
If any of these acts (touching, kissing, or looking) leads to ejaculation, prohibition is not established.
Ejaculation from a non-penetrative act removes the prohibition; this is a well-known condition.
5
Apply caution
In practice, avoid ambiguous situations. For example, do not touch a sleeping woman among others without certainty she is one's wife, as a mistake could lead to prohibited affinity.
The margin note emphasizes taqwa and caution.

د قدیم نسخہ کے حاشیہ: عورت کو شہوت ہونے سے حرمت مصاہرت کا مضمون است موقعہ پر ایک مسئلہ یہ بھی پیش نظر ہے کہ لمس و تقبیل یعنی چھونے اور بوسہ لینے کے وقت اگر مرد کو شہوت نہ تھی لیکن عورت کو شہوت ہو گئی، جب بھی یہی حکم ہے اسی طریقہ سے اگر عورت نے (کسی مرد کو) ہاتھ لگایا یا بوسہ لیا جب بھی دونوں میں سے ایک کو شہوت ہونا کافی ہے البتہ نگاہ کے حرمت کا سبب ہونے کے لیے یہ شرط ہے کہ جو دیکھے اسی کو شہوت ہو صرف دوسرے کی جانب سے ہونا حرمت کا سبب نہیں، نیز لمس و تقبیل یعنی چھونے اور بوسہ لینے میں ایک مشہور یہ بھی ہے کہ اس قسم کا کپڑا حائل نہ ہو۔ جو کہ بدن کی گرمی محسوس ہونے کو روک دے پس اگر کس شخص نے اس قسم کا کپڑا درمیان میں آنے کے باوجود کپڑے کے اوپر سے چھوا، یا بوسہ لیا تو اس قسم کی مصاہرت حرمت کی وجہ نہیں، یعنی اس سے حرمت نہ ہوگی چاہے عورت کرے یا مرد کرے، اس کے علاوہ ایک شرط یہ بھی ہے کہ ان افعال کی وجہ سے انزال نہ ہوا ہو پس اگر چھونا، یا بوسہ لینا، یا صرف دیکھنے سے ہی انزال ہو جائے تو حرمت ثابت نہ ہوگی، ایسی قسم کی گندی حرکتیں ہرگز نہ کی جائیں، اور نہ ہی اس قسم کا کوئی کام کر دیں میں احتمال ہو مثلا جس بستر پر بیوی لیٹتی ہو اگر وہاں پر دوسری خواتین بھی ہوں تو جب تک اس کو بیدار کر کے اور [گفتگو؟] کر کے عمل یقین نہ ہو جائے کہ یہ سودی ہے تو اس وقت تک ہرگز ہاتھ نہ لگائے صرف چار پائی وغیرہ مقرب ہونے کے کافی نہ سمجھے کیونکہ اس میں بعض مرتبہ غلطی ہو جاتی ہے۔
From the old manuscript's margin: At this juncture regarding the subject of the prohibition of affinity due to the woman experiencing lust, there is also this issue to consider: in the case of touching and kissing, if the man did not have lust but the woman experienced lust, the same ruling applies. Similarly, if the woman touched (a man) or kissed, it is sufficient for one of the two to have lust. However, for a glance to be a cause of prohibition, the condition is that the one who looks must experience lust; lust only on the other side is not a cause. Also, regarding touching and kissing, there is a well-known [condition] that the cloth should not be a barrier that prevents feeling the body's heat. So if a person touched over the cloth despite such a cloth being in between, or kissed, then this kind of affinity is not a cause of prohibition, i.e., prohibition will not occur, whether done by woman or man. Apart from this, there is also the condition that no ejaculation occurred from these acts. So if from touching, kissing, or mere looking, ejaculation occurs, then prohibition is not established. Such dirty acts should never be done, nor should any such act be done where there is a possibility [of mistake]. For example, on the bed where the wife lies, if other women are also there, then until he awakens her and by [talking?] he is certain that it is his wife, he should not touch her at all. The mere nearness of the bed etc. should not be considered sufficient because sometimes a mistake occurs in this.

Commentary
This margin note refines the conditions for haramat musaharat. It clarifies three points: (1) For touching and kissing, it is sufficient that only one party—man or woman—experiences lust; both are not required. For glancing, however, only the looker's lust counts. (2) If there is a thick cloth barrier that prevents feeling body heat, touching over it does not cause prohibition. (3) If ejaculation occurs from such acts (touching, kissing, or even looking), prohibition does not take effect. The note then advises caution: one should avoid any act that might lead to such mistakes, e.g., touching a sleeping woman among others without certainty it is his wife.

Key Terms
شہوت (shahwat): Sexual desire or lust.
حائل (ha'il): A barrier that prevents direct sensation, here specifically a thick cloth that blocks body heat.
انزال (inzal): Ejaculation of semen.
