The Foundation: Wisdom and Understanding for Fatwa
Book title page with Quranic epigraph

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the relationship between hikmah (wisdom) and fahm al-din (understanding of religion) as foundational prerequisites for issuing fatwas according to the Hanafi tradition.

Core Idea
Wisdom and understanding of religion are the keys to sound fatwa.

Every great journey begins with a guiding star. For the student of ifta, that star is the Quranic verse that opens this book: 'Whoever is given wisdom has been given abundant good.' This lesson unpacks how the author, Mufti Saeed Ahmad Palanpuri, uses this verse to set the stage for the serious study of fatwa-giving according to the Hanafi school. Understanding this connection will help you see why knowledge alone is not enough — deep insight (hikmah) and true comprehension of the religion (fahm al-din) are essential for a mufti.

Guided Path
1
Identify the Quranic verse and its translation
Read the Arabic verse and its Urdu translation. The verse is Surah al-Baqarah 2:269. The translation given links 'al-hikmah' to 'understanding of the religion' (fahm al-din).
The verse does not explicitly mention 'fahm al-din'; the author interprets hikmah in this context as deep religious understanding.
2
Understand the author's use of the verse
The author places this verse at the very beginning of a book on fatwa methodology. This signals that the content of the book — the principles of issuing fatwas — is a manifestation of hikmah.
3
Connect the verse to the book's purpose
The book is a commentary on 'Sharh Uqud Rasm al-Mufti', a foundational text on the etiquette and methodology of muftis. The author emphasizes that true fahm al-din is required for a mufti to apply the rulings correctly.
Fahm al-din goes beyond memorization; it involves understanding the spirit of the law.
4
Reflect on the personal takeaway
As a student, internalize that your study of ifta is not merely technical but a pursuit of hikmah. The verse promises abundant good for those who attain it.
This motivational frame is key to the memorization_anchor purpose of this opening.

Introducing the Core Text: Sharh Uqud Rasm al-Mufti
Title page and publication details of the book

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the main subject of the book 'Aap Fatwa Kaisay Den?' and state its importance for learning the methodology of issuing fatwas in the Hanafi school.

Core Idea
The methodology of issuing fatwas is a disciplined science; this text teaches its rules and ethics.

This lesson introduces the book you are about to study: 'Aap Fatwa Kaisay Den?' which is a translation and commentary of the classical Hanafi work 'Sharh Uqud Rasm al-Mufti' by Ibn Abidin. Understanding the bibliographic context helps you appreciate the authority and purpose of this text in training muftis.

Guided Path
1
Identify the main title and its translation
Look at the title of the book in Arabic and Urdu. The English equivalent is 'How Do You Give a Fatwa?' This immediately tells you the subject is the process of issuing fatwas.
2
Recognize the original classical work being commented upon
The subtitle mentions 'Sharh Uqud Rasm al-Mufti' by Ibn Abidin (Allama Shami). This is the classical commentary on 'Rasm al-Mufti', the foundational text on mufti methodology.
Rasm al-Mufti was originally authored by Abu Bakr al-Khasaf.
3
Name the author of the original and the commentator/translator
The original author of 'Sharh Uqud Rasm al-Mufti' is Muhammad Amin Ibn Abidin (Allama Shami). The translator and commentator for this edition is Mufti Saeed Ahmad Palanpuri, a teacher at Darul Uloom Deoband.
This shows continuity of the Hanafi tradition from Damascus to the Indian subcontinent.
4
Understand the subject: the rules and ethics of issuing fatwas
The core subject is usul al-ifta (methodology of fatwa) and adab al-mufti (ethics of the mufti). This includes how to determine the reliable opinion in the Hanafi school, how to handle conflicting transmissions, and how to respond to modern issues.
This is distinct from usul al-fiqh, which deals with derivation of rulings from sources.
5
Note the publication details and their significance
The book is published by Maktaba Rahmania in Lahore, a well-known publisher of Hanafi texts. The printing details indicate it is a standard edition for madrasa students.
The number 11 likely refers to the edition or print run.

طبقات الفقهاء (Tabaqat al-Fuqaha): The hierarchical classification of Hanafi jurists by their level of authority and era.

Introduction to the Book and Key Concepts of Hanafi Fatwa Methodology
Table of contents listing chapters and page numbers

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to outline the main topics covered in 'Aap Fatwa Kaisay Den' and identify the key concepts of Hanafi fatwa methodology.

Core Idea
Aap Fatwa Kaisay Den maps the entire Hanafi fatwa methodology from sources to application.

This table of contents is more than a list—it is a roadmap to mastering Hanafi fatwa methodology. It highlights the essential topics: the definition of Rasm al-Mufti, the hierarchy of narrations (Zahir al-Riwayah, Nawadir, Nawazil), the qualifications of a mufti, the books of the school, and the rules for preferring opinions. Understanding this structure will help you navigate the detailed lessons ahead.

Guided Path
1
Identify the main sections
Scan the table of contents to recognize major divisions: preliminary matters, core methodology (Rasm al-Mufti, Zahir al-Riwayah, etc.), introductions to key books, and rules for preference and conflict resolution.
The TOC is organized from general principles to specific applications.
2
Note key terms and their significance
Look for terms like Rasm al-Mufti, Zahir al-Riwayah, Nawadir, Nawazil, and Jami' al-Saghir. These are foundational to Hanafi fatwa methodology.
Rasm al-Mufti refers to the rules a mufti must follow; Zahir al-Riwayah are the most authoritative narrations.
3
Understand the hierarchy of narrations
The TOC distinguishes between Usul (Zahir al-Riwayah), Nawadir, and Nawazil. This hierarchy determines which opinions are relied upon in fatwa.
Zahir al-Riwayah is the primary source; Nawadir are rare transmissions; Nawazil are later cases.
4
Recognize the role of key books
Notice mentions of Jami' al-Saghir, Jami' al-Kabir, Siyar, al-Mabsut, and al-Kafi. These are canonical texts of the Hanafi school.
Each book serves a specific purpose in transmission and fatwa.
5
Observe discussions on preference and conflict
The TOC includes sections on how to prefer one opinion over another and how to resolve conflicts between evidences or narrations.
This is crucial for a mufti weighing different riwayat.

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to explain the methodological choices made by Maulana Saeed Ahmad Palanpuri in his translation of Sharh Uqud Rasm al-Mufti and identify the significance of this work's origin in the Hanafi iftaa tradition.

Core Idea
The origin of a fiqh work shapes its methodology — knowing the author's context is essential for using the book correctly.

This preface is not a legal ruling but a window into the author's world. Maulana Saeed Ahmad Palanpuri, a senior Hanafi scholar and teacher at Darul Uloom Deoband, wrote 'Aap Fatwa Kaisay Den' as an explanatory translation of Allama Ibn Abidin's Sharh Uqud Rasm al-Mufti — a key text on the methodology of issuing fatwas. The story behind the book — his son's request, the tragic accident, and the author's decision to publish despite the loss — reveals both the human dimension of scholarship and the practical approach to making classical iftaa literature accessible. By understanding this preface, you will learn to appreciate the context in which Hanafi iftaa works are produced and how an author's choices affect the utility of the text.

Guided Path
1
Identify the Author's Motivation
Read the first few lines: why did the author write this book? Notice that it was his son, a student of ifta, who expressed a desire to read Sharh Uqud Rasm al-Mufti. The author responded by providing daily translations and explanations.
This shows that the work was born from a pedagogical need, not a scholarly ambition.
2
Describe the Author's Methodology
Scan the section where the author explains his method: he gave an explanatory translation (توضیحی ترجمہ) for most parts, literal translation where needed, added headings, and provided introductions for scholars mentioned. He did not include the full Arabic text except verses.
This approach balances fidelity to the original with accessibility for students.
3
Recognize the Impact of Personal Tragedy
The author narrates his son's death in a car accident in 1995. The manuscript remained half-finished for years. After recovering, he decided to publish the incomplete work without the intended extensive introduction.
This emotional context explains the work's character: it is a practical tool, not a polished magnum opus.
4
Evaluate the Significance for Iftaa Study
Ask yourself: why is this preface important for a student of Hanafi iftaa? It shows that the work is a bridge between the classical text and the Urdu-speaking student. It also highlights the role of Darul Uloom Deoband in transmitting Hanafi fatwa methodology.
Knowing the author's background and method helps you assess the book's authority and intended readership.

Navigating Hanafi Fiqh References: The Book Symbols
Author's preface explaining textual references

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the four key Hanafi fiqh works (Bidāyah, Hidāyah, Fayd, Inayah) and explain their roles in the Hanafi fatwa tradition.

Core Idea
A mufti must know which book to cite: Bidāyah for matn, Hidāyah for commentary, Fayd for fatawa, Inayah for detailed sharh.

When studying Hanafi fatawa, you will encounter abbreviated references like 'Bidāyah' and 'Fayd'. This introduction from 'Aap Fatwa Kaisay Den' decodes these symbols, orienting the reader to the foundational texts of the school. Understanding these references is essential for any mufti to correctly source rulings and navigate the Hanafi tradition.

Guided Path
1
Identify the literary device
Recognize that the author uses barā'at al-istihlāl to hint at the purpose of the book through the initial hamd and salawat. This indicates that the introduction will point to the key references.
2
List the symbols and their full titles
From the text, extract the four symbols: Bidāyah, Hidāyah, Fayd, and Inayah. Note that each stands for a specific work: Bidāyat al-Mubtadi, al-Hidāyah, Fayd al-Mawla al-Karim, and al-Inayah fi Sharh al-Hidayah.
Memorize these correspondences.
3
Classify each work by genre
Determine the genre: Bidāyah is a matn (primary text), Hidāyah is a sharh (commentary), Fayd is a fatwa collection, and Inayah is another sharh. This helps in using them appropriately.
Matn and sharh are foundational; fatwa collections are for later rulings.
4
Trace the scholarly chain
Note that all four works are authored by Hanafi scholars: al-Marghinani wrote both Bidāyah and Hidāyah; Ibn al-Karak (al-Karaki) wrote Fayd; al-Babarti wrote Inayah. This shows the continuity of the school.

The Six Books of Zahir al-Riwayah: Foundations of the Hanafi Madhhab
Commentary introducing the six books of Zahir al-Riwayah

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the six books of Zahir al-Riwayah, distinguish between the 'saghir' and 'kabir' books, and explain their significance for fatwa.

Core Idea
The six books of Imam Muhammad are the bedrock of the Hanafi madhhab — a mufti must know them inside out.

This lesson orients you to the foundational texts of the Hanafi school. Imam Muhammad al-Shaybani's six books, known as the usul sittah, are the primary sources of Zahir al-Riwayah. Understanding their nature and hierarchy is essential for any aspiring mufti. We'll focus particularly on al-Jami' al-Saghir and al-Jami' al-Kabir, and the key difference between 'small' and 'large' books.

Guided Path
1
Identify the six books
List the four books mentioned in the passage: al-Jami' al-Saghir, al-Jami' al-Kabir, al-Siyar al-Saghir, al-Siyar al-Kabir. Note that the full set of six also includes al-Mabsut and al-Ziyadat, though these are not named here.
The passage explicitly names four; the six are standard in Hanafi parlance.
2
Distinguish between Saghir and Kabir
According to the passage, books with 'Saghir' in the name are easy and simple, while those with 'Kabir' are extremely precise and difficult, even for great imams.
Imam al-Awza'i's comments illustrate this: he dismissed al-Siyar al-Saghir but acknowledged the depth of al-Siyar al-Kabir.
3
Understand the significance for fatwa
These books are called 'usul' (foundations) because they are the source from which fatwas are derived. The mufti must start with the easier texts (Saghir) before tackling the more advanced ones (Kabir).
Many later works like al-Hidayah compile from these sources.

The Rasm al-Mufti and Hanafi Source Hierarchy for Fatwa
List of Hanafi references for fatwa

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to classify Hanafi fiqh references by authority level (Zahir al-Riwayah, Nawadir, later compilations) and apply the hierarchy to determine which text to prioritize when issuing a fatwa.

Core Idea
A mufti must know the hierarchy of Hanafi texts: Zahir al-Riwayah first, then Nawadir, then later fatwa collections.

This lesson introduces the foundational bibliography of the Hanafi school for fatwa practice. The source list from Rasm al-Mufti includes the essential works every mufti must know. By learning to categorize these texts, you will gain the tool to navigate the madhhab's vast literature and issue sound fatwas.

Guided Path
1
Identify the core Zahir al-Riwayah texts
Look for the works of Imam Muhammad al-Shaybani: al-Jami' al-Saghir, al-Jami' al-Kabir, Kitab al-Asl (al-Mabsut), al-Ziyadat, Ziyadat al-Ziyadat, and the Siyar books. These form the six authoritative sources.
Zahir al-Riwayah means 'apparent transmission' and these texts have the highest authority in the school.
2
Locate the early fatwa compilations
Identify collections such as Fatawa Walwaljiyah, Fatawa Bazaziyyah, Fatawa Tarsusiyyah, Fatawa Tatarkhaniyyah, and others. These gather rulings from later imams.
These are relied upon when the primary texts do not explicitly cover a case.
3
Recognize the commentarial and biographical works
Note works like al-Jawahir al-Mudiyyah (biographies) and shuruh (commentaries) like Mafatih al-Asrar. They explain the primary texts and resolve ambiguities.
Biographical works (tabaqat) help verify the reliability of transmitted opinions.
4
Understand the role of Rasm al-Mufti
The list begins with 'Shuru Rasm al-Mufti' indicating that this is a guide to the sources a mufti must use. It is itself a methodological text outlining the mufti's conditions and procedures.
Rasm al-Mufti is a distinct discipline in Hanafi usul al-iftā.
5
Apply the hierarchy to a fatwa scenario
When faced with a novel case, start with the Zahir al-Riwayah texts. If unclear, consult the early fatwa collections, then later compilations. Commentaries and biographies provide context.
The order of priority is crucial: never rely on a later collection if a Zahir al-Riwayah text directly addresses the case.
