Navigating the Sharh Uqood Rasm al-Mufti: A Mufti's Roadmap
Table of contents listing sections of the book.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to navigate the table of contents of Sharh Uqood Rasm al-Mufti to locate specific sections on transmission, preference, and correction.

Core Idea
The table of contents is the mufti's compass — it shows where to find rules on preference, correction, and transmission.

This lesson introduces the table of contents (fihris) of Sharh Uqood Rasm al-Mufti, a comprehensive guide to Hanafi fatwa methodology. By understanding the structure of this index, you will be able to efficiently locate rulings and principles within the text. The TOC is not a ruling itself but a navigational tool that organizes the book's rich content.

Guided Path
1
Identify the matlab titles
Scan the TOC for distinct section headings (مطلب in Arabic). Each heading introduces a specific topic in fatwa methodology.
The TOC lists them in order, often with a brief summary of the section's key point.
2
Group by thematic clusters
Observe that many matalib cluster around themes: transmission differences, preference rules, correction, and concept. This grouping mirrors the book's structure.
For example, multiple matalib near the end deal with tas-hih (correction).
3
Locate the tarjih section
Find the matlab beginning with 'مطلب في قواعد الترجيح التسعة'. This contains the nine rules of preference among different statements.
This is a critical section for a mufti when reconciling conflicting narrations.
4
Understand the hierarchy of sources
Notice the matlab that mentions the order: 'المتون مقدم على الشروح، والشروح على الفتاوى' (matn precedes commentary, commentary precedes fatwa collections). This is a key principle.
This hierarchy guides the mufti in prioritizing evidence.

Navigating the Table of Contents of Sharh Uqood Rasm al-Mufti
Table of contents listing topics and page numbers.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the major sections of Sharh Uqood Rasm al-Mufti and explain how the table of contents reflects the structure of Hanafi usul al-ifta.

Core Idea
This table of contents maps the entire methodology of Hanafi fatwa—from textual hierarchy to custom and tarjih.

A table of contents is more than a list of page numbers; it is a roadmap of the author's methodology. In Sharh Uqood Rasm al-Mufti, the TOC reveals the systematic treatment of fatwa principles: rules of preference (tarjih), the role of implied meanings (mafhum), custom (urf), and the hierarchy of sources (matun, shuruh, fatawa). This lesson will help you navigate the book and understand the priorities of a Hanafi mufti.

Guided Path
1
Scan for Main Themes
Read through the TOC quickly and identify recurring terms such as 'tarjih', 'mafhum', 'urf', and 'istihsan'. Notice the grouping of sections.
These themes correspond to the core topics of usul al-ifta.
2
Identify the Rules of Preference
Locate the sections labeled 'مطلب في قواعد الترجيح' and note their page numbers. Observe that there are multiple sections on tarjih, indicating its importance.
Tarjih is central to resolving conflicts between narrations and opinions.
3
Understand the Source Hierarchy
Find the section that states 'المتون مقدم على الشروح، والشروح على الفتاوى' (matun precede shuruh, and shuruh precede fatawa). This hierarchy governs how a mufti prioritizes texts.
This is a foundational principle in Hanafi fatwa methodology.
4
Note the Role of Custom
Look for sections on 'العرف' (custom). The TOC includes multiple entries on definition, conditions, and the permissibility of following new custom.
Hanafis give custom significant weight, as reflected in the space devoted to it.
5
Recognize the Sections on Weak Opinions
Spot sections on 'الإفتاء على القول الضعيف' and note the conditions under which weak opinions may be used. This shows the practical flexibility in fatwa.
Even weak opinions have a role in case of necessity.

Commentary
This is the table of contents for the book 'Sharh Uqood Rasm al-Mufti'. It lists the various topical sections (maṭālib) and their page numbers. The book covers the methodology of issuing fatwas, including rules for preferring between different narrations, the hierarchy of sources, the role of custom (ʿurf), the concept of mafhum, and conditions for acting on weak opinions. The TOC indicates a structured work divided into many short sections, typical of classical Hanafi usul al-ifta works.

Key Terms
المفهوم (al-mafhūm): The implied meaning; what is understood from the text beyond the explicit wording.
العرف (al-ʿurf): Custom or prevailing practice that is considered a source of law in the absence of explicit texts.
الترجيح (al-tarjīḥ): The process of preferring one evidence or opinion over another based on specific criteria.

Introduction to Adab al-Fatwa by Imam al-Nawawi
Title page introducing a work on mufti etiquette

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the work 'Adab al-Fatwa' by Imam al-Nawawi, state its author, scope, and explain its significance in Hanafi fatwa methodology.

Core Idea
Imam al-Nawawi's Adab al-Fatwa is a classic on fatwa etiquette, accepted across madhhabs including Hanafi.

This lesson introduces a foundational text on the etiquette of issuing fatwas. Although written by the Shafi'i scholar Imam al-Nawawi, his work on fatwa methodology is widely respected and studied in Hanafi circles. Understanding the scope and content of this work is essential for any mufti-in-training, as it sets the standards for proper conduct in fatwa practice.

Guided Path
1
Identify the Title and Author
Look at the Arabic title: آداب الفتوى للإمام النووى. Note the author's full name and death year (676 AH).
Imam al-Nawawi is primarily known as a Shafi'i jurist and hadith scholar, but his works on fatwa are inter-madhhab.
2
Understand the Scope
The work is divided into three parts: etiquette of the fatwa, the mufti, and the mustafti (seeker). Recognize that proper fatwa practice involves all three.
This tripartite division is standard in mufti adab literature.
3
Recognize Inter-Madhhab Acceptance
Although the author is Shafi'i, the work is used in Hanafi training. Reflect on why a Hanafi mufti would study a non-Hanafi work.
The principles of fatwa etiquette are largely independent of madhhab-specific rulings.

Introduction to Adab al-Fatwa by Imam al-Nawawi
Preface explaining the extraction of Adab al-Fatwa from al-Majmu'

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the source of 'Adab al-Fatwa' and explain its significance as a foundational text for fatwa etiquette in the Hanafi tradition.

Core Idea
Adab al-Fatwa is the core of fatwa etiquette, extracted from al-Nawawi's al-Majmu' to guide muftis before they issue rulings.

This lesson introduces the famous work 'Adab al-Fatwa' by Imam al-Nawawi. While al-Nawawi was a Shafi'i scholar, his work on the etiquette of issuing fatwas is widely studied across all madhhabs, including the Hanafi school. Hanafi scholars have their own works on rasm al-mufti (e.g., Ibn Abidin's 'Rasm al-Mufti'), but al-Nawawi's preface is often used to teach the universal principles of adab that every mufti must uphold before delving into the specific rulings of a madhhab.

Guided Path
1
Identify the work and its origin
Read the first line: 'Adab al-Fatwa by Imam al-Nawawi'. Then note the sentence: 'Indeed, the Adab al-Fatwa which we are in the process of annotating is taken from the introduction of al-Majmu''. This tells us the work is an excerpt from al-Nawawi's larger commentary.
Al-Majmu' is a commentary on al-Muhadhdhab, a Shafi'i fiqh text.
2
Understand the purpose of this preface
The author explains that al-Nawawi began his commentary with an introduction on etiquette for the scholar, student, mufti, and seeker of fatwa. This was deemed of such high quality that it was extracted and published separately to serve as a dedicated guide for those involved in fatwa.
The phrase 'core of this subject' emphasizes its importance.
3
Relate to the Hanafi iftaa tradition
Although the text is by a Shafi'i imam, Hanafi scholars also recognize the value of these adab. The Hanafi tradition has its own works, like Ibn Abidin's 'Rasm al-Mufti', which address similar etiquette but with Hanafi-specific rules (e.g., the hierarchy of authorities). This lesson helps situate the text within the broader study of iftaa.
Cross-madhhab study of adab is common and beneficial.

Front matter index of scholars cited in the book.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to locate page references for a specific scholar in the Fihris al-A'lam and state the importance of such indices for fatwa research.

Core Idea
A fihris al-a'lam is an essential map for navigating classical fatwa works and tracing the authorities behind their rulings.

While this page appears to be a simple list of names, in the tradition of fatwa writing the Fihris al-A'lam is a vital tool. It allows a mufti to quickly locate where a particular scholar's opinion is cited, enabling cross-referencing and verification of authority. Understanding how to use such indices is part of the mufti's research methodology.

Guided Path
1
Identify the title and purpose
Recognize that 'Fihris al-A'lam' means an index of notable persons. Its purpose is to direct the reader to pages where each scholar is mentioned.
The title is given in Arabic at the top of the passage.
2
Scan the list for key scholars
Quickly identify prominent jurists such as al-Shafi'i, Ahmad ibn Hanbal, al-Ghazali, and others. Note that the list includes scholars from multiple madhhabs, especially the Shafi'i school.
The index contains a mix of classical authorities; many are used as references in fatwa compilations.
3
Explain the utility for a mufti
Understand that when a later text cites a scholar's opinion, the fihris helps locate the original discussion. This aids in verifying the istidlal (legal reasoning) and ensuring the fatwa is based on sound authority.
In Hanafi usul al-iftaa, tracing the source of an opinion is critical for determining its authority level (zahir al-riwayah, nawadir, etc.).

Core Idea
A mufti is either independent or affiliated; the affiliated mufti has four distinct levels of authority.

This lesson introduces the Hanafi classification of muftis as outlined in the classical work on fatwa etiquette. Understanding these categories is essential for knowing the authority behind a fatwa and the scope of a mufti's ijtihad. Define the independent mufti
Look at entries 17-21. The independent mufti must have full ijtihad ability, but it is not required to memorize all rulings by heart; memorizing most and being able to perceive the rest suffices. Partial ijtihad (تجزئة الاجتهاد) is accepted.
