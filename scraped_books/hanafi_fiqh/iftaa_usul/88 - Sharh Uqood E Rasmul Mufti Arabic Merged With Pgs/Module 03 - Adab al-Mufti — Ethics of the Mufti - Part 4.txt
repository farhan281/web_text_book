Salvation Through the Kalima: Hadith Analysis for the Mufti
Hadith citation with linguistic commentary on the erosion of Islam

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the hadith regarding the survival of the kalima and explain its significance for the mufti's understanding of salvific faith.

Core Idea
Even when all rituals fade, the kalima 'La ilaha illallah' suffices for salvation.

This passage from Adab al-Mufti cites a prophetic hadith that paints a vivid picture of the end times: knowledge of Islamic practices disappears, the Quran is lifted, but the testimony of faith remains. Silah's question challenges the efficacy of mere words without deeds, and Hudhayfah's emphatic response underscores a profound truth for the mufti: the kalima is inherently salvific. The lexical commentary enriches our understanding of the verbs 'yadrusu' (wears out) and 'yusra' (taken away at night), reminding us that faith, even when stripped of formal knowledge, retains its power to save from the Fire.

Guided Path
1
Identify the core narrative
Read the hadith and identify the stages of erosion: Islam's knowledge fading, Quran being lifted, and the remnant clinging to the kalima.
Focus on the sequence: from loss of practices to survival of the kalima.
2
Analyze Silah's objection and Hudhayfah's response
Understand the concern: can a mere phrase save someone ignorant of rituals? Note how Hudhayfah initially turns away but then affirms three times that it saves from the Fire.
The repetition emphasizes certainty.
3
Examine the lexical commentary
Learn the two meanings of 'yadrusu' (effaced trace vs. worn cloth) and the meaning of 'yusra' (taken at night). This enriches the imagery of gradual and sudden loss.
The dual root highlights both gradual decline and sudden removal.
4
Relate to mufti adab
Consider how this hadith informs a mufti's attitude: never despair of Allah's mercy for those who hold tawheed, even if they are ignorant of details.
This hadith is a pillar of hope and a check against excessive judgment.
5
Assess the hadith's authenticity
Note the commentary grading: authentic on Muslim's condition, reported by Ibn Majah and al-Hakim. This establishes its reliability for fatwa use.
Even as a hadith ahad, it is sound enough for practical ruling.

The Prophet (peace be upon him) said: 'Islam will become worn out as the embroidery of a garment becomes worn out, until one does not know what fasting is, nor prayer, nor pilgrimage, nor charity. And the Book of Allah will be taken away at night, and not a single verse will remain on earth. And there will remain groups of people: the old man and the old woman, saying: 'We found our fathers upon this word: 'La ilaha illallah' so we say it.' So Silah bin Dhifr al-Hudhayfi said: 'What will 'La ilaha illallah' avail them when they do not know what prayer is, nor fasting, nor pilgrimage, nor charity?' Hudhayfah turned away from him. He repeated it three times, each time Hudhayfah turned away. Then on the third time he turned to him and said: 'O Silah, it will save them from the Fire, it will save them from the Fire, it will save them from the Fire.' Reported by Abu Abdullah Ibn Majah in his Sunan, and al-Hakim Abu Abdullah al-Hafiz in his Sahih, and he said: This hadith is authentic on the condition of Muslim but they did not record it. And Allah knows best. (Adab al-Mufti wa al-Mustafti, pp. 40-41). His saying: 'Yadrusu al-Islam' is from 'darasa al-rasmu durusan' if it becomes effaced and perishes; and from 'darasa al-thawbu darsan' if it becomes old. And his saying: 'Yusra ala Kitabillah' means it is taken away at night.

Commentary
This passage is a hadith citation followed by lexical commentary. The hadith, narrated by Hudhayfah, describes the end times when knowledge of Islamic practices fades, leaving only the verbal testimony of faith. Silah's question about the efficacy of the kalima without deeds is answered emphatically: it saves from the Fire. This is cited in the context of mufti adab, emphasizing that the core of faith remains salvific. The commentary clarifies two linguistic points: 'yadrusu' can mean becoming effaced (like a trace) or worn out (like old cloth), and 'yusra' means to take away at night. The hadith is graded sahih according to Muslim's criteria. The lesson underscores the importance of the kalima even in times of widespread ignorance.

Key Terms
يدرس (yadrusu): To become effaced or worn out; applied to Islam becoming unrecognized.
يسرى (yusra): To be taken away at night; refers to the Quran being lifted from the earth.
