The Fourth Condition of Intention: No Contradictory Act
Expansion of a rasm rule: condition for valid prior intention

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the Hanafi condition that intention is invalidated by a contradictory act (manāfi) between niyyah and tahrimah, and apply it to resolve issues like apostasy during worship and the status of a Companion's companionship.

Core Idea
Intention must be free of any contradictory act between it and the start of worship; a mere mental cutoff suffices for faith but not for acts.

This passage from the Ashbāh wa al-Naẓā'ir tradition explains the fourth condition of intention (niyyah) in Hanafi fiqh: that no act contradictory to the intended worship occurs between the intention and the takbīr al-taḥrīmah. The condition is then applied to two key issues: apostasy during worship and the status of a Companion's companionship (ṣuḥbah) after apostasy. It further distinguishes between intending to cut faith and intending to cut an act of worship—a crucial nuance in Hanafi legal theory.

Guided Path
1
Identify the Rule
Extract the core rule from the text: 'The intention preceding the tahrimah is permissible on condition that one does not bring after it something contradictory that is not from it.' Understand that this condition ensures the intention remains connected to the act of worship until commencement.
This rule is a rasm (directive) for muftis: the validity of a prior intention depends on the absence of a contradictory act.
2
Define 'Contradictory Act' (Manāfi)
Use the text's examples: walking to the mosque (related to prayer) is not contradictory; eating, drinking, or playing between intention and takbīr is contradictory. The act must be from a different genus (jins) than the intended worship.
The key is whether the act is from the same category (jins) as the worship. If it is, it does not break the connection.
3
Apply the Rule to Apostasy During Worship
The text states that worship is invalidated by apostasy during it. Apostasy is a contradictory act because it severs the connection with all acts of worship. Then consider the debate: some scholars (e.g., Mulla) argued this application is not directly derived from the condition, but Ibn Nujaym defends it by positing that if a person apostatizes and then returns to Islam, the prior intention is insufficient due to the intervening contradiction.
The practical fatwa: if someone apostatizes during prayer and then re-embraces Islam, the prayer is void and must be restarted.
4
Apply the Rule to Companion Status (Ṣuḥbah)
The text asks: does apostasy permanently invalidate the honor of being a Companion? According to the majority of Hanafis, if a Companion apostatizes and dies in that state, companionship is void. If he returns during the Prophet's lifetime, it returns. After the Prophet's death, there is consideration (nazr). Compare with al-Shāfi'ī's view that companionship once attained is never lost.
The depended-upon (muftā bihī) Hanafi position, supported by al-Hamawi and Fath al-Bari, is that such a person is still counted as a Companion, as exemplified by Ash'ath ibn Qays.
5
Analyze 'Intention to Cut' (Niyyat al-Qaṭʿ)
The text distinguishes: intending to cut faith (īmān) immediately causes apostasy because faith is an act of the heart—the intention itself breaks it. Intending to cut prayer does not invalidate it; only an action like takbīr to enter another prayer actually cuts the first. This highlights that for acts of worship, intention alone (without an action) does not nullify.
Exception: if one intends to cut faith, the mere intention suffices for apostasy because faith is entirely based on inner conviction.

الرَّابِعُ: أنْ لا يَأْتِي بِمُنَافِ بَيْنَ النِّيَّةِ وَالْمَنْوِي قَالُوْا: إِنَّ النِّيَّةَ الْمُتَقَدِّمَةَ عَلَى التَّحْرِيْمَةِ جَائِزَةٌ بِشَرْطِ أَنْ لا يَأْتِيَ بَعْدَهَا بِمُنَافٍ لَيْسَ مِنْهَا، وَعَلَى هَذَا تَبْطُلُ الْعِبَادَةُ بِالْاِرْتِدَادِ فِي أَثْنَا ئِهَا، وَتَبْطُلُ صُحْبَةُ النَّبِيِّ لا بِالزِّيَادَةِ إِذَامَاتَ عَلَيْهِ، فَإِنْ أَسْلَمَ بَعْدَهُ، فَإِنْ كَانَ فِي حَيَاتِهِ عَلَيْهِ الصَّلَاةُ وَالسَّلَامُ، فَلَامَانِعُ مِنْ عَوْدِهَا، وَإِلَّا فَفِي عَوْدِهَا نَظْرٌ، كَمَا ذَكَرَهُ الْعِرَاقِيُّ.

ترجمه: چوتھی شرط یہ ہے کہ نیت اور منوی کے درمیان کوئی ایسا عمل نہ کرے جو منافی ہو، فقہاء فرماتے ہیں کہ تحریمہ پر نیت متقدمہ جائز ہے اس شرط کے ساتھ کہ اس کے بعد کوئی ایسا کام نہ کرے جو منوی کے قبیل سے نہ ہو اور اسی بنا پر دوران عبادت ارتداد سے عبادت باطل ہو جاتی ہے اور نبی کریم صلی اللہ علیہ وسلم کی صحبت ارتداد سے باطل ہو جاتی ہے جبکہ اس پر انتقال ہو پھر اگر اس کے بعد ایمان لے آئے تو اگر آپ صلی اللہ علیہ وسلم کی حیات مبارکہ میں ہو تو اس کے لوٹ جانے سے کوئی چیز رکاوٹ نہیں ہے ورنہ اس کے لوٹنے میں نظر ہے جیسا کہ عراقی نے ذکر کیا ہے۔

تشریح: چوتھی شرط یہ ہے کہ نیت اور منوی کے درمیان کوئی ایسا عمل نہ پایا جائے جو نیت کے منافی ہو۔ اگر وہ عمل عبادت سے ہی متعلق ہو تو کوئی رکاوٹ نہیں جیسے گھر سے وضو کر کے نماز کی نیت سے مسجد گیا اور اسی نیت سے جا کر نماز پڑھی تو درست ہے اگر چہ اس کے گھر اور مسجد کے درمیان چلنے کا عمل بھی پایا گیا لیکن وہ منافی صلاۃ نہیں ہے۔ اور اگر منافی صلاۃ ہو جیسے نیت صلاۃ اور تحریمہ کے درمیان کھیل کود یا کھانا پینا تو اس نیت سے نماز درست نہ ہوگی منافی عمل کے پائے جانے کی وجہ سے۔

قالوا ان النية المتقدمة: نیت اور عبادت کے درمیان اگر کوئی منافی صلاۃ عمل نہ پایا جائے تو پیشگی نیت تحریمہ پر معتبر ہے اور منافی صلاۃ وہ عمل ہے کہ جس کا عبادت یا نماز سے کوئی تعلق نہ ہو۔ النية المتقدمة على التكبير كالقائمة عند التكبير اذا لم يوجد ما يقطع وهو عمل لا يليق بالصلاة. (ہندیہ ج ۱ ص: ۶۷)

و على هذا تبطل العبادة بالإرتداد: علامہ ابن نجیم المصری فرماتے ہیں کہ اس اصول کی وجہ سے دوران عبادت ارتداد کی وجہ سے عبادت باطل ہو جاتی ہے لیکن علامہ مولی نے اس ماہ کام کے سلام متفرع کرنے پر اشکال کیا ہے کہ اس مسئلہ کا نیت اور منوی کے درمیان امر منافی کے پائے جانے سے کوئی تعلق بھی نہیں ہے اس لئے یہ تفریع درست نہیں ہے، اس کی صورت یہ ہوسکتی ہے کہ کسی نے عبادت شروع کی اور دوران عبادت وہ مرتد ہو گیا اس کے بعد پھر اسلام قبول کر لیا تو آیا پہلی ہی نیت کافی ہے اس عبادت کے لئے؟ تو جواب یہ ہے کہ پہلی نیت کافی نہیں ہے اس لئے کہ نیت و منوی کے درمیان منافی عمل پایا گیا۔

و تبطل صحبة النبي صلی اللہ علیہ وسلم: اگر کوئی صحابی رسول مرتد ہو گیا اور پھر اسی حالت میں انتقال کر گیا تو شرف صحابیت ارتداد کی وجہ سے باطل ہو جائے گی۔ اگر دوبارہ اسلام قبول کر لیا خواہ آپ کی حیات طیبہ میں یا آپ کے وصال کے بعد، امام شافعی کے نزدیک شرف صحابیت لوٹ آئے گا جبکہ جمہور احناف کے نزدیک دوبارہ اسلام قبول کرنے کے بعد اگر صحبت آپ صلی اللہ علیہ وسلم کی میسر ہو گئی تو شرف صحابیت لوٹ آئے گا ورنہ نہیں۔ اور علامہ حموی نے ذکر کیا ہے کہ جو شخص مرتد ہونے کے بعد اسلام میں لوٹ آیا خواہ حضور کے زمانہ میں یا وصال کے بعد صحابہ میں ان کا شمار ہو گا جیسا کہ محدثین نے اشعث بن قیس کو صحابہ میں شمار کیا ہے جبکہ عراقی فرماتے ہیں کہ اگر آپ کی حیات طیبہ میں ہی اسلام کی طرف لوٹ آیا تو شرف صحابیت لوٹ آئے گا اور آپ کے وصال کے بعد قبول اسلام کی صورت میں شرف صحابیت لوٹے گا یا نہیں اس میں نظر ہے۔

فلو ارتد ثم عاد إلى الإسلام لكن لم يره ثانيا بعد عوده فالصحيح انه معدود في الصحابة لاتفاق المحدثين على عد الاشعث بن قيس و نحوه ممن وقع له ذلک و اخراجهم احادیثهم في المسانيد. (فتح الباری ج : ۳)

وَمِنَ الْمُنَافِى نِيَّةُ الْقَطْعِ، فَإِذَا نَوى قطع الإيْمَانِ صَارَ مُرْتَدًا لِلْحَالِ، وَلَوْ نَوى قطع الصَّلَاةِ لَمْ تَبْطُلْ وَكَذَا سَائِرُ الْعِبَادَاتِ إِلَّا إِذَا كَبَّرَ فِى الصَّلاةِ، وَنَوَى الدُّخُولُ فِي أُخْرَى فَالتَّكْبِيرُ هُوَ الْقَاطِعُ لِلْأَوَّلَى لَا مُجَرَّدُ النِّيَّةِ.

ترجمہ: اور قطع کی نیت بھی منافیات میں سے ہے چنانچہ اگر کسی نے ایمان ختم کرنے کی نیت کر لی تو فورا مرتد ہو جائے گا اور اگر نماز ختم کرنے کی نیت کرلی تو وہ باطل نہیں ہوگی اور جملہ عبادات کا یہی حکم ہے الا یہ کہ وہ نماز کے درمیان تکبیر کہے اور دوسری نماز میں داخل ہونے کی نیت کرے تو وہ تکبیر پہلی نماز ختم کرنے والی ہوگی نہ کہ محض نیت۔

تشریح: اس جگہ یہ مسئلہ ہے کہ کسی شخص نے دل میں قطع ایمان کی نیت کر لی تو وہ اپنی اس نیت سے ہی مرتد ہو جائے گا کیونکہ ایمان قلب کے عمل کا نام ہے نیت قطع ہو جانے کی وجہ سے وہ عمل باقی نہیں رہتا تو اس کی غیر موجودگی میں ایمان بھی باقی نہیں رہتا لیکن علامہ حموی کو اس مسئلہ کو اس مقام پر ذکر کرنے پر نظر ہے۔
The fourth [condition]: that one does not bring something contradictory between the intention and the intended. They said: The intention preceding the takbirat al-ihram is permissible on condition that one does not bring after it something contradictory that is not from it. Based on this, worship is invalidated by apostasy during it, and the companionship of the Prophet is invalidated by apostasy if one dies upon it. Then if one becomes Muslim after it, if it was during his lifetime (peace be upon him), there is no obstacle to its return; otherwise, there is consideration, as al-ʽIraqi mentioned.

Translation: The fourth condition is that one should not perform any act between the intention and the intended that is contradictory. The jurists say: The intention preceding the takbir is permissible with the condition that after it one does not perform an act that is not from the category of the intended. And on this basis, worship is invalidated by apostasy during it, and the companionship of the Prophet (peace be upon him) is invalidated by apostasy if one dies in that state. Then if one brings faith after it, if it is during his blessed life, there is no obstacle to its return; otherwise, there is consideration, as al-ʽIraqi mentioned.

Explanation: The fourth condition is that no act should be found between the intention and the intended that is contradictory to the intention. If the act is related to worship itself, there is no obstacle, like going from home to mosque with the intention of prayer after wudu, and then praying with that intention – it is valid even though walking between home and mosque occurred, but it is not contradictory to prayer. But if it is contradictory to prayer, like playing, eating, or drinking between the intention of prayer and the takbir, then the prayer will not be valid with that intention due to the presence of a contradictory act.

They said: The preceding intention: Between the intention and worship, if no act contradictory to prayer is found, the preceding intention is valid upon the takbir. The contradictory act to prayer is an act that has no connection to worship or prayer. 'The intention preceding the takbir is like its existence at the time of takbir when nothing that cuts it is found, and it is an act not befitting prayer.' (Al-Hindiyya, vol. 1, p. 67)

And on this, worship is invalidated by apostasy: Ibn Nujaym al-Misri says that based on this principle, worship is invalidated by apostasy during it. But Mulla [?] objected to deriving this issue under this principle, because this issue has no connection to the existence of a contradictory act between intention and intended. Therefore, this derivation is not correct. The scenario could be that one started worship and during worship became an apostate, then accepted Islam again: is the first intention sufficient for this worship? The answer is that the first intention is not sufficient because a contradictory act occurred between the intention and the intended.

And the companionship of the Prophet is invalidated: If a companion of the Messenger became an apostate and then died in that state, the honor of companionship is invalidated due to apostasy. If he accepted Islam again, whether during his lifetime or after his passing, according to Imam Shafi'i, the honor of companionship returns, whereas according to the majority of Hanafis, if after re-accepting Islam, companionship with the Prophet (peace be upon him) becomes possible, then the honor returns; otherwise not. And al-Hamawi mentioned that a person who returns to Islam after apostasy, whether during the Prophet's time or after, will be counted among the Companions, as the hadith scholars counted Ash'ath ibn Qays among the Companions. Al-ʽIraqi says that if he returned to Islam during his lifetime, the honor of companionship returns; but if he accepted Islam after his death, there is consideration whether it returns or not.

'If he apostatizes then returns to Islam but does not see him again after his return, the correct view is that he is counted among the Companions, due to the consensus of hadith scholars on counting Ash'ath ibn Qays and others like him who experienced that, and they include their hadiths in the Musnads.' (Fath al-Bari, vol. 3)

And among the contradictory things is the intention to cut. So if one intends to cut faith, he becomes an apostate immediately. And if he intends to cut prayer, it is not invalidated, and similarly for all other acts of worship, unless he says takbir during prayer and intends to enter another prayer. Then the takbir is what cuts the first, not mere intention.

Translation: And the intention to cut is also from the contradictory things. Thus, if someone intends to terminate faith, he becomes an apostate immediately. And if he intends to cut prayer, it does not become invalid, and the same applies to all other acts of worship, except if he says takbir during prayer and intends to enter another; then the takbir is what cuts the first, not mere intention.

Explanation: Here the issue is that if a person intends in his heart to cut faith, he becomes an apostate by that intention alone, because faith is an action of the heart. Due to the intention to cut, that action does not remain, so in its absence, faith does not remain. But al-Hamawi has a consideration regarding mentioning this issue in this context.

Commentary
This passage is a commentary on the fourth condition of intention (niyyah) in Hanafi fiqh: that no act contradictory (manafi) to the intended worship occurs between the intention and the commencement (tahrimah). The author begins by stating the rule: the intention preceding the takbir is valid only if no act not from the kind of worship intervenes. This is a critical principle because it determines whether a prior intention remains effective. The text then applies the rule to two examples: apostasy during worship and the status of a Companion's companionship after apostasy. Regarding apostasy, if one apostatizes during an act of worship, the worship is invalidated because apostasy is a contradictory act. Ibn Nujaym and another scholar debate whether this application is correct, concluding that if the apostate later returns to Islam during the same worship, the prior intention is insufficient due to the intervening contradiction. Regarding companionship, the text discusses whether apostasy permanently nullifies the honor of being a Companion. The Hanafi position, citing al-ʽIraqi and al-Hamawi, distinguishes between returning to Islam during the Prophet's lifetime (companionship returns) and after his death (difference of opinion). The passage also covers the specific category of 'intention to cut' (niyyat al-qat'), where intending to cut faith immediately causes apostasy, but intending to cut prayer does not invalidate it unless accompanied by an action like takbir. This distinction highlights the Hanafi principle that the validity of intention is tied to action in acts of worship, but not in matters of faith. The overall lesson is that intention is not static; it must remain consistent and uninterrupted by any contradictory act or intention.

Key Terms
مناف (manāf): An act that is not from the genus of the intended worship and severs the connection between intention and action; it invalidates the prior intention if it occurs between niyyah and tahrimah.
نية القطع (niyyat al-qatʿ): The intention to terminate an act of worship or faith. For faith, it instantaneously causes apostasy; for prayer, it does not invalidate unless accompanied by an action like takbir.
صحة الصحبة (ṣiḥḥat al-ṣuḥbah): The legal status of being a Companion of the Prophet (peace be upon him), which requires having seen him in a state of faith and dying as a Muslim.
