Front matter: title and author introduction.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the author, his epithets, and the significance of Sharh Uqud Rasm al-Mufti in Hanafi fatwa methodology.

Core Idea
Ibn Abidin's commentary on Rasm al-Mufti is the definitive guide for Hanafi fatwa methodology.

This lesson introduces a foundational text for Hanafi fatwa practice. We examine the title page and its context, and meet the author, the great jurist Ibn Abidin al-Shami, whose work remains the standard for mufti training in the later tradition.

Guided Path
1
Identify the title and author
Look at the Arabic title: 'Sharh Uqud Rasm al-Mufti'. Note that it is a commentary (sharh) on an earlier matn by Ibn Kamal Pasha. The author is identified as Ibn Abidin al-Shami, with his full name and epithets.
The text is a commentary, so the original is 'Rasm al-Mufti' by Ibn Kamal Pasha.
2
Note the author's attributes and dates
The author is described as 'the seal of the investigators, the jurist, the mufti'. His name: Sayyid Muhammad Amin ibn Umar ibn Abd al-Aziz. He is famously known as Ibn Abidin al-Shami. His birth and death years are given as 1198–1252 AH.
His death year (1252 AH / 1836 CE) is also the publication year of this print, indicating a posthumous edition.
3
Understand the text's purpose in Hanafi iftaa
This commentary is a core manual for training muftis in the Hanafi school. It explains the principles and hierarchy for issuing fatwas, relying on the authoritative positions of the school.
It is not a book of fiqh rulings, but a manual on how to derive and issue fatwas correctly.

Editorial preface and methodology for a reprint

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the purpose of the book 'Sharh Uqud Rasm al-Mufti' and explain why a modern edition was necessary for contemporary students of Usul al-Iftaa.

Core Idea
The modern edition revives a classical Hanafi text on fatwa methodology by correcting errors and adapting to today's readers.

This lesson introduces the background of the book 'Sharh Uqud Rasm al-Mufti', a foundational text in Hanafi Usul al-Iftaa. You will learn why even experienced scholars need updated editions to preserve the utility of classical works. The editorial committee's methodology—correcting spelling, adding headings, modernizing punctuation, and vocalizing difficult words—reflects a careful balance between tradition and accessibility.

Guided Path
1
Identify the book and its discipline
The text being introduced is 'Sharh Uqud Rasm al-Mufti', a commentary on the manual for muftis. The discipline is Usul al-Iftaa (principles of issuing fatwas), which is distinct from fiqh itself.
Remember that 'Rasm al-Mufti' refers to the method and protocol a mufti should follow.
2
Recognize the challenges of old editions
The preface notes that changes in printing technology and reading habits have made old editions hard to use. Spelling errors, lack of headings, and difficult typography hinder students.
This is a practical issue: a student may misunderstand a ruling if the text is unclear.
3
List the editorial improvements
The committee corrected spelling, added red section headings, used black for main text explained in footnotes, modernized orthography and punctuation, and vocalized ambiguous words.
These are concrete steps that enhance readability without altering the original content.
4
Understand the target audience
The new edition is primarily for students in religious seminaries in the Indian subcontinent, but it serves anyone studying Hanafi Usul al-Iftaa.
The Indian subcontinent has a strong tradition of Hanafi scholarship; this edition responds to their specific needs.
5
Reflect on the significance of updating classical texts
The preface shows that preserving scholarly heritage requires ongoing effort. A text's relevance depends not only on its content but also on its presentation to each generation.
This lesson is not about fiqh rulings but about the meta-discipline of how we access and transmit fiqh knowledge.

The Foundational Rule of Fatwa: Tarjih and Zahir al-Riwaya
Author's statement of the foundational rule for muftis.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the foundational rule of fatwa in the Hanafi madhhab as given by Ibn 'Abidin in his introduction to 'Uqud Rasm al-Mufti.

Core Idea
The mufti must follow the preponderant view or Zahir al-Riwaya – not personal preference.

Ibn 'Abidin opens his commentary by laying down the fundamental principle for issuing fatwas. This principle anchors the entire work and serves as the guiding rule for muftis in the Hanafi school. In this lesson, we will unpack this rule and understand its significance.

Guided Path
1
Identify the two sources
Find the two sources of authoritative rulings that Ibn 'Abidin says a mufti must follow.
Look for the words 'ترجيحة عن أهله' and 'ظاهر الرواية'.
2
Understand 'preponderant from its people'
This refers to the opinion that the mujtahids of the madhhab have determined to be strongest through tarjih.
Not any opinion, but one that has been weighed and chosen.
3
Understand Zahir al-Riwaya
Zahir al-Riwaya are the authoritative transmissions from Imam Muhammad's six core books. They are primary sources.
These take precedence over other narrations unless a tarjih overrides them.
4
Recognize the phrase 'they did not prefer contrary'
This implies that if there is no tarjih to the contrary, Zahir al-Riwaya stands as the binding rule.
It reinforces the hierarchy.
5
Apply the rule
Consider a scenario: can a mufti follow a weak (marjuh) opinion? No, because it violates this principle.
Only the preponderant or Zahir al-Riwaya is obligatory.

Commentary

This sets the methodological foundation for the entire work.

Author's Introduction: The Purpose of the Commentary on Rasm al-Mufti
Author's opening praise and description of the book

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the author's stated goals and the intended audience for the commentary 'Uqud Rasm al-Mufti.

Core Idea
A mufti's manual needs both a clear system and a clarifying commentary to bind every rule.

This lesson examines the opening of Ibn 'Abidin's commentary on his own poem about the rules governing a mufti. Here, the author sets the stage: he praises Allah, prays upon the Prophet, and then explains why he wrote this work — to clarify his poetic system and secure its subtle points. Understanding this front matter is crucial because it frames the entire text as a tool for both the acting mufti and the practitioner of ifta'.

Guided Path
1
Identify the three-part khutba structure
Most Hanafi works begin with praise (hamd), prayers (salawat), and the author's statement (amma ba'd). Pinpoint each in the passage.
This structure is standard and signals the text's religious seriousness.
2
Note the author's self-description and humility
Ibn 'Abidin calls himself 'the poorest of creation' and 'the sinful slave.' Why do Hanafi scholars begin with such humility?
Humility is both a religious virtue and a sign of scholarly caution.
3
Recognize the dual nature of the work (nazm + sharh)
The author wrote a poem (nazm) called 'Uqud Rasm al-Mufti and then a commentary (sharh) on it. Distinguish the two layers: the poem provides a concise system, the commentary expands and clarifies.
This layered approach helps memorization and deep understanding.
4
Identify the target audience
The author says the work is needed by 'the practitioner' (al-'amil) or 'the one who issues fatwas' (man yafti). Who are these two?
The practitioner applies the rules; the mufti issues the fatwa. Both need the framework.
5
Extract the author's stated purpose
The author says the commentary is 'to clarify its purposes and to capture its elusive points' (li-idahi maqasidiha wa taqyid awabidiha wa shawariha). What does 'taqyid' mean in this context?
Taqyid (restricting) here means to secure and pin down the rules that might otherwise be ambiguous.

Commentary
This passage is the table of contents of the book 'Sharh Uqud Rasm al-Mufti'. It lists the chapters and topics that will be covered, including: the division of correction, signs for issuing fatwa, the precedence of explicit statements over implicit concepts, the authority of custom, acting on weak (marjuh) opinions, and judging according to another school. The page numbers indicate where each topic begins. This front matter gives the reader an overview of the manual's structure, which is essential for navigating the detailed rules of ifta according to the Hanafi school.

Key Terms
رسم المفتي (rasm al-mufti): The code or manual outlining the rules and etiquette for a mufti in issuing fatwas according to the Hanafi school.
