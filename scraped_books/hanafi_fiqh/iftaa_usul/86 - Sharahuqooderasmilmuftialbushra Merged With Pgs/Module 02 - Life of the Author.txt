The Authority of al-Jami' al-Saghir and the Order of Zahir al-Riwayah
Explaining the order and authority of Muhammad al-Shaybani's books

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the six books of Zahir al-Riwayah, explain the relationship between al-Asl (al-Mabsut) and al-Jami' al-Saghir, and state the historical reason for its compilation.

Core Idea
Al-Jami' al-Saghir is the most relied-upon book among the six of Zahir al-Riwayah, superseding even al-Mabsut.

As a mufti, you must know the hierarchy of authority among the six core texts. This passage from Ibn Abidin's Rasm al-Mufti explains that while al-Mabsut (al-Asl) is the earliest, al-Jami' al-Saghir was compiled later at Abu Yusuf's request and is the final authority. This lesson will clarify the classification of these books and why al-Jami' al-Saghir holds a unique status.

Guided Path
1
Identify the six books of Zahir al-Riwayah
List the six core texts: al-Mabsut, al-Jami' al-Saghir, al-Jami' al-Kabir, al-Ziyadat, al-Siyar al-Saghir, and al-Siyar al-Kabir. Note that the passage mentions only four, but the standard list includes the two Siyar.
The passage says 'al-Usul' are 'the two Jami's, al-Ziyadat, and al-Mabsut', but later all six are considered al-Usul.
2
Understand 'al-Asl' as al-Mabsut
When Muhammad al-Shaybani says 'in al-Asl', commentators interpret it as al-Mabsut, which was authored first.
Al-Asl is a proper noun referring specifically to al-Mabsut.
3
Trace the order of compilation
The order is: al-Mabsut (first), then al-Jami' al-Saghir, then al-Jami' al-Kabir, then al-Ziyadat. The two Siyar are not mentioned in this order.
This order establishes a timeline for authority.
4
Recognize the hierarchy of reliance
Because al-Jami' al-Saghir was compiled later, its rulings take precedence over al-Mabsut in case of conflict. This is a key principle for muftis.
The principle is: al-muta'akhkhir muqaddam (the later is given precedence).
5
Understand the historical reason for compilation
Abu Yusuf requested a book to narrate from Muhammad from Abu Hanifa, leading to the compilation of al-Jami' al-Saghir, which contains 1,532 issues.
Abu Yusuf carried it with him always, and it was used to test judges.
