Copies of al-Mabsut and Their Commentaries
Documents the multiple transmissions and commentaries of al-Mabsut

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the most authoritative copy of al-Mabsut and explain how later Hanafi commentaries are cited in practice.

Core Idea
The Mabsut of Abu Sulayman al-Juzajani is the most authoritative transmission; later commentaries are often cited as the original.

When studying Hanafi fiqh, you will frequently encounter references to al-Mabsut by Imam Muhammad. However, not all copies are identical—some are later commentaries that mix the original text with explanations. This lesson clarifies which copy is most reliable and how to interpret citations from later scholars like Qadi Khan.

Guided Path
1
Identify the main work
Recognize that al-Mabsut is a foundational text by Imam Muhammad al-Shaybani, part of the Zahir al-Riwayah corpus.
2
Distinguish the copies
Note that multiple copies exist, but the most apparent (azhar) is by Abu Sulayman al-Juzajani. Later scholars like Khuwahir Zadeh and al-Hulwani wrote commentaries that are often mixed with the original text.
This mixing can lead to confusion: what is cited as 'al-Mabsut' may actually be a commentary.
3
Apply the citation rule
When a later scholar like Qadi Khan says 'mentioned in al-Jami al-Saghir', he typically means his own commentary on that work, not the original. The same applies to other texts.
This is crucial for accurate fatwa: verifying the actual source.
4
Trace the source of this summary
Know that this information is derived from commentaries on al-Ashbah wa al-Naza'ir by al-Bayri and on Sharh al-Durar by Ismail al-Nabulsi.
These are authoritative later Hanafi works.

Definition and Authority of al-Siyar in Hanafi Fiqh
Defining the term al-Siyar in Hanafi legal literature

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to define the term al-Siyar in Hanafi legal literature, state its correct pronunciation, and identify the hierarchy of authority among Imam Muhammad's six books, specifically the status of al-Siyar al-Kabir as mu'tamad.

Core Idea
Al-Siyar al-Kabir is the authoritative work on jihad; its pronunciation and place in the six-book hierarchy are essential for fatwa.

This lesson clarifies the technical term 'al-Siyar' as used by Hanafi jurists, distinguishing it from its linguistic meaning. It also corrects a common pronunciation error and establishes the hierarchy of Imam Muhammad's six books, showing why al-Siyar al-Kabir is the relied-upon source for fatwas on jihad and international law. Understanding these foundational points ensures that you cite sources correctly and authoritatively.

Guided Path
1
Identify the term and its meaning
Recognize that al-Siyar is the plural of Sirah, which in Shariah specifically refers to the Prophet's military expeditions (maghazi). This is stated in al-Hidayah.
The term is distinct from general biography; it has a legal focus on campaigns and rulings.
2
Correct the pronunciation
The correct pronunciation is 'al-Siyar al-Kabir' with a kasra on the sin (sī) and fatha on the ya (yar), in the plural form. Avoid pronouncing it as 'al-Sair' (singular) which is a common error.
The passage explicitly warns against saying 'Sair al-Kabir'.
3
Understand the hierarchy of the six books
Al-Mabsut is the first and foremost (al-Asl), followed by al-Jami al-Saghir. The latest compilation is al-Siyar al-Kabir, which is considered the authoritative (mu'tamad) source for rulings on jihad.
The precedence is based on order of composition: earlier books are foundational, but the later compilation is comprehensive and relied upon.
4
Apply to fatwa practice
A mufti must know that for siyar-related questions, al-Siyar al-Kabir takes precedence over earlier works, even though al-Mabsut is the origin. This hierarchy ensures accurate and authoritative fatwa.
The mufti should also pronounce the title correctly to maintain scholarly credibility.

Commentary
This passage is a terminological definition of 'al-Siyar' as used in Hanafi fiqh. It begins as an appendix explaining that 'al-Siyar' is the plural of 'Sirah', literally meaning a way or method. In legal context, it specifically refers to the military expeditions (maghazi) of the Prophet Muhammad, as stated in al-Hidayah. The author then cites al-Maghrib to clarify the correct pronunciation and grammatical form of the title 'al-Siyar al-Kabir'. It must be pronounced with a kasra on the sin and fatha on the ya (as a plural), not with a fatha on the sin and sukun on the ya (as a singular). The latter is a common mistake. The passage ends with a note on the order and authority of the six books of Imam Muhammad: al-Mabsut is the first and foremost (al-Asl), followed by al-Jami al-Saghir, and then the later compilation is al-Siyar al-Kabir, which is considered authoritative (mu'tamad).

Key Terms
السير (al-Siyar): Plural of Sirah; in shariah, specifically refers to the Prophet's military campaigns and related rulings.
السيرة (al-Sirah): A method or way; in religious context, the biography of the Prophet, especially his military expeditions.
السير الكبير (al-Siyar al-Kabir): The title of Imam Muhammad's work on the law of war and peace; one of the six usul.
