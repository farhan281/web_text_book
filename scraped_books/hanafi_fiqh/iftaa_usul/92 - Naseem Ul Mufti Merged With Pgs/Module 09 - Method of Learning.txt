Hanafi Division of Textual Indications: Mantiq and Mafhum
Explains Hanafi division of textual indications into mantiq and mafhum, contrasting with Shafi'i school

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to classify a given textual indication as mantiq or mafhum according to Hanafi usul, identify its sub-type, and apply the hierarchy of strength in case of conflict.

Core Idea
Hanafis divide textual indications into four, but only three are mantiq; dilalah al-nass is mafhum, and strength declines from 'ibarah to iqtida.

A mufti must weigh textual evidence precisely. The Hanafi school provides a sophisticated classification of how texts convey meaning—some directly (mantiq) and some indirectly (mafhum). This lesson unpacks these categories and their hierarchy, a core tool for resolving apparent contradictions.

Guided Path
1
Identify the four types of textual indication
From the passage, list the four types: 'ibarah al-nass (direct meaning), isharah al-nass (inferred meaning), dilalah al-nass (reasoned meaning, similar to qiyas jali), and iqtida' al-nass (implied meaning necessary for correctness).
Associate each with an example from the text.
2
Sort into mantiq and mafhum
According to Hanafis, the first three ('ibarah, isharah, iqtida') are mantiq (explicit); dilalah is mafhum (implicit). Note the contrast with Shafi'is who only have two categories.
The passage states: 'حنفیہ کے نزدیک منطوق کے ضمن میں دلالت کی تینوں اقسام یعنی عبارة النص، اشارۃ النص اور اقتضاء النص آجاتی ہیں اور دلالۃ النص کو حنفیہ مفہوم سے تعبیر کرتے ہیں.'
3
Understand the strength hierarchy
Memorize the order: 'ibarah > isharah > dilalah > iqtida'. In case of conflict, the stronger indication overrides the weaker.
The passage provides this hierarchy explicitly.
4
Compare with the Shafi'i classification
Shafi'is and mutakallimun divide only into mantiq (explicit) and mafhum (implicit), without subdividing mantiq further. This difference affects how each school handles conflicting evidence.
The thamarah al-khilaf: in conflict, Hanafis use the hierarchy; Shafi'is weigh mantiq over mafhum as a whole.
5
Apply to a new example
Given a Quranic verse or hadith, identify which type of indication is at work and its strength. For instance, the verse 'Do not say 'uff' to your parents' (17:23) is 'ibarah for forbidding harsh words, but its mafhum muwafaqah (via fahwa al-khitab) also forbids harsher acts like hitting.
This shows the practical use of the classification in istinbat.

Commentary
This passage provides a detailed exposition of the Hanafi classification of textual indications (dalalat al-nass) and contrasts it with the Shafi'i/mutakallimun approach. The passage opens with an example of iqtida' al-nass (implicative meaning): the prohibition of carrion in the Quran necessarily implies the benefit (intifa') which is hidden but essential for the meaning to be correct. Then it highlights important rules: all four types of indication ('ibarah, isharah, dilalah, iqtida') produce definitive rulings unless contradicted, and they have a hierarchy of strength: 'ibarah > isharah > dilalah > iqtida'. In case of conflict, the stronger prevails. These four are collectively called dalalat al-mantiq (explicit indication) in Hanafi terminology. The passage then explains that Shafi'is and mutakallimun divide text indication into only two: mantiq and mafhum. For Hanafis, mantiq includes 'ibarah, isharah, and iqtida'; dilalah al-nass is considered mafhum. Then mafhum is defined and subdivided into mafhum muwafaqah (coinciding meaning) and mafhum mukhalafah (opposite meaning). Mafhum muwafaqah is further divided into fahwa al-khitab (greater reason) and lahn al-khitab (implied meaning). The author follows the Hanafi school throughout, presenting its classification as the primary reference point.

Key Terms
اقتضاء النص (iqtida' al-nass): A meaning implied by the text as necessary for its correctness, even if not explicitly stated
عبارة النص ('ibarah al-nass): The direct meaning of the text for which it was brought
اشارة النص (isharah al-nass): The meaning inferred from the text not directly but as a necessary consequence
دلالة النص (dilalah al-nass): A meaning derived from the text through reasoning (Qiyas jali), considered mafhum by Hanafis
منطوق (mantiq): Explicit meaning directly indicated by the words of the text
مفہوم (mafhum): Implicit meaning understood from the text but not directly stated
مفہوم موافق (mafhum muwafaqah): When the unmentioned case is given the same ruling as the mentioned case
مفہوم مخالف (mafhum mukhalafah): When the unmentioned case is given the opposite ruling of the mentioned case
