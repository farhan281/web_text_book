The Textual Hierarchy: From Zahir al-Riwaya to al-Mabsut
Clarifying the commentary lineage of Hanafi texts

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to trace the chain of authority from Imam Muhammad's Zahir al-Riwaya to al-Sarakhsi's al-Mabsut via al-Hakim al-Shahid's al-Kafi.

Core Idea
Al-Sarakhsi's Mabsut is the authoritative commentary on al-Kafi, which itself summarizes the six books of Zahir al-Riwaya.

This lesson clarifies the bibliographic landscape of early Hanafi texts, essential for understanding fatwa authority. Knowing which works are commentaries on which is crucial for correctly citing sources and assessing their weight in fatwa.

Guided Path
1
Recognize the core books
Identify the six books of Zahir al-Riwaya by Imam Muhammad al-Shaybani as the primary authoritative sources in the Hanafi school.
These are al-Mabsut (the original), al-Jami' al-Saghir, al-Jami' al-Kabir, al-Siyar al-Saghir, al-Siyar al-Kabir, and al-Ziyadat.
2
Understand al-Kafi as a summary
Al-Hakim al-Shahid compiled al-Kafi, which is a concise work (mukhtasar) that summarizes the six books of Zahir al-Riwaya into a single volume.
This made the content more accessible while preserving authoritative rulings.
3
Identify al-Mabsut as a commentary
Al-Sarakhsi wrote al-Mabsut as a detailed commentary (sharh) on al-Kafi, explaining and expanding its rulings.
When 'al-Mabsut' is used unrestrictedly, it refers to al-Sarakhsi's work.
4
Distinguish from other Mabsuts
Note that there are other Mabsut works by earlier Hanafi scholars like Abu Yusuf and al-Jurjani, but al-Sarakhsi's Mabsut is the most famous.
This distinction prevents confusion when citing sources.

The book "Al-Kafi" by al-Hakim. The Hanafis have many Mabsut works: some by Abu Yusuf, and by Muhammad called "Al-Asl", and Mabsut al-Jurjani, al-Khawahirzada, Shams al-A'imma al-Halwani, Abu al-Yusr al-Bazdawi, his brother Ali al-Bazdawi, Sayyid Nasir al-Din al-Samarqandi, and Abu al-Layth Nasr ibn Muhammad. When Mabsut is mentioned unrestrictedly, what is meant is this Mabsut of al-Sarakhsi, which is a commentary on al-Kafi. And this al-Kafi is Kafi al-Hakim al-Shahid, the great scholar, Muhammad ibn Muhammad Ahmad ibn ibn Abdullah, he was appointed judge of Bukhara, then the glorious prince, the ruler of Khurasan, appointed him to his vizierate. He heard hadith from many, and compiled the books of Muhammad ibn al-Hasan in this concise work. Al-Dhahabi mentioned him and praised him. Al-Hakim said in Tarikh Nisabur: I have not seen among those from whom I wrote among the followers of Abu Hanifa one more memorizer of hadith, more guided by its forms, and more understanding of it than him. He was killed while prostrating in Rabi' al-Awwal 334 AH. I say: Al-Hakim al-Shahid also has "Al-Mukhtasar", "Al-Muntaqa", "Al-Isharat", and others. Al-Sarakhsi's statement: "I saw that the correct thing is to author a commentary on al-Mukhtasar" does not indicate that al-Sarakhsi's Mabsut is a commentary on al-Mukhtasar, not a commentary on al-Kafi, as al-Khair al-Ramli imagined in his gloss on al-Ashbah. For al-Kafi is also a concise work because he summarized in it the books of Zahir al-Riwaya, as you have learned. And he frequently quotes from al-Kafi in Ghayat al-Bayan by saying: Al-Hakim al-Shahid said in his concise work called al-Kafi. And Allah Almighty knows best.

Commentary
This passage is a bibliographic note on major Hanafi works, particularly clarifying the relationship between al-Kafi by al-Hakim al-Shahid and al-Mabsut by al-Sarakhsi. The author lists several early Mabsut works and then specifies that the unrestrictedly mentioned Mabsut is that of al-Sarakhsi, which is a commentary on al-Kafi. He provides a brief biography of al-Hakim al-Shahid, praising his hadith knowledge. He then corrects a misunderstanding by al-Khair al-Ramli: al-Sarakhsi's intention to write a commentary on al-Mukhtasar does not mean his Mabsut is on al-Mukhtasar; rather, al-Kafi itself is a mukhtasar (concise work) as it summarizes the books of Zahir al-Riwaya. The author cites evidence from Ghayat al-Bayan where references to al-Kafi are made as 'al-mukhtasar'. This is important for usul al-ifta because it clarifies the authority of works used in fatwa.

Key Terms
المبسوط (al-Mabsut): The extensive commentary work by al-Sarakhsi, which is a commentary on al-Kafi.
الكافي (al-Kafi): A concise compilation of the books of Zahir al-Riwaya by al-Hakim al-Shahid.
ظاهر الرواية (Zahir al-Riwaya): The six core books of Imam Muhammad that form the basis of Hanafi fiqh.

Al-Hakim al-Shahid: Scholar, Judge, and Compiler of al-Kafi
Biographical note on al-Hakim al-Shahid

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to describe the contributions of al-Hakim al-Shahid to Hanafi fiqh and list his major works.

Core Idea
Al-Hakim al-Shahid, a judge and vizier, compiled al-Kafi, the foundational concise work that later scholars like al-Sarakhsi expounded.

This lesson highlights the life and achievements of al-Hakim al-Shahid, emphasizing his role in preserving the Hanafi tradition through his concise compilation and his other scholarly works.

Guided Path
1
Identify the full name and titles
Locate the full name: Muhammad ibn Muhammad ibn Ahmad ibn Abdullah. He is known as al-Hakim al-Shahid due to his martyrdom.
Al-Hakim means the wise/judge, al-Shahid means the martyr.
2
Note his official positions
He was appointed judge (qadi) of Bukhara and later vizier by the ruler of Khurasan.
His high rank shows his scholarly and political influence.
3
Recognize his hadith expertise
He studied hadith from many scholars, and was praised by Al-Hakim of Nishapur as the best Hanafi in hadith knowledge.
This expertise lends credibility to his fiqh compilation.
4
List his major works
Besides al-Kafi, he authored al-Mukhtasar, al-Muntaqa, al-Isharat, and others.
Some of these are also concise works, showing his focus on summarization.
5
Understand his martyrdom
He was killed while prostrating in prayer in 334 AH.
His death is a reminder of the sacrifices of early scholars.
