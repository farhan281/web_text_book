Understanding this context helps you appreciate the book's authority and practical focus. The author's careful revision process shows the importance of precision in ifta' training.

In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful. Praise be to Allah, Lord of the worlds, and blessings and peace be upon our master, the Seal of the Prophets, and upon his family and all his Companions, and upon all who follow them in righteousness until the Day of Judgment. To proceed: While teaching students specializing in ifta' at Jamia Darul Uloom Karachi, I dictated to them a memorandum in which I summarized 'Sharh Uqud Rasm al-Mufti' by Ibn Abidin, may Allah have mercy on him, and added to it some benefits regarding the reality of fatwa, its history, conditions, and etiquettes from various books. Commentary
This passage is the author's preface. It describes the origin of the book: it began as a memorandum (mudhakkara) summarizing Ibn Abidin's 'Sharh Uqud Rasm al-Mufti', dictated to students specializing in ifta' at Darul Uloom Karachi. The author added material on the reality, history, conditions, and etiquettes of fatwa from various sources. Students circulated the notes, and many asked for publication. However, the author delayed to revise and expand it into an independent work. After years, he revised thoroughly, consulting many books, refining complex issues, and deleting some content. The result is this comprehensive book. The preface shows the author's meticulous methodology and his aim to serve students of ifta'.

Key Terms
الإفتاء (al-ifta'): The process of issuing fatwas; legal responsa.
شرح عقود رسم المفتى (Sharh Uqud Rasm al-Mufti): Ibn Abidin's commentary on his own work 'Uqud Rasm al-Mufti', a foundational text on the rules and conditions of issuing fatwas in the Hanafi school.

The Gravity of Fatwa: A Call to Responsibility
Author concludes introduction and announces next section on the seriousness of ifta

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the title of the next section and explain why the author emphasizes the gravity of fatwa.

Core Idea
Issuing fatwa is a grave responsibility—ignorance can lead to sin.

Having expressed gratitude to his assistants, the author turns to the main subject. The very title he announces—'The Fatwa and Its Gravity'—signals that ifta' is not a casual matter. In this lesson, we pause to absorb the weight of that announcement before diving into the detailed rulings.

Guided Path
1
Identify the author's purpose in the acknowledgments
The author thanks his assistant and explains the method for including jurist biographies. Note that he only introduces lesser-known figures; major figures like the four imams and the two Sahibayn need no introduction.
This reflects the Hanafi tradition of relying on well-established authorities.
2
Locate the transitional phrase
The final line of the source passage is 'الفتوى وخطورتها'—'The Fatwa and Its Gravity'. This is the title of the next section.
Memorize this title: it encapsulates the central theme of the book's first major part.
3
Reflect on the word 'خطورة' (gravity)
The author could have used 'أهمية' (importance) but chose 'خطورة' (gravity/danger). Why? Because a wrong fatwa can lead to haram, invalid worship, or injustice.
In Hanafi usul al-ifta, a mufti must be acutely aware of the consequences of his rulings.

The Hanafi Mufti's Curriculum: Tabaqat and Rasm al-Mufti
Book table of contents listing tabaqat and rasm al-mufti

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the components of the Hanafi fatwa curriculum as outlined by Muhammad Taqi al-Uthmani and explain the hierarchy of authoritative sources within the madhhab.

Core Idea
A mufti must know the layers of jurists and the hierarchy of sources: Zahir al-Riwaya is the strongest, followed by Nawadir and Fatawa.

This table of contents is not just a list; it reveals the structure of Hanafi ifta training. It tells a mufti where to look for rulings, how to prioritize conflicting narrations, and which principles govern the fatwa process. Understanding this roadmap is essential before diving into the detailed rules.

Guided Path
1
Identify the two major parts of the book
The table of contents divides the text into two main sections: (1) Layers of Jurists and Layers of Hanafi Issues, and (2) Summary of the Rules of the Mufti (Rasm al-Mufti).
This division shows that a mufti must first understand the historical and textual hierarchy before applying procedural rules.
2
Break down Part 1: Layers of Jurists
List the layers: first Hanafi jurists, then Shafi'i jurists. This shows the importance of knowing the scholarly pedigree and authority of different generations.
Tabaqat helps determine which later opinions carry weight relative to earlier ones.
3
Break down Part 1: Layers of Hanafi Issues
Identify the subcategories: Zahir al-Riwaya (principles), the six books of Imam Muhammad, Nawadir (rare transmissions), Fatawa wa Waqiat, and the classification by Shah Wali Allah al-Dahlawi.
Zahir al-Riwaya is the highest authority; Nawadir are less authoritative but may be used when no clear ruling exists in Zahir al-Riwaya.
4
Examine Part 2: The Six Principles of Rasm al-Mufti
List the six usul: conditions of the mufti, single ruling case, two narrations from Abu Hanifa, adopting the opinion of preferential scholars (ashab al-tarjih), dealing with unreliable books, and explicit vs. implicit preference.
These principles form the procedural framework for issuing fatwas.
5
Connect the parts to the mufti's task
Explain that the mufti must first locate the ruling within the hierarchical sources (Part 1) and then apply the rules of preference and procedure (Part 2) to arrive at the fatwa.
This table of contents is a checklist for the mufti's methodology.

Commentary
This passage is the table of contents from 'Usul al-Ifta wa Adabuhu' by Muhammad Taqi al-Uthmani. It outlines the major sections of the book. The first part deals with the layers (tabaqat) of jurists, first Hanafi then Shafi'i, showing the historical hierarchy of scholarship. Then it covers the layers of Hanafi legal issues: the principles (usul) which are also called Zahir al-Riwaya—the six books of Imam Muhammad al-Shaybani (al-Mabsut, al-Jami' al-Saghir, al-Jami' al-Kabir, al-Ziyadat, al-Siyar al-Saghir, al-Siyar al-Kabir). Next are the Nawadir (rare transmissions) and then Fatawa and Waqiat (fatwas and real cases). A special classification by Shah Wali Allah al-Dahlawi is also noted. The second major part is 'Talkhis Qawaid Rasm al-Mufti' (Summary of the Rules of the Mufti's Protocol), which contains six principles (usul): conditions of the mufti, cases with a single ruling, cases with two narrations from Abu Hanifa, adopting the opinion of the preferential scholars, dealing with unreliable books, and explicit versus implicit preference. This table of contents serves as a roadmap for the mufti's training, emphasizing the authoritative sources and the methodology for issuing fatwas.

Key Terms
طبقات الفقهاء (tabaqat al-fuqaha): Layers or generations of jurists, used to categorize scholars by time and authority.
رسم المفتى (rasm al-mufti): The protocol or standard procedure for a mufti to follow when issuing fatwas.
ظاهر الرواية (zahir al-riwaya): The well-known and widely transmitted rulings from the Hanafi founding imams, forming the core of the madhhab.
النوادر (al-nawadir): Rare or less commonly transmitted rulings within the Hanafi school, often from single narrations.
أصول ستة (usul sitta): The six principles of Rasm al-Mufti as listed in the table of contents.

Navigating the Index of Scholars (قائمة الأعلام)
A list of scholars indexed with page numbers for biographies.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to locate and interpret the index of scholars in 'Uṣūl al-Iftā’ wa Ādābuhu' to identify key Hanafi authorities cited in the text.

Core Idea
This index is your roadmap to the scholarly authorities underpinning every fatwa in this book.

Before diving into the rules of iftā’, you need to know who the authorities are. This front-matter index lists over 40 scholars, from early companions to later Ḥanafī jurists, with page references to their biographies. Mastering this index will help you understand the weight of different opinions and the chain of transmission.

Guided Path
1
Identify the structure
The list is alphabetical by scholar name in Arabic. Each entry includes the scholar's full name, often with honorifics, and a page number where their biography appears.
In this extract, page numbers are omitted for brevity, but in the original they are present.
2
Recognize key titles
Notice titles like 'al-Imām', 'al-‘Allāmah', 'al-Faqīh', 'al-Qāḍī'. These indicate the scholar's rank and specialization.
3
Find familiar Hanafi giants
Scan for names like al-Qudūrī, al-Kāsānī, Qāḍīkhān, al-Karkhī. These are core authorities in Hanafi fiqh.
4
Connect to the main text
When reading a fatwa or opinion, you can refer to this index to see if a biography is provided and on which page.
5
Appreciate the breadth
The index includes scholars from other schools as well, showing the book's comprehensive scope.

Commentary
This passage is a front-matter index of scholars (قائمة الأعلام المترجم لهم) featured in the book 'أصول الإفتاء وآدابه' by Muhammad Taqi al-'Uthmani. It provides a list of names of prominent Hanafi scholars (and some others) along with page numbers (not shown in this extract) where their biographies or contributions are discussed in the book. The list includes famous Hanafi jurists like al-Quduri, al-Kasani, Qadikhan, and al-Karkhi, as well as scholars from other disciplines like al-Ghazali and al-Qushayri. For students of Hanafi fiqh, this index helps locate the authority and background of scholars cited in the text.
