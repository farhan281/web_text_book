Biography of Imam al-Nawawi: A Scholar's Legacy
Presents the life and scholarly achievements of Imam al-Nawawi.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state key facts about Imam al-Nawawi's life, identify his major works, and explain his relevance to the Hanafi school.

Core Idea
Imam al-Nawawi authored essential works in hadith and fiqh, despite his short life, and his legacy spans all madhhabs.

This lesson introduces you to Imam al-Nawawi (d. 676/1277), a Shafi'i scholar whose works are cornerstones of Sunni learning. Even though he belonged to the Shafi'i school, his hadith commentaries and collections are indispensable for Hanafis studying hadith and fiqh methodology. Understanding his life and achievements provides context for why his works appear frequently in the Hanafi curriculum.

Guided Path
1
Identify the person
Read the name and lineage: Yahya ibn Sharaf al-Nawawi, known as Muhyi al-Din Abu Zakariyya.
2
Note his educational background
He moved to Damascus at age 19 and studied for 6 years without wasting time.
3
List his major works
From the text: Sharh Muslim, Riyad al-Salihin, al-Majmu', Rawdat al-Talibin.
4
Recognize his personal characteristics
He was a celibate scholar, pious, and a master of hadith and Shafi'i fiqh.
5
Memorize his death details
Year 676 AH, age 45, in his hometown Nawa.

Imam al-Nawawi: Yahya ibn Sharaf al-Nawawi, the shaykh, imam, scholar, Muhyi al-Din Abu Zakariyya. He was born, may Allah have mercy on him, in Nawa, a village in Syria, and came to Damascus at the age of nineteen, and devoted himself to seeking knowledge and jurisprudence. The scholar Ibn al-Attar, his student, said: 'My shaykh told me that he never wasted any time, night or day, except in study, even on the roads, and he continued this for six years, then he began authoring, benefiting, advising, and speaking the truth.' As al-Hafiz al-Dhahabi, may Allah have mercy on him, said: 'He was, with what he had of self-struggle and acting on the subtleties of piety and self-purification, a memorizer of hadith, its sciences and transmitters, and a master in knowledge of the Shafi'i school.' He was among the celibate scholars who preferred knowledge over marriage. He authored, in a short lifespan, many verified works, including Sharh Sahih Muslim, Riyad al-Salihin, al-Majmu' Sharh al-Muhadhdhab (but he did not complete it, reaching only the chapter on al-mudarra), and Rawdat al-Talibin which he abridged from Sharh al-Wajiz by Imam al-Rafi'i, may Allah have mercy on them all. When his time drew near towards Nawa, he fell ill for a few days then passed away, may Allah have mercy on him, returning to his Lord in the year 676 AH at the age of forty-five. Summary from Tadhkirat al-Huffaz 4:1470-1474, and Tabaqat al-Shafi'iyya al-Kubra 8:395-400 with footnotes, and al-Ulama' al-Uzzab by Abd al-Fattah Abu Ghudda, p.92 onwards.

Commentary
This footnote provides a biography of Imam al-Nawawi (d. 676/1277), a towering Shafi'i scholar. Though he is from a different madhhab, his works are widely used in Hanafi circles for hadith and fiqh methodology. The biography highlights his extraordinary dedication: he began serious study at nineteen, spent six years in continuous learning, then authored major works. He was known for his piety and asceticism, remaining unmarried to devote himself to knowledge. His most famous works include Sharh Sahih Muslim (a standard commentary), Riyad al-Salihin (a collection of hadith on ethics), and al-Majmu' (an unfinished comprehensive fiqh work). He died at only 45, leaving a immense scholarly legacy. For Hanafi students, understanding his contribution is important because his works, especially Sharh Muslim and Riyad al-Salihin, are used across madhhabs.

الإمام النووي (al-Imam al-Nawawi): A Shafi'i scholar and author of major works in hadith and fiqh, known for his piety and prolific writing despite a short life.
العلماء العزاب (al-ulama' al-uzzab): Celibate scholars who preferred knowledge over marriage.

Biography of Muhammad Zahid al-Kawthari: A Defender of Hanafi Orthodoxy
Biographical note on a Hanafi scholar's life.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the key biographical details of Muhammad Zahid al-Kawthari and explain his significance in defending the Hanafi school against modernist and secularist challenges.

Core Idea
Al-Kawthari defended the Hanafi school through scholarship and sacrifice, opposing secularizing reforms.

This lesson presents the life of Muhammad Zahid al-Kawthari, a towering Hanafi scholar of the 14th century AH. His biography illustrates the struggle of traditional scholars against the wave of secularization during the late Ottoman Empire and the early Turkish Republic. Understanding his legacy helps contextualize the Hanafi tradition's resilience against modern challenges.

Guided Path
1
Identify the scholar's full name and lineage
Note his complete name, geographic origin, and ethnic background (Circassian). This establishes his roots in the late Ottoman scholarly world.
2
Track his education and scholarly rise
He studied at the Fatih Mosque in Istanbul and became head of the teaching council. This underscores his high standing in the Ottoman religious establishment.
3
Understand the political persecution he faced
The 'Unionists' (Young Turks) persecuted him for opposing the replacement of religious sciences with modern sciences. Later, the Kemalists issued an arrest warrant.
4
Map his migration and settlement
He fled to Alexandria, traveled between Egypt and the Levant, and finally settled in Cairo. This reflects the diaspora of Ottoman scholars after the secular reforms.
5
Catalog his major works and their significance
He authored refutations of anti-Hanafi polemics, treatises on early Hanafi imams, and about 100 articles. These works are foundational for defending the school's legacy.

(ملخص من مقدمة مقالات الكوثري للعلامة محمد يوسف البنوري رحمه الله تعالى والأعلام ٩:١٢٩)
The scholar Muhammad Zahid al-Kawthari: He is Muhammad Zahid bin al-Hasan bin Ali al-Kawthari al-Hanafi, the scholar, hadith master, and jurist. He was born in 1296 AH in a village of 'Duzce' in eastern Istanbul and grew up there, of Circassian origin. He studied jurisprudence at the Fatih Mosque in Istanbul, then became the head of the teaching council. The 'Unionists' persecuted him during World War I because he opposed their plan to replace religious sciences with modern sciences in most study hours. When the 'Kemalists' came to power and prepared for atheism, an arrest warrant was issued for him, so he boarded a ship to Alexandria (1341 AH = 1922 CE) and moved between Egypt and Syria before settling in Cairo. He was proficient in Arabic, Turkish, Persian, and Circassian. His works include: 'Ta'nib al-Khatib' on the lies in al-Khatib's biography of Abu Hanifa, 'al-Nukat al-Tarifa' on Ibn Abi Shayba's refutations of Abu Hanifa, 'al-Istibsar' on predestination and free will, and treatises on the biographies of Imam Abu Yusuf, Muhammad ibn al-Hasan al-Shaybani, Zufar, al-Hasan ibn Ziyad, Muhammad ibn Shuja', al-Tahawi, and Badr al-Din al-'Ayni—all published. He has about a hundred articles collected in the book 'Maqalat al-Kawthari'. He died in Cairo in 1371 AH. (Summary from the introduction of 'Maqalat al-Kawthari' by al-Allama Muhammad Yusuf al-Banuri and al-A'lam 9:129).

This is a detailed biographical note on Muhammad Zahid al-Kawthari, a prominent Hanafi scholar of the late Ottoman period. The note outlines his birth, education, persecution by the Unionists and Kemalists, migration to Egypt, linguistic skills, and his numerous writings defending the Hanafi school. His works include critical responses to anti-Hanafi polemics and biographical dictionaries of early Hanafi imams. The note is extracted from the introduction of his collected articles by Muhammad Yusuf al-Banuri and from al-A'lam.

And it appears that al-Jami' al-Kabir was authored by Imam Muhammad, may Allah have mercy on him, after al-Jami' al-Saghir. The list is titled 'قائمة الأعلام المترجم لهم' (Index of Notable Persons with Biographies). It contains names of scholars followed by honorifics and page numbers referencing their biographies in the footnotes.
The page numbers may appear as scattered digits or phrases like 'ص ٤٦' (page 46).

Identify the Scholar's Madhhab
Look at the titles: 'الإمام' (Imam) for major mujtahids, 'العلامة' (Allamah) for high-ranking scholars. The name may hint at madhhab (e.g., 'ابن حزم الظاهري' is Zahiri, 'ابن عابدين الشامي' is Hanafi). Cross-reference with known Hanafi authorities like Ibn Nujaym or Ibn al-Humam.

3
Locate the Biography using Page References
Each entry has a page reference (e.g., 'ص ١٧' means page 17 in the original Arabic edition). Use this reference to find the full biographical note in the footnotes of your copy.
Modern editions may have different pagination; consult the index key.
4

A scholar's name is a key to his legal rulings and texts.

In the Hanafi tradition, knowing who authored a text is as important as the text itself. This lesson introduces you to the biographical index of scholars referenced in Usul al-Iftaa wa Adabuhu, a standard work for Hanafi muftis. By learning to recognize these names and their associated works, you will navigate classical sources with confidence.

Guided Path
1
Identify the scholar's full name and lineage
Read the entry carefully. Note the full name (e.g., 'al-Marghinani, 'Ali ibn Abi Bakr ibn 'Abd al-Jalil al-Farghani'). This helps distinguish between scholars with similar names.
Some names include 'ibn' (son of) indicating lineage.
2
Note the honorific and title
Titles like 'al-Imam', 'al-Allamah', 'al-Faqih' indicate the scholar's status. For example, 'Malik al-'Ulama' (king of scholars) is used for al-Kasani.
Honorifics help assess the level of authority.
3
Recognize the author's major work
If the entry includes 'sahib' (author of) a famous book, note it. For instance, 'sahib al-Hidaya' refers to al-Marghinani, whose work is a core Hanafi fiqh text.
Knowing the work links the scholar to a specific fiqh tradition.
4
Connect the scholar to his period and role
Use contextual clues: early figures like al-Layth ibn Sa'd (d. 175 AH) were tabi‘in, while later ones like al-Kasani (d. 587 AH) are muta’akhkhirin. This helps situate the source.
Period influences the weight of a scholar's opinion in fatwa.

Using the Index of Biographical Entries in Hanafi Studies
Index of scholar biographies with page references.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to locate and identify key Hanafi scholars from the index and explain the purpose of such an index in usul al-iftaa works.

Core Idea
A scholar's index is a map of authority — knowing who the faces of the madhhab are is the first step to understanding its chain.

This page is an index of biographical entries (قائمة الأعلام المترجم لهم) from the book 'Usul al-Ifta wa Adabuh' by Muhammad Taqi al-Uthmani. It lists the names of scholars cited or discussed, along with page references for their biographies. While it may seem like a simple list, it reveals the scholarly network behind Hanafi jurisprudence. Learning to navigate such an index is essential for any student of usul al-iftaa.

Guided Path
1
Identify the Page's Purpose
Recognize that this is an index, not a narrative. It lists scholar names and page numbers where their biographies appear.
Look for the title 'قائمة الأعلام المترجم لهم' which means 'Index of Biographical Entries'.
2
Read the Scholar Entries
Each entry includes the scholar's name, honorific titles, often a key work they authored, and sometimes their madhhab affiliation.
For example, 'المرغيناني' is introduced as 'صاحب الهداية' (author of al-Hidayah).
3
Distinguish Hanafi from Non-Hanafi
Among the list, identify Hanafi luminaries (e.g., al-Kawthari, al-Marghinani, al-Nasafi, Mulla Khusraw) and those from other madhhabs (e.g., al-Nawawi is Shafi'i).
Knowing a scholar's madhhab helps assess the weight of their opinion in Hanafi fatwa.
4
Understand the Usul al-Iftaa Context
Such an index helps a mufti quickly find biographical information about scholars cited in fatwa discussions, aiding in evaluating the reliability of their opinions.
The chain of authority is crucial in usul al-iftaa.
5
Apply: Use the Index for Research
Practice locating a specific scholar's biography using the page reference. For instance, find al-Marghinani's biography on page 95 (as per the index).
These page numbers are key to efficient research.
