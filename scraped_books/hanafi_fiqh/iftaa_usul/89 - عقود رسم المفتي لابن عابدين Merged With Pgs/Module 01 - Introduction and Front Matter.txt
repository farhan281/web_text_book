Naming and Hierarchy of the Six Books of Zahir al-Riwayah
Explains the naming of al-Mabsut as al-Asl and the ordering of the six canonical books.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the order of the six books of Zahir al-Riwayah and apply the rule of later authorship to resolve conflicts between them.

Core Idea
When the canonical books conflict, the later-penned work prevails — it supersedes the earlier as if retracted.

Muftis rely on a fixed set of authoritative texts. Among the Hanafis, the six books of Zahir al-Riwayah form the bedrock. But which one rules when they disagree? Ibn Abidin explains that the order of composition determines precedence: the later the book, the stronger its authority. This principle is crucial for correct fatwa extraction.

Guided Path
1
Identify the six books
List the six books of Zahir al-Riwayah as given: al-Mabsut, al-Jami' al-Saghir, al-Jami' al-Kabir, al-Ziyadat, al-Siyar al-Saghir, al-Siyar al-Kabir. Note that al-Siyar al-Kabir is sometimes considered the last and most relied-upon.
Remember that al-Mabsut is also called al-Asl.
2
Understand the naming 'al-Asl'
Read the quote from Dr. Boynukalin explaining why al-Mabsut is called al-Asl: it was the first comprehensive compilation, forming the foundation of Hanafi fiqh.
The term 'al-Asl' may have originally meant 'primary source' before becoming a proper name.
3
Learn the chronological order
Memorize the order of authorship provided: al-Mabsut, then al-Jami' al-Saghir, then al-Jami' al-Kabir, then al-Ziyadat, then al-Siyar al-Kabir. This order is the basis for the hierarchy.
Al-Siyar al-Saghir is mentioned in the list of six but not in the order; it is assumed to be before al-Siyar al-Kabir.
4
Apply the conflict-resolution rule
The rule: if two books of Zahir al-Riwayah differ on a ruling, the later-authored book is taken. The later work is considered a retraction (ruju') of the earlier.
Example: al-Mabsut vs. al-Ziyadat → al-Ziyadat wins because it is later.
5
Practice with a scenario
Given a conflict between al-Jami' al-Saghir and al-Jami' al-Kabir, determine which ruling to follow. Answer: al-Jami' al-Kabir, because it was authored later.
Always check the order of authorship before issuing a fatwa.

Learning Objective

A mufti's authority depends on knowing which sources are reliable and how to use them.

A bibliography is not just a list—it is a map of the scholarly tradition. When you open a critical edition of 'Uqud Rasm al-Mufti, you find references to dozens of works. This lesson will help you read that map, so you can trace the chain of transmission and understand why the editor chose certain editions. Guided Path

Identify the genre of each source
Look at the title and author. Is it a hadith collection (Sahih al-Bukhari, Sahih Muslim), a fiqh matn (al-Wiqayah via its hashia), a sharh (Fath al-Qadir), a fatwa collection (al-Fatawa al-Khaniyyah), or a biographical work (al-Fawa'id al-Bahiyyah)?

Modern critical editions often correct earlier printings.
4
Map the work's role in fatwa derivation
A mufti uses these sources differently: hadith collections for textual proof, usul works for interpretive principles, fatwa collections for precedents, and biographical works to verify a narrator's reliability.
This mapping helps you quickly locate the evidence you need.
