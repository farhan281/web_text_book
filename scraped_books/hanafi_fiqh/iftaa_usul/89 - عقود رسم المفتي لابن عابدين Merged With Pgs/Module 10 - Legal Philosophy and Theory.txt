The Role of Rasm al-Mufti in Practical Fiqh
Diagnosing regression of fiqh due to neglect of Rasm al-Mufti

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to explain the three essential functions of Rasm al-Mufti as stated by Ibn Abidin, and evaluate its importance for the practical application of fiqh.

Core Idea
Neglect of Rasm al-Mufti turned fiqh theoretical; its recovery is essential for tarjih, understanding ikhtilaf, and real-world fatwa.

This passage from Ibn Abidin's Rasm al-Mufti diagnoses why fiqh has become a theoretical science rather than a practical guide. He identifies the root cause: the neglect of the discipline of fatwa methodology. The passage then states three indispensable roles of Rasm al-Mufti that every student must grasp.

Guided Path
1
Identify the problem
Read the first sentence: what does Ibn Abidin say happened to fiqh in his time? He says it has become a theoretical science in all domains of life.
Look for the phrase 'هذا التّراجِعَ الذي حَصَلَ لعلم الفقه'.
2
Recognize the cause
What is the root cause of this regression? The passage explicitly states: 'إهمال علم رسم المفتي' (neglect of the science of Rasm al-Mufti).
Note that the cause is not external but internal—neglect of a core methodology.
3
List the three functions
The passage states three things that cannot be done without Rasm al-Mufti: (1) tarjih between opinions, (2) understanding disagreement among scholars of the school, (3) applying fiqh to reality.
Memorize these three as the pillars of the science.
4
Understand the implications
Why is each function critical? Tarjih ensures authoritative fatwa; understanding ikhtilaf prevents confusion; application to reality keeps fiqh alive.
Reflect on how neglecting any one leads to theoretical fiqh.

E. Indeed, this regression that has occurred in the science of fiqh in this era, such that it has become a theoretical science in our economic, social, political, military, and judicial lives, is due to matters including the neglect of the science of Rasm al-Mufti. 6. Rasm al-Mufti should receive great attention from those engaged in fiqh, for we have no way to weigh between juristic opinions except through it, nor to understand the disagreement that has occurred among the scholars of the school except through it, nor to apply fiqh to reality without it.

Commentary
This passage is a call to revive the discipline of Rasm al-Mufti. The author observes that fiqh has become merely theoretical because the tools of fatwa—specifically the rules for preferring one opinion over another—have been ignored. He asserts three essential functions of Rasm al-Mufti: first, it provides the criteria for tarjih (weighing between conflicting opinions); second, it is necessary to understand intra-madhhab ikhtilaf; third, it is indispensable for applying fiqh to real-world situations. The passage underscores that without this science, fiqh loses its practical relevance.

Key Terms
رسم المفتي (Rasm al-Mufti): The science of the rules and conditions governing the issuance of fatwas, including the hierarchy of opinions within a madhhab and the method of tarjih.
