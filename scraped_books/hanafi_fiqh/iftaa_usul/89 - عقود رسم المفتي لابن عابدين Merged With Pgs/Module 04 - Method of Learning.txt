The Subject of Rasm al-Mufti: Principles of Fatwa
Defines the subject and principles of the poem on fatwa rules.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the definition of Rasm al-Mufti and list its seven foundational principles, explaining how they affect fatwa issuance.

Core Idea
Fatwa is not static; it adapts through necessity, custom, and time.

This lesson introduces the discipline of Rasm al-Mufti, the science of fatwa rules. It defines its subject and enumerates the principles that guide a mufti in applying fiqh to reality. Understanding these principles is essential for grasping how Hanafi fatwas evolve with context.

Guided Path
1
Define Rasm al-Mufti
Identify that Rasm al-Mufti refers to the rules of issuing fatwas, a science that studies application of fiqh in reality.
The term 'Rasm' here implies a set of guidelines or methodology.
2
List the seven foundational principles
Memorize the principles: necessity (darurah), need (hajah), removal of hardship (raf' al-haraj), facilitation (taysir), change of times (taghayyur al-zaman), custom ('urf), and public interest (maslahah).
These are the usul (fundamentals) of fatwa according to this text.
3
Connect principles to fiqh rulings
Understand that all fiqh rulings are related to these principles, meaning rulings can be adjusted based on these considerations.
This is a key feature of Hanafi fatwa methodology.
4
Recognize the impact of environment and time
Note that differences in time, place, and custom lead to differences in ijtihad, even causing a single mujtahid to change his rulings when facing a new context.
This explains the dynamic nature of Hanafi fatwa.

The Second Section: Study of the Poem

First Subsection: The Subject of the Poem 'Rasm al-Mufti':
What is meant by the rules of Rasm al-Mufti are the rules of issuing fatwas. It is a science that studies how to apply fiqh in reality. Its principles are: necessity, need, removal of hardship, facilitation, change of times, custom, and public interest. The general body of fiqh rulings are connected to these matters. Thus, they had a profound impact on the differences from one mujtahid to another, due to differences in the environments and eras of jurists. Indeed, this had a great effect on their differences in many rulings and subsidiary issues, to the extent that a single jurist would retract many of his opinions and adopt others when encountering a new environment different from the one he was in.

Commentary
This passage is part of the introduction to the study of the poem 'Rasm al-Mufti'. It defines the subject: the rules of fatwa (qawaid al-ifta'). This discipline examines how to apply fiqh to reality. Its foundational principles (usul) include: necessity (darurah), need (hajah), removal of hardship (raf' al-haraj), facilitation (taysir), change of times (taghayyur al-zaman), custom ('urf), and public interest (maslahah). All fiqh rulings are connected to these principles. The passage highlights that differences in environments and eras cause differences in ijtihad, even leading a single mujtahid to change his rulings when facing a new context. This is a key concept in the Hanafi tradition: fatwa is dynamic and context-sensitive.

Key Terms
رسم المفتي (Rasm al-Mufti): The rules and guidelines for a mufti to issue fatwas correctly.
قواعد الإفتاء (Qawaid al-Ifta'): The principles governing fatwa issuance, such as necessity, custom, and change of times.
الضرورة (al-darurah): A situation of extreme need that permits relaxation of normal rulings.
