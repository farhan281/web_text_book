The Mufti's Source Hierarchy: Zahir al-Riwaya, Nawadir, and Nawazil
Describes the compilation of Nawazil books and their relation to earlier texts

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to distinguish between Zahir al-Riwaya, Nawadir, and Nawazil, and apply the source hierarchy to evaluate the authority of fatwa collections.

Core Idea
A mufti must distinguish Zahir al-Riwaya, Nawadir, and Nawazil; mixing them leads to flawed fatwa.

This passage introduces the historical development of Nawazil literature, emphasizing the critical need for a mufti to classify fatwa sources correctly. You will learn to identify the three layers of authority and see how early and later compilations approached this task.

The Nawazil issues were derived by the scholars with proofs. Its meaning: that the books of Nawazil and Waqi'at are issues deduced by the later scholars from the companions of Muhammad (al-Shaybani) and the companions of his companions, and so on after them... And the first book compiled in Nawazil that is known is 'al-Nawazil' by Abu al-Layth al-Samarqandi (d. 375 AH). He collected in it the fatwas of the later mujtahids among his teachers and the teachers of his teachers, such as Muhammad ibn Muqatil, Muhammad ibn Salama, and Nasir ibn Yahya, and he also mentioned his own choices. Then the scholars compiled books in it, such as the collection of 'al-Nawazil' and 'al-Waqi'at' by al-Natafi and al-Sadr al-Shahid. Then later scholars compiled fatwas, but they mixed together issues of Zahir al-Riwaya, Nawadir, and Nawazil, as in 'Jami' Qadi Khan' and 'al-Khulasa', and other fatwa collections. Some distinguished between them, as in 'Muhit Radi al-Din al-Sarakhsi', for he mentioned first the issues of the principles (usul), then the Nawadir, then the fatwas. See: al-Nafi' al-Kabir p. 18-19, etc.

Commentary
This passage is a historical and methodological overview of the development of Nawazil literature. It defines Nawazil books as issues deduced by later Hanafi jurists from the students of Imam Muhammad al-Shaybani and their successors. The first known collection is by Abu al-Layth al-Samarqandi, which gathered fatwas of later mujtahids. Later compilations like Jami' Qadi Khan mixed together Zahir al-Riwaya (the core authoritative transmissions), Nawadir (rare transmissions), and Nawazil (new cases). Some authors, like Radi al-Din al-Sarakhsi in his Muhit, kept them separate to maintain clarity for the mufti. The passage underscores the importance for a mufti to know the source hierarchy.

Key Terms
النوازل (al-nawāzil): Issues or cases that arise later, deduced by later Hanafi jurists based on the principles of the madhhab.
ظاهر الرواية (zāhir al-riwāya): The well-known and authoritative transmissions from the three eponyms (Abu Hanifa, Abu Yusuf, Muhammad al-Shaybani) as compiled in the six books of Imam Muhammad.
النوادر (al-nawādir): Rare or less-known transmissions from the founding imams, often collected separately.
