Biographical sketch and historical context of Ibn Abidin

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to explain why Ibn Abidin is called the 'Seal of the Verifiers' and describe the political context in which he wrote.

Core Idea
Ibn Abidin's authority as the final verifier of Hanafi fiqh makes his works the ultimate reference for fatwa.

This lesson introduces the great Hanafi jurist Muhammad Amin Ibn Abidin, known as the 'Seal of the Verifiers.' Understanding his biography and the political turmoil of his time provides essential background for studying his monumental contribution to fatwa methodology in Rasm al-Mufti.

Guided Path

2
Understand why he earned the title
Analyze the phrase 'اجتمعت العلماء على وصفه بخاتمة المحققين' — scholars agreed he is the Seal of the Verifiers because of his thorough verification of legal issues; all later scholars depend on him.
This establishes his authority in the Hanafi madhhab.
3
Place him in historical context
Note that Ibn Abidin lived during the Ottoman Empire's decline (13th century AH). The passage mentions Sultan Selim III's attempts to revive the empire and the pressure from European powers.
This context helps understand the challenges faced by scholars of that era.

The Political Crucible of Ibn Abidin's Era
Historical preface: political state of the Ottoman Empire

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to explain how Ottoman political decline and external threats shaped the need for fatwa codification in the Hanafi tradition.

Core Idea
Ibn Abidin's fatwa code was born from Ottoman collapse — crisis demands legal clarity.

This lesson sets the historical stage for Ibn Abidin's seminal work, 'Rasm al-Mufti'. The Ottoman Empire, during his lifetime, faced military defeats, internal revolts, and European encroachment. Understanding this context is crucial for appreciating why a systematic fatwa manual became urgently needed — to preserve Hanafi legal authority amid political chaos.

Guided Path
1
Identify the historical period
Note the dates of Ibn Abidin's life (1198-1252 AH / 1784-1836 CE) and the overall condition of the Ottoman state — severe weakness and successive defeats.
This period marks the beginning of the 'Eastern Question' in European diplomacy.
2
Recognize the state's internal turmoil
Understand the brief reigns of Selim III and Mustafa IV, the Janissary revolts, and how the mufti was used as a political tool (e.g., delivering the deposition notice).
The mufti's involvement shows the intersection of state power and religious authority.
3
Connect to the need for fatwa codification
Reflect on how political instability threatened the consistency of Islamic legal practice. Ibn Abidin's 'Rasm al-Mufti' provided a clear hierarchy of sources and procedures to guide muftis in turbulent times.
Codification becomes essential when established institutions are weakened.
4
Appreciate the Hanafi response to crisis
See Ibn Abidin's work as an example of Hanafi scholarship responding to real-world pressures — preserving the school's methodology and authority for future generations.
This is not mere history; it is the backdrop for a major legal text.

Third: Praise of the Scholars for Him:
Al-Baytar said: The shaykh, the imam, the scholar, the highly learned one, the brilliant and intelligent, the pole of the Syrian lands, the support of the Levant and Egypt, the exegete, traditionist, jurist, grammarian, linguist, rhetorician, prosodist, intelligent, distinguished, Damascene in origin and birth, noble and well-born, honorable in essence and lineage....

Fourth: His Death
He died, may Allah have mercy on him, in the forenoon of Wednesday, the 21st of Rabi' al-Thani in the year (1252 AH), and his life span was approximately fifty-four years. He was buried in a cemetery in Bab al-Saghir in the upper grave, and twenty days before his death he had prepared for himself the grave in which he was buried, and that was by a will from him to be adjacent to the graves of the two great scholars: al-Shaykh al-'Ala'i, the commentator of 'al-Tanwir', and al-Shaykh Salih al-Jinini, the imam of hadith.

Commentary
This passage is a biographical section from the modern study of Ibn 'Abidin's work 'Uqud Rasm al-Mufti.' It presents praise from three notable scholars and details of his death. Al-Baytar lauds his comprehensive mastery across numerous disciplines—fiqh, hadith, tafsir, Arabic sciences—highlighting his distinguished lineage and intellect. His son, 'Ala' al-Din, provides an eloquent tribute, emphasizing his father's unparalleled status ('la yujad lahu thani'), his combination of knowledge and practice, and his role as a reference for all. Al-Shatti echoes this, calling him 'hallal al-mushkilat' (solver of problems) and the jurist of Syria. The passage then records his death on 21 Rabi' al-Thani 1252 AH at around age 54, noting his forethought in preparing his burial place to be near two other luminaries. The mention of his funeral prayer in absentia indicates his widespread renown. This biographical sketch establishes Ibn 'Abidin's authority as the leading Hanafi jurist of his time, which is essential for understanding the weight of his work 'Radd al-Muhtar' and his contributions to Hanafi ifta' methodology.

Ibn Abidin's Death and the Hanafi Ruling on Salat al-Gha'ib
Biographical account of Ibn Abidin's death and burial.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to describe the key biographical details of Ibn Abidin's death and burial and identify the Hanafi ruling on salat al-gha'ib as evidenced by his funeral.

Core Idea
A scholar's final moments reflect his devotion; Ibn Abidin prepared his own grave and received absentia prayers across lands.

Ibn Abidin's death and burial are not merely biographical footnotes—they embody Hanafi legal principles in practice. His preparation of his own grave twenty days before his death demonstrates taqwa and foresight, while the widespread performance of salat al-gha'ib for him underscores the Hanafi permissibility of this prayer. This lesson connects his personal story to the fiqh of janazah.

Guided Path
1
Identify key biographical facts
Extract the date, time, age, and location of Ibn Abidin's death and burial from the passage.
His death occurred on Wednesday, 21 Rabi' al-Thani 1252 AH, around age 54.
2
Analyze the preparation of his grave
Consider the legal and spiritual significance of a person preparing his own grave before death. Relate it to the Hanafi ruling that it is permissible and commendable (mandub) to prepare burial sites in advance.
This act reflects thoroughness and avoiding burden on others.
3
Examine the practice of salat al-gha'ib
The passage notes that the funeral prayer in absentia was performed for him in most cities. Identify the Hanafi position on salat al-gha'ib: it is permissible when the deceased is in a distant land (over 3 days' journey) and the body is not present.
Hanafis require permission from the deceased's family and that the prayer be performed within a reasonable time after death.
4
Reflect on scholarly recognition
The widespread performance of salat al-gha'ib indicates Ibn Abidin's high status and the reach of his scholarship. This practice is a form of du'a and honor for the deceased.
It shows that even after death, scholars can be a source of blessing.
