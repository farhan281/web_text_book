The Role of Taqariz (Endorsements) in Hanafi Scholarship
Endorsements from senior Hanafi scholars

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to explain the purpose and function of scholarly endorsements (taqariz) in the Hanafi tradition and evaluate their role in establishing a book's credibility.

Core Idea
Endorsements from senior scholars certify a work's reliability and guide students toward trusted sources.

The passage presents endorsements from four senior Hanafi scholars for the book 'Naseem al-Mufti' by Mufti Ridwan Nasim Qasimi. These endorsements (taqariz) are a traditional feature of Islamic scholarly publishing, serving to authenticate the work and recommend it to students and scholars. In this lesson, we will explore the function of taqariz within the Hanafi ifta tradition and understand what these particular endorsements reveal about the book's value.

Guided Path
1
Identify the endorsers and their titles
List the names and honorific titles of the four scholars who provided endorsements. Note what their titles (e.g., Faqih al-Asr) signify about their expertise.
Titles often indicate specialization: Faqih al-Asr (Jurist of the Age), Mutakallim al-Islam (Theologian of Islam), Faqih al-Nafs (Jurist of the Soul).
2
Analyze the key phrases of praise
Examine what each scholar specifically praises about the book. Is it the author's character, the style, the structure, or the content?
Distinguish between praise for the author (e.g., 'ability and righteousness') and praise for the work itself (e.g., 'easy, logical, and evidence-based').
3
Connect praise to the needs of ifta students
Consider how the highlighted features (easy style, inclusion of jurist biographies) meet the practical needs of someone training to give fatwas.
The endorsement by Mufti Salman Mansurpuri explicitly states that the biographies 'will especially facilitate students of ifta.'
4
Understand the tradition of taqariz
Place these endorsements in the broader context of Islamic scholarly tradition, where senior scholars vouch for a work before it gains acceptance.
Taqariz function as a quality-control mechanism, akin to peer review, and help students avoid unreliable texts.

Commentary
This passage contains endorsements from three prominent Hanafi scholars. The first endorsement is from Faqih al-Asr Maulana Khalid Sayfullah Rahmani, who notes that the author compiled a brief beneficial treatise after recognizing a need, and that he observed it during a trip to Patna. The second is from Mutakallim al-Islam Mufti Muhammad Rashid Azami, who praises the book for being easy, logical, and evidence-based. The third and fourth are from Faqih al-Nafs Mufti Muhammad Salman Mansurpuri and Faqih al-Nafs Mufti Muhammad Junayd Alam Nadwi Qasimi, who highlight the unique summarization and inclusion of brief biographies of Hanafi jurists. These endorsements serve to establish the book's credibility and usefulness for students of ifta.

Key Terms
تقاریظ (taqārīẓ): Endorsements or commendatory prefaces written by senior scholars to recommend a book.
فقیہ العصر (faqīh al-ʿaṣr): Jurist of the age; a title indicating the scholar is considered the leading jurist of his time.
