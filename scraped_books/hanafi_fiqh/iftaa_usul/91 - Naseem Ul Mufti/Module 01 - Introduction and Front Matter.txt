Learning Objective

Core Idea
Mastering iftaa requires knowing how to evaluate sources and prefer rulings — this book is a comprehensive guide.

This lesson introduces a unique text in Hanafi iftaa: 'Naseem Ul Mufti', a summary and commentary on the classic poem 'Uqud Rasm al-Mufti'. The title page reveals the book's comprehensive scope: translation of the poem, principles of iftaa, signs of reliable and unreliable books, and rules for preferring conflicting narrations. Understanding this scope is essential for any student of iftaa, as it outlines the core skills a mufti must acquire.

Guided Path
1
Identify the core subject
Recognize that this is a title page describing a book on iftaa methodology, not a collection of fiqh rulings.
2
List the four thematic areas covered
Identify the four areas mentioned: translation of the poem, principles and methodologies of iftaa, signs of authoritative books, and rules for preference among conflicting narrations.
These correspond to key competencies of a mufti.
3
Explain the purpose of each area
For each area, consider how it contributes to proper fatwa issuance: Translation makes the original poem accessible; principles guide the process; evaluation ensures use of reliable sources; preference rules resolve conflicts.
4
Connect to the larger Hanafi tradition
Note that the original poem is by Ibn Abidin, a major Hanafi authority, and this work is part of a larger corpus of iftaa methodology.
This book is a later compilation, part of fatawa mutakhkhirin tradition.

Naseem Ul Mufti is a contemporary commentary on Ibn 'Abidin's foundational poem on fatwa methodology.

This page introduces the book and sets the stage for our study. Naseem Ul Mufti (The Breeze of the Mufti) is a unique work that explains the principles of issuing fatwas, how to distinguish reliable and unreliable books, and the rules for preferring one opinion over another. It is a summary and commentary on the famous poem 'Uqud Rasm al-Mufti' by Ibn 'Abidin, the great Hanafi jurist. Understanding this book is essential for any serious student of ifta.

Guided Path
1
Identify the title and its meaning
The title is 'Naseem Ul Mufti fi Hill Mubahith Sharh Uqud Rasm al-Mufti'. 'Naseem' means breeze, symbolising a gentle, accessible explanation. Note that it is a commentary on the 'Sharh Uqud Rasm al-Mufti', which itself is a commentary on the poem by Ibn 'Abidin.
The title indicates the book's purpose: to elucidate (hill) the discussions (mubahith) of the commentary.
2
Note the author and his credentials
The author is Maulana Mufti Rizwan Naseem Qasimi, a teacher of Fiqh and Ifta at Ma'had al-Dirasat al-'Ulya. His title 'Mufti' indicates he is qualified to issue fatwas, and his affiliation shows academic rigour.
Knowing the author's background helps assess the reliability and authority of the work.
3
Understand the scope of the book
The description states it covers translation of the poem, principles and methods of ifta, signs of reliable vs unreliable books, and principles for preferring conflicting statements. This is exactly the core of Usul al-Iftaa.
The scope is not just commentary but a complete guide to fatwa methodology.
4
Recognize the importance of the source text
The foundation is 'Uqud Rasm al-Mufti' by Ibn 'Abidin, a mandatory text in the Hanafi tradition. This book makes that dense poem accessible.
Ibn 'Abidin is the leading authority in later Hanafi fiqh; his poem is a concise summary of ifta principles.
5
Appreciate the editions and publication
The book is in its second edition (1443 AH / 2022), indicating demand and updates. Published by Maktaba Dar Arqam in Nepal.
The publication details confirm the work is contemporary and in circulation.

Overview of Principles of Ifta in Naseem Ul Mufti
Table of contents outlining tarjih principles

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to outline the three sets of tarjih principles in the Hanafi fatwa methodology as presented in the table of contents of Naseem Ul Mufti.

Core Idea
A mufti must systematically prefer opinions using a hierarchy based on person, book, and evidence.

This lesson explores the comprehensive table of contents of Naseem Ul Mufti, a manual on Hanafi fatwa methodology. The TOC serves as a roadmap, outlining three categories of tarjih (preference) principles: personality-based, book-based, and evidence-based. Understanding this structure is essential for any mufti to navigate conflicts and issue sound fatwas.

Guided Path
1
Identify the three main categories of tarjih
Examine the TOC headings to find the three sets: 13 principles based on personality of transmitters, 6 principles based on type of book, and 5 principles based on strength of evidence.
These are explicitly listed under the second chapter.
2
List the number of principles in each category
Count and memorize the numbers: 13 personality, 6 book, 5 evidence.
Confirm by scanning the Arabic text for the phrase '13 اصول', '6 اصول', '5 اصول'.
3
Note specific assignments for personality-based principles
Read principles 10-13: in worship Abu Hanifa is mufta bihi, in judiciary Abu Yusuf, in inheritance Muhammad, and in 17 issues Imam Zufar.
These assignments are crucial for fatwa.
4
Understand the book-based hierarchy
Principles: Zahir al-Riwaya is obligatory; in Zahir al-Riwaya, later works take precedence; matn over sharh, sharh over fatawa; fiqh books over usul books.
This determines which textual source to rely on.
5
Understand the evidence-based hierarchy
Principles: a statement based on famous hadith is preferred; if a mujtahid's opinion contradicts hadith, it is weakened; istihsan over qiyas; explicit adherence over correction; follow style of authors.
These are applied after personality and book principles.

Commentary
This passage is the table of contents of Naseem Ul Mufti, a comprehensive manual on the principles of ifta and the methodology of fatwa in the Hanafi school. It provides an outline of the book's structure: the first chapter introduces the famous poem 'Uqud Rasm al-Mufti' by Ibn Kamal Pasha, which succinctly sets down the rules for a mufti, along with its commentaries and translations. The second chapter and core of the book details the principles and methods of issuing fatwas, crucially including three sets of tarjih (preference) principles: 13 based on the personality of the transmitter, 6 based on the type of book, and 5 based on the strength of evidence. This structure shows the Hanafi emphasis on a systematic hierarchy of sources: the statements of the founding imams (Abu Hanifa, Abu Yusuf, Muhammad, and Zufar) are weighted; within their madhhab, Zahir al-Riwaya (the well-known transmissions) is given precedence; and when evidence conflicts, hadith and istihsan (juristic preference) are prioritized. The table of contents thus serves as a roadmap for the mufti, illustrating how to navigate the vast corpus of Hanafi fiqh to arrive at a sound fatwa.

Key Terms
ظاہر الروایہ (Zāhir al-Riwāyah): The well-known and authoritative narrations within the Hanafi school, primarily transmitted by Imam Muhammad from Abu Hanifa and Abu Yusuf.
مفتی بہ (Muftā bihī): The preferred statement upon which a fatwa is issued, as determined by the rules of tarjih.
رسِم المفتى (Rasm al-Muftī): The established rules and conventions that a mufti must follow in issuing fatwas.

The Principles of Ifta: An Overview (Usool 6–13)
Table of contents listing eight principles of ifta

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the eight principles of ifta from the Hanafi tradition and state their main focus.

Core Idea
The mufti must master the hierarchy of jurists, texts, and methods to issue sound fatwas.

This lesson introduces the core principles that govern the practice of ifta in the Hanafi school, as outlined in Naseem Ul Mufti. These principles form the backbone of a mufti's training, covering everything from the levels of jurists to the use of custom. Use this as a roadmap for the detailed discussions in the following lessons.

Guided Path
1
List the eight principles
Identify principles 6 through 13 from the passage. Each principle has a specific focus.
Principles 1–5 are covered earlier in the book.
2
Categorize by theme
Group the principles into three themes: (a) qualification of the mufti and classification of issues, (b) sources and reasoning methods, (c) external considerations like custom.
Principles 6–8 deal with hierarchy and authority; 10–12 with textual interpretation; 13 with custom.
3
Memorize the classes and categories
Note the seven classes of jurists (Ibn Kamal Pasha) and the three tiers of Hanafi issues (Zahir al-Riwayah, Nawadir, Fatawa/Takhrijat).
This hierarchy is essential for determining the weight of a transmitted opinion.
4
Recognize key debates
The ninth principle questions the inclusion of the Sahibayn's statements; the twelfth discusses mafhum al-mukhalafah. Be aware that these topics involve scholarly differences.
These will be elaborated in later lessons.
5
Apply the roadmap
Understand that these principles structure the entire work. When studying a specific fatwa, you will refer back to these principles.
This lesson serves as an orientation.

Commentary
This passage is the table of contents from Naseem Ul Mufti, specifically from Sharh Uqud Rasm al-Mufti. It lists the principles (usool) that govern the process of issuing fatwas in the Hanafi school. The sixth principle introduces seven classes of jurists according to Ibn Kamal Pasha, ranging from the absolute mujtahid (mujtahid mutlaq) down to the pure muqallid. The seventh principle categorises Hanafi transmitted issues (masail) into three levels: Zahir al-Riwayah (the core transmitted rulings from Imam Muhammad's six books), Nawadir (rare transmissions), and later Fatawa and Takhrijat of the scholars. The eighth and ninth principles address whose opinions to rely on—whether the students of Abu Hanifa or the Sahibayn (Abu Yusuf and Muhammad). The tenth principle stresses the mufti's need to know the Imam's own evidences (dalail). The eleventh deals with answering issues not explicitly found in texts. The twelfth principle is a detailed section on mafhum al-mukhalafah (inferred opposite meaning) and the textual indications: ibarah, isharah, dalalah, and iqtida'. The thirteenth principle discusses consideration of custom (urf) in fatwa. Each of these topics is given page numbers in the original text. As a front-matter list, this passage serves as an orientation for the student to the structure of the work.

Key Terms
ظاہر الروایہ (Zahir al-Riwayah): The category of Hanafi issues transmitted through reliable chains in the six core books of Imam Muhammad (al-Mabsut, al-Jami al-Saghir, al-Jami al-Kabir, al-Siyar al-Saghir, al-Siyar al-Kabir, al-Ziyadat). These are the most authoritative and form the basis of fatwa.
نوادر (Nawadir): Rare or less widely transmitted rulings, often from Hasan ibn Ziyad or other early Hanafis, that are not part of Zahir al-Riwayah.
فتاوی واقعات تخریجات مشائخ (Fatawa Waqi'at Takhrijat Mashayikh): Later fatwa collections and legal rulings derived (takhrij) by later Hanafi scholars based on the principles of the school.
مجتهد مطلق (Mujtahid Mutlaq): An absolute mujtahid who can derive rulings directly from the Quran and Sunnah without being bound to a particular madhhab.
مفہوم مخالف (Mafhum Mukhalafah): The inferred opposite meaning from a text. E.g., if the Prophet said 'In zakatable animals, zakat is due on grazing livestock,' the mafhum mukhalafah is that non-grazing livestock are not subject to zakat.
عرف ('Urf): Custom or common practice that is accepted as a source for legal rulings when no textual evidence exists.

Introduction to Naseem Ul Mufti and the Importance of Ifta
Front matter: dedications and preface explaining book's purpose

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the purpose and significance of 'Naseem Ul Mufti' and explain its relationship to 'Rasm al-Mufti' in the Hanafi ifta tradition.

Core Idea
'Naseem Ul Mufti' unpacks 'Rasm al-Mufti', the essential Hanafi guide on ifta principles.

This lesson orients you to the book 'Naseem Ul Mufti' and its place in the Hanafi ifta tradition. The preface, written by the renowned jurist Maulana Khalid Saifullah Rahmani, highlights the critical nature of issuing fatwas and the need for specialized training. It introduces 'Rasm al-Mufti' by Allamah Shami as the foundational text, which this book aims to explain. Understanding this context is essential before diving into the technical principles of ifta.

Guided Path
1
Identify the book's full title and base text
The book is 'Naseem Ul Mufti fi Halli 'Uqud Rasm al-Mufti', meaning 'The Breeze of the Mufti in Explaining the Knots of Rasm al-Mufti'. The base text is 'Rasm al-Mufti' by Allamah Shami (Muhammad Amin Ibn Abidin), a classical work on the principles and ethics of fatwa-giving.
Rasm al-Mufti is a standard textbook in Hanafi ifta training in South Asia.
2
Understand the significance of ifta
The preface emphasizes that issuing fatwas is a delicate responsibility because the mufti acts as a representative of Allah and His Messenger, guiding on halal and haram. Therefore, scholars have always been extremely cautious and have formulated detailed principles.
This highlights why studying ifta principles is a specialized field, not for public consumption.
3
Recognize the scholars mentioned
The author dedicates the book to his parents, grandmother, and three prominent scholars: Maulana Khalid Saifullah Rahmani (a jurist and leader of Islamic Fiqh Academy India), Mufti Muhammad Salman Mansurpuri, and Mufti Muhammad Junayd Alam Nadwi Qasimi. Their influence shaped the author's methodology and motivation.
These dedications show the chain of learning and mentorship in the Hanafi tradition.
4
Grasp the purpose of the book
The preface explains that while many Islamic sciences have accessible Urdu works, the principles of ifta remain specialized and are only for those training in or practicing ifta. 'Naseem Ul Mufti' was compiled to fill this gap, providing a concise explanation of 'Rasm al-Mufti' for muftis and students.
The book is intended as a practical guide, not a general reference.

Jurist of the era, His Eminence Maulana Khalid Saifullah Sahib Rahmani, member of the Muslim World League, Makkah Mukarramah, General Secretary of the Islamic Fiqh Academy of India, General Secretary of the Muslim Personal Law Board, founder of Al-Ma'had al-'Ali al-Islami Hyderabad.
Ifta is an extremely delicate and important responsibility, because the mufti guides people from Allah regarding halal and haram, permissible and impermissible, and in guiding Islamic rulings, his status is that of a representative of Allah and His Messenger. Therefore, scholars have always exercised utmost caution in performing this duty and have tried to avoid this service as much as possible. For this reason, scholars have compiled principles of ifta and authored independent books on this subject. One of the most important books in this series is Allama Shami's "Rasm al-Mufti", in which he first organized the principles of ifta and then himself wrote a commentary. The Role of Taqriz in Authenticating a Hanafi Ifta Text
Two scholarly endorsements praising the book

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the function of scholarly endorsements (taqriz) in the Hanafi tradition and state the key topics covered in a foundational usul al-ifta text.

Core Idea
Taqriz by senior scholars authenticates a work and establishes its reliability for teaching and fatwa.

Before using a fiqh book, a mufti must ascertain its authority. Endorsements (taqriz) from recognized scholars serve as a guarantee of quality and adherence to the madhhab. This lesson examines two such endorsements for 'Sharh Uqud Rasm al-Mufti', a key text for Hanafi ifta training, and its commentary 'Naseem al-Mufti'.

Guided Path
1
Identify the purpose of taqriz
What does an endorsement communicate about the book and its author? Look for praise of the author's qualifications and the book's utility.
Endorsements often appear at the beginning of a book to recommend it to readers and students.
2
Summarize the content of the original text as described
Mufti Muhammad Rashid Azami lists topics covered: authoritative books, preferences among them, and the duties and etiquettes of a mufti. Note the breadth of usul al-ifta.
The original text is known for its comprehensiveness and brevity.
3
Recognize the challenge for beginners
The endorser states that access to the original's discussions is difficult for first-time students. Understand why a commentary is needed.
The commentary by Mufti Rizwan Naseem Qasmi provides easy, logical, and reasoned explanations.
4
Note the qualifications of the endorsers
Both endorsers are senior scholars from Darul Uloom Deoband. Their positions (e.g., Deputy Director, Teacher of Tafsir) give weight to their praise.
Endorser authority matters: a mufti mu'tamad relies on such recommendations.

The Role of Taqriz in Hanafi Ifta Literature
Taqriz (endorsement) for a Hanafi ifta work.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to explain the function of a taqriz in the Hanafi ifta tradition and identify its role in establishing a book’s authority.

Core Idea
A taqriz certifies a book's reliability, guiding students to authoritative sources in Hanafi ifta.

When a major Hanafi institution like Dar al-Ulum Deoband endorses a commentary on a classical ifta text, it sends a clear signal to students and scholars: this work is trustworthy. But an endorsement is not a blank check—it must be understood within the hierarchy of Hanafi authority. In this lesson, we examine a taqriz (endorsement) by Mufti Muhammad Rashid Azami for 'Sharah Uqud Rasm al-Mufti', and explore what such endorsements mean for the student of ifta.

Guided Path
1
Identify the endorser and his credentials
Who wrote the taqriz? What is his position? (Mufti Muhammad Rashid Azami, deputy rector and teacher at Dar al-Ulum Deoband). Note that his authority adds weight.
The endorser's seniority matters—Deoband is a hub of Hanafi ifta.
2
Understand what the endorser praises
The taqriz highlights that the commentary makes the difficult discussions of Uqud Rasm al-Mufti accessible. It also mentions the book covers reliable sources, preferences among books, and the adab of ifta.
The endorser focuses on the commentary's pedagogical value, not necessarily its rulings.
3
Recognize the role of the taqriz in scholarly tradition
A taqriz is a certification of quality. It helps the student trust the commentary and invest time in it. However, it does not make the commentary’s rulings automatically mufta bihi.
Taqriz are not fatwas; they are recommendations for study.
4
Apply the lesson to your own study
When you encounter a commentary with a taqriz from a recognized scholar, you can be confident it is a reliable guide. But you must still verify its positions against mainstream Hanafi fatwa.
Always cross-check with later authorities (fatawa mutakhkhirin).

Commentary
This passage is the author's introduction to his book 'Naseem Ul Mufti', a commentary on Ibn Abidin's 'Sharh Uqud Rasm al-Mufti'. The author begins by highlighting the immense significance and honor of the office of ifta (fatwa-giving). He proves this by citing verses from the Quran where Allah Himself attributes the act of giving fatwas to His own being, thereby elevating the station of the mufti. He then traces the historical transmission of this office: from Allah to the Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him), then to the Companions, the Followers, the four Imams, and the righteous scholars of the ummah. The author emphasizes the delicate nature of this responsibility: a mufti acts as an intermediary between Allah and people and represents the Prophet. Therefore, issuing fatwas requires extreme caution and avoidance of reckless haste. This concern prompted earlier scholars to write works in Arabic and Urdu codifying the principles of ifta. Among them, Ibn Abidin's 'Sharh Uqud Rasm al-Mufti' is the most comprehensive, but its style—relying heavily on citations rather than independent exposition—can be challenging for students. To address this difficulty, the author undertook to simplify and summarize those discussions in easy Urdu, adding missing topics. The introduction concludes with acknowledgments and gratitude to Allah, parents, and various scholars who supported him. This front matter sets the stage for the technical content that follows, establishing the gravity and scholarly lineage of the discipline.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to explain why Naseem Ul Mufti was written and identify the key features of Ibn Abidin's style that necessitated simplification.

Core Idea
Ibn Abidin's dense citation style makes his ifta principles hard; Naseem Ul Mufti makes them accessible.

In this lesson, we explore the rationale behind Naseem Ul Mufti. The author identifies a pedagogical gap: Ibn Abidin's Sharh Uqud Rasm al-Mufti, while comprehensive, is written in a style that assumes prior knowledge and relies heavily on citations. To bridge this gap, the author produced a simplified Urdu summary. Understanding this context helps us appreciate the work's place in the Hanafi tradition of usul al-ifta.

Guided Path
1
Identify the core text

Sharh Uqud Rasm al-Mufti is itself a commentary on another treatise, but the focus here is Ibn Abidin's commentary.
2
Analyze Ibn Abidin's style
Understand that Ibn Abidin's style is citation-heavy: he rarely states positions directly but quotes other jurists. This style can be challenging for beginners who need clear, direct statements of rulings and principles.
This is a common characteristic of many later Hanafi works, requiring a teacher's guidance.
3
Understand the author's response
The author simplified the content into easy Urdu, reorganized it, and added explanations for topics that were missing or unclear in the original. This makes the material more accessible to students.
The author also removed some topics to include in a separate work, Aasan Usul Ifta.
4
Recognize the significance
Accessible texts like Naseem Ul Mufti are crucial for training Hanafi muftis because they preserve the integrity of the original principles while lowering the entry barrier for learners.

Opening Verses of Uqud Rasm al-Mufti: Basmalah, Hamd, and Salawat
Presentation and translation of the first three verses

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to recite and explain the first three verses of Uqud Rasm al-Mufti, understanding the adab of beginning a fatwa with the basmalah, hamd, and salawat.

Core Idea
The mufti begins every fatwa with the name of Allah, praise, and blessings upon the Prophet, following the sunnah and established adab.

In this lesson, we examine the opening three verses of Allama Shami's poem. These verses establish the proper etiquette (adab) for a mufti: beginning with the name of Allah, praising Him as the Enactor of Shariah, and sending blessings on the Prophet (saw). Understanding this opening is essential because it reflects the mufti's humility and reliance on divine guidance before issuing any legal ruling.

Guided Path
1
Identify the opening formula
Read the first verse: 'Bismillah Shar'ih al-Ahkam'. Note that the mufti begins with the name of Allah, specifically mentioning His role as the legislator (shari').
The phrase 'shari' al-ahkam' emphasizes that Allah is the source of all rulings.
2
Explain the legal significance of starting with bismillah
In Hanafi adab, every important act begins with bismillah. For a mufti, this acknowledges that the fatwa is a sacred trust from Allah.
This is consistent with the general sunnah of beginning with bismillah in all matters of significance.
3
Analyze the hamd (praise)
The second line: 'ma'a hamdihi abda'u fi nizami'. The mufti praises Allah before presenting his poem. This humbles the mufti and attributes all knowledge to Allah.
Hamd includes gratitude and acknowledgment of dependence on Allah.
4
Recognize the salawat (blessings on the Prophet)
The second verse: 'thumma al-salat wa al-salam sarmada 'ala nabiyyin...' The mufti sends eternal blessings on Prophet Muhammad, his family, and companions. This reflects the mufti's connection to the Prophetic tradition.
The poet specifies 'sarmada' (eternal) indicating that the salawat is continuous.
5
Connect to the mufti's adab
These opening verses set the tone: a mufti must start with tawhid, praise, and salawat. This adab is taught in Hanafi manuals and is practiced by muftis when writing or delivering fatwas.
The poem itself is a model of the adab it teaches.

The Role of Scholarly Endorsements in Preserving Hanafi Ifta Methodology
Front matter: scholarly endorsements for a book on Hanafi ifta methodology

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the purpose of scholarly endorsements (taqāriẓ) and explain how 'Naseem Ul Mufti' fits into the Hanafi usul al-ifta tradition.

Core Idea
Scholarly endorsements verify a work’s reliability and connect it to the living tradition of Hanafi ifta.

This passage presents endorsements from four Hanafi scholars for the book 'Naseem Ul Mufti'. These taqāriẓ are not mere praise; they serve as a certification that the book is a reliable and beneficial addition to the Hanafi ifta tradition. They also demonstrate the chain of scholarly validation—an essential feature of Islamic knowledge transmission.

Guided Path
1
Understand the function of taqriz
Identify that a taqriz is a formal commendation by a scholar that vouches for the book's quality and adherence to the madhhab, helping readers assess its reliability.
2
Analyze the scholars' claims
Examine what each scholar praises: conciseness, comprehensiveness, inclusion of jurist biographies, and utility for students of ifta.
3
Connect to usul al-ifta
Recognize that 'Naseem Ul Mufti' is based on 'Sharh Uqud Rasm al-Mufti', a core Hanafi text on fatwa methodology, making it a practical guide for aspiring muftis.
