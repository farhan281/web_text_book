Two Methods for Zawil Arham: Abu Yusuf vs Muhammad
Comparing two Hanafi methodologies for Zawil Arham distribution

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to compare the methodologies of Abu Yusuf and Muhammad in applying the qawida 'للذكر مثل حظ الأنثيين' to Zawil Arham and identify the thamarah al-khilaf in a given case.

Core Idea
Abu Yusuf applies the 2:1 rule to living heirs; Muhammad applies it to intermediate ancestors first, then passes shares — opposite distributions.

How do we distribute inheritance among distant relatives (Zawil Arham) when the only rule we have is 'the male takes twice the female'? Two great Hanafi imams, Abu Yusuf and Muhammad al-Shaybani, disagreed on the method. One applies the rule directly to the heirs present; the other first applies it to the intermediate ancestors linking the deceased to the heirs. This difference can lead to opposite results, as shown in the example from the text. This lesson will walk you through the dispute and its practical consequences.

Guided Path
1
Identify the Heirs and Roots
List the surviving heirs (Hajira, female via Zaid; Abdullah, male via Umm Kulthum) and their intermediate roots (Zaid, male; Umm Kulthum, female). Note the gender of each heir and each root.
Zaid and Umm Kulthum are already deceased; Hajira and Abdullah are alive.
2
Abu Yusuf's Direct Application
Apply 'للذكر مثل حظ الأنثيين' directly to Hajira (female) and Abdullah (male). Since Abdullah is male, he gets two shares; Hajira gets one share.
No consideration of the roots' genders.
3
Muhammad's Root-First Application
First apply the rule to the roots Zaid (male) and Umm Kulthum (female): Zaid gets two shares, Umm Kulthum one share. Then pass Zaid's two shares to his daughter Hajira, and Umm Kulthum's one share to her son Abdullah.
Hajira receives two shares, Abdullah one — opposite to Abu Yusuf's method.
4
Compare the Thamarah al-Khilaf
In this case, the practical difference is that Hajira (female) gets double under Muhammad, while Abdullah (male) gets double under Abu Yusuf. Identify which method leads to which distribution.
The thamarah al-khilaf is the actual shares received.

Umm Kulthum had passed away. At the time of Ayesha's death, Zaid's daughter Hajira (Ayesha's great-granddaughter) and Umm Kulthum's son Abdullah (Ayesha's great-grandson) are alive. There is a difference in their [intermediate] roots: one root has Ayesha's grandson (Zaid) as male, the other root has Ayesha's granddaughter (Umm Kulthum) as female. Imam Abu Yusuf, in order to give according to the rule 'for the male is the share of two females', without looking at the masculinity and femininity of the roots (i.e., Zaid and Umm Kulthum), considers the masculinity and femininity of the grandson's daughter (Hajira) and the granddaughter's son (Abdullah), giving the grandson's daughter (Hajira) one share and the granddaughter's son (Abdullah) two shares. Whereas Imam Muhammad, in order to give 'the share of two females' according to the rule 'for the male is the share of two females', first considers the masculinity and femininity in the roots and divides the inheritance upon their roots according to the rule 'for the male is the share of two females', then gives each root's share to their descendants. Therefore, according to Imam Muhammad's statement in this case, he first divides the estate among the roots of the present heirs, i.e., grandson (Zaid) and granddaughter (Umm Kulthum), and gives the grandson two shares and the granddaughter one share according to the rule 'for the male is the share of two females', then gives the grandson's two shares to his daughter (Hajira) and the granddaughter's one share to her son (Abdullah). Summary: Hadrat Imam Abu Yusuf, in the matter of distributing inheritance among Zawil Arham, holds to dividing according to the rule 'for the male is the share of two females' by considering the masculinity and femininity of the present individuals. Whereas Imam Muhammad first divides inheritance there according to the rule 'for the male is the share of two females' by considering the masculinity and femininity in the roots of the present individuals, then gives the share of their roots to the present Zawil Arham individuals, regardless of the rule 'for the male is the share of two females'. This is why in the present example, according to Imam Muhammad, Hajira (Ayesha's great-granddaughter), despite being female, receives two shares, and Abdullah (Ayesha's great-grandson), despite being male, receives one share.

Commentary
This passage presents an internal Hanafi dispute between Imam Abu Yusuf and Imam Muhammad regarding the application of the inheritance rule 'للذكر مثل حظ الأنثيين' (the male receives the share of two females) to distant relatives (Zawil Arham). The example involves Ayesha's great-grandchildren: Hajira (female, via Zaid) and Abdullah (male, via Umm Kulthum). The difference lies in the methodology: Imam Abu Yusuf applies the rule directly to the existing heirs (Hajira and Abdullah) based on their gender, giving Abdullah double Hajira's share. Imam Muhammad, however, first applies the rule to the intermediate ancestors (the link between Ayesha and the heirs: Zaid and Umm Kulthum), dividing the estate between them according to the rule (Zaid gets two shares, Umm Kulthum one share). Then he passes these shares to their descendants, so Hajira (Zaid's heir) gets two shares, and Abdullah (Umm Kulthum's heir) gets one share. The 'خلاصہ کلام' summarizes: Abu Yusuf looks at the present individuals' gender; Muhammad looks at the gender of the intermediate roots. The passage does not indicate a preferred position, implying both are valid within the school.

Key Terms
ذوی الارحام (Zawil Arham): Distant relatives who are not Quranic or agnatic heirs, inheriting through uterine or female links
لِلذَّكَرِ مِثْلُ حَظِّ الانْثَيَيْنِ (li al-dhakari mithlu hazzi al-unthayayn): The male inherits twice the share of a female; a general principle in inheritance distribution
تذکیر و تانیث (tadhkeer wa taneeth): Masculinity and femininity of the heir or intermediate root
