Definition of Bayan al-Darurah
Defining Bayan al-Darurah with example from inheritance verse

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to define Bayan al-Darurah, identify its characteristics, and derive a ruling from a Quranic text using this concept.

Core Idea
Bayan al-Darurah: an implicit clarification made binding by necessity.

In Hanafi usul, not all clarifications come through explicit speech. Sometimes the understanding is forced upon us by the very structure of the wording. This lesson introduces Bayan al-Darurah, a type of bayan where the intended ruling is understood by necessity from the context. We will explore its definition, its first aspect, and a powerful example from the inheritance verse.

Guided Path
1
Understand the concept
Identify that Bayan al-Darurah is a clarification arising by necessity from the wording itself, without the speaker explicitly stating the intended meaning.
It is a type of dalalat al-lafz (indication of the wording).
2
Examine the Quranic example
Look at the verse 'وَوَرَثَهُ أَبَوَاهُ فلامه الثلث' and see how specifying the mother's share necessarily implies the father's share.
Attribution to both parents triggers the implication.
3
Distinguish from silence
Note that the father's share is not derived from silence (sukut) but from the contextual indication (dalalat sadr al-kalam).
Silence alone would not be sufficient; the wording forces the conclusion.
4
Relate to terminology
Understand that this implication is considered 'fi hukm al-mantuq' (in the ruling of the uttered) and is part of dalalat al-lafz, not qiyas.
It is treated as binding as if the father's share were stated explicitly.
5
Apply the concept
Practice identifying similar necessary implications in other texts, such as prohibitions of greater acts inferred from lesser ones.
This sharpens your ability to derive rulings from the Qur'an and Sunnah.

تعريف بيان الضرورة
بيان الضرورة هو بيان حاصل بطريق الضرورة، وهو على ثلاثة أوجه:
(۱) ما يكون في حكم المنطوق، كقوله تعالى: ﴿وَوَرَثَهُ أَبَوَاهُ فلامه الثلث﴾ صدر الكلام أوجب الشركة؛ لأن الإرث أضيف إليهما، ثم خص الأم بالثلث، فكان ذلك بياناً أن للأب ما بقي وهذا البيان لم يحصل بمحض السكوت عن نصيب الأب، بل بدلالة صدر الكلام فيصير نصيب الأب كالمنطوق.
بطريق الضرورة: أي هو نوع بيان يقع بما لم يوضع له المنطوق خلاف المفهوم، وهو مجرد دلالة اللفظ، دون نظر إلى ما يستنبط منه.
Definition of Bayan al-Darurah: Bayan al-Darurah is a clarification obtained by way of necessity, and it has three aspects: (1) What is in the ruling of the uttered, like His saying the Exalted: 'And if he is inherited by his parents, then for his mother a third.' The beginning of the utterance necessitated partnership because inheritance was attributed to both, then the mother was specified with a third, so that was a clarification that the father gets what remains, and this clarification did not occur merely by silence about the father's share, but by the indication of the beginning of the utterance, so the father's share becomes like the uttered. 'By way of necessity': i.e., it is a type of bayan that occurs by means not placed for the utterance, contrary to the understood (mafhum), and it is mere indication of the word, without consideration of what is derived from it.

Commentary
This passage defines the third type of Bayan: Bayan al-Darurah, or clarification by necessity. This type of bayan does not involve an explicit statement from the speaker; rather, the meaning becomes clear through the necessary implication of the words used. The author states it has three aspects, but only the first is detailed here: what is in the ruling of the uttered (fi hukm al-mantuq). The example is from the inheritance verse (An-Nisa 4:11): when the Quran says that if the deceased's heirs are parents, the mother gets a third, it necessarily implies that the father gets the remaining two-thirds. This is not explicitly stated, but it is understood by necessity from the structure of the verse: the inheritance is attributed to both parents, then the mother's share is specified, so the father's share is what remains. The clarification is not from mere silence (sukut) but from the contextual indication (dalalat sadr al-kalam). The passage also explains that this type of bayan operates on the level of the literal indication (dalalat al-lafz) without needing further derivation. This lesson is important for understanding implicit meanings that are legally binding due to contextual necessity.

Key Terms
بيان الضرورة (bayan al-darurah): A type of bayan where the meaning is understood by necessity from the wording, without the speaker explicitly stating it, and it is considered like an explicit statement in effect.
في حكم المنطوق (fi hukm al-mantuq): In the ruling of the uttered; meaning that the implied understanding is given the same legal status as if it were explicitly stated.
دلالة اللفظ (dalalat al-lafz): The indication of the word itself, as opposed to derived or analogical reasoning.
