Decoding a Hanafi Colophon: Nur al-Anwar
Colophon of the commentary Nur al-Anwar

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the key features of a Hanafi colophon and explain their importance for textual transmission.

Core Idea
A colophon is not a footnote—it traces the scholarly chain and locates the text in time and place.

When you open a classical Hanafi text, the final page often contains a colophon (khatima) written by the author or translator. These paragraphs seem like mere formalities, but they are crucial for understanding the text's authority, chain of transmission, and historical context. In this lesson, we will examine the colophon from Nur al-Anwar, a famous commentary on al-Manar in Hanafi usul al-fiqh, and learn how to extract meaningful information from such passages.

Guided Path
1
Identify the author and his credentials
Look for the author's name, lineage, and nisbahs (geographic or school affiliations). Here, the commentator is Shaykh Ahmad, known as Jiyun ibn Abu Sa'd, with affiliations Hanafi, Makki, Salihi, Indian, Lucknowi.
The title 'Shaykh' indicates scholarly recognition.
2
Determine the work and its relation
Nur al-Anwar is a commentary (sharh) on al-Manar, a foundational Hanafi usul text. Recognize that commentaries often serve as teaching tools that expand and clarify the matn.
Knowing the base text helps assess the commentary's scope.
3
Extract the date and place of completion
The author completed the work in Jumada al-Ula 1100 AH (originally written as 100, a common scribal error) in Medina. Place and date indicate the scholarly environment and potential influences.
Always verify dates using the translator's or publisher's colophon if available.
4
Note the author's age and duration
The author was 58 years old and took only three months to write. This reflects his mastery and dedication. Such details humanize the scholar and emphasize the work's labor.
Speed does not diminish value; it may indicate prior preparation.
5
Observe the prayer and translator's note
The author prays for acceptance and benefit. The translator's note provides a modern chain: Muhammad Hasan Bandawi, teacher at Dar al-'Ulum Deoband, completed the Urdu translation in 1983 CE/1403 AH.
Translator’s colophons show the living tradition of Hanafi scholarship.

Commentary
This passage is a colophon (khatima) of the commentator of Nur al-Anwar, Shaykh Ahmad. He states that he completed writing this commentary in Medina, in the sacred precincts, in the month of Jumada al-Ula of the year 1100 AH (the text says 100, likely a scribal error). He was 58 years old at completion, and the entire commentary took only three months to write. He prays for acceptance and benefit. The second part is a note from the Urdu translator, Muhammad Hasan Bandawi (a teacher at Dar al-'Ulum Deoband), who completed the translation in 1983 CE/1403 AH. This biographical information helps situate the work within the Hanafi scholarly tradition, showing it is a commentary on al-Manar (a famous Hanafi usul text) and that it was composed in Medina, reflecting the author's connection to the sacred city.

Key Terms
نور الانوار (Nur al-Anwar): The title of the commentary written by Shaykh Ahmad, which is a commentary on al-Manar.
المنار (al-Manar): A foundational text in Hanafi usul al-fiqh written by Abu al-Barakat al-Nasafi.
خاتمة (khatimah): Colophon; the concluding section of a book where the author provides information about the completion of the work.
