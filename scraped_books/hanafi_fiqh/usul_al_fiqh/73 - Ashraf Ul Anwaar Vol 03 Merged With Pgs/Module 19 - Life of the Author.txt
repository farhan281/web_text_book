The Colophon of Nur al-Anwar: Author, Date, and Scholarly Context
Author's statement of completion, date, and prayer

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the author of Nur al-Anwar, state the date and place of its completion from the colophon, and explain the significance of colophons in the Hanafi manuscript tradition.

Core Idea
A colophon tells us more than dates—it reveals the author’s humility, his hope for acceptance, and the sacred context of composition.

This lesson focuses on the colophon of Nur al-Anwar, a famous Hanafi usul commentary. While it does not contain legal rulings, it provides essential biographical information about the author, Shaykh Ahmad al-Makki al-Hanafi, and the circumstances under which he wrote. Understanding the colophon helps us situate the text in the Hanafi scholarly tradition and appreciate the personal dimensions of scholarly work.

Guided Path
1
1. Identify the author
Read the author's full name and epithets in the Arabic text. Note his nisbas: al-Hanafi, al-Makki, al-Salihi, al-Hindi, al-Lakhnawi. This shows his geographical and madhhab affiliations.
The name includes 'al-Hanafi' indicating the madhhab.
2
2. Locate the date of completion
Find the date: 7 Jumada al-Awwal, year (likely 1100s AH). Compare with the starting date: beginning of Rabi' al-Awwal of the same year. This indicates a composition period of about three months.
OCR uncertainty: the year may be 1100 AH or 1105?
3
3. Identify the place
The colophon states it was completed in the noble sanctuary of Medina (al-Haram al-Sharif in al-Madinah al-Munawwarah).
This highlights the sacred setting.
4
4. Understand the author's prayer
The author prays for the book to be accepted and to benefit beginners and Muslims. This reflects the traditional humility of Hanafi scholars.
Common in colophons.
5
5. Appreciate the translator's note
The Urdu translator, Muhammad Hasan Bandawi, completed the translation in 1983. This shows the continued use of Nur al-Anwar in Deoband circles.
Darul Uloom Deoband is a major Hanafi institution.

Commentary
This is the colophon of the author, Sheikh Ahmad, known as Sheikh Jiyun. He states that he completed his commentary 'Nur al-Anwar' on the famous usul text 'al-Manar' on the 7th of Jumada al-Awwal of the year (likely 1100s AH, though the OCR is unclear) in the Prophet's Mosque in Medina. He started writing at the beginning of Rabi' al-Awwal of the same year, at the age of 58. He prays for Allah's acceptance and that the book benefit beginners and Muslims. The colophon then includes an Urdu translation of this note, and finally a note from the translator, Muhammad Hasan Bandawi, teacher at Darul Uloom Deoband, who completed the Urdu translation on March 31, 1983. This colophon provides biographical information about the author and the context of the work's composition.

The colophon of Nur al-Anwar confirms that it was authored by Shaykh Ahmad al-Makki al-Hanafi in Medina in about three months in the 1100s AH, and it includes a prayer for the book to benefit students of Hanafi usul.
