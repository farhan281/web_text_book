Conditions for Valid Ijazat in Hadith Transmission
Rule on validity of ijazat for sanad muttasil

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to explain the condition required for ijazat to be valid for connected narration and apply it to scenarios.

Core Idea
Ijazat alone is insufficient for Sanad Muttasil; it requires actual study and teaching ability.

In the Hanafi tradition, the transmission of hadith via permission (ijazah) is valued but not automatically valid for a connected chain. The recipient must personally engage with the material through study and be able to teach it. This lesson examines that condition and its implications.

Guided Path
1
Identify the Rule
What does the passage state about ijazah and its validity for connected narration?
2
Determine the Condition
Identify the specific requirement: the recipient must study, understand, and be able to teach the transmitted material.
3
Understand the Consequence
If the condition is not met, what is the result? The ijazah does not grant sanad muttasil; only tabarruk.
4
Apply the Rule
Consider a scenario: A student receives ijazah but never opens the book. Can he narrate from the teacher's connected chain? No, only tabarruk.
5
Distinguish from Tabarruk
Note that ijazah still has a blessing (tabarruk) value even without the condition, but not the authority of connected transmission.

If one has confidence that after receiving permission (ijazat), he will understand it through effort and study and will teach it. Then this permission is not sufficient, meaning he cannot narrate Mishkat from our connected chain. However, due to the permission, he will obtain blessing (tabarruk).

Commentary
This passage states a rule regarding the validity of ijazat in hadith transmission. The author clarifies that mere permission to narrate is not enough for the recipient to transmit with a connected chain (sanad muttasil); rather, he must actually study and understand the material and be capable of teaching it. If he does not do so, the ijazat only gives him a blessing (tabarruk) but not the authority to narrate from the teacher's connected chain. This principle is from the Hanafi tradition, emphasizing the importance of actual acquisition of knowledge.

Key Terms
اجازہ (ijazah): Permission given by a scholar to a student to transmit his works or narrations.
سند متصلہ (sanad muttasilah): A continuous chain of narrators reaching back to the Prophet (ﷺ) or the author.

Key Takeaway
