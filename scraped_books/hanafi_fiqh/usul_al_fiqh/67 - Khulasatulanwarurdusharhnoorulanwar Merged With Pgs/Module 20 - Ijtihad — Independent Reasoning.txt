The Legal Effect of 'Aw' in Mahr and Kaffarah
Discusses the ruling of 'aw' in dower and expiation among Hanafi imams

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to explain the Hanafi rule regarding the particle 'aw' when it appears between two or more options, and compare the positions of Imam Abu Hanifa and the two companions (Sahibayn) in the contexts of mahr and kaffarah.

Core Idea
The particle 'aw' grants choice (takhyir), not substitution; only one option is wajib, and extra performance is voluntary.

This passage from a Hanafi usul commentary examines the legal implications of the Arabic particle 'aw' (or) when used in contractual stipulations (mahr) and expiatory obligations (kaffarah). It highlights an internal Hanafi disagreement between Imam Abu Hanifa and his two disciples, Abu Yusuf and Muhammad al-Shaybani, regarding the specification of mahr, and also refutes an external view that treats multiple options as equally obligatory alternatives. Understanding this principle is essential for correctly applying Hanafi rulings on choice in obligations.

Guided Path
1
Identify the general principle of 'aw' in Hanafi usul
The particle 'aw' generally indicates takhyir (choice) among alternatives, not that all are required. Recognize that this is a linguistic rule.
2
Apply to mahr: distinguish Sahibayn and Imam Abu Hanifa
Sahibayn allow specification with 'aw' only if there is benefit (one amount advantageous); otherwise the lesser is due. Imam Abu Hanifa defaults to mahr al-mithl with complex takhyir based on cash/deferred and comparison.
3
Apply to kaffarah: refute the badal (substitution) view
In kaffarah, 'aw' grants choice; only one is wajib. The contrary view that all are wajib on a substitutive basis is rejected as contrary to Shari'ah intent.
4
Synthesize the practical implication
The common thread: 'aw' does not impose multiple obligations; it provides a single duty with a menu of options. Extra performance is voluntary (nafl).

He said: And in the mahr likewise according to them (the two companions: Abu Yusuf and Muhammad) if the change is valid? And in the two currencies, the lesser is obligatory. And according to him (Abu Hanifa), the mahr al-mithl is obligatory if the mahr al-mithl is two thousand. And in the kaffarah, one of the things is obligatory, contrary to some. According to the two companions, bringing 'aw' in mahr is valid when there is some benefit of choice, i.e., profit for one and loss for the other. And if there is no benefit of choice, then the smaller among the two or the two currencies becomes obligatory, like one thousand and two thousand. The example is that one thousand cash and two thousand deferred. According to Imam (Abu Hanifa), in these cases mahr al-mithl is obligatory. But if one thousand is cash and two thousand deferred, then if mahr al-mithl is two thousand or more, the choice is for the woman; if mahr al-mithl is one thousand or less, the choice is for the man. Every kaffarah (expiation) in which the particle 'aw' is brought among several things, there is choice, and only one thing is obligatory, such as kaffarah yamin (expiation of oath), kaffarah halq ra's (expiation of shaving head), etc. And if he pays all the things among which there was choice, the kaffarah is fulfilled by only one; the payment of the rest is not as kaffarah but as voluntary charity. And if he does not pay any of the distinguished things, punishment is only for one, due to non-payment of kaffarah. But according to some people, all these things are obligatory on a substitutive basis, i.e., if he pays one, obligation falls, but if he pays none, punishment is for non-payment of all. We say: Your statement is contrary to the principles of the Shari'ah, therefore it cannot be accepted.

Commentary
This passage discusses the application of 'aw' in mahr (dower) and kaffarah (expiation). For mahr, the two companions (Abu Yusuf and Muhammad al-Shaybani) hold that using 'aw' between two amounts (e.g., 'your dower is one thousand or two thousand') is valid only if takhyir (choice) provides some benefit — meaning the two amounts are not equal; otherwise, the lesser amount is due. If the two amounts are in different currencies (e.g., dirhams and dinars), the lesser value is taken. Imam Abu Hanifa holds that in such ambiguous specifications, the woman is entitled to mahr al-mithl (the standard dower of her peers), but if one amount is cash and the other deferred, and mahr al-mithl is equal to or more than the larger, the woman chooses; if mahr al-mithl is less, the man chooses. For kaffarah, the rule is consistent: when 'aw' appears (e.g., feed or clothe or free a slave), the mukallaf may choose any one, and only one is wajib. If the person performs more than one, the excess is regarded as voluntary (nafl). This refutes the view that all are obligatory as alternatives (badal) such that non-payment of all incurs sin for all. The author rejects the opposing view as contrary to Shari'ah's principles.

Key Terms
مهر (mahr): Dower, an obligatory payment from husband to wife upon marriage.
كفارة (kaffarah): Expiation for certain sins or broken oaths, consisting of specified acts like feeding, clothing, or freeing a slave.
مهر المثل (mahr al-mithl): The standard dower given to women of similar status to the bride.
تخییر (takhyir): Choice; the legal effect of 'aw' in such contexts allowing the obligated person to select one option.
