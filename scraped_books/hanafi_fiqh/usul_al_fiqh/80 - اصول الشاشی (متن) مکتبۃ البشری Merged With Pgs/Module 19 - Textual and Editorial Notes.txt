When 'أو' Means 'Until': A Hanafi Usul Principle
Explaining a secondary meaning of 'أو' as 'until' from the Quran

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify when 'أو' functions as 'حتى' in a textual context and apply the Hanafi usul principle of figurative interpretation.

Core Idea
When literal disjunction fails, 'أو' shifts to 'حتى'—context determines meaning.

In Arabic, the particle 'أو' primarily indicates disjunction (i.e., 'or'), but Hanafi usul recognizes that context can override this literal meaning. This lesson explores a case from the Quran where 'أو' must be interpreted as 'until' (حتى), demonstrating the importance of contextual analysis in usul al-Fiqh.

Guided Path
1
Recognize the literal meaning
Identify that the default meaning of 'أو' in Arabic is disjunction, presenting a choice between two alternatives.
This is the haqiqah (literal) meaning.
2
Test the literal meaning in the verse
Apply the disjunction interpretation to Quran 3:128. If 'أو' meant 'or', it would imply the Prophet has no influence and God either turns or not—but the context is a declaration of God's exclusive authority, making disjunction logically problematic.
Disjunction would contradict the certainty of God's action.
3
Identify contextual incongruity
Recognize that the literal reading leads to an absurd or impossible meaning within the verse's flow.
4
Apply Hanafi figurative interpretation principle
When the literal meaning (haqiqah) is impossible, and a valid figurative usage exists in Arabic, the word is interpreted figuratively. 'أو' has a known secondary meaning of 'حتى' (until) in certain contexts.
This is a standard usul approach: al-haqiqah tuqaddu al-majaz.
5
Derive the intended meaning
Thus, 'أو يتوب عليهم' means 'until He turns to them', indicating the temporal endpoint of the Prophet's lack of involvement.
The verse now conveys a complete and coherent meaning.

Commentary
This brief passage introduces another usage of the particle 'أو': it can carry the meaning of 'حتى' (until). The author provides a Quranic example: 'ليس لك من الأمر شيء أو يتوب عليهم' (You have no part in the matter, or He turns to them). In this context, 'أو' cannot be disjunctive (since the two clauses are not alternatives but sequential), so it is interpreted as 'until' — meaning 'until He turns to them'. This is a recognized usul principle: when the literal meaning (disjunction) does not make sense due to the context, the word may be interpreted metaphorically to mean 'until' or 'except' etc. The Hanafi school acknowledges such figurative uses based on context and Arabic usage.

Key Terms
أو (aw): In this verse, it means 'until' (حتى), indicating a temporal limit or result.
حتى (ḥattā): A particle meaning 'until', indicating the endpoint of a process.
