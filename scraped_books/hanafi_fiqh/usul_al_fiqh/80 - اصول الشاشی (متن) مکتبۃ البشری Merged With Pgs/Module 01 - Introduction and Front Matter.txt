Introduction to Usul al-Shashi: A Foundational Hanafi Text on Usul al-Fiqh
Front matter: title, author, edition, and Quranic epigraph.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the author and significance of Usul al-Shashi and explain why this text is foundational for Hanafi usul al-fiqh.

Core Idea
Usul al-Shashi is a concise Hanafi primer on deriving rulings from evidence.

This lesson introduces Usul al-Shashi, a classic text in the Hanafi tradition on the principles of Islamic jurisprudence (usul al-fiqh). Written by Imam Nizam al-Din al-Shashi (d. 325 AH), the book is renowned for its concise yet comprehensive coverage of the key topics in usul al-fiqh. The edition we are using includes helpful marginalia and hadith verification.

Guided Path
1
Identify the book and author
The title is 'Usul al-Shashi', authored by Imam Nizam al-Din al-Shashi, who died in 325 AH. This establishes the text's place in the early Hanafi tradition.
The death date (325 AH) places him after the major early Hanafi imams but still within the classical period.
2
Understand the edition
This is a new colored corrected edition with topic headings in page headers, based on the marginalia (hashiya) of Muhammad Barakat Allah al-Lakhnawi titled 'Ahsan al-Hawashi'.
The inclusion of a hashiya shows this text is often taught with commentary.
3
Connect the Quranic verse to the subject
The verse (4:83) mentions 'istinbat' (derivation), which is the core of usul al-fiqh: deriving rulings from the Quran and Sunnah.
This verse is frequently cited to justify ijtihad and the need for usul al-fiqh.
4
Recognize the publisher details
The edition is published in Karachi, Pakistan, indicating its widespread use in the South Asian Hanafi tradition.
The numbers '66' and '99' are likely printer's marks, not part of the original text.

Introduction to Usul al-Shashi and the Concept of Istinbat
Front matter with Quranic epigraph and book details

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the meaning of 'istinbat' as used in Quran 4:83 and identify the role of this verse as an epigraph for Usul al-Shashi.

Core Idea
Istinbat — the derivation of rulings — is the heart of usul al-fiqh.

This lesson opens the study of Usul al-Shashi with a Quranic verse that defines the very purpose of usul al-fiqh: to derive rulings from revelation and authoritative sources. The verse serves as an epigraph, setting the theme for the entire text. We will explore its relevance and the background of the book.

Guided Path
1
Identify the Quranic verse and its context
Locate the verse (4:83) that begins the book. Note the key term 'yastanbiṭūnahu' (they derive it). Understand that the verse speaks about referring matters to the Messenger and those in authority, so that those capable of istinbat can extract the correct ruling.
This verse is not being exegeted; it serves as a thematic banner.
2
Connect the verse to the subject of usul al-fiqh
Recognize that 'istinbat' is the core activity of usul al-fiqh. The book Usul al-Shashi is a textbook that teaches the principles and methods by which a mujtahid performs istinbat.
In Hanafi usul, istinbat includes qiyas, istihsan, and other tools.

Methodology of al-Shashi in his book Usul al-Shashi
Indeed, al-Shashi in this book of his adopted the methodology of deriving branches from the fundamental principles of usul, which is the general methodology of the Hanafis in usul. He relied in his methodology on the following axes:
First: Considering the 'illah (effective cause) of the text and understanding the 'illah of the ruling so that other matters can be correctly attached to it. Second: Building branches upon usul principles and deriving them upon general Shari'i rules. Third: Preponderance between conflicting evidences by considering the strength of the evidence and the presence of reasons for preponderance.
Al-Shashi's Conventions in Writing Usul
Al-Shashi agreed in this book on the following matters:
1 - His saying: "Our companions (ashabuna) said" means Imam Abu Hanifa, Abu Yusuf, and Muhammad (may Allah have mercy on them).
2 - His saying: "If it is said (fa'in qila)" means the speaker from the Shafi'is.
3 - His saying: "We said (qulna)" means the Hanafis.
4 - His saying: "According to him ('indahu)" means Imam al-Shafi'i (may Allah have mercy on him).
5 - His saying: "According to us ('indana)" means the Hanafis.
6 - His saying: "He said in al-Jami'" means al-Jami' al-Kabir by Imam Muhammad ibn al-Hasan al-Shaybani (may Allah have mercy on him).
7 - His saying: "We all said (qulna jami'an)" means the Hanafis and Shafi'is.
8 - His saying: "Our mashayikh (shuyukhuna) said" means the scholars of Transoxiana from the Hanafis, such as the scholars of Samarqand, Tashkent, and Bukhara, like Abu Mansur al-Maturidi and al-Qadi Abu Zayd al-Dabusi.
9 - His saying: "Our scholars ('ulama'una) said" means the Hanafi scholars encompassing the school of Transoxiana and Kufa.
The Rank of al-Shashi among Mujtahids
Al-Shashi is considered among the people of the third rank, the masters of takhrij (derivation) who are proficient in deriving rulings from the texts of their Imam. They follow their Imam in what he concluded of usul and furu', and they know the proofs of rulings that their Imam derived. Abu al-Hasan al-Karkhi and Fakhr al-Razi (may Allah have mercy on them) are also counted among this rank.

This passage is the introduction (front matter) of Imam al-Shashi's work 'Usul al-Shashi', describing the author's background, his methodology, and his terminological conventions. Learning Objective

Core Idea

The author of Usul al-Shashi opens his book with a precise definition of his subject. This definition, both linguistic and technical, lays the foundation for all subsequent discussions. Understanding it is the first step to mastering Hanafi usul.

The commentator explicitly mentions this: sometimes it means the rules of fiqh, sometimes the proofs.
4
Connect to the rest of the book
Understand that this definition sets the scope: the book will discuss the universal rules and their conditions that enable a mujtahid to derive rulings.
This definition excludes mere memorization of fiqh; it's about the methodology.

The Mustanbit and the Dispute on Salutations

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to explain the role of the mustanbit (mujtahid) and the reward structure, and evaluate the ikhtilaf on sending salutations to non-prophets.

Core Idea
The mujtahid who extracts rulings from texts earns double reward when correct, single when mistaken.

In the opening, the author praises Allah for distinguishing the mustanbitun. The commentator elaborates on their methods and rewards. Guided Path

Define 'mustanbit' and his methods
The term 'al-mustanbiṭīn' refers to mujtahidun who extract rulings from texts. Their methods include: 'ibara (explicit expression), dalala (indication), ishara (allusion), iqtida (implication), and qiyas.
These are the five methods of textual derivation in Hanafi usul.
2
Understand the reward structure
When a mustanbit is correct, he earns two rewards; when mistaken, he earns one reward. This is based on the hadith and is a key principle in usul.
The commentator cites 'al-Multaqat' as source.
3
Analyze the ikhtilaf on salutation
Some scholars deem it makruh or haram to send peace on non-prophets independently; the majority view is that it is permissible only as a follow-up (taba'iyya) and not initially.
The author follows the majority: it is permissible when joined to the Prophet (e.g., 'peace be upon Muhammad and Abu Hanifa').
4
Connect to the author's madhhab
The author explicitly mentions Abu Hanifa to indicate his Hanafi affiliation, and he appends the Companions to show that Abu Hanifa is a Tabi'i.
This shows the importance of madhhab identity in classical scholarship.

Commentary
This passage is the opening (diibaja) of the book 'Usul al-Shashi'. The author begins with basmala and hamdalah. The Author's Preamble and Definition of Usul al-Fiqh
Author's opening of his usul al-fiqh text

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to define usul al-fiqh according to the Hanafi school and distinguish it from fiqh itself.

Core Idea
Usul al-fiqh is the methodology for deriving rulings, not the rulings themselves.

This lesson introduces the foundational definition of usul al-fiqh from a classical Hanafi text. The author opens with praise of Allah and the Prophet, then clarifies that usul al-fiqh is the knowledge of universal principles that guide the mujtahid in extracting specific legal rulings from the sources. Understanding this distinction is critical before studying any fiqh ruling.

Guided Path
1
Recognize the preamble structure
Identify that the author begins with basmalah, then hamd (praise of Allah), followed by salawat upon the Prophet and peace upon Abu Hanifa. This is a standard opening that signals the text's Hanafi affiliation.
The author thanks Allah for elevating the scholars, especially the mujtahids who extract rulings.
2
Understand the praise of the mustanbitun
The author praises those who extract rulings (mustanbitun) for their ability to derive rulings from texts using wording, indication, implication, and requirement, as well as qiyas.
This highlights the Hanafi emphasis on multiple dimensions of textual analysis.
3
Locate the definition of usul al-fiqh
Find the sentence defining usul al-fiqh: 'al-ilm bil-qawa'id al-kulliyyah wa ma'rifat ahwaliha allati tu'addi ila ma'rifat al-furu' al-fiqhiyyah'.
The definition explicitly separates the universal rules from the derived branches.
4
Distinguish usul from fiqh
Note that usul al-fiqh is not the rulings themselves but the methodology (qawa'id) that enables derivation. The author also mentions that the term may refer to the evidences of fiqh.
This distinction prevents confusion between the two disciplines.
