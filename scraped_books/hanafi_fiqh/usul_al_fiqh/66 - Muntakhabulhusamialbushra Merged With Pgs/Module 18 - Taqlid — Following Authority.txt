Taqlid of a Companion: Obligation and Dispute
Analyzing the obligation of following a Companion's opinion over qiyas

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the three positions on taqlid of a Companion, explain the proofs for each, and apply the Hanafi tarjih to determine the relied-upon view.

Core Idea
A Companion's opinion may override qiyas due to possible direct transmission.

Should a mujtahid follow a Companion's opinion over his own analogical reasoning? This is a classic usul dispute. The passage presents three views: one holds it obligatory in all cases, another only in non-qiyasi matters, and a third absolutely forbids it. We will dissect each position's proof and see how the Hanafi school navigates this ikhtilaf.

Guided Path
1
Identify the Three Positions
Read the passage and list the three main positions: (1) Abu Sa'id al-Barda'i et al.: taqlid of a Companion is wajib and qiyas is abandoned. (2) Abu al-Hasan al-Karkhi et al.: taqlid only allowed in matters beyond qiyas. (3) Al-Shafi'i (new opinion), Mu'tazila, Ash'aris: never allowed.
The first position is also attributed to two shaykhs (likely Abu Hanifa and Abu Yusuf) and others.
2
Extract the Proofs
For each position, find the key proofs. Position 1: possibility of sama' wa tawqif; Companions' opinions stronger due to witnessing revelation. Position 2: in non-qiyasi matters, hearing is certain and lying inconceivable; in qiyasi matters, fallible like others. Position 3: Companions gave fatwa by ra'y, and are not infallible.
Notice the analogy to khabar al-wahid in position 1.
3
Analyse the Hanafi Internal Difference
Note that the Hanafi school has two views: the majority of early Hanafis (al-Barda'i, al-Razi) and later mufta bihi is the first view. The second view is held by al-Karkhi and al-Qadi Abu Zayd.
The text prefers the first view, calling it the choice of the two shaykhs and Abi al-Yusr.
4
Determine the Thamarah al-Khilaf
The practical difference: If a Companion gives a ruling on a matter that qiyas can address (e.g., sale contracts), the first view says follow the Companion; the second says do not; the third says never. This affects how a later mujtahid weighs evidence.
For example, a Companion's fatwa on riba might override analogical reasoning according to the first view.
5
Evaluate the Tarjih
The author inclines to the first view and notes it is also the old view of al-Shafi'i and the view of Malik and Ahmad. This gives it substantial weight.
The tarjih is based on the epistemological weight of Companions' statements.

Abu Sa'id al-Barda'i said: Taqlid of the Companion is obligatory, and qiyas is abandoned for it due to the possibility of hearing and divine instruction, and due to the excellence of their correctness in opinion itself from witnessing the circumstances of revelation and knowing its causes. Abu al-Hasan al-Karkhi said: It is not permissible to make taqlid of the Companion except in that which cannot be perceived by qiyas. Al-Shafi'i said: None of them should be followed. Abu Sa'id al-Barda'i, Abu Bakr al-Razi in some narrations, and a group of our companions said: It is obligatory upon the Followers and those after them of the mujtahids, not upon a Companion. 'Hearing and divine instruction' from the Prophet, peace be upon him, rather it is probable in his case even if he did not attribute it to him, so giving precedence to the Companion's statement from this perspective is like giving precedence to the khabar al-wahid over qiyas. And if it is conceded that it is not heard from him but rather his opinion, it is also suitable that qiyas of others be set aside for it; because the opinion of the Companions is stronger than the opinion of others. He alluded to this by his saying: 'and due to the excellence of their correctness etc.' So with this consideration they have superiority over others, and this is a way of giving preference to their opinion over the opinion of others. Just as when two qiyas conflict for those after them, one is given preponderance over the other by a type of preponderance, so too should their qiyas be given preponderance over the qiyas of other mujtahids due to the increased strength of their opinion from the aspects mentioned. And by this is refuted what the opponents imagined: that the Companion's statement is subject to opinion, so how can the qiyas of others, who are equal in opinion, be abandoned for it? Al-Shafi'i said in his new opinion, and many of the Mu'tazila and Ash'aris went to it: (he set it aside) whether it is perceptible by qiyas or not; because giving fatwa by opinion appeared among them, which cannot be denied, and the possibility of error in their ijtihad is established due to the lack of infallibility as is established in the ijtihad of other mujtahids, and there is no difference between that which is not perceived by opinion from measures and the like and other than that.

Commentary
This passage addresses the usul al-fiqh dispute on the obligation of taqlid of a Companion (تقليد الصحابي). The chapter title indicates it is about following the Companions. The author presents three main positions:

1. Abu Sa'id al-Barda'i and others: Taqlid of a Companion is wajib (obligatory), and qiyas is abandoned in favor of the Companion's statement. The reason is twofold: (a) The Companion may have heard (sama') or received divine instruction (tawqif) from the Prophet directly, even if he does not explicitly cite him. This makes it like a khabar al-wahid which can override qiyas. (b) Even if it is purely his opinion, the Companion's opinion is stronger than others' because he witnessed revelation and its context.

2. Abu al-Hasan al-Karkhi and others: Taqlid is only allowed in matters that cannot be reached by qiyas (e.g., specific measures like the amount of zakat). In such cases, the Companion must have heard from the Prophet, and since lying is inconceivable, it is binding. But if qiyas can reach it, the Companion's statement may be his own ijtihad, and like all ijtihad, it is fallible, so cannot outweigh the qiyas of others.

3. Al-Shafi'i (new opinion), Mu'tazila, Ash'aris: Taqlid of a Companion is never allowed, whether the matter is reachable by qiyas or not. This is because Companions gave fatwas by opinion, and they are not infallible.

The author supports the first view, calling it the choice of 'the two shaykhs' and noting it is also the old view of Shafi'i and the view of Malik and Ahmad. The text also defines taqlid as 'following another with the assumption of correctness without examining evidence.' The key point: the Companion's closeness to revelation gives his opinions a weight that can justify setting aside analogical reasoning, though some restrict this to matters of direct transmission.

Key Terms
تقليد (taqlid): اتباع الغير على ظنّ أنه محق بلا نظر في الدليل — following another with the assumption that he is correct without examining the evidence.
صحابي (sahabi): A companion of the Prophet Muhammad (s) who met him as a believer and died as a believer.
سماع وتوقيف (sama' wa tawqif): Hearing directly from the Prophet or receiving divine instruction (tawqif) that is not necessarily attributed explicitly.

The Obligation of Following the Tabi‘in: A Hanafi Dispute
Discusses whether taqlid of Tabi‘in is obligatory

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the two narrations from Abu Hanifa regarding taqlid of the Tabi‘in and evaluate the tarjih between them.

Core Idea
Taqlid of the Tabi‘in is disputed within the Hanafi school; Abu Hanifa's apparent madhhab rejects it.

This passage addresses a critical methodological question in Hanafi usul: is a later mujtahid bound to follow the fatwas of prominent Tabi‘in? The answer is not straightforward. Within the Hanafi school, there is a significant internal dispute, with some scholars elevating certain Tabi‘in to the rank of Companions and others firmly denying their authority for taqlid. Abu Hanifa himself reportedly held two different positions, one of which is considered the apparent madhhab. Understanding this dispute illuminates the Hanafi approach to ijtihad and the authority of early generations.

Guided Path
1
Identify the core issue
Determine the exact point of disagreement: whether a later mujtahid must make taqlid of a Tabi‘i whose fatwa was known during the time of the Companions.
This is an intra-Hanafi usul dispute about the authority of the Tabi‘in.
2
List the main positions
Identify all positions mentioned: (1) Some Hanafi mashayikh say taqlid is obligatory because such Tabi‘in are like Companions; (2) Others say it is not permissible; (3) From Abu Hanifa, two narrations: the first rejects taqlid, the second permits it.
The first narration from Abu Hanifa is stated as the apparent (zahir) madhhab.
3
Trace the reasoning for each position
For the obligatory view: Companions referred to them and considered them knowledgeable. For the non-obligatory view: Tabi‘in lack the privilege of witnessing revelation. For Abu Hanifa's first narration: 'We are men who exercise ijtihad.' For the second: their rank became like Companions.
The argument from witnessing revelation is key to the difference in authority.
4
Apply the tarjih
The text indicates that the first narration from Abu Hanifa is the apparent madhhab (zahir al-madhhab). The second is from al-Nawadir, a weaker transmission. Thus, the relied-upon position is that taqlid of Tabi‘in is not obligatory for later mujtahids.
Tarjih is based on the strength of transmission and consistency with Abu Hanifa's general approach to ijtihad.
5
Determine the practical consequence (thamarah al-khilaf)
The practical effect: a later mujtahid may choose to exercise his own ijtihad rather than follow the fatwa of a Tabi‘i, even if that Tabi‘i was highly respected. This keeps the door of ijtihad open in the Hanafi school.
This distinguishes Hanafi methodology from a rigid reliance on early authorities.

Chapter of al-Bayan: Following the Companions of the Messenger of Allah, may Allah bless him and grant him peace – according to some of our mashayikh, contrary to others. The summary: If his fatwa appeared in the time of the Companions, such as al-Hasan al-Basri, Sa‘id ibn al-Musayyab, ‘Alqama, al-Nakha‘i, al-Sha‘bi and others, then he is like the Companions, to the extent that taqlid of him is obligatory upon those after him from the mujtahids. Commentary
This passage discusses a dispute among Hanafi scholars about whether it is obligatory for later mujtahids to follow (make taqlid of) the Tabi‘in (the generation after the Companions). The issue arises when a Tabi‘i's fatwa became well-known during the time of the Companions. Some scholars say such a Tabi‘i is equivalent to a Companion, making taqlid obligatory. Others disagree because Tabi‘in lack direct witness of revelation. From Abu Hanifa himself, there are two narrations: the first (the apparent madhhab) rejects obligatory taqlid, saying all mujtahids are equal; the second, from rare transmissions (Nawadir), permits it due to the Tabi‘in's elevated status. The passage illustrates how later Hanafi scholarship weighed different transmissions and opinions within the madhhab.

Key Terms
متابعة (mutāba‘a): Following the Tabi‘in in their fatwas.
تقليد (taqlīd): Following the opinion of a mujtahid without knowing his evidence.
صاحب (ṣāḥib): A Companion of the Prophet Muhammad صلى الله عليه وسلم.
تابعي (tābi‘ī): A Muslim who met a Companion and died as a Muslim.
