Islam Not a Condition for Truthfulness — Suspicion (Tuhma) as a Ground for Rejecting Reports
Stating that Islam is not a condition for truthfulness but rejection due to suspicion

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to explain the Hanafi usul principle that Islam is not a condition for truthfulness (sidq) and apply the concept of tuhma (suspicion) to justify the rejection of a disbeliever's report in religious matters.

Core Idea
Truthfulness does not require Islam; disbelief creates suspicion that justifies rejecting religious reports.

In Hanafi usul, the integrity of a reporter ('adala) includes truthfulness, but Islam is not a prerequisite for being truthful. However, when it comes to reports about religion, the disbeliever's enmity towards Islam creates a suspicion of bias, leading to rejection. This lesson unpacks the reasoning and the analogy to a father testifying for his son.

Guided Path
1
Identify the core claim
The passage states that Islam is not a condition for truthfulness. Note that disbelief does not negate truthfulness; a disbeliever can be truthful.
2
Understand the exception for religious matters
In the context of religious reports, disbelief creates a suspicion (tuhma) that the reporter may intend to harm the religion, thus requiring rejection.
The term 'bab al-din' refers to matters of religion.
3
Analyze the analogy to father-witness
The passage compares the disbeliever's situation to a father testifying for his son: both generate suspicion due to partiality or motive.
In Hanafi law, a father's testimony for his son is rejected due to suspected bias.
4
Apply the principle to the example of testimony
The passage gives the example: testimony of a disbeliever against a Muslim is not accepted due to enmity and lack of guardianship (wilaya).
5
Summarize the Hanafi position
Islam is not a condition for sidq, but tuhma from disbelief is a valid reason to reject a report in matters of din.

وأما الإسلام فليس بشرط لثبوت الصدق؛ لأن الكفر لا ينافي الصدق، ولكن الكفر في هذا الباب يوجب شبهةً يجب بها ردُّ الخبر ؛ لأن الباب بابُ الدِّين، والكافِرُ ساع لِمَا يَهدِم الدينَ الحقِّ، فيصير متّهَماً باب الدين، فثبتت بالكفر تهمة زائدة لا نقصان حال، بمنزلة الأب فيما يشهد لولده، ولهذا لم تُقبل شهادة الكافر على المسلم؛ لما قلنا من العداوة، ولانقطاع الولاية، والله أعلم.
As for Islam, it is not a condition for establishing truthfulness, because disbelief does not contradict truthfulness. However, disbelief in this subject necessitates a suspicion that requires the rejection of the report, because the subject is the subject of religion, and the disbeliever strives to destroy the true religion, so he becomes accused in the matter of religion. Thus, due to disbelief, an added suspicion is established, not a deficiency of condition, like the father regarding what he testifies for his son. For this reason, the testimony of a disbeliever against a Muslim is not accepted, due to the enmity we mentioned and the severance of guardianship. And Allah knows best.

Commentary
This passage is a ruling on the condition of Islam for accepting a report (khabar) in religious matters. The author clarifies that Islam is not a condition for the truthfulness (sidq) of the reporter, because a disbeliever can be truthful. However, in the context of religious reports, disbelief creates a suspicion (tuhma) that the disbeliever might intentionally lie to undermine the religion. Therefore, his report is rejected due to this suspicion, not due to any inherent deficiency in his state (like being a liar). The author draws an analogy to the case of a father testifying for his son, where the father's natural bias creates suspicion. Similarly, the disbeliever's enmity towards Islam causes suspicion. This is why the testimony of a disbeliever against a Muslim is not accepted, due to enmity and lack of guardianship (wilaya). The passage follows the Hanafi usul position that Islam is not a condition for 'adala (integrity) but is relevant only in matters of religion due to suspicion.

Key Terms
الصدق (al-sidq): Truthfulness, a personal quality of the reporter that does not require Islam.
التهمة (al-tuhma): Suspicion of partiality or motive to lie, which arises from disbelief in religious matters.
