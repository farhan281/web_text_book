Three Types of Performance (Ada') in Hanafi Usul
Defines and classifies the three types of adaa' in Hanafi usul

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to define the three types of adaa' (performance) in Hanafi usul and provide examples for each.

Core Idea
Performance in Hanafi usul is not just timing—it is also about conformity to prescribed method and commitment.

In this passage from Nur al-Anwar, the author classifies 'ada'' (performance) into three types: pure, complete, deficient, and a unique category resembling qada'. These distinctions are not mere academic; they affect rulings like obligation to recite aloud or change of traveler's intention. Let's unpack each type with examples.

Guided Path
1
Survey the three main categories
Read the passage to identify the three types: adaa' mahd (pure), adaa' kamil (complete), adaa' qasir (deficient), and adaa' shabih bi al-qada (resembling qada). Note that pure is further divided.
The author notes that 'pure' has no resemblance to qada in time or commitment.
2
Define pure performance (adaa' mahd)
Pure performance is an act performed within its prescribed time without any resemblance to qada, neither in time nor in commitment.
Resemblance in time would mean performing outside the time; in commitment means not doing as originally intended.
3
Distinguish complete (kamil) vs deficient (qasir)
Complete is performed exactly as prescribed; deficient is the opposite. Deficient is still valid but lacks perfection.
Example: congregation is prescribed for prayer, so praying alone is deficient but valid.
4
Analyze the latecomer example (adaa' shabih bi al-qada)
The lahiq starts with the Imam but breaks wudu, then completes after the Imam finishes. This is adaa' because time remains, but resembles qada because he did not perform as he originally committed.
The commitment aspect is key: he undertook to pray fully with Imam but changed his pattern.
5
State the legal benefit (thamarah)
The classification affects rulings: e.g., the lahiq's traveler status does not change during completion because it is adaa' shabih bi al-qada.
If it were actual qada, different rules would apply.

لأن الأقسام لا تقابل فيما بينها، وينبغي أن يقول: والأداء أنواع: أداء محض وهو نوعان: كامل وقاصر، وأداء هو شبيه بالقضاء، ويُعنى بالأداء المحض ما لا يكون فيه شبه بالقضاء بوجه من الوجوه لا من حيث تغير الوقت، ولا من حيث التزامه، ويُعنى بالشبيه بالقضاء ما فيه شبه به من حيث التزامه، ويعنى بالكامل ما يؤدى على الوجه الذي شرع عليه، وبالقاصر ما هو خلافه. كالصلاة بجماعة مثال للأداء الكامل، فإنه أداء على حسب ما شرع، فإن الصلاة ما شرعت إلا بجماعة؛ لأن جبريل عليه السلام علم الرسول صلى الله عليه وسلم بالجماعة في يومين والصلاة منفردًا مثال للأداء القاصر، فإنه أداء على خلاف ما شرع عليه، ولهذا يسقط وجوب الجهر في الجهرية عن المنفرد. وفعل اللاحق بعد فراغ الإمام حتى لا يتغير فرضه بنية الإقامة مثال للأداء الشبيه بالقضاء؛ فإن اللاحق هو الذي التزم الأداء مع الإمام من أول التحريمة، ثم سبقه الحدث فتوضأ وأتم بقية الصلاة بعد فراغ الإمام، فإن هذا الإتمام أداء من حيث بقاء الوقت، وشبيه بالقضاء من حيث أنه لم يؤد كما التزم، ولما كان معنى الأداء من حيث الأصل، ومعنى القضاء من حيث التبع جُعل أداء شبيهاً بالقضاء، ولم يجعل قضاء شبيهاً بالأداء؛ وثمرة كونه أداء ظاهرة، وثمرة كونه شبيهاً بالقضاء أنه لا يتغير فرضه حينئذٍ بنية الإقامة.
Section on the Specific. The types of performance are three. Because the divisions are not comparable among themselves, and it should be said: Performances are types: pure performance, which is of two types: complete and deficient, and performance that resembles qada. By pure performance is meant that in which there is no resemblance to qada in any respect, neither from the perspective of change of time nor from the perspective of its undertaking. By that which resembles qada is meant that in which there is resemblance to it from the perspective of its undertaking. By complete is meant that which is performed in the manner in which it was prescribed, and by deficient is meant the opposite of that. Like prayer in congregation is an example of complete performance, for it is performance according to what was prescribed, since prayer was only prescribed in congregation because Jibrael (peace be upon him) taught the Messenger of Allah (peace be upon him) the prayer in congregation over two days. And prayer alone is an example of deficient performance, for it is performance contrary to what was prescribed, and because of this the obligation of loud recitation is dropped for the one praying alone in loud prayers. And the action of the latecomer after the Imam finishes, such that his obligatory prayer is not changed by the intention of residence, is an example of performance resembling qada; for the latecomer is the one who undertook performance with the Imam from the first takbir, then an event preceded him so he performed ablution and completed the rest of the prayer after the Imam finished. This completion is performance from the perspective of remaining time, and resembles qada from the perspective that he did not perform as he undertook. And since the meaning of performance is primary and the meaning of qada is secondary, it was made performance resembling qada, and not made qada resembling performance. The benefit of it being performance is apparent, and the benefit of it resembling qada is that his obligatory prayer is not changed at that time by the intention of residence.

Commentary
This passage is a definitional section from Nur al-Anwar on the types of adaa' (performance) in Hanafi usul. The author first states that adaa' is of three types: pure adaa' (mahd), which is further divided into complete (kamil) and deficient (qasir), and adaa' that resembles qada (shabih bi al-qada). Pure adaa' is defined as having no resemblance to qada at all, neither in terms of time change (i.e., performing within the prescribed time) nor in terms of commitment (i.e., performing exactly as one undertook). The type resembling qada has resemblance in commitment, meaning the person undertook to perform in one way but performed in another. Complete adaa' is performing the act exactly as prescribed, while deficient adaa' is performing it contrary to the prescribed manner. The author illustrates with examples from prayer: praying in congregation is complete adaa' because the prayer was originally prescribed with congregation—Jibrael taught the Prophet the prayer times in congregation. Praying alone is deficient adaa' because the prescribed manner includes congregation, and thus the obligation of loud recitation in loud prayers is dropped for the individual—this shows deficiency. The third type, resembling qada, is exemplified by the latecomer (lahiq) who starts with the Imam but has to leave due to an event, then returns to complete the prayer after the Imam finishes. This is considered adaa' because the time remains, but it resembles qada because he did not perform as he initially committed. The benefit of calling it adaa' is that he is not required to repeat the prayer, and the benefit of resembling qada is that his obligation (e.g., as a traveler) is not altered by intending residence during the process.

Key Terms
أداء محض (adā' maḥḍ): Pure performance: an act performed within its time without any resemblance to qada.
أداء كامل (adā' kāmil): Complete performance: performed exactly as prescribed by the Lawgiver.
أداء قاصر (adā' qāṣir): Deficient performance: performed contrary to the prescribed manner, causing loss of some attributes.
أداء شبيه بالقضاء (adā' shabīh bi al-qaḍā'): Performance resembling make-up: performed in time but deviates from the original commitment.

Key Takeaway
Hanafi usul distinguishes three types of performance: pure (complete/deficient) and resembling qada, based on timing and commitment to the prescribed method.
