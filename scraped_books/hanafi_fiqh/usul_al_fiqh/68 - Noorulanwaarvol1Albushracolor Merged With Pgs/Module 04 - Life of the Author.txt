Biography of Mulla Jiyun al-Siddiqi
Biographical sketch of Mulla Jiyun al-Siddiqi

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state five key facts about Mulla Jiyun’s life and explain why his background is relevant to his Hanafi usul work.

Core Idea
Mulla Jiyun’s photographic memory and royal patronage shaped the Hanafi usul classic Noor al-Anwar.

Every great text has a human story. Mulla Jiyun al-Siddiqi, the author of Noor al-Anwar, was not only a scholar but also a memoriser of extraordinary ability and a teacher of Mughal emperors. This lesson introduces the man behind the book, giving you a deeper appreciation of his work.

Guided Path
1
Identify the author’s full name and lineage
Find his complete name (Ahmad, known as Mulla Jiyun al-Siddiqi) and his ancestral link to Abu Bakr al-Siddiq.
2
Trace his early education
Locate where he memorised the Quran and travelled for studies, and note his main teacher Mulla Lutfullah al-Korawi.
3
Assess his relationship with Mughal rulers
Read how Sultan Aurangzeb received him as a student and how later rulers like Shah Alam respected him.
4
Analyse his remarkable memory
Consider the two feats: reciting pages without looking and memorising a long poem after one hearing.
5
Summarise his later life and legacy
Note his hajj, teaching, writings, death in Delhi in 1130 AH, and burial in Ameti.
This sets the context for Noor al-Anwar's composition.

Biography of the author: He is Shaykh Ahmad known as Mulla Jiyun al-Siddiqi al-Amitawi; his lineage traces back to al-Siddiq al-Akbar (Abu Bakr). His birthplace is Ameti, in the suburbs of Lucknow. He memorized the Quran and traveled among the districts of al-Furub, and took the academic disciplines from its scholars. He completed his studies under Mulla Lutfullah al-Korawi, relative to Korah, a town in the districts of al-Furub. Then he set out to Sultan Alamgir (Aurangzeb); the Sultan received him with honor and respect, and became his student, and he observed his etiquette to the utmost. Similarly, Shah Alam and other sons of the Sultan respected him, following his method. Mulla Jiyun had a strong memory; he would read passages of academic books page by page without looking at the book, and he would memorize a long poem by hearing it once. He was honored to visit the two noble Sanctuaries (Mecca and Medina) and spent his precious life in teaching and authorship. He died in the capital Delhi in the year 1130 AH, and his body was transferred to Ameti and buried there.

Commentary
This is a biographical passage about the author of Noor al-Anwar, Mulla Jiyun. His full name is Ahmad, known as Mulla Jiyun al-Siddiqi al-Amitawi, tracing his lineage to Abu Bakr al-Siddiq. He was born in Ameti near Lucknow. He memorized the Quran and studied in various places, eventually completing his education under Mulla Lutfullah al-Korawi. He then went to the Mughal emperor Aurangzeb 'Alamgir, who honored him and became his student. He also gained the respect of later rulers. The text highlights his extraordinary memory: he could recite books from memory without looking. He performed Hajj and spent his life teaching and writing. He died in Delhi in 1130 AH (1718 CE) and was buried in his hometown. This gives context to the work Noor al-Anwar, which is a Hanafi usul text.

نور الأنوار (Noor al-Anwar): A foundational text in Hanafi usul al-fiqh, authored by Mulla Jiyun.

Presenting the biography of the author Mulla Jiyun

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to describe the key events in Mulla Jiyun's life and explain his role in the Hanafi scholarly tradition.

Core Idea
Mulla Jiyun's extraordinary memory and imperial patronage made him a pillar of Hanafi scholarship in 18th-century India.

This biography unveils the life of Mulla Jiyun, the author of Noor al-Anwar. It tells of his lineage, his education across northern India, his relationship with Emperor Aurangzeb, and his remarkable memory. Understanding his life gives us insight into the transmission of Hanafi fiqh in the Mughal period.

Commentary
This passage is a biography of the author, Mulla Jiyun (Ahmad al-Siddiqi al-Amirtawi). He was a Hanafi scholar from India, born in Amiti near Lucknow. He studied under various scholars in the region of al-Furdb (eastern Uttar Pradesh) and completed his education with Mulla Lutf Allah al-Kurawi. He then went to the court of Sultan Alamgir (Aurangzeb), who treated him with great respect and became his student. He also enjoyed the respect of Shah Alam and other princes. He was known for his prodigious memory: he could recite entire pages of books without looking and memorize long poems after one hearing. He performed pilgrimage to Mecca and Medina, and spent his life teaching and writing. He died in Delhi in 1130 AH (1718 CE) and was buried in his hometown. The biography highlights his scholarly authority and his role in the Hanafi tradition.
