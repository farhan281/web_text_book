Key Terms

خاص (khāṣṣ): A word that applies to a specific subset or individual.

The fihrist is a roadmap: mastering its layout unlocks the entire course.

Before diving into the first lesson, you need to know where you are going. This passage is the fihrist (table of contents) of Talkhees al-Anwar, a core text in Hanafi usul al-fiqh. Guided Path

Understand the hierarchical structure

4
Relate to Hanafi usul categories
Many terms (e.g., zahir, nass, khafi) are categories of textual clarity used in Hanafi istinbat. Locating their definitions is the first step in applying them.

(1) Definition of Usul al-Fiqh: There are two definitions: (1) Additive definition (hadd idafi), (2) True definition (hadd haqiqi). (1) Additive definition: that in which the definition of the mudaf (the term added) and the mudaf ilayh (the term to which it is added) is given separately. (2) True definition: that in which a single definition is given for the whole combination of mudaf and mudaf ilayh. Definition of additive definition: Usul al-Fiqh: Usul is mudaf; Usul is the plural of 'asl' which means 'that upon which something else is built' – like a wall is the basis for a roof. Fiqh is mudaf ilayh; the lexical meaning of fiqh is 'al-shaqq wa al-fath' – to clarify, to open. And in terminology, fiqh is 'knowledge of the soul of what is for it and against it' – a person knowing those things that benefit him and those that harm him. Definition of true definition: It is a science of rules by which the mujtahid arrives at deriving rulings from their detailed evidences. Usul al-Fiqh is the knowledge of such rules through which the mujtahid reaches the derivation of rulings from detailed evidences. (2) Subject matter of Usul al-Fiqh: The evidences and the rulings – evidences and rulings. (3) Aim and objective of Usul al-Fiqh: Acquisition of the ability to derive rulings from their detailed evidences. To gain the ability to extract rulings from detailed evidences.

Commentary
This passage is the opening introduction (muqaddimah) of the book. It begins with the basmalah and hamdalah, then states that the books Manar and Noor al-Anwar are both texts and commentaries on Usul al-Fiqh. The author then lists five introductory topics necessary before studying the discipline. The present passage covers the first three: definition, subject, and purpose. The definition of Usul al-Fiqh is given in two ways: the additive definition and the true definition. In the additive definition, the term 'usul' is explained as the plural of 'asl' meaning foundation, and 'fiqh' is defined lexically and technically. The true definition is the well-known one: 'a science of the rules by which the mujtahid derives rulings from detailed evidences.' The subject matter of Usul al-Fiqh is the evidences (adillah) and the rulings (ahkam). Its purpose is to enable the mujtahid to derive rulings from the detailed evidences. This sets the stage for the entire discipline.
