First Codifier of Usul and Author of Manar
States the first codifier of Usul al-Fiqh and biography of Manar's author

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the first codifier of Usul al-Fiqh according to the Hanafi view and identify key biographical details of Hafiz al-Din al-Nasafi.

Core Idea
Abu Hanifa was the first to write Usul al-Fiqh; al-Nasafi authored Manar, a foundational Hanafi usul text.

This lesson introduces two foundational figures in the history of Hanafi Usul al-Fiqh. You will learn who the first codifier of the science was according to the Hanafi school, and you will become familiar with the life and works of the author of Manar al-Anwar, a key matn studied in Hanafi usul.

Guided Path
1
Identify the claim about the first codifier
Locate the sentence that states who is considered the first codifier of Usul al-Fiqh. Understand that this is presented as the correct and preponderant view.
The text uses 'صحیح اور راجح قول کے مطابق' indicating a preferred position.
2
Note the name and lineage of the author of Manar
Read the biography: the author's name, kunya, laqab, and the origin of his nisba 'al-Nasafi'.
Nasaf is a region in Turkestan, giving the author a geographical attribution.
3
List the teachers of al-Nasafi
Identify the three teachers mentioned: Muhammad ibn 'Abd al-Sattar al-Kardi, Hamid al-Din al-Darir, and Badr al-Din Khawahir Zadah.
These scholars represent his transmission chain in Hanafi usul and fiqh.
4
Enumerate his major works
Note the works listed: Madarik al-Tanzil (tafsir), Haqa'iq al-Ta'wil, Kanz al-Daqa'iq (fiqh), al-Wafi and its commentary al-Kafi (usul), etc.
Manar is the most famous usul text among these, hence his recognition.

Imam Abu Hanifa: The First Codifier of Usul al-Fiqh
Biographical statement asserting priority in codification.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the Hanafi claim regarding the first codifier of Usul al-Fiqh and identify the significance of this claim for the school's methodology.

Core Idea
Imam Abu Hanifa, not al-Shafi'i, was the first to codify the principles of Islamic jurisprudence — a claim that anchors Hanafi usul.

The history of Usul al-Fiqh is often traced to Imam al-Shafi'i's Risala. However, the Hanafi school maintains a different genealogy. In this lesson, we examine a short but foundational statement that credits Imam Abu Hanifa as the first to systematically write on usul. This claim is not merely biographical; it shapes how Hanafi scholars view the authority and antiquity of their usul principles.

Guided Path
1
Identify the claim
Read the Arabic text and identify what is being stated about the first codifier of Usul al-Fiqh.
The claim directly names Imam Abu Hanifa as the first codifier.
2
Note the qualifier
Observe the phrase 'صحیح اور راجح قول' (correct and preferred opinion). What does this imply about the status of this claim?
This indicates that there are differing opinions, but the Hanafi school holds this as the strongest view.
3
Contrast with common perception
Reflect on the common attribution of the founding of Usul al-Fiqh to Imam al-Shafi'i. How does this Hanafi claim differ?
The Hanafi school credits its own founder, not al-Shafi'i, with the first systematic codification.
4
Consider the implications
Why does it matter for Hanafi methodology that the founder of the school also founded its usul?
This claim strengthens the authority of Hanafi usul principles as being earliest and most authentic.
5
Memorize the key phrase
Internalize the Arabic text as a historical anchor: 'اصول الفقہ کا مدون اول امام اعظم ابوحنیفہ ہیں۔'
This phrase is a memorization anchor for the school's historical claim.

This establishes the foundational role of the Hanafi school in the development of usul al-fiqh.

Key Terms

Hafiz al-Din al-Nasafi: Author of Manar
Providing biographical details of the author of Matan al-Manar

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify Hafiz al-Din al-Nasafi and state his key works, especially his contribution to Hanafi usul al-fiqh through Manar.

Core Idea
Hafiz al-Din al-Nasafi, author of Manar, is a key Hanafi usul scholar whose works span tafsir, fiqh, and usul.

Before diving into the text of Manar, it is essential to know its author. This lesson provides a brief biography of Hafiz al-Din al-Nasafi, his teachers, and his major works. Understanding his scholarly background helps appreciate the authority of Manar in the Hanafi tradition.

Guided Path
1
Identify the author's name and lineage
Note his full name: Abdullah ibn Ahmad ibn Mahmud, his kunya Abu al-Barakat, title Hafiz al-Din, and nisba al-Nasafi (from Nasaf in Turkestan).
2
List his prominent teachers
His teachers include Muhammad ibn Abd al-Sattar al-Kardari, Hamid al-Din al-Darir, and Badr al-Din al-Khwarizadah. These link him to earlier Hanafi authorities.
3
Name his major works beyond Manar
Works include Madarik al-Tanzil (tafsir), Kanz al-Daqa'iq (fiqh), al-Wafi and its commentary al-Kafi. This shows his breadth across disciplines.
4
Recognize the significance of Manar in Hanafi usul
Manar is a foundational text in Hanafi usul al-fiqh, widely studied in madrasas. Knowing its author's credentials strengthens confidence in the text.

Mullā Jīwan: Life and Legacy of the Author of Nūr al-Anwār
Biographical sketch of Mullā Jīwan.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the key biographical facts about Mullā Jīwan and explain their relevance to the study of his commentary Nūr al-Anwār.

Core Idea
Knowing the author's background illuminates his scholarly context and the authority of his work.

This lesson introduces you to the life of Mullā Jīwan (also known as Mullā Jeevan), the celebrated Hanafi scholar who wrote Nūr al-Anwār, a foundational commentary on the uṣūl text Manār. Understanding his biography—his teachers, his travels, and his legacy—helps you appreciate the authority and transmission of his work within the Hanafi tradition. As we explore his journey from memorizing the Quran at seven to being buried in his own madrasa, you will see how personal dedication shapes scholarly output.

Guided Path
1
Identify the author and his lineage
Read the passage to find the author's name (Ahmad), his father's name (Abu Sa'id), and his lineage tracing to Abu Bakr al-Siddiq. Note the honorific 'Mullā Jīwan' by which he is known.
Lineage to a Companion carries weight in the scholarly tradition.
2
Note his early education
He memorized the Quran at seven and studied most books with Shaykh Muḥammad Ṣādiq Turkhī. His sanad (certificate) was from Mullā Luṭfullāh Gōravī Jahānābādī. This shows the chain of transmission.
The sanad connects him to earlier authorities.
3
Record his birth and death years
Birth: 1048 AH, Death: 1130 AH. Note the 50-day delay between death in Delhi and burial in Amethi.
These dates place him in the 11th-12th centuries AH.
4
Connect biography to his work
Reflect on how his teachers and his role as a teacher (buried in his madrasa) influenced the content and style of Nūr al-Anwār.
His commentary reflects the Hanafi uṣūl tradition of his time.
