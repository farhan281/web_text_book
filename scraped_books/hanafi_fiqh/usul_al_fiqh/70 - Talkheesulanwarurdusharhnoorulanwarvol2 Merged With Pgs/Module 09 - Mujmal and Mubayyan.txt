Definition and Ruling of Mushtarak (Homonym)
Defining the homonym and its rule of suspension until preference

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to define mushtarak (homonym) in Hanafi usul and apply the rule of tawaqquf (suspension) to determine the preponderant meaning.

Core Idea
A word with multiple distinct meanings requires suspension of judgment until reflection yields a preponderant meaning.

This passage introduces the concept of mushtarak (homonym) — a word that has multiple distinct meanings used alternatively. In Hanafi usul, such a word cannot be acted upon arbitrarily; instead, one must suspend judgment (tawaqquf) and reflect (ta'ammul) to prefer one meaning over others. The ruling example of the word 'qur' illustrates how the rule operates in practice between the Hanafi and Shafi'i schools.

Guided Path
1
Understand the definition
Analyze the phrase 'ما يتناول افرادا مختلفة الحدود على سبيل البدل' – mushtarak is a word that covers individuals with different definitions in an alternative manner. Note that it does not mean all meanings simultaneously but one at a time.
Alternative (badal) is key; the word is like a label that picks one meaning at a time.
2
Grasp the ruling
The ruling is 'التوقف فيه بشرط التامل' – suspension with the condition of reflection. You cannot proceed until you have reflected and found a preponderant meaning (tarjih). This is a temporary suspension, not a prohibition.
Tawaqquf applies only when there is no immediate preponderance; if one meaning is clearly stronger, no suspension is needed.
3
Analyze the example
The word 'qur' (قرء) is a homonym meaning either menstruation (hayd) or purity (tuhr). Imam Abu Hanifa interpreted it as hayd, Imam al-Shafi'i as tuhr. This shows how the rule of preponderance is applied in ijtihad.
The passage hints that taking qur as hayd 'realizes both blood and days' – this may be a clue for tarjih.
4
Relate to usul al-fiqh
Mushtarak is a category of ambiguous words (mujmal) that require interpretation. In Hanafi usul, the method of tarjih involves examining context (siyaq), usage (urf), and other evidence to determine the most likely meaning.
This rule prevents arbitrary interpretation and ensures systematic derivation of rulings.

Bayan Taqrir and Bayan Tafsir: The First Two Types of Clarification
Defines Bayan Taqrir and Bayan Tafsir with Quranic examples.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to define Bayan Taqrir and Bayan Tafsir, provide their respective examples, and distinguish between their functions in Hanafi usul.

Core Idea
Bayan Taqrir removes doubt of metaphor or specification; Bayan Tafsir explains the intended meaning of an ambiguous word.

In Hanafi usul, the way texts from the Quran and Sunnah become clear is classified into five types of bayan (clarification). This lesson focuses on the first two: taqrir and tafsir. Understanding these helps you see how general or ambiguous statements are made definitive and how the Prophet's explanations elaborate on the Quran's commands.

Guided Path
1
Define Bayan Taqrir
Start by articulating the definition: it is strengthening the speech with something that cuts off any possibility of metaphorical or specific meaning. The key is the removal of uncertainty.
The Arabic definition uses 'ta'kid al-kalam bima yaqta' ihtimal al-majaz aw al-khusus.'
2
Analyze the example of Bayan Taqrir
Consider the verse 'So the angels prostrated, all of them' (فَسَجَدَ الْمَلَائِكَةُ كُلُّهُمْ). Without 'all of them,' one might think only some angels prostrated. The word 'kulluhum' eliminates that possibility.
This is an example of amm made emphatic.
3
Define Bayan Tafsir
Bayan Tafsir occurs when a word's intended meaning is not apparent, and the speaker clarifies it with an explanation. It resolves ambiguity (ijmal) by providing detail.
The definition: 'ma idha kana al-lafz ghayr makshuf al-murad fa kashafahu bi bayanihi.'
4
Analyze the example of Bayan Tafsir
Take the command 'Aqimu al-salah' (establish prayer). The Quran does not detail its pillars or conditions. The Prophet (PBUH) clarified this through his sayings and actions, thus removing the ambiguity.
This shows how the Sunnah acts as a mubayyin for the mujmal in the Quran.
5
Distinguish between the two types
Bayan Taqrir reinforces a statement that already has a clear literal meaning to prevent figurative or restricted interpretations. Bayan Tafsir explains a statement whose meaning is not yet understood. One is emphatic; the other is explanatory.
Both are essential for deriving correct rulings from primary texts.
