Narrator Denial: Inkar Jāmid and Inkar Mutawaqqif
Defects from narrator: denial types

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to distinguish between inkar jāmid and inkar mutawaqqif, apply the Hanafi ruling for each, and evaluate the ikhtilaf with the Shafi'i and Maliki positions.

Core Idea
A narrator's absolute denial voids the hadith; uncertainty leaves room for ikhtilaf.

This passage introduces two types of narrator denial that affect hadith acceptance. Inkar jāmid is clear-cut and unanimously rejected. Inkar mutawaqqif involves the narrator's uncertainty, leading to a dispute among schools. Understanding this ikhtilaf sharpens your ability to assess hadith authenticity in Hanafi usul.

Guided Path
1
Define each type
Identify the two types from the text: absolute denial vs uncertainty. Inkar jāmid: shaikh says 'you lie' or 'I did not narrate.' Inkar mutawaqqif: shaikh says 'I do not know if I narrated.'
These are defects from the narrator side.
2
Identify the ruling for inkar jāmid
The text states there is no disagreement: such a hadith is discarded (sāqiṭ al-ʿamal).
Unanimous across schools.
3
Identify the ruling for inkar mutawaqqif
Note the ikhtilaf: Hanafis (via Imam al-Karkhi) and Hanbalis discard it; Shafi'is and Malikis accept it.
The disagreement is based on differing usul principles.
4
Map the positions
Organize the schools: Hanafi/Hanbali reject; Shafi'i/Maliki accept. Understand the evidence each side uses.
Hanafi: doubt cannot establish certainty; Shafi'i: lack of denial does not automatically impugn.
5
Extract the usul principle
From the Hanafi perspective, uncertainty in the chain is a defect that prevents reliance on the hadith. This reflects a stricter standard for proof.
This principle applies to other areas of usul.

(۲) انکار متوقف: طالب علم حدیث پڑھے اور شیخ کہے کہ میرے علم میں نہیں کہ یہ حدیث کبھی میں نے تم سے بیان کی تھی ۔

The explanation of the defect that afflicts the hadith from the side of the narrator. It has two types: (1) Inkar Jāmid (absolute denial), (2) Inkar Mutawaqqif (suspensive denial). (1) Inkar Jāmid: A student narrates a hadith before the shaikh, and the shaikh says, 'You lie,' or says, 'I did not narrate this hadith to you.' The ruling of inkar jāmid: Such a hadith is not proof; it is discarded, and there is no disagreement on this. (2) Inkar Mutawaqqif: The student recites the hadith and the shaikh says, 'I do not know that I ever narrated this hadith to you.' The ruling of inkar mutawaqqif: According to Imam Hasan al-Karkhi and Imam Ahmad bin Hanbal, this type is also discarded; according to Imam al-Shafi'i and Imam Malik, this hadith is not discarded but rather must be acted upon.

Commentary
This passage presents two types of defects arising from the narrator's denial. Inkar Jāmid is when the shaikh flatly denies narrating the hadith or accuses the student of lying; such a hadith is unanimously rejected. Inkar Mutawaqqif is when the shaikh expresses uncertainty about whether he narrated the hadith. The Hanafi position (represented by Imam al-Karkhi) agrees with Ahmad bin Hanbal that it is also rejected, while Shafi'i and Malik hold it is valid and must be acted upon. This ikhtilaf stems from differing principles of jarḥ wa taʿdīl: whether uncertainty amounts to impugnment.

Key Terms
انکار جامد (inkār jāmid): The narrator's absolute denial of having narrated the hadith, making it void.
انکار متوقف (inkār mutawaqqif): The narrator's uncertainty about whether he narrated the hadith, leading to disagreement on its validity.

Key Takeaway

Narrator Denial and External Criticism in Hadith
Explains two types of narrator denial and one type of non-narrator criticism with rulings

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to distinguish between categorical and conditional denial of a narrator and apply the Hanafi ruling in cases of uncertainty in transmission.

Core Idea
When a narrator denies or is uncertain about a hadith, the Hanafi school rejects it; the Shafi'i accepts it if uncertain.

This passage from Talkhees al-Anwar equips you with a critical tool in hadith criticism: how to evaluate a hadith when the narrator himself denies it or expresses doubt. You will encounter a clear intra-madhhab disagreement on the second type of denial, and learn the Hanafi position that uncertainty in transmission invalidates the hadith. The passage also addresses criticism from a Companion's action, showing that such external criticism does not automatically impugn the narrator.

Guided Path
1
Distinguish the two types of narrator denial
Identify categorical denial (explicit rejection) and conditional denial (uncertainty).
Categorical denial is unanimous; conditional denial is disputed.
2
Identify the unanimous ruling
For categorical denial, all schools agree the hadith is invalid.
The teacher says 'you are lying' or 'I did not narrate this.'
3
Analyze the disputed ruling
For conditional denial, note the ikhtilaf: Karkhi (Hanafi) says invalid; Shafi'i and Malik say valid.
The Hanafi position follows Karkhi.
4
Apply the Hanafi principle
The Hanafi school requires certainty in transmission; uncertainty invalidates the hadith.
This is a strict usul principle.
5
Evaluate external criticism
A Companion's action contradicting a hadith does not automatically impugn the narrator unless unanimously agreed.
The hadith remains actionable.

اس طعن کا بیان جو راوی کی طرف سے حدیث کو لاحق ہو اس کی دو قسمیں ہیں (۱) انکار جامد (۲) انکار متوقف (۱) انکار جامد شیخ کے سامنے شاگرد کوئی حدیث بیان کریں اور شیخ کہے کہ تم جھوٹ بولتے ہو یا کہے کہ میں نے تم سے یہ حدیث بیان نہیں کی ہے۔ انکار جامد کا حکم ایسی حدیث حجت نہیں ہے یہ ساقط العمل ہے اور اسمیں کسی کا اختلاف نہیں ہے۔ (۲) انکار متوقف: طالب علم حدیث پڑھے اور شیخ کہے کہ میرے علم میں نہیں کہ یہ حدیث کبھی میں نے تم سے بیان کی تھی ۔ انکار متوقف کا حکم : امام حسن کرختی ، امام احمد بن حنبل، کے نزدیک یہ قسم بھی ساقط العمل ہے، امام شافعی ، وامام مالک کے نزدیک یہ حدیث ساقط العمل نہیں بلکہ واجب العمل ہے۔ اس طعن کا بیان جو غیر راوی کی طرف سے حدیث کو لاحق ہو اس کی ایک قسم ہے۔ وہ یہ کہ صحابی کا فعل کسی حدیث کے ظاہر اللفظ والمعنی کے خلاف ہوتو یہ حدیث کے لئے طعن کا سبب ہے۔ طعن مہم کا حکم اس قسم کی حدیث پر عمل کیا جائے گا، اس لئے کہ طعن مہم کے راوی پر جرح کے لئے یہ کافی نہیں کہ جب تک اس کی تفسیر یوں نہ کی جائے کہ وہ بالا تفاق جرح قرار پاتی ہو۔
Statement of the criticism that attaches to the hadith from the narrator's side. There are two types: (1) categorical denial, (2) conditional denial. (1) Categorical denial: when a student narrates a hadith before the teacher and the teacher says 'you are lying' or says 'I did not narrate this hadith to you.' The ruling of categorical denial: such a hadith is not a proof; it is invalid in practice, and there is no disagreement about it. (2) Conditional denial: when the student reads a hadith and the teacher says 'I do not know that I ever narrated this hadith to you.' The ruling of conditional denial: according to Imam Hasan al-Karkhi and Imam Ahmad ibn Hanbal, this type is also invalid in practice; according to Imam al-Shafi'i and Imam Malik, this hadith is not invalid but rather obligatory to act upon. Statement of the criticism that attaches to the hadith from the non-narrator's side: there is one type. That is when the action of a Companion contradicts the apparent wording and meaning of a hadith; this causes criticism of the hadith. The ruling of [this] criticism: such a hadith will be acted upon, because this criticism is not sufficient for impugning the narrator unless it is interpreted in a way that it constitutes a unanimous impugnment.

Commentary
This passage from Talkhees al-Anwar discusses the types of criticism (ta'n) that can affect the authenticity of a hadith. It first addresses criticism arising from the narrator himself. Two categories are presented: categorical denial (inkar jamid) and conditional denial (inkar mutawaqqif). In categorical denial, the teacher explicitly denies narrating the hadith or accuses the student of lying; such a hadith is unanimously rejected. In conditional denial, the teacher expresses uncertainty about whether he narrated it. Here there is a dispute: Imams Abu Hasan al-Karkhi (a Hanafi authority) and Ahmad ibn Hanbal hold that the hadith is also rejected, while al-Shafi'i and Malik hold that it remains valid and must be acted upon. The Hanafi position aligns with Karkhi's view. The passage then turns to criticism from outside the narrator, citing the example of a Companion's action contradicting the apparent meaning of a hadith. In such a case, the hadith is still acted upon because this type of criticism is not sufficient to impugn the narrator unless it is unanimously considered a valid impugnment.

Key Terms
انکار جامد (inkar jamid): The teacher's explicit denial of having narrated the hadith, by saying 'you are lying' or 'I did not narrate this.'
انکار متوقف (inkar mutawaqqif): The teacher's statement that he does not know whether he narrated the hadith.
طعن مہم (ta'n muhimm): Criticism from a non-narrator, e.g., a Companion's action contradicting a hadith's apparent meaning.
