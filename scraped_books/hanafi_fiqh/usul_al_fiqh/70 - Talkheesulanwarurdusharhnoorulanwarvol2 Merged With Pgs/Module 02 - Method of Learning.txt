Definitions of Usul, Fiqh, and Usul al-Fiqh
Definitional text outlining the key concepts of Usul al-Fiqh.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the definitions of asl and fiqh (linguistic and terminological), recite the formal definition of usul al-fiqh, and identify its subject matter and purpose.

Core Idea
Usul al-Fiqh is the methodology for deriving rulings from detailed evidence, distinct from the rulings themselves.

This foundational passage opens the study of Usul al-Fiqh by clarifying key terms. Understanding the precise meanings of 'asl' and 'fiqh' prevents confusion with other Islamic sciences. The formal definition of Usul al-Fiqh establishes it as the tool for a mujtahid to derive rulings, and its subject and purpose define the scope of the discipline.

Guided Path
1
Analyze the component 'usul'
Recognize that 'usul' is plural of 'asl', defined as that on which something else is built. Note the example of the wall for the roof.
This is the ordinary linguistic meaning, not the technical usul sense.
2
Analyze the component 'fiqh'
Distinguish the linguistic meaning (understanding/opening) from the terminological (knowledge of what is for and against a person).
The terminological definition covers both obligations and prohibitions.
3
Synthesize the definition of Usul al-Fiqh
Combine the two: the knowledge of rules enabling a mujtahid to deduce rulings from detailed evidences.
This definition is from 'al-Qunmi' and is standard in Hanafi works.
4
Identify the subject and purpose
The subject is the evidences and the rulings; the purpose is to acquire the ability to deduce.
These define the scope and goal of the science.

Definition of addition: Usul al-fiqh, usul is idafah (construct). Usul is the plural of asl. Asl means: that on which something else is built, as the wall is the asl for the roof. Commentary
This passage is a definitional introduction to the science of Usul al-Fiqh. It first defines the two components of the term: 'usul' (principles) and 'fiqh' (jurisprudence). 'Asl' is defined as that on which something else is built, giving the example of a wall as the foundation for a roof. 'Fiqh' is defined both lexically (understanding) and terminologically (knowledge of what benefits and harms a person, i.e., knowledge of religious obligations and rights). The formal definition of Usul al-Fiqh is then given: it is the knowledge of rules (qawa'id) that enable a mujtahid to derive legal rulings from their detailed evidence. The passage concludes by stating the subject matter of this science (the evidences themselves and the rulings) and its purpose (acquiring the ability to derive rulings). This is a standard Hanafi textbook opening, setting the scope and aim of the discipline.

Key Terms
اصل (asl): That on which something else is built, e.g., the wall for the roof.
فقہ (fiqh): Knowledge of what is for and against a person (i.e., knowledge of legal obligations and entitlements).
اصول الفقہ (usul al-fiqh): The knowledge of rules by which a mujtahid deduces rulings from detailed evidences.

Classification of Khabar by Ittisal: Mutawatir, Mashhur, and Ahad
Introduces the first division of khabar and lists its three types

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the three types of khabar based on kayfiyyat al-ittisal and explain the significance of this classification in Hanafi Usul al-Fiqh.

Core Idea
Khabar is classified into mutawatir, mashhur, and ahad by the manner of its transmission chain.

In this passage, the author introduces the first division of khabar by its manner of connection (kayfiyyat al-ittisal) from the Prophet (S). He emphasizes that this classification is rooted in Usul al-Fiqh, not Hadith principles, although there is overlap. Understanding this distinction is crucial for properly weighing the epistemic value of different reports in deriving rulings.

Guided Path
1
Identify the classification scope
Recognize that the passage is discussing the first of four divisions (presumably of Sunnah) and focuses on the manner of transmission chain continuity.
2
List the three types of khabar
The passage explicitly states: khabar mutawatir, khabar mashhur, and khabar ahad. Each represents a different level of transmission reliability.
3
Understand the disciplinary context
The author clarifies that this classification belongs to Usul al-Fiqh, not Hadith terminology. This means it is used for juridical reasoning, not just textual criticism.
Although there is overlap with Hadith classifications, the Hanafi usul framework prioritizes the legal implications of each type.

Commentary
This passage is a classification lesson. The author begins by stating that the division of Sunnah into four categories (mentioned earlier) each have multiple sub-types, and clarifies that this classification is based on Usul al-Fiqh, not Hadith terminology, though there is overlap. Then he explains the first division: 'the manner of connection (kayfiyyat al-ittisal) from the Prophet to us.' Under this, he lists three types of khabar: mutawatir (mass-transmitted), mashhur (well-known), and ahad (single). This sets the framework for the detailed discussion of each type that follows.

Key Terms
خبر (khabar): A report or narration, specifically from the Prophet (ﷺ), used as a source of Islamic law in Usul al-Fiqh.
کيفية الاتصال (kayfiyyat al-ittisal): The quality or manner of connection of the hadith from the Prophet (ﷺ) to the later generations.
