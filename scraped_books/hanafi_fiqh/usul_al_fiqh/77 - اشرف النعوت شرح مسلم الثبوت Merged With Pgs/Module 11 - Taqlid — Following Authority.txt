Why the Muqallid is Excluded from the Definition of Fiqh
Explaining the exclusion of muqallid from the definition of fiqh

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to explain why the Hanafi usulists exclude the muqallid from the definition of fiqh and identify the precise source (mujtahid's statement) that obligates his action.

Core Idea
The muqallid's knowledge comes from the mujtahid's statement, not conjecture — this excludes him from fiqh's definition.

In defining fiqh as knowledge of practical shari'a rulings from detailed evidences, the Hanafi scholars had to clarify the status of the muqallid. A common doubt is whether the muqallid's reliance on the mujtahid's conjecture gives him the same certain knowledge. This passage resolves that doubt and reinforces the boundary of fiqh.

Guided Path
1
Identify the definition of fiqh in Hanafi usul
Recall that fiqh is defined as 'knowledge of practical shari'a rulings derived from detailed evidences' (al-ʿilm bi al-aḥkām al-sharʿiyya al-ʿamaliyya min adillatihā al-tafsīliyya).
This definition is the baseline for determining who is a faqih.
2
Recognize the muqallid's source of knowledge
The muqallid obtains his knowledge of the ruling solely from the statement (qawl) of the mujtahid, not from his own or the mujtahid's conjecture (zann).
The mujtahid's statement is not considered a detailed evidence in the technical sense.
3
Analyze the misconception (wahm)
Some may think the muqallid's knowledge derives from the mujtahid's conjecture, bringing him under the definition. The passage refutes this by showing that if the mujtahid does not communicate his conjecture, the muqallid is not obligated.
The obligation to act depends on the statement being conveyed, not on the internal conjecture.
4
Understand why conjecture is insufficient
Neither the muqallid's own conjecture nor the mujtahid's conjecture can obligate the muqallid. Only the transmitted statement creates the obligation for him.
This distinction separates the muqallid from the mujtahid and excludes him from the definition.

Cannot be a means of the rulings, nor is the mujtahid's conjecture authoritative for him. Therefore the muqallid is not included in the definition in any way. His statement: 'As for the muqallid, his authority is the statement of his mujtahid...' Through this expression, a misconception is removed. Here a misconception could arise: The muqallid also obtains certain knowledge through the conjecture of his mujtahid; so what is the difference between the knowledge of the mujtahid and the knowledge of the muqallid, since both have certain knowledge? The answer to the misconception: In obtaining knowledge of the obligation to act on the rulings, the muqallid's authority is not his own conjecture nor his mujtahid's conjecture. That is, the proof for the muqallid knowing the shar'i ruling is the statement of the mujtahid. His own personal conjecture or his mujtahid's conjecture is not sufficient for the muqallid's knowledge. Thus, if the mujtahid conjectures a ruling but does not tell his muqallid, the muqallid is not obliged to act, even though the mujtahid is obliged to act according to his conjecture. In short, neither the mujtahid's conjecture nor the muqallid's conjecture benefits the muqallid. Therefore, the muqallid's knowledge is excluded from the definition of fiqh from this aspect as well: that the muqallid's knowledge of the obligation to act is obtained neither from the mujtahid's conjecture nor from his own conjecture, but from the mujtahid's statement. And it is obvious that the mujtahid's statement is not from the detailed evidences, whereas the definition mentions detailed evidences.

Commentary
This passage is a commentary on the definition of fiqh, addressing the status of the muqallid (follower). The author explains why the muqallid does not fall under the definition of a faqih. The key point is that the muqallid's knowledge of the obligation to act on a ruling comes solely from the statement of the mujtahid, not from any conjecture—neither the muqallid's own nor the mujtahid's. This is illustrated by the scenario where a mujtahid forms a conjecture (zann) about a ruling but does not communicate it; the muqallid is not obligated to act. The mujtahid, however, is obligated by his conjecture. Thus, the muqallid's source is the mujtahid's statement, which is not part of the detailed evidences (adilla tafsiliyya) required in the definition of fiqh. Hence, the muqallid is excluded. The passage also clarifies that even if the mujtahid's conjecture leads to certain knowledge for him, it does not transfer that certainty to the muqallid.

Key Terms
مقلد (muqallid): One who follows a mujtahid without knowing the detailed proofs; his authority is the mujtahid's statement.
ظن (zann): Conjecture or predominant opinion, which is the basis for a mujtahid's rulings but does not directly obligate the muqallid.
فقه (fiqh): Knowledge of practical shari'a rulings derived from detailed evidences.
