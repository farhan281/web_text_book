Including the Muqallid in Mutawassil: Hanafi Definitional Expansion
Refuting an objection to expanding mutawassil definition

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the Hanafi definition of mutawassil and state how it includes both mujtahid and muqallid, using the concept of relative tahqiq.

Core Idea
Mutawassil includes muqallid: evidence is the imam's statement; tahqiq is relative.

This passage from the sharh addresses a crucial definitional point in Hanafi usul al-fiqh: who qualifies as mutawassil (one who reaches rulings through evidence)? While a mujtahid's tawassul is direct, a muqallid follows his imam's rulings. The objection that muqallid lacks tahqiq is refuted by showing that tahqiq is relative—for the muqallid, certainty in the imam's correctness suffices. This expansion defines mutawassil broadly, modifying the phrase from 'ila al-fiqh' to 'ila masail al-fiqh' for the muqallid.

Guided Path
1
Understand the two categories
Recognise that 'mutawassil' in Hanafi usul is a general term that can apply to both mujtahid (direct from sources) and muqallid (via imam's statement). The passage explicitly includes both.
The mujtahid's evidence is the four primary sources; the muqallid's evidence is the mujtahid's qawl.
2
Identify the objection
The opponent argues that even if we change 'ila al-fiqh' to 'ila masail al-fiqh', the muqallid still fails the condition of 'ala wajh al-tahqiq' because only the mujtahid performs tahqiq.
The objection assumes tahqiq is uniform.
3
Apply the Hanafi response: Relative Tahqiq
The text argues that tahqiq is relative: for the mujtahid, it is derivation from sources; for the muqallid, it is certainty that his imam's opinion is correct. Thus the muqallid does have tahqiq in his own way.
The muqallid can say: 'The statement of my mujtahid is verified for me.'
4
Note the definitional modification
When including the muqallid, the definition of mutawassil is slightly altered from 'reaching fiqh' to 'reaching masail al-fiqh' because fiqh proper is the mujtahid's knowledge.
This preserves the technical meaning of fiqh while including the muqallid.

مجتہد اور مقلد دونوں مراد ہوں اب مجتہد کا متوصل ہونا ظاہر ہے اور مقلد بھی متوصل بن سکتا ہے اگر اولہ میں تقسیم کر دیں کہ ادلہ عام ہیں کہ ادلہ اربعہ ہوں یا ان کے ماسواہ تو اب مقلد بھی متوصل بن سکتا ہے کیونکہ مقلد بھی احکام کو دلیل سے معلوم کرتا ہے لیکن فرق صرف اتنا ہے کہ مجتہد کے لئے دلیل ادلہ اربعہ سے ہے اور مقلد کے لئے دلیل مجتہد کا قول ہے چنانچہ مقلد کہے گاهذا الحكم ثابت لانه ادى اليه رأى ابي حنيفة وكل حكم ادى اليه رأى ابي حنيفة فهو ثابت عندى لیکن جب متوصل عام ہو گا تو تعریف میں تھوڑی سی تبدیلی کریں گے۔ الی الفقہ کی بجائے الی مسائل الفقہ کہیں گے ۔ کیونکہ مقلد مسائل فقہ کو جانتا ہے۔ فقہ تو ادلہ اربعہ سے احکام کو جانے کا نام ہے اور یہ مجتہد کا کام ہے اور مقلد تو صرف مسائل جان سکتا ہے۔ وقولنا علی وجہ التحقیق:۔ اس عبارت میں مائن نے سوال مقدر کا جواب دیا ہے۔ سوال:۔ متوصل کو عام کرنا درست نہیں۔ اسلئے کہ اگر چہ الی الفقہ کی بجائے الی مسائل الفقہ کہ دیں کیونکہ پھر بھی مقلد علی وجہ التحقیق کی قید سے نکل جائے گا کیونکہ تحقیق مجتہد کرتا ہے ملقد نہیں کرتا۔ جواب:۔ یہ علی وجہ اتحقیق کی قید مقاید کے متوصل ہونے کے منافی نہیں ہے کیونکہ ہر ایک کی تحقیق علیحدہ ہے۔ مجتہد کی تحقیق یہ ہے کہ وہ ادلہ اربعہ سے احکام کو ثابت کرے اور مقلد کی تحقیق یہ ہے کہ وہ یہ جان لے کہ اس کے امام اور مجتہد کا قول حق ہے۔ تو مقلد کہہ سکتا ہے کہ میرے مجتہد کا قول میرے نزدیک محقق ہے۔ یہ میرے امام کا قول ہے۔ لھذا پھر بھی مقلد متوصل ہو سکتا ہے۔
Both mujtahid and muqallid are intended. Now the mujtahid being mutawassil is apparent, and the muqallid can also become mutawassil if we divide the evidences into general: whether they are the four evidences or other than them. Then the muqallid can become mutawassil because the muqallid also knows rulings from evidence. But the only difference is that for the mujtahid the evidence is from the four sources, and for the muqallid the evidence is the statement of the mujtahid. Thus the muqallid will say: 'This ruling is established because the opinion of Abu Hanifa led to it, and every ruling that the opinion of Abu Hanifa leads to is established for me.' But when mutawassil is general, we will make a small change in the definition: instead of 'ila al-fiqh' we will say 'ila masail al-fiqh' because the muqallid knows the masail of fiqh. Fiqh is to know rulings from the four sources, and that is the job of the mujtahid; the muqallid only knows masail. And our statement 'ala wajh al-tahqiq' — in this expression the author responds to an implied objection. Objection: Expanding mutawassil is not correct because even if we say 'ila masail al-fiqh' instead of 'ila al-fiqh', still the muqallid would fall outside the condition of 'ala wajh al-tahqiq' because tahqiq is done by the mujtahid, not the muqallid. Answer: This condition of 'ala wajh al-tahqiq' is not contrary to the muqallid being mutawassil because each one's tahqiq is separate. The mujtahid's tahqiq is to establish rulings from the four sources, and the muqallid's tahqiq is to know that the statement of his imam and mujtahid is true. So the muqallid can say: 'The statement of my mujtahid is verified according to me. It is the statement of my imam.' Hence the muqallid can still be mutawassil.

Commentary
This passage is a definitional discussion from the sharh. The text clarifies that the term 'mutawassil' (one who reaches fiqh through evidence) is general enough to include both the mujtahid (who derives rulings directly from the four primary sources) and the muqallid (who follows a mujtahid). The objection that a muqallid cannot be mutawassil because his knowledge is not based on 'tahqiq' (verification) is refuted by distinguishing two levels of tahqiq: the mujtahid verifies rulings from the sources; the muqallid verifies the correctness of his imam's opinion. This is a typical Hanafi usul position that allows the muqallid to have a form of reasoning (tawassul) even if it is through the authority of a mujtahid. The passage also modifies the definition of mutawassil from 'knowing fiqh' to 'knowing masail al-fiqh' when applied to the muqallid, since fiqh proper is restricted to the mujtahid. The discussion reflects the Hanafi school's inclusive approach to legal epistemology, recognizing the muqallid's legitimate place in the process of knowing rulings.

Key Terms
متوصل (mutawassil): One who reaches knowledge of fiqh/masail through evidence
مجتهد (mujtahid): One who derives rulings from the four primary sources (adilla arba'a) with independent reasoning
مقلد (muqallid): One who accepts the ruling of a mujtahid without knowing the detailed proof from primary sources
تحقیق (tahqiq): Verification or certainty in the truth of a ruling; different kinds for mujtahid and muqallid
الی الفقہ (ila al-fiqh): Reaching fiqh (knowledge of rulings from evidence)
