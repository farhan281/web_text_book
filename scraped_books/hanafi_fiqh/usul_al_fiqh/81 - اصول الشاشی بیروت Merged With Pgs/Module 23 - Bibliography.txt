The Role of Bibliographies in Classical Usul Works
Listing of sources and references for the book

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the types of sources cited in a classical usul al-fiqh bibliography and explain their significance for establishing scholarly authority.

Core Idea
A bibliography in a usul text is a map of the scholarly tradition, not a mere list.

At first glance, a bibliography may seem like a dry appendix. But in classical Islamic scholarship, the fihris (index of sources) is a deliberate statement of the work's intellectual lineage. It tells you which authorities the author relies on, which schools are engaged, and what evidence base supports the usul principles. This lesson uses the bibliography from Usul al-Shashi to train your eye to read a source list as a scholarly argument.

Guided Path
1
1. Scan the list for work genres
Read through the bibliography and categorize each entry by genre: usul al-fiqh, hadith, biography (tabaqat), or reference (e.g., Kashf al-Zunun).
Notice that the list includes both Hanafi and Shafi'i usul works.
2
2. Identify the madhhab of each author
For each author, determine their madhhab affiliation based on known biographical data. Authors like al-Amidi (Shafi'i), al-Isnavi (Shafi'i), al-Halabi (Hanafi), and al-Khabbazi (Hanafi) appear.
The presence of multiple madhahib shows the text engages with the broader usul tradition.
3
3. Recognize the function of each source type
Usul works provide the theoretical framework; hadith collections supply primary evidence; biographical works ensure the reliability of narrators; reference works like Kashf al-Zunun help locate other texts.
Each type serves a distinct role in the usul methodology.
4
4. Reflect on why the bibliography is placed at the end
In many printed editions, the fihris appears at the end of the book. This placement allows the reader to verify sources encountered in the text and to explore the tradition further.
It is a tool for the serious student to trace the chain of knowledge.

Key References in Hanafi Usul al-Fiqh: A Bibliography
Lists classical references for usul al-fiqh and related disciplines

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the major classical works of Hanafi usul al-fiqh and their authors, and classify them by genre (usul, hadith, biography).

Core Idea
Knowing the key texts of a madhhab is the first step to mastering its methodology.

This bibliography, drawn from the end of an usul al-fiqh text, lists essential references a student of Hanafi fiqh should be familiar with. It includes foundational usul works (like al-Sarakhsi's al-Usul and al-Nasafi's Kashf al-Asrar), major hadith collections (Muslim, Nasa'i, Muwatta), and biographical dictionaries (Tabaqat al-Shafi'iyya, al-Jawahir al-Madiyya). Understanding these sources helps contextualize the rulings and usul principles encountered in your studies.

Guided Path
1
Identify the genre of each work
Look at the title: does it contain 'Usul', 'Sharh', 'Sunan', 'Tabaqat', etc.? This tells you whether it is a work of usul al-fiqh, a commentary, a hadith collection, or a biographical dictionary.
For example, 'Kashf al-Asrar' is a commentary on a usul work, while 'al-Mustasfa' is an independent usul text.
2
Group works by discipline
Sort the listed works into three categories: Usul al-Fiqh (e.g., al-Usul, al-Mustasfa, Irshad al-Fuhul), Hadith (e.g., Sahih Muslim, Sunan al-Nasa'i, Muwatta), and Biographies/Tabaqat (e.g., Tabaqat al-Shafi'iyya, al-Jawahir al-Madiyya).
Some works like al-Jami' al-Kabir (by al-Shaybani) are fiqh compilations, which also serve as sources for usul.
3
Note the Hanafi affiliation
Identify authors known to be Hanafi (e.g., al-Sarakhsi, al-Tahawi, al-Laknawi, al-Nasafi) versus those from other schools (e.g., al-Shafi'i, al-Subki, al-Ghazali). This helps you understand the madhhab perspective.
Works by non-Hanafis are still valuable for comparative usul.
4
Understand the significance
For key works like al-Sarakhsi's 'al-Usul' or al-Nasafi's 'Kashf al-Asrar', note that they are central to Hanafi usul al-fiqh and frequently cited. Similarly, al-Shaybani's 'al-Jami' al-Kabir' is a primary source of Hanafi fiqh.
Knowing the status of each work helps prioritize reading.
5
Memorize key references
Focus on the most cited works: al-Usul (al-Sarakhsi), Kashf al-Asrar (al-Bukhari and al-Nasafi), Musallam al-Thubut (al-Bahari), and al-Mustasfa (al-Ghazali). Commit author and title to memory.
These are the works you will encounter most in your usul studies.
