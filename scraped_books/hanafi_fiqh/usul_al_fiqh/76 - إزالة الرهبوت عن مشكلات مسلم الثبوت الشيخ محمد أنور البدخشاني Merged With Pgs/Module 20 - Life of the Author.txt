Author's Colophon and Completion Note
Author's concluding prayer and date of completion

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to identify the author, work, date, and place of completion from the colophon, and explain its significance for the transmission of Hanafi usul al-fiqh.

Core Idea
A colophon pins down the time and place a scholarly work was completed, grounding its transmission history.

This short concluding passage from Muhammad Anwar al-Badakhshani's commentary on Musallam al-Thubut is more than a signature—it is an anchor. By recording the exact date and location, the author provides a vital link in the chain of Hanafi usul scholarship. For students of the madrasa, such colophons confirm the authenticity and historical context of the texts they study.

Guided Path
1
Identify the author and his role
The colophon names 'Muhammad Anwar al-Badakhshani' as the writer. Note that he is commenting on a foundational Hanafi usul text.
He is the commentator, not the original author of Musallam al-Thubut.
2
Identify the base text
The passage refers to 'Musallam al-Thubut', a well-known Hanafi usul al-fiqh work. Recognize its importance as a matn (text) that later scholars comment upon.
Musallam al-Thubut was authored by Ibn 'Abd al-Shakur.
3
Locate the date and place
Find the specific date: 'the night of Wednesday, the sixth of Sha'ban 1430 AH' and place: 'his residence in Karachi'. This provides a precise historical marker.
This date corresponds to July 2009 CE.
4
Reflect on the significance
Consider why the author included these details. Colophons help authenticate works and show the scholarly chain (sanad) of transmission, a core concern in Hanafi usul.
Knowing the context aids in understanding the development of usul scholarship in the modern era.

Source Text

اللهم لك الحمد والفضل والكرم، ومنك التوفيق والحول والقوة بأن وفقتني باختتام شرح هذا الكتاب المسلّم صعوبته، المعروف تعويته المشتمل على الإيجاز المخل، وعلى الإعجاز المذل أعنى مسلم الثبوت الذي ألهم الله المصنف بتأليفه وبيان تأريخه. قاله وكتبه محمد أنور البدخشاني بمنزله في كراتشي في ليلة الأربعاء السادس من شهر شعبان ١٤٣٠هـ
O Allah, to You is praise, grace, and generosity, and from You is success, might, and power, that You enabled me to complete the commentary of this book, whose difficulty is acknowledged, whose difficulty is well-known, containing both injurious brevity and humbling inimitability—I mean Musallam al-Thubut, which Allah inspired its author to compile and to clarify its date. Said and written by Muhammad Anwar al-Badakhshani in his residence in Karachi on the night of Wednesday, the sixth of Sha'ban 1430 AH.

Commentary
This is a colophon by the author, Muhammad Anwar al-Badakhshani, marking the completion of his commentary on 'Musallam al-Thubut', a foundational text in Hanafi usul al-fiqh. The author praises Allah and acknowledges the difficulty and style of the original text. He records the date of completion as the night of Wednesday, 6 Sha'ban 1430 AH (corresponding to July 2009 CE) in Karachi, Pakistan. This biographical note provides historical context for the work.

Key Terms
خاتمة (khatimah): Colophon; a concluding note in a manuscript providing details of completion.
مسلم الثبوت (Musallam al-Thubut): A standard Hanafi usul al-fiqh text by Ibn 'Abd al-Shakur.
شرح (sharh): Commentary; an explanation of a base text (matn).
