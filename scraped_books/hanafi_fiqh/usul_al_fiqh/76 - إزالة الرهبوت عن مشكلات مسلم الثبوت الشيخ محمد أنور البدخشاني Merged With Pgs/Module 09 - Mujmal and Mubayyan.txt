The Multiple Meanings of Salat in Usul al-Fiqh
Defining 'salat' linguistically and technically, and its contextual meanings

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to distinguish the linguistic and technical definitions of 'salat' and identify how its meaning changes when attributed to humans, angels, and Allah in Quranic usage.

Core Idea
The word 'salat' carries different meanings in the Quran depending on who performs it: supplication, seeking forgiveness, or praise.

In usul al-Fiqh, understanding a word's meaning is essential before deriving rulings. This passage from Muslim al-Thubut defines 'salat'—a key term in Sharia—by first giving its linguistic root (supplication) and then explaining how the Sharia redefines it as the specific ritual prayer. The author also shows that the same word takes on different nuances when attributed to different beings, a crucial observation for interpreting Quranic verses correctly.

Guided Path
1
Identify the linguistic definition
Locate the phrase 'fi al-lughah al-du'a'' and note that the literal meaning of salat is 'supplication' or 'prayer' in a general sense.
This is the haqiqah lughawiyyah.
2
Extract the technical (Sharia) definition
Find the phrase 'fi istilah al-shar''i barat 'an al-af'al al-ma'lumah wa al-arkan al-makhsusah' and understand that in Islamic law, salat refers to specific known actions and specific pillars.
This is the haqiqah shar'iyyah.
3
Analyze the contextual shifts in meaning
Note the three attributions: when salat is 'musnadat ila al-ibad' (attributed to humans) it means du'a; when to angels, it means istighfar; when to Allah, it means thana' (praise) or rahmah (mercy).
This illustrates how context (qarina) can shift meaning from the literal to a figurative or specialized usage.
4
Apply to the verse about sending blessings
Read the final line: 'al-murad bi al-salat fi al-ayah anna Allah wa mala'ikatahu yusallun 'ala al-nabi…' and see that here al-salat is interpreted as 'talab ifadat al-khayr al-kathir' (seeking the bestowal of abundant good) via general metaphor.
This demonstrates the concept of majaz (metaphorical usage) in usul.

2. Linguistically, salat means supplication. In the terminology of Sharia, it refers to known actions and specific pillars; rather it is an act of worship that is the foundation of the religion. When attributed to humans, its meaning is supplication. When attributed to angels, it means seeking forgiveness and asking for mercy. When attributed to Allah, it means praise, as in Sahih al-Bukhari in the interpretation of Surah al-Ahzab; according to others, it means mercy. Here the statement is in the sense of supplication: 'O Allah, send blessings and peace upon our master Muhammad.' The intended meaning of salat in the verse is that Allah and His angels send blessings upon the Prophet, seeking the bestowal of abundant good, by way of general metaphor.

Commentary
This passage defines the term salat linguistically as supplication, and technically as the specific acts of worship (prayer). It then explains that the word takes different meanings depending on the subject: from humans it is supplication, from angels it is seeking forgiveness, from Allah it is praise or mercy. The author clarifies that in the context of sending blessings upon the Prophet, it means supplication from us.

Key Terms
الصلاة (al-salat): Linguistically: supplication; technically: specific acts of worship (the five daily prayers).
