Biography of Mahbub al-Bahari: The Author of Muslim al-Thubut
Biographical account of the author of Muslim al-Thubut

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the key biographical details of Mahbub al-Bahari and explain why his work Muslim al-Thubut occupies a central place in Hanafi usul.

Core Idea
Mahbub al-Bahari, a chief justice under the Mughals, authored Muslim al-Thubut, a foundational text in Hanafi usul.

This lesson introduces the life and scholarly contributions of Mahbub al-Bahari, the author of Muslim al-Thubut, one of the most important matns in Hanafi usul al-fiqh. Understanding his background helps appreciate the authority and transmission of this work.

Various places, then he completely dedicated himself to the study circle of Qutb al-Shams Abadi, becoming an ocean of knowledge and a full moon among stars. He traveled to the Deccan and attended Sultan Alamgir, who appointed him judge of Lucknow, then after a while judge of Hyderabad, which is the capital of the eastern regions of Deccan, then dismissed him, then ordered him to teach his grandson Rafi' al-Qadr ibn Muhammad Mu'azzam. Then when Alamgir at the end of his life delegated the governance of Kabul to his son Muhammad Mu'azzam titled Shah Alam, and he traveled with his son Rafi' al-Qadr from Deccan to Kabul accompanied by the judge. When Alamgir died in Deccan in 1118 AH, and Shah Alam set out from Kabul to the Indian territories, he gave the judge a high position and appointed him as the chief justice (sadr) of all the kingdoms of India, and titled him Fadil Khan in 1119 AH, and he died in that year. His works include: Sullam al-'Ulum on logic, Muslim al-Thubut on usul al-fiqh, and al-Jawhar al-Fard on the indivisible part; these three are accepted and circulated in the schools of scholars. In al-A'lam by al-Zirkili: al-Bahari (d. 1119 AH = 1707 CE): Muhibb ibn 'Abd al-Shakur al-Bahari al-Hindi, a leading judge from the people of Bihar, a great city east of Burb in India, born in a place called 'Karh' (with two fatha). He served as judge of Lucknow, then judge of Hyderabad Deccan, then became chief justice of India, titled Fadil Khan, and soon died. His books: Muslim al-Thubut in usul al-fiqh, al-Jawhar al-Fard a treatise, and Sullam al-'Ulum in logic.
