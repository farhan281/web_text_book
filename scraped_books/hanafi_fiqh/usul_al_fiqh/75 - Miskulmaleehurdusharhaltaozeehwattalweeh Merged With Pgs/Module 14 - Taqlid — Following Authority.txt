Three Madhhabs on Names of Disciplines
Three madhhabs on names of disciplines.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to state the three positions on whether names of disciplines are ism jins, alam shakhsi, or jins, and identify the Hanafi-preferred position.

Core Idea
Names of disciplines are proper nouns (alam shakhsi) in the Hanafi view, not common nouns.

This lesson examines a key usul dispute: are terms like 'al-fiqh' and 'usul al-fiqh' common nouns, proper nouns, or genus nouns? The answer affects how we understand the scope of definitions. We will explore three madhhabs, their proofs, and the Hanafi preference.

Guided Path
1
Identify the three positions
List the three scholars and their classifications: Subki (ism jins), Ibn al-Humam (alam shakhsi), Shidh Sanad Sharif (jins).
Also note the supporter of each: Taftazani aligns with Ibn al-Humam.
2
Examine Subki's proofs
Subki gives three reasons: (1) names apply to a general set, (2) issues increase/decrease (universal property), (3) alif-lam can be added.
These are characteristics of ism jins.
3
Examine the proper noun position
Ibn al-Humam argues that like 'Zayd', the name applies to a whole collection, not individual parts. Taftazani supports this.
This is the Hanafi preferred view.
4
Examine the jins position
Shidh Sanad Sharif differentiates jins from ism jins by mental determination (ta'yin dhihni). Disciplines have this determination, so they are jins.
This is a nuanced intermediate view.
5
Determine the practical impact
The classification affects how definitions are constructed; proper nouns cannot be made general by adding alif-lam.

The names and proper nouns of sciences and disciplines, such as al-nahw, al-sarf, al-mantiq, al-fiqh, etc. — these names of disciplines and sciences are either ism jins (common noun) or jins (genus noun). There are three madhhabs regarding this. (1) The madhhab of 'Allamah Subki: these names are ism jins, like al-rajul, al-ma', al-sharab are ism jins. He has three proofs: (i) just as ism jins is placed for a universal matter and applies to few or many, similarly, these names are placed for a universal matter, i.e., a group of specific issues. (ii) The issues of a discipline increase and decrease, which is also a characteristic of a universal, because the actual referent of a discipline does not increase or decrease. (iii) The definite article alif-lam can enter upon these names, like al-mantiq, al-sarf, etc., whereas it cannot enter upon proper nouns. (2) The second madhhab, that of 'Allamah Ibn al-Humam: these names are from the category of proper nouns (alam shakhsi). His proof: they possess the characteristics of a proper noun. Just as 'Zayd' applies to a whole composite, not just a hand or head, similarly usul al-fiqh applies to the entire collection of issues, not just a single issue. The commentator Taftazani supported his own madhhab by saying 'wa usul al-fiqh is a name' — that these names are proper nouns. (3) The madhhab of Shidh Sanad Sharif: these names are jins (genus noun), not ism jins, because in ism jins there is no consideration of mental determination, while in jins there is. In these names, mental determination is considered. For example, usul al-fiqh is the subject for issues that exist in the mind cumulatively. Therefore, these are jins, not ism jins. His statement: 'As for defining it with respect to addition (idafah), it requires defining the mudaf, which is al-usul, etc.' The purpose of the commentator is to explain the text. In the matn, the author said that the definition by addition requires defining the mudaf and mudaf ilayh. The commentator gives the proof for that. Summary of the proof: usul al-fiqh is an idafi compound, and the rule is that the definition of a composite is dependent on defining its constituent parts that are unclear and not obvious. This is because the composite is the whole and its parts are like components, and knowledge of the whole is dependent on knowledge of the parts. Therefore, the definition by addition requires defining the mudaf (which is al-usul) and defining the mudaf ilayh (which is al-fiqh). Question: Why did the commentator say 'al-mufradāt ghayr al-bayyinah'? Answer: Because if the constituent parts are clear and obvious, then the definition of the composite is not dependent on them, nor is there need to define those parts. Question: Usul al-fiqh is an idafi compound, and the rule is that knowledge of the mudaf in respect of being mudaf is dependent on the mudaf ilayh. Therefore, the definition of the mudaf ilayh (al-fiqh) should be given before the definition of the mudaf (al-usul), as some scholars defined al-fiqh first and al-usul later. But the author Sadr al-Shari'ah and Ibn al-Hajib gave the definition of the mudaf first. What is the reason and point? Answer (1): Prioritizing the definition of mudaf ilayh over the mudaf is obligatory only when both mudaf and mudaf ilayh are absolutely unknown. Where the definition of each is known in some respect, as in usul al-fiqh, then prioritizing mudaf ilayh is not obligatory, only preferable. And leaving what is preferable is permissible for some benefit or point. Here the benefit and point is that discussion on mudaf is brief, while on mudaf ilayh (al-fiqh) it is lengthy. So the definition of mudaf was given first in order to finish quickly and then turn to the definition of fiqh. Commentary
This passage contains a detailed discussion from the sharh (commentary) on two related issues. First, it presents three madhhabs on the nature of names of disciplines (such as al-fiqh, al-nahw, etc.): (a) 'Allamah Subki considers them generic nouns (ism jins) applying to a set of issues; (b) 'Allamah Ibn al-Humam considers them proper nouns (alam shakhsi) because they refer to a specific collection; (c) Shidh Sanad Sharif considers them genus nouns (jins) with mental determination. The commentator Taftazani supports the view that they are proper nouns, as indicated by his earlier statement 'wa usul al-fiqh 'alam' (and usul al-fiqh is a proper noun). In the Hanafi usul tradition, this classification affects how such terms are treated in definition and derivation. Second, the passage addresses the order of definition for compound terms like usul al-fiqh. The matn had defined 'usul' before 'fiqh', but the logical rule (since the mudaf is known only through the mudaf ilayh) would require defining fiqh first. The commentator provides three justifications for the matn's order: (1) priority is mandatory only if both are completely unknown; here they are partially known; (2) following the order of mention; (3) an incomplete third reason cut off by the page. This reflects the Hanafi method of careful analysis of textual sequence and pedagogical efficiency.

Key Terms
اسم جنس (ism jins): A common noun that applies to a genus or kind, allowing alif-lam and of indefinite scope.
علم شخصی (alam shakhsi): A proper noun that designates a specific individual or collection.
علم جنس (jins): A genus noun involving mental determination (ta'yin dhihni).

Order of Defining Compound Terms in Usul
Order of defining compound terms like usul al-fiqh.

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to apply the rule that defining a compound requires defining only its unclear parts, and justify the order of definition in usul al-fiqh.

Core Idea
The definition of a compound term only depends on defining parts that are unclear; the order of definition is flexible for pedagogical benefit.

This lesson explores the rule that a compound term like 'usul al-fiqh' requires defining its constituent parts only if they are unclear. We then examine why the author defined 'usul' before 'fiqh' despite the logical priority of mudaf ilayh, and learn three justifications from the commentator.

Guided Path
1
Identify the rule for defining compounds
The rule is: definition of a composite depends on defining its parts that are unclear (ghayr bayyinah). Clear parts need not be defined.
This is a standard usul principle.
2
Apply to usul al-fiqh
Usul al-fiqh is an idafi compound (mudaf + mudaf ilayh). Both parts are partially clear, so definition does not require full definition of each.
3
Examine the logical order
Logically, mudaf ilayh (fiqh) should be defined first because the mudaf is understood through it. But the author defined mudaf (usul) first.
This creates a need for justification.
4
Analyze the three justifications
The commentator gives three reasons: (1) precedence is only obligatory when both are absolutely unknown; here they are partially known. (2) Following the order of mention in the text. (3) (incomplete) If both need definition with respect to idafah.
The third reason is cut off by the page break.
5
Evaluate the practical benefit
The first justification highlights pedagogical efficiency: defining usul first (brief discussion) allows quick completion before tackling the lengthy fiqh definition.

The names and proper nouns of sciences and disciplines, such as al-nahw, al-sarf, al-mantiq, al-fiqh, etc. — these names of disciplines and sciences are either ism jins (common noun) or jins (genus noun). There are three madhhabs regarding this. (1) The madhhab of 'Allamah Subki: these names are ism jins, like al-rajul, al-ma', al-sharab are ism jins. He has three proofs: (i) just as ism jins is placed for a universal matter and applies to few or many, similarly, these names are placed for a universal matter, i.e., a group of specific issues. (ii) The issues of a discipline increase and decrease, which is also a characteristic of a universal, because the actual referent of a discipline does not increase or decrease. (iii) The definite article alif-lam can enter upon these names, like al-mantiq, al-sarf, etc., whereas it cannot enter upon proper nouns. (2) The second madhhab, that of 'Allamah Ibn al-Humam: these names are from the category of proper nouns (alam shakhsi). His proof: they possess the characteristics of a proper noun. Just as 'Zayd' applies to a whole composite, not just a hand or head, similarly usul al-fiqh applies to the entire collection of issues, not just a single issue. The commentator Taftazani supported his own madhhab by saying 'wa usul al-fiqh is a name' — that these names are proper nouns. (3) The madhhab of Shidh Sanad Sharif: these names are jins (genus noun), not ism jins, because in ism jins there is no consideration of mental determination, while in jins there is. In these names, mental determination is considered. For example, usul al-fiqh is the subject for issues that exist in the mind cumulatively. Therefore, these are jins, not ism jins. His statement: 'As for defining it with respect to addition (idafah), it requires defining the mudaf, which is al-usul, etc.' The purpose of the commentator is to explain the text. In the matn, the author said that the definition by addition requires defining the mudaf and mudaf ilayh. The commentator gives the proof for that. Summary of the proof: usul al-fiqh is an idafi compound, and the rule is that the definition of a composite is dependent on defining its constituent parts that are unclear and not obvious. This is because the composite is the whole and its parts are like components, and knowledge of the whole is dependent on knowledge of the parts. Therefore, the definition by addition requires defining the mudaf (which is al-usul) and defining the mudaf ilayh (which is al-fiqh). Question: Why did the commentator say 'al-mufradāt ghayr al-bayyinah'? Answer: Because if the constituent parts are clear and obvious, then the definition of the composite is not dependent on them, nor is there need to define those parts. Question: Usul al-fiqh is an idafi compound, and the rule is that knowledge of the mudaf in respect of being mudaf is dependent on the mudaf ilayh. Therefore, the definition of the mudaf ilayh (al-fiqh) should be given before the definition of the mudaf (al-usul), as some scholars defined al-fiqh first and al-usul later. But the author Sadr al-Shari'ah and Ibn al-Hajib gave the definition of the mudaf first. What is the reason and point? Answer (1): Prioritizing the definition of mudaf ilayh over the mudaf is obligatory only when both mudaf and mudaf ilayh are absolutely unknown. Where the definition of each is known in some respect, as in usul al-fiqh, then prioritizing mudaf ilayh is not obligatory, only preferable. And leaving what is preferable is permissible for some benefit or point. Here the benefit and point is that discussion on mudaf is brief, while on mudaf ilayh (al-fiqh) it is lengthy. So the definition of mudaf was given first in order to finish quickly and then turn to the definition of fiqh. Answer (2): The definition of mudaf was given first because it is mentioned first, so it is given priority in definition as well. Commentary
This passage contains a detailed discussion from the sharh (commentary) on two related issues. First, it presents three madhhabs on the nature of names of disciplines (such as al-fiqh, al-nahw, etc.): (a) 'Allamah Subki considers them generic nouns (ism jins) applying to a set of issues; (b) 'Allamah Ibn al-Humam considers them proper nouns (alam shakhsi) because they refer to a specific collection; (c) Shidh Sanad Sharif considers them genus nouns (jins) with mental determination. The commentator Taftazani supports the view that they are proper nouns, as indicated by his earlier statement 'wa usul al-fiqh 'alam' (and usul al-fiqh is a proper noun). In the Hanafi usul tradition, this classification affects how such terms are treated in definition and derivation. Second, the passage addresses the order of definition for compound terms like usul al-fiqh. The matn had defined 'usul' before 'fiqh', but the logical rule (since the mudaf is known only through the mudaf ilayh) would require defining fiqh first. The commentator provides three justifications for the matn's order: (1) priority is mandatory only if both are completely unknown; here they are partially known; (2) following the order of mention; (3) an incomplete third reason cut off by the page. This reflects the Hanafi method of careful analysis of textual sequence and pedagogical efficiency.

Key Terms
مضاف (mudaf): The first term in an idafah construction (e.g., 'usul' in 'usul al-fiqh').
مضاف الیہ (mudaf ilayh): The second term in an idafah construction (e.g., 'al-fiqh').
مرکب اضافی (murakkab idafi): A compound term formed by idafah (construct state).
