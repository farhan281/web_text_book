Critiquing the Stoning Example: Taqyid by Mashhur or Mutawātir?
Critique of examples of taqyid by mashhur hadith

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to evaluate the classification of a hadith as mashhur vs. mutawātir in meaning and determine its validity for taqyid of a Qur'anic mutlaq.

Core Idea
A ruling that is mutawātir in meaning cannot serve as an example of taqyid by khabar mashhur.

In Hanafi usul, taqyid of a mutlaq text can be done by a hadith that is mashhur (well-known) if it meets certain conditions. However, not every widely-known hadith qualifies as mashhur in the technical sense. This passage examines three common examples and critiques one, arguing that the stoning hadith is actually mutawātir in meaning, not mashhur, and thus belongs to a different category.

Guided Path
1
Identify the Examples
List the three examples given in the passage: (1) flogging verse restricted by stoning hadith, (2) marriage-after-three-divorces verse restricted by hadith al-ʿusayla, (3) wudu' verse restricted by wiping over khuffs report.
The passage focuses on the critique of the first example.
2
Understand the Critique
The author, citing Fath al-Qadir, states that the stoning ruling is mutawātir in meaning (tawātur maʿnawī), meaning it has been transmitted through multiple chains with varying wording but a consistent core meaning that reaches certainty.
This tawātur elevates the hadith above the mashhur level.
3
Apply the Concept of Mutawātir al-Maʿnā
Recognize that a hadith that is mutawātir in meaning is epistemologically qatʿī (certain) in both transmission and meaning. Therefore, it cannot be cited as an example of khabar mashhur, which is zannī.
This distinction is crucial for proper usul classification.
4
Evaluate the Correct Application
Among the three examples, only the hadith al-ʿusayla is accepted as a valid case of taqyid by mashhur. The wiping example is deferred and the stoning example is rejected as it belongs to the mutawātir category.
This exercise sharpens the understanding of hanafi epistemology.

Commentary
This passage is a textual analysis that provides examples typically cited for the restriction of the Qur'an's muṭlaq by a mashhur hadith, and then critiques one of them. The first example is the verse of flogging (al-Nur 24:2) being restricted by the stoning of Ma'iz (i.e., the hadith that establishes stoning for the muhsan adulterer). The author, referencing Fath al-Qadir, notes that this example is problematic because the ruling of stoning is actually mutawātir in meaning (tawātur maʿnawī) — it is so widely known that it has reached tawātur. Therefore, it is not a case of mashhur restricting the Qur'an but rather of mutawātir, which is a different category. The author even mentions that stoning was witnessed in animals like monkeys as a miracle, reinforcing its massive transmission. The second example is the restriction of the verse on divorce (al-Baqarah 2:230) by the hadith of al-ʿUsayla (the hadith that a divorced woman must actually consummate a second marriage before returning to the first). That is accepted as correct. The third example is the verse of wudu' restricted by the report of wiping over khuffs. The author notes that this is also under consideration because the report of wiping is mutawātir in meaning as will be discussed later. Thus, only the second example serves as a valid illustration of taqyid by mashhur.

Key Terms
متواتر المعنى (mutawātir al-maʿnā): A report where the specific wording varies but the meaning is so widely transmitted that it reaches certainty.
خبر العسيلة (khabar al-ʿusayla): The hadith that a woman divorced thrice cannot return to her first husband until she marries another and consummates the marriage.
خبر مشهور (khabar mashhur): A hadith that was transmitted by one or two narrators in the first two generations but later became widely transmitted; it is zannī in thubūt.

When Restriction Enters the Cause: Exception to Haml al-Mutlaq 'ala al-Muqayyad
Hamli rule exception explained via khiyar

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to apply the Hanafi rule that haml al-mutlaq 'ala al-muqayyad is not applied when the restriction (taqyid) enters the cause (sabab), using the example of khiyar al-mutaba'i'ayn.

Core Idea
When restriction enters the cause, the absolute is not carried to the restricted.

In Hanafi usul, the general rule is that an absolute term (mutlaq) is carried to its restricted counterpart (muqayyad) when both share the same ruling and cause. However, this passage from Musallam al-Thubut's commentary discusses a key exception: if the restriction enters into the very cause (sabab) of the ruling, then haml is not applied. This is illustrated through the khiyar al-mutaba'i'ayn (option of dispute between buyer and seller), where the dispute itself is the cause of the mutual oath and return. The qualification that the commodity must still be present modifies the cause, not the ruling, so the absolute remains operative.

Guided Path
1
Identify the rule and its exception
State the general principle of haml al-mutlaq 'ala al-muqayyad and the Hanafi exception: when the taqyid enters the sabab, haml is not applied.
The exception is based on the cause being modified.
2
Understand the khiyar example
Recognize that in khiyar al-mutaba'i'ayn, the sabab is the dispute (ikhtilaf). The muqayyad is 'dispute while the commodity is present'.
The restriction is qiyam al-sil'ah (existence of the commodity).
3
Analyze why haml is not applied
The restriction modifies the cause itself. If haml were applied, the ruling would change, which is not intended. The cause remains the dispute, but the restriction limits the scenario.
Haml would imply the ruling is restricted to cases where the commodity is present only because of the text; here, the context provides the restriction.
4
Address the objection about deletion
A possible objection: could the restriction be understood as deleted from the cause? The author responds that deletion is allowed if meaning is still clear; here, the word 'return' (taradd) implies the commodity's existence.
The context (qarina) suffices, so the absolute text is not changed.
5
Derive the practical fiqh ruling
Conclude that the mutlaq (dispute) is not restricted; the khiyar applies only when the commodity is present, but this is understood from the context of 'return', not from haml.
Thus, the ruling is the same as if haml were applied, but the legal reasoning differs.

Commentary
This passage is a ruling concerning the application of the usul principle of haml al-mutlaq 'ala al-muqayyad (carrying the absolute to the restricted) in the context of khiyar al-mutaba'i'ayn (the option of dispute between buyer and seller). The author argues that in this specific case, the absolute term (such as 'dispute' between them) is not to be restricted by the qualification (that the commodity is still present) because the qualification enters into the very cause (sabab) of the ruling: the dispute itself is the cause for the mutual oath and return. When the cause is qualified, the mutlaq cannot be carried to the muqayyad. Then a research (bahth) is presented: why cannot the restriction be understood as a deletion? The answer: deletion of a part that changes the meaning is only impermissible if the meaning becomes incomprehensible; here, even without explicit mention, the context of 'return' (taradd) implies the commodity is present, so deletion is permissible and the restriction is understood from context. Thus, the absolute remains operative.

Key Terms
الإطلاق والتقييد (al-ITlāq wa al-Taqyīd): The state of being absolute (unrestricted) and restricted (qualified) in legal texts; here, the dispute is absolute but may be restricted by the commodity's existence.
