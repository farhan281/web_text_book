Core Idea
Maulana Ilyas Attar revived the Sunnah through a non-political global movement, embodying Hanafi scholarship and Qadri spirituality.

This passage introduces the founder of Dawat-e-Islami, a contemporary Hanafi scholar and spiritual guide. Understanding his biography gives insight into how classical Hanafi fiqh and Sufi spirituality are transmitted and propagated in the modern world.

Biography of the commentator: He is the embodiment of virtue and perfection, the reference for the people of excellence, envied by his peers, the most learned of the scholars of the time, our master Hafiz Muhammad Barkatullah, may Allah preserve him. His birth was in Sha'ban in the year 1296 AH of the Prophetic Hijra, may the best prayers and greetings be upon its owner, in the land famously known as 'al-Kanū' (with fatha on the lam, sukun on the kaf, fatha on the nun, and a final silent waw), a great town in the land of India. He began the Quran with his uncle al-Mulla Muhammad Fadlullah, who had favor from Allah, the Possessor of generosity and honor. Then he started studying English, but his aforementioned uncle prevented him from it, but he did not pay attention to his words. Then his eyes became inflamed and he lost his sight. Biography of the Commentator
He is the gathering of virtue and perfection, the reference for the people of excellence, envied by peers, the most learned of the scholars of the age, our master al-Hafiz Muhammad Barakat Allah, may Allah preserve him.
His birth was in Sha'ban of the year 1296 AH from the prophetic hijra - may the best prayers and greetings be upon its owner - in the homeland famous as "al-Kan'u" (with fatha on the lam, sukun on the kaf, fatha on the nun, and final waw with sukun), a great city of the land of India. He began the Quran with one who had grace from Allah, the possessor of generosity and honor, his paternal uncle al-Mulla Muhammad Fadl Allah. Then he started studying English, and his aforementioned uncle used to prevent him from it, but he did not heed his words. Then his eyes became sore and their sight went, and the doctors treated them but failed. So his aforementioned uncle said: 'If you leave the English language and undertake to memorize the Quran, you will be cured, Allah willing.' So he accepted that, and Allah cured him by His generosity, favor, and grace. He then occupied himself with memorizing the Quran and completed it in four years. Then he started studying the Arabic sciences and read books upon the virtuous of his time. Then he occupied himself with Persian and Hindi love poetry. Allah had given him a subtle understanding, especially in the science of literature, so he arranged two collections (diwans) in Hindi, and many men studied love poetry under him. When he turned to authorship and composition, he burned his two diwans and turned away from it because it discredits scholars.
He taught Arabic sciences to a group of people, and also many countless people studied Persian sciences under him. He gave the oath of allegiance at the hand of his brother our master al-Hajj al-Shaykh Muhammad Abd al-Ra'uf.
He has many compositions, including: al-Tahqiqat al-Mantiqiyya 'ala Sharh al-Shamsiyya known as Qutbi; al-Ta'liq al-As'ad 'ala al-Hashiya lil-Sayyid; Raf' al-Ishtibah 'an Sharh al-Sullam li-Hamd Allah; Tahqiq al-Atqan 'ala Sharh al-Sullam li-l-Ahsan; Is'ad al-Fuhum 'ala Sullam al-'Ulum; Barakat 'ala Sharh Hidaya al-Hikma li-l-Maybudhi; Tanwir al-Misbah 'ala Mirah al-Arwah; al-Tartib al-Qayyumi 'ala Sharh al-Jami; Hall al-Matalib 'ala al-Kafiya li-Ibn al-Hajib; Irshad al-Tullaba 'ala Ikhwan al-Safa; Izalat al-Khafa' 'an Tarikh al-Khulafa'; Ta'lim al-'Ammi fi Tashrih al-Hussami; Khil'a Rahmani fi Ahwal al-Shaykh al-Jilani - may Allah be pleased with him; Buka' al-'Aynayn fi Shahadat al-Hasanayn - may Allah be pleased with them both; Anwar al-Atqiya' tarjama Tadhkirat al-Awliya'; Mir'at al-Wa'izin tarjama Durrat al-Nasihin; Munyat al-Raghibin tarjama Ghunyat al-Talibin; Anwar al-Hidaya tarjama Sharh al-Wiqaya; al-Ta'liq al-Man'ut 'ala Musallam al-Thubut; Ahsan al-Hawashi 'ala Usul al-Shashi; al-Zawahir al-'Umda tarjama Jawahir al-Khamsa; tarjama Fusus al-Hikam; Risala fi Wiladat al-Nabi salla Allahu 'alayhi wa sallam; Risala fi Ahwal al-Khulafa' al-Rashidin. He has numerous marginalia on most books, including: Panj Ganj, al-Zubda, al-Zanjani, Sarf Mir, al-Dariri, al-Kubra, al-Tahdhib, Mukhtasar al-Mizan, Aysaghuji, Qala Aqul, Ghunyat al-Mutamalli, al-Quduri, Sharh al-Asbab wa al-'Alamat, Mukhtasar al-Ma'ani, Nafkhat al-Yaman. He has a large commentary on al-Fusul al-Akbari and Hashiya al-Rashidiyya, and others. He has endorsements on most Arabic, Persian, and Hindi books - countless; we left them out for fear of verbosity. Most of them have been printed repeatedly, and his compositions indicate the depth of his knowledge.
He has pleasing morals and good deeds, including: moderation in his clothing and food, avoidance of ostentatious dress, true dreams, not wasting money on amusements, humility towards the humble, service to the dear ones, and other things. Speech has been limited due to the narrowness of the place in describing him, and what has been written about him is little compared to what he is in himself.

Ahsan al-Hawashi, a prolific Hanafi scholar, authored extensive glosses on Usul al-Shashi and many other works, exemplifying scholarly breadth and humility.

Place in Context
