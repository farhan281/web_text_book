Taqlid — Following Authority

Definition of Fiqh Excludes Muqallid from Tawassul
Critiques matn's proposal to generalize tawassul

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to explain why the muqallid cannot be said to reach 'fiqh' according to the Hanafi definition, and distinguish between reaching fiqh and reaching masā'il fiqh.

Core Idea
Fiqh is direct derivation from the four proofs; the muqallid only reaches the rulings, not fiqh itself.

The matn proposed that 'tawassul' (the means) could be generalized to include the muqallid. The commentator refines this, arguing that the muqallid does not reach fiqh itself but only the masā'il (positions) of fiqh. This distinction is rooted in the precise Hanafi definition of fiqh as knowledge of sharʻī rulings directly from their four proofs: the Quran, Sunnah, Ijmaʻ, and Qiyās.

Guided Path
1
Identify the matn's proposal
The matn suggested that it is not far-fetched to generalize tawassul (the means to fiqh) to include both the mujtahid and the muqallid, because the muqallid also uses rules to reach the masā'il of fiqh.
2
Locate the commentator's critique
The commentator (shārīḥ) responds that this generalization is far-fetched and that no scholar holds this view. Discussions of taqlīd are only mentioned by way of contrast to ijtihād, because things are known by their opposites.
3
Understand the key distinction: fiqh vs. masā'il fiqh
The commentator explains that if tawassul were to include the muqallid, the definition would need to be changed from 'reaching fiqh' to 'reaching the masā'il of fiqh'. Fiqh is defined as 'knowledge of rulings from their four proofs', which the muqallid does not possess directly.
4
Apply the Hanafi definition of fiqh
Fiqh requires direct derivation from the four sources (Quran, Sunnah, Ijmaʻ, Qiyās). The muqallid's knowledge comes from the authority of a mujtahid, not from the proofs themselves. Therefore, the muqallid does not reach fiqh.
This is a core tenet of Hanafi uṣūl al-fiqh.
5
Conclude the refinement
The definition of tawassul must be restricted to the mujtahid when referring to reaching fiqh. If applied to the muqallid, it must be explicitly rephrased as reaching the masā'il of fiqh.

Explanation: The author (matn) had said that it is not far-fetched to generalize tawassul (the means) to include both mujtahid and muqallid, because just as the mujtahid reaches fiqh through rules, similarly the muqallid reaches (masā’il fiqh) through rules. The commentator says that the apparent is that this is far-fetched, because no one holds this generalization. Those scholars who mention taqlid in their books do so only by way of contrast, because when ijtihad is mentioned, it becomes necessary to mention taqlid as well, since things are known by their opposites.

The commentator said: His statement: “and it is not said ‘to fiqh’ because the muqallid reaches by his rules the masā’il of fiqh, not to fiqh itself, which is knowledge of rulings from their four proofs, because his knowledge of them is not from the four proofs.”

Explanation: That is, if the root is taken as general and includes the muqallid, then a slight change would have to be made in the definition: instead of “by which one reaches fiqh” it would have to be said “by which one reaches the masā’il of fiqh.” The commentator explains the reason: fiqh means knowing rulings directly from the proofs, and the muqallid does not have that ability to derive rulings from the proofs. However, it is possible for him to reach the masā’il of fiqh through the statements of his mujtahid.

Commentary
This passage is a critical refinement where the commentator (shārīh) refines the matn's definition of 'tawassul' (the means by which fiqh is reached). The matn had suggested that tawassul could be generalized to include both mujtahid and muqallid. The commentator rejects this, arguing that no one holds that view, and discussions of taqlid are only for contrast. He further refines the definition: if tawassul included the muqallid, it should be redefined as 'reaching the masā’il of fiqh' rather than 'reaching fiqh', because fiqh is defined as knowledge of rulings from their four proofs (Quran, Sunnah, Ijma, Qiyas), which the muqallid does not directly possess. The muqallid only reaches the positions of fiqh through the authority of a mujtahid.

Key Terms
فقہ (fiqh): Knowledge of shar‘i rulings directly from their four proofs (Quran, Sunnah, Ijma, Qiyas).
توصل (tawassul): The means by which one arrives at fiqh or its masā’il. The sharh distinguishes between reaching fiqh (mujtahid) and reaching masā’il fiqh (muqallid).
