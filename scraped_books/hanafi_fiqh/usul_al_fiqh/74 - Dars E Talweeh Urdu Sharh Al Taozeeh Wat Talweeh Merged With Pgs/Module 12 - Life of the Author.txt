Sadr al-Shari'ah's Motivation and the Concept of Ta'reedh
Lexical explanation and author's purpose for writing

Learning Objective
By the end of this lesson, you will be able to explain why Sadr al-Shari'ah wrote al-Tawdih and identify the respectful nature of ta'reedh (indirect criticism) in Hanafi scholarly discourse.

Core Idea
Sadr al-Shari'ah wrote al-Tawdih to purify and reorganize al-Bazdawi's work, not to disparage it.

In this passage from Dars-e-Talwih, the commentator explains the lexical details of Sadr al-Shari'ah's praise and critique of Fakhr al-Islam al-Bazdawi's foundational work on usul al-fiqh. Understanding this biographical context is crucial for appreciating how later Hanafi authors engaged with prior texts — not just by repeating, but by refining and improving while maintaining respect. This lesson focuses on the concept of ta'reedh and the methodology of scholarly critique within the Hanafi tradition.

Guided Path
1
Identify the central purpose
Read the passage to determine what Sadr al-Shari'ah claimed he did with al-Bazdawi's book. Note the verbs tanqeeh (purification), tahdheeb (refinement), tanzim (rearrangement), and tawdih (clarification).
The purpose is constructive, not destructive.
2
Understand key lexical terms
Focus on the definitions of tanqeeh, tanzim, tanzim, and ta'reedh as given in the lexical commentary. Tanqeeh means removing extraneous matter; tanzim means stringing pearls in order; ta'reedh means indirect criticism.
These terms indicate methodological refinement.
3
Analyze the nature of the criticism
Consider why Sadr al-Shari'ah used ta'reedh (indirect criticism) rather than direct refutation. The commentary implies that the shortcomings (extra material, disorder, lack of logical categories) were respectfully addressed to improve the work's clarity.
Ta'reedh reflects scholarly adab.
4
Relate to Hanafi commentary tradition
Connect this approach to the broader tradition of sharh and hashya, where later scholars often refined earlier works without disrespect. Examples include al-Haskafi's al-Durr al-Mukhtar and Ibn 'Abidin's Radd al-Muhtar.
This is a hallmark of Hanafi methodology.

Dars-e-Talwih. Big stones (rocks) 'al-sa'bah': difficult. 'al-jazlah': thick, big. 'jawahir': pearls. 'nafisah': precious. 'shafataan': both lips. 'al-haajib': eyebrow. 'marmuz': hinted. 'ghawamid': plural of ghamidah, subtle difficulty. 'al-munaqqahah': cleaned. 'al-qadeeb': stick. 'uumiya': past tense passive, hinted. 'mu'akhkhar al-'ayn': back of the eye. 'b': brain. 'tanzim': stringing. 'al-durar': plural of durrah, pearls. 'silk': thread. 'mutanasigah': proportional. 'ta'reedh': striking. 'Usul Fakhr al-Islam': the book 'al-Usul' by Fakhr al-Islam 'Ali al-Bazdawi on usul al-fiqh. 'shati'at': plural of shatit, various. 'maghanid': plural of mughlaq, closed, difficult. Commentary: The author intends to explain the lexical meanings and meaning of the complimentary words the matn said about Fakhr al-Islam 'Ali al-Bazdawi's book 'al-Usul'. 'Jaleel al-shaan' means great in stature. 'Bahir al-burhan' means overwhelming proof: the evidences and proofs of this book are dominant and superior to other books. 'rukooz' is derived from the phrase 'razzartu al-rumha' meaning I thrust the spear into the ground. 'al-kunooz': buried wealth (gold and silver). 'al-sukhoon': 'al-hijaarah al-'izaam': the difficult expressions of Usul Fakhr al-Islam are compared to rocks whose removal is difficult. Its meanings are compared to precious gems under the rock: just as extracting gems and treasure from under rocks is difficult, understanding the meaning from the book of the scholar was equally difficult. 'rumooz': 'ramz' means to signal with lips or eyebrows. The original phrase was 'marmuuz ila ghawamid'; later 'ila' was omitted and 'ghawamid' was made the na'ib fa'il of 'marmuz'. Grammarians call this 'hadhf wa al-isaal' i.e., removing the preposition and directly connecting the verb. 'al-nuktah': a fine point; this word is taken from the phrase 'nakata fi al-ardi bi al-qadeeb' meaning he struck the ground with a stick, leaving a mark. 'al-nazar': looking at something with the full eye and scrutiny. 'al-lahz': looking at something with the corner of the eye, squinting. This is why when addressing a great person for observation, one says 'hadrat mulahizah farmaa dein' meaning only look from the corner of your eye. 'al-tanqeeh': al-tahdheeb, to purify, to sort. It is said 'naqqah al-jidh'a' meaning I pruned the palm trunk, i.e., removed unnecessary extra branches that are not counted as part of the tree's core. 'tanzim' means to string pearls in a suitable order on a thread. Now a summary of the text is presented so that the commentator's lexical explanation becomes clear. Sadr al-Shariah 'Ubayd Allah ibn Mas'ud says: The book of usul al-fiqh composed by 'Allamah Fakhr al-Islam 'Ali was a very great book; great treasures of knowledge were stored in its difficult expressions and subtle points were hinted at. This is why great scholars were completely attentive to benefit from it. But some people, due to their own intellectual weakness, used to object to this book, saying 'what kind of book is this whose meaning is unclear?' whereas this was due to their personal weakness; they could not even understand after much effort what could be understood with minimal attention. Therefore, I intended to do its tanqeeh (purification) and tahdheeb (refinement), give it a new arrangement, clarify its meaning, and bring its divisions according to the rules and principles of logic. That is, the matn made a ta'reedh (indirect criticism) of 'Allamah Fakhr al-Islam that this book had extraneous things that must be omitted, some disorder that needed ordering, some ambiguity that needed clarification, and like the ancients, it did not consider non-interference of categories and did not observe logical conditions, for example, the condition of definition is that it be by genus and differentia, genus first and then differentia, etc. The matn removed all these defects and shortcomings and revised it. The commentator said: his statement 'feehi' means in that described munaqqah, i.e., his book; similarly the pronouns that come after. Here he intends to determine the referent of the pronouns: the pronouns in 'feehi', 'ta'abeh', 'mu'allifihi', 'qaarini' etc. refer to the book 'munaqqah', i.e., al-Tawdih.

Commentary
This passage is a lexical commentary (لغوی تشریح) and background explanation from the Urdu sharh of Dars-e-Talwih. The author first explains the difficult words used by the matn (Sadr al-Shari'ah in his original text Al-Tawdih) when he praised Fakhr al-Islam al-Bazdawi's book Al-Usul. Words like 'jaleel al-shaan' (great stature), 'bahir al-burhan' (overwhelming proof), 'sukhoon' (rocks for difficult expressions), 'kunooz' (treasures), 'ramz' (hints), 'tanqeeh' (cleaning/refining), 'tanzim' (arranging), 'ta'reedh' (indirect criticism) are individually defined. The explanation shows that al-Bazdawi's book was highly regarded but had obscure passages that needed clarification. The commentator then summarizes Sadr al-Shari'ah's own statement: he admired the book's depth and noble intent, but recognized that some scholars struggled with its complexities due to its dense style and lack of logical ordering. Thus, Sadr al-Shari'ah resolved to write Al-Tawdih to 'purify and refine' (tanqeeh wa tahdheeb) the original, reorganize its content, make its meanings clear, and present its divisions according to logical principles. This is not a disrespect; it is a constructive scholarly work of explanation and improvement, as indicated by the word 'ta'reedh' (indirect criticism) used to describe his respectful, indirect critique. Finally, the commentator clarifies that the pronoun 'feehi' and subsequent pronouns refer to the refined book, i.e., Al-Tawdih itself. This passage establishes the context and motivation for Al-Tawdih as a commentary that revitalized al-Bazdawi's work.

Key Terms
تنقيح (tanqeeh): Cleaning, purification, removal of extraneous matter; here specifically the process of revising a text by removing unnecessary parts.
تعریض (ta'reedh): Indirect criticism or allusion; here it refers to Sadr al-Shari'ah's respectful but pointed remarks about the shortcomings of al-Bazdawi's book.
توضیح (tawdih): Elucidation, clarification; the title of Sadr al-Shari'ah's commentary on al-Bazdawi's al-Usul.
تنظیم (tanzim): Arrangement, stringing pearls in order; used here to mean reorganizing the content of the book.
